Double Dutch: Liberal Arts vs Professional Degrees

Kate Meersschaert

February 8, 2013

While searching for this morning's High Five leads I came-across this Inside Higher Ed article that explains a shift in the Netherlands towards liberal arts education. This is a change from the previous emphasis on professional degrees and "professionally" oriented education. The authors assert that this trend towards an emphasis on the liberal arts is spreading and growing throughout the world. However, I have read articles very much to the contrary, that instead, assert that more US liberal arts schools (and those abroad) are moving towards more of a "professional" focus to meet the work needs of students after graduation. Check-out the 5th article on this past High Five for an example. Questions... What do you think these differing trends mean? Is the growing student loan crisis the reason US schools are returning to more of a professional model? Do Dutch and other students worldwide have less of this financial concern? Will we eventually find a new liberal arts/professional degree hybrid? Are the Dutch behind or ahead in their relatively new focus on liberal arts? Here is a quote from the Inside Higher Ed piece:

“You might say it's a paradigm shift: we're now looking more at the talents of students rather than the disciplinary base,” said Dittrich, of the university association.
