Sanctioning College Hackers to Breach Information Systems

jvazquez

January 11, 2015


Cyberthreats are serious business, with big names like Target, Apple's iCloud, and Sony falling prey to big time data breaches. But anyone and everyone is at risk! Seizing an opportunity, Carnegie Mellon University is training a hard line of defense against these attacks through inventive course programming.

Armed with know how that could take down a government (or at least a CVS), students set out to find security vulnerabilities in real systems over the course of a semester. They are actually required to hack into systems as part of their grade. These students are among the best in the world at what they do. Case in point: the CMU hacking team,Plaid Parliament of Pwning, has won numerous Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions. CTF is a computer security competition that allows participants to practice attacking and defending computers and networks.
