Just In Time

Dan Mallinger

November 4, 2006

To miss the midterm elections... Check out Project Vote Smart, a site dedicated to archiving information on America politicians (and you thought libraries were boring!). I had a thought to wrap this site in a fun new way to create either a new site or maybe even a set of tools for political blogs (of all leanings), for schools, or even for a pre-vote catch up. I know a few Chicago schools were switching around curricula to focus on community activism and the claim heard from time to time is that schools will continue in this direction all across the country (but seemingly more so in urban areas). I'm inclined to begrudgingly agree with this if for no other reason than "grassroots" seems to be the new keyword of modern progressive education. What about creating tools to aggregate voting records to present a true view of candidates. One could bias to votes which generated "buzz" on the internet (so the Unintended Pregnancy Amendment is probably more relevant than this bill which reads like a rock floats). One could also bias to votes that were at least contendable (e.g. ignore Kennedy's lone nays where he's clearly making a point with a bill he knows will pass). With a growing public interest in social networks and cognition, how can new tools add transparency to the hotdog making process? Tonight, I'll see what Borat has to say!
