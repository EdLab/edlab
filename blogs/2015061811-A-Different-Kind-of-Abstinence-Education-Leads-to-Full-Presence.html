A Different Kind of Abstinence Education Leads to Full Presence

Patrick Williamson

June 18, 2015

In my undergraduate years at Boston College, I went on many service trips and retreats in which phones were strictly prohibited. What began as mass amounts of anxiety and confusion, ended up teaching me a lot about myself and the attachments that I have in my life. While being constantly connected can seem to be a necessity in this world (in certain situations, missing an email can make or break your goals and aspirations for the next couple of years!), being in situations where everyone around me was disconnected as well lead to the quickest genuine connections that I have felt since growing up in this technology age. Plus, if you are like me and have ever wondered how long the "..." bubble shows up on people phones when you are typing to them, there is a video embedded in the article called the Bubble Test that will answer all of your questions!
