My First Survey

Dan Mallinger

May 16, 2007

I was so excited by Jeff's blog about his brand spanking new site that I decided to try out a similar new tool I had heard about. Although the product was not what I expected, Wufoo turns out to be an amazing survey tool disguised as a "form" tool. Wufoo uses very powerful AJAX to allow users to create a variety of questions, offer additional help, etc. The layout can be modified, there are optional email receipts, and the whole survey can be embedded into an existing page. For example, see an exclusive eDan survey! There are even some light statistics offered (see attached photo). The tool does, however, lack a few fundamental features: there is no educational component, one can't maintain lists of respondents, there are no statistics regarding individual questions - only the survey as a whole, etc. But Wufoo is an incredible starting point with the type of quality user interface which requires careful iterations and which we never have time to produce. Because of this, I would suggest reaching out to Wufoo and seeing if we can't work with them to produce a survey-specific branding of their "form" package. It would save us an uncountable number of man-hours, create a partnership that would increase our "buzz," and be a great way for the EdLab to have an ongoing outside partnership that stretches beyond our standard "client" relationships.
