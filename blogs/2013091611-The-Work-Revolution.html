The Work Revolution

Brian.Sweeting

September 16, 2013

*Private* This Friday I'll be representing EdLab at The Work Revolution Summit, a conference based on Julie Clow's guide to changing the workplace, The Work Revolution. Many of her suggestions for building open and productive work environments are based on her tenure at Google and her experience working with startups with similar cultures. I know at EdLab we've put a lot of thoughtful consideration into intentionally creating a workplace that supports innovation and open communication. So as I prepare to share at the summit, I would love to hear your thoughts about what methods and edtech tools you've either used or researched at EdLab that enable students and workers to:

  • approach problem-solving in creative ways
  • feel empowered as individuals
  • prioritize the most important tasks
  • focus on strengths and passions
  • get into the "flow" (when you get swept along by the positive energy that comes from producing something you're satisfied with)
What are your favorite experiences?
