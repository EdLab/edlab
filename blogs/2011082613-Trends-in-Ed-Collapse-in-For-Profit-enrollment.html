Trends in Ed: Collapse in For-Profit enrollment

Fred Rossoff

August 26, 2011

Well I think the big education news this week was the decline in for-profit new student enrollment reported by the Wall Street Journal. In response to some high profile cases and widespread criticism, the schools have reigned in some of their more aggressive recruiting tactics, which seems to have led to declines in enrollment of 20-40%. Skanda and I discussed for-profit schools a few weeks ago, and it does seem like some of these schools really skate the line between educational institutions and the movie Boiler Room. It's striking that these large drops in enrollment are a direct result of changing recruiter compensation. As the WSJ reports: “Cutting recruiter commissions had a rapid and profound effect at Capella Education Inc., which introduced a new pay structure in January: New-student enrollment dropped 35.8% in the first quarter, compared with a 10.7% decline in the period immediately before the launch.” So these changes look like a step in the right direction, although I personally would like to see less scrutiny of recruitment methods (where only for-profit schools come out looking poorly), and more scrutiny of outcomes, like graduation rates and outgoing debt loads, which are, after all, where the real problem lies.
