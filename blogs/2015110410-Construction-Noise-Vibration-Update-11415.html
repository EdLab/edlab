Construction, Noise, Vibration Update 11.4.15

HSC

November 4, 2015

Since it is too loud to speak or be heard on the 5th floor, I am writing this blog post to communicate recent construction (noise, vibration) developments. The current/loudest source of the noise (and soothing vibrations) on the 5th floor is the chopping of concrete on 120 locations on the junior structural steel beam in the 4th floor ceiling. This work is necessary to hang the pipe grid in the Learning Theater. This work is scheduled to end TODAY (Nov. 4, 2015) but it might run into tomorrow morning. I have been told there is no danger of the 5th floor collapsing onto the 4th floor. The work on the 5th floor setbacks (outside our windows) will be complete by Nov 20. The majority of brick chopping work has been completed. The contractors are primarily doing masonry work on the setbacks. There will noise and smells but significantly less than a month ago. The work on the roof is expected to be completed by the end of the year. There will be some noise but nothing like the concrete chopping on the 4th floor or the brick chopping on the 5th floor setbacks. RH 305 and 306 have been reserved for us through the end of the month. If you cannot work on the fifth floor you can move to 305 or 306. If you need any equipment, we can put it on a mobile cart for you so you can move it back and forth between 4 and 5. Sorry for all the trouble everyone. And thank for all your patience. Learning Theater Games winners will be announced later today.
