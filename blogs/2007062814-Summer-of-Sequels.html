Summer of Sequels

Lu Fan

June 28, 2007

This summer's numerous blockbuster hits are definitely worth spending money on. As opposed to the disappointing Hollywood flicks last year, this summer's movies are actually worth the ten dollars they charge, and maybe even the ridiculously overpriced popcorn and nachos. So far, I've spent nearly a hundred dollars on all the movies that came out in theaters already, and have probably another fifty to go by the time school starts again. What began this summer of sequels was Spiderman 3, supposedly the most expensive movie ever made. Although I'm not a particular fan of Spiderman, I have to say that the sound effect itself was worth the trip to the theater. Next came Pirates of the Caribbean — At World's End. I liked this movie so much I actually saw it twice at the movie theater, and of course Johnny Depp himself made it worthwhile. The list goes on and on, including Shrek the Third, Ocean's Thirteen, Fantastic Four — Rise of the Silver Surfer, Hostel Part II, Evan Almighty, Live Free or Die Hard, and the upcoming Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix, The Bourne Ultimatum, and Rush Hour 3. The trailers for these movies look so incredibly amazing that I stopped flipping channels during commercials just to catch a glimpse of the clip. If you're bored and have nothing to do this summer, I suggest grabbing a few friends and just spending Saturday night at the movies.
