New Learning Times Website Report (Jan - Aug/Sep 2019)

Christine.Odenath

September 25, 2019

I don't want to call this a post-mortem, because that nomenclature is both tragic and imperfect. NLT isn't DEAD per se, moreso in an induced coma. So while the insights here take a past-tense perspective, the hope is that they'll help us build a strategy for the continuation of some of these content streams we (and thousands of users!!) have grown to love.

In case the images don't load, here are some highlights for 2019 YTD:

  • Articles Published: 533
  • Site Visits: 58K
  • Pageviews: 89K
  • Actions: 127K
  • Time spent on site: 271K minutes --> 4.5K hours --> 188 days



Key Takeaways - All-Site:


> 89K total pageviews (PVs) were driven by 58K site visits.


> Had NLT continued to publish through the end of 2019, page visits likely would have exceeded those earned in 2018 and pageviews likely would have been on par with those earned in 2018.¬†


> When compared with 2018 acquisitions stats, a smaller percentage of 2019 visits were direct entries to the site (36% in 2019 vs. 42% in 2018), but a larger portion of 2019 visits were driven by search engines (48% in 2019 vs. 38% in 2018). Given the transition of content from the NLT page to the EdLab blog, increased search traffic may help ease the transition.¬†

Key Takeaways - Content Streams:


> As a content stream, Research Digest earned 2x as many unique PVs (13K) as the cross-stream avg (6.4K).


> Research Digest articles also earned the highest average number of unique PVs* (155 unique PVs per article). EdLab Review and Profiles tied for second with an average of 82 unique PVs per article.¬†


> Outside of the homepage, visitors spent more TOTAL time on Research Digest and EdLab Review articles than any other stream. However, AVERAGE time spent on articles was highest for NL Sector, VisualizED, High Five, and Vialogues streams.¬†


> In addition to boasting higher average view times than their sister streams, NL Sector and Research Digest articles also had lower average exit and bounce rates, suggesting users may find these two streams particularly engaging.¬†
