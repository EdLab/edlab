Trends in Ed: Rise in Student Loan Defaults

Fred Rossoff

September 13, 2011

Yesterday the U.S. Department of Education released data on the default rate for student loans in 2009, and the numbers aren't pretty. The total default rate rose from 7% in 2008 to 8.8% in 2009. The worst numbers, as per usual, came from the for-profit sector, where students were defaulting on their loans at a rate of 15%. Others have argued that these numbers actually substantially under-count the actual default rate, by only measuring the students who default in the first two years. Obviously our malingering economy must be a factor, but we haven't seen default rates this high in about 15 years. One could reasonably expect that more up-to-date numbers would be even worse, but I also hope that the recent moves made against the recruiting practices of for-profit will have cut into the default rate for that sector.
