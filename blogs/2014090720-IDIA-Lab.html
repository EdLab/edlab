IDIA Lab

Carmen James

September 7, 2014


Technology For Good
The IDIA Lab, located at Ball State University and funded by the Lilly Foundation, is a groundbreaking science meets virtual reality think tank that is re-imagining how we learn core subjects. IDIA stands for Institute for Digital Intermedia Arts and it is at the frontier of innovation, engaging specifically with hybrid design technologies and information technology. The Lab, along with its creative and pedagogical projects, also hopes to leverage virtual reality technology for social issues, for example, to teach critical skills needed to combat poverty. In an interview, IDIA director John Fillwalk, stated, "Part of our mission at IDIA is to develop new technology solutions for our expanding range of clients that are interested in engaging next generation media design, to the economic benefit of both the state and the university." Fillwark sees technology as having huge potential for closing the economic and social divide. One project IDIA lab developed, in partnership with Clarian Health, aimed to train nurses before beginning at a new hospital. The project consisted of training videos and interactive media; Clarian Health used the resources to transition nurses to the newly constructed Ball Memorial Hospital New South Tower. IDIA also created a software platform to support Blackboard/Second Life integration that they believe will help expand ideas of distance learning.

Augmented Reality Meets Art
IDIA engages in a wide range of projects that draw on diverse technologies, such as augmented reality. An example of a recent project is Virtual Chichen Itza in Blue Mars. Like Second Life, Blue Mars is a 3D virtual world visitors enter using avatars. IDIA Lab also created an entire city in Blue Mars, where visitors "can virtually tour and examine stunning, realistic re-creations of artifacts and artworks in their original historical contexts, including sculptures at the 1915 Panama-Pacific International Exposition in San Francisco and a centuries-old Buddha at a Japanese temple."
