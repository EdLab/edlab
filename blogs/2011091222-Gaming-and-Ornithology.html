Gaming and Ornithology

Kate Meersschaert

September 12, 2011

I have never played the mobile game Angry Birds. However, while waiting to engage in my civic duty in a lounge with other King's county residents, I overheard a group exclaiming excitedly about their affinity for the game. A nearby yet-to-be-initiated asked innocently, "Why are the birds so angry?" After initial incredulity, the group of Angry Birdophiles responded with "duh" in their voices, "because the pigs stole their eggs." Naturally. Three-hundred million downloads later, Rovio has recently declared that they are the fastest growing consumer brand in history. With merchandise ranging from toys to books, to an upcoming movie deal, the Finnish company has managed to build a global brand around a solitary game. We have explored game dynamics as we develop new projects here are EdLab... what elements of the Angry Birds phenomena can we borrow to make say, a math game more enticing and addictive? What is it about a game that makes it truly "sticky?" I have downloaded Angry Birds and will hopefully be able to intelligently answer the "why are they so Angry?" question soon myself.
