When the Human Body Gets a 4D Treatment

saraehardman

February 12, 2017


Anatomy 4D is an augmented reality app that brings the human body off the page and onto your tablet or phone. Produced by the cutting edge augmented reality company Daqri, this app works with two targets that users can print from the website. When you hold up your screen to a target, the app displays a 4D visualization of either the human body or heart, digitally hovering in the air.

Pros:

The most useful aspect of the app is that it allows users to isolate various parts of the human body and heart. For instance, in the human body visualization, there are 11 systems (including skeletal, respiratory, and reproductive) that you can choose to view either all together, separately, or in various combinations. Likewise, the heart visualization contains 10 different parts (including the aorta, right atrium, and pulmonary trunk) that you can choose how to piece together.

In addition, the anatomy images seem so realistic; you can even hear and see the heart beating. And the app is responsive. When you rotate either the target or the screen, the anatomy image rotates as well, providing a complete 360 degree view of the object.

Cons:

Currently the app only has two available printed targets, so the learning implications are limited to the heart and the human body at large. If there were more targets available, such as one for each organ of the human body, this app could become a standard tool for any anatomy class. As it stands, however, it would be worthwhile only for a few learning sessions.

Our Takeaway:

Anatomy 4D is a perfect example of how augmented reality can enhance science learning. Its 4D visualizations of the heart and human body provide much more realism and interactivity than a 2D image could.
