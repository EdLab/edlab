Santiago: Chilicon Valley of South America

Brian.Sweeting

October 21, 2013

A few years ago I was living in downtown Santiago and I watched as protests for education reform turned violent right outside my living room window. There were many nights when I would lean over my balcony at Universidad Catolica Metro to watch water cannons spraying down groups of students who were throwing rocks at police, riot trucks slamming through makeshift barricades, and even a department store going up in flames. One night I came home from work to see my building's lobby turned into an emergency care room, and there were many days when I would wake up coughing from clouds of lingering tear gas. The frustration over education isn't as vehement now in Chile; while the policies may not have changed significantly, the mood has. Stories about student uprisings have been replaced by stories about young entrepreneurs taking advantage of the small business and startup culture to creatively address problems. In fact, this interview with members of Startup Chile highlights how the capital city is attracting entrepreneurs who can't get a visa in the U.S. or whose business models might not fit in Brooklyn. Maybe it's not surprising news from a city that prides itself on its Starbucks and has nicknamed its new business neighborhood "Sanhattan", but it's encouraging to see Santiago transition from battling protests in El Centro to become South America's center for innovation.
