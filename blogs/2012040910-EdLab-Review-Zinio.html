EdLab Review: Zinio

Kate Meersschaert

April 9, 2012

Zinio, is an interactive ePublication publishing platform. Utilizing a proprietary ZINIO UNITYâ„¢ reading platform and a virtual newsstand of tens of millions of ePubs, they are the largest provider of digital magazine content in the world. What is their special sauce? According to research quoted on their site, Zinio aims to capitalize on the "practice by doing" aspect of multimedia content delivery, that shoots information retention up to 80% from 10% for reading alone. Pros: The company has an "affiliate" program that allows groups to promote their pubs and make a 20% commission on all sales made through banners and other ads on their site. Also, Zinio provides content from multiple publishers, unlike incumbents like NextIssue. Finally, Zinio has "unified" content delivery with a truly device agnostic platform. Cons: There are no price breaks for downloading multiple publications, this is were something like NextIssue, really shines, allowing a user to pay a monthly fee to access all content from a consortium of publishers. Also, there doesn't seem to be a content authoring tool built-in... this would allow DIY publishers to enter the field. Instead, the platform favors established pubs already in print. EdLab Relevance: As we think about options for NLT content delivery, Zinio may be a model to consider, but perhaps not for actual delivery, but instead for revenue. We could explore the "affiliate" program as an alternative to traditional ads. Providing a portal to Zinio's online, global newsstand, while not straying too far from our mission with ads that don't seem to truly fit mission, seems like a win-win possibility. Our Takeaway: Zinio is a slick, design agnostic ecosystem for both promoting and consuming the world's leading digital magazines.
