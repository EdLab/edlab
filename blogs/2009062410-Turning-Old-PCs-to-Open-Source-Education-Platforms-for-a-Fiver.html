Turning Old PCs to Open-Source Education Platforms... for a Fiver!

Molly Riordan

June 24, 2009

Sugar Labs, started by the former president of One Laptop Per Child (OLPC), has split from the $100-laptop organization to found a new venture. He calls his free educational software "Sugar," which can be run from a $5 USB stick. Dubbed "Sugar on a Stick," it turns old Macs and PCs into usable classroom tools. Check out the full article from Technology Review here and look forward to a blog entry from Patrick which will elucidate what the oft-misused term "open source" (or Open Source) actually means.
