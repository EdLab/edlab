Zaption Shutdown Leaves Users In Search of Alternative

jvazquez

September 12, 2016

Following its acquisition this past June by Workday, a publicly-traded human capital management software company, Zaption has announced that it will be shutting down its service on September 30, 2016. Zaption allowed users to add annotations to videos in the form of questions, comments, surveys, and quizzes. It’s impending end is leaving users scrambling for an alternative site to host high-quality, interactive video content. If you find yourself in this boat, let me to offer you a life raft in the form of Vialogues, a video discussion platform created by EdLab of Teachers College Columbia University. There are many solid reasons you might want to give Vialogues a whirl. Among them...

  • It’s free! Really, there’s no catch. Vialogues is absolutely free to use. You can create as many vialogues as you want and invite an unlimited number of people to participate in your video discussions.

  • Test your audience. Get a quick read on whoever is engaging with your vialogue by administering a poll or survey; test their knowledge of a topic with multiple choice questions. This gives you, the moderator, a chance to gauge sentiment and understanding across your audience.

  • Embed vialogues anywhere. You can create portable video discussions by pulling the embed code off of any play page and posting it anywhere that reads HTML tags (i.e., websites, blogs, and LMSs like Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard, and Edmodo). This allows your audience to interact with your video discussion in whatever context you’d prefer.

  • Pick a source, any source. You have an array of options when it comes to sourcing your video discussion. 1. If you have a video file from, say, a lecture or screencast you or your students made, cool! You can upload files in a wide range of supported formats (i.e., mov, flv, mp4, mpg, mpeg, avi, wmv, 3gp). 2. If you’re not sure what video you’d like to use, have a look around the site and see what other educators across a wide range of disciplines are doing. If you see something you like, chances are you’ll be able to create a new vialogue based off of the video they are using (provided that they’re using a public video). 3. You can also choose to embed videos straight from YouTube or Vimeo, our most popular option.

  • We have research on our side. We hold ourselves accountable to our mission to make learning better. As such, we are constantly collecting and analyzing data that allows us to investigate video’s evolving role in online learning and how Vialogues specifically contributes to a conducive learning environment. Here is a sampling of our research: Hsiao, I.H., Chae, H.S., Malhotra, M., Baker, R.S., & Natriello, G. (2014). Exploring engaging dialogues in video discussions. The 7th International Conference on Educational Data Mining. Hsiao, I. H., Malhotra, M., Chae, H. S., & Natriello, G. (2013). Dissecting video discussions and coordination strategies. Vialogues: Videos and dialogues based social learning environment. In Proceedings of the 12th IEEE International Conference on Advanced Learning Technologies, ICALT 2012. (pp. 629-633).
