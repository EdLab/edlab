Puppet Knows Best: Participatory Culture

jvazquez

January 10, 2013


You know who loves puppets? Everyone. Itâ€™s even better when they are smart puppets like The Professor.

In this brief lesson, The Professor explains what a participatory culture is. Spoiler alert: it is essentially a supportive community in which everyone is encouraged to contribute original content, whether it is advice, an answer to a question, art, or personal stories. In doing so, members are able to learn from each other. There is also an informal mentorship component, where experts pass along their knowledge to novices.

Participatory cultures are self-contained environments wherein people get out of it what they put into it. Have you seen good examples of a participatory culture in action, whether online or in person? If so, leave it in the comments of the vialogue.
