Learn and practice math in video game

zhou

May 11, 2010

This is a potential project I propose to do during this summer, and I look forward to getting feedback in May 11's D&R meeting. I have been doing researching in educational games in the past few years, and math game is one of my favorite topics. My theory is a simple math problem-solving game, provided that it is properly designed and scaffolded, is able to not only strengthen skills, but also teach effective strategies. Unfortunately, almost all educational math games that we have in the market are designed for practices instead of improving strategies. Some of them are even so poorly designed that the gameplay has nothing to do with the math concept. In my proposed game, I hope to reverse the above mentioned patterns. This game will be designed based on cognitive and math-learning principals. The stages of this game will help learn and practice strategies to solve different types of elementary math problems. My envisioned platform is iPad/iPhone, because they are the only hardware currently available that supports multi-touch and accelerometer (what are they?). By utilizing these technologies, I expect to strengthen brain-body coordination and spatial abilities, which are another big missed point in most existing video games.
