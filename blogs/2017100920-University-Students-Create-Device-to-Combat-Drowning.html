University Students Create Device to Combat Drowning

Ryan.Allen

October 9, 2017


Due to riptides and other unsafe conditions, drownings in the Great Lakes region have spiked in recent years. Engineering students at Michigan Technological University sought a solution to help curb this growing threat to public safety. One key problem was that rescuers had to jump into the water in order to save a victim, exposing a second person to unsafe conditions.

The team’s solution for these hazardous situations is the Nautical Emergency Rescue Drone (NERD), a remote-controlled lifeguard that can be used from the safety of shore in order to conduct a rescue. The prototype can handle six foot waves, heavy winds, and torrential downpours. NERD can even be flipped upside down and still make its way to the victim.

The team hopes that NERDs can be given to all first-responders and be placed at every beach in the region, saving countless lives. While the price was not mentioned, the concept seems like it would be lower-cost and safer than man power as a first response, making NERD an affordable rescue solution for most any municipality.
