The Surprisingly Slow Growth of Educational Opportunities

Nantwi

November 23, 2014


When it comes to international education, the focus always seems to be on developing countries and the lack of educational (and career) opportunities for students. However, this problem is also prevalent in the developed world. This video highlights data and an interview with an international education expert about the slow growth of educational mobility in many industrialized nations.

According to recent data and analysis from the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), there is a lack of upward educational mobility for individuals in most industrialized countries in Europe and North America. The trend is worse among young and recent graduates with lower educational attainment than their parents. The greatest barrier to educational attainment is often the socio economic background of students. This is especially true in countries (e.g., France, Italy) with inequitable school systems.

How can we reverse this disturbing trend in developed countries? Do you agree with the OECD’s recommendations for improving school systems? Join the conversation on Vialogues.
