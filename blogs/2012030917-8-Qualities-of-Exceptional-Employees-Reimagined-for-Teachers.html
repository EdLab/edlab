8 Qualities of Exceptional Employees... Reimagined for Teachers

Kate Meersschaert

March 9, 2012

Inc.com featured the following list of "8 qualities of remarkable employees". The list was compiled by Jeff Haden, a leading "ghostwriter" in the tech field. I am curious if we can take these qualities and imagine them for teachers and instructors? I have included the original list from the article in bold and followed with my own ed-focused take: 1) They ignore job descriptions. Teachers who go above & beyond what's expected are always exceptional. 2) They're eccentric... Favorite past teachers often are the outspoken, dynamic & yes, sometimes slightly "strange" ones. 3) But they know when to dial it back. You have to work within the system after all. 4) They publicly praise... Giving credit were credit is due for measurable, specific reasons never goes out of style. 5) And they privately complain. Or save it for the teacher's lounge... 6) They speak when others won't. The lone dissenting voice in a meeting, whether with peers, school board members or parents can help push the boundaries of Ed reform. 7) They like to prove others wrong. In this case, past paradigms and beliefs about education. Ed rebels unite! 8) They're always fiddling. Trying new technologies, methodologies and practices in their classes... forward-motion and constant reinvention is good for companies and our school system. What additional "qualities" would you add? Are these important aspects of successful teachers? Why or why not? Employees?
