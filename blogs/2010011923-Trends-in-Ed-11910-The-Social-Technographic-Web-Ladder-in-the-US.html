Trends in Ed, 1.19.10: "The Social Technographic Web Ladder" in the U.S.

Ting Yuan

January 19, 2010

Forrester Research Inc.'s recent survey of 10,112 U.S. web users aging 18 to 88 in November 2009 produced an interesting report about the type of people using social media tools online in the U.S. (see below). One in every three online Americans is what Forrester calls a “conversationalist,” someone who updates her/his status on social networking sites such as Facebook or Twitter at least once a week. As reported, conversationalists are also older, wiser than other online denizens – and predominantly female.

Conversationalists are a new type of online content creator who accounts for 33% of the online population, and they're sandwiched in between the most involved web users who either blog or publish web content – called “creators” (24%) – and the more passive “critics” (37%), who participate online as commenters and reviewers. In the social hierarchy, critics are followed by collectors (20%), joiners (59%), spectators (70%) and inactives (17%).
Some other highlights:
- Taking up 21% of the web population, Gen Y online users (between the ages of 18 and 29) have close concentrations in three categories: Creators (37%), Conversationalist(36%), and Collectors(35%). - The average conversationalist has a annual household income of $81,300. - 48% of collectors have earned a college degree or higher. - In contrast, 81% of mobile internet users are Spectators only (which is something we may anticipate changes in the near future).
