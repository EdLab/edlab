How a Logo Saved New York City

Panisuan Chasinga

July 15, 2015

Last time round I posted about Milton Glaser's book cover design. I want to tell a brief story of how this legendary graphic design icon created the simplest logo that has been distributed and imitated all over the world and how it saved New York from bankruptcy. Consisting of rounded slab serif typeface American Typewriter and a red fillet of a heart, the "I <3 NY" logo was created by graphic designer Milton Glaser and first used in 1977 to promote the city and state after one of its toughest moment in the 1970s.

Crime was at an all-time high, and tourism was at an all-time low; in 1975, President Ford denied federal assistance to save New York City from bankruptcy, and 1977 saw a widespread blackout that led to extensive looting and 4,500 arrests. Tourists stayed away from New York as a result of the negative publicity that followed. The New York State Department for Economic Development tapped Madison Avenue advertising firm Wells Rich Greene (aka Mad Men) to create a tourist-friendly campaign to encourage visitors to The Big Apple.
Young Milton, having already designed a Bob Dylan historic album cover was selected as the logo designer for the "I love NY" campaign. He doodled the initial sketch on his cab ride, and he did it pro bono to save the city. His doodle pleased the Wells Rich Greene and they further developed it into what has become one of the most recognized logo of our time. Here is the more comprehensive version of the story.
