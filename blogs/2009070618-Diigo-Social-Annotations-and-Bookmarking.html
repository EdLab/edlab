Diigo: Social Annotations and Bookmarking

Jeannie Crowley

July 6, 2009

At first glance Diigo may seem a lot like Delicious and other social bookmarking pages. However, Diigo's highlight and comment functions take it to the next level. The Diigo toolbar allows users to share comments about specific elements of a page with anyone in their group. The highlight tool has the potential to impact both the research and design phases of a project. Research Users can add comments to any online publication to share with group members. Imagine an online theory paper covered with stickies that relate specific elements of the publication to the design of the project. Design Users can post prototypes online and group members can highlight and comment upon specific elements of the project. This eliminates the need to have a separate online workspace where group members discuss the design of the prototype. Preview Diigo here: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0RvAkTuL02A
