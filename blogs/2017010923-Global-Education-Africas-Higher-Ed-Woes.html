Global Education: Africa's Higher Ed Woes

idrissa10

January 9, 2017

The increase in online learning opportunities, especially MOOCs and higher education, have made education more accessible. However, opportunities to higher education are increasingly becoming unattainable all across Africa. The continent’s population is predicted to grow to 2.8 billion by 2060, by far the largest increase in any region in the world. The population increase coupled with lack of resources means universities on the continent will struggle to accommodate students. For example, my native Sierra Leone is bigger in size and population than Maryland. However, Maryland has about 55 universities, and Sierra Leone has a total of three. It is predicted that about 17 million people will not have the opportunity to attend higher education in the coming years in Africa. As aforementioned, the increase in online learning opportunities could provide the remedy to Africa’s higher education ills. Technology will inevitably form the heart of how tomorrow’s students learn. This means they could seek online learning opportunities as traditional higher education looks increasingly out of reach.
