VisualizEd - An Elevated Meme Account?

Christine.Odenath

December 13, 2018

Among EdLab employees, VisualizEd seems to be a favorite project.


When I started at EdLab, I asked George whether anyone had considered making VisualizEd into an Instagram account. He said no, then enthusiastically assigned me the task of figuring out where a hypothetical VisualizEd account might fit within the Instagram landscape.  


If you have an Instagram account, you probably follow at least one “meme” account (e.g. @F*ckJerry, @Betches -- *warning* accounts potentially NSFW). Why do meme accounts have such broad appeal? An article by MediaKix attributes the success of meme accounts to some key components:


  1. Quickly digestible content

  2. Appeal to the masses - especially Millennials

  3. Highly shareable


...Sound like VisualizEd? I thought so too. VisualizEd posts have the same short-form wit as many meme accounts, and provide a similar caliber of comic relief. Yet unlike many meme accounts, VisualizEd humor is SFW and, dare I flatter ourselves, intellect-friendly?


The MediaKix article also mentions the rise of category-specific meme accounts, such as the @overheard franchise (check out @overheardnewyork for a painful reminder of why you love to hate/hate to love this overpopulated, beautiful, historied garbage can of a city). College campuses are also hotbeds for content, with accounts like @collegefession putting cringeworthy undergraduate antics on display. While it helps to be an “in” member of these category-specific meme account groups, you don’t necessarily need to be “in” to get the jokes… the second-hand embarrassment is universal.   


The potential audience for VisualizEd is expansive. We’re based in NYC, housed within the storied walls of ivy league academia, and we stand at the intersection of education and technology. Our potential audience could be students, teachers, EdTech fans, New Yorkers, or anyone who keeps up with the daily news. In a nutshell, I’d say our audience could be anyone who might find a crossword puzzle enjoyable.


Because we need to juggle a variety of VisualizEd characters, my recommendation would be either taking the @sesamestreet approach (see below) or just posting the content as-is.


  


...Yes, I follow Sesame Street on Instagram and I’m not ashamed. It’s charming and adorable.


Those are my thoughts. Let me know what you think!
