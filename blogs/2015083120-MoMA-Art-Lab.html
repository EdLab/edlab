MoMA Art Lab

Rebecca Rubenstein

August 31, 2015


MoMA Art Lab is a free app ideal for children ages 6-8. It’s a digital extension of the Art Lab in-person sessions at MoMA. The app features a variety of drawing activities that allow users to experiment freely and to explore the styles of MoMA artists through structured projects involving the use of color, shapes, and even audio.

Pros:

You can create drawings and save them to an individual "gallery" space. You can stop there or you can also explore ways in which to weave your drawings in with larger ideas in the style of artists such as Elizabeth Murray, Arshile Gorky, Henri Matisse, and Alexander Calder. Project activities include making collages, creating mobiles, or experimenting with drawings based on the exquisite corpse game. You can also expand the compositions of your free form drawings by tapping the light bulb, which opens up a series of "ideas" screens.

Cons:

On mobile you can only work with the app in a horizontal orientation. The buttons are just icons with no text so it makes it a bit hard to decipher the function behind each button before tapping it. The "Ideas" screens are separate from the drawing screens. It would be easier to visualize the ideas if these two screens were integrated.

Our Takeaway:

Overall, this is a wonderful, hands-on way to have fun combining shapes, color and line with both structured and more free form projects. You can also learn about the MoMA’s collection, and get more information about their family programs and in-person art labs. Plus if you choose, you can share your gallery of artwork with MoMA.
