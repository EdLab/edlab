Sprinting to the TC Channel?

Eric Strome

April 20, 2007

Folks at Studyplace are putting on a Commonality Sprint after the semester ends to explore possibilities for a peer produced version of a core curriculum. The idea being that, by virtue of its production method, the results could be understood as provisional with respect to the composition of the peer group rather than the traditionally normative & prescriptive project of dubbing (vs. negotiating) a certain tradition as "core". I mention this for two reasons, one of which is shameless solicitation of effort. The other is more directly relevant to EdLab and the TC Channel project. First, I think all of us in the EdLab have unique and valuable perspectives that could contribute to the studyplace Commonality Sprint, specifically where & to what extent work on technology and innovation should figure in every Ed School graduate's program given the trends of the ed sector. Second, how could the general idea of the sprint, pioneered by the open source software community, be put to the use of generating experimental content for the TC Channel? Does the idea of a video production sprint make sense? If so, both the process and product might help to jumpstart the Channel in concrete ways, and help to determine which of our TC Channel brainstorms are feasible and attractive outside the hypothetical environment we explored in the ideation session.
