Picmonic

LauraCostello

July 29, 2013


Picmonic is a mnemonic learning system for prospective medical students taking the MCAT, medical students preparing for certification, and nursing students. The site, which overtly teaches to major tests, was created by Ron Robertson and Adeel Yang, two students then in their second year of medical school. The information is memorization-based and claims to instruct across the learning style spectrum with visual, auditory, and kinesthetic content based around mnemonics. Picmonic has a particular emphasis on visual cues and aims to fix content in the user’s mind through humor and absurdity.

Pros:

Mnemonic learning already has a place in the edtech community, with sites like Quizlet and Memrise aimed at general memorization learning. The effectiveness of mnemonic learning for memorizing content has long been studied and seems effective. Applying this strategy to content-heavy medical testing is a natural step, though it has not been proven that this strategy increases retention for medical professionals in the long run. Picmonic’s method is more specific and comprehensive than either Quizlet or Memrise, with multiple voice and tone options and extremely complex visual mnemonics.

Cons:

Many medical schools have partnered with Picmonic to offer content to students, but reviews seem to indicate that individual subscriptions are still quite expensive. The memorization process, which consists of descriptive slide card explanations, seems time consuming, though this may be a failure of content rather than process.

Our Takeaway:

Picmonic is a novel way to memorize the dry and seemingly endless information required of aspiring medical professionals and in the game of retention, novelty may be a significant advantage. Picmonic is making a valiant effort to inspire students towards greater memorization. The site was clearly developed with an understanding of the difficulty of the content and seems to make light work of a famously arduous task.

The Bottom Line:

Picmonic seems like an effective strategy for memorizing medical content.
