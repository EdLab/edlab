Mind-Reading machines

Max Sklar

June 21, 2010

An article surfaced today highlighting one of the companies working on technology for a direct brain-computer interface: This is a video of them from back in 2007: We're going to see some very basic forms of this technology over the next few years, but as you can see from the videos, it's still a little clunky. The need for this type of thing, especially from the elderly and disabled, is going to drive innovation. Eventually, we'll start using these devices to operate our machines, whether it's gaming or driving generic software applications. This technology will eventually open up a new paradigm for learning software, just as multitouch is now allowing us to try things that didn't work as well before. As the ability to manipulate objects in a virtual space increases, it allows us to take a more hands-on and intuitive approach to learning. However.. a machine that can read your thoughts?? Even if it's just your mood (or concentration level) for now, it seems a bit invasive to me.
