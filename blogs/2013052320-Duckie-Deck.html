Duckie Deck

LauraCostello

May 23, 2013


Duckie Deck is a directory of educational online games for 2 to 5-year-olds. The simple site features ready-to-play games with optional sign-up in the web interface and registration with analytics in the apps for Android and iPhone. The interface is designed for pre-readers with text links tucked away and camouflaged against the background and game links in colorful, wordless blocks in the body of the page. The games are easy to grasp without written or verbal instruction and most focus on social and creative development rather than teaching specific skills or information.

Pros:

The site promotes several target skills for young gamers like hygiene, social skills, ecology, scientific curiosity, and artistic creativity. The games on the site are plentiful and diverse; each one is approved by child psychologists for age-appropriateness. The result is a site that feels safe for individual exploration, though the site recommends that children play with a parent.

Cons:

Limiting screen time is a major concern for parents of young children. Duckie Deck is working toward greater parental control with the development of a browser plugin to limit web users of the site to reasonable play times. The blog is also full of ideas for non-screen interaction and real life activities that promote and reinforce skills learned in-game.
