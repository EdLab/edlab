Vialogues 2012

Angela Lee

July 18, 2011

Do you know why we eat Turkey on Thanksgiving Day? Did you know Trick or Treating existed since Medieval times and Shakespeare mentions the practice in his comedy, The Two Gentlemen of Verona ? There are many holidays and traditions with rich history for us to explore. Learning history through videos is certainly more fun and interactive than textbooks. Kate and I are putting together a "Vialogues Calendar" where short clips of different holiday origins can be uploaded on to Vialogues for discussion. Please see the below list and feel free to add to it. January - New Year's Day, MLK day, Chinese New Year. February - Black History Month, Valentines day, Mardi Gras March - Women's History Month, Daylight Saving Time, St. Patrick's Day April - Poetry Month, April Fool's day, Easter Sunday May - Asian Pacific American Heritage Month, Mother's Day, Memorial Day June - Flag Day, Summer Begins, Father's Day July- Canada Day, Independence Day, Ramadan August- September - Hispanic Heritage Month, Labor day, 9/11 October- Halloween, Columbus day November-American Indian Heritage Month, Veterans' Day, Thanksgiving, Diwali December - Christmas
