Building a community in a powerful and fun(ny) way

Arianna Choi

October 28, 2009

While giving a final shot at shopping for interesting video options for the Educause workshop next week, I found two that are better fitting for us here than for the audience in Denver. #1: Non-traditional Communication/Presentation SkillZ for Academics How about research share-out fun for doctoral students at the Gottesman Libraries? Watch Science Speed-Dating [2min44sec] by Institute for Genome Sciences and Policy at Duke University #2: Online Book Club for Children's Book Lovers for TC or non-TC students, alumni, educators, and parents hosted by the Gottesman Libraries For busy graduate students, professionals, and parents, reading children's books (for roughly, pre-K to grade three) do not require an overwhelming amount of time. A book a week, handpicked by a library staff or a TC community member. Open up asynchronous online discussion forum throughout the week. Then top it off with live group text/voice chat interviewing the author or something like that... it just so happens that these ideas all come from Duke. Blue devils, you good.
