The Apple- Samsung Conflict - Part 2

Oumar Soumahoro

July 5, 2012

I blogged awhile back about the ongoing conflict between Apple and Samsung. The battle focused on Apple suing Samsung for copying their design and other aspects of their products. A judge recently banned the sale of Samsung's Galaxy Tablet citing Apple's claims to patents. Apple has been trying for years now to ban the sale of Samsung's Galaxy Tab 10.1 as it is said to be to the primary rival to the iPad. Considered as the first innovator of the tablet, this ruling essentially means Apple now controls the tablet market, and decides who can or cannot create something similar and it now seems like Apple is trying to adopt a monopoly economic strategy, because they also successfully argued that Samsung had stolen their idea for the Nexus Galaxy. The current court ruling is considered the second big win by Apple over Samsung. I personally think that such actions by Apple is not fair to the competition, because a capitalist economy and a Laissez-Faire business system should allow other companies to create and put on sale items that will satisfy the demand of costumers and bring an equilibrium to the market. Branding and design of products is vital to the success of that product and as such how does or should the EdLab approach the development of future products in light of this ruling and Apple's eagerness to crush competitors?
