Making Math conversation

Demetri Lales

February 14, 2012

STEM Point of View: I bumped into an interesting blog post by Dan Meyer, some of you might of heard his TED talk before about making over the subject of Mathematics. In his interesting blog post, he starts the discussion by showing a picture of a street and asking how tall the lamppost is. Then the flow of the discussion becomes more and more rich with mathematical concepts, all leading to actually finding the height of the lamppost. Read the blog post for yourself to see how he basically goes from something very concrete to something very abstract. Most math classes in public schools only look at the abstract examples, and not at examples that could be relevant to students. Students would become more engaged in the subject if it's more relate-able, more connected to real life, more hands on, and more interactive, among others. STEM subjects does indeed need a major makeover, which many educators are currently in the process of doing, but we still need to do much more for our students so they can better grasp these subjects.
