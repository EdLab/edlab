A Study on Mobile Learning for Young Children

Arianna Choi

February 5, 2009

The Joan Ganz Cooney Center at Sesame Workshop issued a study documenting the untapped potential of mobile learning. This one-month old report draws market trends and model programs and also outlines the first-ever national mobile learning strategy. Topics below were covered in the report as key headings: - Analysis on the current state of mobile learning + future challenges and goals - Inventory of 25+ handheld learning projects in the U.S. and beyond Of the current mobile learning projects noted in the report, Children's Digital Library for the iPhone and Panwapa World for the iPhone seem relevant to what some of us at EdLab are interested in. Both projects look as though they are seeking further R&D but Panwapa World in particular sounds interesting since users are able to see themselves on the Panwapa Map, which is mapped to the GPS grid, and use fingertips to swoop (for spinning) and pinch (for zooming) in order to embed their own photo items from their local environment and browse the photos that others uploaded. Note however that it feels like more of a game than a learning experience as of now. Attached is the full-length report titled, Pockets of Potential: Using Mobile Technologies to Promote Children's Learning
