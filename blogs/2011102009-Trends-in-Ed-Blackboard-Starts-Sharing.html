Trends in Ed: Blackboard Starts Sharing

Demetri Lales

October 20, 2011

There's a growing trend of more open-education materials available online, and now it seems like it's going to become more mainstream soon, with the news that Blackboard will soon have an option for professors to share their curriculum with more people online. There has been increasing requests lately for Blackboard to have a sharing feature, so that professors can share their learning materials, that is one reason Blackboard has changed it's policy on sharing. Professors and other college officials can now share their materials with other educators, accreditation teams, and prospective students without having to worry about being billed for the additional users. This will make for more learning materials free and open online. Blackboard has partnered with Creative Commonsto help make this “Share” feature possible, Creative Commons offers licenses for free content from educators. Blackboard CTO and President Ray Henderson says that the change confirms that they are supporting the mission to deliver education beyond traditional boundaries. Even though they're other websites that allows open sharing it's nice to see this become more mainstream, which will lead to more sharing and collaborating between educators.
