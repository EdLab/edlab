You Say You Want a Printing Revolution?

Ryan.Allen

October 9, 2017


Additive manufacturing, more colloquially known as 3D printing, is a computer guided process of layering materials to generate a product. This technology actually dates back to the 1980s, but these early printers were crude and simplistic. Today, innovation in the sector is rapidly advancing and some are now saying 3D printing will usher in the Third Industrial Revolution.

The commentary from California Management Review asserts that 3D printing will democratize and decentralize the entire manufacturing process. Highly localized 3D printing will become the most efficient and cost-effective means of production. The medical sector provides one revolutionary example. Through additive manufacturing, expensive prosthetics can now be made for just $50.

Despite the positives, this revolution will render some jobs obsolete, especially in low-skilled manufacturing. How can we ensure that education is keeping up with these changes to curb worker displacement? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues
