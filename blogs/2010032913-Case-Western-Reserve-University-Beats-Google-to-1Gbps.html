Case Western Reserve University Beats Google to 1Gbps

Jeannie Crowley

March 29, 2010

In February of this year, Google announced it would place an open access 1Gbps fiber optic network in a local community to serve anywhere from 50,000 to 500,000 residents. This has led a number of communities to engage in desperate and embarrassing name changes to get Google's attention.

Cleveland, Ohio is not one of the competitors. Why not? Case Western Reserve University has launched a research project to see how 1Gbps Internet access will affect the local community. A test block of 104 homes have already been wired. It will be interesting to see how the university invests in upgrades at local libraries in light of new research that indicates over half of poor Americans access the Internet from the local library.
