Trends in Ed 6.17.09

Calli Kosch

June 17, 2009

Innovations in Learning "Games" On June 20th the NY Hall of Science will open its first Science Mini-Golf Course. Designed to make learning about physics and specifically astrophysics fun and interactive, this 9-hole course is full of parabolic arcs, black-holes, and first-hand lessons on gravity. A recent NYTimes article discusses the effectiveness of disguised learning, if you will. While children and tweens may not pay attention to every single informative sign or landmark on the golf course (although it may be hard to ignore the 3 full-size NASA rockets), the course's designers, docents, and parents still believe that visitors will leave with a better understanding of physical science. "There's always this challenge at our museum of accommodating people's desire to play while imparting cognitive change," says the museum's director. Online Interactive Learning Games Sporcle.com is an online learning site that provides trivia and quizzes in several subjects, ranging from entertainment to religion. This might be a fun tool for students, especially middle and high school students, who spend much of their time on the internet anyway (who can forget the days of spending your night chatting on AIM?). For example, this periodic table quiz is an applicable lesson to just about any biology or chemistry class. Sporcle.com is updated with lessons daily. Oh, the trends in education...
