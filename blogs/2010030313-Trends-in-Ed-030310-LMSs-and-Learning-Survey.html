Trends in Ed 03.03.10: LMSs and Learning Survey

Jeannie Crowley

March 3, 2010

As we work to develop a Social LMS, we are thinking about how (and if) learning occurs within traditional LMSs. Blackboard is starting point for discussion. What actually happens within the LMS? I read my books offline, my articles need to be downloaded and read on my computer, links lead me out of the system to other learning areas, I create content outside of the system and then upload it into Blackboard. The question I have is: Are Blackboard, and tools like it, really Learning Management Systems or are they merely content containers in disguise? Simply adding a space for collaboration, such as a discussion board, should not be enough to earn the title "Learning Management".

  • How much learning is occurring and how much work is produced within the system itself?
    • To further explore this question, and to get an idea of how the structure of the system shapes the type of learning that occurs, I am asking for your responses to the very short poll below. I have been known to resort to bribery for feedback, so please let me know your price. :)

    *Note: Our project is titled "Social LMS". By definition, all LMSs are supposed to have a collaborative element. What does it say about the perception of current LMSs' ability to satisfy the collaboration needs of students that we need to add the term "Social" to our project?
