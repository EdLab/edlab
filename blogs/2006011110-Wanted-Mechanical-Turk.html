Wanted: Mechanical Turk

Frank Webster

January 11, 2006

I was playing around with Squidoo (And preparing to trash it) when I stumbled across a lens with a reference to: AmazonMechanicalTurk: Artificial Artificial Intelligence The name is based on an 18-century hoax, a "mechanical" chess-playing automaton that defeated nearly every opponent it faced, including Benjamin Franklin and Napoleon Bonaparte. The secret behind the Mechanical Turk, a life-sized wooden mannequin wearing a fur-trimmed robe and a turban, was a human chess master concealed inside. Amazon now poses the following question for our consideration: "...When we think of interfaces between human beings and computers, we usually assume that the human being is the one requesting that a task be completed, and the computer is completing the task and providing the results. What if this process were reversed and a computer program could ask a human being to perform a task and return the results? What if it could coordinate many human beings to perform a task?" Aside from the fact that I find the concept of the Mechanical Turk hilarious, this may be a clue as to how work will get done in the not-so-distant future.
