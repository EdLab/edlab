Test Run 1 for Learning Theatre (4th Floor) Outreach

jvazquez

September 18, 2013

How do we involve the TC community as we continue to plan for the renovation of the library's 4th floor? How can we be assured that we're addressing the needs of our future Learning Theater consumers? Today's seminar will [hopefully] help us find the answers. Main activity 1. What are three things you wish were available to you that would make you a better student (i.e., more productive, more efficient, more knowledgeable, etc.)? 2. Design at least one idea, focusing on: -Logistics -Personnel/staffing implications -Aesthetics -Etc. Seminar footnotes Resources currently available to students: Instructional MediaLab (265 Macy Hall)- provide equipment, space, and training for shooting and editing video and audio. Help with converting between different digital formats. Provide access to media servers for streaming video content. Game Research Lab(Eggplant) (322 Thompson Hall) - focuses on helping faculty and students discover the richness of game-based play, with a particular emphasis on introducing non-gamers to games beyond the mainstream. Academic Computing (242 Horace Mann) - provides technical support to TC students. Library Education Program- offers students, faculty, and staff diverse ways to engage with educational experts and resources, and also provides an understanding of work being done throughout the College. Graduate Writing Center (155 Thorndike Hall) - offers a variety of writing services to the Teachers College community. During private consultations, students have the opportunity to focus on any aspect of their writing with one of our qualified consultants.
