Economics & Education: (Meta-Yglesias) Yay School-Based Early Childhood Education

Skanda Amarnath

June 13, 2011

While the media's and my own focus tends to point toward secondary and higher education, it is worth stepping back and remembering the importance of early childhood education. I am reminded of the attached graphic from an article in The University of Wisconsin Madison Institute for Research on Poverty on "Inequality in America: What role for human capital policies?" The graphic simply states that most of the inequality develops at early ages and if we really want to attack the problem of unequal opportunity, early childhood education is where to start. That's why this study (via Yglesias done by the Institute of Child Development at the University of Minnesota should be viewed as good news. They examined the effects of the Child-Parent Center Education program on lifetime outcomes such as socioeconomic status and educational attainment. Here's what they found: "Relative to the comparison group receiving the usual services, program participation was independently linked to higher educational attainment, income, socioeconomic status (SES), and health insurance coverage, as well as lower rates of justice-system involvement and substance abuse. Evidence of enduring effects was strongest for preschool, especially for males and children of high school dropouts. The positive influence of 4 years or more of service was limited primarily to education and SES."
