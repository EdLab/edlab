Ten9Eight, a movie about teen entrepreneurship competition

Arianna Choi

November 16, 2009

Last Friday, a couple of the D&R team members went to watch Ten9Eight, a documentary movie about entrepreneurship competition for inner-city youth. The tail-end of synopsis runs like this:

The final competition in New York City convenes 35 young entrepreneurs chosen from over 24,000 students from across the country, winnowed down through a series of city and statewide business plan competitions held throughout the year. The winner receives $10,000 to launch his or her business â€” but more importantly, these finalists have the opportunity to interact with high profile entrepreneurs...Although there can be only one winner, each student is empowered with the promise of a future they never thought possible.
The winner Jessica Cervantes's business plan was to sell cake on edible sticks - called Popsy Cakes. Other plans I remember are customized music service for weddings and private events, organic vegetarian dog treats, custom handmade guitars, innovative eye shield for football helmets, and dance education company for inner city youth. Some of my favorite plans didn't end up making it to the finals. Oh well, but in case you are curious about what happened to Popsy Cakes after the actual competition, here goes www.popsycakes.com!
