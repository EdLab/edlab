Trends in Ed: Clout Beyond Citation

LauraCostello

February 9, 2012

The citation network has long been held as a metric for measuring the impact of scholarly literature, but with readers today far likelier to tweet than cite, new practices have developed to track clout on the web. These methods, called altmetrics, measure the network of references from Twitter and blogs to Mendeley and Zotero. These methods are still developing. Total-Impact, one technology that has developed around the ideas of altmetrics, warns that it shouldn't be used “as indication of comprehensive impact...take it all with a grain of salt.” Because Total-Impact means to track not only traditional scholarly articles, but also other types of files, code, and ideas, structuring the criteria has been difficult. Articles, which buzz with metadata in traditional spheres, lack identifiers beyond a changeable url in ad hoc citations on Twitter. The creators of the system admit that even when reliable identifiers can be found, they'll have to continually monitor for changes and potential system abuse. Altmetrics is still an imperfect system, but In the wake of the Elsevier boycott and the rise of small and open source publishing, it seems prudent to develop ideas around value measurement in academic publishing independent from journal and provider titles.
