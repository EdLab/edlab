Women Can Spur Economic Development

rendy

January 16, 2014

Statistically, women usually reinvest 90 percent of their income to their families and communities. Since the ratification of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW), there've been good promotions in women's education around the world. According to the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR), improving primary and secondary education can increase a girl's future wages between 10-25 percent. In some countries, women don't have the right information, resources or skills and as a result they are unable to contribute to their community's economic growth. As young women eventually become adults, they can act as role models for young girls in their communities. Nowadays, young women should be well prepared for future jobs in STEM. ICT careers are in high demand at the moment, and expected to continue growing. Women who have ICT skills can help develop and deliver these services, even in places where the sexes are traditionally kept separate.
