Understand the City Through Social Media

Sharon Hsiao

August 27, 2012

Every citizen has had long associations with some part of his city, and his image is soaked in memories and meanings.
Kevin Lynch, The image of a City Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) launched the project Livehoods, which analyzes the city by using the tweets, check-ins etc., of everyday live experiences from real people. It uses machine learning techniques to cluster and discover areas of the city. A couple of weeks ago, I blogged about visual data mining, here. That was a story about you-don't-really-need-to-visit-the-city to understand a city. Here's another one: we can study people's live feeds through social media and get to understand a city and identify characteristics of a city. One of the project goals of Livehoods' is to extract the cultural knowledge from social media. By studying people's social interaction routines, Livehoods is able to draw boundaries of neighborhoods. This is pretty amazing and potentially very useful for Amazon local deals, Livesocial, and GroupOn to do the localized commerce marketing. I could also imagine it being used for tourism, and explore the popular city experiences by neighborhoods. For educational purposes, we could use the same technique to identify study groups and resources in our neighborhood.
