Trends in Ed 6.23.09

Calli Kosch

June 23, 2009

Ok, so this isn't 100% education related, but an intriguing and hot topic nonetheless. An article, How the Foodmakers Captured Our Brains details Dr. David Kessler's (author of The End of Overeating: Taking Control of the Insatiable American Appetite take on how combinations of ingredients (i.e. salt, sugar, fat) stimulate the brain's "reward system". This "reward system" works to make us want to eat and do so past the point of full. The sight of chocolate chip cookies, for example, leaves us wanting them even though we may not necessarily be hungry. Simply thinking of the ingredients, the flavors, and the "bliss" (as Dr. Kessler refers to it) of their taste makes them rather irresistible. Let's face it: What might help is the recall of Toll House cookie dough ...making cookies slightly less appealing for the time being. 66 people from 28 states, mostly women and children, have become sick from ingesting the dough (particularly raw) due to traces of E.Coli. As my mom always said in catching me in the act of raw cookie-doughing, "you'll get sick from that!" No worries people, cookie dough ice cream and pre-baked cookies were not infected.
