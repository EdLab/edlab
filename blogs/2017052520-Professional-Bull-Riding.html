Professional Bull Riding

EdLab Studios

May 25, 2017


There's something about being bucked off and stepped on by a 2,000-pound bull that helps you never make the same mistake again. And while the sport of professional bull riding can't be taught, lessons are gleaned from broken bones, pointers from peers, and simply experience. A round of bull riding lasts only 8 seconds but can take an entire career to fully master. And as riders get smarter, so do the bulls who learn to anticipate the moves of their human adversaries over time. What originated on Mexican haciendas as a show of ranch and horsemanship skills during the 16th century is now not only one of the most dangerous sports but also the fastest growing in America. Madison Square Garden becomes a sea of Stetsons and cowboy boots when the annual major league tour of Professional Bull Riders comes to town; creating something of a melting pot of histories and traditions. The thirty-five best riders in the world hail chiefly from the Southwestern and Midwestern United States as well Brazil, bringing their rural culture and competitive attitudes to the big city.
