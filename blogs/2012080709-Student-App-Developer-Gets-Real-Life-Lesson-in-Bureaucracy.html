Student App Developer Gets Real Life Lesson in Bureaucracy

Greg Schrank

August 7, 2012

Dateline Central Florida -- Tim Arnold, a senior at UCF, created an mobile app that checked the UCF class registration site every 60 seconds and let students know if seats in a class had opened up. He sold the app for $0.99 and had 500 users in 6 days. That's when UCF intervened. Charging Arnold with “misuse of computing and telecommunications resources”, he now faces 3 semesters of academic probation, among other disciplinary measures. Obviously, a school has to be concerned with bandwidth, network usage, etc., but was this the appropriate response? Should the school be working with Arnold, instead of punishing him for making a product that improved efficiency at the university? In the end I'm sure UCF will back down and chalk it upto a "misunderstanding", and this kid will go work at Google or Facebook and be wildly successful. Until then, however, what do you think schools should do with students who simply have a better grasp on current technologies than the institution they attend?
