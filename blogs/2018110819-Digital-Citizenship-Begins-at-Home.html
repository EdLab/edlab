Digital Citizenship Begins at Home

Rebecca.Sullivan

November 8, 2018


The concept of digital citizenship is not primarily concerned with what technology can do, but rather with questions about how it should be used. This study examined the influence of parental involvement and socioeconomic status on three areas of digital citizenship: access, etiquette, and safety. Digital access refers to the availability of media to the teenagers, etiquette involves ethical and responsible consumption and sharing of content, and digital safety concerns matters of privacy and well-being.

The researchers surveyed 270 teenagers and their parents. They questioned the parents about their level of education, annual income, and concerns relating to their teenagers’ online behavior and safety. Teenagers were queried about what devices they own, their access to digital media, and their habits of sharing information, receiving content, and engaging in relationships online.

The data analysis revealed that more parental involvement in children’s technology use correlated with higher levels of online etiquette and safety, but had no significant effect on the teens’ access to digital media. Higher socioeconomic status had the greatest influence on digital access, yet was also correlated with better etiquette and safety. The researchers advocated promoting awareness of how parental involvement can improve teenagers’ digital citizenship. They also called for greater consideration of the impact of socioeconomic status on digital citizenship, and highlighted the necessity of supporting children from lower income backgrounds in developing these skills.

Wang, X. & Xing, W. (2018). Exploring the influence of parental involvement and socioeconomic status on teen gigital citizenship: A path modeling approach.Educational Technology & Society, 20(1), 186-199.
