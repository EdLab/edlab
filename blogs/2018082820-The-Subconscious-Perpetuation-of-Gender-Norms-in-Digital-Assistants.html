The Subconscious Perpetuation of Gender Norms in Digital Assistants

saraehardman

August 28, 2018


The first digital assistants that spoke to us were navigation devices like Garmin. I can still remember swapping the Garmin voice between American and British accents, but one thing that never changed was the fact that the Garmin was a she. This trend has continued with digital assistants today like Siri, Alexa, and Google Home. While you can change these devices to speak with a masculine voice, the feminine default setting definitely says something about societal gender norms.

Studies have found that people tend to listen to female voices a little bit more and feel more comfortable asking them to complete tasks. One reason for this could be that assistant, secretary, and phone operator roles have mostly fallen upon women, so people are more accustomed to speaking to female voices in these contexts and therefore might trust a female-voiced digital device more.
