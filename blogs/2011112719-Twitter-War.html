Twitter War

Oumar Soumahoro

November 27, 2011

Couple of days ago, I was browsing the international edition of CNN when I came across this article showing a new battlefield between the NATO and Taliban in Afghanistan: Twitter. After a decade of fighting between the Taliban and NATO forces on the ground, the fighting has also been taken to cyberspace. Both sides are using the social tool as a source of propaganda in a war that sees no foreseeable end. As we have seen with their videos and other promotional tools, the Taliban are very knowledgeable in using media to advance their mission. NATO, which is a combination of all its member states, are usually tight lipped about their missions and seldom use video or other online tools as a form of promotion, which makes this “Twitter War” all the more surprising. Some view this as NATO's strategy in engaging in some sort of talks with the Taliban and also selling the war to an eager West about who is winning the fight in Afghanistan. Is NATO right to engage in this sort of war with the Taliban and what does this say about the war in Afghanistan?
