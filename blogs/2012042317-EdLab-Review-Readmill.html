EdLab Review: Readmill

Kate Meersschaert

April 23, 2012

Readmill provides a platform for users to create a more social community and experience around reading an eBook. Users have the choice of either a bookmarklet for the Kindle or an iPad-based app to share highlights, make recommendations and comment on passages from their eBooks. Basic metrics and data, including the amount of time users spend on each eBook are are also available. Pros: Simple social-media integration. You are always only a few clicks away from sharing your highlights and recommendations on Twitter, Facebook and Tumblr and following other users whose reading habits interest you. Also, the "closing remarks" option provides a fresh take on how readers rate their books. Finally, the platform offers DropBox integration so that users can easily add eBooks to their Readmill account. Cons: Privacy. Your reading habits are more open to the world than say, Goodreads, where they are only shared with your list of approved contacts. However, if you are upset by the lack of "book badging" in the era of eReaders, than this may be perceived as a positive! EdLab Relevance: Social media integration is always a tricky ocean to navigate, and it seems that Readmill has done this well and provides a great example of effective social sharing. Perhaps we can use this as a case for adding more social media integration to PocketKnowledge? Our Takeaway: Readmill is a fun, social way to read on a tablet or eReader, just be sure you are ready to share your reading habits with strangers.
