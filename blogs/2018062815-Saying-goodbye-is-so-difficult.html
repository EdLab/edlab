Saying goodbye is so difficult

zhou

June 28, 2018

Dear EdLab colleagues,


After working 10 years at EdLab, today is my last day here. It is so difficult for me to say goodbye because there has been so much good time and so many good friends at this awesome place. I have received tremendous support all these years from many people especially Gary and Hui Soo. I have learned so much from my colleagues and discovered the career that I have real passion in. I appreciate the opportunities of working on all those cool projects like Vialogues, New Learning Times, YoungArts Master Class, Pressible, and so on, as well as all the research on video discussion assessment, iPad E-readers, Quuppa... These valuable experience and cherishable memories will company me forever.


EdLab is a unique place. I have never heard of any other organization or company like EdLab which has such a rich heritage of history, culture, and scholarship, yet is also modern, innovative, and transformative. Yes we have a lot of problems -- not just at EdLab but also Teachers College, New York City, as well as the whole U.S. But EdLabbers are so willing to fix those problems and make tomorrow better for the children, for other people, and for ourselves. This is the mindset of true educators nowhere else to be seen except for the wonderful places like EdLab. I hope our heritage will be passed on and the great spirit will ignite the motivation and inspiration of more people to embrace educational innovation.


I wish everyone of you a wonderful, fulfilling career at EdLab. I will drop by for sure next time I come back to NYC!


Zhou
