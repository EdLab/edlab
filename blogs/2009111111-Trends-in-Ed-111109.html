Trends in Ed 11.11.09

Jeannie Crowley

November 11, 2009

Bringing the Fun Back I recently stumbled across the Let Them Sing it to You site. This site allows you to enter a string of text and then translates it into a song using popular song snippets for each word. Think of Bumblebee from the Transformers. A site like this would keep my former students (and me) endlessly entertained. Is it possible to keep the spirit of a site like this and apply it to an educational tool? Imagine a site that encourages students to create their own mnemonic devices for math formulas or other information. Song can be a powerful (and fun) long-term memory device that has potential for all ages. I still remember the quadratic formula thanks to a creative 7th grade teacher. To the tune of Row Your Boat, you will also have it stuck forever in your brain:

  • "X equals minus B, plus or minus radical, B squared minus 4 A C, all over 2 A"
