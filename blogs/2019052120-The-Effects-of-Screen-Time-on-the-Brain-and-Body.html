The Effects of Screen Time on the Brain and Body

Melanie.Hering

May 21, 2019


In this day and age, it’s nearly impossible to live a screen-free life. Whether it’s our computers at work, our televisions at home, or our phones at any moment in between, it’s hard to imagine how we would communicate, connect, learn, entertain ourselves, or do our jobs without them. Despite their ubiquity and importance in everyday modern life, many are concerned about the effects digital screens have on our bodies and brains.

Because humans didn’t evolve to spend such long periods of time staring at bright electronic screens, many scientists believe our eyesight is being damaged and that the color emitted by devices is interrupting our circadian rhythm. More alarming is how screen time might be affecting the developing brains of children. While it’s uncertain whether or not these changes are impeding children's learning and lowering school performance, the possibility that they might be is certainly cause for concern.
