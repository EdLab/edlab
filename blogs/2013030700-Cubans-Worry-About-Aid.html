Cubans Worry About "Aid"

Bismark Appiah

March 7, 2013

A few days ago, the President of Venezuela, Hugo Chavez, passed away after a battle with cancer. Chavez was first diagnosed with cancer several years. His death came as something of a shock to many especially as he Chavez was seen not only as a leader but also an advocator who dedicated his life to providing a better living for most Latin Americans, especially Cubans as Chavez shared a particularly close relationship with Fidel Castro. In this article, the publisher discloses the fear that has come upon Cubans since the Chavez's death. This comes mainly from the fear that the supply of oil to their country will be terminated if Venezuela's opposition wins next month's election. Though many Venezuelans and Westerners saw Chavez as an autocrat, Cubans believe he helped shaped “their rigid government in a better position.” The question now is, what is next for Cubans and the people of Venezuela?
