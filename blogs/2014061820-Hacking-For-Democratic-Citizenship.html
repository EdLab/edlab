Hacking For Democratic Citizenship

chingfu

June 18, 2014


For people who have convenient access to the Internet, its importance to their daily lives is clear. People forget that the Internet is structured and engineered with values and choices. Whatâ€™s more, the openness, transparency and participatory culture that everyone enjoys are among its founding principles. Current controversies around net neutrality remind people that the internet might not be neutral, but could be political. Similarly, there are many cases in which technologies do not facilitate or contribute to a more just, open, and equal society. Thus, the speaker of this vialogue, Rafi Santo, argues that kids should not be passive consumers of technologies, but should be active and critical users and makers of them. Furthermore, equipping youth with hacking literacy is fundamental to preparing them to be competent citizens in the future.

Unlike the negative connotations normally associated with this behavior, Rafi sees hacking as not only beneficial to society, but to those who practice it. Do you agree with his ideas? Which parts, if any, seem practical to you? Share you thoughts with us on Vialogues.
