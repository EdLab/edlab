Shmoop me to better thinking about history/literature

bhughes

February 12, 2009

Ran into Shmoop recently, which is a site that wants to lure young learners with the promise of short, hip introductions to everything a student needs to know. So it's a funny site. It seems like it's meant to be a somewhat encyclopedic review of all the topics that might be in a standard curriculum (it calls itself an early beta). And it also highlights a reasonable view of plagiarism with an emphasis on attribution (I like). But how much better than Wikipedia could it be? Well, check out the copy, and you'll find an editorial voice with "young people" in mind. I guess this may be desirable/useful. Time will tell. I wonder how much effort it will take to keep this up to date over time. I think this is another good example of a curriculum-like resource being market towards "professional" students (which I am trying to consistently refer to as a parallel world problem)... An interesting trend to keep an eye on as networked resources replace textbooks.
