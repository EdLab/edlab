NYC’s Newest Campus Is the First Built for the Digital Age

saraehardman

March 18, 2018


Like most aspects of life, academia is changing in the digital age by focusing more on technology and entrepreneurship than ever before. Seeing the digital age as an opportunity for a new kind of higher education experience, Cornell Tech opened its new campus on Roosevelt Island in September 2017 as a shared space for industry and academia. Cornell Tech calls this space "the first ever campus built for the digital age." Among the new campus buildings is one called the Bridge, where students use 30% of the space and industries use 70%.

The goal of the Bridge is to encourage collaboration and dialogue between students and companies. With this in mind, the Bridge’s designers created large, open spaces that allow for both movement and rest where people from various backgrounds can randomly meet and have authentic encounters. Opening the space allows for collaboration to occur across different programs so that students can make connections and companies can stay connected to fresh minds and ideas.

Why might it be beneficial to bring business and academia together? How does environment impact education? Answer these and more in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
