Trends in Ed, 9..25.09

jvazquez

September 25, 2009

Writing for the social web When you publish something on the web, do you find yourself taking on a different writing style? I do. There is something about addressing an internet audience that just makes me write with an air of informality. With an abundance of bloggers, tweeters, and social network status updaters (sorry, couldn't think of a snappy name for them), the web has become more personal and intimate… and I like it. The movement towards a social web means more collaboration, more networking… and more writing. People read differently on the web as opposed to on paper, so writers should hone their craft accordingly. Intrigue, inspire, infuriate, instigate... but do it all while you have their attention. Read this article for links and tips on how to hook and reel in those internet perusers and how to best present yourself as a bonafide internet ink slinger.
