Cubetto, The Code-Teaching Bot For Kids

jvazquez

March 10, 2014


Primo is out to prove that there is no better time to teach kids the basics of programming than when their brains are at their spongiest. The company's debut toy features Cubetto, a small remote-controlled robot on wheels built on the open Arduino standard. The objective is to get Cubetto to his home (represented by a large cube) by creating a simple string of commands on a dashboard-like control box. Kids plug directional wooden blocks into the control box to get Cubetto moving. Simple directions like forward, left, and right form the foundation for algorithmic thinking. The box is a physical representation of a programming interface; Cubetto is the real-life version of a program's front-end. Through simple game play, kids grow accustomed to the thought processes and logic behind computer programming.

Before they can even write their names, kids can learn the basics of computing with Cubetto. This is a notable distinction for the wooden robotic set; most other code-teaching programs for kids rely on literacy and screen time whereas Primo is wholly tangible.
