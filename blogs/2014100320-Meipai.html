Meipai

Ting Yuan

October 3, 2014


Meipai is the first free mobile app of its kind for microvideo creation and social networking. The app debuted in May 2014 and has quickly gained popularity in China and abroad with a total number of four billion microvideos created and shared online in less than three months. The distinguishing features of Meipai include instant conversion of long videos into ten-second microvideos and many artistic choices in movie effects and soundtracks.

Pros:

Using machine intelligence technologies, Meipai helps to transform a regular video to a microvideo by selecting and organizing frames from the original clip. Users can share their microvideo artwork on several external social networking sites as well as on Meipai itself. Thus, Meipai is not only a microvideo production tool but also a video network where users can communicate with friends by instantly creating and sharing microvideos.

Cons:

Users must be seventeen years old to download this app. The size of each video is cut down to a fixed length before sharing. The microvideo production process is quick but lacks manual editing choices. For users who might want to personalize their productions, the flexibility is limited; users cannot add personal audio or written text to their creations.

Our Takeaway:

The microvideo creation and sharing phenomenon is an example of new forms of digital literacies produced in social networks. In view of the rise of microblogging (e.g., Twitter) and its challenge to "traditional" blogging, microvideo raises new questions for researchers and educators. The Bottom Line:

Meipai is an innovative app for microvideo sharing with great potential for online learning.
