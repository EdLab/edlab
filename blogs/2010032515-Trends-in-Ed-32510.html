Trends in Ed, 3.25.10

jvazquez

March 25, 2010

Do digital natives really have the upper hand? There's a wide assumption that the Net Gen adeptly handles all things technology and digital media related but there's a growing number of researchers who have serious doubts as to the legitimacy of this claim. Take Net gen skeptic for one. Mark Bullen has been faithfully compiling research that exposes the hype surrounding net genners and their abilities. The Economist also tackles this growing belief and asks that teachers take heed before catering to the [supposed] tech savvy crowd. People like Mark Bullen aren't refuting that the net gen is using tech in abundance nor that they lack the capabilities to use tech for learning; rather, they are wary of wide generalizations many researchers make and ask that any claim, as with all good research, be adequately corroborated. They further argue that “the idea of a new generation that learns in a different way might actually be counterproductive in education, because such sweeping generalisations “fail to recognise cognitive differences in young people of different ages, and variation within age groups".” (from the Economist article above). Bottom line: It's time to stop believing that so-called “digital natives” are better at navigating the internets and handling the flimflangled doodads of the 21st century than people of any other given age. Continue to use tech to teach, of course, if that's what you do... but don't assume that you're doing this generation a favor (or that another generation couldn't handle it!).
