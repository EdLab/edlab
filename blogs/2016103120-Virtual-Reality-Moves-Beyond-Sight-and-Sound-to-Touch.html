Virtual Reality Moves Beyond Sight and Sound to Touch

saraehardman

October 31, 2016


Virtual reality lets you see and hear things in an artificial world, but what about feel? A new product is going to make that possible. Still in prototype, the haptic skinsuit allows users to fully immerse themselves in a virtual experience by responding to sound and pulsing in various parts of the suit to correspond with it. For example, the skinsuit can react to sounds in a virtual world such as a knock on the door with a light tap on the arm. It seems almost like an instrument that plays human skin.

The creators of the haptic skinsuit expect its main use to be in video games and entertainment, but they also think it has educational potential. This might be best realized in health fields, such as in medical school training or physical and occupational therapy where it could be used as a cost-saving measure that improves learning. If the technology develops sufficiently, however, it may also be used for teaching athletes how to adjust their bodies for maximum performance.
