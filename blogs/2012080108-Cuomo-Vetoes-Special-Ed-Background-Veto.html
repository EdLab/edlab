Cuomo Vetoes Special Ed Background Veto

Haymar Lim

August 1, 2012

A recent New York Times article tells us of how Governor Cuomo has vetoed a bill concerning the placement of special ed students. The proposed law would mandate New York State officials to consider the background of a special ed child before determining placement. This would result in the placement of many special ed students in religious or private schools. Cuomo vetoed the bill, saying it would put too much of a burden on the tax payers. Although I find this to be a legitimate reason, I wonder if placement in private schools would be better for special ed students. Do you think Cuomo did the right thing?
