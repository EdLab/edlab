The 411 on Getting Published

Allen.Foresta

March 20, 2012

There are a number of first-rate electronic databases that can be of great help in identifying and evaluating potential publication opportunities.  Among these are directories searchable by subject, publishing details, refereed (peer-reviewed) status, and many other criteria:

  • Cabell Educational Directories (Educational Curriculum & Methods; Educational Psychology & Administration; Educational Technology & Library Science) -
  • Cabell Psychology (Psychology & Psychiatry) -
  • UlrichsWeb Global Serials Directory -
  • WorldCat -
In addition, there is a very good print reference work which includes a section titled "Guide to Professional Publishing":
  • The Educator's Desk Reference:  A Sourcebook of Educational Information and Research (2002) - Though the work is 10 years from current, chapter 1 of the professional publishing section, titled "Journals in Education and Related Fields," is divided into Journals by Publishing Field and Journal Guidelines for the featured journals.
There are also several e-resources that provide the means to evaluate journals in a given subject field by a variety of criteria, including impact factor, immediacy, the frequency with which a journal is cited in other works, etc.  These include:
  • ISI Journal Citation Reports -
  • Scopus -
