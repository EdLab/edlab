Should You Be Worried About Genetic Surveillance?

Rebecca Beeson

February 24, 2016


Our privacy concerns live in various arenas; social media, internet tracking, and public surveillance. It is no longer just about what we do in public and what we search on the Internet. We have entered the era when public spaces can become the laboratories of ubiquitous and exposed genetic material. In this Vialogue, information artist and bio-hacker Heather Dewey-Hagborn attempts to address the eerie reality of genetic tracking with an evocative art installation. Based on small findings of human material in public spaces, she curates a collection of genetically-generated faces.

With this new social justice concern invading the scope of our daily fears, could an invisibility spray, (erasing ninety-nine percent of genetic material from surfaces) come to be as commonplace as hand sanitizer? How can we avoid an existence in a genetic database? Tell us about your concerns about genetic surveillance in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
