Science and Programming Advance New Lab Automations

Jenny Abamu

October 31, 2016


The merger of science and engineering disciplines yields seemingly endless possibilities for advancements in our society. The worldâ€™s first automated DNA testing lab, Counsyl, located in South San Francisco, is taking the merger a few steps further as it transforms work processes and output.

Counsyl has become a genetic factory that tests thousands of human samples, 24 hours a day, without human assistance. "Our robots perform these experiments with insanely high precision," says Kyle Lapham, vice president of automation. They also lay the foundation for scientists to begin synthesizing their discipline with programmers to create smarter lab bots, meaning that future developers might need to interpret genetic code in addition to writing computer code. Such cross-disciplinary jobs reinforce the importance of learning across science and technology subjects for today's young learners.

This speed up could lead to some transformative discoveries in the near future, but it could also result in the loss of individual knowledge as more systems become automated.
