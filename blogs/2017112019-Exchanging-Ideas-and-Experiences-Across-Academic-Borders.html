Exchanging Ideas and Experiences Across Academic Borders

Melanie.Hering

November 20, 2017


"ePals is a platform for teachers and their students to connect, communicate, and collaborate across the globe." (About ePals)

Make Connections

One of the great benefits of ePals is it provides a free platform for K-12 teachers from around the world to connect with each other. On the site, educators can create profiles specifying the subject they teach, language(s) of instruction, and age range of their students along with their specific interests and goals for utilizing the exchange program. Members can search and scroll through other classroom instructors and invite good matches to collaborate with them.

Collaborate

ePals offers many different types of collaboration, ranging from a simple penpal exchange to international joint projects. For the latter, ePals provides a variety of experiences, ranging from the exchange of recipes and geographically-located food traditions to designing biomimicry robots. Instructors can customize their own collaborative project based on the type of experience theyâ€™d like to create for their students, their options being a cultural exchange, subject exchange, language exchange, or some combination of the three. While ePals provides templates complete with resources and discussions to engage with and exchange, teachers have the ability to edit and change content, objectives, activities, and discussions to personalize the project and make it more relevant to a specific context. Finally, built-in discussion boards allow students from both classrooms to comment and engage with each other.

Relevant and Meaningful Interactions

Providing students the opportunity to connect with each other across borders allows for important skill development in communication, collaboration, digital literacy, and global citizenship while widening their perspective beyond the context of their own hometown. In addition, teachers also benefit from the exchange of practices and knowledge. ePals creates authentic, first-hand opportunities for developing cultural and global awareness while meeting standards and benchmarks required in modern classrooms.
