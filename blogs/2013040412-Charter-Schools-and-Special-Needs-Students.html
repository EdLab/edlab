Charter Schools and Special-Needs Students

abagigah92

April 4, 2013

Charter Schools are very big around the New York City area as it brings a different approach to educating children, especially those in the inner-city and tough neighborhoods. Charter School is very much different from regular public schools. The number of students are smaller and the bond between teachers, students and parents are much stronger compared to that of a regular public school. Thus, charter schools have an edge over public schools. However, policy makers, through research, have concluded that most charter schools do not serve their fair share of students with disabilities. In many cases, charter schools serve fewer children with disabilities than they are required. There are elements of special education in charter schools that are problematic but the organization of New York state's special education shows how these challenges needs more a sophisticated approach.
