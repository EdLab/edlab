"Forget the Year of Mobile-- It's Actually the Year of Video"

Diana Diroy

May 22, 2013

"Forget the Year of Mobile-- It's Actually the year of Video"That is the title of the article I came across the other night. It's a short and interesting article on America's growing viewership of online video. Here are some interesting numbers.

  1. Overall video consumption is up year on year by about 3.7%
  2. On average, 183 million Americans view 215 video each month online.
  3. Cisco forecasts that by 2016, two-thirds of mobile traffic will be viewing video.
The article is also about original programming and television and is directed mostly to advertisers, but I think we can take something from it. Do you think our Seen in New York episodes fade away too quickly in our current format? When we talk about NLT 2.0, it would be worth considering a way for media content to stay on the main page longer than an evening. Also, If we are looking to reach more subscribers, let's attract them with more of our original video content. Maybe embedding the promo video on the "create an Edlab account" log-in pop up? Just to give a visual tease of what NLT offers? But that's just one idea. What do you guys think?
