OWS Library Demolished

Kate Meersschaert

November 15, 2011

Occupy Wall Street protestors created many utopian resources from free WiFi networks to bike generators... and a library. Known as the "People's Library" (for complete posthumous catalogue of offerings visit this site) the loosely organized collection of books was created and maintained by a group of dedicated volunteers who even generated very specific guidelines for maintaining the collection. For more on these "Library Ground Practices" and the library as it functioned on-site visit before today's removal by the owners of Zuccotti park visit their WordPress site. What will happen to the more than 5,500 books in the collection? What do you think of grass-roots library movements or even book rental groups like Book Renter? Do you think that as we move towards digital and the ability for libraries to lend e-books, the need for grassroots library movements would be eclipsed?
