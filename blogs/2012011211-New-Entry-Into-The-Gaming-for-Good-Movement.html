New Entry Into The "Gaming for Good" Movement

Kate Meersschaert

January 12, 2012

According to this Mashable post Nicholas D. Kristof and his wife, Sheryl WuDunn, both Pulitzer prize winning journalists with extensive track records reporting on humanitarian and women's rights issues around the world, will launch a game for social good this year. The as-yet-unnamed game will be created in partnership with Games for Change and correspond with a release this year, of a documentary based on Kristof & WuDunn's larger "Half the Sky" movement. Half the Sky is a movement that hopes to raise awareness and funds for women's-rights issues around the world. The game will also employ dynamics similar to FarmVille and like many Zynga creations, be played initially at least, mainly on Facebook. Players will be able to both buy virtual goods with the real funds going to NGO's and also complete free "missions" and contribute to social media campaigns to add their support. What are your thoughts on this game-for-good? Do you think that gaming is a viable way to bring about social change?
