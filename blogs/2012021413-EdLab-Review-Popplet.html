EdLab Review: Popplet

Lu Wang

February 14, 2012

Popplet is a tool for users to visualize connections between ideas by organizing them into a graphic map. Users simply post and share their ideas by creating “bubbles” on a blank canvas, and freely link one idea to another. It is user friendly and inspirational, and especially helpful for visual learners as all information is integrated into an image in which their interrelations are clearly presented. One of the features of Popplet is that it provides multi-language support, which makes it applicable in foreign language classes. EdLab Relevance: As education has been undergoing a reform in digital age, traditional ways of learning and teaching may be less and less emphasized. It can be foreseen that teaching materials like textbooks are also facing a change. How to create more interactive, creative, inspirational teaching materials by using technologies would be a major study for tech companies (like Apple) who would like to occupy a share out of this rising market. In this sense, teaching materials would be more like a knowledge transfer platform than just a bunch of paper attached together with texts and images. Let's think about this idea as we develop online curriculum for mSchool. A good example of learning material can be found here. And tools like Popplet are probably able to help realize it.
