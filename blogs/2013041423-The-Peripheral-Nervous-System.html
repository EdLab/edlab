The Peripheral Nervous System

Bismark Appiah

April 14, 2013

Hi everyone, In my last blog, I shared some information about the system that helps human beings gather and process information. The system is known as the Nervous System; two main parts of the nervous system are the central nervous system (CNS) and the peripheral nervous system (PNS). In today's blog, I want to concentrate on the peripheral nervous system because it is where most of our nerve interaction occurs. As discussed in the last blog, the peripheral nervous system consist of the sensory and motor nerves and all portions of the nervous system outside the brain and spinal cord. Two subdivision of the PNS are: Somatic Nervous System and Autonomic Nervous System, these two parts of the PNS controls the reaction we send out depending on the cause. Somatic nervous system consists of nerves that are connected to sensory receptors. It is sometimes also called skeletal nervous system because our nerves are connected to our skeletal muscles. For instance, when a person feels a bug on his/her arm or when a person writes his/her name, then the somatic nervous system is active. On the other, autonomic nervous system regulates glands and internal organs such as the bladder, stomach, and heart. An example would be the pounding of your heart once you encounter your crush. As a result, your hands begin to sweat and your cheeks feel hot, this reaction is controlled by the autonomic nervous system.
