On the topic of genomics and education

Stephanie Hunt

July 29, 2009

For those of you with genomics on your mind after attending today's EdLab Seminar with special guests from Hugebrow, I wanted to share a few follow-up things to check out. First, I wanted to provide a link to the Broad Institute, whose goal includes "transforming medicine with the power of genomics." Their outreach program will be an invaluable resource for anyone interested in informal or formal education opportunities around the topic of genomics. Second, for a bit of inspiration to get your mind racing about the possibilities for learning about the human body using interactive technology, check out the work of Snibbe Interactive. They create lots of different types of social/interactive experiences, primarily for museums. The specific piece that today's seminar got me thinking about was the body mirror. Here's a description of the body mirror from the Snibbe Interactive website:

The Character Mirror / Body Mirror gives people a chance to see what they look like on the inside. As a person raises his or her arms, squats, and moves about, the character mimics the moves as if in a magic mirror. People switch between four different body systems and view both male and female anatomy to see what lies beneath our skin.
I think it would be so exciting to explore the human body through an interface like the body mirror, but with a large scale multitouch screen overlay. This would add a definite "Powers of Ten" style vibe to the experience, which could be so powerful in group learning settings like museums.
