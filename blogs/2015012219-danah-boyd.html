danah boyd

Ting Yuan

January 22, 2015


danah boyd is a Principal Researcher at Microsoft Research, a Research Assistant Professor in Media, Culture, and Communication at New York University, and a Fellow at Harvard's Berkman Center. Her research examines social media, youth practices, tensions between public and private sectors, social network sites, and other intersections between technology and society. Her new book It's Complicated: The Social Lives of Networked Teens was published in February 2014. danah has been named as the smartest academic in the technology field and "the reigning expert on how young people use the Internet" by Fortune. boyd received her B.A. in Computer Science from Brown University and her M.S. in Media Arts and Sciences from MIT, and earned her Ph.D. from the School of Information at University of California-Berkeley.

EXCLUSIVE NEW LEARNING TIMES INTERVIEW

Question: How did your educational trajectory and past professional experience shape your current work?
Answer: I started out as a computer scientist, did my master's in the Media Lab at MIT, and retrained under anthropologists for my PhD. I moved between industry, non-profits, and academia. In short, I purposefully worked to obtain as wide a perspective as possible. I also worked the entire time that I was in school, both out of necessity and also because it allowed me to better engage with the dynamics I was seeking to understand.

Question: How do you hope your work will change the learning landscape?
Answer: I want people to understand youth and the cultural context in which they are operating. And I believe that we have a responsibility to help young people engage more productively with this world. To make that happen, we need to start by listening to them. In this way, I see my role as translation.

Question: What broad trends do you think will have the most impact on learning in the years ahead?
Answer: Even when I was a teenager, getting access to information was hard. Today, people have unprecedented access. As a result, what youth desperately need is media literacy and computational skills to critically make sense of the information that's available to them. But all of this comes from the trend of unprecedented quantities of information.

Question: What are you currently working on & what is your next big project?
Answer: I'm starting a new research institute called Data & Society to address the social, cultural, ethical, and legal issues that emerge because of data-driven technologies (a.k.a. "big data"). I want to enable deep thinkers to bridge the conversations happening in academia and advocacy and to help build roadmaps for dealing with a data-saturated world. It's a fun project!

Question: Who are the most interesting people you are following on Twitter?
Answer: Well, I'm most interested in my close family and friends but I doubt that you'd be as enamored with their posts as I am.
