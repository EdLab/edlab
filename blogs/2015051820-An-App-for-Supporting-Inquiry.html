An App for Supporting Inquiry

Ting Yuan

May 18, 2015


A recent study examined sixth graders’ use of VoiceThread, a multimodal conversational tool, as part of a science inquiry project on photosynthesis and carbon dioxide emissions. The authors analyzed students’ written and audio annotations across 21 presentations published on VoiceThread. Students used the many digital communication choices available on the system to describe the phenomenon portrayed in images and infer causal relationships between different phenomena as part of their scientific inquiry process.

The authors identified four major classroom use functions of VoiceThread: multimodality, collaboration, interactivity, and connectivity. Students used these functions in different ways to highlight their understanding and interpretation of the science concepts. Some examples included using the doodle tool in VoiceThread to create additional visual clues to complement written and audio annotations, adding images to represent the causal relationships between photosynthesis and carbon dioxide emissions, employing concrete descriptive language to share their inquiries with an online audience, and analyzing their selected images to identify potential alternative meanings and understand their multimodal annotations.

The study highlights the need for K-12 teachers to frame the use of digital tools in terms of the quality of students’ experiential learning. Teachers should explore the functions and affordances of any digital tool before applying it to their classroom curriculum. The digital extension of common classroom practices needs to build upon an in-depth understanding of teaching content and students’ learning needs, as well as the targeted digital tool itself, moving from superficial technological implementations to utilizing digital media as new ways of thinking and authoring to enrich students’ learning experiences. The study further informs both practitioners and researchers to bring a new definition of variety into classroom teaching, designing hybrid learning experience for today’s diverse learners.

Beach, R. & O'Brien, D. (2015). Fostering students’ science inquiry through app affordances of multimodality, collaboration, interactivity, and connectivity. Reading & Writing Quarterly, 31(2), 119-134.
