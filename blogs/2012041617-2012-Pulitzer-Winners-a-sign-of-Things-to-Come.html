2012 Pulitzer Winners... a sign of Things to Come?

Kate Meersschaert

April 16, 2012

Future is in the awards... Possible future trends in journalism were on display in the announcement, today, of the 2012 Pulitzer Prize winners (the Pulitzer board is located here at Columbia University). Among the usual "traditional media" winners, like the New York Times, two upstart "new media" outlets were given awards. Both POLITICO for Editorial Cartooning and The Huffington Post for national reporting. Will the NLTs be next? With the announcement of today's winners, is it possible that we are starting to genuinely move-away from traditional outlets for "real news?" Will this mean that blogs, usually relegated to entertainment, will be given even more "real-world" respect? How is reporting in the education space different than other fields? If there were a Pulitzer dedicated to reporting on ed-related issues what subject and new outlet would win? I am betting on the New Learning Times for the 2013 prizes!
