Museum of Interesting Things

EdLab Studios

August 27, 2015


Though years of filmmaking provided New York native Denny Daniel with a lengthy career and stories to match, what this trajectory lacked was the opportunity to give back. In the company of friends, family, and a handful of cronies from Mad Magazine and WBAI radio, Denny began testing out his vision; sharing the vast collection of rarities he'd been acquiring for decades, antiques and inventions of yesteryear that would inspire and educate like no glass-enclosed museum ever could. Today his passion project, known as the Museum of Interesting Things is a traveling, interactive exhibition that brings the past to the public, making its way to schools, galleries, libraries, and every venue in between including a once-a-month Secret Speakeasy event. Covering a range of crowd-sourced topics that range from toys to music, photography and quack medicine, guests are invited to partake in demonstrations and actually get their hands on, maybe for the first time ever, the kinds of often farcical contraptions that paved the way for the devices and developments we enjoy today. The museum encourages attendees to explore objects’ functions, question their efficiency and validity, and exercise their curiosity to the fullest. By revisiting the "foolishness" of the past, Denny hopes participants emerge feeling informed, creative, and empowered to design their future.
