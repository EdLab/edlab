Can we really multitask?

Jamison Judd

March 26, 2007

An interesting article in yesterday's New York Times presented results from several studies investigating multitasking. The conclusion: the human brain is not very good at concentrating on two or more activities simultaneously. This runs contrary to previous research showing that younger people were better at multitasking because they grew up in the "digital age" amongst various distractions. The evidence shows that technology can be successfully leveraged to aid us in managing the large number of varied tasks we encounter in everyday life... but it still has a long way to go.
