Accelerators on the Rise

Michelle Lee

June 4, 2012

A highly relevant WSJ article came out recently describing the recent proliferation of Accelerator programs like TechStars and YCombinator. This was especially interesting in light of the conversations Hui Soo and I had with several incubator programs in Boston and a recent conversation with Heather Gilchrist, the founder of Socratic Labs - an edtech focused accelerator (thanks Kate for the connection). On one hand, accelerators are a great way to get cheap funding for founders and allows VCs and companies to get a first look at interesting companies. On the flip side there is concern that a huge increase in programs may make it harder for entrepreneurs to determine which are quality programs and may encourage giving away a lot of equity in a new company for little gain. My view is that these are helpful programs for inexperienced entrepreneurs that require structured attention and mentoring. However, I do agree that the level of connections and exposure an accelerator fund can bring the first things to assess if you're going to give away part of your company. What are your thoughts on accelerator programs?
