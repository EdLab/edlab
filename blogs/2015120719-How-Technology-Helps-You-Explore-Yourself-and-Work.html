How Technology Helps You Explore Yourself and Work

dinosossi2

December 7, 2015


Reform is a perpetually hot topic in education. With low U.S. scores in the Programme for International Standards Assessment (PISA) and Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study (TIMSS), there is a constant drumbeat for better schooling. Unfortunately, the pace of improvement in educational outcomes has been unbearably slow.

Why is education so static? Teachers lecture in front of their classes. Students write notes and fill out worksheets. Homework has perpetually been the bane of childhood existence. We may have moved out of the proverbial little red schoolhouse, but the culture of practice is virtually the same—we’re boxed in.

Although we have been educating people for generations, students still don't know how to deal with two fundamental aspects of existence—how to approach basic life problems, and how to succeed in work. Lost in the babel of school reform discourse, we tend to miss out on some of our simpler, but more fundamental, needs in life.

Are there technologies that foster the exploration of self? Can tech prepare us for work? If so, how do we develop deeper reflection through technology in ways that are not formulaic, reductive or instrumentalist? What role should curriculum play in life after school ends?
