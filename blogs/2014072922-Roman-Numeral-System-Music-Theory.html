Roman Numeral System--Music Theory

Daniel Kim

July 29, 2014

This is for anyone that is or has any interest about taking Music Theory. Personally, I have been exposed to music for most of my life, but I have never been introduced to music theory, so I found the class to be slightly difficult. As a start, this vialogue shown below shows an outline that classifies each chord as a major or minor chord in a specific key. Not having this prior to my music education, I was hopelessly lost when my teacher in Music Theory I decided to move fairly quickly, albeit the class had all been exposed to music when they were young. Chord progressions, roman numerals, and the like far exceeded my grasp of music knowledge and I found myself struggling through Music Theory I, despite the reputation that it had for being one of the easiest courses. Having simple videos like these shown at the beginning of Music Theory I class would have helped me almost an infinite amount, and by just even simply introducing the idea of Roman Numerals would help new students. No longer will students be baffled by this archaic numbering system and be left wondering what these symbols mean. http://bit.ly/1pDs5Xq
