A Touchscreen Wall

Mable Yiu

August 29, 2013


Many businesses and schools are looking to advance the future of education through technology, and HP is no different. Attempting to develop the "classroom of the future," HPâ€™s newest creation is the VantagePoint touchwall, a six-panel touchscreen device that focuses on enhancing collaboration within the classroom. Not only can teachers use the wall for presentations, students can use it to work on projects together. Another way to incorporate student interaction with the touchwall is to connect individual touchscreen computers for impromptu testing scenarios. In this video, viewers are shown an example of how the VantagePoint touchwall can be used in the classroom to find the tallest building in the world. While the teacher shows the building on the touchwall, students are asked to quickly find the exact height of the building using their individual computers.

HP is using touchscreen technology to foster interactive learning within the classroom. However, is it a step toward becoming the "classroom of the future?" The VantagePoint touchwall, as attractive as it is, is not a simple or low-cost device that can be incorporated in all classroom settings. Do you think if HP were to help more schools install the touchwall at lower costs, it could become a new approach to using classroom technology?
