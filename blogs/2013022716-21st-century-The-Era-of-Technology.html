21st century - The Era of Technology

Ashish Mehta

February 27, 2013

I often hear fellow students complain about procrastination and not being able to concentrate on their studies because of the large amount of time they spend on smartphones and tablets. Therefore, I am curious to identify the thin line that differentiates wastage from productivity. Web on smartphone/tablet often turns out to be a distraction, unless channelized into a direct instructional role. As a graduate student, I wonder whether one can productively use tools such as POLLEVERYWHERE to respond to questions discussed in class and VIDDY to record the class interactions and important parts of the lectures for revision later. Additionally, as a policy student, I am curious to find out whether the government can control our access to technology by taxing each corporation/web-portal differently and mandating the corporations/web-portals to attach a price to their services ? Also, as an individual, can I identify the opportunity cost of using web-portals? Last, but not the least, what role can ed-tech play to help leverage the time that we spend online ?
