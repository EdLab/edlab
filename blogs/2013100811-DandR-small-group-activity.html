D&R small group activity

bhughes

October 8, 2013

*keep private* Here is information about today's 2nd floor activity: I invite you to contribute one or more items to the TC125 Digital Time Capsule, the world's first online time capsule! You can add: - Anything you've written (stories, papers, emails, etc.) - Photos and videos you've taken - Links you want to share . . . practically anything you own that can be digitized! To submit items to the Capsule, you will need to upload them to PocketKnowledge, TC's Institutional Archive hosted by the Gottesman Libraries. As a contributor, you are helping write the history of Teachers College for future researchers, historians, and anyone with a passion for education. Please follow these steps to submit one or multiple items: Step 1: Go to http://pocketknowledge.tc.columbia.edu and log in. Step 2: Use the "Add an item" feature to upload and describe your item. - We encourage you to add a short description for the public and your Digital Time Capsule representative (who will summarize the contributions by students, faculty, staff, and alumni). - Add tags to make your item more discoverable, and please add "student," "staff," "faculty," or "alumni" depending on your status at the college. - Please be sure to add your item to the "The TC125 Digital Time Capsule" Pocket. Now you're done entering your item(s) into the Capsule! At the end of the submission period, all items that have been entered into the Capsule will be "buried" for 125 yearsâ€“library staff will make them private (even to you!). Optional: If you don't want library staff to make your item private, you may choose to add it to the "Provisional" Pocket. Items added in this way will simply be part of the archive, and you will continue to control your item in perpetuity.
