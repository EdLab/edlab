Project Noah

saraehardman

May 26, 2016


"Project Noah is a tool to explore and document wildlife and a platform to harness the power of citizen scientists everywhere" (About Project Noah)

Into the Wild

Budding scientists and nature lovers will go wild for Project Noah. This organization aims to foster the scientist in people of all ages by giving them a platform to share their experiences with wildlife. On both their app and website, users can create or join different missions, each of which asks members to collect and share pictures from specific nature environments. For instance, Global Schoolyard Bioblitz asks teachers and students to share pictures of wildlife, both plants and animals, seen near their schools. Users post their photos and notes to the online forum and can comment and like the posts of others.

Anyone can create a mission and open it either to a specific group or to anyone within a certain radius (missions can extend up to 300 miles). Whether youâ€™re interested in birds, flowers, ocean life, or insects, Project Noah hopes to connect you with a community of people excited by the same thing.

Educational Environment

Although Project Noah serves people of all ages and interests, it may be a particularly great tool for the classroom. Science teachers can use Project Noah to give their students a mission, such as identifying trees or flowers, and collect their pictures and nature notes in one shared space. It is simple to set up a teacher account and create a classroom mission; hundreds of educators use Project Noah now, and multiple resources are provided for new teachers to get started. Project Noah is giving students and teachers an easy way to share their wildlife experiences so they can connect with their environment and get excited about nature.
