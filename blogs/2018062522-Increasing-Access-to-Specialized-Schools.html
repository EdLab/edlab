Increasing Access to Specialized Schools

Junior.Trujillo

June 25, 2018

New York City (NYC) has the largest public school system in the country. NYC also has specialized high schools that serve strong academic and artistic students. According to this New York Times article, these specialized high schools do not have enough black and Latino students. Latino and black students make up two-thirds of the 1.1 million students in NYC public schools. This past year, the specialized high schools offered 6.3 and 4.1 percent of seats to Latino and black students, respectively. In order to be admitted to one of these specialized schools, students must take a special test. Many Latino and black students do not have the proper resources to study for this test. Additionally, public middle schools do not properly prepare students who might be interested in attending one of the specialized high schools.


Mayor de Blasio has taken action and affirmed that the special test will be replaced. Over a three-year period, the top seven percent of students in each middle school will be considered based on grades and scores on state exams.
