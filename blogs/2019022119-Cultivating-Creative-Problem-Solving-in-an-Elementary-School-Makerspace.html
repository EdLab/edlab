Cultivating Creative Problem-Solving in an Elementary School Makerspace

Melanie.Hering

February 21, 2019


While makerspaces are environments that can serve a wide variety of populations effectively, children in particular benefit from the skills gained in a makerspace. Experiential learning and creative design allow children to experiment independently and collaboratively, which can positively affect their classroom experience as well as their success later in life.

One school hoping to harness the power of makerspaces is Van Ness Elementary School. The Washington, D.C. school is working to foster problem-solving, self-regulation skills, autonomy, and growth mindsets in their students by incorporating a makerspace into their classrooms. Students independently come up with projects they’d like to create, receive approval on their plan, and build their creations in the makerspace. Teachers hope that this type of project-based learning will help their students to develop many important life skills.

What other benefits to learning do makerspaces have? What can a makerspace do that a traditional classroom can’t? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
