Education Shows/Channels

HSC

February 28, 2007

I recently saw a program on FOX called "Are You Smarter Than a Fifth Grader". The show tests adults' knowledge of subjects such as art, geography, math, and social studies. The questions are all taken from grade 1-5 textbooks. Here is a sample: - In what state is Mount Rushmore located? - What are the colors of a rainbow? - What are the names of the five Great Lakes? I have to admit that I almost watched the entire program. Although the adults were not in direct competition with the fifth graders, it was entertaining to watch the contestants rely on the students for assistance. The show motivated me to find other "education" related content. Here's some stuff I found being broadcast on the web: The Research Channel: An education station affliated with several university research facilities that provides science and technology related programming. Annenberg Channel: A station that broadcasts various programming related to teaching, and the education field. The Michigan Channel: A University of Michigan station that serves as a platform for discussion, debates, documentaries and public affairs. I am not sure about the form and content of the different "shows" on these channels, but they are good models to look at to further our planning about the EdLab Channel.
