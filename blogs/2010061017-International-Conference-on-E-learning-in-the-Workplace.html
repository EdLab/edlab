International Conference on E-learning in the Workplace

Stephen Asunka

June 10, 2010

The International Conference on E-learning at the workplace (ICELW) is currently ongoing at Columbia University (Faculty House). The 3-day (Wed - Fri) event is featuring over 80 presentations on various issues pertaining to e-learning initiatives in both educational and corporate environments. Activities for this (Thursday) morning began with a plenary session titled "How Web 2.0 and the Immersive Internet are Changing the Game for Learning," and it was delivered by Tony O'Driscoll from Duke University. His presentation was largely based on a book he co-authored; Learning in 3-D, and he demonstrated the limitless possibilities of learning within internet based 3-D virtual environments - what he called the Immersive Internet (Immernet). The speaker was of the opinion that the Web 2.0 paradigm will eventually be replaced by the Immernet, and with the possibilities of Immersive, Immediate, Interactive and Intuitive (i4) learning within these environments, educational practices will most likely change, particularly as learning will now mean optimizing one's networks to achieve desired results rather than simply acquiring knowledge. His presentation slides are available on slideshare.
