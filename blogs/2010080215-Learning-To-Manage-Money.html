Learning To Manage Money

Kwahiwi Edwards

August 2, 2010

One of the projects I have been working on this summer is finding additional resources for the Understanding Fiscal Responsibility project. The primary objective for the project is to teach the national debt, national deficit and federal budget to students.

I came across this useful manual that inform students how to manage personal finances such as using a credit card, the difference between a credit and debt card, and taking out a loan, among others. As a manual, it is useful as it presents scenarios and offers explanations and answers to key concepts such an open-end loan and a closed-end loan.

It also notes that most employers now check applicants' credit reports before hiring them. The differences among credit cards, debit cards, and charge cards are also explained, particularly with regard to the cost of these types of cards. It is also relevant to students, especially those interested in taking loans to further their education. For instance, one learns that the five Cs of credit are: character, capacity, capital, collateral, and conditions. In addition, there is an explanation about why it is important to keep a good credit report and the consequences that await those who cant keep a good credit report.
