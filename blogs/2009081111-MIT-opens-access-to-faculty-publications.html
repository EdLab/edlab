MIT opens access to faculty publications

Stephanie Hunt

August 11, 2009

This news will interest Dan P. especially, but it definitely gives us all something to chew on: Last March, MIT faculty voted unanimously to make their scholarly articles available online to the public for free. From the MIT News office:

"The vote is a signal to the world that we speak in a unified voice; that what we value is the free flow of ideas," said Bish Sinyal, chair of the MIT Faculty and the Ford International Professor of Urban Development and Planning.
Freeing access to faculty writings is growing into a mini-trend, with Harvard and Stanford at the forefront of the pack (though in more piecemeal ways - MIT is the first to make this a university-wide commitment). The faculty publications will be provided via DSpace, MIT's online institutional repository.
