Research Digest: Mutualism and Information Literacy

LauraCostello

April 5, 2012

Weetman DaCosta, J. & Dubicki, E. (2012). From Lampitt to Libraries: Formulating State Standards to Embed Information Literacy across Colleges. Library Trends, 60(3), 611-636. The 2007 Lampitt Law standardized credit transfer between educational institutions in New Jersey. By 2010 curriculum standards were being built and the movement became a vehicle for the information literacy cause. Based on the ACRL Information Literacy Competency Standards for Higher Education and the standards of other states, a task force of library professional organizations drafted The Information Literacy Progression Standards and ensured that it was integrated in the transfer standards and curriculum. This bundling strategy has been a part of the development of several information literacy curriculums including The Southern Association for Colleges and Schools and the University of Central Florida. EdLab Relevance: This article speaks to the power of mutualism in the landscape of higher education today. Partnerships and diversification like libraries hosting publishers, research partnering with industry, and education schools advancing tech development may help us prepare for a demanding and uncertain future.
