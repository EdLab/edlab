Game that contributes to genome research

zhou

July 30, 2009

In today's seminar I mentioned a game where players can contribute to genome research. I finally found the article - DNA Sudoku: Logic Of 'Sudoku' Math Puzzle Used To Vastly Enhance Genome-sequencing Capability. This game is still in progress but it already has a couple of press releases as this genius idea may save millions of dollars in genome sequencing research by having the players, instead of biological scientists or super computers, figure out the missing segments in genome sequences. The basic idea of this game is to transform the genome sequencing process into an ancient Chinese game called Sudoku. Simple and "boring" a Sudoku game might be but millions of people (my wife, for one) still love to solve these puzzles - for nothing. So one might think: I'm playing Sudoku anyway. Why not contribute to scientific research? The idealizer of this game is a scientist at the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, a non-profit research institution focusing on cancer, genomics, etc. Several Nobel laureates have worked at this institute.
