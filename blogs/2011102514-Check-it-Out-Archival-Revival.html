Check it Out: Archival Revival

LauraCostello

October 25, 2011

For the past month we've been toiling in chilly subbasements and mining increasingly opaque social archiving websites for the content that'll put fire in the hearts of believers! It's my honor and privilege to formally announce the debut of Archival Revival! Thanks so much for your contributions, comments, and props thus far. I have the ideas generated by our extremely helpful EdLab Development & Research meeting saved in my dubiously helpful get started guide. Your feedback is much appreciated! Below are the questions I've been considering as we enter our second month in publication: -Where does something like this fit into the EdLab mission? Could a stream like this be tweaked to fit into New Learning Times? Inspired by Janet's blog post, is there a space for examining historical curriculum materials with an eye towards current and future educational tech? -For all you library folks, do you think this structure (especially Items in Their Natural Habitat) has innate organizational value? I'm starting to debut entries that contain their own finding information in tags and the photos themselves seem to me a better way to ensure things stay in place. Would an organizational blog for our archive work going forward make more sense than the technique we have now (a huge secret spreadsheet)? If you'd like to contribute to Archival Revival, all the better! Time commitment and writing obligations are very low, fun content is exceedingly high! Get it while it's young and amorphous!
