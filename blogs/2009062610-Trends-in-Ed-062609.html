Trends in Ed 06.26.09

Rebekah Judson

June 26, 2009

Search Engines That Won't Get You Where You Want to Go But Are More Fun to Use Than Google Sometimes, searching can get a little banal, right? Sure, you find what you're looking for (at least in theory) but the experience isn't all that exciting. Well, here are a few sites that inject a little inspired serendipity and style into the search process (though you may not end up where you intended...). [Sorry if any of these are old news!] Spezify might be my new favorite. It won't really guide you towards specific information, but it will generate a screen full of images, tweets, websites, and more that let you surround yourself with a topic and absorb it in a more aesthetic sense. oSkope is more traditionally "functional" than Spezify. It generates images based on search terms from a few select sites, like Flickr and Amazon. Then, it allows you to browse the results, physically manipulating the images or displaying them in different ways, like "pile" or "stack." There's even a feature that will display cover images of books from Amazon on a graph based on price and sales rank. Finally, there'sHunch. Not really a search engine so much as an intelligent decision-maker, the site gathers information about your interests/habits as well as those of other users to determine what's best for you. Not sure how accurate it is at this stage, but still lots of fun. So far, it's told me to eat Vietnamese food for dinner, study international relations, become a cat burglar as well as a college professor, eat coffee toffee ice cream and believe in free will. Looks like I have my life all planned out!
