A universal application database

Reynaldo Cortorreal

August 4, 2009

The process of finding the right tools and software for any application, including education, has always been a challenge. Application download oriented websites such as Cnet and Softpedia have for a long time offered a small array of choice, usually confined to non professional applications for the desktop.

Meet AlternativeTo.Net a free service that allows you to find, favorite, comment and submit applications in order to make it easier to find alternatives to the applications you use the most. The site has received over 700,000 visitors a month in its short life of just four months. Individual users have submitted thousands of applications they find useful and linked them to applications with similar features. Its success is not hard to understand when we consider the sheer amount of applications in the wild without a real way to sort through them and find the one that's best for the job.

Say for example that you need to create some graphics while on the field and you only have a netbook without Photoshop. Sure you could do a sketch up on paint, or you could use AlternativeTo and find an alternative for Photoshop, there you'll find that Paint.NET and GIMP are the most favorited alternatives. This is NOT just for desktops, it also has a tightly integrated mobile database that contains alternatives for iPhones, WinMo, S60 and Android.
