Die cutter madness at EdLab

Stephanie Hunt

May 7, 2009

We have a new toy in the EdLab, and I'd like to tell you about it. It's a die cutter. A die cutter is a computerized cutting machine: you send it a digital file (just like a printer), but a tiny swiveling blade replaces the ink nozzle. We got the die cutter to ramp up the quality of exhibits and signage in the library. You may have noticed that we've been playing quite a bit with hand-cut graphics that we adhere to the walls of exhibits on the second and third floor of the building. With this new cutter, we'll be able to accomplish much more sophisticated designs. But, as the image above will indicate, there's SO MUCH MORE that we can do with this machine! I think there's lots of potential to use it in all sorts of EdLab projects, and I'd love to hear your ideas. Once we master the machine, I'd like to offer a skill sharing session so that others in the lab can learn how to use it. Let me know if you're interested! And now, a few of my thoughts on things we can cut and etch (after the break....) Of course, we can use it for text on the walls... We can also cut wall graphics... And floor graphics... How about stencils?... Cardboard automata?... Fuzzy flock iron on t-shirt transfers?... Your thoughts? Please share!
