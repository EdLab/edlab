Food Price Watch

abagigah92

November 26, 2013

The prices of food on an international scale have been declining since June 2013. This is mainly due to favorable weather and improved production that is helping to keep prices down. The World Bank food Index has decreased by 6% since June 2013. Prices of grains have tremendously dropped, which is the major factor behind the decline of prices. Grains such as maize and rice are heavily consumed or converted to other products that have been helping the food market. However, the prices of wheat during this period have increased due to a bad harvest period in producing countries such as Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay and Russia. Although prices have declined, there is a big uncertainty and bad weather in the future in South America, China and other farming countries. The Food Price Watch also explores the role that extra-large scale farming may play in boosting agricultural productivity and poverty reduction.
