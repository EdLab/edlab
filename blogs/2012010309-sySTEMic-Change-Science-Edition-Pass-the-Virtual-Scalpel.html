sySTEMic Change Science Edition: Pass the Virtual Scalpel!

Demetri Lales

January 3, 2012

I came across an interesting website that got my thinking about the “S” in STEM. Virtual Eye Dissection allows users to virtually dissect the human eye. The goal of the website is to teach users about the anatomy of the eye by virtually dissecting and taking apart each piece of the eye. The website's user interface is simple and straight-forward, no fancy graphics, plus it is a free way of exploring the basic parts of the human eye. This got me thinking about how we should have more websites or apps like this where users can explore and dissect different parts of the human anatomy, especially for K-12 students. Potential for sySTEMic Change: During my academic career, I've only had the chance to dissect and explore the anatomy of a living thing once: a frog during high school, and only had about an hour to do so. I understand that schools do not have the budget to provide students with more chances to explore different types of anatomy, and free websites like Virtual Eye can be helpful for students and the school's budget. I believe that such websites can also be very helpful in introducing younger students to biology and sparking their interest in STEM education. We definitely have the technology to create a more realistic version of Virtual Eye, plus this type of application can be a part of the new Science Interactive Textbooks that will be coming out soon. Although virtual dissecting may not be as realistic than actual dissecting, it seems like a cost efficient way to teach young students about anatomy and hopefully spark interest in more students into the awesome fields of STEM. Let's keep dissecting everyone.
