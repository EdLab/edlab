Trends in Ed, 2.2.10: Blio, An eReader Cool for Education

Ting Yuan

February 2, 2010

As reported by eSchool News, Blio, a new and free eReader software, might hold more educational promise than Apple's much buzzed about IPad. While providing a free software download, Blio aims to sell books from its bookstore, which was created in partnership with Baker & Taylor, the world's largest distributor of literary content and value-added services, and the world's largest publishers (see more functional highlights below). Users as reportedly can:

  • A unique read-aloud function
  • Create a personalized list of reference web sites, for one-touch lookup of highlighted phrases;
  • Adjust reading speed and font size;
  • Translate to or from English in an embedded translation window; and
  • Insert text, drawings, audio, images, or video notes directly into the content. These are saved and can be exported to create lists or study materials.
Blio is said to be the first eReader to preserve the image-rich format of books and magazines, including layout, typesetting, images, color and graphics, while also supporting full media functionality, including video, graphics, and web links. The software application will be available for desktop and tablet PCs, netbooks, and mobile devices, and available for download at blioreader.com this month.
