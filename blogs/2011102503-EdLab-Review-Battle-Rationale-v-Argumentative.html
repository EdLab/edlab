EdLab Review Battle: Rationale v. Argumentative

Skanda Amarnath

October 25, 2011

Descriptions: Let's get ready to rumble! This is the battle over argument mapping applications. In one corner we have Argumentative, a free and open-source application, and in the other we have Rationale, a proprietary software. Rationale is a touch expensive at $70 but the license can be used for many computers at once and its makers are particularly aiming to market their software to schools. Yet functionality between Argumentative and Rationale are roughly comparable, although Rationale has the unique feature of translating an argument map into an essay. Rationale sets itself apart and above from Argumentative in terms of the design and user-interface features. Rationale is simply more visually appealing and easier to use/manage. Rationale also contains fewer bugs, although that should be expected if the consumer is expected to dish out $70. Verdict: While Rationale is a superior product in terms of UI design and the quality of its features, it's difficult to argue they provide better value for the effort and money expended. Rationale does have features that make writing a formulaic essay quite simple, but that's different from improving as a writer. Argument mapping software likely has limited scope and is more appropriate for structuring arguments than entire essays. Thus, unless you place a high premium on UI features, Argumentative should suffice. EdLab Relevance: These EdLab Review battles serve to help us better understand which development strategies are best suited for a given product. The premium we place on UI, design features, and functionality translates itself into the audience we seek to cater to and what business model we wish to employ.
