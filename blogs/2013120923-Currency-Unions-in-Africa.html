Currency Unions in Africa

abagigah92

December 9, 2013

Europe's current currency crisis has scared many countries in creating a single currency union. However, this trend has yet to catch on among African nations. As a result, neighboring nations in some African nations are joining alliances to create a common currency that will spur economic growth. In West Africa, some leaders have agreed to a shared current, Eco, which they plan to roll out in a few years. Eco is derived from ECOWAS, the regional organizational bloc for West Africa. The currency will eventually subsume other West African nations. In East Africa, leaders of five countries of the East African Community (EAC) have also agreed to form a monetary union within ten years. By 2015, five countries will adopt the eco currency and five years later (2020), members of the West African Economic and Monetary Union (WAEMU) will adopt it as well, creating a currency union of over 300 million people. Members of WAEMU currently share a currency called the West African CFA franc. Economists have argued that a single currency like the West African CFA franc in a particular region usually yields success. With this in mind, many African leaders are pushing for integration of a single currency. This integration will facilitate trade, a key component for sustainable economic growth and social development. There are some eventual pitfalls of a single regional currency as epitomized by the current status of the Eurozone. Some downsides include high interest rates and a decrease in GDP for some countries.
