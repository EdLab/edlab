The Ingestible Sensor That Can Help Diagnose Gastrointestinal Diseases

Melanie.Hering

September 16, 2018


What if instead of getting your blood drawn and waiting for a bunch of tests to be conducted, you could instead simply swallow a pill to learn your diagnosis? Except that pill wasnâ€™t really a pill, but a pill-shaped sensor that can communicate with a smartphone? Currently, many diagnosis procedures for gastrointestinal diseases or internal bleeding are invasive, but researchers at MIT have developed a new diagnosis tool in the form of a tiny, ingestible sensor that could change all that.

The researchers developed this ingestible by combining genetically engineered bacteria with a sensor that can detect biological signals in the body in near real time. The bacterial response is converted into a wireless signal through a very low-power electronic so that it can be read on a smartphone. With this new diagnosis tool, medical professionals could learn about and diagnose a patient's condition much faster, painlessly, and cheaply.
