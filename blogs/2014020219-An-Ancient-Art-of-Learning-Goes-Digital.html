An Ancient Art of Learning Goes Digital

jvazquez

February 2, 2014


If Dumbledore had Google Glass, could Harry Potter have Wingardium Leviosaâ€™d from the comforts of home? What would it mean for the young wizard to have such a master at his disposal?

So goes the theory behind a most magical academy: the Arcanum. The Arcanum is based on the ancient master-apprentice relationship that nurtured many a novice artist. It takes advantage of the growing trend of augmented reality to transport learners and teachers from different locations to an alternate world where they are in the same place. While owning a pair of Google Glass is not required, many masters tend to use them as they offer unique vantage points for their apprentices (being able to take hands-free video is also a huge plus for art lessons).

However, technology is only the medium, not the true draw of the Arcanum. What raises its educational profile is its adherence to personalized, one-on-one learning. There are even opportunities to learn from fellow apprentices. As learners progress, they advance to higher levels, eventually finding themselves on the path to becoming masters in their own right.

Is the Arcanum bringing education back to its roots or is it reintroducing the issue of access? Discuss that and more in the ongoing conversation on Vialogues.
