The Radical Power of a Prison Pen Pal

AbdulMalik

December 24, 2014

Corrections systems are designed to isolate criminals and deter others from a life of crime. Family and the general public at large already neglect most criminals. Even those who are released often have to face the stigma of their stint behind bars. Additionally, prisoners often complain of inhumane conditions and lack of access to basic necessities.

Pen pal as a universal term refers to the exchange of letters from people in different regions of the world. However, penpalship has proved to be a very vital tool for most prisoners to express their feelings and feel a sense of family that is missing from their lives. I believe the extremely high incarceration rate in America makes it "normal" to imprison someone for minor offenses. It is therefore necessary to consider that prisoners are humans and must be afforded some level of humanity. Penpalship reveals that society's notion of inmates needs to be reviewed.
