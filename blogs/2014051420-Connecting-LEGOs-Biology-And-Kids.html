Connecting LEGOs, Biology, And Kids

jvazquez

May 14, 2014


What are plastic bricks doing in a botanical garden? More than you might think! Nature Connects is a traveling sculpture show that puts thousands of LEGO bricks in the midst of beautiful, natural displays of flora and fauna. It is based on the principle that all living things on Earth are interconnected through complex networks. Anyone who has ever created anything out of LEGOs knows that it would be impossible to build something without fitting blocks together, and the bigger the structure, the more complicated it gets. And so the lesson begins.

The exhibit is accompanied by LEGO buy-and-build stations, design competitions, and interactive sites that demonstrate connections in nature. While the sight of pixelated buffalo grazing in a grass field may initially be jarring, it serves as a reminder that the networks in nature may not always be so transparent, but they are ever present.

If this exhibit found its way to your local botanical garden, would you go to it? In what other ways have you seen LEGO and learning combine? Jump into the ongoing discussion on Vialogues and share your thoughts on all of the above.
