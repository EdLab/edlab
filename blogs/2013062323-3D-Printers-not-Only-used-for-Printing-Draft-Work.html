3D Printers not Only used for Printing Draft Work

Khalil Abubakar

June 23, 2013

Last Tuesday's special seminar described how 3D printers revolutionized education, especially for engineers and other core subjects that require 3D printing. This article describes how researchers at Harvard University and the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign have used 3D printing to make lithium-ion microbatteries the size of a grain of sand. This could be used to power tiny robots, tiny devices and compact electronics. Sometimes electronics such as laptops, desktops and other learning gadgets that run on these huge batteries either don't last long enough or get too hot and uncomfortable to use, but with the printing of lithium batteries the size of a grain of sand taking place, it gives us a hint of how future electronics might be.
