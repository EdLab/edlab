Trends in Ed: Survey sheds new light on college education

Nantwi

May 16, 2011

With college graduations in full swing, the ongoing debate about what a college education ought to be, who pays for it and whether students' are getting what they pay for are some of the concerns a new Pew Research survey of adults and college presidents sought to answer. The survey, Is College Worth It? College Presidents, Public Assess Value, Quality and Mission of Higher Education, posed college related questions to 2,142 to American adults and the other to 1,055 college presidents (small, large, public, private, and for-profit). Some of the results of the survey: - Nearly 2/3 of college presidents agree that the largest share of college costs should come from students and families while only 48% of the general public share that sentiment - 58% of college presidents note that high schools these days are doing a worse job of preparing students for college than a decade ago - Of the surveyed adults who had at least one child under age 18, 94% said they expect that child to attend college, and 53% said they are saving to pay for it. - 57% of 18- to 34-year-olds surveyed who had no bachelor's degree and weren't enrolled in college said they would rather work and make money than go to school The full survey can be found here.
