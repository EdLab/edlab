The Art of the Overly Engineered Machine

jvazquez

April 5, 2015


Rube Goldberg machines are celebrated for their whimsical, convoluted means to perform one simple task. Their eponymous creator, Reuben Goldberg, was an engineer, inventor, and cartoonist who sought to lampoon the pitfalls of modern technology. A series of Goldberg’s cartoons featured the comically resourceful Professor Lucifer Gorgonzola Butts, inventor extraordinaire. Long before the selfie stick, Butts figured out how to snap a self-portrait in as little as 19 steps (circa 1930).

A century after Goldberg envisioned his first contraption (the automatic weight-reducing machine of 1914), young adults are carrying the torch for meticulous inventors everywhere by way of the Rube Goldberg Machine Contest for high school and college students. Held at regional, national, and international levels, the contest teaches students to work together to think of unconventional uses of everyday objects (points are awarded for creativity).

We’ll never know how many rolling balls were lost in the making of these wild machines (or how many creative cuss words were coined in the process), but with all their failed attempts behind them, witness the winning run of a high school team from Santa Monica, CA in the video above.
