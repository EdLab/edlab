Earth Day Potluck 4/22

Dina Elsibay

April 18, 2016

Good afternoon! Weather permitting, this Friday, April 22 we are going to celebrate with a potluck at noon in the Russell Courtyard. Please check out the Google Document to add your contribution - http://bit.ly/1VyjEBr The reason for this celebration? It is Earth Day - a day (truly a week) dedicated to focus on the environment. Earth Day became official in 1970 as a response to growing environmental concerns around use of pesticides and air/water/food pollution linked to illness and destruction of our ecosystem. Two copies of the classic "Silent Spring", by Rachel Carson, are available in our stacks (call number SB959 .C3 1962) if you'd like a trip down memory lane. This year's official Earth Day 2016 theme (according to the EPA website) is food recovery. I did a bit of research around what we can do to be more environmentally conscious at TC. First, the resources already available! For one, there are recycle bins all over campus. Please use them! Some other tips: - Throw your food waste (including coffee grounds) in the compost bins available in Grace Dodge Cafeteria - Bring reusable containers to the potluck to take food home! Since it's Friday, we don't want to leave anything out or in the fridge that may spoil over the weekend. - We can join the Twitter impact conversation by using #IwillEarthDay16 #NoWastedFood and #EarthWeek Any other ideas, please post below!
