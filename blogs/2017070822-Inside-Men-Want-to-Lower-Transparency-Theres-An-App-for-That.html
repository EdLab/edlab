Inside Men: Want to Lower Transparency? There's An App for That

dinosossi2

July 8, 2017


Davos, Switzerland.

This is the place for the world’s elites.

Think SXSW, but take away generic indie rockers and substitute will.i.am.

Or think the Tribeca Film Festival and swap Robert DeNiro for David Cameron.

Sorry, make that Theresa May. I was looking at the 2016 list.

The discussions happening during these types of epic global events drive the global agenda in terms of much larger trends in politics, finance, and international affairs.

One of the big fads this year is the use of chat apps, like WhatsApp or WeChat. 


What’s their benefit?

Encrypted end-to-end communication.

And why the need for encryption?

Much of this year's Davos discussions were informed by the fresh memory of the hacked communications of the Democratic National Committee (a.k.a. the DNC, a-okay?).

In short, if you’re the type of power player who attends Davos, you are the kind of person of interest who is ripe for hacking.  

Power?

Yep.

Influence?

Sure.

Money?

Power + influence usually = Money.


So what’s the big deal about encrypting the communications of the elite?

More simply, why wouldn't these types of people do it?

However, when it comes to Davos-level power players, there is a distinct need for a minimum level of exposure of their more private affairs.

For example, twenty years ago, a legal case investigating corporate fraud would necessitate a team full of junior lawyers tasked with going through bankers boxes teeming with financial documents. 

Ten years ago, that type of work morphed into an analysis of electronic documents, email communications, and phone records.

With encrypted apps, the truly essential pieces of today's digital evidence to potentially find fault with alleged guilty parties are inaccessible.

And with governmental agencies, similar types of critical records also may not be able to be accessed if they are communicated via secure apps.

As a result, stricter rules, regulations, and statutes need to be put in place to ensure essential communications and transactions are recorded, saved, and disclosed.

If not, transparency as we know it is gone.

And the types of cloak and dagger politics typically hidden behind mahogany walls will be the order of the day.

"Would anyone like a free Swiss-made TAG Heuer before this private meeting anyone?

If so, just WhatsApp me…"
