Failure Can Lead to Important Ideas

Ryan.Allen

February 26, 2018


If necessity is the mother of invention, then failure must be the father. Even if they are painful, failures can actually help to foster new ideas. Hahna Alexander, inventor and entrepreneur, tells her story of how one failed idea became a life-saving innovation.

When Alexander was in college, she designed a boot that charged as a person walked. The idea was that people could always charge their phones or other devices. After years of work, she realized that the boot didn’t click with users because they mostly prefered to simply carry a battery.

Instead of abandoning the concept, Alexander and her team reconceptualized it into a smart shoe connected to sensors. Now, these boots are geared for rescue teams to save lives by sending out location data during disasters.

First responders have a new resource because Alexander didn’t give up after her first failed attempt. What are some other examples of early failures that became brilliant ideas? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues
