Analyzing Readership Data

Brian.Sweeting

February 17, 2014

*Private* As we expand and refine outreach strategies for NLT, we continue to analyze the readership data from different angles, exploring when and how our audience is growing and engaging with our content. A challenge in this data analysis is determining what's worth measuring. As The Atlantic points out in Why It's So Hard to Measure Online Readership, it may be easy to collect data on digital publications, but relying on a single metric won't provide a full or accurate picture of your audience or their behavior. The Guardian is a publication that seems to be excelling at using real-time metrics to tweak their publication and outreach strategies on a daily basis. Their team has developed an internal dashboard tool that tracks how their audience finds an article from the moment it's published. After accessing real-time data, they may feature a piece that's popular on social media or expand promotion for an article that's interesting and well-written but isn't being seen (read more via Fast Company: How The Guardian Uses "Attention Analytics" To Track Rising Stories). Based on data analysis that's already happening at EdLab, including third party analytics reports that Kate shares (see: NLT Weekly Insights from Visual.ly) and the information available via Data Dashboard (I squeeze some of these metrics into a weekly usage report), what do you think are the most important readership metrics for NLT? What are you most interested in knowing about how our audience interacts with our content? How could knowing that inform our outreach strategies?
