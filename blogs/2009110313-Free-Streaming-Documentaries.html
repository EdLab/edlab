Free Streaming Documentaries

Molly Riordan

November 3, 2009

I just read a post from last week's Brain Pickings about a new FREE video-streaming service. SnagFilms has a huge collection of documentaries that you can stream anytime, as many times as you'd like, for no cost. The "snagging" bit comes in with a widget that enables users to embed the video into a webpage, blog, or even a Facebook post. Interesting, too, is the "support" button for each documentary that takes users to the website of a charity relevant to the documentary they're watching. If the purpose of documentaries is to inspire social action, this is one (easy) way to do it. I'm a big fan of this for two reasons beyond my love of free: 1) the catalogue features major documentaries (Future of Food, etc.) but also takes submissions; 2) it helps anyone with an internet connection get access to educational and inspiring video content that they might not see otherwise. Living in New York, we get access to a lot of great documentaries--SnagFilms helps share the wealth.
