How an Interactive Tabletop Will Transform Reading

saraehardman

August 13, 2016


Sony’s Interactive Tabletop might change the way people experience stories. By placing a book on its surface, the words and pictures on each page come to life with the touch of a finger. In the demo, a user demonstrates the tabletop’s functions using Alice in Wonderland. He touches an illustration of Alice and she becomes animated. Footsteps trail behind as he moves her across the surface, interacting with other characters from the book as well as with inanimate objects along the way. The audience gasps in wonder as the demonstration unfurls.

Users can also highlight and animate phrases and individual words from the text. For beginning readers and English language learners, the educational possibilities for this function are immense. On top of that, the tabletop can be a collaborative experience; it can be controlled by up to eight fingers at a time.
