New PK - First Month's Usage Report

Nikita.Bogdanov

December 3, 2018

The front display screens have for the past week been showing data from the new PocketKnowledge, launched in late October of this year. This report covers the period of time from October 21st through November 18th, 2018. Again, I've linked to the full report from which the right screen's loop presentation is compiled.


Briefly, here are the recommendations I've developed based on this data:

  1. To better understand search satisfaction, and to understand if the search function isÂ turning up the right content, we might employ surveys that activate on download, that ask the following, simple questions, or questions like them. On a scale of 1 to 10, how easy was it to find what you were looking for? What is the purpose of your download: school assignment or school research, work, other? Choose the identifier that describes you best: k-12 teacher, higher education instructor, researcher, other professional, k-12 student, higher education student.
  2. To better understand motivation, we might also employ surveys that pop-up at random that ask respondents about the purpose of their visit and their role, much like the first two questions above.
  3. To better understand acquisition and motivation, require uploaders, outside of the Pocket Masters, to select a reason for their upload: requirement, personal motivation, other.

Let me know what you think and if you have any questions.
