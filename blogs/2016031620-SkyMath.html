SkyMath

Rebecca Beeson

March 16, 2016


SkyMath is a new app that functions as a math mentor for young students. This app was designed for personal use by students to provide an easy, engaging, and effective math experience.

Pros:

SkyMath introduces learners to the program with a quick but comprehensive placement test to locate a student on a personal math journey. A math path materializes in the form of SkyMath’s expertly generated selection process, which prompts students to choose to work on their weak areas. Children are given the option to watch videos to learn new skills, feeding their sense of educational autonomy. The app interface is incredibly sweet—Candyland meets Dragon Tales—populated with whimsical creatures and personal avatars that celebrate student victories. As they reach milestones, students are able to advance levels to new, colorful islands. Students can win ice cream or other physical rewards from parents when they accomplish learning milestones.

Cons:

SkyMath is a simplified solution to a student’s personal math struggle. The app provides students with agency while they navigate their own journey into new math skills, but it cannot be the only tool at play. In terms of optimizing downtime for kids when they would normally be playing mindless games or watching substanceless movies, this app provides substantial math help disguised as fun island hopping. Although the app provides great incentives for students along the way, it doesn’t necessarily promote learning for learning’s sake.

Our Takeaway:

Picture-perfect may be the final word on this app. It’s design is creative, engaging, and riveting to the eye. SkyMath has the right aesthetic to engage little minds in an incentivized game.
