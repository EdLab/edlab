Library Funding Report

Jessica Pang

October 24, 2008

Have anyone read the From Awareness to Funding: A study of library support in America report by OLOC and Bill and Melinda Gates foundation? My favorite is Chapter 4 -- Library funding is an attitude, not a demographic. Here they suggest that a voters' perception of the library (e.g. how friendly their local librarian is) predicts whether or not they will vote for more funding. Numbers such as how frequently they visit the library, has almost no relationship with how they will vote. What I find most relevant to edlab or the library is the differences in perception of the library between voters who say they will definitely vote for more funding, and voters who say they are not sure. Here we see voters in the first category putting the library in the transformation and purpose category, they find the library a place for empowerment. Take a look at the attached picture. Voters who say they are not sure put the library in the information and purpose category, just like amazon and other bookstores. With all the work we are doing in edlab, are we bridging the gap between transformation and information, helping people to realize their educational opportunities more? And how do we view our position in the chart?
