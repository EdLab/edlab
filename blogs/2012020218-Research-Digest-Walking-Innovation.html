Research Digest: Walking Innovation

LauraCostello

February 2, 2012

Jantz, R. (2012). Innovation in academic libraries: An analysis of university librarians' perspectives. Library & Information Science Research, 34(1), 1-78. Librarians talk the talk of innovation, but do we really walk the walk? The author of this study found it has more to do with institutional policies than external pressures. All the librarians surveyed agreed that innovation was important in an environment quickly shifting away from traditional services and strategies. The author found that creation of a dual-core strategy was paramount for innovation. Dual-core refers to resources devoted to research and idea initiation in addition to efforts already in place to deliver service and content. EdLab Relevance: While the focus of this article was on barriers to innovation for librarians, it could have easily been academia in general. The roadblocks cited included strict hierarchy, strongly defined job descriptions, and no institutional push towards research and development. In the development of our service and delivery units, it seems like we've been moving quite naturally towards a dual-core strategy. It's important as we grow to ensure we're fostering the sentiment that innovation and critical thinking should always accompany more traditional library service. In this way we can best serve our current populations and be ready as demands develop to meet the future needs of our patrons.
