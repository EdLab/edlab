Hero for a Whiteboard: Solar Power!

jvazquez

September 22, 2013


While the vast majority of schools in Sub-Saharan Africa lack electricity, students across poorly-equipped Senegal have become accustomed to their interactive whiteboard. But how could such a bulky, expensive, and energy-reliant piece of equipment exist in so many classrooms in the area? Enter CyberSmart Africa, an organization dedicated to reaching the poorest classrooms and supplying them with an affordable 21st century education. Currently at work at 9 schools in Senegal, CyberSmart Africaâ€™s marquee product, the interactive whiteboard, functions beautifully in the absence of electricity.

Powered simply by one solar panel, the advanced classroom device is as practical as it is portable. The entire board can be broken down in seconds, allowing it to travel from class to class. Students often take the lead in setting up the board without the need of formalized instructions. However, students are not the only ones learning the ways of the whiteboard. As part of the implementation, teachers undergo professional development training that helps them create active, learner-centered environments that incorporate technology.
