Ramadan as a Correctional Institution

Jamal Bagigah

August 16, 2010

As part my weekend routine, I attended Islamic School. Since it is the Islamic month of Ramadan, the main focus of the lessons in class was on the fast being observed. My Ustaz (teacher in Arabic) often describes Ramadan as "Correctional Institution". He gives it this description because Ramadan is not just about refraining from food and drink. It also involves avoiding everyday evil such lying and backbiting, and also showing kindness and offering charity. Ramadan is suppose to be a period where Muslims focus on spiritual cleansing and establishing a closer link to God.
