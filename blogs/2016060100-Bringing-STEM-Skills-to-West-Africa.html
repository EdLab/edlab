Bringing STEM Skills to West Africa

ABARRY

June 1, 2016

The growing emphasis (or overemphasis) on science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) in schools is not restricted to just America. It is a full blow global movement. Professor Sidy Ndao a professor at the University of Nebraska recently led a robotic event in Dakar, Senegal. The event's primary goal was to help students showcase their STEM skills. Dr. Ndao believes STEM education can help improve many aspects of life in West Africa from sanitation systems to agriculture and can create jobs, especially when you consider rising unemployment. Across the continent, many countries emphasize math and science. The majority of students have a strong foundation in math. The main issue is the system is arranged to memorize concepts rather than concentrating on contextual education. Students rarely connect concepts learned with practical experience.
