Women and Trade in Africa

abagigah92

December 3, 2013

Realizing the potential of women around the world has been a major factor in the growth of the global economy. This is especially true in Africa, where women make a major contribution to trade and other sectors. Women are proving essential to the continent's current and future success. A recent study by the World Bank highlights the many ways women are playing a key role in trade in Africa. In the report, the World Bank calls on African governments to realize the role and the impact women have on the market. They ask African governments to make policy supporting and giving women the necessary freedom to become active and permanent members in trading. Women are more likely to have their good confiscated, face physical harassment and lack of access to stable trade networks. It also calls for removing obstacle that will prevent women from taking part in the market. The potential benefits of these opportunities could be huge. It will ensure better security, job creation, poverty reduction and less gender discrimination. Governments must make sure that improvements in trade do not only benefit women but to all citizens.
