RFID Talks Back

Frank Webster

May 4, 2006

New design assists visually impaired library visitors

With a small electronic reader on the finger, visually impaired people can listen to a description of a book simply by pointing at its spine. This is the result of a design research project at the Umeå Institute of Design, Umeå University. On Friday, March 3rd, the pioneering accessibility tool AudioIndex will be launched in the audio book section of Umeå City Library.
