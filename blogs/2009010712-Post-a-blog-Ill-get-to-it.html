Post a blog? I'll get to it..

jvazquez

January 7, 2009

Behold! The story of my life: I was reading a post by someone the other day (I forget who and what blog-- I think I may be following too many on my Reader) who was blogging just to get out of doing his actual work. I may [or may not] have been reading his post for the exact same reason. Well, the idea of structured procrastination came up, as did the name of John Perry, who wrote an essay on the subject. John Perry, of course, only wrote it to get out of doing more pressing work. What can you learn from the essay? Well, first off, we procrastinators aren't lazy people. Just the opposite! In an attempt to avoid whatever it is that's weighing us down, we put some structure in our lives. That's right, we make lists (I even make lists of the lists I have to make). In these lists we highlight all of our priorities, the most important things at the top. It's just... those other items... they're so much easier to do! So we do them. We feel useful. We are productive citizens. Some consider us busy people. Deadlines are our adrenaline boosts, our motivation makers. We get our work done. And whatever we don't... well, that can all be taken care of tomorrow.
