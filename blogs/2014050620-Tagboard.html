Tagboard

Carmen James

May 6, 2014


Tagboard is a platform that allows users to organize tweets around particular hashtags. Tagboard can offer an essential helping hand to users, groups, and organizations looking to start a discussion on a topic, raise awareness of an issue, or promote an event, product, or idea. Users can also search through social media platforms beyond Twitter, like Instagram and Facebook, and identify popular hashtags for relevant topics. Are you a teacher planning to use augmented reality to understand topography in your sandbox? Curious if others are doing the same too? Search and find: #ARsandbox and connect to the already lively discussion. Tagboard encourages users to use hashtags to tap into the power of shared discussion on social media platforms.

Josh Decker, founder of Tagboard, stated that he sees Tagboard as a way to leverage the hashtag and "bring together all of the disconnected and siloed conversations and communities[â€¦]Our goal is to break down the walls that currently exist between the different social networks to really promote discovery and engagement."

Pros:

Tagboard can be used by teachers in the classroom. Teachers can, for example, display tweets with a particular hashtag that relates to a homework assignment, a group project, or a current event. Since teachers can create closed fence twitter accounts for students, Tagboard could be a great way to organize tweets in a class around classroom work. For educators hosting an event or trying to build a community behind a cause, Tagboard works with social media to provide a live selection of posts with hashtags arranged in an interactive display.

Cons:

Tagboard is free for individuals, groups, or clubs, but larger schools and districts have to pay for the service.

Our Takeaway:

As we increasingly use hashtags to describe photos, videos, and tweets, searching by hashtag will become more fruitful and accurate. Tagboard makes adding hashtags more valuable because it works as an organization and filing device across platforms. Users on Tagboard are able to unite many types of media using this already popular strategy.

The Bottom Line:

Maybe Google has plans to allow searching by hashtags. For now, we have Tagboard.
