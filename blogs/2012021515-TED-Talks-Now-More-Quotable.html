TED Talks... Now More Quotable!

Kate Meersschaert

February 15, 2012

TED talks are now more "quotable" than ever with the launch of their new "Quotes" page. Both logged in and logged out users can easily search a library of hand-picked quotes from past TED talks and then either select to watch the full-length talk or share the quote and a link to the full talk on Facebook, Twitter or via email. What is especially interesting is that these quotes pulled from transcriptions of past talks can now be easily discovered and shared. Previous discovery of TED content occurred by simply searching through lists of TED videos, getting recommendations from friends or simply scrolling through the TED site. Finding specific quotes from speakers usually meant linking to the speakers personal written work archived on other platforms or doing the work of navigating through a video to try to transcribe content. Now, discovery, experience and sharing is united in one intuitively designed portal. EdLab Relevance I wonder as we continue to evolve Vialogues, could we at some point, create a "VQuote" feature that would let users search archives of quotes pulled from transcriptions and then either watch the full Vialogue or share the quote and link to the Vialogue across social media sites? The big money question is... would the benefit of this kind of promotion be overshadowed by the cost of transcription? Perhaps we could initially only transcribe vialogues featured on the home page? Note: the corporate sponsorship for this feature is an interesting way to think of funding a new "free" offering!
