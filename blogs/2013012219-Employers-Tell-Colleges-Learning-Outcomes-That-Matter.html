Employers Tell Colleges Learning Outcomes That Matter

Janice Joo

January 22, 2013


The American Association of Colleges and Universities surveyed 302 employers to get their opinion on what students should be learning in college to adequately prepare them for today’s job market. According to the survey results, only 25% of surveyed employers were satisfied with how colleges are preparing students. When asked about necessary improvements, 89% of employers believed that colleges should place more emphasis on oral and written communication. Social responsibility was another top priority, with 75% advocating that colleges focus on fostering ethical decision makers. And finally, 70% of employers were rooting for learning experiences that encourage students’ ability to innovate and be creative.
