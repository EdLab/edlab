How Will the Education Space Look Like in 2013?

Megha Agarwala

January 7, 2013

What may be the moving pieces in education in 2013? While researching on the same, I came across the following list , which I thought was quite comprehensive and compelling. Some ideas that stood out for me while reading were: - Slight shift from social media platforms to social media API's We should see less of new social platforms but more of existing social media embedded into apps and software platforms. - Data experimentsâ€”visual data, data sharing; less data, more data. While 2012 may have been the year of infographics, there is still an opportunity and a need to crunch learning data on a regular basis and present it to learners, teachers and administrators in easy to understand visuals. - (Crowdsourcing of content (YouTube, Udemy, etc.) With Youtube's launch of YouTube EDU program and previous successful attempts by Khan Academy on creating educational content, creating quality learning content through crowd-sourced expertise will continue to gain momentum. - Fight between start-ups and corporate entities for control of learning spaces Will any startups be able to scale well and collide with big education giants? - Rise in credibility of entrepreneurial learning As new skills become obsolete in 5 years and academic proficiency hitting a roadblock in practical world, can we create teaching platforms that promote "entrepreneurial learning", i.e. "How do you learn new things informally?"
