Behind the Scenes at an MIT Hackathon

saraehardman

January 25, 2017


This past November, MIT hosted its fourth annual Hacking Arts Hackathon. Hacking Arts is a group that ignites the fusion of art and technology and brings together technologists, artists, and hackers. At each Hackathon, groups of students work over a 24-hour period to develop an idea, create a prototype, and pitch their product to judges. The winning teams come away with prize money that they might want to put toward further developing their products.

Although the products vary drastically in use, all of them merged art and technology into innovative new applications. One team aimed to make a virtual reality poetry experience, another created a wearable light-up suit for a specific type of Brazilian martial arts, and yet another created a robot that could paint. The Hackathon allows students an opportunity to learn and create. As Helen Smith, the co-director of Hacking Arts, says in the video, the Hackathon "releases this pent-up hunger to stretch the imagination, to work with a lot of different people, and to just get down and build something."
