Thoughts from Brian's Panel

Kate Meersschaert

May 27, 2011

Private: Hi Everyone! I was able to "sneak-into" Brian's Pressible panel discussion today at TC's Creativity, Play and Imagination across disciplines conference and here are some of my thoughts. Peter Beaugard, Assistant Professor in the College of Architecture and Design at Lawrence Tech, was also presenting and some insight from his fascinating presentation on design and a student-created learning platform is shared below as well. Check-out Peter's amazing student-driven design studio in Detroit. Thoughts from Brian with my questions mixed-in: 5 characteristics of a social learning tool: Real - authentic Public - not school only Shared - community (who defines this?) Helpful- automated, data driven Deep - emph. on content creation How similar is Pressible to Tumblr as a microblogging hybrid? The Media Show Talked about media like YouTube in context of Pressible - importance of Recontextualizing media to be relevant to Ed & subject area - ex: would be UFR how are we making the media relevant to the material? Too many scholarly rituals - instead, lets play in an academic context. A social learning tool is: Real Public Shared Helpful Deep Potential problems with social learning tools: Copy & paste - unthinking behavior (publicness can help) May not reinforce scholarly norms like citation & attribution May not be appropriate for peer-based learning Peter Beaugard from Lawrence Tech: Design Naturally linked to the arts & language Old paradigm - user error New paradigm - creator error Link of mythology to design Democratization of design Importance of not giving too much info to users - info overload Don't want to be redundant Design all about anticipation Intrinsic v extrinsic motivation Ex: wanting to do a course for personal joy vs to get a grade - finding a balance (very important for Edfluences!) Use of online education as a repository - asynchronous Idea of millennial learner- many questions - high level of parental involvement? How design for parents? Do teachers give-out personal email? To do: email Dino re putting videos from conference on Vialogues? We should contact Peter re using Vialogues! Visual literacy of the time Use a variety of deliverables IE print & digital to access many learners Chalkboard project (course management tool a student of Peter's designed for 5th graders - we need to see if we can access his slides, as the product is not launched). Very graphically appealing, large "button-like" icons Student parent & teacher login - very graphical & iconic Idea of "breadcrumbing" navigation trail Assessment? How do you test students for understanding Idea of reward - ex only place to find out what's for lunch Importance of being able to customize your own page What is "recess in an online environment?" Importance of metaphors as icons! Vialogues would be perfect to show how students engage with video
