Trends in Ed, 4.5.10

Arianna Choi

April 5, 2010

Virtual journey just doesn't seem good enough for you because it simply isn't real? But some may be just as satisfied and intrigued by the great Trans Siberian Railway trip taken online. I would have argued for the former group...until I tried this Moscow-Vladivostok virtual journey on Google maps. (Click below sample screen shot for a journey of your own) Five minutes after my departure in Moscow, I became curious about "books and music to take along" option. And what do we know, I am now listening to a Russian radio station (with yes, all the commercial breaks in between!) as I am blogging about my trip. Try skipping to Baikal lake and continue to enjoy your real-time Russian radio or have Tolstoy's "War and Peace" read to you in Russian while at the same time you let yourself be in awe of the world's greatest lake. This isn't just a journey, there's a plenty of educational components embedded in this project. Wow.
