Soccer Ball Doubles as a Generator

Nantwi

December 15, 2014


Energy has and continues to be one of the major problems affecting every facet of life in developing nations. Power outages and lack of access to electricity is a daily aspect of life. For teachers and students, this is especially problematic. Without access to electricity, many schools cannot run after school programs and students are prevented from completing homework or studying at home. This problem is especially troubling in rural areas where lack of basic infrastructure makes it extremely difficult for teachers and students in and out of the classroom.

For teachers, students, and their families in Abuja, Nigeria the solution to their energy issues comes in the form of an everyday object: a soccer ball. Soccerâ€™s global popularity means that soccer balls can be found in almost every corner of the globe. Since soccer balls are common and their movement, especially during play, produces kinetic energy, students and residents can use them as sources of electricity or alternative energy. They can power lamps to provide students with the light to study and complete assignments.
