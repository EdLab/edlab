mBot is a Powerful Robot for Beginners

chingfu

September 18, 2016


mBot is a programmable robot that kids control through their computer or mobile device. It uses an Arduino-based board and has a graphical programming platform, mBlock, that learners use to program the robot's actions. In addition to two major motors, it's equipped with several sensors and has sound and light outputs for users to design dynamic interactions with the robot.

Pros:

Unlike many other educational robots that come out of the box as ready-to-use toys, mBot requires learners to build the robot by assembling its parts. The instructions are clear and easy to follow, and the hands-on assembly helps learners better understand how robot parts work. Its mobile app, mBlock, is similar to Scratch and is easy for learners to use. Learners who are more advanced and familiar with Arduino can use C to program. mBot’s sensors, LEDs, and sound outputs work well and provide rich opportunities for students to design activities like following a line, avoiding obstacles, making simple sounds and melodies, and lighting up in different colors and patterns. The mobile app enables users to use the motion sensors on their device to tilt and control movements as well.

Cons:

The website provides some instructions; however, more documentation is needed to help users learn advanced robot activities and troubleshoot when mBot is not working properly. Currently users have to rely on mBot’s forum to get advice from online community members if they have problems using the robot.

Our Takeaway:

mBot is a powerful and comparatively affordable robot with great features. The visual programming platform makes it a great educational robot for kids to learn about programming and design their own activities. Its transparent hardware helps learners to understand robotics and engineering.
