The Zip Code Problem

jvazquez

November 14, 2013


Jessie Woolley-Wilson is the CEO of DreamBox Learning, an adaptive math program popular in classrooms across the country. She believes that adaptive technologies are the key to democratizing education. In this TEDTalk, she asks her audience, "Do you know how important your zip code is to giving you access to a quality education?" With schools being forced to do more with less money, the disparities among students’ learning readiness levels continue to grow. Could intelligent adaptive systems like DreamBox make the advantages and disadvantages based on zip code obsolete?

Adaptive learning software supports personalized learning. It gauges students’ levels of understanding of a subject (in this case, math) while providing tailored sequences of games/exercises that meet a student’s needs. Lessons are scaffolded and presented in ways that challenge and encourage learners. Jessie supports the notion of blended learning, combining DreamBox engagement with classroom instruction for the optimal learning experience.

Is a quality adaptive learning program alone as effective as a great teacher, or do the two have to co-exist in the same learning ecosystem, as Jessie argues? Add your thoughts to that debate and more in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
