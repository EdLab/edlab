Playful Conspiracies in Practice

EdLabStudios

May 29, 2018


From Interactive Telecommunications Program graduate to MediaLab director at the Metropolitan Museum of Art to current research fellow at the Brown Institute for Media Innovation and adjunct professor at the Anthropology School , Marco Castro Cosio has an extensive history with collaborative innovation and play in the learning environment. Cosio visited the EdLab in April of 2018 to share examples of his past work including Bus Roots; an initiative that aims to install gardens atop city buses.

Watch the full seminar on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion:

@08:02 Sara Hardman: I like the idea of viewers contributing to the art piece. It makes for a more interactive and engaging experience... especially in the current "experience age".

@13:36 Renata Gaui: So many challenges to work with public spaces and art intervention! Love the idea of the bus / mobile garden.

@40:07 Sara Hardman: More museums are taking up augmented reality as a new way to experience old artifacts and paintings. I think it could add a really interesting dimension for museum goers.
