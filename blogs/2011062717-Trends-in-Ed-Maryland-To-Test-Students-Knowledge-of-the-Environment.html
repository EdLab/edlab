Trends in Ed: Maryland To Test Students' Knowledge of the Environment

Nantwi

June 27, 2011

Maryland's public school students are set to be the first in the country to prove their knowledge of the environment before graduating high school under new regulations adopted by that state's board of education. The new regulation requires schools to include lessons on conservation and other environmental related topics into core subjects. The onus is firmly on school districts in Maryland to modify the curriculum accordingly so it reflects state standards in environmental literacy. The districts are also charged with creating ways to assess students' knowledge of environmental related topics as highlighted in the curriculum and standards. The plan goes into full implementation as early as this coming fall and will not require additional funding for staff or courses.
