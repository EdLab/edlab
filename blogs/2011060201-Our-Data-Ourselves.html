Our Data, Ourselves

Melanie Hibbert

June 2, 2011

This article explains a forthcoming paper by Brooklyn Law professor Jane Yakowitz on the concept of a "data commons." This idea proposes that the vast amount of digital data being generated should be anonymized and reposited in a public space for purposes of the common good. Privacy concerns, as well as ownership of data in the cloud has been the subject of fierce debates; however, Yakowitz argues that the societal benefits from capitalizing on our massive amounts of personal data outweigh the potential risks from hackers and other criminal activity.
