Trends in Ed 7.16.09

Calli Kosch

July 16, 2009

Planet Diary I recently came across Planet Diary, an interactive online tool that archives and updates natural occurrences on planet Earth. The most recent view (Summer 2009) is a world map with icons representing what has happened in that specific region, such as cyclones or droughts: A short explanation is given when you scroll on each icon (on the Planet Diary website-hyperlinked above). Searching through the Earth's calendar tab, viewers can read dated information on what has happened during a particular month and even read about the origins of month names. Useful for educators, the Activities tab provides direct links to a list of natural disasters and astronomic words with accompanying lessons (complete with ready-to-print worksheets). I find this information really interesting and great for polishing up your trivia skills, and it's a quick resource for teachers and students. No need to sign up and it focuses on the subjects pertaining to geography and the Earth only-so you know exactly what you're getting. Potential applications of this tool's features could inspire the Obama project's idea of a map detailing Obama's life (where he has lived and worked). Any other thoughts?
