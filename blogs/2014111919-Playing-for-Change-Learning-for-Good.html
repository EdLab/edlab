Playing for Change, Learning for Good

Nantwi

November 19, 2014


Music is one of the few agents that transcend borders, cultures, and religions. This is the spirit behind the Playing for Change Foundation (PCF). Through collaboration with local artists, PCF has taken the music gospel to some of the most impoverished corners on the planet. Their mission is also timely. Schools are focusing more of their efforts on the STEM subjects to prepare students for life in a knowledge-based global economy. As a result, creative subjects such as music and art have been relegated, and in some cases eradicated, from the school curriculum. This is especially true in developing countries, where educational opportunities of any kind are limited.

To date, PCF has created nine music schools for over 700 children in communities in Nepal, South Africa, Thailand, Ghana, and Mali. The sheer joy and exuberance of students at the music schools highlight the impact of the program on their lives. As one student states, "music is an indispensable part of life.
