Live Blog: 25 Things to Drive Traffic to your Website 04.24.12

Kate Meersschaert

April 24, 2012

Event Title: 25* Things to Drive Traffic to your Website Sponsor: Center for Nonprofit Success Location: Bowery YMCA Date: 04.24.12 Agenda: What are your goals? What are the challenges? What are the solutions? Intro Notes: Who is your target audience? What do they want? Education as a goal? Special log-in for alumni? How to highlight this target? Don't have too many functions on your website... streamline & don't confuse your audience. What are your key messages that you want to target? Goals: What are the most popular pages on your website? What makes them the most valuable? Psychographics & demographics... check-out what links drive the most traffic! What resources do you already have? mailing lists etc., collect email addresses at every possible chance! Simple giveaways @ conferences... raffle, have people drop-off business cards, have email everywhere etc. Important to segment these email lists! Create a detailed plan, timeline, schedule & test. Do A/B testing ---> Bisect to two different pages... like an eye test, A is better? Why? Do you prefer B... (Is this always necessary?) Everything on your site should be modular & buildable. Don't over-promise! Main Challenges: Shift in culture from old-school to new-school thinking. ROI! Shifting to digital from paper. Invite board members to engage through social media! Taproot Foundation provides donated services. ID site's priorities.... education, tools, outreach, fundraising etc. ---> for us awareness, tools & education. #1 reason someone goes back to your site is to DISCOVER SOMETHING NEW! Importance of IMMEDIACY! Heatmapping... ("F-shaped pattern" explained below...) Social media harder to track.... social media relationships take time! Solutions: Evaluate what your org is currently doing? Do you have an integrated marketing strategy? Does that include your website? Social media? Define or refine your marketing strategy. Define your goals & where your site fits-in to these goals. Empower all stakeholders including senior-level execs & all staff to be engaged in & participate in your marketing plan. 1) Design & content - "F-shaped pattern" (Jakob Nielsen) for reading web content - basically most attention on left-side. 2) SEO - practices of applying methods & decisions to optimize a site's design, content & structure to increase traffic a site gets from search engines. Same for good user experience - free Google guide to SEO! Descriptive titles Simple, intuitive design What are keywords you use to describe your site? Google grant for advertising Google checks relevance of your adwords & will turn-off your ad if not relevant Check-out "Google Trends" Partnerships with other sites to improve your search standing 3) Marketing, communication, & Social Media Think of yourself as a DIGITAL PUBLISHER! Create an editorial calendar to plan & schedule ALL communication, tie mission to national mo's, weeks & days. Have an active blog (1 post per week ;)) Position org. as an EXPERT! Meet & network with journalists & bloggers.. FOLLOW the journalists you want to cover you. Make compelling pitches. Send thank-you notes to any press who covers you. Directory listings... idealist, Google places, foursquare, LinkedIn etc., wikipedia. Blog: Active, use meta tags, cross-promote blog posts on social media (tweet posts etc!) Add relevant news & articles on your topic, comment & send to author of original article. Social Media: Free way to bring traffic to your site! Develop a plan that is manageable & reasonable! Be sure you have only what you can manage! Use free data management systems like Hootsuite. Cross-promote to your blog. Bitly analytics... bitly shortened url & add .info to get analytics! 80/20 rule: don't just make announcements... 80% engage audience, ask questions, get user opinions... 20% news about your org. Email Marketing: Great for busy professionals Monthly e-newsletters Targeted emails 4) Data: measure your impact. Decide what we want to measure? Set-up benchmarks & track tools... Hootsuite has analytics Constant Contact has analytics for newsletters. Klout score... vialogues, TCR? Share your results ---> select which metrics to share, "Conview" paid analytics viewer service, Facebook marketing now viewable through the new timeline. Google Analytics Goals etc. ---> user flow Important is where people aren't going. Main EdLab take-aways: What is the main goal of our website (NLT)? Tweet & share blog posts is a missed opportunity. Wiki & directory entries up-to-date for EdLab as a whole.... boosts your Google algorithm rating! HooteSuite for Tweets? Follow Journalists you want to follow you! Editorial calendar for special days, weeks & months etc. Are our SEO terms & keywords up-to-date? Site descriptions - simple & intuitive? Explore Google Trends & Google Webmaster... for tips on best keywords to use. F-shaped Pattern for most site heat-maps (Nielsen) Newsletter through Constant Contact? Contact info for speakers: NY Academy of Medicine: sching@nyam.org ReServe: jdean@reserveinc.org Huntington's Disease Foundation of America: ftaubman@hdsa.org Chinese-American Planning Council: mwilke@cpc-nyc.org Break & group work... Use an imaginary non-profit case study to identify targets, goals, messages & solutions... & write a tweet & Google headline. *There were many non-specific suggestions & potential solutions given, but they weren't designated numerically... the 25 is listed in the attached handout.
