Collaborating in Virtual Reality is Possible

saraehardman

January 21, 2018


One of the main problems virtual reality (VR) faces is that only one person at a time can use it, and the entire experience can quickly lose its appeal. It would be like only being able to play video games alone: fun for a bit, but not as interesting as challenging your older brother to a battle. For this reason, from Facebook to NYU, VR developers are experimenting with new ways to bring multiplayer experiences to VR.

The MIT Media Lab is testing how well users can collaborate within room-scale virtual reality. In this experience, users can’t see each other, but they can see the changes they affect in the virtual world. Users can paint together to create a collaborative work of virtual art or play a game of virtual Pictionary. Although this type of collaboration doesn’t have many uses right now, it could lead to other forms of social VR that make this technology more useful and fun.

How could collaboration in VR change how we use this emerging technology? Do you think this development could have implications for how we learn using VR? Answer these and more questions on Vialogues.
