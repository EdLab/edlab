Financial Literacy and American Students

Oumar Soumahoro

July 13, 2014

As a result of Wednesday's seminar on Financial Literacy, I came to understand that a lot of us (especially students) know about money, but do not know about what else it could be used for, besides spending it on unnecessary items. When I was in high school, I noticed that a big part of my money was spent on shoes, and clothes. This was due to my financial illiteracy. When I started college last fall, I found that I could spend my money more smartly. Maybe this was a result of the harsh realities of living alone and adapting to college life. It seems I am not alone when it comes to financial illiteracy.

This article notes that American students are far behind their international peers when it comes to understanding financial and money management concepts. I think it will be extremely helpful to future college students to take courses on financial literacy, even if it is basic stuff. This will potentially help them make better decisions about their spending habits and other financial matters.
