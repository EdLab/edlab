The Unexpected Technological Marriage of Snakeskin and Japanese Paper Art

Melanie.Hering

May 10, 2018


Researchers at Harvard have developed a new type of soft robot inspired by Kirigami, an ancient Japanese paper cutting art, and the way in which snakes move. Snakes are able to grip the ground with their scales to propel themselves forward, a skill that allows them to move in environments more smoothly.

In order to mimic the friction-assisted locomotion of snakes, engineers used Kirigami, laser cutting small slices into a flat plastic sheet wrapped around an elastomer actuator to replicate snake scales. The result is a soft robot with the capability to traverse difficult environments for inspection, search and rescue or monitoring purposes, or potentially perform complex, laparoscopic medical procedures.
