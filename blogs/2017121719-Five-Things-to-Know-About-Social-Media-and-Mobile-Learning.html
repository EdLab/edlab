Five Things to Know About Social Media and Mobile Learning

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2017


While professional and educational spheres once strictly excluded social media use, the last year, in particular, has seen the tables turn as social media and mobile learning have risen to new levels of importance in everything from classrooms to national politics. Here are some of the findings we found most intriguing this year:

1. It's Time to Admit That There's No Such Thing as a Digital Native
Despite popular belief, researchers report that there are no significant generational advantages in technological expertise between those who have grown up in a digital environment and those who have not.

2. When Is Connected Too Connected?
Researchers find that while smartphones are intended to connect people, they can actually create the opposite effect in certain situations when users complete a task.

3. The Five Things You Need to Know to Successfully Use Twitter in the Classroom
Researchers offer suggestions to instructors to optimize student learning outcomes based on ten years of reviewing Twitter use in classrooms.

4. How Social Media Affects News Sharing, Network Diversity, and Political Participation
A study reveals how quality and depth of news engagement online affects political participation.
