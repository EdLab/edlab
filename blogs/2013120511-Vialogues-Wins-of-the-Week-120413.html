Vialogues Wins of the Week, 12.04.13

Kate Meersschaert

December 5, 2013

Private! We'd like to start this post by giving special recognition to the people behind the latest Vialogues release, especially Zhou and Pranav for putting in countless hours of dev time and Daniel for creating the overall design vision. The newness has not yet been announced publicly, so in the meantime, we invite everyone to explore [read: try to break] our newest features-- share central (on the play page) and closed captioning-- and send us your feedback. For more on what's new, check out our release notes. Here's to you, team Vialogues. In other Vialogues news... Usage Stats (from 11/20 - 12/3) New users - 134 New vialogues - 265 Total visits - 5,513 Vialogues Sightings on the World Wild Web Lots to report this week- it's two weeks worth of sightings! We didn't go to SLOAN-C this year, but we were still there! How's that, you say? David Wicks, Associate Professor of Curriculum & Instruction at Seattle Pacific University, is the first author on a very interesting study entitled, "Synchronous and asynchronous video conferencing tools in an online-course: Supporting a community of inquiry". In it, he compares the effectiveness of Google Hangouts, Vialogues, and Wordpress. Which tool best supported student discussions? Read the study to find out! TL;DR: Professor uses Vialogues in research study with students. Finds out how well it performs under pressure. We got an honorable mention on Ditch That Textbook's list of 8 Digital Tools for 21st Century Learners. Power user Paul Allison created vialogues for the site YouthVoices, a collaborative of teachers that encourages lively conversations with young people over a variety of interesting subjects. His vialogues focus on water education. WM Irvin Elementary school in North Carolina lists Vialogues as an Audio/Visual resource for its teachers. Elementary school teachers using Vialogues! It's totally a thing. Sarah Frick, will you be our bff? Check yes or no. We fell in love with the comment you made on this post. Thanks for spreading the good word. The American Council on the Teaching of Foreign Languages held their 2013 convention on 11/23. One of the workshops, entitled "Measuring Student Literacy When Completing Speaking, Listening, Reading and Writing Tasks in the Foreign Language Class", showed participants how to use Vialogues to teach a language.
