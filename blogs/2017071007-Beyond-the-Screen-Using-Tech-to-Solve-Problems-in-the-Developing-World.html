Beyond the Screen: Using Tech to Solve Problems in the Developing World

dinosossi2

July 10, 2017


Apps lead to gaps in the physical world.

Most of our greatest challenges reside in physical spaces beyond the contours of the screen.

And this may not be better seen than in the developing world.

The American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) Innovation Showcase highlights the absolute finest hardware work in the world.

The showcase touts itself as “the first global competition for hardware with a social purpose.”

And this year’s edition highlighted some highly promising technology.


  • The BioLite HomeStove cooks as well as generates electricity for mobile phones and lights. It needs 50% less fuel than open cooking and reduces home smoke emissions by up to 90%.
  • The Ev8 is a powerless collapsible cooling chest that can add 20 days or more to the life of fruits and vegetables. It will retail at $25.
  • And Plenoptika is a one-stop-shop eyesight exam before buying lenses.

The developing world is coping with many challenges. And the ASME Innovation Showcase is just one way for people to collectively improve lives on a global scale.
