How to Make College Cheaper

bhughes

December 4, 2008

The New York Times article, College May Become Unaffordable for Most in U.S., highlights the alarming trend of education's rising costs. Here at EdLab, we acknowledge this fact (it's built into our core mission) and strive to create components of a new, sustainable infrastructure for education. So, I'd like to invite EdLab staff and others to read the Times article, and post some comments here on some basic ways you think we can address this issue. No idea can be too small or too big! Just some off-the-cuff responses... For example, if attending a college is partly about living on campus, then I think students could help defray the cost through external service to a community. A more drastic measure, I think, is staffing down campuses... creating opportunities for students to live and learn together, without huge administrative costs. Services like professors showing up in person at lecture halls seems a bit decadent against the backdrop of rising costs, no? What kinds of educational experiences could we create to make education better and cheaper?
