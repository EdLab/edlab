Teaching Old Dogs New Tricks on iPads

Ryan.Allen

January 23, 2018


Can dogs benefit from innovations in edtech? Annie Grossman, co-owner of Manhattan’s School for Dogs, thinks so. She incorporates iPad training into her sessions with canines. Her dogs can already distinguish between different kinds of devices, from smartphones to iPads, and perform other simple tasks, like pushing on-screen buttons or answering basic yes–no questions.

This training isn’t just for funny tricks. Phillip Agnert, a customer who also designed an app for dogs, hopes that he can train his dog to help him with basic functions while he flies an airplane, such as pressing a screen to display the weather gauge. Another owner uses the iPad as a reward for her service dog after stressful days of navigating New York City streets.

While there aren’t too many apps for dogs right now, Grossman believes that in the future more innovations will incorporate man’s best friend. What are some possible uses of technology that can be adapted for our four-legged friends? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues
