LibriVox

Carmen James

April 19, 2015


LibriVox is a platform for distributing free audio versions of public domain content. Users can download the LibirVox app to their phone to listen to the audio files or listen on the internet. Users can also download audio files to their computer and reappropriate the files for other uses. The audiobooks are recorded by volunteers.

Pros:

All the books you could want to hear are at your fingertips. LibriVox audiobooks are great for commuting, listening to the classics while taking notes for class, or multi-tasking by integrating literature into other activities. There are no ads, so unlike commercial products, LibriVox provides educational and recreational users a commercial free listening experience. The book content that is adapted for the audiobooks is in the public domain and LibriVox releases its audio content back to the public domain. This allows users to repurpose the material in any way they want. Additionally, LibriVox volunteers read in many languages.

Cons:

The books are recorded by volunteers, which means the quality can vary. Moreover, Librivox encourages creation over quality, as it encourages "anyone with a microphone and a computer" to record. This means a massive amount of audio with an equally massive range of quality. LibriVox might consider allowing users to rank favorite readers and books. Currently, the website does not offer this feature and users can only search by author, title, genre, and language.

Our Takeaway:

This is an excellent resource that can continue to be refined and tweaked, such as by adding a system of quality ranking or "most popular" lists. Librivox is a promising way to engage students and may be a means for teachers to reach students with different learning styles. Teachers also might consider having their students volunteer to read texts as a way to practice public speaking.
