Google Street View Takes Search to a New Level

DebraLee

October 15, 2017


In 2007, Google delivered a digital mapping database of more than 80 billion photos taken in 85 countries. A decade later, the Google Street View rig is capturing not just clearer, closer shots, but also detailed information: signs, business names, even a notice posted in the window of a local store. The success of the database has likewise raised consumer expectations for richer, deeper information, and increased demands of Google’s image recognition algorithms for answers to more complex questions.

To help in its quest to map the world, Google is inviting pro photographers, travelers, and organizations to participate in a camera loan program that captures areas of cultural, historical, and touristic significance, plus business interiors. Participants can borrow from a fleet of specially designed cameras: Street View Trolly fits through museum doorways and navigates around sculptures; Street View Snow Mobile can take photos on hilly, snow-capped terrain; Street View Trike fits into narrow alleyways in Barcelona and Paris; and Street View Trekker is for maneuvering through tight, narrow spaces or locations only accessible by foot.

What learning opportunities will be created by opening up Google Maps to the interiors of more locations? How can you imagine this technology being developed even further? Tell us what you think in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
