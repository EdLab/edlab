Raising Student Engagement in Higher Ed With Top Hat

Personalized instruction for lectures

Top Hat is a Toronto-based software company that specializes in creating tools to connect teachers and professors in higher ed. Its platforms are used by over 500 universities in Canada and the US. In a changing higher education landscape, student engagement is a difficult feat. To overcome the disengagement students may feel when lost in a lecture hall of hundreds, Top Hat provides tools that allow teachers to personalize their instruction during lectures and beyond.

Seeking new tools for engagement

The cloud-based software platforms are geared toward helping professors and students connect inside and outside the classroom. The company has three products: Lecture, InteractiveText, and Content Marketplace. Lecture has an integrated set of tools that control slides, attendance, online discussion, and live quizzes. InteractiveText allows professors to help students study through pre-made content that connects to students through text, videos, and interactive elements. The Content Marketplace aggregates teaching resources such as textbooks.

In a 2016 Pulse Survey, professors reported that their biggest teaching challenge was students not paying attention or participating in class. Research has previously shown that Facebook and other social media use affect student engagement in various ways. Platforms like Top Hat could encourage professors to embrace classroom tech to address this growing concern and increase engagement, despite growing lecture attendance.
