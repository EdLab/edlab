Job Interview Common Mistakes

Jeffrey Hado

July 15, 2015

Job interviews can be very intimidating and the pressure and anxiety that comes along with it makes matters worse. Many people do not perform well under pressure and this can prove very costly when it comes to the interview process. Additionally, lack of experience can be a major cause for a “bad” job interview. Here are some common mistakes that lead to bad job interviews:

  • Not having an elevator pitch–One of the most popular interview questions, “please tell us about yourself” can be either easy or difficult depending on your preparation. This question is an opportunity to inform the employer the values you’re bringing to the table. It is essentially the narrative behind the resume. Candidates must be well prepared with an answer that will catch the employer’s attention.
  • Feedback after Rejection-Asking for feedback after rejection only tells the employer about your interest in personal growth. Even though it might not change the employer’s decision, it’s a sign of humility and can certainly leave the hiring manager thinking and keeping you in mind for future opportunities.
  • Lack of questions after interview–It is very important to ask thoughtful questions at the end of an interview. It can be a sign of interest and might tip the balance in your favor.
