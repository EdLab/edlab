How to get a show on Manhattan Neighborhood Network

Gus Andrews

August 4, 2008

Hi everyone, I've been suggesting to Joachim and others that now that we have some 200-odd episodes, we have enough content to run a show on Manhattan Neighborhood Network. MNN, for those of you who don't know, consists of four public-access cable channels which anyone who is a Manhattan resident can put a show on with a little bit of effort. FCC regulations and agreements between the city of Manhattan and local cable providers stipulate that commercial cable broadcasters must pay fees which support public access (as MNN's site explains). Manhattan is particularly fortunate to have not just one such channel, but four; as far as I know this is unprecedented elsewhere in the US. Instructions on getting a show on MNN are posted online here. I am gonna try to submit a proposal that AfterEd get a weekly half-hour slot making use of old and new content. Stay tuned for progress reports!
