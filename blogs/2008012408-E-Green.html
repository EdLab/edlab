E-Green

Frank Webster

January 24, 2008

The Green Movement is becoming a worldwide force to be reckoned with: GREENER GADGETS CONFERENCE - February 1st 2008, New York City We are excited to announce that in just over one week BusinessWeek and the McGraw Hill Conference Center in Midtown Manhattan will host the first ever Greener Gadgets Conference on Friday, February 1st 2008, organized in conjunction with Inhabitat.com and Marc Alt + Partners. This groundbreaking conference will focus on emerging technology innovation in the consumer electronics industry and the business case for sustainable consumer electronics design: http://bwso.businessweek.com/c.asp?690364&4464c149a5c8af8a&1 The first-of-its kind Greener Gadgets Conference will feature keynote presentations from digital environmental artist Chris Jordan, inventor and artist Natalie Jeremijenko, Mary Lou Jepsen from One Laptop Per Child Project, as well as environmental sustainability leaders from the world's largest consumer electronics brands, such as HP, Nokia, Sony and Philips. The event will include provocative panel discussions on electronics recycling programs, energy efficiency, and emerging renewable energy sources for cell phones and laptops, finishing up with a live, on-stage presentation and audience judging of the Greener Gadgets Design Competition: SCHEDULE FOR GREENER GADGETS 10:00 OPENING KEYNOTE: Chris Jordan makes the case for greening the consumer electronics industry through his stunning photography of e-waste 11:00 DESIGN KEYNOTE: Mary Lou Jepsen presents the One Laptop Per Child project and talks about the design of the world's most sustainable laptop 12:00 PANEL DISCUSSION: Materials, lifecycle and recycling with HP, Nokia, Sony & EPEAT 2:00 TECHNOLOGY KEYNOTE: Markus Terho from Nokia presents Nokia's environmental vision for the future of greener mobile communications 2:30 PANEL DISCUSSION: Electronics & Energy Efficiency with Intel, Philips, IDEO & OLPC 3:00 PANEL DISCUSSION: New Forms of Mobile Renewable Energy (Featuring kinetic mobile power, solar, wind, and hydrogen fuel cell technologies with Solio, M2E, MTI, Hymini and Boston Power 4:30 CLOSING KEYNOTE: Natalie Jeremijenko presents her always-compelling electronic inventions and design installations 5:30 GREENER GADGETS LIVE DESIGN COMPETITION: Finalists from the Greener Gadgets Design Competition will be presented on stage, and winners selected by the audience - moderated by Allan Chochinov of Core77 The Greener Gadgets Conference is happening next week and close to selling out, so if you are interested in attending this pioneering event, we encourage you to purchase tickets as soon as possible. http://bwso.businessweek.com/c.asp?690364&4464c149a5c8af8a&1 PRESS & OTHER INQUIRES: info@greenergadgets.com
