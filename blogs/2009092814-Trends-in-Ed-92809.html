Trends in Ed, 9.28.09

Arianna Choi

September 28, 2009

The latest New York Times Magazine has the School Issue out, and an article from it is currently ranked no. 1 as the most emailed on nytimes.com in the last 24 hours and no. 5 in the last week. Now that's a lot of article forwarding. Can Right Kinds of Play Teach Self-Control? by Paul Tough reports on the Tools of the Mind methodology which states that the key to developing self-regulation is lots of mature and dramatic play. Some case examples from experiments:

  • 4-year-old children were first asked to stand still for as long as they could. They typically did not make it past a minute. But when the kids played a make-believe game in which they were guards at a factory, they were able to stand at attention for more than four minutes.
  • Prekindergarten-age children were asked to memorize a list of unrelated words. Then they played “grocery store” and were asked to memorize a similar list of words. In the play situation, on average, the children were able to remember twice as many words compared to the non-play situation.
