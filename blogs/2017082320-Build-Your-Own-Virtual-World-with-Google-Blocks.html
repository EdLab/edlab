Build Your Own Virtual World with Google Blocks

saraehardman

August 23, 2017


Virtual reality (VR) designers currently face an interesting problem: how do you create 3D environments on a 2D computer screen? To bypass this, new apps are emerging that let people actually design 3D objects within virtual reality. One of the most popular ones at the moment is Google Blocks.

Pros:

Designed for the HTC Vive and Oculus Rift, Google Blocks is a virtual reality app that has two goals. First, it lets VR designers create 3D models directly in virtual reality, which they can then export to their other creations or put them directly online. Second, it’s a fantastical sandbox world for any user. Put on a VR headset with Google Blocks and you are transported to a virtual world that bends to the whims of your imagination. Create a forest you can actually walk through, or a life-size donut, or a planet fit for Antoine de Saint-Exupéry’s Little Prince.

Similar to Google Tilt Brush, Google Blocks feels like stepping into a virtual canvas. While the controls might not be intuitive to people unfamiliar with VR in general, they are easy enough to learn that even inexperienced users will find control in a matter of minutes. Some of the features are particularly cool. When you flip over your controller for the first time to see the pallet of paint colors on the back, it’s difficult not to smile in wonder.

Cons:

The two goals mentioned before also come with downsides. While Google Blocks lets designers create 3D models directly in VR, it cannot create environments or objects as realistic as Oculus Rift’s 3D modeling app, Medium. This might suit some designers’ needs, but not those who want more lifelike models. In addition, while the app provides a wide-open space for virtual creation, it does not establish goals or direction. Because of this, some users might become quickly bored.

Our Takeaway:

Google Blocks can be both a useful tool for VR designers and a fantastical experience for all. When trying it out with my colleagues, most everyone had smiles plastered beneath their headsets. That said, its awe-inspiring qualities could diminish the longer you spend in the virtual world, which, combined with the lack of end goal, could deter some users from coming back.
