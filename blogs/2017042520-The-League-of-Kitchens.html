The League of Kitchens

EdLab Studios

April 25, 2017


It is said that food is medicine. Food is love. And even more so, food is a portal to another culture. The League of Kitchens was founded by Lisa Gross, the daughter of a Korean immigrant and a Jewish NYC native, with this very intention in mind. The organization provides epicurious New Yorkers with immersive international culinary experiences by going right to the source, namely skilled immigrant women from places like Lebanon, Trinidad, India, Argentina, Uzbekistan, Greece, Nepal , and more as the League of Kitchens grows. Home-cooks train to become instructors, turn their kitchens into classrooms, and translate intuition into easy-to-follow recipes for 5.5 hour "Immersion" or 2.5 hour "Taste of" workshops. Participants not only learn the steps and techniques required to cook their way through a multi-course meal, but explore the historical and ethnic importance of these foods through the first-hand experience of their host. The immersive cultural exchange ends with students and teacher sitting down to share a meal, enjoying the fruits of their labor while creating a dialog for greater cultural understanding.
