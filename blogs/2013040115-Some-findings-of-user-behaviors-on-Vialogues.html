Some findings of user behaviors on Vialogues

Sharon Hsiao

April 1, 2013

***PRIVATE please! I was wondering how people used Vialogues, such as time codes, play pause behaviors, etc., My assumptions: H1. The longer the video, the more likely people click on pause and play. H2. More interactions (comments), more likely people click on pause and play. H3. The longer the video, the more likely people click on the time codes to locate the fragments of dialogues. H4. More interactions (comments), more likely people click on time codes. Data: this year, January and February videos, except the seminar videos and EdLab EdTech videos, and found... - There's a high correlation between comments and play-pause actions. (The more interactions of the Vialogues, the more likely users pause and play). - No correlations between the video length and the play-pause actions. - There's even a negative correlation between the comments and time-code clicks. - No correlations between video length and time code clicks. So, only H2 holds. What does it mean? The good thing is the more comments result in more play-pause. It shows, users are more engaging, when the interaction is more pronounced. Perhaps, when an active conversation is going on, we should promote those conversations and demote less active ones. However, the time code feature wasn't really effective enough to demonstrate users' engagement. Possibly, the comments are really sparse, do not indicate "interesting" conversations or simply users do not know the time-code feature? Perhaps, more focused learning settings can demonstrate otherwise.
