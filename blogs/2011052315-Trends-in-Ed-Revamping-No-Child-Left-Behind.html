Trends in Ed: Revamping No Child Left Behind

Nantwi

May 23, 2011

With President Obama officially declaring that the No Child Left Behind Act should be scrapped off the books, Republicans in the House are wasting little time in ensuring many of the provisions of the act are removed, a first step in hopefully changing or making serious upgrades to the Act. The bill, heavily disapproved by Democrats, seeks to eliminate 43 educational programs, including those on teacher literacy and dropout prevention, among others. The 43 programs marked for elimination were divided into five broad categories: -those that were denied funding in the recently approved budget bill, Programs that lost their funding in the most recent budget bill -those that President Obama proposed in his 2012 budget to be consolidated -those that haven't been funded in recent years -those approved but never funded -those that the Republicans see as “duplicative or inappropriate for the federal government” For more on the plans to full revamp NCLB, read here.
