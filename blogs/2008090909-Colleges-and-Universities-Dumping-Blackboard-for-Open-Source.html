Colleges and Universities Dumping Blackboard for Open Source

Frank Webster

September 9, 2008

Anita Lauer forwards this article from The Chronicle about institutions of higher ed who are disenchanted with Blackboard's rising prices, poor customer support and bullying competitors. Although smaller colleges may find a cost advantage in Moodle or Sakai, ...officials say open-source programs can end up costing just as much as, or even more than, Blackboard's software when staff time is taken into account. It all depends on how much customization a college wants, or how many features it needs.
