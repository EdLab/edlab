Trends in Ed 10.14.09

Jeannie Crowley

October 14, 2009

Christian Crumlish, the curator of the Yahoo! Design Pattern Library, recently described the guiding principles that should be followed when adding a social experience to an existing site or application. A brief overview of the principles is included below.
To Do

  • Pave the Cowpaths: Look where the paths are already being formed by behavior and then formalize them.
  • Talk Like a Person: If a website does not communicate from the get-go that it is peopled — and written — by ordinary human beings, how will people ever feel comfortable there? The antiseptic air of a hospital or the bureaucratic formality of the department of motor vehicles is no environment for fostering connections, relationships or collaboration.
  • Play Well with Others: Embrace open standards, share data outside of the bounds of your application, accept external data within the sphere of your application, and support two-way interoperability.
  • Learn from Games: Even in the enterprise, interfaces don't have to be dry and tedious. Think about how to delight your users and encourage them to engage with each other.
    • To Avoid
  • Potemkin Village: The overly elaborated set of empty community discussion areas or other collaborative spaces, created in anticipation of a thriving population rather than grown organically in response to their needs.
  • Cargo Cult: Don't imitate superficial features of successful websites and applications without really understanding what makes them work.
  • Don't Break Email: Using email as a one-way notification or broadcast medium while disabling your users' ability to hit reply is unfriendly and standoffish.
  • The Password Anti-Pattern: Asking users to give you their passwords on other systems so that you can import their data for them trains them to be loose and insecure with their private information.
  • Ex-Boyfriend Bug: You try to leverage a user's social graph without realizing that some of the gaps in a person's network may be deliberate and not an up-sell opportunity.
