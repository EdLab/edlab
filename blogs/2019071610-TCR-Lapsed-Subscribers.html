TCR Lapsed Subscribers

Christine.Odenath

July 16, 2019

For this analysis, I was provided a list of 32k individuals who lapsed on their TCR subscriptions at some point over the last 20 years. This means they had been TCR subs, but they are not subscribing anymore. Why would ANYONE stop subscribing to TCR, you ask? Surely death could be the only plausible reason. But unless ~40% of people die within 1 year of subscribing to TCR (in which case we should really check the library basement for John Dewey hauntings), there must be another explanation.


Rather than googling 32,000 individuals, I focused on length of subscriptions, and the years in which these subscriptions began and ended. I wanted to find trends in subscriber lapses and explanations for those trends. I found trends, but I honestly don't know enough about TCR's marketing (ahem, sorry, OUTREACH) history to know what factors may have influenced these trends... This is where I'd love everyone's insights.


The link to my report is below. I've made it open for commenting so please comment away! Any questions I feel I cannot answer with my limited knowledge are written in red. I'm not providing any insights here because I want you to look at the full report. Selfish click bait!
