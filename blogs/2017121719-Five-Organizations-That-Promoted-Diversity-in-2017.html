Five Organizations That Promoted Diversity in 2017

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2017


This year we highlighted the work of organizations that are leveraging technology to provide more accessible learning opportunities to women, seniors, refugees, and diverse youth. Whether helping women run for elected office or teaching displaced Iraqi refugees to code, these are our favorite organizations that positively promoted diversity in 2017:

1. VoteRunLead
This platform is encouraging women to share leadership stories and is readying them for public office and civic life. The creators hope that increased visibility in election outcomes will elevate policies that are important to women.

2. Re:Coding Refugee Camps
Supported by the United Nations Development Program, this program is training displaced Iraqi youth in web development and preparing them for careers in technology.

3. Road Scholar: Travel Geared for Lifelong Learners
This non-profit travel agency caters to lifelong learners by providing searchable expert-guided adventures and tours.

4. Girl Develop It
Initially just a single classroom in NYC, this program now offers classes in 57 cities, teaching adult women computer science and development skills.

5. BLUE1647
This innovation center is dedicated to preparing diverse individuals in entrepreneurship and technology skills.
