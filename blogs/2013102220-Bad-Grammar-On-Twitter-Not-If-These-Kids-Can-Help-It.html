Bad Grammar On Twitter? Not If These Kids Can Help It.

jvazquez

October 22, 2013


As debates rage on concerning the effects that social media and texting have on students' grammar, a school in Brazil decided to take a more proactive approach. Noticing that an alarming number of tweets from their students' English-speaking celebrity role models contained spelling and grammatical errors, teachers at the Red Balloon School concocted a creative way to engage their English-learning students. Using Twitter as a medium, students were soon pointing out grammatical missteps and replying directly to the offending celebrity! (To date, no celebrity has shown appreciation for their impromptu grammar lesson.)

The relevance of this activity, both in regards to the lesson and students' interests, serves to increase student engagement and participation in an otherwise notoriously droll subject. It also heightens students’ awareness of grammar in a wide variety of contexts and may even extend to their own personal, social communications.

Have you noticed your grammar and/or the grammar of others slipping over the years since the advent of social media and texting? Is it just a matter of context (i.e., there’s a time and place for everything)? Get in on the ongoing discussion on Vialogues to leave your thoughts.
