EdLab Review: SlideShare

Janet Jang

October 11, 2011

SlideShare is a professional and educational community-based presentation hosting service where you can upload your Powerpoint and Word Document presentations and receive comments and feedback by a wide audience. It is used to share ideas, conduct research, and establish networks. Pros: Once the Powerpoint is uploaded onto the website, it is converted into Flash format, which makes it easier for the user to view the presentation (they don't need to go through the hassle of downloading the actual powerpoint). Because Slideshare is linked with Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn, it helps expose your content to a wider audience with more opportunities for it to go viral. They also offer Zipcast, a place to schedule web meetings and group chats. All of these resources are free unless you choose a Pro account which can range from $19-$249 a month. Cons: Because the files are converted to Flash format, any animation or sound effects that may have been in the presentation will be lost. As a solution, SlideShare allows users to sync mp3 audio to the slides to create a webinar. However, that also results in mismatching slides and sounds quit often. EdLab Relevance: I think we can learn from the success of this company that the concept of an online social community is crucial even for more professional tools like SlideShare and EdLab's very own Vialogues and Ednode. I think it wouldn't hurt to emphasize and establish the link between Vialogues and Ednode EVEN more. Our Takeaway: SlideShare and other professional and educational tools shows us how social media plays an important role in facilitating healthy, academic, and professional discussion.
