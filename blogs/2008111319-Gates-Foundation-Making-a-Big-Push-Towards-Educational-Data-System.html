Gates Foundation Making a Big Push Towards Educational Data System

fanwar

November 13, 2008

I attended a really nice talk today with Gilberto Conchas (a professor and program officer at the Gates Foundation). One of the interesting things that came up in the Q/A was that the Gates Foundation is making a big push towards funding new data systems for use in both K-12 and post-secondary school environments. I checked this out online and found that there is a big new push - with the foundation putting forward $500 Million towards this objective. The article below references this and many other new initiatives. http://gothamschools.org/2008/11/12/gates-foundation-will-steer-its-education-giving-in-a-new-direction-but-how-much-impact-will-the-billions-have/ I know the Edlab had some effort in the past in this vain (the NED education database). It would be interesting to explore whether we could participate in this initiative and what new technologies we might be able to create to support the mission of putting data to use in the service of education. Others' experiences in this area would be appreciated.
