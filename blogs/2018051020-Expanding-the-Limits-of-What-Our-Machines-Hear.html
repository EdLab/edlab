Expanding the Limits of What Our Machines Hear

Melanie.Hering

May 10, 2018


"Speech technology for the real world." (About Soapbox Labs)

Raising Children’s Voices

Whether encountered on the Amazon Echo or through an AR game, most basic speech recognition technology struggles with accuracy when capturing children's voices because children’s speech behavior is markedly different from that of adults. In order to combat this problem, Irish startup Soapbox Labs has used real-world data to develop real-time speech recognition technology that can be used for voice control in interactive toys and smart devices, educational apps, and for speech assessment purposes. Additionally, its program interface is cloud-based, making it easy to integrate into third party apps and web services and allowing it to work across any device or platform.

Increasing Accuracy

Soapbox Labs uses "deep neural networks based on speech technology and a large diverse database" that utilizes accents and dialects from all over the world. Through combining their expansive database, the team’s knowledge of children’s voices and behaviors, and machine learning, Soapbox Labs has created algorithms and software to detect and react to child voices. Also, attentive to the noisy environments kids often find themselves in, the technology was created with the intent to work on all mobile devices and laptops in classrooms, playgrounds, cars, and homes.

With the edtech and smart home appliance markets growing, accurate and reliable child voice recognition software is critical, making Soapbox Labs’ work potentially relevant and useful. Though still a very young startup, Soapbox Labs is clearly off to a promising start, creating technology that almost any modern program targeted towards young children and their caregivers will value.
