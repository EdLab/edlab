Harvard Education Review website: a new face lift or quick paint job?

Karen Bryner

November 3, 2006

Harvard Education Review has a new website. The previous design left me feeling like I had just stepped into the sparse waiting room in a large warehouse: no inviting colors or design, no comfortable reading spot with a variety of materials to peruse, just the stripped-down basics telling me how to sign up for a subscription or submit an article. Trying to navigate other links on the site gave me the sense that there was an amazing amount of information that was securely stored well beyond my unsubscribed-status reach. The new HER home page can be linked to from the nicely designed Harvard Education Publishing Group home page. Unfortunately, however, it seems the 'waiting room' has only gotten a new paint job. Although HER website is easier to navigate, I am still left wanting. HER's website has always made me appreciate the richness and more personal feel of the TC Record website; and it still does. (Although the comments above may appear to be flowing from TC loyalty, where I am currently studying, it is more lamentation for my previous alma mater.)
