Trends in Ed, 4.2.09

jvazquez

April 2, 2009

Has the LMS gone too far? The latest blackboard app posts (see here and here) got me thinking. While my LMS experience is limited to Moodle, BlackBoard, and Classweb, I think I have seen enough to make some suggestions. Actually, one blogger took the words right out of my mouth when he said,

“I constantly hear people (across many organizations) complain about their learning management system (LMS). They complain that their LMS has a terrible interface that is nearly unusable. Upgrades are difficult and cumbersome. Their employees' data is locked in to a proprietary system. Users hate the system. It's ugly. (Did I miss anything?)
They are pretty unattractive to look at. What's more is that I have been trying to comprehend the hubbub surrounding the social interactivity of them. I've created courses on Moodle and added wikis and blogs and thought... what's the advantage of putting a blog here? Will people actually use this wiki? Maybe I'm being too critical... but I have never actually seen anyone use the chat or forum functions in an LMS. Others may be questioning the addition of such socially based activities, as well; the next move for LMSs seems to be an integration with other systems that offer educational management, such as Microsoft's SharePoint. I think BlackBoard's recent move to the iTunes app store was a step in the right direction. It's a useful way for students and teachers to engage with the LMS and doesn't [seem] to give either party sensory overload. I guess if I could give Blackboard any advice, it would be [cue Full House meaningful moment music] you can't be all things to all people. Sometimes it's better to just be the best at what you are expected to do…
