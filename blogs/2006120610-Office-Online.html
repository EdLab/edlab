Office Online

Dan Mallinger

December 6, 2006

Check out ThinkFree.com a service for editing Word, Excel and PowerPoint documents online. The Java based app also has a free download available for your home machine so you can work both on-and-offline on your documents with no fuss (or even muss!). Also, feel free to check out the ThinkFree Server I've set up locally here. (It's limited to a few users and has a time limitation, so use it while it's hot!) One hard to detect feature is that under the open/save menus "Look In" option, if you look closely you'll see a folder called "http://www.thinkfree.com..." which is your new way of saving/opening files stored online. This way you don't have to worry about sending files back and forth through email or using jump drives. Of course, it only works if you already have an active session from ThinkFree.com... Originally I hoped to use this technology for Doc.Life (Ez.D. to some of you), but after some testing I'm having my doubts. If you have any opinions regarding ThinkFree or more generally on the use of online technologies for collaborative writing/editing of dissertations I would be thrilled to hear what you have to say. Currently, I'm pushing away from online document editing but still considering offering a crippled version of ThinkFree (if possible) for online viewing and conversion.
