TCETC: Fluensee

jvazquez

May 13, 2009

Presenters: Jo, Faisal, Pranav When Faisal, Pranav, and I arrived at the presentation room, we discovered that we were lacking the cord that would allow us to project the Fluensee demo on the big screen. At the last minute, Faisal came up with the clever idea to pass around the actual iPhone that holds our test app so that the audience could experience Fluensee for themselves. It worked–they all seemed to like it! Among some feedback questions: Q: What translation tool do you use? I know that Google Translate has proven to be more literal than semantically accurate. A: We chose to use Google Translate because we found that while it may give a literal translation, this is still more accurate than other translation services. Q: Will the enhanced features (dictionary and Wikipedia searches) be built within Fluensee or will they be a separate app altogether? A: The enhanced features will be a part of the Fluensee app. Q: Who do you see using this product? How can you “test” it? A: We plan on giving this tool to international high school students and track data as to how, why, when, and where they are using it. In addition, we see this app being used by travelers or anyone who is in an environment in which they are unfamiliar with the text. As we continue to work on the development of Fluensee, we welcome this and all feedback in order to produce the most intuitive and useful app.
