Making the Ability to See Around Corners a Reality

Melanie.Hering

May 10, 2018


For anyone who's ever fantasized about being a secret agent, the laser system recently developed at Stanford University is beyond your wildest dreams. Stanford researchers' new system utilizes computational reconstruction models that analyze reflections of scattered light to image objects outside the line of sight; basically, it's technology that lets you see around corners.

Picking up information about the geometry of unseen objects isn't just of value to would-be spies, though; it can give self-driving cars the power to make safer, smarter decisions when navigating, and the fact that the system responds in mere seconds allows those decisions to be made very quickly. Additional applications of this technology include rescue scenarios where difficult terrain might prevent aerial vehicles from otherwise spotting people.

Would you trust a vehicle you're riding in to "see around a corner?" What else could this technology be used for? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion:
