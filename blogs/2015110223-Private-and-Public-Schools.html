Private and Public Schools

Abdulrahman Sanda

November 2, 2015

There is an assumption that private schools provide students with a better education. A recent studysuggests that a student’s academic achievement is determined more by their social economic status (SES) than the academic quality of their schools. The study found that when students’ family background and income levels are being compared, there is no difference in the quality of education. The comparison of the socioeconomic status of students in public and private schools weren't surprisingly distinct. It showed great comparison between both educational sectors, as there was correlation in many categories of success though private schools excelled in test taking.
