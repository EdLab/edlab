GallopNYC (Part 1)

EdLab Studios

January 26, 2014


The emotional connection with an animal. The calming rhythm of a steady walk. The feeling of empowerment to be up high and in charge. The sense of freedom. Anyone who has ridden or imagined riding a horse will be familiar with these feelings. At GallopNYC, they are put to therapeutic use for children with autism as well as other individuals with a wide range of disabilities. GallopNYC is on the forefront of emerging research in horsemanship therapy that helps its participants with motor, developmental, interpersonal, and emotional skills. These skills are much harder to develop in traditional classroom or therapy settings, because they simply don't provide the same motivation to focus and work hard that the opportunity to ride and connect with a horse does. Patience and self-control are developed alongside confidence, and the calm and joy of riding belie the balance, coordination, and strength required to ride, creating a perfect setting for a variety of therapeutic and learning activities.
