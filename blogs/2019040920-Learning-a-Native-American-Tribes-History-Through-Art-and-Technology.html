Learning a Native American Tribe's History Through Art and Technology

Melanie.Hering

April 9, 2019


Sculptures always tell a story, but thanks to technology, a piece of art in Dublin, California is able to do so with the voices of its story's main characters. A 150-foot-long bronze sculpture in this city pays homage to a spring that was responsible for sustaining the Native American tribe indigenous to Dublin as well as the settlers who established Dublin. The sculpture also pays homage to language; it is embedded with the harmonic pattern of voices across generations speaking the local Native American language lost and then rediscovered through a series of 1910 audio recordings.

This piece of art uniquely combines old technology (audio recordings) with new technology (each piece was created using 3D printing) to produce a sculpture that invites interaction and kinesthetic learning. Beyond bringing together old and new innovative techniques, the piece encourages awareness of Native American history in Dublin and teaches the importance of both language and nature in shaping identity.
