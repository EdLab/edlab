Charter Schools and Difficult Students

abagigah92

January 28, 2016

Charter schools are significantly changing the school system. They are providing education reforms that deals with hard to educate students. Students with disabilities, students learning English and low performing students excel at charter schools than they do at public schools. Critics argue that charter schools enroll less hard to teach students and push out underperforming students. Based on analysis gathered from charter schools in New York City, Denver and other independent school districts, low performing students are likely to leave charter school just as traditional public school. Although findings does not throw away the fact that some charter school do push out their weaker students. However; most charter schools retain hard to teach students and work with them just like traditional public schools are doing.
