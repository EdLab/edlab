EdLab Space

Angela Lee

November 17, 2010

Hello everyone, After all the positive feedback we've received from the changes we made to the kitchen, it is now time to tackle a bigger project: the EdLab Space. As you see, there are many old bookshelves laying around and not being used. They not only take up precious space, but are not that pretty. My former boss - an architect from Landis L Dooley Architect Pllc where I once interned-was able to come in this morning (11/17) to take a look at the space and give some suggestions. Below are a few key points, and please provide feedback on the post so we get a better idea of how to move forward with the space. - To maximize the use of the space, we can take out the old bookshelves - maybe we can start with the smaller ones in the meeting space so people can see how it looks. -After the bookshelves are taken out, we can paint the wall with a white board paint which will essentially turn the walls into white boards. This will eliminate the existing white boards and give us even MORE space!--I personally think this will make the space look very "lab" and "techy" like! -With the scrape wood from the bookshelves, we can build it around the column and make little stands for note pads? books? coffee? --Wrapping all the exposing wires running around the ceiling with PVC pipe. This will make the space look cleaner - and safer! Hope you are as excited about transforming EdLab as I am! Please use this post as a place for different ideas regarding to EdLab space:)
