The Edward M. Kennedy Institute

Nantwi

December 19, 2016


The recent general election raised questions about the role of civics in our evolving education system. There are concerns that civics has been ignored in favor of science and technology subjects, when one of the primary goals of education should be to prepare students for life as citizens in a democratic society.

The Edward M. Kennedy Institute for the United States Senate in Boston is positioned to reignite interest in civics education. Named after the long-serving U.S. Senator from Massachusetts, the institute offers resources and activities to engage visitors. Students can participate in virtual simulations that guide them through legislative processes in the Senate. The hope is that they'll be inspired to continue learning about all aspects of government and the democratic process.

What are some potential ways educators can use the Institute to teach civics and government? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
