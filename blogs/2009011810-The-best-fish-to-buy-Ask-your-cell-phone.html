The best fish to buy? Ask your cell phone

Stephen Asunka

January 18, 2009

Continuing the conversation on the affordances of mobile devices, I recently read an article about the Blue Ocean Institute's FishPhone - a text messaging service that assists fish shoppers make informed choices. To use FishPhone: simply send a text message that says FISH and the variety you're considering to 30644; you'll receive an instant text reply on the likely effects of your choice on your health as well as the health of the planet. This kept me wondering how practical it will be for Teachers College to equally implement such a service - the TCPhone. Users can text, for example, TCFACULTY or TCLIBRARY etc followed by a word (faculty member's name or book title) to 123... and instantly get all relevant facts and figures pertaining to the request. Sounds interesting.
