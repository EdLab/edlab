The Lower East Side Ecology Center

EdLab Studios

November 19, 2013


Everyone in New York is surrounded by the waterways upon which this city was built, but few people actually know much about them. Estuaries? Brackish water? Mercury levels? These foreign terms may not entice passers-by into learning about their surroundings, but the Lower East Side Ecology Center has found a good hook in its public fishing program. With a rod and a reel, anyone can learn about life below the surface and, along with it, just what is going on environmentally, ecologically, and biologically right outside our doors. But it's no bait-and-switch. Here, learning and recreation go hand in hand, where the primeval fun (and practicality) of fishing is located within an understanding of environmental influences on health, history, and future success.
