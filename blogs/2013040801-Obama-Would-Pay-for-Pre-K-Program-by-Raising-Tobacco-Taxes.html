Obama Would Pay for Pre-K Program by Raising Tobacco Taxes

abagigah92

April 8, 2013

According to New York Times, the number one plan to generate revenue to support President Obama's plan to help states expand prekindergarten programs is to increase the tax in the sale of cigarettes and other tobacco products. The tax on these products will go up by 50 cents, which could trim the deficit by $42 billion over 10 years. The Obama administration also proposed other incentives for states that create programs to help middle-class families. State will be given grants to offer full day kindergarten, bolster early head start programs for young children and expand home visiting services. Moreover the administration plans to promote spending cuts in order to provide revenue to help create new programs for prekindergarten students at the state level.
