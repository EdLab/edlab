Trends in Ed, 5.04.09

Arianna Choi

May 4, 2009

One Page, One Story: New Web Design Experiment @msnbc.com MSNBC.com has quietly begun a web design experiment that ‘re-engineers' how stories are consumed. An interactive designer at the media channel describes it as an initiative to advance beyond providing text supported by some photos. By carefully rearranging navigation features, the new design tries to make richer content easier to find and harder to ignore. I agree with the writer at Fast Company that it is a bit disorienting at first, but I quickly learned that intuition takes me farther than I was willing to allow. Obsv 1. Right below the headline, all related media elements are lined up to ‘jump to.' Obsv 2. Once you scroll down far enough that you no longer see the related media menu at the top of the page, a navigational dashboard automatically appears at the bottom to ‘jump to' other ways of learning more about the story. Obsv 3. All related media are available on a single page, so when you jump from text to data then to video or whatever else is available…you never leave ‘the story' you are consuming.
