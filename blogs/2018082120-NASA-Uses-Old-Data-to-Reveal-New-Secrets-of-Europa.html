NASA Uses Old Data to Reveal New Secrets of Europa

Ryan.Allen

August 21, 2018


Scientists have long suspected that Europa, a large moon orbiting Jupiter, could harbor large oceans twice the size of Earth's own oceans. In the 1990s, the Galileo satellite explored the Jupiter system, obtaining data with its camera and other sensors. Through this mission, scientists confirmed that the moon held vast oceans under its icy surface, but there were other readings that could never be understood at that time.

NASA has recently gone back through Galileo’s data from Europa to find new insight. As Robert Pappalardo, Senior Research Scientist in the agency’s Planetary Science Section, put it, "There are better tools now, better computational techniques, better computing." Using the latest technology available on the old dataset, scientists realized that the satellite had actually flown through liquid plumes emanating from the moon’s surface. Now they are using this new data to setup a future mission to the moon, possibly looking for life.
