Event Recap: Sal Khan & Carlos Slim @ NYPL

Kate Meersschaert

May 10, 2013

Billionaires, books and oh, yeah LEARNING! Jo and I traveled down to the main branch of the New York Public Library (NYPL) at the edge of Bryant Park for this much anticipated Sal Khan/Carlos Slim discussion re: online learning and access to technology with the head of NYPL. Overall, the event served to introduce Carlos Slim's foundation, Sal Khan's new book and to promote libraries as democratizing access points for technology. All worthy goals and accomplished with thoughtful insight and grace under fire (see below). The moderator and host both masterfully steered discussion towards interesting harbors and made sure the event stayed on-time and on-topic. Interesting to note that the event followed the EdLab Demo Night tradition of a Q&A mic at the end... with mixed results as the questions turned-into rambling political statements. Libraries are clearly now great seats of political debat and free speech! Overall, it was an awesome night (Jo and I recorded specific takeaways & points of interest via Twitter - see below.) Laughter is the best weapon... Throughout the discussion members of the audience laughed... at Carlos Slim. Literally, laughed. It was a staged protest and while, extremely well orchestrated, effective and quite honestly, a brilliant example of democracy safely at work, it distracted from the goal of promoting open access to learning and seemed sadly out-of-place at a LIBRARY. Especially out-of-place given the proposed cuts to libraries and free public institutions. Occupy Wall Street was contextually brilliant and "effective"... staging a protest in a space that provides a 1/4 of NY'ers with free access to technology (not to mention books - well, ok maybe not main branch... soon?!) was sadly out-of-synch.
