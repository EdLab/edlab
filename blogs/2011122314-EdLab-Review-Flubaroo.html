EdLab Review: Flubaroo

LauraCostello

December 23, 2011

Flubaroo (pronounced floo-ba-roo, not flub-a-roo as I initially thought) is a Google Docs add on meant to assist teachers with grading. Flubaroo checks against a teacher-created key to automatically grade, create statistics, and distribute grades to students via email. Pros: Flubaroo is an extremely simple, need-based tool created by a teacher and it fulfills its role well. They system is meant to be automated, but alerts teachers when more than half of the class gets a particular question wrong. It's fast and simple, if a little impersonal, and seems like it could dramatically decrease the workload for the right teacher. Google Docs integration means that it takes very little setup or sign-up to begin working and the process is natural for someone used to working with the Docs platform. Cons: Flubaroo is a niche product and requires right/wrong answers for automatic grading. It has the potential to eliminate the drudgery of grading single answer or multiple choice questions, but won't do much for long form assignments. EdLab Relevance: I love to find a tool that automates a mindless task! Flubaroo does one thing very well and there's something commendable in that. In concentrating on the big questions of education in the digital age, it's easy to forget that the biggest leaps we have made are a decrease in non-intellectual work for teachers and a way to communicate and share ideas between schools and classrooms. Flubaroo's existence demonstrates these essential improvements. Our Takeaway: Flubaroo is a simple tool that has a lot of time-saving potential.
