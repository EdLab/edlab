My take-aways from visiting Knewton, Gawker Media, VYou and Gojee: -Maintaining the happiness of employees seemed to be of utmost importance. For example, Knewton had an amazing snack room, Gawker had a shower (for bikers), a lounge and a roof-top garden for after-work parties. -White boards seemed to be the preferred ideation format - every company had at least 1. -Agile, responsive iteration through daily site updates, live feeds and constant monitoring of audience and client feedback is crucial. -A website that expresses the unique, expansive perspectives of team members can be a great way to project a big message (despite the actual group's size) about the culture of your group. Gojee has a great example of this here. -Openness among employees also appeared to be key, conversations and impromptu meetings seemed to occur organically and there were no cubicles in site. -Playfulness in the form of Legos, surfboard decor, parties and team-building was evident and seemed to contribute to a laid-back atmosphere. None of the groups we visited were running-around like a crazed Wall-Street trading floor. -Almost everyone we talked to was interested in coming to EdLab to present a seminar! Many thanks to Yudan for working his audio wizardry... iphone is a great tool, but not the easiest to convert audio output to usable web files!
