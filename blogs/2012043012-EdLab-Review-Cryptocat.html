EdLab Review: Crypto.cat

Kate Meersschaert

April 30, 2012

Crypto.cat is a free open source web app that lets users establish safe, encrypted conversations in the browser of their choice (especially secure if you are using Chrome) or on their smartphone. Crypto.cat describes their free service as aiming "to leverage both the ease of use and accessibility afforded by web applications and the security provided by client-side cryptography." Conversations are encrypted using "asymmetric keys", automatically "wiped" after an hour of inactivity, and users are able to send private encrypted files and images. Read more about Crypto.cat's young founder in this New York Times article. Pros: In addition to a multitude of advanced privacy features and compatibility with a wide variety of browsers & devices, Crypot.cat works with Facebook, creating a seamless integration and realistic alternative to the leading chat options. Cons: II couldn't find many... perhaps just that the chat interface is almost too simplistic and a bit daunting for a first time, non-technically advanced user (see screenshot below). If you find any additional issues, please add them as comments. EdLab Relevance: As we work to make our applications and projects more social, open source web apps like Crypot.cat are an interesting model to examine. Also, many team members are invested in online privacy issues and it will be interesting to track this project and the whole online "freedom" movement over the coming months. Our Takeaway: Free, easy-to-use instant messenger interface paired with advanced cryptography that is more secure than most bank vaults... what more could you want in an "IM" experience?
