Pure Data Focus Group event at TC

Stephanie Hunt

February 19, 2009

Gottesman Libraries hosted a "Pure Data Focus Group" event yesterday that I thought I'd report back on. Organized by Joe Deken, the event featured the original author of the PD programming language, Miller Puckette, and Hans-Christoph Steiner, one of the project's major developers. Pure Data (PD) is a graphical programming environment that's really quite flexible, supporting a range of projects from electronic music to screen-based graphics to physical computing. A number of resources, including presentations and project samples, are available on the <"http://puredata.info/docs/workshops">PD wiki. One of my favorite things about the presentation was Hans' focus on the importance of read/write computer programming literacy. His message reminds me of that of Seymour Papert. His presentation also got me thinking about Alan Kay's work on the creation of authoring environments that truly support the exploration and expression of powerful ideas. I'm interested to know whether anyone else is interested in promoting read/write programming literacy using beginner-friendly programming platforms. I'd love to meet with folks to explore the pros and cons of different programming environments from a programming literacy perspective. I've worked extensively with various LOGO programming environments, but I'd love to expand my knowledge to include other languages.
