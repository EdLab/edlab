The Year of the Robot

Renae Lesser

December 21, 2016


Robots are on the rise both inside and outside of the classroom. Here are some of the robotics studies we found most interesting this year.

1. Robots Imitate Cognitive Development Capabilities
This research shows that robots can learn to recognize both humans and avatars based on their physical attributes and behaviors.

2. Building Better Robot Tutors
This study hypothesizes new strategies that will make it easier for learners to ask robot tutors for help.

3. It's Getting Easier for Pre-K Kids to Learn Robotics
Researchers designed a set of wooden blocks that can be used by kids to program real robots.

4. Playful Talk Enhances Collaborative Robotics Learning
This study investigates how teasing, joking, and word play can enhances collaborative robotics learning.

5. Rethinking the Robot
Researchers in this study examine how elementary and middle schoolers in a summer robotics camp think about robots.
