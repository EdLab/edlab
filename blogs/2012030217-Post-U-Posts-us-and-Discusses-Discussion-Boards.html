Post U Posts us... & Discusses Discussion Boards

Kate Meersschaert

March 2, 2012

Private! Check it out! We have been cross-posted by Post U... so many P-O-S-T-S! This article both quotes Jo and reports on Mr. Mulgrew's experience presenting his seminar here at the EdLab. I found it while searching Post's blog for an entry on discussion boards mentioned by a guest during the seminar. I found a few including this one re: best practices for facilitating discussions boards and it then led me to this post describing the ideal faculty interaction on a discussion board as analogous to hosting a dinner party. What do you think of this "dinner party" analogy? For those of you who don't want to follow the links above, I've summarized the instructor's main tips for discussion board success: -Make sure all students feel welcome through your tone & personally "greeting" them when they first interact with the board. -Respond to each student's post. -Never remain silent. -Serve "delicious" content. -Extend return invitations both to the board & the online program. -Spread your attention around to all students, not just a few. -Spend extra time with struggling students. -Start new conversations & end stale ones. Do you agree? Should we consider these points as we develop mSchool/EdDias discussion boards? Do you feel these rules cultivate a "guide on the side" relationship or further the existent "sage on the stage?" What "rules" & caveats would you add?
