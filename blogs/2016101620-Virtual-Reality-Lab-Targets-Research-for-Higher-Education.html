Virtual Reality Lab Targets Research for Higher Education

saraehardman

October 16, 2016


"The Augmentarium is a revolutionary virtual and augmented reality (VR and AR) facility that brings together a unique assembly of projection displays, augmented reality visors, GPU clusters, human vision and human-computer interaction technologies, to study and facilitate visual augmentation of human intelligence and amplify situational awareness." (About the Augmentarium)

Expanding Human Potential

A lab at the University of Maryland is revolutionizing education through virtual and augmented reality research. The lab, called the Augmentarium, researches methods in which these technologies can improve medical, artistic, mechanical, and scientific learning. The Augmentarium is the first lab to use VR and AR to research such in-depth applications in these fields.

Simply put, the Augmentarium is using VR and AR to expand human capabilities in both practical and artistic experiences. For instance, the lab is testing ways to improve surgery and medical training by allowing doctors to see into a patient before making an incision. Similarly, the lab is creating VR environments that integrate cognitive and physical training to improve sports medicine and occupational athlete training. In addition to improving practical instruction, the lab also researches ways to enhance creative productions, such as using VR to fully immerse audience members into artistic performances. Other areas of research at the Augmentarium include astronomy, fluid dynamics, atmospheric and oceanic instabilities, cybersecurity, and big data.

VR Camps for Students

In addition to their research, the Augmentarium also hosts a VR summer camp for kids called CompSciConnect that focuses on promoting girls’ interest in the field. CompSciConnect encourages students to solve problems in various disciplines with technology. Last year, the campers created a video game for Oculus Rift, a VR headset.

New Horizons

Between the research and the education, the Augmentarium is exploring augmented technologies in ways that have never been done before. If VR and AR possibilities aren’t limitless, the Augmentarium wants to find and push against their outer bounds.
