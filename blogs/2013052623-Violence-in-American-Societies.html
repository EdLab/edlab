Violence in American Societies

Oumar Soumahoro

May 26, 2013

I'm sure most of you have heard the recent shooting of a 14 year old girl in Queens on a bus as she was returning home from a gathering. The shooting was the subject of an intense discussion in my English class last week. The young girl's senseless death was highlighted, among many recent shootings, about the frequency of gun violence in our nation. Though tragic mass shootings such as the one in Sandy Hook brought the gun violence debate to the national conscience, the killing of the young girl is an everyday occurrence in our major urban cities There were two major questions that has stayed with me following the classroom discussion: Is violence apart of the American culture? Does our culture (i.e. movies) play a role in how my fellow citizens view guns?
