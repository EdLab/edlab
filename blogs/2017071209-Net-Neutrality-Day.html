Net Neutrality Day

Kaitlin.Kehnemuyi

July 12, 2017

You might be getting funny pop-ups today when you visit different sites. Today has been marked as a day of action to support Net Neutrality with people and organizations (including the ALA!) joining together to encourage everyone to reach out to the FCC. It has been coordinated in response to the the FCC's first comment deadline on July 17th.  


Battle for Net Neutrality has complied a great list of videos (including the John Oliver piece that crashed the FCC) and articles about why Net Neutrality is important. The two cent recap is that it prevents ISPs from charging companies more to provide content to you faster or slowing that content down as you request it. For example, I have Verizon Fios at home which will rent me movies, but maybe the same movie is on Netflix and I already have a Netflix account so I chose to watch it that way. Verizon could slow down or charge Netflix a fee to let me watch that movie. And if results in buffering (ugh) or higher charges for me, I will probably abandon Netflix and just give more money to Verizon. 


The pop-ups provided by Battle for Net Neutrality are more prominent. I was sad to see that a lot of the companies involved opted for smaller banners at the top. They are easy to overlook as you click around.
