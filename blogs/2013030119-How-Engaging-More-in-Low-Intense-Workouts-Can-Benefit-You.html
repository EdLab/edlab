How Engaging More in Low Intense Workouts Can Benefit You

Khalil Abubakar

March 1, 2013

This article describes how low intense workouts like walking, standing and being active for long periods of time can benefit your health. After a study of 18 normal weighted people, ages 19 to 24, showed low intense workouts improved insulin, which regulates carbohydrates, fat metabolism and lipids, which stores energy and are components of the cell membrane levels. During the study, participants went through three regimes. First, each participant had to sit and not engage in any form of exercise for 14 hours as a control. The next activity was for participants to sit for 13 hours and do an hour of vigorous exercises. The third was for participants to sit for six hours, walk for four hours and stand for two hours. Researchers recorded the insulin and lipid levels of each participant after every regime. The results showed an hour of vigorous exercise improved cholesterol and lipid levels slightly, whilst long periods of sitting, walking and staying active improved cholesterol and lipids levels significantly. So based on this study and research, it seems hours of vigorous exercise is not enough to improve your metabolism or keep you healthy. Do you think this is an accurate assessment?
