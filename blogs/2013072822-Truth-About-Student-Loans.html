Truth About Student Loans

Bismark Appiah

July 28, 2013

After watching the video below, I didn't feel surprise that a year's tuition fee at Harvard was about $600 in 1952 but the amount increased enormously in 2012 to about $30,000 a year per student. As result, students have engaged in borrowing money to pay for their schooling. They have been borrowing so much money that 2/3 of those students are in debt; students national debt is much higher than total national credit card debit. Another important fact is tuition fees has increased more than inflation rate from 1987 to today. It is very sad because even if students are able to get a better job after graduating from college, some are going to be working and live to pay off debts.
