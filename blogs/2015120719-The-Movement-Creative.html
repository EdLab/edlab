The Movement Creative

EdLab Studios

December 7, 2015


When Nikkie Zanevsky first discovered parkour, the only resource she really had to learn it were videos on YouTube. That's why, along with some friends, she founded the Movement Creative, an organization that seeks to provide comfortable settings for kids and adults to practice parkour. Parkour is a sport that challenges the use and meaning of public space by teaching people to move through an urban environment as quickly, efficiently--and often atypically--as possible. By designing unique play spaces for an intergenerational public, the Movement Creative blends the practice of parkour with other movement techniques to teach students of all ages how to simultaneously express themselves and engage in physical education in their city. Classes take place in parks and public spaces, as well as on temporary, uniquely designed playgrounds and installations. The idea is to give students access to a type of physical education they've often lost by adulthood.
