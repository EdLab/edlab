"Lego Friends" - Lego for Girls! Wait, What?

Janet Jang

December 20, 2011

"If it takes color-coding or ponies and hairdressers to get girls playing with Lego, I'll put up with it, at least for now, because it's just so good for little girls' brains..." (Source: Bloomberg) Hmm.. I don't know about this. I thought there already was a lego for girls. It was called "Lego". But Lego says they conducted extensive research and found that girls favor role-play, but they also love to build–just not the same way as boys. Whereas boys tend to be “linear”–building rapidly and to finish a kit so it looks just like what's on the box–girls prefer “stops along the way,” and to begin storytelling and rearranging. Some might believe that this new approach only reinforces gender stereotypes. But I guess I can see how it's an active effort to make the company stand for something that isn't just for boys.
