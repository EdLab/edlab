Technology for the Adventure-Driven Classroom

Carmen James

March 23, 2014


Title: Hybrid Online Education: Identifying Integration Models Using Adventure Learning (2008)

Authors: Aaron Doering & George Veletsianos

Source: Journal of Research on Technology in Education

Research Question: How does the design of K-12 blended learning programs and a teacher’s strategy for implementing the technology affect student learning experiences?

Study Design: This study examined Adventure Learning within the context of a blended learning approach. Adventure Learning is experience-based learning in game and virtual space that allows students to consider real-world problems through authentic collaborative experiences. The study included twelve classroom observations, personal interviews, and eleven focus groups to collect data in three public elementary schools in a large city in the Midwest. The online education program GoNorth! served as a control.

Findings: The study revealed four models for the ways teachers can integrate technology into their classrooms: curriculum based, activities based, standards based, and media based. Overall, the most successful classroom implementations included more authentic interactive experiences. Perhaps most important for program designers, the study highlighted the challenge of implementation in the unpredictable teaching and learning environment of K-12 schools.

Moving Forward:

The authors shifted from believing that teachers needed to apply the program in a faithful sequential manner and concluded that it is more important for teachers to feel confident and empowered to integrate technology into their classroom practices in ways that build on their own experiences. They found that collaborating with other teachers in implementing the Adventure Learning curriculum led to better implementation and student learning. Collaborating teachers were able to talk, share ideas, brainstorm and build off each other.
