The VOICE Road Diary: Harvard Graduate School of Education

Kate Meersschaert

June 20, 2012

Ariel, Josh and I just arrived at the beautiful Harvard Graduate School of Education and are setting-up our command station in the Eliot Lyman Room on the second floor of Longfellow Hall. Today we will interview the following Teachers College Record authors, discuss their correspondent articles and share both subscription opportunities and ResearchBroker with them before heading to Wellesley later tonight: Susan Moore Johnson: "How Context Matters in High-Need Schools: The Effects of Teachers' Working Conditions on Their Professional Satisfaction and Their Students' Achievement" John Collins: "Proposal for the Creation of a Subdiscipline: Education Informatics" Hunter Gehlbach: "The Social Perspective Taking Process: What Motivates Individuals to Take Another's Perspective?" Gutman Library: Longfellow Hall: Ceiling of the Eliot Lyman Room: Ariel in Lyman pre-setup: Josh in-front of the backdrop: Manning the blog & promo station: Marketing materials ready to share! Ariel setting-up! More soon!
