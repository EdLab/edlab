When Kiddies Read to Kitties

jvazquez

March 9, 2015


The Animal Rescue League of Berks County, PA has created what could quite possibly be the most adorable literacy program ever. Book Buddies pairs kids in grades 1-8 with receptive shelter cats in a mutually beneficial arrangement: the kids get to practice their reading skills while the cats become more socialized and comfortable with human interactions.

Book Buddies is a community outreach program. In order to generate buzz, coordinators sent word to all local schools and public libraries about the special opportunity. They also posted to their well-liked Facebook page. Kids lined up to take part in the program "within days." One parent remarked, "[Kids] won’t read by themselves, but they’ll read to the cats."

What book would you read to a friendly feline? Pete the Cat? The Cat in the Hat? Or perhaps the antagonistic, If You Give a Mouse a Cookie? Let us know in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
