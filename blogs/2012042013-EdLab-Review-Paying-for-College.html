EdLab Review: Paying for College

LauraCostello

April 20, 2012

Paying for College is a site recently created by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. The project is part of a larger legislative push towards education on both sides of the loan with a tri-focus on student debt, credit cards, and mortgages. Pros: This site lets students compare the average prices of up to three schools, with good breakdown and a dash of other relevant information like default and graduation rates per school. The information is presented in a very clean and straightforward manner and the data is pretty sound and well-explained. Cons: The site is definitely in beta, features like switching from on-campus to off-campus projections don't always work. The organization of the site could use some help too, the absence of a menu or breadcrumbs of any kind make it difficult to visualize the resource as a whole and differentiate it from the other offerings on the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau page. The site is also very text heavy, it would be nice to see more data visualization. EdLab Relevance: This almost seems like a response to the NERA call for greater pre-enrollment education about student loan debt. The nice thing about the site is that it clearly associates cost with individual schools and projects a 10-year debt repayment forecast. Neither the site nor the legislation may have any affect on the trillion dollar student loan debt, but perhaps the modeled austerity will inspire further efforts to educate around these topics perhaps in conjunction with curriculums like UFR. Our Takeaway: Paying for College is a good start.
