EdLab Review: Kidspiration

Janet Jang

October 25, 2011

Kidspiration: Visual Thought Mapping Almost every elementary teacher I've met and worked with, they've raved about this product. Kidspiration is a visual learning software for students in kindergarten through 5th grade. It uses a colorful collection of symbols, integrated with reading, writing, math, and audio components, to help kids develop basic skills in reading, writing, social studies, science, and math, as well as reasoning, comprehension, and problem solving. Pros: Kidspiration offers one of the biggest libraries of symbols that I've ever seen compared to other similar types of software. This program essentially uses visual thought mapping to help children learn not only words for reading and writing but also numbers and concepts for more “abstract” subjects like math. This type of learning develops strong thinking skills in young students and builds conceptual understanding early on. It also helps students who consider themselves stronger visual learners rather than verbal learners. Cons: It is on the higher price range ($65) for educational software. Also, for a company that emphasizes visual learning, the visuals in the software are not very aesthetically pleasing and seem to be of amateur quality. They definitely utilize colors to appeal to young children, but some of the visuals in their symbols library seem very raw and unsophisticated. EdLab Relevance: Visual/TPR learning seems to be a growing trend in education technology and software. As we develop more products here in EdLab, we should keep this in mind :)
