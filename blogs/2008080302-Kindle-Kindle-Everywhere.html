Kindle, Kindle Everywhere?

Pravin Sathe

August 3, 2008

Techcrunch reported 240,000 Kindles had been sold since the hardware was introduced in November. The estimates from one analyst are upwards of 750k will be sold in the next 4 quarters and techcrunch mentions the textbook/school market as the next obvious crowd that Amazon will try and reach. Give me a platform that is DRM free (which the Kindle is not) with ala carte service of chapter/section(s) of books and more memory and I think you've got the makings of an iPod like success story - and we all know how successful that is. So when do libraries get in the action and start letting patrons check out Kindles?
