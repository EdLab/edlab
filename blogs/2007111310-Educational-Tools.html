Educational Tools

Dan Mallinger

November 13, 2007

In the complex modern world of education, it's tough to know what efforts will best suite your child's needs. There was a time when playing Mozart to your child was believed to raise her IQ, though today this lore is disregarded (though one researcher still believes jazz can depress intellectual skills). Should you use antibacterial cleaners to protect your child from disease, or will it give them asthma? Are summer tutoring sessions a great way to avoid "summer slide" or the key to your child's burnout? Is the internet a powerful set of learning tools, or a congregation of deviants? Even cupcakes are called into question. But when your child enters school, there's one tool that's sure to work. The bulletproof backpack. And no, it's not a joke.
