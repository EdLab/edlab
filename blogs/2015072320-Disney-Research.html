The Happiest Place in Science

Walt Disney Studios is home to the main branch of Disney Research. The research arm of Disney focuses on technologies like advanced computer graphics, 3D printing, robotics, and machine learning. Their research is focused on producing cutting edge technology for Disney's theme parks and cruises, but the research produced by these labs has many applications for education and game-based learning. Disney Research combines academia and industry through research, publications, and community engagement.

The research areas explored by Disney Research include video processing, human computer interaction, materials research, behavioral sciences, machine learning and optimization, wireless computing, and mobile computing. Many of their research projects and focal areas center on user experiences when watching video or playing games, though they have a capitalist goal in mind for these research activities, there's still a lot to learn about how we learn from media.

From Robots to Virtual Reality

Current projects at DIsney Research Labs include: Data Driven Procedural Landscape Modeling, Visible Light Communication, and Take or Wait? Learning Turn-Taking from Multiparty Data. One project in particular, the REVERIE research project, aims "to develop a virtual environment service for realistic inter-personal interaction." Disney researchers believe the development of virtual communication platforms will allow users to completely immerse themselves in an interaction, which will require full participation and will ultimately create a complex sensory experience.

All projects are complemented with ongoing online publications and updates accessible to the public. Disney Research has labs located throughout the United States, including Los Angeles, Pittsburgh, and Boston, along with one lab in Zurich, Switzerland.
