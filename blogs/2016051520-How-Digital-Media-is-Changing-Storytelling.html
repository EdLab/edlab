How Digital Media is Changing Storytelling

Rebecca Beeson

May 15, 2016


When telling a story through digital media, narratives can move past the traditionally linear structure of print writing and take on more layered forms. The author of a recent study focused on fiction’s evolution with technology, drawing parallels between current technology and the adaptation of voices in fiction.

The study traces the emergence of flashbacks and multiple perspectives in narrative frameworks to innovations in film and camera technology. Similarly, features of digital media like hyperlinks, audio, videos, and gifs have evolved the first-person narrative into a multimodal and even interactive experience, often fragmented and complex.

The author focuses on a digital fiction piece titled Færwhile: A Journey through a Space of Time, written to determine the effects of digital media on a written piece. The author compares her published print stories to the style and structure of Færwhile, noting that the flow of the latter was much more unnatural and segmented than the former.

She also notes that the chapters of Færwhile alternate between different perspectives by incorporating hyperlinks. In one chapter the reader can empathize with a character's side of the story in a "competitive storycrafting contest;" readers receive parts of the story based on their personal responses to a character. The author notes that this type of digital fiction writing also enables the writer to use ontological metalepsis, or character interactions between different story worlds or between the story’s world and the real world. This is often inherent in digital storytelling, because the reader is physically interacting with features like hyperlinks that allows him or her to become immersed in the on-screen text. This study suggests that we can expect parallel mechanics from our technological state to manifest in the voices of our fictional narrators. As we advance technologically, the evolution of our stories will follow.

Skains, R. L. (2015). The fragmented digital gaze: The effects of multimodal composition on narrative perspective. Qualitative Inquiry, 1–8.
