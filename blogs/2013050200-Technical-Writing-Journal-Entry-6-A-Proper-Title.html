Technical Writing Journal Entry 6: A Proper Title

Sebastian Sackey

May 2, 2013

The interesting thing about titles is that they should basically sum up a paper or a written document and give the reader a general idea of what they are about to read. Titles also capture the readers attention and arouse their interest with only a few words. The function of a title for a lab report, however, is more concerned with giving as much detail about the paper it refers to as possible. This means that a lab report's title is much longer than most would expect. It is about two to three times longer and looks more like a run on sentence than a title. It includes information about the subject, the procedure and the results of the lab. I was actually really surprised by this irony because all this time we had been learning how to keep our papers concise and straight to the point. However, the title of our paper turns out to be longer than most of the sentences we were writing before we even got into this class.
