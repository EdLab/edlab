Choosito!

Alexandra Lederman

May 18, 2015


Choosito! is an internet filtering tool meant to help classroom teachers curate content and fearlessly guide their students through web research. Choosito! filters the web and limits search results by reading level, subject area, images, Choosito! Web, or Choosito! Library in order to deliver only relevant, approved, and understandable content to students. Once a search is performed, users can click on related search topics, change the reading level or subject area, and hide certain topics (like shopping, social networks, Wikipedia, or dictionaries) from the results. Students and educators can rate, share, add results to the Choosito! Library, or remove the results from their individual search results.

Students control their own reading level which encourages them to think critically about their research methods. The app is driven by a text analysis algorithm developed through NSF-funded research. This tool is a good introduction to information literacy research practice for late elementary and early middle school students.

Pros:

Choosito! is easy to navigate and the website is intuitive. Students will be able to maneuver and operate the website easily after a teacher briefly explains information literacy and introduces the interface. When early and emerging readers were chosen as a condition, the search results were solid, age and reading level appropriate, and trustworthy. The related search topics provided by Choosito! are descriptive and allow the user to further their research in different ways. The related search topics will also influence the studentsâ€™ future searching habits and vocabulary.

Cons:

The subject areas provided by Choosito! limit the search results which may make it difficult for younger students to use and understand. Older students will be able to use the subject areas to complement their information literacy skills. The search results for fluent and advanced readers were not as strong, varied, or reciprocal to the search parameters.

Our Takeaway:

Choosito! is a great and easy way to introduce information literacy to young children. The website and app can be accessed from a desktop, tablet, or mobile phone, allowing children to practice outside of the classroom. Teachers can curate materials for a class online and spend little time learning Choosito!, allowing for more time spent on teaching children about the website and information literacy. Choosito! is great for the young researcher.
