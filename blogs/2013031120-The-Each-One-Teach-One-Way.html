The "Each One Teach One" Way

jvazquez

March 11, 2013


As the world’s citizens grow evermore connected, the need to teach kids global competence has reached a pivotal point. Nicole Silver, a 4th grade teacher in Seattle, is determined to help her students understand the world’s many different cultures. She believes that cultivating tolerance and empathy are key parts to being global citizens.

As part of her global competence curriculum, she has her students put together Powerpoint presentations that explain one particular culture’s "festival of lights." Students then compare and contrast similar celebrations within their own cultures and traditions.

Says Nicole, "We take action by teaching others... We have a certain knowledge and now it’s our responsibility to go out and share that with other people."
