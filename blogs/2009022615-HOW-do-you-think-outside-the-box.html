HOW do you think outside the box?

Arianna Choi

February 26, 2009

The European Commission has been announcing specific themes each year to increase public awareness and fuel political debates since 1983 and this year's theme is dedicated to creativity and innovation. To address the goal, Dr. Edward de Bono, who holds several professorships at universities across the globe, has been appointed as a special ambassador for the field of education. Frankly, I didn't know who he was until today and I'm excited to learn more about his work on “Serious Creativity” moving forward. (Just FYI — de Bono's Personal Website | Wiki Entry on de Bono) Putting the long list of his scholarly accomplishments aside, his theory on Lateral Thinking is very interesting. Lateral Thinking basically promotes that: a) creativity is a learned skill and b) unstructured process/method of reaching creative solutions such as brainstorming is not as effective as the techniques he prescribes. It looks like de Bono's Six Thinking Hats has won considerable popularity and is adapted by professional trainers at business organizations more so than by those in K-12 education. Hmmm…what would happen if we incorporated this exercise into curriculum design or collaborative seminars here?
