Pathways to Scientific Discovery

Marcina Zaccaria

August 13, 2015


How does a ball move to the back of a wagon? How do we chart moments that happen simultaneously? What new technologies help us to measure the speed of light?

Discussing physics and sub-atomic particles can be dizzying, but cutting edge graphics make even the most difficult ideas perceptible. No theory is explained without justification. Adam Savage reminds us that the first ideas about the Earth's shape were formed by early observations of the moon "…. every time you saw the shadow on the moon, it was circular." After looking at shadows, you could define distance, and eventually measure the earth’s circumference. These complex theories, developed over time, started with a great observation.
