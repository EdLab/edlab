Workshop on Meaning: Language & Socio-cultural Processes

Kate Meersschaert

December 8, 2011

Today Manav, Demetri & I will attend and live blog the following workshop led by Duncan Watt's, a principal research scientist at Yahoo! Research and a past professor of sociology at Columbia University: "Using the Web to do Social Science" Social science has examined how collective behavior emerges from interactions among many people, but has encountered a severe measurement problem: difficulty of observing verbal interactions between people, at scale and over time, along with observing behavior. This talk argues that internet-based research is lifting that constraint, though methodological and procedural obstacles remain; it draws on research involving, e.g., Twitter and Facebook, to help make the case.
