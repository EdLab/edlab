Textile Arts Center

EdLab Studios

May 10, 2016


"Without persona, what do you have?" asks guest instructor Joey Casey from the Textile Arts Center to a room of 20 juniors at the High School of Art and Design. Twice a week for the last semester students enrolled in the "Exploring the Arts" program have been gathering at drafting tables to examine this question. From rough sketches to finished, wearable garments; young designers were challenged to reflect a point of view in their piece and create with a client, a purpose, and most importantly, a process, in mind. Aligned with the Textile Arts Center's advocacy for the handmade, the processes introduced to the class were all variations of hand weaving as well as cold water dying. Through this and other programming for children and adults, TAC promotes accessibility and community surrounding traditional crafts. Millennia old processes reach the next generation and invite new interpretations and ingenuity; the kind that will be found walking down the runway at the culminating Brooklyn Fashion + Design Accelerator event.
