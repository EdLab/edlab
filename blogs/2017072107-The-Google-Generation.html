The Google Generation

dinosossi2

July 21, 2017


Ease can lead to lethargy, convenience can paradoxically become an inconvenience.

You can't argue too loudly against the Google search engine. What you need is provided easily and quickly.

But unfortunately, it can have negative cognitive and learning consequences.

For example a British study indicates that using Google has led to a decrease in student abilities, at least according to the administrators who deal with them.

Their concerns are what you would probably imagine.

An inability to think or learn independently.

Time management issues.

Writing difficulties.

And a strange, but not wholly unexpected problem.


An inability to remember basic facts do to a Google first mentality.

Fumbling through dictionaries or encyclopedias seem like burdens from way back in the industrial age for today's youth.

But maybe something cognitively sticks when students engage in these types of rote physical activities.

Hopefully we can eventually figure out a way to seamlessly weave the ease and convenience of immediate digital information and traditional analog learning.
