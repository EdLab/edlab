Access at Brooklyn Museum

EdLab Studios

April 24, 2016


The portrait of Washington Roebling proudly displayed in the Beaux-Arts Court is an oil on canvas, painted by classic French artist Th√©obald Chartran in 1899. On this rainy day at the Brooklyn Museum none of that matters. Once a month, people with memory loss and their caregivers are invited to the museum; open just for them, to spend time with each other and original works of art through the Brooklyn Afternoons program. Individuals with Alzheimer's, dementia, or brain trauma and their caretakers are often isolated; rarely leaving home or engaging with others. Works of art, like the Roebling portrait, generate conversation and help these individuals express themselves while living in the present moment. It's not about the dates and details, but looking at cues to tell a story and share an experience around inspiring art. Access programming opens up institutions like the Brooklyn Museum to every member of the community, taking steps towards realizing the mission of art for all no matter what.
