PeopleExplorer: Connect Researchers by Relevancy, Authority and Social Aspects

Sharon Hsiao

February 25, 2013

I'm glad to have met Shuguang Han at CSCW 2013 this year. I've actually went skiing with him twice without knowing his research work (well, not with him, with him in the same group). He developed PeopleExplorer to connect researcher to researcher by three measures. 1. Relevance: measures the content relevance between candidates' expertise (represented by candidates' publications) and the users' issued queries. 2. Authoritativeness: denotes the importance of each candidate in the coauthor networks of our dataset (i.e., the global network feature). 3. Social: indicates the social similarity between each candidate and the user in terms of how many common neighbors they shared (i.e., local network feature). This is how I imagine ResearchBroker: connecting researchers to companies by simply issuing queries (like the current site). Except, we do not use the co-author network. We apply companies' project profiles as each individual node in the network. However, the searches should be adapted to users' purposes, goals, etc., to do easy adjustments for his/her own decision making. (This is also what I've found trending at the conference - interactive viz for information finding and decision making).
