Standards and Personalization

abagigah92

November 24, 2013

Good teachers believe that their job is to teach students, not subjects. According to Sir Ken Robinson, personal learning is a great way to achieve success in academia. He describes personal learning as the ability to deal with and recognize strengths and weaknesses in different areas as an individual. On the other hand, education critics believe that it's a must to have standards that are national and international benchmarks. Standards are very important but politicians focus much of the data toward failing public school systems. This is the reason behind much of standardized testing and regulation governing public schools. Is it possible to have standards without standardized tests? The problem may not be the standards, but what we do with it. This also goes for the common core state standards. What we do with the standards we set is far more important than the standards. Setting a standard means nothing if there is no impact or programs to support it. Standardized exams are evaluations and are not meant to improve a student's ability to learn.
