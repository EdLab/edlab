EdLab Review: Switchcam

LauraCostello

November 2, 2011

Description: Switchcam is a crowdchiving site for fan-snagged concert footage. Users can switch between synced phone-camera footage to get a global perspective. Pros: The idea is pretty awesome and builds on a concert merch market sorely lacking in business model innovation. I could see the fansourcing element of Switchcam blowing up, it's a perfect participatory +1 even if you have to spend the entire show with a phone in front of your face. (Not a change for many the modern concert goer) The sync is surprisingly good. I can't figure out how it does this, but the multiple camera angles seem to resolve themselves into sync as I watch. It's like magic! Cons: The pickings are a little slim right now. To work well, this site will have to have a large usership. Switchcam is still in private beta and at this point, very few of the videos have the cool multiple perspective feature that sets the site apart from ordinary digital concert footage. The site might not appeal if you're picky about the video quality and steadiness of your view fodder. It's all cameraphone level footage at this point, though I could see partnerships of the concert DVD variety in the future. EdLab Relevance: Though Switchcam is focused on the concert experience but there are inklings that it'll expand to seminars, meetings...classrooms? Some of my 700-seater lectures from undergrad could have been enhanced by the multi-camera experience. Our Takeaway: Switchcam is a lot of fun and has a lot of market and possible classroom potential.
