The Power of Creating Autonomous Techies

Christina Terracino

September 16, 2015


A school in Pennsylvania is doing something that is both refreshing and brave—they are putting their trust entirely in the kids. They are allowing students to have power over their own education. By granting students at Penn Manor High School the freedom to use their own technology in the classroom, the school encourages students to become successful leaders and collaborators.

The principal, Dr. Philip Gale, is adamant that the students should have laptop technology in order to best prepare them for life after high school. Even though the school is responsible for the laptops, students are allowed to take them apart and then put them back together, learning about the structure and functionality of technology through this hands-on experience. This is a demonstration of the trust that these educators have put in their students by encouraging them to make mistakes and learn from them. Instead of punishing failure, it is celebrated as a necessary step of the learning process.

The school has also put students in charge of the technology help desk, another way this school promotes an equitable learning environment. By placing value on the skills of students, teachers can act as collaborators and guides instead of authority figures.
