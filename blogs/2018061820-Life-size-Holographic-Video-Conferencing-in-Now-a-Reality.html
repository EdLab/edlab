Life-size Holographic Video Conferencing in Now a Reality

Melanie.Hering

June 18, 2018


A research team at Queens University School of Computing is unveiling the TeleHuman 2, a system composed of a ring of intelligent projectors mounted above and around a retroreflective, cylindrical human-sized pod. This pod projects every image as a light field, capturing life-sized holographic videos that people can walk around, view, and interact with in real time.

While this technology is still being refined, researchers hope that someday recreating face-to-face interaction through the use of these holograms could not only revolutionize meetings with remotely located clients or coworkers, but also affect events like concerts, conferences, or other public gatherings where having speakers or performers on site is not possible.
