Leadership and Managing

Frank Obeng

April 8, 2015

Leadership is the act of directing a group of people towards achieving a goal and managing is having the supervisory control or authority to lead. One of the most talked about issues is if leadership is the same as managing and if they can be taught in business schools.

I think you can't teach a person how to lead. Nature plays a great role when it comes to leadership skills. Some leaders receive their skills from their society and their environment. Others hone their leadership skills through their work and achievements. This type of leader does so from experience. Some might argue that managing can be taught, as you need education to be able to exercise supervisory control and authority. Do you think there is a difference between leadership and managing?
