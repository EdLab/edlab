Harvard, Indian Politics, and Academic Freedom

Skanda Amarnath

August 1, 2011

Inside Higher Ed has a piece on the controversy at Harvard over a Indian politician, Subramanian Swamy, who was a former economics professor that now teaches for the summer. The controversy revolves around an op-ed he wrote in an Indian magazine DNA (Daily News & Analysis) on how India should respond to the recent Mumbai terrorist attacks. His comments predictably and understandably offended Indian Muslims, as he claimed their right to vote ought to be taken away until they admit to their Hindu ancestry. Needless to say, his views remain outside the mainstream and have upset many Harvard academics and students, who have called on Harvard to remove him. This gets to the question of what freedom of speech protections ought to be in place for professors and intellectuals at the university-level. On the one hand, Professor Swamy seems to be spreading intolerance, and yet to remove him would imply squashing his freedom of expression.
