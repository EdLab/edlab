How NOT to Create an Enhanced Podcast

Kate Meersschaert

October 12, 2011

Private! Hi Team Edlab, Over the last few weeks I have intermittently endeavored to create an enhanced podcast from my interview with Jennifer Openshaw, CEO of SuperFutures.org. The goal was to use Power Point to create a "movie" with correspondent visuals and use the audio from the interview as the soundtrack, then uploading the whole kit & caboodle as a vialogue to then embed in a correspondent blog piece on the future of college prep/guidance. Seems simple enough... right? Here is what not to do (followed by suggestions for a better approach!): 1) Don't assume that an HD flip video camera, though adequate for internally viewed video capture, is appropriate quality for non-EdLab human consumption. 2) Don't take pictures while you film a video, lest you want a "press corps" effect, which could be desirable some of the time. (George you did a great job - my direction was off). 3) DO NOT under ANY circumstance assume that creating a Power Point on a MAC with very specific timed animations will transfer well as a .MOV file. Microsoft, somehow knew that us MAC users would want to do this and did not make it an easy task. I am just saying... transitions fine, animation timing NOT FINE! Ok now for the positive, potentially easier path to Power Point/Vialogues bliss: 1) When capturing audio from an interview be sure to borrow the Editoral audio recorder from the video team. Test this multiple times for the audio levels etc & be sure it is on before starting your interview. 2) Yes, do use Audacity (Thanks Melanie!) to edit these audio files... super easy to use and thanks to Zhou's future mSchool course you too can be an audio/Audacity expert! 3) Be sure to select only images from Google images/Flickr/The Commons that are labeled for reuse. Also, be sure to download the highest quality version of the file. 4) When using Power Point to convert your presentation into a movie here are a few tricks for getting the audio just so... -To develop your animations/transitions with the audio select "play across slides" & not "automatically" from the audio menu. -If the player icon doesn't "hide" when you are finished and ready to upload change the transparency of the slide and it will disappear (Thanks Jo!). -When you are ready to save as a .MOV file or .WMV (more on this later) file select from the movie options pop-up menu the soundtrack drop-down menu and add your audio file to the presentation here (thanks Fred!). 5) Now for the .WMV vs .MOV file issue... if you created a presentation on a MAC without, I repeat without animations proceed to save your file as a .MOV file. If you are an avid PPT animator, stop, transfer your files to a PC and save the whole shebang as a .WMV file and then upload to Vialogues (thanks Pranav for discovering this!!!). A .WMV file type is not listed on the vialogues A-OK list... but not to worry it is an accepted format. 6) Vialogues somehow manages to make everything look better! Bravo Vialogues team! Here is the final product on Vialogues & correspondent EdLab blog post. Ok, there you go! Hopefully, you, unlike me, will have an expedited first enhanced podcast creation experience! Go forth and Power Point & feel free to ask me for any additional tips... it does as I learned the hard way, take a village to create a vialogue.
