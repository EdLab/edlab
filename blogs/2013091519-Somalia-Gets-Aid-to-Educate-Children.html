Somalia Gets Aid to Educate Children

Bismark Appiah

September 15, 2013

Peace now reigns in Somalia after two decades of war. The recent end of the civil war symbolized the beginning of a new chapter in the country's history and hopefully a peaceful one for its citizens. To provide a better life and a prosperous future for its citizens, especially the children, Somalia has started a three-year,$117 million, basic education program, which will receive financial support from Unicef, Unesco, the U.N. World Food Program and a consortium of international world agencies. Upon all the countries in the world, Somalia has one of the lowest primary school enrollment rates. According to Unicef, 4.4 million children are still out of school after the civil war ended. Thus, the purpose of this program is to provide the best education possible, starting with 6-13 year olds.
