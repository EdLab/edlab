SuperModels: Disruptive Business Models?

Skanda Amarnath

December 9, 2011

Michael Horn, co-founder of Innosight Institute, a non-profit think tank devoted to applying the theories of disruptive innovation to problems in the social sector, gave a talk at TEDxSF. Hearing 'disruptive', 'innovation', and 'social sector'...it was tailor-made for EdLab. The whole talk is interesting but one of the key points he brings up is the need for a re-thinking of the 'business model' for higher education. While his diagnosis of the current problems in the economy as one of skills match is fundamentally flawed, he is correct in citing the roles higher learning institutions have historically fulfilled in increasing access to higher education. Yet both the long-term and short-term trends have been pushing students out of higher education as a result of higher costs and increased selection. It is absolutely clear to me that these trends are here to stay and there needs to be a way to disrupt the zero-sum game that comes with attempting to gain access to higher education. The whole talk is excellent, and while I think he underrates just how big a role credentialization plays, he gets the big point that higher education functions as too many things at this time
