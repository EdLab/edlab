Research Digest: How Millennials Search

LauraCostello

January 19, 2012

Porter, B. (2011). Millennial Undergraduate Research Strategies in Web and Library Information Retrieval Systems. Journal of Web Librarianship, 5, 267-285. Many articles on the topic of Millennial search strategies quickly devolve into talk about reforming these methods through information literacy training. While it is true that the search strategy of born-digital users is shallower than that of a p-book trained researcher and single search bars dominate the future landscape of many databases, Millennials are working with a deeper pool and reaching out to these users requires an understanding of the environment in which these behaviors developed. The author studied the library and non-library digital search strategies of undergraduate users at SUNY Oswego. She found that many younger users prefer a horizontal search strategy to one that is strictly hierarchical. Users flit from resource to resource, piecing together an idea of their quarry instead of spending up-front time researching the best resources and how to obtain them. Students were frustrated by systems that rejected the natural language they preferred to use, but quickly reformatted search strings that were not returning desired results. Students also often found their way to reputable and library resources, but their search strategy began with high-list Google results and Wikipedia. The study conclusion emphasizes evaluation of web resources and development of Millennial-friendly library research platforms over re-training in search strategy. EdLab Relevance: Millennium's not serving Millennials! Google's abandonment of advanced search is a harbinger of search strategy change. It's more important than ever for libraries to put quality content in front of users in a way they understand. Dulcinea Media is a good example of this.
