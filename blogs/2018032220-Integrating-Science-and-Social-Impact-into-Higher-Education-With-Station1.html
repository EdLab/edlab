Integrating Science and Social Impact into Higher Education With Station1

Ryan.Allen

March 22, 2018


"A model of higher education based on inclusion and equity, learning through frontier project-based inquiry and research, and the integration of science and technology with societal perspective and impact." (Station1)

The Future of Higher Education

Station1 is a Boston-based non-profit learning institution that focuses on a higher education model of self-directed learning and technological project-based pedagogy. The organizationâ€™s founders have impressive backgrounds in edtech: Dr. Christine Ortiz, a former dean at MIT, Dr. Ellan Spero, Ph.D., MIT, and Elliot Mandel, M.Ed., Harvard University.

Drawing upon their experiences and networks, the founders have built Station1 on a foundation of scientific inquiry, social impact, and diverse inclusion. Right now, the organization links with university and business partners for educational programming. However, the nonprofit is transitioning to become a fully accredited degree-granting institution and soon plans to launch in other cities around the world.

Summer@Station1

The organization is gearing up for its Summer@Station1, a summer residential education program for undergraduate students interested in the intersections of technology and society. Students will be exposed to new technologies and innovations, such as VR and AR, 3D printing, and blended learning environments. This is a paid opportunity that provides participants with summer housing, a stipend for living expenses, and learning materials.

Summer@Station1 will also connect all of its students with startups, larger companies, or research institutes from around the Boston area for part-time internships. Additionally, the students will be paired with mentors who will provide guidance well beyond the summer sessions, helping them to navigate early careers in edtech.
