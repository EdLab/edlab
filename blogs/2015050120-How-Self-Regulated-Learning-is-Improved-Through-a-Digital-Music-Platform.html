How Self-Regulated Learning is Improved Through a Digital Music Platform

Brian.Sweeting

May 1, 2015


Being a good musician doesn't just happen. In addition to logging countless practice hours to achieve musical excellence, a good music student needs to develop self-regulated learning skills. The Royal Conservatory of Music in Toronto knows that the most successful students are motivated, determined, independent, and reflective.

That's why they've built an online digital music learning platform for students to strengthen these skills. Through the iScore platform students are guided through the steps of planning, doing, and reflecting, "a process for thinking about what a person wants to achieve and how they want to get there."

Providing a digital space for students to record, watch, and reflect on their performances should encourage them to be more deliberate and thoughtful in their practice time. It's also easy to imagine that once developed, these skills might translate well to other subject areas.
