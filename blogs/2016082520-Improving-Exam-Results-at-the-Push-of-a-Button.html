Improving Exam Results at the Push of a Button

dinosossi2

August 25, 2016


The promotion of innovation and technical skills to achieve twenty-first century learning outcomes pushes educators to make their lessons more interactive than ever before. With cheap mobile devices, incorporating technology to foster interactivity has never been easier. A recent study examined if audience response system (ARS) technology, more commonly known as clickers, improved academic performance in higher ed.

A group of 119 students who used clickers were compared against a larger control group during a Principles of Economics class at a Spanish university. The study focused on whether clicker use increased the likelihood that students would pass their final economics exam involving theoretical and mathematical sections.

The results show that ARS use increases student performance in the theoretical portion of the exam but not the mathematical. The study also suggests that ARSs should be used frequently and regularly to improve student outcomes. Less frequent, more scattered use would not appear to help student performance.

This study is important because clickers are becoming less expensive and more apps could be developed to enable smartphones to engage in similarly interactive classroom activities. The study also provides preliminary, but not definitive, clues in using these devices effectively to improve exam performance. As such, you may want to hesitate using clickers until more research is conducted. However, you also may want to check your device’s app store in the future for inexpensive tech that may improve your teaching. One day, a click may do the trick in making exam scores soar.

Castillo-Manzano, J. I., Castro-Nuño, M., Sanz Díaz, M. T., & Yñiguez, R. (2016). Does pressing a button make it easier to pass an exam? Evaluating the effectiveness of interactive technologies in higher education. British Journal of Educational Technology, 47(4), 710–720. doi:10.1111/bjet.12258
