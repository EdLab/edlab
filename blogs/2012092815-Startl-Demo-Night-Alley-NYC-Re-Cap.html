Startl Demo Night @ Alley NYC - Re-Cap!

Kate Meersschaert

September 28, 2012

Entryway... This particular demo night was unique in that Startl, the hosts were re-entering the edtech demo night world after a five month hiatus. The event was comprised of standard 5-10 minute demos with a few minutes for audience Q&A after each one. The following companies demo'd: Citia DecisionDesk The Skimm Torsh (EdLab Seminar/Tech Talk Alum!) Future Project The stand-out applications/concepts for me were Decision Desk and Future Project. Decision Desk is a unique, proprietary portfolio-based admissions automation platform. As more and more programs move to a "portfolio" or "project"-based applications system this seems like an idea and platform to watch. Future project was refreshingly focused on both project-based learning and creating a documentation framework for mentorship. Google recently partnered with the project to "adopt" a local school. Not overly tech-heavy, but a potentially life-changing program. Courtney Williams from Torsh gets major points for recovering from a technology fail! He was graceful and clearly won-over the crowd! One audience member noted, "happens to everyone!" It does & is all in the recovery! Additional Notes: People in attendance were definitely disappointed by the pizza & soda. Feedback I over-heard was that they would prefer alcohol and no food. Excited to see the Seen in NY for NLT that will hopefully come-out of the video team's valiant filming efforts! The environment can be a bit hectic! Way to go & persevere Diana & Jennifer! The opportunity to "stand-up" and make an announcement at the end of the evening was a great idea and positive way to engage the edtech community. Finally, great idea re: timed demos... clap the demo'er offstage once their time is up, regardless of whether or not they are finished. Positive way to "keep time!" The group...
