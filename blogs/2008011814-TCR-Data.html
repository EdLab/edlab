TCR Data

Dan Mallinger

January 18, 2008

Just a heads up: TCR will now store some additional data. Every time a user views an article, the site logs the user, the article, the time, and whether or not the user had permission to view the content. I hope this will give us some much needed data to drive our vision of TCR 2.0. I'd like to see these series of decisions be motivated by careful analysis of user behavior. Unfortunately, I have little or no knowledge of how market research firms go about this process. So I'll be taking a shot at the dark. The goal of the current data collection is to see if users can be broken up into psychographic profiles based on readership. Here, I'll be looking to see if we can define meta-categories of readership which encompass one or more of the 30+ TCR article "topics." For example, if we have 30+ topics but readers generally fall into three or four meta-categories (e.g. a "Social Context, Curriculum, and Diversity readership," an "Adult Ed, Administration, and Technology readership," etc.), then this may motivate us to alter the UI accordingly. In short, I'm looking to gather data which will suggest how to design TCR such that the information immediately presented to the user will engage the largest percentage of our potential customers. If you have any ideas or relevant knowledge, I would love to hear from you. Any thoughts?
