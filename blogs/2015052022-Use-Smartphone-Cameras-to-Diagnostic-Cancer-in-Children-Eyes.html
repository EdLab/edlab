Use Smartphone Cameras to Diagnostic Cancer in Children Eyes

Kafoumba Doumbia

May 20, 2015

Technology is almost capable of making anything possible nowadays. Through technology, we can diagnose cancer in children under the age of five by using smartphone cameras. The flash from a smartphone camera can find the retinoblastoma, a dangerous form of cancer that generally affects children. A British non-profit that focuses on helping children in need , made this discovery after numerous attempts. This is a prime example of the need for increased STEM instruction in K-12 classrooms. Students are using and are comfortable with tools (i.e. smartphones) that they can use to make real impact in our lives. STEM education is at the heart of technological innovation and would help us better our future and cure many diseases, and find solutions to a wide range of issues.
