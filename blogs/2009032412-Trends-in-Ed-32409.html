Trends in Ed, 3.24.09

Arianna Choi

March 24, 2009

Locaccino A lovely name with creepy power. But it's only creepy if someone you don't want has the power to locate you anytime anywhere. Locaccino is a user-controllable, real-time, location-sharing tool which allows you to define the contacts, times, and regions you want. Here's an example of how it is being used on campus: Fooducation Supported by the Gov't In January 2008, the Secretary for State for Children, Schools and Families [of UK], Ed Balls announced that cookery lessons would be made compulsory for 11-14 year olds. He commented: “Leaving school being able to cook healthy dishes from scratch is an essential everyday skill all young people should have.” As a result, cooking is well and truly back on the curriculum. Phil's Cooking Academy features a series of recipes and podcasts that kids can cook-along either in class or at home. Kids are also invited to submit their own recipes and Phil has committed to visit the creative young cooks' schools.
