Unequal Education

Bismark Appiah

May 27, 2014

Educational inequality usually revolves gender. However, racial inequality is also another form of injustice prevalent in education circles. The video below offers interesting views from students and educators on racial inequality in many South African schools. Even though apartheid ended nearly two decades ago, many blacks still experience racial inequality in so far as education is concerned. Their schools lack proper equipment and are largely ignored by the government. However, their biggest grievance seems to concern the lack of qualified teachers. Some have even called for the government to recruit teachers from other countries.

Education is supposed to empower students and serve as the driving force behind a thriving democracy. What is the incentive for black South Africans to attend school when the educators are not deemed as qualified?
