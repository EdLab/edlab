Mini Satellites are the Future of Deep Space Exploration

Melanie.Hering

June 25, 2018


NASA’s launch of the InSight Mars Lander this May brings exciting new prospects for space exploration and scientific discovery on the red planet. When thinking of NASA, most people envision the organization’s iconic spacecraft, giant machines that are responsible for collecting important data. However, one of their latest creations for this Mars mission proves that downsizing also has its advantages.

Along with InSight, NASA launched twin spacecraft called Mars Cube One. These satellites are no larger than briefcases and will be expelled into deep space to test new miniaturized technologies that could improve communication and navigation for future missions. If they make it to Mars, they will be able to transmit data about InSight within minutes as it enters the Martian atmosphere and lands.
