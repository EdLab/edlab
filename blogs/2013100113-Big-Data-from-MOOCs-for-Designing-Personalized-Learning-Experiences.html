Big Data from MOOCs for Designing Personalized Learning Experiences

chingfu

October 1, 2013

I ran into this short blog post, MOOCs plus big data, by Dr. Norma Ming on the untapped potentials of MOOCs. One thing we noticed so far is that MOOCs have revolutionized access for many learners. But the potential of big data collected from MOOCs hasn't been fully explored yet. As she wrote: "The opportunity to study such large numbers of students across such disparate contexts is rare in traditional academic settings, and it permits discoveries of learning trajectories and error patterns that might otherwise get missed as noise amidst smaller samples." The results of such large-scale analysis would contribute to the design of truly personalized and adaptive learning experiences. I didn't pay attention to this aspect before and think it's a great point.
