How can you get fired from a company you started?

bhughes

February 22, 2009

(OK, I'm comming late to the Steve Jobs Lovefest, so I'm going to reblog it... Good find Pranav.) Steve Jobs was 29-years-old when the first Macintosh came out (he had started working on it ~9 years earlier). In this commencement address, he tells three stories "from his life," 1) about "connecting the dots" (and not attending college), 2) about "love and loss" (and getting fired from Apple), and 3) about "death" (and getting diagnosed with cancer). There are a lot of great parts, but I just wanted to point out on the whole how we should continue to strive to make EdLab the kind of place that embodies his message. (Saw this again while trying to figure out what makes Stanford U's YouTube Channel tick. Playlists and the "Director" status seem to be important...)
