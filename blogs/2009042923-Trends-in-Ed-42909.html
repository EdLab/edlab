Trends in Ed, 4.29.09

jvazquez

April 29, 2009

mHealth is a new term that describes the use of mobile technology for medical and public health practice. These technologies are especially useful in areas with limited internet connectivity. An example of an mHealth tool is EpiSurveyor, which was used to track and contain a recent polio outbreak in Kenya. It allows users to quickly gather and assess data and create forms, view records, and share data with other people. The World Health Organization made EpiSurveyor the data collection tool of sub-Saharan Africa.
