Are We Becoming Information Addicts?

Christina Terracino

August 19, 2015


The internet has quickly become a significant part of our daily lives. We are spending more and more time looking at glowing screens to connect with each other and to learn about what is going on in the world. But what effect does this have on our bodies, both physically and mentally? What about the influence it has on child development?

Perhaps what is most disconcerting is the way that the brainâ€™s filtering method changes. Rather than favoring important information, we instead become fixated on the newest information. Finding the latest tidbit for our brains to take in provides us with bursts of endorphins. We are always looking for that next rush. This has, in essence, made our relationship with technology comparable to a drug addiction. We have grown dependent on having access to the internet wherever and whenever we want, and turn to it for a quick fix when the flow of information seems to halt. Just like any drug, this lifestyle has its affects on the brain.
