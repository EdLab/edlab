D&R Digital Archives Exploration

Alexandra Lederman

March 31, 2015

*private* Why Explore Digital Archives? Digital archives have two goals: to preserve digital content and making it searchable. Some digital archives are better at addressing only a single goal, what makes a good digital archive? Activity 1: Digital Archive Speed Dating 1. PocketKnowledge 2. Harvard Library Digital Collections 3. NYPL Digital Collections 4. Digital Public Library of America 5. Wilson Center Digital Archive Activity 1: Questions 1. What do you like about this site? What don't you like? Does the content match the format? 2. Perform some searches, does the site work as expected? 3. Is this a good website? A good digital archive? Activity 2: Look at Some Other Sites 1. Vialogues 2. YouTube 3. Flickr 4. Internet Archive 5. Google Books Activity 1: Questions 1. How is the feature media type represented on the site? How is it preserved, made findable and featured? 2. Are there any takeaways from the site we could use to improve the representation of this media type on PK? Discussion Are there any standout features of either the digital archives of the other sites that we should about adding to PK? What makes a good digital archive? Are any of these sites fulfilling both goals of archival websites?
