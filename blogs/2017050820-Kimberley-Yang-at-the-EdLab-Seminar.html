Kimberley Yang at the EdLab Seminar

EdLab Studios

May 8, 2017


Kimberley Yang is a psychotherapist based in Palm Springs, California where she meets with patients who were seeking bariatric surgery to lose weight through surgical intervention. In March of 2017 she spoke to EdLab about the low academic and low career outcomes associated with morbid obesity.

Watch the full seminar on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion

@15:35 ZKL: Impact of shame on decision making and health.

@46:16 dinosossi2 I wonder if the proportion of ACES is any higher than it was in the past? Perhaps a bigger problem is the dissolution of societal institutions (e.g., schools, community centers, social services), especially post-2008 recession. Further, the rise of social media and negative political and social discursive spaces have seemed to make society more cynical and less generous.

@52:35 Kimberley.Yang It's the totality of the combination of childhood maltreatment, societal changes in family structure, the mass use of extremely bad street drugs and prescribed opioids, easy and cheap access to processed food and a more sedentary lifestyle.
