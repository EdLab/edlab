Metaphors for Organizing Learning in the mSchool

gnatriello

May 25, 2012

Keep Private A key defining feature of the mSchool is the ability of the platform to support a wide variety of metaphors to organize learning and teaching. As part of the development process, we are generating a list a metaphors with the potential to serve as powerful learning organizers. This means that they suggest coherent ways of organizing materials, experiences, and roles to generate learning experiences. We need everyone's input to generate a list of metaphors. To get things started, I have developed the following initial list: atelier archive camp Chautaugua assembly charette competition concert debate demonstration dialogue discussion drill exhibit journey laboratory lecture library mistake project reflection story seminar simulation study group teach-in theatre workshop Please suggest additional metaphors that we might want to consider. You can draw on things you have heard about, or better yet, powerful learning experiences in your past. One source of additional metaphors might be learning experiences that are common in cultures other than the US.
