Can Disruptive Innovations Really Save Our Universities?

Laura Scheiber

October 28, 2012


Our country is currently facing a curious paradox, according to Michael Horn, the co-founder of Innosight Institute. Though we are experiencing record levels of unemployment, there are 3,000,000 job openings. Worse yet, employers complain that they canâ€™t find individuals with the proper training and necessary skills to fill these jobs. Historically, we have turned to postsecondary institutions to educate and prepare our work force for the labor market. But in this particular moment in history, Michael believes that, in many cases, universities are actually deterring potential students from getting the necessary education to excel in the job market.

But all is not lost. Horn believes that disruptive innovations in higher education are currently happening that can transform this traditionally expensive system to a more accessible student-centered one in which learning is the constant standard of measurement instead of time.
