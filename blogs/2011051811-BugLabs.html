BugLabs

Ajish George

May 18, 2011

Like Arduino, BugLabs provides hardware modules and an integrated programming environment to rapidly prototype new device functionalities. "BUG is a collection of easy-to-use, open source hardware modules, each capable of producing one or more Web services. These modules snap together physically and the services connect together logically to enable users to easily build, program and share innovative devices and applications. With BUG, we don't define the final products - you do." BugTunes City College, New York, NY Dan Steingart uses BUG in the CabLab project, in which cabs act as mobile sensory networks. Emily Carr University Vancouver, British Columbia Jer Thorp is building artwork that will be displayed on site at the 2010 Olympics in February. Code.lab investigates surveillance with small, portable, smart nodes, combining a CPU with a camera, mesh network, GPS, and other senso What would you make with a Bug and a day?
