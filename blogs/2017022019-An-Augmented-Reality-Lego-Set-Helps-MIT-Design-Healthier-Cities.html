An Augmented Reality Lego Set Helps MIT Design Healthier Cities

saraehardman

February 20, 2017


MIT Media Lab is known for technology and design innovation, but what is the secret to their success? To the people who work there, it’s all about collaboration. When designers team up with engineers and computer scientists, the results can be greater than what any group produces on their own. Take, for instance, the augmented reality Lego set created in order to visualize urban design.

This construction used augmented reality to display data visualization as a moving process, keeping track of statistics like population density and transportation patterns. This information allowed the team of urban designers to come up with ways to decrease the use of private cars in favor of healthier and more environmentally-friendly options. The team’s goal is to use the augmented reality Lego cityscape to analyze the impact of design on wellness. As they say in the vialogue, this couldn’t have been done without collaboration.
