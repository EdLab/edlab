Graph search? That's so 2006.

bhughes

January 16, 2013

For anyone at EdLab in 2006, the big "graph search" announcement from Facebook probably brought to mind memories of EdLab's first app, PocketKnowledge. Back in 2005, when the PocketKnowledge project began, the online world looked pretty different... there were no "app stores" to speak of, and Facebook had just emerged from its Boston/Ivy birthplace. A skeptic today might pull up PocketKnowledge and scoff -- sure, it looks a bit dated, the UX is lacking polish, and it doesn't work like other institutional repositories -- but they would be missing the gem at its core: graph search! Not only did the small EdLab team succeed at envisioning a powerful kind of graph search in 2005, but we launched it nine months later in mid-2006! So, join with me in scoffing a bit at Facebook's latest accomplishment... sure, I'll use it (I can't help myself, Anne!), but only with a increased sense of pride in PocketKnowledge: - Providing such a vast organizational possibilities that it can't display page numbers... due to its graph database. - Having no traditional index or "Table of Contents" guide to the archive... due to its graph database. - Having a GUI so confusing that it's hard to browse for specific content without hands-on training... due to its graph database. - Being a "cloud" solution for storing your intellectual property... before the sky was clouded over! So, do you have ideas for improving PocketKnowledge and once again leading the world in graph search capabilities? I bet you do! (Let's wait another six years... and do it in 2018!)
