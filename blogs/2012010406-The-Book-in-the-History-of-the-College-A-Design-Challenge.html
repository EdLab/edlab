The Book in the History of the College: A Design Challenge

gnatriello

January 4, 2012

As this piece from the Chronicle suggests, the educational potential of books continues unabated in a world now filled with electronic alternatives. In fact, as modern reading options grow more diverse, the traditional print book may take on a new mystique. With this in mind I would like to issue a design challenge to the EdLab. As we prepare to celebrate the 125th Anniversary of Teachers College, we are looking for ways to celebrate the role of the library at the College. This could be an opportunity to design something that features the important role of books and libraries in education in general and at the College in particular. Let me be clear that I am not talking about something like a set of books in glass cases or anything we might have seen somewhere else. How can we kick things up a notch and make a contemporary impact while telling an important historical story? A few ideas to get things started: 1) Build a book holodeck in the 6th floor tower room 2) Create a scrolling wall of books used in TC courses by drawing on a decade's worth of docdel/e-reserve data 3) Assemble a book maze that allows people to walk through books important in the history of the College (even better if it involves riding through on golf carts) None of these may be quite right, but you get the idea.
