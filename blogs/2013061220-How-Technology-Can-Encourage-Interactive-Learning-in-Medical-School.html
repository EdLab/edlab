How Technology Can Encourage Interactive Learning in Medical School

Laura Scheiber

June 12, 2013


The last time medical schools in the United States seriously embraced educational reform was at the turn of the 20th century. Since then, we have discovered a tremendous amount about how people learn. Despite this knowledge, medical schools have been slow to change.

Stanford Medical School is looking to transform its classes by incorporating technology into its program to better meet the needs of todayâ€™s medical students. This video shows what flipping the medical school classroom looks like. It highlights how technology can foster collaborative interactive learning, a big departure from the medical school lecture models that dominated the 20th century.
