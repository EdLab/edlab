Analyzing Vialogues Discussions

xk2120

August 9, 2017

My current project is to analyze discussions on Vialogues. There are several directions that the research project could lead. I’m coding the conversations with a focus on fundamental theoretical frameworks.

There are multiple coding systems in previous research studies that serves different purposes from various perspectives. I would like to observe the knowledge construction process in collaborative learning through the developmental psychological perspective. In social constructivism, there is the knowledge constructed through social interactions in the context of history and culture (Vygotsky, 1930/1978). Using social constructivism as the framework, I would like to implement Bales' interaction process analysis for the first discussion analysis prototype. 

Bales (1950) developed an analytical system with 12 types of interactions categorized with four dimensions: emotion (positive, neutral, negative), interaction categories (show solidarity, tension release or agreement; give suggestion, opinion or orientation; ask for orientation, opinion or suggestion; show disagreement, tension or antagonism), reaction types (positive or negative reaction; question; answer), and phrases in problem solving (communication, evaluation, control, decision, tension reduction and reintegration). He also observed and calculated the frequencies of each type of interactions and concluded that people were more voluntary to give opinions, suggestions, and information than asking (Bales & Borgatta, 1955). Bales’ interaction process analytical system was applied to understand the conversational interactions during the family discussion, psychological therapy interaction, and children gaming activities, among others. 

I have a concern that Bales' analytical system is more focus on face to face discussion while Vialogues is an online video discussion platform. Bales' system might not capture all the features in the online discussion. Therefore, I am trying to extend Bales' coding system with new representations emphasized on the computer-support collaborative learning forum features, and implement the new coding system into the algorithms for natural language processing. I will keep working on this new coding system, and welcome anyone to discuss the ideas with me. 


References 

Bales, R. F. (1950). Interaction process analysis; a method for the study of small groups.

Bales, R. F., & Borgatta, E. F. (1955). Size of group as a factor in the interaction profile. Small groups: Studies in social interaction, 396-413.

Vygotsky, L. (1930/1978). Mind and society: The development of higher psychological processes. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press.
