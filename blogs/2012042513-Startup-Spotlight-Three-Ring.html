Startup Spotlight: Three Ring

Kate Meersschaert

April 25, 2012

The Details & Back-story: Three Ring, founded by three friends, one of which was an LA-area teacher, offers smartphone and tablet-friendly applications that allow educators to easily capture and organize traditional paper-based student work. Three Ring goes beyond Google Docs to offer image, video and sound-capture capabilities that empower educators to collect student artifacts and share this material easily on its dedicated platform. Educator Diane Horvath describes her interest in Three Ring on her Tumblr "the techMonstah": "I'm going to give this one a tryâ€¦ I've been looking for an easy solution in keeping track and organizing work samples from my students." Three Ring just won the 2012 New York Tech Day Award for best Educational Startup. Unique Point of Difference: A dedicated, education-focused online hub paired with multiple capture points allows teachers and students to interact with and categorize material in a variety of new ways and clearly differentiates this platform from DropBox or Google docs. EdLab Relevance What role could image capture applications play in EdLab projects like PocketKnowledge? We are exploring ways to organize archival material, and I wonder how our focus would shift or grow if we also added multimedia capture capabilities to our roster of current and future applications? Our Takeaway: Three Ring is a simple, powerful way to collect, organize, and store a variety of student artifacts that were not originally created in a digital format.
