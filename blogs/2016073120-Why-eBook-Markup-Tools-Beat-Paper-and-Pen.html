Why eBook Markup Tools Beat Paper and Pen

Jenny Shen

July 31, 2016


You may remember being asked in English class to highlight and write notes in the margins as you read through a novel. With the adoption of ebooks, such note taking practices are evolving. The authors of this study asked whether and when students use markup tools in e-textbooks, and whether that correlates at all to their course grades.

Students from eight different courses were given an e-textbook through a course management system. (The authors noted this was a new practice for the instructors: only one of the eight had used e-textbooks and required the use of markup tools.) The text vendor provided data on studentsâ€™ behavior while reading the text, such as whether each page was read (counted when student was on a page for at least 10 seconds) and what types of markups each student made, including adding highlights, notes after a text, annotations in the text, bookmarks, and questions.

The authors found that when students used the mark-up tools in the e-textbook, they also tended to access the book earlier in the course and more frequently throughout. When students used the bookmarking tool more and clocked in more hours of reading, their grades were higher. This might indicate an effective studying strategy for students using e-textbooks. However, the authors also note that students may need to be more formally trained on how to use these tools in order to fully see how they can benefit them throughout the course.

Van Horne, S., Russell, J., & Schuh, K. L. (2016). The adoption of mark-up tools in an interactive e-textbook reader. Educational Technology Research & Development, 64(3), 407-433.
