What Is An E-book Without An E-Reader?

Brian.Sweeting

June 17, 2015


The thrill of opening something new, the promise of unread adventure, and the crispness of freshly bound print: these are the sensations that used to reward kids who flipped the pages of a new book. These are also privileges that weren't afforded to students who lived far from bookstores or were priced out of catalogs like the Scholastic Reading Club.

In an effort to put more books in the hands of more kids, the most popular publishers will make $250 million worth of children's literature freely available through a New York Public Library mobile app. Now kids who already have smart phones and tablets may miss out on the joys of unwrapping a new book order, but will have far greater access than they enjoyed before. Unfortunately for kids without reading devices, ebooks will be just as elusive as a pricey book club or a trip to the mall.
