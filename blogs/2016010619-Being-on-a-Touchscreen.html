Being on a "Touchscreen"

Sarah van den Berg

January 6, 2016


Slam poet Marshall Jones presents his poem "Touchscreen" at Common Sense Media - MTV Town Hall. Uploaded to Vialogues for a discussion in conjunction with a conference on digital literacy, the powerful poem has provoked strong thoughts on how technology mediates our humanity.

The repetition of the line "Doesn’t it feel good to touch" reinforces the way in which technology has transformed our interaction with texts and each other. Jones’s poem provides relevant material for educators thinking about incorporating digital literacy into their professional practice, but they might also consider how to enact a pedagogy responsive to this touchscreen culture of their students. What are the implications of a touchscreen world for socio-emotional learning? How might humanities respond to a world in which "I face Facebook more than books face me" and "voices are only read and we are desperate to feel that we hope our techno-logic can reverse the universe until the screens touch us back...and one day it will, when our technology is advanced enough to make us human again"?

How can teaching respond to the changes technology brings to our modes of interaction with people and information? Tell us your thoughts on digitally responsive teaching at the discussion on Vialogues.
