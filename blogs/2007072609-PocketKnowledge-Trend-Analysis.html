PocketKnowledge Trend Analysis

Anthony Cocciolo

July 26, 2007

I am currently in the process of writing a paper for presentation at the Association of Internet Researchers this October. The paper is titled “Does Web 2.0 Matter? Investigating How Learning Environment Design Affects User Community Engagement” and the system being investigated is PocketKnowledge. Since today's seminar is about the future of PK, I thought I would discuss some interesting things I have found while analyzing the data. One interesting thing is how PK use has changed since I first analyzed the data back in November 2006. One interesting trend is the increased prominence of doctoral students and faculty in comparison to masters students in the role of key actors within the system (see chart below). One point I had made in an earlier paper is that Web 2.0 systems, because of the low barriers to entry, provide the opportunity for relative novices to move up into more key roles (think here of the renegade blogger who steals the limelight from established media outlets). It would seem; however, that PK may be evolving more into an expert system than it was when first launched. Although there are many masters students who are key actors, the trend seems to indicate that they are not keeping up with faculty and doctoral students.
