What Are Your Top 5 Edtech Trends for 2013?

Kate Meersschaert

December 18, 2012

Here are "Edupunk" blogger, Audrey Waters educational technology predictions for 2013 - what are yours?! Here are my top 5 (in honor of NLT's High 5! Also, here is my recent post on the top 7 tech trends from an Inc.com writer - interesting to compare & contrast the VC/EdTech focus! My Top 5 #Edtech Trends for 2013: 1) Personalization and "curation" will continue to rule content delivery and discovery... as information continues to multiply, educators and learners will need clearer recommendations re: courses, course material and resources. 2) More schools will implement eReaders like Nook & Kindle (tablets will still play an increasing role... but this isn't news!). 3) School violence & gun laws will sadly inform much more than safety policies and seep-into pedagogy (lesson content, discourse etc.). 4) More and more schools will embrace and integrate BYOD (bring your own device) into the curriculum and culture. Smartphones/hybrid tablets (iPad Mini etc.) will be used for polling, attendance...Tweeting your assignment. 5) Entrepreneurship will be taught even earlier... and earlier... as founders become younger and younger. Digital natives...go native! 5.1) The MOOC craze will continue to grow and spawn an anti-MOOC movement! More in-person learning opportunities that mimic the apprenticeship model will be integrated into a "buffet-style" learning approach (best-of online, in-person etc.). Tacit knowledge cannot be taught online... 5.2) Sensor-based technologies will increasingly butt-up-against privacy laws... RFID (radio-frequency identification)-enabled backpacks anyone? YOUR TURN!
