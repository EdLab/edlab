Too Much Tea Could Lead to Chronic Bone Disease and Tooth Loss

Khalil Abubakar

March 28, 2013

According to the New England Journal of Medicine , a woman who believed to have cancer, found out the chronic bone pain in her arms, hips, and legs were caused by high intake of strong tea. The woman's daily intake was a pitcher made with 100 to 150 tea bags, which caused her to have a high amount of fluoride, resulting in extremely dense bones and brittle teeth. Doctors found her blood fluoride level was 0.48 mg per litre, higher than the normal concentration of less than 0.10 mg per liter. The woman's case was compared to that of people in India where fluoride levels in the water were naturally high, which causes skeletal fluorosis due to high ingestion of fluoride. This causes bones to be seven times denser than normal.
