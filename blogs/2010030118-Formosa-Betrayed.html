Formosa Betrayed

chingfu

March 1, 2010

Thanks for the rare snow day in NYC last Friday, I got the chance to watch Formosa Betrayed. The movie is based on the mysterious murder of a 31-year-old statistics professor from Carnegie-Mellon University, Wen-chen Chen, a native of Taiwan (see this New York Time 1981 article), and reveals the nature of the authoritative Kuomintang (KMT) government and how it brutally treated Taiwanese people in 1980s, which was a crucial social context for the following democratization in Taiwan. Many of my friends (myself included) were a little bit disappointed by the rather flat storytelling and lack of in-depth illustrations of why democracy matters for Taiwanese people in the movie. However, I'm still glad to see this neglected history being told in this format of popular media.
