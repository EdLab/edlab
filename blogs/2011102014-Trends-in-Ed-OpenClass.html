Trends in Ed: OpenClass

LauraCostello

October 20, 2011

Google and Pearson are joining forces to birth the newest LMS on the block. Their website is just a shell at present, but expect it to be big and sleek (check out the fun slide-y signs and hilarious desert-chic vibe.) The site promises to be fully integrated with Google Apps for Education, free, open, and “ridiculously easy.” Perhaps the most significant shift in this new style of LMS versus something like Blackboard is the focus on social. Google has made a concentrated push towards online identity with Google + (even now that they allow pseudonyms) and this might just make online learning more meaningful. Our recent conversation (blog entries by Joanna, Kate, and Skanda) around personal connection and the importance of humanity in learning make me think that a learning system organized around class members might make a bigger impact than the current content-centric systems. OpenClass relies on a news feed and high identity community. The idea is to create cliques of learning around shared indicators much as one would in a physical classroom. The system also facilitates cross-institutional sharing, connecting students and professors around shared classroom topics. This might be one of the reasons Blackboard has been so active lately in generating open content and creating enticing partnerships. OpenClass is still in beta, so we'll have to wait and see if it'll be the Moodle and Blackboard killer it claims, though a push for innovation in these systems is never unwelcome.
