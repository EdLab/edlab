A Lifetime at the Museum

Nantwi

May 2, 2015


Museums and other traditional learning spaces such as schools and libraries are currently in the midst of reimagining their physical spaces and content offerings. The Smithsonian in Washington, D.C., is hoping to lead the way in this revolution. With 19 museums and nine research centers, the Smithsonian is a hub of activity that draws around 30 million visitors every year. This highlights a popular and varied interest in the museum’s offerings. To this end, the Smithsonian is working on a project to deliver its resources to an even larger and broader audience.

The Smithsonian Learning Lab is an ambitious endeavor that brings the museum’s digital collection of well over a million items to users across the globe. The Lab is specifically focused on engaging educators and students. A major component of the Lab is a digital learning resource that offers thousands of lesson plans and online content, combining the Smithsonian’s collections with learning initiatives. Educators have been particularly impressed with the ability to merge lesson plans from the Lab with learning goals for their students.
