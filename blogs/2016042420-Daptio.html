Don’t Lecture Me

Daptio is an African edtech startup based in South Africa that aims to stop the long reign of college lectures. Why have one sage on the stage when you can leverage the wisdom of the student crowd, one learner at a time? Daptio improves adaptive learning by collecting learner data on an individual level to support a model that gives students key content at just the right time.

Daptio leverages the power of student smartphones to send small amounts of data to their teachers—these individual data points aggregate to create a holistic learning profile. Teachers can then use this granular level of information to provide vital learning analytics such as student preferences and progress. As teachers use this data to improve their curriculum and pedagogy, they promote higher student engagement to create a virtuous learning circle.

Adapting to a New Market

Some African economies may have been late to develop online platforms due to problems with student dropout rates and teacher shortages, but startup spaces seem ready to address these areas, especially with companies like Daptio. The market for mobile learning in Africa is expected to grow by 39%, and the market for e-learning as a whole should be approximately $530 million in 2017. As such, the next thing that Daptio may need to adapt to is a much bigger demand for personalized learning tools.
