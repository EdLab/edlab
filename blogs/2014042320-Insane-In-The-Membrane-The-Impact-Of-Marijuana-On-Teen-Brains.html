Insane In The Membrane: The Impact Of Marijuana On Teen Brains

Brian.Sweeting

April 23, 2014


The number of states blazing toward some form of marijuana legalization is at an all time high; however, our understanding of how smoking impacts brain development is still hazy. As politicians pass weed laws, new research may plant seeds of doubt or blunt their momentum.

A recent study by researchers in Boston indicates the minds of young smokers "show abnormalities in two key areas of their brain related to emotion, motivation, and decision-making." Although not directly tied to declines in brain function, the results are enough to stir the pot and call for another round of studies.

The age limit for recreational marijuana use in Colorado and Washington is 21. As politicians and parents in other states hash out laws, they may want to make similar joint decisions on acceptable use until the smoke clears on any negative long-term effects on brain development. Although it might look a little better on the surface, legalized marijuana could still be frying young brains.
