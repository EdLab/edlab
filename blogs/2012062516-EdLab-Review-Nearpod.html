EdLab Review: Nearpod

Kate Meersschaert

June 25, 2012

Winner of a 2012 EdTech Digest Award for Best Collaboration Solution, Nearpod allows teachers to share and manage presentations synchronously across all student devices. With a separate app for educators and students, the platform allows users to connect and share content, quiz responses, and interactive whiteboard content. Teachers are able to push content out to learners' devices and access detailed student performance metrics all the while controlling what appears on the student's application. Pros: Synchronous sharing across devices seems to be both the future of learning and a valuable way for educators to harness the power of mobile in their classrooms. Additional features offered by Nearpod include rich metrics and data dashboard for teachers and instant feedback for students. This takes the platform from a classroom "nice to have" to a "must have." Cons: Set up. While easy to use once accounts are created and synched, being sure that the right pin number has been added to each student device and the conducting the initial account configuration seems a bit cumbersome. Ease of setup might be the one barrier to adoption for this app. EdLab Relevance: As we consider mobile platform development for both learning and content distribution, it is important to remember that set up and ease of "getting up to speed" is crucial to the success of your platform or application. This can take the form of automated login and mass uploading of class information. Our Takeaway: Nearpod is a valuable tool for educators to push content to student mobile devices and tablets; just be sure to reserve a bit of extra patience for the initial setup.
