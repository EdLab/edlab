Trends in Ed, 9.14.09

Arianna Choi

September 14, 2009

"10 Things I've Learned About Teaching Online" - Higher Ed Perspective Michelle Everson, the recipient of the College of Education and Human Development's 2009 Distinguished Teaching Award at the University of Minnesota, teaches statistics in the Department of Educational Psychology. According to a recent article in the eLearn Magazine, she is particularly interested in exploring ways in which active learning can be fostered in the online environment and has shared ten things she learned from her five years of teaching statistics online. She gives her stories-in-context here: 1. Teaching online is a lot of work. I'm sometimes asked if I feel it takes more or less time to teach online, compared to teaching in the classroom... 2. Students appreciate regular communication and timely feedback on their progress. 3. Many great tools exist but aren't always necessary. 4. Assignments and activities take more time online. 5. Students need extrinsic motivation. 6. Give deadlines. 7. Online courses are not right for all students. 8. Ask students what works and what doesn't. 9. Share ideas, collaborate, and commiserate about the online teaching experience. 10. Teaching online can inform what you do in the classroom if you have opportunities to teach both online and classroom-based courses.
