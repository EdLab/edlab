Pop Sensation Psy Apologizes for Past Anti-American Lyrics

Francisco.Mendoza

December 10, 2012

South Korean pop sensation Psy has apoligized for anti-American comments he made prior to his sudden fame. Psy is known for hit song "Gangman Style" and its accompanying video is now the most viewed video on YouTube with more than 900 million views. Psy was referring to anti-American comments he made in 2004 in the wake of the death of two teenage South Korean girls who were ran over by a US tank stationed in the country and also for the US-led invasion of Iraq. The apology comes as Psy is scheduled to perform in front President Obama and the rest of the first family this week at the Christmas in Washington TV special. Psy, in his apology, acknowledged there are limits to freedom of speech, especially when dealing with sensitive issues such as national pride, military operations, etc. Despite his apology, should Psy be allowed to perform in front of the Commander in Chief?
