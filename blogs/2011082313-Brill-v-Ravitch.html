Brill v. Ravitch

Fred Rossoff

August 23, 2011

The journalist Steven Brill has recently published a book “Class Warfare: Inside the Fight to Fix our Failing Schools,” which has gotten a fair amount of press. The book builds off his enormously influential New Yorker article exposing the “Rubber Room,” a Temporary Reassignment Center in which teachers who have been found incompetent, or otherwise unqualified to teach, are sent to kill time (and collect paychecks), sometimes for years, until the charges against them are resolved. The Rubber Room article provided a great deal of ammunition for the progressive case against teachers unions, and now Brill has jumped right into the debate, lauding reformers and attacking unions for dragging their feet on necessary reforms. I mention all this because I fear that many of you, who are not such regular viewers of C-SPAN's excellent BookTV program as I, may have missed a great debate between Steven Brill and Diane Ravitch that ran a little while ago. You can watch it here on the C-SPAN website, and from the way Brill throws around the word “cherry-picking,” you can tell he's out for blood.
