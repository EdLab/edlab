Information Revolutionary in India Set to Provide Internet Access to Billions

Jenny Abamu

October 16, 2016


India, the second most populated country is the world, is undergoing a digital revolution in the telecommunications and information sector. The richest man in India, Mukesh Ambani, is leading this transformation through the launch of Reliance Jio, a Mumbai-based provider of mobile telephony, broadband, and digital services. The company seeks to provide free mobile calls and online data until December and will then charge as low as 75 cents per gigabyte in the coming year.

With as little as 22% of the Indian population currently connected to the internet, this expansion of access is expected to connect upwards of 80% of the country's 1.3 billion inhabitants. Such a large influx of users is destined to reshape the digital landscape, creating new markets and destroying old ones. As India is one of the largest education technology markets, this also promises to spur innovation in that sector as a larger consumer base becomes available.

This massive shift in internet access is bound to change the country, giving millions access to information and learning technologies that were previously inaccessible.
