Our Five Favorite Language Learnings Apps of 2017

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2017


Instead of idling the in-between hours away with mindless games, learning a new language can be as simple as spending a few minutes each day on a worthwhile app. This list of our favorite language learning apps of 2017 will let you skip the deep research (we’ve done that!) and plonge right into the fun part.

1. Drops
Visual learners aspiring to speak another language, look no further than the aesthetically pleasing colors, illustrations, and user capabilities of Drops. Oh, also, the app has a few creative learning features that differentiate it from the pack, such as requiring learners to swipe rather than type.

2. Tandem
Tandem is like a social media app where you befriend and chat with strangers to learn a new language, as well as help them practice one you’re fluent in. Access to this collaborative language learning community is well worth stepping out of your comfort zone to initiate the first hello.

3. ChineseSkill
If you’re interested in learning Mandarin, ChineseSkill is almost overflowing with different lessons and activities to help you practice your vocabulary, writing, and pronunciation. From faux text conversations to tracing Chinese characters, ChineseSkill is sure to hone your Mandarin linguistic abilities.

4. FluentU
Learn a language by watching clips in FluentU’s impressive video library selection. The language learning platform enhances each video clip with subtitles, vocabulary definitions, and progress tracking so you can practice your listening and reading skills simultaneously.

5. HelloTalk
The philosophy behind HelloTalk proposes that there’s no better way to learn a language than by talking with native speakers. This app lets you play the part of both student and teacher as you swap languages with others from around the world.
