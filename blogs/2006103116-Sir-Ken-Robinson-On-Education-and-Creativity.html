Sir Ken Robinson On Education and Creativity

Dennis Stevens

October 31, 2006

I ran across an interesting lecture by Sir Ken Robinson, Senior Advisor to the J. Paul Getty Trust, in which he speaks about the importance of nurturing creativity within education. In the piece inserted below, he suggests that we should reflect upon how we encourage or discourage creativity in the formative educational experiences of our youth. Here, he seems to be tangentially mirroring the sentiments expressed by Daniel Pink in his text, A Whole New Mind. In my view, what connects these two, is the notion that the economy of the near future is going to require a much different approach to thinking and problem solving.
