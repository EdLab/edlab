Are Newswires Jealous of Twitter?

Kate Meersschaert

November 17, 2011

According to this internal memo obtained by New York Magazine, possibly yes. The gist of their envy is that employees of traditional newswire the Associated Press Tweeted that they were arrested at Tuesday's Occupy Wall Street site BEFORE the wire reported the news. Instead of speeding-up to compete with the people's "distributed digital-information network (aka Twitter)," the wire reprimanded the reporter internally and made a request for journalists to wait until news is available through the wire service before tweeting. As GigaOm points-out eloquently in this article, isn't instantaneous the whole point of Twitter? Why would a news organization committed to unbiased, expeditious reporting of facts, ask an employee to "delay" their reporting? Oh right, because it makes them look bad. How can newswires, journals, websites, blogs and general disseminators of knowledge, facts or human experience catch-up to the speed of social networking? As we at EdLab work to develop the New Learning Times platform, how can we tap-into the 24/7 instant news-feed? Should we draft behind the force that is Twitter or create something new that surpasses the ease-of-use and low-barrier to entry... but perhaps tailor it to the world of education? What would this look like and how would it function & stand-out against existent offerings in the world of social media and the inundation of information that has become the hallmark of modern life?
