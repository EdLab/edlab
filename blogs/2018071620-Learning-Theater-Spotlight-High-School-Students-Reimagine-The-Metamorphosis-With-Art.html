Learning Theater Spotlight: High School Students Reimagine The Metamorphosis With Art

EdLabStudios

July 16, 2018


Ariela Robinson has a passion for fusing art and literature. In fact, she researches the intersection of the two fields as a doctoral candidate in the interdisciplinary program at Teachers College, Columbia University. Also a high school English teacher for the past 10 years, Robinson has regularly asked her students to create pieces of art based on books they study together. Robinson’s most recent student showcase took place in the Smith Learning Theater, where students and professional artists alike gathered to create art inspired by Franz Kafka’s Metamorphosis.

"It’s such a sad piece," says Robinson of the novella, which tells the story of a man who turns into a giant insect. "The art really beautifies it." From costume makers to painters to video game coders, 10 artists contributed to the exhibit, each bringing to life a different aspect of the book. Robinson’s goal for the evening was for participants to unpack the novella in creative ways, and in doing so to foster a deeper appreciation or unique understanding of the text that they wouldn’t have received from simply reading it. The exhibit was an exploration of how art can bring a difficult piece of literature to life, and how teachers and students can reimagine English education.
