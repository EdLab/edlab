Casino School

EdLab Studios

February 2, 2016


Walking into a casino, you can't help but notice the cacophony of excitement erupting from the crowded Craps table. Players win big and lose big and stick around when the dice are hot. But between the chips and the calls, Craps can be an intimidating game on both sides of the table. For an aspiring dealer, knowing the iconic table game is a real asset; increasing the chances of locking down a gig and standing out from the competition. Founded by Wendy Rubin in 2014,Big Deal Casino Academy is the first licensed dealer school in all of New York state with a mission to prepare students with the knowledge and skills to shine in any casino audition. The rigorous 240 hour Craps Dealer class takes place in a simulated casino; adorned with opulent chandeliers and red velvet curtains making for a smooth transition from classroom to live table. Over a ten-week period, students move from fundamentals to dice calls and stick handling to the intellectually demanding process of taking bets and calculating payoffs and odds to spotting cheaters and ensuring game protection.
