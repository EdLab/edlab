Got technology? Great!...Now what?

Skanda Amarnath

October 20, 2011

This recent NYT article on an Indiana school district going shifting entirely to laptops is both exciting and worrisome. On the one hand, it's great that districts are still open to investing in better technology for education, especially given the current fiscal conditions for state and local governments. On the other, I still see little explanation for the radical change. Is the school district trying to improve the quality of education it is delivering? Is it hoping that online textbooks and other educational resources will make the laptops an effective cost-cutting measure? Are the laptops merely a signal to upper-middle class family that this school district is the one for them? That the laptop involves more complex technology than the textbook does not imply that districts should move so quickly to buy or rent laptops for every student. I suspect that the cost-cutting gains are the most likely to materialize as more publishers have to reveal what they have. On the other hand, I grow weary of those that don't have a stated purpose or reason for massive investments in technology for the classroom.
