Your Next Spoken Language Exam Could Be Graded By A Computer

jvazquez

November 9, 2014


Mandarin is one of the most spoken languages in the world and it’s one of the most popular to learn. But with an exponentially growing number of students and only so many Mandarin language teachers, what’s a learner to do?

A new software program, developed by publishing giant Pearson in conjunction with Peking University, is introducing students to the first automated oral Chinese exam. The software is able to grade students on their spoken Mandarin; there is no human assessor involved in the process. For $30, students are able to receive feedback on their grammar, vocabulary, fluency, pronunciation, and even tone immediately after taking the test. What’s more, the exam can distinguish between different dialects and accents with its voice recognition software.

For people who need to prove proficiency in a language, like students trying to skip a year of beginner lessons, the quick-graded alternative to in-person exams could be a sought after solution. How do you feel about computer-graded exams? Add your thoughts to the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
