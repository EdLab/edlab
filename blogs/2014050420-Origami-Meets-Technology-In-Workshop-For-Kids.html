Origami Meets Technology In Workshop For Kids

jvazquez

May 4, 2014


Juan Gonzales, creator of kid-friendly, tech-centric FabSpaces, has crafted an unexpected hero in the form of a paper cube. Through this simple block, he teaches kids the perplexing concept of the Internet of Things.

While teaching a complicated idea, Juan finds that it's best to keep explanations as straightforward as possible. Explaining the Internet of Things could easily become convoluted but the origami cube allows the concept to be clearly demonstrated using very few words. At the most basic level, coins and magnets are inserted discreetly into the pockets of the cube so that they can "talk" to each other -- either by repulsion or attraction. Cubes that are attracted can subsequently be built into larger structures. The plot thickens when simple electric circuits are added to the folds (pun intended).

In this activity, kids are able to see how seemingly distinct objects can communicate with each other (provided they are suitably equipped), creating a network of interconnected items. It’s a great exercise in analytical thinking as kids work to explain the reasoning behind the behavior.
