hackNY Brings Students to NYC for Hackathons and Fellowships

Ryan.Allen

May 10, 2018


"hackNY aims to federate the next generation of hackers for the New York innovation community." (hackNY)

Hackathon in the City

hackNY is a New York City-based nonprofit founded by professors from NYU and Columbia University that hosts community hackathons and student fellowship programs. Each semester, university students from around the country flock to New York for hackNY’s Student Hackathon event. Attendees network with the city’s rich startup and tech community, gain skills through workshops, and, of course, compete in a fierce hackathon. For the competition, students race through coding challenges and project-based learning in a 24-hour breakneck sprint.

Following the challenges, students must present their projects to a panel of judges who decide on a range of awards: good design, technical skill, learning outcomes, and the newest category, "Best Hack for Social Good." hackNY is proud that both its participants and judges come from diverse backgrounds with varying experiences.

Fellowship Opportunity

The organization also offers the hackNY Fellows Program, an intensive summer program that introduces students to the tech community in the New York City metro area. The fellowship is paid and participants are housed together in the heart of the city. Each fellow receives a professional mentor to coach them through meaningful projects that are assigned during the program.

The hackNY Fellows Program has had seven cohorts. Alumni are active in supporting the participants and fostering community once the program finishes, hosting meetups and sharing job postings with each other. Past participants of the program have gone on to diverse careers, such as founding startups, becoming game designers, and even playing music professionally.
