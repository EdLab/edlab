AT&T's Solution to Self-Destructing E-mails

Khalil Abubakar

July 3, 2013

If you use your mobile device to send emails, you realize that sometimes old messages you sent no longer exists on your phone and if you don't normally log on to a computer, the email is gone forever. AT&T is trying to resolve this problem. A method, system, and apparatus for providing self-destructing e-mail messages was filed on June 20, 2013. An e-mail client application provides a user interface through which the sender of an e-mail message can enter the message and a time period for destruction of the message. This method really secures emails we send to our clients. This new method sender of an email can prevent the client from forwarding the message. I think with this new feature from AT&T will help workplaces in order to prevent vital information from leaking.
