A Design Thinking Accelerator to Uplift Girls

saraehardman

September 18, 2017


Spring Accelerator wants to change the fact that 250 million adolescent girls are living in poverty worldwide by funding businesses that create educational and career opportunities in East Africa and South Asia. Currently in nine countries throughout these areas of the world, the accelerator program hopes to reach 200,000 girls by 2019. They are built on the philosophy of the Girl Effect, or the idea that intergenerational poverty and related problems can significantly decrease when girls are better educated. When girls have more opportunities, families tend to become stronger economically, student numbers increase, and disease and child marriages decrease.

This London-based accelerator program works with businesses for nine months in an intense design prototyping program to ensure that they are best able to provide innovative solutions needed in order to make real impact in countries where girls often have fewer professional opportunities. The prototype process relies on listening and empathizing with people, and could provide businesses with a stronger foundation upon which to grow.
