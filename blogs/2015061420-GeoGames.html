GeoGames

Alexandra Lederman

June 14, 2015


GeoGames and the geography proficiency concepts that go along with the game were researched and produced by Reach the World, a non-profit organization, along with Dr. Susan Lowes at Teachers College, Columbia University, and funded by the National Geographic Society Education Foundation. GeoGames is available on both web browsers and iOS and Android mobile platforms. The free version of the game allows users to "build planet Earth" by testing their knowledge of oceans, continents, and others geographic features. The paid version ($.99) allows users to develop their geography skills by adding countries, cities, and other features.

GeoGames is a great game for younger elementary students. The users drag and drop the North and South Poles, continents, oceans, rivers and mountains in the correct locations. The game is staged in easy to hard levels, starting with the poles and ending with rivers and mountains. The levels are timed, allowing for students to improve their scores and geography memory skills. The first drag and drop of each level provides the student with a "sneak peek" of the proper locations. If the user needs assistance, there is a sneak peek button that provides geographic placement information.

Pros

The design of GeoGames, especially the drag and drop feature, makes this geography game intuitive and easy to use. The game is perfect for the younger elementary student beginning their geographical explorations.

Cons

Unfortunately neither the desktop version nor the app features a zoom function. This makes it difficult for students to learn the exact locations of rivers and mountains, as they try to develop specific geographical skills.

Our Takeaway

GeoGames is a great way to introduce young learners to geography. The game is interactive, visually stimulating, and intuitive. Each level scales in difficulty, keeping students actively engaged. Though GeoGames does not provide enough specificity to stand alone as a teaching tool, this engaging game is a good way to reinforce classroom geography lessons.
