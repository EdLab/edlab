Live Blogging a 500-Point Loss Day on the Exchange

HSC

September 16, 2008

Starting at 5:55 am, Floyd Norris--chief financial correspondent of The New York Times--began live blogging the major financial stories of the day (e.g., Lehman, B of A, AIG). There were several aspects of the post that I found interesting. Among them: 1) The way Norris interjected humor (e.g., "The bulls must have gone to sleep at lunch. Or perhaps all the optimists were in Europe, and the slide started when they went to dinner.") 2) The comments; there were over 200 comments made during the live blog 3) The lack of images If you want to know what a live blog for a really bad day on Wall Street looks like, click here.
