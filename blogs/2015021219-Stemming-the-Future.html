Stemming the Future

Nantwi

February 12, 2015


It seems all the current education discourse revolves around increasing student interest and achievement in science, technology, engineering and math (STEM). Educators, parents, policymakers and education organizations have all chimed in on solutions to what is becoming the education topic of the 21st century. The Global STEM Alliance, made up of partnerships among schools, governments, corporations, non-profit organizations and research institutions, hopes to help the cause by reaching and motivating students across the globe to engage in STEM.

The overall goal of the Alliance is to reach students who would otherwise run from STEM. The Alliance is particularly focused on attracting students in foreign countries and distant communities. One of the major vacuums it hopes to fill is providing students with mentors from around the world who can further water the seeds of their interest. This goal has been made easier by the seemingly endless ways individuals can connect virtually these days. The ultimate goal of the Alliance is to reach a million students in over 100 countries by 2020.

Can the Alliance achieve this lofty goal by 2020? What are some potential impediments? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
