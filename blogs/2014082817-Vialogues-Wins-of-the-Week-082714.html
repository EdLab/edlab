Vialogues Wins of the Week: 08/27/14

Kate Meersschaert

August 28, 2014

***Private! After a short summer hiatus... please find this week's wins, social shares and buzz around everyone's favorite video discussion platform! Mentions on the Interwebs: Kelsey Greene gets us! She's a masters student at SUNY Buffalo, interested in media education. She's identified Vialogues as a way to build "robust video discussions" and even listed some use cases. This video's got legs We find ourselves among a list of resources for design thinking educators. Guess which vialogue they've chosen to highlight? It's like a help desk but better, right? Though we know of an active cohort at SPU using Vialogues, it's nice to see that we made it onto their library's service-oriented tool, the Tech Desk! Two sides, one coin In a wiki for tech integration for public schools of North Carolina, two versions of the same video are posted; one on YouTube, one on Vialogues. Interesting! Trans-what? Did you know Vialogues is included in transmedia processes? Unsure of what that means? Check out this post about it. A researcher gets her due Julie Edmunds's Voice episode was proudly referenced on the University of North Carolina Greensboro's site. Quality reading Another proud subject, this time of NLT's SiNY stream, Chris Lawrence wants his readers watch his Digital Ready Maker Party episode. That's some fine APA style As part of an eloquent anecdote, teacher Sheri Edwards quotes James Paul Gee and also cites a vialogue featuring him. And if you made it this far...
