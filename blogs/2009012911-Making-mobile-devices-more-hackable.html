Making mobile devices more hackable

Stephanie Hunt

January 29, 2009

Next Wednesday we'll go head to head in a tournament-style smack down. EdLab members will bring their finest application ideas for mobile devices to the table, and only the strongest will survive. Why not continue your mobile app-themed Wednesday by attending the February 4 meeting of dorkbot? One of the presenters next Wednesday will be the fabulous Hans-Christoph Steiner, who has been leading an effort to breathe new life into discarded electronic devices by hacking them to run Linux. Interested? What: Dorkbot Meeting When: Feb 4, 7pm Where: Location One, SoHo More from the dorkbot site:

Hans-Christoph Steiner: Reware
We are surrounded by gadgets, from iPods to phones to PDAs. These little black boxes are in fact full-fledged computers and are eminently hackable. Yet these devices are locked down to try to prevent you from doing anything but consuming: consuming media and consuming minutes. On top of that, we discard these devices at an alarming rate. Thanks to many hardcore hackers, you can now run the Linux kernel on many of them. We are building on top of that work to turn these devices into an Arduino-like platform for hacking. We are starting with Pd and Python firmwares, now available in alpha releases.
