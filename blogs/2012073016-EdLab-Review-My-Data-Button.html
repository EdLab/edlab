EdLab Review: My Data Button

Kate Meersschaert

July 30, 2012

The Office of EdTech and the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy have partnered to offer students a "My Data Button" and corresponding "Personal Learning Profile." This multi-tiered initiative aims to help make educational data more accessible and transparent by urging companies and groups who hold student data to make this "button" a part of each group's platform. Anywhere student data is being tracked by a third party, from learning management systems to track meets, a student could then access and add this info to a "personal data profile." Explore the attached fact-sheet for more specifics. Pros Transparency and standardization. Instead of separate, often silo'd platforms containing student data, learners would have access to one learning profile, thereby streamlining performance assessment, and will ultimately help content providers and educators to create more adaptive learning resources based on this data. Cons Adoption. While a worthy idea and important issue, the chances of many third-party platforms integrating a data button into their sites is a bit pie-in-the-sky. Also, it would be helpful if the site listed content providers and partners who have integrated the button on their platform. EdLab Relevance As we develop our own data dashboard to service and connect EdLab applications, it is important to remember how we will allow users to access and upload this data. Will we integrate a button, or another more seamless approach? Our Takeaway A great idea that will hopefully increase the relevancy and transparency of educational technology.
