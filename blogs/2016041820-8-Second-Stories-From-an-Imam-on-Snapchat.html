8 Second Stories From an Imam on Snapchat

saraehardman

April 18, 2016


Snapchat: love it or hate it, it's probably here to stay for a while. Though most normally associate the popular social media app with selfies and silly filters, could it have a different use, one that’s serious and even educational?

This Vialogue features an Imam who uses Snapchat as a platform for educating people about Islam. Suhaib Webb records his eight-second long videos for Muslim teens and young adults who have questions about their faith or want to learn about more the religion in general. Because Webb is a Muslim scholar, he has even created a series of Snapchat Fatwas, or Snapwas, to answer questions about Islam.

Fusing religious lessons with pop culture, this Imam is certainly reaching a large audience. He has almost 90,000 followers on Twitter and even more on Snapchat. Webb’s approach to the app might cause us to reconsider its value as an educational tool. It is the most popular social media tool used by teens, after all. Maybe it’s time to find ways to incorporate it into education.

What do you think about this Imam’s educational approach to Snapchat? Can watching a snap be an effective way to learn? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
