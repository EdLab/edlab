Mystery Science

Ryan.Allen

August 7, 2017


"Open-and-go lessons that inspire kids to love science!" (About Mystery Science)

Demystifying Science for Elementary Students

Mystery Science is an educational company founded by two long-time friends, Doug Peltz and Keith Schacht, that promises to get elementary school students hooked on science. Courses are designed around questions that require students to use the scientific method to solve. For instance, in the Power of Flowers course, the first lesson is "Why do plants grow flowers?", another is "Why are some apples red and some green?" Teachers sign into Mystery Science to choose a lesson and they receive several items: instructions, a list of needed supplies, printable handouts, a short video introducing the mystery, and clues for the students. The company promises that each lesson will allow students to discover an ah ha! moment when solving the mysteries.

The Personal Touch

Co-founder Peltz is the face of Mystery Science. He has seven years of classroom teaching experience and science curriculum design. The frontman is featured quite prevalently throughout the companyâ€™s website and he professes his passion for teaching science in the testimonial. He even promises to personally answer questions that educators may have when teaching their courses. His presence provides a nice personal touch to Mystery Science's mission, though it might be difficult to uphold as the company and community continues to expand.

In a recent bid to grow the userbase, Mystery Science has offered promotional free memberships up to the end of June, 2017. However, the company's model is not free. Schools can sign up for unlimited service for around $1,500. Individual teachers can sign up their classrooms for the service at around $300 annually.
