Understanding of web style guide

Ariel Han

June 18, 2013

I start to brainstorm how I can make a web style guide for the Vialogues. I took a look at some nice web sites that teach me what I should include for the document. Branding guidelines document is an instruction manual for how to use the company's branding materials. The style guide explains the values of brand and where relevant, its history and its evolution. It explicitly states the appropriate usage of other design elements of the brand, like the type choices and the use of imagery, as well as the appropriate writing tone to be used in all communication. The style guide is an important part of the branding process, but it is just a part. Who is the style guide for? And why is it necessary? It's depending on the nature of the company, style guide may have different audiences, with differing levels of familiarity with the brand. Perhaps there's and internal design team responsible for the company's communications, both witching the company and client facing. The style guide saves time, money, and frustration by avoiding off-brand communication. I found one good example of brand style guide. http://issuu.com/bondo/docs/skype_brand_book_-_look BRANDING GUIDELINES: WHAT TO INCLUDE? - Strategic brand overview - Logos - Show examples of what and what not to do - Spacing - Colors - Fonts - Layouts and grids - Tone of voice - Copy-writing guide - Imagery - Bring it all together WEB GUIDELINES: WHAT TO INCLUDE? - Button hierarchy - Icons - Navigation (Logged in / Out states) - Basic coding guidelines
