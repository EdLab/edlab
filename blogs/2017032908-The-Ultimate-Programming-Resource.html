In the Experience Age, courses and other formal educational opportunities are increasingly unlikely be the go-to resource for the majority of learners (and if you are a fan of Nick Shackleton-Jones, you would argue classes and e-learning are broken). Instead, resources and experiences are more important than ever in the learning process. This story about Stack Overflow from New York Magazine highlights just how important resources (knowledge) and experiences (social dimensions) are to supporting self-directed learning.
