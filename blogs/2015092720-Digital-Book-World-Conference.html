Digital Book World Conference

Christina Terracino

September 27, 2015


"Now celebrating its seventh year, the Digital Book World Conference + Expo is the preeminent conference on digital content and digital publishing strategies. In 2015 it brought together over 1,500 media, publishing and technology professionals from around the world focused on developing, building and transforming their organizations to compete in the constantly evolving digital media ecosystem." Digital Book World Conference site.

Succeeding in the Digital Sphere

The Digital Book World Conference (DBW) is an annual event that focuses on methods and tools to help those in the publishing world adapt to increasingly digitized books and materials. The organizers state that attendees will learn how to market to digital readers and work with retailers of digital publications. For many in the publishing world, digital content is an emerging and intimidating market, but this conference seeks to prepare publishers to transition into a profitable and fruitful digital age.

Connecting Professionals
Conference Chair Mike Shatzkin, who is the founder and CEO of the The Idea Logical Company, has been involved in publishing for nearly half a century, and is a leading thinker on the shift to digital publishing. The conference markets to owners and CEOs like Shatzkin, but addresses just about everyone in the publishing sphere who will be affected by the increasing move toward digital content. Authors, librarians, editors, and publishers will all find information and insights relevant to them for adjusting to the move toward digital publishing. The conference brings these professionals together to discuss strategies to benefit from this transition and to help them remain relevant and successful in the present and future of publishing.
