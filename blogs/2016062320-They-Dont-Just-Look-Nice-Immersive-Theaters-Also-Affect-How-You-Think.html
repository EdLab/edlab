They Don't Just Look Nice, Immersive Theaters Also Affect How You Think

dinosossi2

June 23, 2016


A recent study analyzed viewer responses within an immersive visualization theater (IVT) (think of a tricked-out IMAX theater that shows multimedia across all its surfaces). The research examined the effects of high-resolution photos on participants’ memories and emotions.

Researchers divided participants into groups and projected landscape photos within the IVT. Participants chose where they would prefer to spend time based on the scenic photos and explained why.

Their explanations reveal that the IVT environment and media (e.g., large screens, high-res photos), when digested in isolated discussion groups, and mixed with social interaction, all prompted attention to detail, triggered emotions, and prompted memories related to the displayed landscapes. As such, it appears that curated media can improve learning outcomes and develop emotional intelligence in viewers.

We have easier access to a wider array of multimedia and low-cost screens than ever before. This study has implications for technology's ability to stimulate sensory responses, and its findings can be applied across multimedia rich learning spaces of all varieties.

Orenstein, D. E., Zimroni, H., & Eizenberge, E. (2015). The immersive visualization theater: A new tool for ecosystem assessment and landscape planning. Computers, Environment and Urban Systems (54), 347–355. doi:10.1016/j.compenvurbsys.2015.10.004
