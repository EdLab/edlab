Where Did You Find That? The Influence of Party Affiliation on News Source Preferences

dinosossi2

July 17, 2017


Partisanship is the name of the game in the current political environment.

Even the most cursory familiarity with the national political discourse would demonstrate this.

However, the American Press Institute, The Associated Press, and the NORC Center for Public Affairs Research combined to show that, paradoxically, Republicans and Democrats access media and follow the news in remarkably similar ways. 

In short, both party affiliates pay for news, find news on social media, actively look for news, access news multiple times a day at similar percentages.


The biggest contrast was in terms of perceptions of accuracy and trust in media according to party affiliation. In terms of accuracy, 31% of Democrats believe that news from the media is very accurate versus 8% of Republicans. In terms of trust, 30% of Democrats trust news information a lot versus only 8% of Republicans.

Although there are obviously huge discrepancies in these numbers, they both demonstrate an underlying problem. 

A general lack of trust in the media across both parties.
