Making Money on YouTube

Nantwi

December 12, 2008

A recent article in the New York Timeshighlighted a number of people making a living solely by posting videos on YouTube. One of the average Joes making a fortune of YouTube is Michael Buckley, who's celebrity chatter show “What the Buck?” averages close to 200,000 viewers per episode. In addition to earning over $100,000 annually, he recently a development deal with HBO. More than a year ago, the creators of YouTube invited members to become “partners” and placed ads next to submitted videos. YouTube's partner program, which includes everyday users to big name companies, provides the opportunity for members of that program to generate revenue through submitted videos on YouTube, similar to Google's AdSense. The videos submitted as part of the partner program must be original, with the producer owning all copyright to the video. The partner program is seen as something of an answer to Google's inability to profit from YouTube since the large majority of its submitted videos are by anonymous users who may not have the copyright to the videos.
