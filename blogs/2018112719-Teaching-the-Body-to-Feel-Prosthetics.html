Teaching the Body to 'Feel' Prosthetics

Melanie.Hering

November 27, 2018


Phantom limb, or the sensation amputees sometimes have that a missing limb is still attached, is painful and confusing. But what if there were a way to manipulate this sensation so that it could be used for good? Researchers at the Swiss university École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne have developed a new method that combines VR and artificial tactile sensations to do just that.

The thought is that by allowing an individual to "see" their missing limb overlayed onto a prosthetic in VR, amputees can train their brains to perceive a prosthetic as part of their physical body. The researchers found that the perception of the phantom limb could be restored to its full length, rather than being foreshortened, and that this even continued for a time after the experiment ended, showing promise for extending the effect indefinitely.
