Passion-Driven Learning With Yo-Yo Ma

chingfu

February 25, 2014


What makes a great music performance? Is it a performer’s mastery over his instrument? Innovative interpretations of the music score? Energetic interactions between the performers and the audience? While these are all part of the answer, for Yo-Yo Ma one key element is the willingness and courage to explore new possibilities fearlessly. His perspective reveals the similarities of essential experiences in the learning of the seemingly uncorrelated subjects of art, science, and technology.

How can educators use arts to engage learners with a deeper understanding about various subjects and help them to be empathetic global citizens? The Arts and Passion-Driven Learning program, hosted at Harvard’s Graduate School of Education, aims to provide educators and artists with an interdisciplinary collaboration platform and helps them explore new teaching and learning possibilities together. As educator Ron Berger states at the beginning of this video, being able to model great performers in real life is much more powerful than reading written texts about them.
