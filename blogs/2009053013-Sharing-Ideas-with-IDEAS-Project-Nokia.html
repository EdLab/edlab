Sharing Ideas with IDEAS Project Nokia

Frederik G. Pferdt

May 30, 2009

I browsed around the web for some future ideas in technology and education and found this interesting project sponsored by Nokia: "Welcome to IdeasProject, an entirely new way to connect with some of the most visionary and influential thought leaders in communications technology and their disruptive ideas. A project of Nokia, hosted at www.ideasproject.com, IdeasProject brings together these important big thinkers to contemplate the big ideas that matter most to the future of communications, joining them up through video clips, links, articles, podcasts and dynamic maps to push the boundaries of Web navigation and the thought process itself." They offer different ways of exploring and following: Twitter, Feeds and the Website itself. The following themes are discussed: - All Themes Business/Investing - Deep Thoughts - Enabling Technologies - Experience - Impact - New Applications It also provides several ways to navigate: "Through our framework of related links, or from the perspective of questions, themes, technologies, people and their Big Ideas, many of which originate on our site." On the home page you'll find a shuffle of images, people mostly, along with an assortment of themes. Click on a Polaroid and see that person's Big Idea, then follow one of the links below to find out more about them, their idea, or both. Next, you can expand your knowledge through links to other related ideas, themes, technologies, content, and people. Click the Ideas Map tab at the top of any page for a visual representation of how it all relates. Or, to get a list of our contents in a given focus area, click on one of the tabs at the top - people, ideas, themes, technology, questions - then choose which one you want to explore. You can also grab one of the theme-based cocktail napkins on the home page and find out which ideas, people, questions and technologies are linked to it."
