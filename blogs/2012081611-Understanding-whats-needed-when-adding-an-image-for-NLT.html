Understanding what's needed when adding an image for NLT

Daniel Um

August 16, 2012

You may have noticed some distorted images, not displaying with their original ratio, in many Web publishings and on many Web pages. The main reason those unfortunate cases appear is a lack of setting-up the correct system in the site development stage, or the lack of the content contributor's understanding about that system. It's very crucial for content contributors, who are providing both text and images, to understand how to prepare an image for their publishing site. Here is what you need to know if you are one of contributors for NLT: 1. All image frame ratio for NLT multi-media (video & image), including small thumbnails, is a 4:3 ratio. This ratio comes from the fact that many default ratios on our cameras are set to 4:3. If you have an original picture from your own camera with this 4:3 ratio, you can use them directly without any customized cropping. 2. If you have long vertical or horizontal images, it's really important to be aware that EdLab image server will crop it to 4:3, starting from the middle of the longer side. So, it's good idea to avoid those type of images, or to crop them by yourself to the 4:3 ratio before submitting them to our EdLab image server which will automatically crop the image focussing on the center portion. 3. All video on NLT is coming from Vialogues. So, if you'd like to use video, you should upload to Vialogues first. If you need any training for cropping images in the 4:3 ratio, please let me know.
