Holoworlds Histodeck

Daniel Um

November 13, 2013

Our team (Daniel, Brian S., Manav, Jamie) created the Holoworlds Histodeck. This learning space is conceived as modular in form and function. Starting from the premise that hard-to-access alternate realities are optimum sites for virtual environments, and not incidentally hard to access from a learning perspective, we created a holodeck program that brings different historical, geographical, and cultural epochs to life. Students (in K - graduate levels) may interact with these worlds as complete landscapes for lessons and missions in culture, politics, history of psychology, resource management, and on and on. They may also, however, choose different elements from different worlds as they move through them, exchanging with other groups, crafting their own utopias or dystopias, using each as "maker spaces" (kitchens, electronics tables, tool sheds) with historically constrained resources that may dynamically change with lesson design and group choice. Walls between columns collapse or expand to allow for different spaces and numbers of experiences (up to 15), as well as insular worlds or fluid ones, exhibiting the tendencies of cultural and technological exchange that dynamize lessons, allow user choice of world-crafting and open-ended problem-solving and exploration as the past comes alive and the future is born...
