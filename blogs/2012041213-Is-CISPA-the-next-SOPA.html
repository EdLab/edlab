Is CISPA the next SOPA?

Kate Meersschaert

April 12, 2012

Why be concerned about CISPA? Just when you thought the world was free of cyber tyranny with the defeat of SOPA legislation... if passed, CISPA (Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act), would allow private and public companies to "swap" data on potential security threats with the government. The main concern about this newly proposed legislation is that it is too broad. For more on these concerns check-out the embedded vialogue below. How exactly is CISPA different? Although the courts would not have the ability to delete a website's DNS information as proposed by SOPA, critics are concerned that CISPA could open-the-door to breaches of personal freedom and privacy. Another distinguishing factor is that a few big tech companies like Facebook who came-out against SOPA are now backing this proposed legislation. If CISPA passes, it will revise the National Security Act of 1947. For more info check-out this LifeHacker article and this US News & World Report piece.
