Planting a New Educational Resource

Jenny Shen

November 2, 2015


What do edtech and gardens have in common? They’re both being used to introduce new hands-on elements to schools. While edtech takes up a great deal of space in media coverage, gardens are not quite so ubiquitous. But Real School Gardens is looking to change that.

These learning gardens are meant to be a way for schools to foster a sense of community between teachers and students and to create a resource for class curricula. Science teachers have integrated these gardens into lesson plans to highlight scientific observations and measurements.

"I think that garden space will become as important as a traditional library or computer lab," says the principal at a school that is making use of one of these learning gardens. Participants have shown an increase in vocabulary as it relates to the garden—not just with respect to plants and insects, but also with design concepts.

Could learning gardens become as much of a mainstay as a school library? What kind of learning could a garden foster that is not part of the current curriculum? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
