12 Educational Tech Trends to Watch in 2012

Scott Streiner

July 18, 2012

I decided to blog for the 2nd time in 3 days (a new record for me!) and I really wanted to find an interesting article on the educational technology trends of 2012. And I found an article that I think is both interesting and useful for all of us EdLabers. The article is from Mindshift, and describes 12 different educational technology trends to follow this year. Most of them have relevance to a lot of the products and services here at EdLab."12 Educational Tech Trends To Watch in 2012" includes:

  1. Mobile Phones
  2. BYOD-Bring Your Own Device
  3. Bandwidth Issues
  4. Natural User Interfaces
  5. Web Apps (HTML5)
  6. Data
  7. Adaptive Learning
  8. Privacy/Security
  9. Open Licensing
  10. Peer to Peer
  11. The Maker Movement
  12. Gaming
Each trend is very useful to keep in mind when thinking about any Edlab product or service. There are many interesting articles in Mindshift regarding "how we will learn". Take a look at this article, and let me know what you think about some of these factors, as well as how Edlab has contributed to solve some of the problems and issues outlined in this article. I'd be interested to know how some of the obstacles are being addressed in our own EdTech contigent.
