Party + Education Talk = Fabulous Combo

Anna Beard

October 30, 2006

FYI: *November 1st @ Cain* (7:00 PM to 10:00 PM) 544 West 27th Street (between 10th and 11th Avenues) (212) 947-8000 "Our Nation's Most Pressing Problem" Educational Inequality and the Need for Education Reform with: *Joel Klein* New York City Schools Chancellor & Former Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Bertelsmann, Inc. (NYCDOE) *Wendy Kopp* President and Founder of Teach for America (TFA) & *Cami Anderson* Superintendent of New York City's Alternative High Schools and Programs The event is complimentary. The bar is cash. Please feel free to forward the invite and bring a friend. Election Day is November 7th. Join the Promotional (only) Host Committee at r@rasjr.com. *Executive Summary* Today in our country, nine-year-olds growing up in our communities are already three grade levels behind. In fact, 58% of all black 4th graders in this nation are functionally illiterate, and half will not graduate from high school. Those who do graduate will, on average, read and perform math at the level of eighth graders in other communities. Of the one million African Americans who enter 10th grade each year, only 90,000 of them will earn a bachelor's degree and only 23,000 will earn a master's. (Keep reading). This vast inequality of educational attainment has been categorized by Teach for America as our nation's most pressing problem and the modern day civil rights struggle. Our guests have staked and dedicated their careers to eliminating it in New York City and across this nation. We hope that you will join us Wednesday, November 1st as we explore our role in this important battle.
