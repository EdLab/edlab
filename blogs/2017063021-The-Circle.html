The Circle

dinosossi2

June 30, 2017

Behold the latest wonder of the world. 


The Apple Park (a.k.a. The Circle, a.k.a. Steve Jobs’s Utopia, a.k.a. The New Center of Cupertino) has officially opened. 


So what’s the word, Big Bird? 


As per any Apple product, it is superbly designed, both outside and in. 


The exterior is made of the longest continuous piece of curved glass in the work. The canopies that provide shade and shelter were designed to prevent rain from streaking the glass on the outer structure. Close to 80% of the building’s footprint is dedicated to parkland. 


The interior is equally impressive. The door handles and water faucets were designed with exquisite care. The garage park hides unseemly extraneous features like electrical outlets. 


It even has toilets! 


Despite their attempts at being environmentally friendly, the parking garage contains a whopping 9,000 parking spots for only, gulp, 12,000 employees. 


Fortunately the building is round. That way the expected traffic congestion will come in a full range of 360 degrees… 


Environment impacts behavior. Will the expansive Apple Park spur innovation? Productivity? How about humane design that appeals to the best of human behavior?


Only the future knows... And given Apple's impact on the tech ecosystem, much of the future will be conceived here.
