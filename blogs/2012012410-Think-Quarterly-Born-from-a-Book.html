Think Quarterly - Born from a Book

Kate Meersschaert

January 24, 2012

Think Quarterly is a stand-alone, quarterly web-based publication created by Google UK's "think" tank which is comprised, in addition to the quarterly, of "Think voices, think insights and think blog." Originally created to share thoughts and data in book form with their corporate partners, Google UK now releases this publication digitally for eReaders and mobile phones. Issues are thematic and the current one is focused-on "speed." Relevance to New Learning Times (NLT) What can we learn from this print to mobile/digital publication and apply to NLT development? Three main aspects of the web-based interface stand-out for me as worth exploring in the context of NLT's: 1) The scrolling, slider-bar navigation featured on the left-hand side of the page (see screenshot below). 2) The concise one-liner that appears as a quote from each piece when you rollover the title, again on the aforementioned slider navigation. 3) That each issue is thematic. I wonder what it would feel like and what value would be added if we explored making the NLT a thematic ePub?
