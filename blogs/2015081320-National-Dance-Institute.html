National Dance Institute

EdLab Studios

August 13, 2015


Jacques d’Amboise began his ballet training at 7 years old in Washington Heights. By 8, he joined the School of American Ballet, and by 15, the New York City Ballet. While a principal dancer with the New York City Ballet in 1976, he founded National Dance Institute on the belief that the arts have unique power to engage and motivate young people. NDI provides New York City public school students with free weekly dance classes. Rather than offering programming as an after-school or extracurricular activity, dance is built into the school day, which helps level the playing field and remove any social stigma that might come from choosing to dance. In this way, NDI provides students with an experience they might otherwise never have. Those who excel are provided free tuition to continue studying dance at the Institute’s Harlem studios that culminates in annual performances. The result? Deep friendships, newfound confidence, experiences in achieving success through practice, and a way to work through challenging circumstances and feelings, which transcends thoughts or words.
