The even darker side of Uber

dinosossi2

July 2, 2017


Oh dear, is there a more embattled company than Uber?


There is a report about its internal machinations.


The departure of CEO Travis Kalanick.


A multibillion drop in valuation.


But it gets worse. 


Uber and similar peer organizations Lyft are expected to provide insurance coverage in case of an accident. Specifically, New Jersey provides for $1.5 million in coverage depending on the circumstances.


To simplify, at least in the Garden State, passengers are provided with insurance through the driver, company, or both up to $1.5 million in medical bills.


For passengers, the most reassuring part is that the insurance is triggered under multiple circumstances (e.g., underinsurance, uninsured, lapse of insurance, etc.).


Although ride sharing companies are under siege, at least their companies can receive protection in the case of an accident.


How much Uber will have to pony up for the invariable accidents that will happen is yet to be seen...
