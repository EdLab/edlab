Educational video sharing + content management system

zhou

December 4, 2008

What online tools do teachers use to teach with video? We have content management systems such as Moodle, Sakai, Wordpress, Moodle etc where teachers can upload and/or embed video in online instructions. But it's often hard to share those video with other teachers, or re-use the video resource in another lesson. We also have YouTube and Google Video to share educational videos. But there's no way to add instructions directly on YouTube or Google Video, unless one has the technology skills to embed those videos on their own websites. So how can teachers create online lessons tech-proofly while ensuring good sharing and re-usability? The idea is to develop a CMS with video sharing capabilities. I've been thinking of tweaking the Survey Sidekick framework for this purpose (see picture). With the survey design tools in Survey Sidekick, we just need one video uploading function, as well as a transition logic function (with which the teacher can define the dynamic sequence of their instruction slides so that students will learn adaptively). Nevertheless Faisal told me it might be even more difficult than creating the tool from scratch because the Survey Sidekick structure may be fundamentally different than this tool. But I like Survey Sidekick's way of drag-and-drop elements because it's very intuitive and convenient (assuming no lag from the server). Also, what name shall we give this tool? Any ideas?
