Publishing Center at Berkeley

Jeff Frank

September 25, 2008

As we think about developing the publishing mission at the Lab, it is worth looking at what other institutions are doing. In particular, The Berkeley Electronic Press offers a host of services to individuals and institutions. While they appear to be relatively successful, I have a number of questions about their model. For instance, I agree that the generic sites that universities offer faculty and students are not as powerful as they could be, I have a hard time seeing how the BEP site really takes us significantly beyond where those generic sites currently are. As well, while I like how BEP brings high-quality scholarship into an online only format, I question if they are really doing enough to break the print model. As it stands, the sites look like an online institutional site for a traditional print journal. And while this may be exactly what they–and their many customers–are after, I think more work can be done to take advantage of the online medium.
