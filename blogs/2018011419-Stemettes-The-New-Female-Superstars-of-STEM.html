Stemettes: The New Female Superstars of STEM

saraehardman

January 14, 2018


"We’re showing the next generation that girls do Science, Technology, Engineering, and Maths too." (About Stemettes)

The STEM Gender Problem

Female representation in Science, Technology, Engineering (STEM) fields is low worldwide. In the UK, only 21% of the STEM workforce is female, and stereotypes about gender differences in STEM fields become entrenched in girls’ minds by the age of six. Stemettes is trying to change these statistics, one new girl coder at a time.

The Stemette Solution

Stemettes’ mission is to inspire girls in the UK and Ireland to dive into STEM subjects. To achieve this, Stemettes hosts regular events such as panels, hackathons, exhibitions, and mentoring schemes. Even better, all of these events are free, making them accessible to all girls regardless of their backgrounds, and they provide food. These events are hosted by women working in STEM fields so young girls can be inspired by real-life role models. Stemettes also partners with teachers and school administrators to create Stemillions Clubs at individual schools. These clubs receive weekly STEM-inspired activity packs that groups of girls can complete together.

The Origin and the Future

Stemettes was co-founded by Anne-Marie Imafidon in 2013, and since then over 17,000 girls have attended their events, and 95% of those girls have reported increased interest in STEM fields after. Although they’ve already had a significant impact in bringing more girls into STEM, they aim to reach two million more by 2025 and to see a STEM workforce with greater female representation.
