Economics & Education: The Economies of Scale When Teaching Students of the Same Level

Skanda Amarnath

August 12, 2011

Yglesias notes in a post today that it was the educationalist Johann Hecker who first popularized the idea that a teacher could teach more students effectively if his or her students were of similar skill level in the given subject. It is interesting how Yglesias compares Hecker to Salman Khan in that both are catering to as large an audience as possible within a given skill level. I'm curious to know what progressive educationalists think of Hecker's ideas though, since I'm sure many would see Hecker's ideas as a means for further stratifying society. At what point do these economies of scale run out and are there any advantages to having a classroom full of students of different skill levels?
