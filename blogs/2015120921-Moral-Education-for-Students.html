Moral Education for Students

AbdulMalik

December 9, 2015

Adults tend to lament the level of manners that middle schoolers exhibit nowadays. Most schools lack programs or courses that teach students in middle school basic etiquette. Basic etiquette in this case ranges from simply teaching students how to butter their bread at a social event to how to speak politely to adults and individuals of their age group. Our society no longer accepts ignorance and intolerance. Such an initiative will be a step in the right direction because instilling discipline in middle schoolers is a way of setting the right standards for the country's future leaders.

This initiative is similar to a course that is built into the curriculum of the education system in Ghana for all middle and high school students. The course, Religious and Moral Education, enables students to learn about every religion and culture in Ghana. Students also learn basic etiquette, which helps them in their daily interactions with other people in society. I think a moral education course should be made mandatory for all students at the middle school level because respect and tolerance are two effective morals that aid in the development of individuals as well as an entire nation.
