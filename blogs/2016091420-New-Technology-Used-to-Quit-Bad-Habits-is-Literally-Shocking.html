New Technology Used to Quit Bad Habits is Literally Shocking

Jenny Abamu

September 14, 2016


The act of curbing cravings, binging, spending, or even biting nails is no easy feat. The bad habits that plague our daily lives and test our self-control are a battle in the mind. However, the recently developed Pavlok Wristband, is making that struggle a physical one by shocking you every time you lapse back into that nasty habit.

The 450 volts of electric shock is meant to help millennials break bad habits, including excessive spending. Just connect the device to your account, set a spending limit, and try not to go over it because you'll be shocked if you do.

Though the effectiveness of this product is still being evaluated, for those desperate enough to break a habit, this might be a useful option. What do you think? Is this an innovative way to learn new behaviors, or a scary lapse back to electro therapy? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
