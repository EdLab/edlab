A Year in Materials Stats

LauraCostello

September 19, 2012

*Please keep private I've been working to compile our purchase and usage statistics for the 2012 school year. Here are some of the highlights: This was our second year of patron driven acquisitions and this year we implemented an automatic PDA system that purchases items automatically based on usage. As a result of this system and an exponentially expanding e-book catalog, we purchased more books this year and many more of them were digital.

For comparison, here are our e v. p -book stats from last year:
This year we also learned a lot more about our patrons' journal and database browsing habits. Thanks to Scotty & Demetri's work on the DocDel statistics, we were able to identify a few holes in our coverage and subscribe to some new journals. Best Loved Databases:
  1. Academic Search Premier
  2. ERIC
  3. JSTOR
  4. Proquest Digital Dissertations
  5. OmniFile Full Text
  6. Sage Premier
  7. Gale LitFinder
  8. CINAHL PLUS
  9. Ebrary
  10. Ovid Medline
Our patrons downloaded 1,662,975 articles this year! We also used our click through and download statistics to cancel a handful of journals that were collecting dust... Least Loved Journals:
  • Cyberpsychology & Behavior
  • Gender and Language
  • Journal of Nursing Measurement
  • Language Acquisition
  • Teaching Geography
  • Forum for Promoting 3-19 Comprehensive Education
  • Infant Mental Health Journal
  • Journal of Child & Adolescent Substance Abuse
  • Journal of Further and Higher Education
  • Folia Linguistica
We fulfilled 10,008 requests in DocDel this year and 6,083 of these were fulfilled with our electronic resources. Our acquisitions were driven primarily by DocDel requests and our PDA program this year. The items we acquired through other means were for our collections in the 3rd floor reading room (new TC Press and faculty, dissertations) and our 2nd floor collections (juvenile and curriculum).
It's been a good year in materials and we're looking forward to a faster, more digital, and increasingly patron-driven 2013!
