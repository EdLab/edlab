I Spy a Mechanical Turk

Dan Mallinger

October 26, 2007

Two computing problems have been elegantly smashed together in a fun single solution :) Digitizing books is hard. In the end, optical character recognition (OCR) just isn't that easy for computers. So I may never get Adobe to extract the text of those dissertations correctly. Web spiders are constantly submitting data to websites they shouldn't. The EdLab site is a great example. Every day we had a dozen new user requests from web spiders trying to get access. Until I added Captcha. So welcome to the world of reCAPTCHA! A new tool from CMU and Intel designed to provide simple Captcha for any webmaster, with a twist. The captchas are generated from digitized books (and altered of course). The data of user entry is then used (a la machine learning) to teach a larger computer system how to recognize characters better than the competition. You can read more here: http://recaptcha.net/learnmore.html
