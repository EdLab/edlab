Digital vs. Analogue: Will Virtual Learning ever Fully Replace Brick & Mortar?

Kate Meersschaert

December 13, 2011

Probably not in the near future, but online courses can and do serve to better supplement and support traditional education, while allowing people to self-credential and continue to learn skills they need to be successful in many fields without entering a formal classroom. Instead of taking sides as this Bloomberg BusinessWeek piece attempts to do, I have compiled resources listed below that seek to do one of three things: Overthrow the current educational system, support the current system through resources for "flipping" the classroom (for more on "flipping" check-out Demetri Lales' content stream) and finally, provide alternative credentialing programs for those who want to pursue certificates, degrees or just keep learning outside the classroom. The Million Dollar EdLab Question... What will the credentialing institutions of the future look like? I think this question is a part of the answer... what won't they look like? I think open-source question/answer forums like Quora, Wikis & social project-based sites like GitHub will provide models for experiential learning and an opportunity for knowledge-holders to share and showcase their work in an online portfolio that is open to all to edit, borrow and comment on. These forums and organic modes of disseminating information provide a digital record that may someday very soon replace and at the very least surpass the value of a diploma for many fields such as computer science. Will the value of a liberal arts style education ever fully disappear for many fields? Probably not. But for now, lets keep asking the right questions and exploring the growing body of resources like the ones listed below, that just might help make traditional education in four-walled classrooms less of a necessity. 1) Overthrow! Anarchy! No more school! UnCollege Movement DIY-U Peter Thiel's Foundation 2) Support & Flip The Classroom!
