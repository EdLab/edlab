EdLab Review Consultation

saraehardman

May 9, 2018

A couple months ago, I wrote an EdLab Review on the Internet of Elephants, an app created by Safari Central. After it was published, the company reached out to me hoping to receive consultation on how to better pivot their product toward educators and schools. They didn’t want to necessarily become an exclusively educational product, but did want a farther educational reach. 

 

Internet of Elephants is an AR conservation app aimed at teaching people about endangered species and conservation efforts. Using the app, you can experience endangered animals in AR (one of the highest quality AR apps I’ve reviewed), snap pictures with them, learn about their lifestyles, and donate directly to animal conservatories. It has a lot of potential as an educational app, but it also has a lot of work to do to get there.


Mweturia is a 40-year-old bull elephant who lives at the Space for Giants conservation center in Kenya. Look, even the tear on his ear and the dent in his tusk comes across in AR!


Ryan, Melanie, and I sat down over Skype with Safari Central’s CEO, Gautam Shah, and two other members of their team for a 30-minute long conversation/very informal consultation session. We advised Safari Central that if they wanted more teachers and schools to use their product, they would do best to create specific lessons plans, curricula, and other classroom activity packs and make them readily available for teachers on their site. We also recommended aligning their activities with specific Common Core standards and incorporating conservation learning in different subject areas.

 

Although I didn’t know this at the time, I later discovered that the advice we gave them reflects in many ways the educational curricula that WWF has created.

 

Gautam and his colleagues were very appreciative with our phone call and promised to remain in contact and give us access to the new features they’re working on—and they sound pretty exciting. They discussed launching life-size AR that would allow people to literally walk around AR animals. Imagine experiencing a full size elephant in AR. It would be so cool. They also discussed a Pokémon Go type of experience, except one where the animals move and you take on the role of scientific observe rather than collector.

 

After the conversation, Ryan, Melanie, and I looked at each other for a moment, then simultaneously realized that consulting conversations could be a thing we do. At this point, we have a pretty good breadth of edtech product knowledge, so we are in a fantastic position to offer consultation services to startups hoping to make their products and apps more educational. This could generate revenue for EdLab, and would likely be challenging and fulfilling for members of the team.
