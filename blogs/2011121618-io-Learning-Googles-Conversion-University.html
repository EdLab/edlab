i/o Learning: Google's Conversion University

Kate Meersschaert

December 16, 2011

Platform: Google Apps/Adobe Presenter 7 Type of course: Self-directed Goal of courses: To Learn more about how to use Google Analytics and potentially take the Google Analytics Individual Qualification (IQ) test. Week 1 Overview So far, my experience with Conversion U has been really positive, the tutorials are short (under 5 mins each) and use Adobe Presenter 7 (see screenshot below), which is easy to use and provides an intuitive interface for controlling the speed of content delivery. I am finding that both of these elements are crucial points-of-difference for this kind of highly technically specific content delivery. In this first week I have covered the following topics: an introduction to Google Analytics, interface navigation, installing the Google Analytics tracking code, guidelines for interpreting reports, pageviews, visits, and visitors, time metrics and more. Pros: So far, my overall take-away is that the lack of a visual "professor" and reliance on an "AV-style" presentation actually works quite well. I think again, it is the technical nature of the material that makes using glorified screencasting effective and useful, because as a learner you don't want to be taken too far from the Google apps world and interface. Cons: The lack of a quiz or test for knowledge before moving on to another section or lesson is a big draw-back. I want to put my new knowledge to test so that it is fully solidified and becomes working and not just theoretical knowledge. Also, I would love a clearer progress report and dashboard (ironic re: content!). Takeaway... The real proof will be in how well I can read reports for Pundit! Perhaps after next weeks lessons I will fully understand the difference between a "returning" and "absolute" pageview!
