The Phenomenon in the Classroom

Nantwi

March 30, 2015


Soccer is undoubtedly the world’s most famous and popular sport. Last year’s FIFA World Cup final drew close to a billion viewers from all corners of the globe. This has made soccer players global icons. The soccer player whose star shines the brightest across the globe is Portugal and Real Madrid’s Cristiano Ronaldo. Fresh off his third straight World Player of the World award, Ronaldo’s every move off the pitch is front-page fodder while his eye catching soccer skills grace the back pages. Such is Ronaldo’s popularity that he is now the subject of a college sociology course.

Professor Luis Aguair at the University of British Columbia is using Ronaldo as the cornerstone of his course on global citizenship. Aguair was first introduced to the Portuguese phenomenon and his broad appeal during a World Cup qualifying game in which the commentators spent the bulk of the game mostly discussing Ronaldo. Ronaldo’s appeal transcends cultures and thus makes him the ideal candidate for a course on global citizenship.

What other cultural icons can serve as the basis for a college level course? What are the pros and cons of teaching a course on a celebrity? Join the discussion on Vialogues. .
