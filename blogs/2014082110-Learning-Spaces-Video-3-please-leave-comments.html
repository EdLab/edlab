Learning Spaces Video #3: please leave comments!

Jihii Jolly

August 21, 2014

(private) Hi everyone, Here's the THIRD video in our new series on learning spaces :) This one features the Franklin Institute's use of augmented reality in their science exhibits and how it can enhance a hands-on learning experience. The series at large explores how space can be conducive to learning and as Luke mentioned in his last post, it is meant to provoke a conversation around the 4th floor specifically, but also about education in general. Ultimately, there will be an exhibit on the 2nd floor about Learning Spaces in which these videos will be a part of. They'll also be published as part of the Vialogues stream on NLT. As with the last two videos, we'd love for you to leave comments on the content of the video (in this case: questions or thoughts about the museum, their research, or augmented reality in general). We'll then invite the researchers and designers at the museum to comment on the video as well. Thanks very much!
