What is "Foolish Consistency" and Do We Still Care About It?

zhou

November 28, 2012

I was researching a related design issue in New Learning Times (NLT) when I stumbled upon an article by Microsoft titled How To Avoid Foolish Consistency, which spells out my philosophy in web design so I think it is worth sharing. Also, don't be fooled by the title. This article actually emphasizes the importance of consistency but also points out that in some contexts, consistency is worth compromising. I am a frontend developer but I am not a designer. So my eyes are not trained to notice minor differences in web designs. But I understand the Principle of Consistency in web design, which basically states webpage elements (e.g. page structures, navigations, graphics, video player controls, etc.) should be consistent as much as possible because otherwise the user experience might not be smooth. However, I also make web pages so I care a lot about the efficiency (in my own word, usability) of my products. For example, if an element required in one webpage does not make sense in another, I may consider modifying or removing this element depending on the circumstance. If consistency cannot coexist with usability, I have to sacrifice one of them. This can be a hard choice because if I make a wrong judgement, my users will be unhappy. I will not go into the specifics where I think usability or consistency is more important. Everyone has their own judgement. So I will recommend you (designers and frontend developers) to read this short article written in 1999 and form your own philosophy. To me the knowledge here does not expire.
