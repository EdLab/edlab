Psychology in Sports

AbdulMalik

January 26, 2014

Athletes have a strategy or mechanism they use in order to become successful at their respective sport. There are numerous ways to achieve success in sports but on the other hand, certain things are inevitable. According to this article by Bleacher Report, being psychologically ready when engaging in sports is one avenue for achieving success. To do this requires three main things: relaxing, thinking and visualizing as a participant in a particular sporting activity. As a college athlete, there've been several instances on the soccer field where I had to critically think about what to do next in the course of a game. In the course of a soccer game, the game can change drastically, and the mind has to be able to adapt accordingly. I never paid attention to these strategies until I read the aforementioned article. Its obvious that such things are crucial to individual and ultimately collective success in sports. During my freshman season, I used these strategies quite often and that played a major role in adapting to the rigors of collegiate sports.
