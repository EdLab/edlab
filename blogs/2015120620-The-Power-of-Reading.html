The Power of Reading

AbdulMalik

December 6, 2015

We have all heard the adage about the importance of reading, especially from a young age. For children, reading tends to be an important factor in their cognitive growth. Research indicates that the educational importance of reading is among numerous benefits of reading. The United Arab Emirates (UAE), under the leadership of President Sheikh Khalifa, have publicly declared 2016 as the "year of reading." This means young people in the small Gulf state are encouraged to constantly read. The year of reading extends to elderly individuals as well. UAE officials indicate the program is geared mainly towards forming a knowledge based economy.

This initiative is a step in the right direction because it is aimed at improving the economy of the UAE. I believe this initiative should serve as a model for governments around the world. In order for children to gain good reading habits, parents need to be good readers as well. Global education will improve tremendously if we all embrace the simply reading a short story each day. Perhaps staring at the person next to you on your train ride will cost more than reading a sentence or two.
