Five Key Studies on Online Learning in Higher Education

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2017


With online class enrollment rapidly increasing each year, researchers are rushing to keep up, tackling countless studies concerning best online learning practices. Among the dozens of studies we read this year on visual cues, gamification, and blogging, (to name a few), here are the ones we think are most helpful to educators and course designers.

1. Quality Means Different Things in an Online Course
When it comes to the most important elements of online courses, students, teachers, and administrators all disagree. This study breaks down these differing opinions to guide future online course designs.

2. How Learner-Learner Interaction Impacts Online Learning
Itâ€™s no secret that online students typically interact with their peers less than students who share the same classroom. But, as this study shows, online learner-learner interactions can still have a big impact on student success and satisfaction.

3. How Visual Cues Impact Learning in Video Lectures
Any online learner will tell you that not all video lectures are created equal, with some clips captivating attention much more than others. It turns out that one of those eye-catching elements relates to the kinds of body gestures lecturers make.

4. Using a Gamified Learning App in Higher Ed
Researchers examine how a learning app used outside of class hours could boost student performance. Not only did this particular game increase scores, it hit class dropout rates hard.

5. Putting Interactive Storytelling in Online Course Design
As online learning continues in an experimental phase, one possible design element to improve student engagement is folding course content into interactive storytelling. This research highlights the steps needed to implement this strategy.
