My data visualization picks for this month

Gus Andrews

February 14, 2008

Buddies of mine from Stamen Design in San Francisco have an exhibition up at the MoMA in a couple of weeks, in collaboration with the Exploratorium. The data visualization they are showcasing at MoMA is called Cabspotting; it tracks the speed, position, and velocity of San Francisco taxi cabs. They have done a number of other nifty visualization projects too. I'm hoping to get them to come talk to us! Also, I found this visualization of New York City's population in comparison to that of various states on Very Small Array, the blog of Dorothy Gambrell, who draws the excellent, brainy comic Cat and Girl. Dorothy has more charts up, too. I think what's awesome about her data visualizations is not just how she chooses to chart (which is slick too) but also what she chooses to chart. Birthplaces of American orchestra members, charted by what they choose to play? awwwsummmmmm.
