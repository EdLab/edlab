Developers Face Off In The Name of Math Education

jvazquez

May 19, 2013


With students falling behind in math, NYC’s Department of Education struck up a first-of-its-kind challenge for software developers: design an app, tool, or game that will ultimately help middle schoolers excel in math. Dubbed the "Gap App Challenge", the competition is designed to "fill the gap in middle school math students' skills, interests and motivation."

Winners of the challenge are expected to be announced on May 22nd, but in the meantime you can view the viable apps on the Application Gallery page. Top awards will go to the "Best Instructional App" and "Best Administrative and Engagement App." Winners may receive cash prizes and the chance to participate in a pilot program in an Innovation Zone (iZone) school. The iZone program encompasses 250 NYC schools that strive to ideate innovative solutions to challenges faced within the education sector.

This competition is apropos for two main reasons: 1) It taps into the vibrant, burgeoning startup community within New York City and 2) It harnesses teens’ fascination with digital technology. It’s a win-win!
