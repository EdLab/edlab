#NYTechDay 2014 Recap

Kate Meersschaert

April 25, 2014

Tweets by @CuriousBrooklyn Yesterday, I ventured down to the 92nd Street Piers in Midtown for this year's #NYTechDay (see this post covering last-year's event). While not ideally situated for public transit, the event space is always an exciting opportunity to venture down to NYC's port. Upon arrival, I was immediately struck by Columbia Business School's branding of the large freight elevators you must take to get to the event floor (see image below). I vote yes on elevator decals! Once inside, I quickly checked-in, grabbed a wrist band and entered the show. Columbia B School was one of the first booths I encountered and it was great to see Friend of EdLab, Zak Schwarzman. Continuing-on I was struck by the size of the crowd and size of the show! Easily double the size of last-year's! I spoke to one of the Columbia B School students staffing one of their booths and they mentioned that the show was MOST packed at 9am (perhaps pre-work visits?). I next encountered what would be the last row of available edtech co's as everyone else seemed to want to make a slightly early exit (having worked trade shows at the piers, I understand completely!). Some of these co's include: @GoEnnounce zearn.com ebindle.com @cpbibliography bookandtable.com Tiggly! (EdLab GroundBREAKERS, Seminar & Demo Night alums!) NyMediaCenter.com YouVisit.com @DstruxInc Betterment.com Canvsly.com UniversityBeyond.com Solidoodle.com Of the company's I had a chance to visit (I needed more time!) non-profit Zearn.com (check-out this free, interactive lesson), Tiggly (obviously), Solidoodle.com (their 3D printers are under 1K + they are dev. an ed curriculum) and Biblionarrator.com (data-driven annotation tool for archival collections). I am excited to follow-up with these company's and to learn more about their work and how we might engage them in EdLab opportunities & projects (NewLearningTimes.com, GroundBREAKERS & Seminars etc.)!
