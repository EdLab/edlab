Youth Take A Stand: Story-Telling And Digital-Aged Civics

Carmen James

April 6, 2014


Youth have been expressing themselves via storytelling in various forms of media such as blogs, videos, audios, and photography as long as they have had the media at their fingertips. New media scholars and activists are interested to see how they can leverage the excitement and engagement of digital storytelling for civic purposes. In this video, scholar Henry Jenkins explains what participatory politics are and how youth today are using new media to tell stories that translate deep social concerns into compelling narratives and bring the public into conversations on important social issues.

This video is artistically crafted to show what politically and culturally participatory media looks like as much as to tell the story of youth using it today. The screen flashes as various youth newscasters report on cultural and political events using dramatically different media and tones. Jenkins believes storytelling is a core skill for youth today, and learning how to share these narratives through a diverse number of media and across geographic locations has deep social implications.

When re-appropriating and re-mixing stories for narration, how do you pick and choose your stories and sources? Share your experiences in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
