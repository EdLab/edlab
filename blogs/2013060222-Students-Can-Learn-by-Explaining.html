Students Can Learn by Explaining

abagigah92

June 2, 2013

A recent study shows that being able to explain what a teacher went over in a classroom reveals how much a student has understood the material. Researchers have shown that students in elementary and preschool learn more when teachers turn to ask them questions related to the subject that was taught. A meeting by the Association for Psychological Science was held to discuss ways to enhance how and when to ask students for explanation. This was design to close the gaps between memorization and actual understanding of rules and concepts. Often times students are very good at restating the facts without actually understanding what led to the answer. This means how teachers frame questions are very important in how students respond to the questions. Generally, students learn more from explanation rather than from feedback and teachers must help students master concepts and rules rather than just memorizing facts.
