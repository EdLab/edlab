IT, looking beyond my nose?

Hugo Ortega Lopez

November 24, 2005

I found myself, like many foriegn students at TC, doing work and catching up on Thanksgiving Thursday. Refreshing my spanish and news from Mexico I came across some facts just release by the latest 2005 census. This information makes me wonder if the skills and knowledge that I am acquiring through the exposure and use of the latest technologies and gadgets available to me are making myself obsolete or at least asynchronous to the possibility of going, literally, back to Mexico. This also brings a reflection on the pertinence of standards, methods and what is relevant knowledge for the foreign cultures existing and learning at TC. How much time will it take some countries to catch up? Would I still be alive when my skills become usefull to the general population? Here are the facts: Only one out of five households in Mexico has a computer (19%). 90% of the households with computer are not connected to the internet. 70% of "internauts" only have access outside the home. the good fact: 60% of usage is related to school activities.
