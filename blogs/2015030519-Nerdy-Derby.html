Nerdy Derby

EdLab Studios

March 5, 2015


Fifty feet of track. No rules. Infinite attempts. That's the recipe for success at Nerdy Derby, the miniature car building and racing competition thatâ€™s speeding towards an educational event near you. The program got its start in at the 2012 World Maker Faire hosted at the New York Hall of Science in Queens as a NYU Interactive Telecommunications Program student project. Since then it has partnered with an ever-growing catalog of schools, hackerspaces, tech collectives, museums, and community organizations around the globe. Nerdy Derby puts kids in the driver's seat of their own informal learning experience, giving them a fun platform to experiment with an array of materials, explore concepts in physics and engineering, and push their ingenuity into overdrive. Cheers, triumphs, and crashes abound while students learn first hand how to improve their car designs to get a little closer to the finish line faster, stronger, and maybe even in one piece.
