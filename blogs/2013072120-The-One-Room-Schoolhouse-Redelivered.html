The One-Room Schoolhouse, Redelivered

jvazquez

July 21, 2013


New Classrooms is an organization dedicated to re-imaging traditional schooling. It addresses the overcrowding problem many teachers face by implementing a radical solution: If there are too many students for one classroom alone, get rid of the classroom-- or, at least, your perception of one. Since students learn at different paces, and it is nearly impossible for one teacher to personalize her lessons so as to reach every student in the class, current instructional approaches inevitably leave much to be desired. New Classrooms collaborates with schools to come up with spatial configurations that address the learning needs of all students.

One such configuration involves combining the forces of multiple teachers in the school to create a mega classroom with a number of instructional stations to support different learning styles. In this way, students can easily find an approach that best meets their academic needs while teachers can impart multiple skills simultaneously. Small groups, large groups, digital tutoring stations... Joel Rose (co-founder and CEO) feels that when you aren’t closed in by walls, the possibilities open up.

Says Rose, "Sadly, we’ve been talking about the future of education in this country for the last 20 years, but it’s been relegated to white papers and conferences."

Could this serve as the new model for in-school education, or does it simply confound an already complicated issue? Leave your thoughts in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
