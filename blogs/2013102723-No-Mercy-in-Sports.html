No Mercy in Sports

Francisco.Mendoza

October 27, 2013

After game two of the World Series, I decided to watch the local news that followed. One of the news stories focused on a high school football game. Parents and staff members were questioning officials at the game for allowing one of the teams to win 91-0. Experts say that this can lead to bullying and also gives the players of the losing team a low self-esteem. I happen to enjoy both playing and watching sports. As true for all sports, there will always be some kind of gap in the ability of the opposing teams. When there is such a big difference in the score, the winning team doesn't show any mercy (most of the time). The idea is that if we can win big, why not? It seems unfair to have to lower your skill level when facing a weaker opponent. Here is the article on the story. Should the winning team have allowed the other team to score a couple of touchdowns or should they have just stopped scoring after a few touchdowns?
