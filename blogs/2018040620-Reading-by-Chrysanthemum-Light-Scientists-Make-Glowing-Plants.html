Reading by Chrysanthemum Light: Scientists Make Glowing Plants

Ryan.Allen

April 6, 2018


Scientists at MIT have been working on bioluminescent plants that they hope can one day curb electricity usage. Utilizing luciferase, the enzyme that allows fireflies to glow, the process involves embedding a specialized mixture of nanoparticles into the leaves of plants. Once infused, the particles within the leaves can be induced to emit a glow for almost four hours.

This new version of artificial bioluminescence differs from previous methods that have relied on genetic engineering. The MIT method is less costly, more efficient, and produces brighter light. Additionally, any types of plants can be infused with the light-emitting nanoparticles, including common kale or spinach.
