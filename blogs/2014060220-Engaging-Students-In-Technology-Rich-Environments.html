Engaging Students In Technology-Rich Environments

Fred Rossoff

June 2, 2014


Title: Students' Engagement in Technology Rich Classrooms and Its Relationship to Professors' Conceptions of Effective Teaching

Authors: Engida Gebre, Alenoush Saroyan, & Robert Bracewell

Source: British Journal of Education Technology

Research Question: What is the relationship between student perceptions of student engagement and teacher ideas about effective teaching?

Study Design: We all have memories of exceptionally dull classes and teachers, and can all point to moments when, as students, we were less than engaged in the classroom. Although research has demonstrated that professors have specific ideas on whether and how technological tools and specific teaching techniques can improve student engagement, little has been done to correlate these ideas with actual student engagement.

This paper surveyed the students and teachers of two technology-rich active learning classes in a Canadian university, and, using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to extract meaningful underlying variables and multivariate analysis to compare them, examined the relationship between student engagement and teacher conceptions of effective engagement.

Findings: The results demonstrate that technology-use is a meaningful independent factor in student engagement, along with mindfulness, collaboration, and meta-cognition. Student engagement was greater in classrooms where the professors thought of teaching as developing self-reliance, and lower in classrooms where the professors thought of teaching as simply transmitting knowledge.
