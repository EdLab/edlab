Teachers College Scratch Day

Ting Yuan

May 20, 2009

Last Saturday (May 16), Stephanie, Ollie, and I had an one-hour session introducing the usage of sensors (e.g., light, sound, resistance.) with Scratch. About 20 people attended the activity, including 6-7 children (from 6 to 11/12 years old) and their parents. Here is an Animoto clip made through some pictures I took there: The attendants started to make their own animated piece with sensors after our brief demonstration of Stephanie's walking pad (by using resistance sensors) and my little cardboard "house" (by using a light sensor). I felt amazed by the children's creativity during the session. About half of the children are old users of Scratch and they were making very interesting animated clips and got their works connected to sensors. The Scratch program gives them a great space for imagination, artistic expression, and hands-on creative experience. I had such a joyous afternoon and I believe the combined experiences not only let children learn "programming" but also motivate them to design, to write, to draw, to compose, and to "experiment".
