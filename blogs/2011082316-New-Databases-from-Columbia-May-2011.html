New Databases from Columbia: May 2011

Allen.Foresta

August 23, 2011

During May 2011, the Columbia University Libraries added 4 new databases to their e-collections.  All of these resources are fully accessible for all current Teachers College students, faculty, and staff. Berg Fashion Library http://www.columbia.edu/cgi-bin/cul/resolve?clio8622085 Provides integrated text and image content on world dress and fashion throughout history. Offers fully cross-searchable access to an expanding range of Berg content collections, including the Encyclopedia of world dress and fashion, e-books, e-journals, museum directory, reference works, images, and more. Browsable by time (1600 to date) and by place (click on world map). Counseling and Therapy in Video: Volume 2 http://www.columbia.edu/cgi-bin/cul/resolve?clio7195347 Counseling and Therapy in Video provides the largest and richest online collection of video available for the study of social work, psychotherapy, psychology, and psychiatric counseling.Volume 2 has been fully integrated with the original product. This current release includes  511 videos totalling 539 hours. Current Index to Statistics Extended Database http://www.columbia.edu/cgi-bin/cul/resolve?ATR1965 Contains cumulative bibliographic citations to statistical books and articles in more than 1,000 journals, with coverage generally extending back to 1974, earlier in some instances. Eurekahedge http://www.columbia.edu/cgi-bin/cul/resolve?clio6879044 Eurekahedge provides access to a range of databases covering hedge funds, private equity funds and specialist funds.
