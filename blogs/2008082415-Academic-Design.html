Academic Design

Pravin Sathe

August 24, 2008

There is a dearth of good design (both aesthetic and functional) in the academic community. And I don't mean the Karim Rashid brand of design (design for design's sake) - you can tell I am not a KR fan ;). I'll be writing more about this in the future, specifically how bad design leads to problems in dissemination of information, acceptance of information and clarity of information. But that's to come. For now, let me highlight a site that is moving academia forward: http://www2.lib.virginia.edu/exhibits/onthemap/index.php The information architecture is wonky as is the interaction design which muddles the information being presented but the graphic design is light years ahead of most academic websites. So we are now looking at a 33% success rate for a site. Better than in my view a close to 5-10% success rate for most academic websites. Is this site perfect, absolutely not! But at least they cared enough to get behind a design direction that is fresh and relevant. Our job is to make sure they aren't on an island alone. In my following blog posts In the posts to come, I'll be writing about how we in academia can be at the forefront of design (aesthetics and functionality). Let the arguments commence!
