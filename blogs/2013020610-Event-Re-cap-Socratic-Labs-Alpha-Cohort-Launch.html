Event Re-cap: Socratic Labs Alpha Cohort Launch

Kate Meersschaert

February 6, 2013

***Private! Private! Private!***** See my original EdLab blog post for details on the event and Socratic Labs program. Event Re-cap: Upon arrival at Alley NYC and after signing-in with what can only be described as a slightly hostile staff member, and trying to discern the slightly confusing colored sticker system (colors denote how you will "help" the cohort of startups) I entered a small room full of chaos. The event was held in the diminutive front room of the Alley NYC space (self-proclaimed "most badass coworking space on the planet"). As opposed to the larger spaces in the back of the co-working space that has held events like the Starl demo night this past summer (Check-out the SiNY here), the front space is tight at best. The event drew about 50-60 people. After about a half hour of attempting to network in a space already full of chairs the demos began. With very little intro or explanation from Heather Gilchrist, the head of Socratic Labs, the first startup began... Here is the line-up: Careerosity Edventory (after many emberassing tech hiccups... apparently the organizers did not test the presis first!) Learn Metrics Loan Hero PenPal News Unbound Concepts Teachley (EdLab Seminar Alum!) (EdLab Edtech Take/Talk alum!) Takeaways: What stands-out most for me was the lack of any structure to the event, the apparent lack of preparation and the unclear "call-to-action" of the paper "sign-up" sheet pictured below. What does sign-up involve? What do you receive for participation? What EXACTLY do the startups get from Socratic Labs if they are asking for help this early on in their accelerator/incubation experience? The value of practice, preparation and "poise" were definitely reinforced by this event and made me feel that our "EdTech Elegant" first demo night stands-out even more clearly as a stellar event in the edtech world. The most lasting impression I got from repeated "EdLab treats startups like rockstars" to "To make it in EdTech you need to first visit EdLab to be 'vetted'" is that EdLab is in a wonderful position to provide meaningful value to the world of "edupreneurship" both with current initiatives (EdLab Seminars, EdTech Takes/Talks, ResearchBroker, Vialogues &NLT) and through future LaunchPad offerings... stay-tuned!!!
