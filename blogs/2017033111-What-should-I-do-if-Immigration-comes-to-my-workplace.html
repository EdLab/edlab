What should I do if Immigration comes to my workplace?

LukeMalone

March 31, 2017

***Please keep private***


I hate that this is a relevant blog post.


If an officer does get permission to enter your work space, the officer is free to ask you questions about your immigration status.

You can tell the officer, “I wish to talk to a lawyer,” in response to any question the officer asks you. 


You do not need tell the immigration officer where you were born or what your immigration status is. 


You do not have to show the officer your papers or any immigration documents.


If the officer asks you for your papers, tell the officer, “I wish to talk to a lawyer.”


You have a right to keep silent. In most states, you don’t even have to tell the agent your name.

Although you may want to provide your name only so your family or attorney can locate you. 


You also have the right to talk to a lawyer before you answer any questions.
