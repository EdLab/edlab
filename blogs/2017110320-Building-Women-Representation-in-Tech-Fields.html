Building Women Representation in Tech Fields

saraehardman

November 3, 2017


"By teaching women around the world from diverse backgrounds to learn software development, we can help women improve their careers and confidence in their everyday lives." (About Girl Develop It)

Building Representation

By now, everyone knows that women are underrepresented in the tech sector. In fact, women make up only 26% of those working in computer science. Thankfully, there are organizations all across the world advancing women representation in tech, including Girl Develop It, which has been working to change that statistic since 2010.

Classes and Community

Girl Develop It is a non-profit organization that helps support and teach adult women interested in web development and computer science. What started in one classroom in NYC has spread to 57 cities throughout the U.S., and it’s only continuing to grow, receiving funding from both sponsors and individual donations. In each city, organizers of Girl Develop It offer in-person classes, community building co-working sessions, and group support. Some of the different opportunities currently taking place are the Code & Coffee events in Pittsburgh, the Intro to Responsive Design course in Fargo, and a panel about A Day in the Life of an Engineer in NYC.

For women who can’t make it to in-person sessions, Girl Develop It also allows free access to its curriculum online. By creating these affordable opportunities for women throughout the U.S., Girl Develop It hopes to bring confidence to women of all backgrounds interested in a computer-related field. They’re committed to increasing the low percentage of women working in tech so that they have greater representation in the field.
