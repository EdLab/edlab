Seminar at UC Berkeley

SeungYeon.Lee

February 22, 2018

Last Tuesday, Feb 20th, I visited my alma mater, UC Berkeley, to present at the BEAR (Berkeley Evaluation and Assessment Research) seminar.

The BEAR seminar is a course in our program and researchers/professors within or outside UC Berkeley are invited every other weeks. 

I presented a part of my dissertation and here is the link for the abstract of my talk. 

http://bearcenter.berkeley.edu/seminar/growth-curve-cognitive-diagnostic-models-longitudinal-assessment

In my dissertation, I proposed a new approach to measure students' skill mastery over time for longitudinal assessment. 

I presented a similar topic at the EdLab seminar last year (the vialogue of my talk), but this time I focused more on technical aspects of statistical modeling.

Check out my presentation slides if you're interested.


When a graduate is invited to the BEAR seminar, as a long-standing tradition, students in our program make a graduation cap. 

This is a picture of me (holding the cap!) after the seminar with some of my friends in our program, my advisor Prof. Sophia Rabe-Hesketh and Prof. Anders Skrondal. 


I also shared a lot about what EdLab does, and they found our research here very interesting!


Other notes on my trip to Berkeley:

- The weather was just perfect there. It was Spring already with flowers. A welcome break from the freezing winter over here. (Only to find out that it hit 60 degrees in NYC yesterday!)

- If you plan to visit the bay area, let me know. I will share all the information about places and foods you should try. 

- A bad news... I got in a car accident a day before the seminar. Both cars were totaled, but thankfully I and others in the car were all safe with no serious injuries although it was a huge crash with another car. I was in the passenger seat but the seat belt really saved my life! So please please wear seat belts all the time even when you are in the back seats and DRIVE SAFE!
