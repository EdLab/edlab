The Design Team Goes to the School of Visual Arts

veronicablack

March 24, 2017

Last month the Design Team signed up and attended a Continuing Education introduction to Exhibition Design at The School of Visual Arts. Our goal for the class was to explore best practices for professional gallery installation and exhibition design strategies. Over a four-day period we explored the methods, tools, and technologies professionals use to create an exhibition in a public gallery.


The EdLab Design Team does many things, and we all came to Teachers College from diverse backgrounds. Zoe is an architect with a passion for 3D modeling and fabrication, SK has a background in user experience and industrial designer, and I am a filmmaker and interactive designer. We all come from various design backgrounds and have experience with installing exhibitions, but as a team we wanted to be on the same page -- combining our joint knowledge into a well oiled exhibition-maker-fun-design team.




Throughout the experience our instructor Carl talked about the his personal experience of designing for exhibition in California and New York, the standards for safety and accessibility, and tools like Google SketchUp that support the install process. We had a crash tutorial in using SketchUp.


SketchUp is an open source online software for 3D modeling. Zoe is a huge fan and had already used SketchUp to design 3D models of our three Gottesman galleries.


But for SK and I it was a great way to learn a new software and practice our modeling and layout.



We had a chance to learn best practices in a gallery. Two class periods were spent learning about different hanging systems and projections mounting -- best paints for vinyl, lighting treatments and tools to have that best support the install process. Though some of these tricks of the trade were new to us, others were not. It turns out that most of the professional methods and tools were ones we are already using as a team. In the end we gained some new skills, but the overall experience showed us that though we may all come from different disciplines of design and we have cultivated our own best practices that fit into the exhibition designers we were aiming for but with our own EdLab twist.
