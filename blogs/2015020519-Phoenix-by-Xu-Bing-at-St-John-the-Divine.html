Phoenix by Xu Bing at St. John the Divine

Panisuan Chasinga

February 5, 2015

Phoenix: Xu Bing at the Cathedral On view through February 2015 THE CATHEDRAL CHURCH OF SAINT JOHN THE DIVINE 1047 Amsterdam Avenue at 112th Street New York, NY 10025 Over the course of two years, pioneering Chinese contemporary artist Xu Bing culled detritus from construction sites across the rapidly changing urban landscape of Beijing, and transformed it into his most monumental project to date: Phoenix (2008-10). A feat of engineering and ingenuity, Phoenix is composed of two birds, a male called Feng and a female called Huang. Feng and Huang–together weighing 12 tons and measuring 90 and 100 feet long, respectively–are now on view at the Cathedral. They hang suspended in the Nave, two majestic birds in perpetual flight beneath its celestial ceiling. Xu Bing with the Sculptures The exhibition and its story is marvelous. The mythical birds were constructed from the leftover material from the construction site in China. The wings themselves are concrete floor slabs. The cathedral is just five minutes away from Edlab. Take a break with a dosage of art.
