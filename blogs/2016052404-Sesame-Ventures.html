Sesame Ventures

HSC

May 24, 2016

Check out this story about Sesame Ventures in the New Yorker. Some of the more interesting excerpts:

...Sesame Ventures anticipates helping the Workshop to approach digital technology in the same experimental spirit with which it once approached TV, and to prepare it for future technological shifts.
(referring to Sesame’s founding) ...“This place started as a disrupter. We started not quite in our garage, but not in these offices. How do we get back to being a workshop?”
And failure might be, in its way, a success. Josh Lerner, a professor of finance at Harvard Business School, told me that venture-capital firms often act as de-facto research-and-development arms. For Sesame, he said, the point might be more “understanding that what seemed like a threatening technology isn’t really that threatening.” Investing a few million dollars in a startup that flops, he suggested, would be a lot cheaper than overhauling the Workshop to target particular platforms, only to see the technology fizzle.
