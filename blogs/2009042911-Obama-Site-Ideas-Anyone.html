Obama Site Ideas, Anyone?

Jessica Blum

April 29, 2009

George and I would like to invite your feedback about our Obama learning site, and thank those who have already offered much-appreciated ideas and direction. At this point, we're hoping for input regarding the following 3 issues: 1) Do you have an EdLab project or skill set you'd like to feature on the site? We're currently considering arranging content around individual EdLab “tools” as portals/frameworks for featured content. If you have such a tool, would like to recommend one, or have been hoping to develop one, please let us know! 2) Can you think of essential themes or ideas that should be highlighted on the webpage? Currently, we've developed content around the following (these will also inform the choice of tools): Race in America, The Civil Rights Movement, Social Action, Democracy, Names & Identity, Technology, Religion, Public Speaking, Education, Global Perspectives, and "An Obama Reading List." 3) Finally, we're interested in designing an engaging “first page” that would represent the site's mission and feel. Perhaps, also, there might be opportunities for a “Teaching Obama” exhibition . . . ? Thanks so much — and we really welcome any and all ideas!
