Organ Donation & Ads ??

Eric Jo

June 17, 2007

When there are approximately 100,000 patients in the United States waiting for an organ donation, it's hard for them to just wait on the line hoping it would be their turn to get saved. According to this article, every 1 out of 12 organ candidates have died before receiving an organ last year.

To avoid death before getting helped, many are now using the internet and billboards as their main method to advertise their need of organs. Many websites, such as matchingdonors.com, have been created to advertise ones need of an organ. While this seems like the better idea to access organs faster, the question that remains is, “Is this the morally and ethically right thing to do?” Or more simply put, is this fair? Although this issue might be hard to decide whether it's right or not, there should be laws that make the organ transplant process fair.
