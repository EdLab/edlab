Mercy College Reaches Out

abagigah92

September 23, 2013

Mercy College's School of Education in Dobbs Ferry, N.Y has started a new initiative with PS 85, a public school on Fordham in the Bronx to engage parents in public schools. Last fall, Mercy College opened the Bronx Parent Care, a program intended to improve the achievements of students by teaching, training and supporting parents to be very much involved in their children's education. The center provides meaningful support for parents to assist with the social and academic lives of their children. The program stresses the importance of watching and maintaining children's behavior and help parents deal with their children's behavior along with their academics from kindergarten through college. This program has become a service learning project for Mercy College as their professors are donating their time to work and help train teachers. Family-engagement coach Jeannette Diaz at PS 85 has seen an improvement in the involvement of parents, which is showing a positive sign in the academics and behaviors of the students.
