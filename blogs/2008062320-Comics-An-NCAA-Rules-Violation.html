Comics: An NCAA Rules Violation

HSC

June 23, 2008

Check out this article in Sports Illustrated about how the Oregon Ducks coaching staff used graphic novels to lure prized high school recruits to their football program. In addition to giving to giving each of the recruiting targets a superhero name, the staff developed custom comic books, with each recruit leading the way to a National Championship. The tactic proved useful in recruiting several stars for the program (so useful that the NCAA ultimately deemed the practice a recruiting violation). Given all the recent talk about the Channel and video, I wonder what additional investments EdLab should consider making with respect to print media. What would an EdLab comic look like? Who would the heroes be? A comic would be useful for several reasons. First, it could build on several of the ideas raised in the Channel Reboot meeting (i.e., Educator superpowers). It could also be a way to test the viability of an EdLab movie. I think this is particularly relevant given all the movies that are being released based on popular comics like Iron Man and the Incredible Hulk.
