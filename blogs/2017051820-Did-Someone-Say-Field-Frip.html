Rethinking Outside the Box Field Trips

School trips can create lasting impressions for students as they step out of the classroom and into the community. However, teachers may be less enthusiastic about the amount of planning and preparation involved in creating fun excursions for their students. Frip is a field trip planning website that aims to make experiential learning accessible to all students and easier for educators, parents, and educational destinations. More than just for convenience, this tool provides unique field trips in local communities that many teachers may not have previously considered. The site was founded by educators for educators and provides many options for trips in the New York City area.

Browse, Book, Get on the School Bus!

The current version of the site allows users to search for fieldtrips in New York City based on grade level and topic type. For example, a search for Grade 2 science related field trips brings up 15 potential field trip locations with star ratings, reviews, and short descriptions. Once a location is selected, Frip provides additional information including: hours, cafeteria, and wheelchair accessibility. Educators can even browse and book trips from within the Frip site. Each booking link describes how many students can attend, an ideal chaperone ratio, whether or not the experience is guided, and the price of the workshop or event.

The site includes destinations that broaden the scope of what is typically considered an educational field trip. While science museums make appearances on the recommended trip list, lesser-known places such as the Morgan Library that features a collection of historical manuscripts are also included. Connecting to communities is a novel approach Frip takes to field trips.
