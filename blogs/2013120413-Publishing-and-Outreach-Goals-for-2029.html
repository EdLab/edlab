Publishing and Outreach Goals for 2029

Brian.Sweeting

December 4, 2013

*Private* Jo, Kate, Fred, & Brian predict that in the year 2029: 1. EdLab will have instant name recognition; we'll no longer have to explain who we are Little steps:

  • Research automation options for publications
  • Assess current outreach efforts
  • Eliminate inefficient outreach efforts
  • Develop new strategic contacts
  • Integrate EdLab classes (and potential alternative degree?) into TC curriculum program
  • Evolve seminar into a promotion vehicle
  • Define the library the future, model it, share it with the world
Big breakthrough:
  • Go on an EdLab world tour; travel to promote EdLab maker space featuring our products
  • Syndicate our content through big publishers
2. New Learning Times, TCR, NSSE will merge into ONE publication (with automated content sourcing, editing, paper reviewing) that will be THE premiere learning publication Little steps:
  • Automate personalized content recommendations for patrons on entering the library
  • Make publication content more personalized and adaptable to users
  • Develop neural content search
  • Publish digital books that are focused less on academic topics and more on pop culture topics
  • Advertise in other digital publications
  • Advertise in physical spaces (pop up holograms?)
  • Provide geo-location-based news updates to users (via Google Glass)
  • Create a virtual learning tour of NYC
Big breakthrough:
  • Combine all the publications overnight. Deal with it.
