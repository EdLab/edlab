Startup Spotlight: Parse.ly Dash

Kate Meersschaert

February 15, 2012

The Details and Backstory Parse.ly is an NYC-based startup founded in 2009 that provides insights to the online publishing world, and allows publishers to optimize their content for a personalized reading experience. At the helm are two NYU grads who hatched their first project through the DreamIT Ventures incubator and have now launched their newest offering, Dash (currently in beta). Dash is described in their intro video, embedded below, as helping online content providers to "track the performance of your authors, articles and topics to follow trends, page views and traffic patterns." Simply put, Dash creates an analytics dashboard for online content delivery. Unique Point of Difference For a fee, Dash will allow you to open up the hood of your URL and see what content users are reading most and who is accessing this content. Additionally, you can see what topics are trending across the web and across your site(s) and explore these trends as well as analyze the popularity of archived posts. Going beyond Google Analytics to provide topical trending information seems especially valuable for online content. EdLab Relevance and Takeaway As we move forward with our own plans for content delivery through the New Learning Times, it may be helpful to either develop or adopt advanced semantic, analytic platforms that will help us to more clearly define the trends in our sector and either ignore, follow, or build our content off of these existent scaffolds— we don't want to consistently reinvent the wheel. If a tool like Dash can help a team to freshen up (sorry, parsley reference unavoidable!) their content delivery, reduce redundancy, and target their efforts then it might very well be a platform worth exploring.
