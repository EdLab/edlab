Microsoft To Force Org's to Straighten Up

Dan Mallinger

November 24, 2006

The new EULA in Windows Vista has been highly contentious. In short, it allows Microsoft to verify your license at any time and deactivate the product if it is in violation. But largely the discourse has concerned pirating and the fear of Microsoft errors. But never forget the little things that magically happen in around your organization (university or otherwise). Just consider our own office: Anytime we buy a machine, CIS 'images' it with a particular setup. The imaging involves placing a copy of Windows on the machine. Now you might think "no problem," after all the new machine came with a copy of the software. Except, the image from CIS has a different serial than the CD that came with the new hardware. Under this principle, TC could potentially own licenses for 10,000 machines but only use a single license covered for 500 machines (the numbers are arbitrary). And with Vista, every machine could be shut down for this action. I'm keeping XP!
