Students Experience the Complexity of Creating Self-Driving Cars

Rebecca.Sullivan

September 25, 2018


Stanford graduate students got a taste of the complexity involved in developing autonomous vehicles through a recent robot competition. Teams were tasked with programming a robot that could explore a simulated neighborhood and identify animals in need of rescue. Despite their small size, the robots utilized most of the sensors found on self-driving cars.

In addition to fulfilling the project requirements, students could develop extensions to equip their robots with other capacities, such as the ability to react to an unexpected bicycle crossing the road. Do you think projects like this help students develop skills they can use beyond the classroom? Could similar projects be implemented in undergraduate education or high school?
