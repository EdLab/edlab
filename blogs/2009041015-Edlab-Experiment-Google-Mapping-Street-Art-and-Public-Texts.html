Edlab Experiment: Google Mapping Street Art & Public Texts

Doug Beacom

April 10, 2009

Part I: collecting data Today Skye and I met up with Martha Cooper and started our experiment project-- combining my background in google-mapping text in public space and Martha's extensive expertise in photographing these urban artifacts, we were able to cover the following area in about an hour:
View LES - Cooper in a larger map I don't want to give away the highlights (wait for the video!) but it will include sticker art, the Hells Angels and finding a (somewhat) rare French artists' work. While debriefing our data collection (and looking ahead to uploading to Flickr) we discussed sites like Wooseter Collective and how they are different from Flickr--the bottom line: Flickr is being appropriated by street artists to share, comment & collaborate--while Wooster tends to have more of an 'outsider' perspective on this type of representation. Of course, this is all up for debate. Finally, I'll share the google-book link to a book I came across while working on my paper: Lingusitic Landscape:A new approach to multilingualism
