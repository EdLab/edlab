Trends in Ed, 10.9.09

jvazquez

October 9, 2009

In light of our recent seminar on recrafting an institution's online presence, I found this cartoon to be particularly relevant.... Here is a link to the original blog post where the artist talks about how he came up with the story idea. He was inspired by the statement “politics are killing your site,” which was made by Paul Boag in reference to the “harshest truth” about institutional websites (his presentation is embedded below–watch it, he's fun!). His comments are really insightful. I especially liked his points on social media (“contribute regularly, encourage discussion, show personality, make it fun”).

Ten harsh truths about institutional websites from Paul Boag on Vimeo.

So, what can we take away from this? I think Boag is trying to emphasize the necessity for institutions to understand who is using their site and realize why they are making certain design decisions. Sounds simple enough but sometimes the most logical solutions take the longest to reach.
