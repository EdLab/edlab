Event Recap: #NYEdtechDay

Kate Meersschaert

April 25, 2013

Team #edlabrepresents (@megi21, @CuriousBrooklyn & @JaniceSunJoo) journeyed to Pier 92 (50th & 12th) for #NYTechDay, an event billed as the "Worlds largest tech event (read my blog post introducing the event here)." Overall, it was an effective, efficient and entertaining event to attend! Bravo #NYTech day team! Takeaways... We were immediately struck by both the size and organization of the event... 1) Size: Even at 10:30 the event was packed and yet, because it was so well organized we did not feel crowded or overwhelmed. 2) Organization: the organizers made you feel welcome, yet not micromanaged or imposed-upon. Also, organizing the booths based on vertical and field was an effective way to organize 100's of companies! Effective Promotion at an event of this size... 1) Dress-up in a costume (see images below) 2) Give-away food (baked goods & mini cupcakes seemed most popular) 3) Be nice... the unfriendly or bored seeming reps were a MAJOR turn-off 4) Be ready to explain EXACTLY what your product DOES and IS in 30 sec's or less 5) Know your competition... when someone says, "Oh, is X like Y..." you should be able to accurately weigh-in. Promo no-no: Don't charge $3 for a cup of @Keurig coffee... when you have the machines "promotionally" displayed and you have to do all the work of making the coffee. Talk about a marketing miss-fire! @Chobani on the other hand got it right! Be friendly and hand-out free yogurt!
