'Right to Be Forgotten' Law And Google's 'Good to Know' Campaign

Kate Meersschaert

January 26, 2012

Internet privacy and security issues continue to grow and evolve and in the process, children and young people continue to be especially vulnerable. The European Union (EU) recently introduced a "right to be forgotten" and “right to data portability” law that would allow users to delete all personal information from a company's website and services and/or remove personal data and transfer it to another site. These new laws were introduced to protect children and young people in particular, who experts in the EU feels are especially vulnerable and may unknowingly create a lasting digital record that could impact a young person's future employment and life. On US soil, Google recently launched their "Good to Know" campaign across the country, buying ad space in key metropolitan areas like NYC's subways (see my pic below taken this past weekend). Perhaps the US search engine has decided to follow the EU's lead and will provide users with better control of their data? Will Facebook create a similar campaign? Do you feel that this new EU internet privacy law, if adopted, would make it easier for teachers to use social media in the classroom?
