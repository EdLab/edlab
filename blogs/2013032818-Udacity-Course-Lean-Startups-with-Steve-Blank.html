Udacity Course: Lean Startups with Steve Blank

Kate Meersschaert

March 28, 2013

I am at it again... another Udacity course! This time instead of the MOOC that started it all (almost), on Artificial Intelligence (This was technically Stanford's course then), I am going fully terrestrial with Steve Blank's Lean Startup course. I also took Udacity's CS 101 course as well! Listed below are my takeaways so far and here are my course notes (please feel-free to add comments & questions)! Evolution of the Udacity platform: -More streamlined sign-in -Clear course discovery -Clear options for engagement: Wiki, Forum etc. as icons in the top right -Course notes and instructor tips are more clearly laid-out Course Experience -Steve blank has a very upbeat, easy to listen to voice -Lectures are often "animated" by the RSA animation artist -I like that the course can be either "listened to" or "watched" -Quiz's as in the first iteration of the platform, are spaced effectively in chunks after each lesson segments -However, I feel that lessons are TOO segmented (Ex: Lesson #1.5a) -I would describe the "lessons" as "units" instead Final thoughts after lesson 1 So far, so good... for casual learning Udacity is streamlined and easy-to-use! I highly suggest! In just a few days I feel I have learned more about the lean methodology, agile development and customer "discovery" than I have from any book or lecture previously! Kudos Udacity team!
