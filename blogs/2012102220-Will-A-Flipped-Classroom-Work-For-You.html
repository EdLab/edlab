Will A Flipped Classroom Work For You?

jvazquez

October 22, 2012


Jonathan Bergmann is one of the pioneers of the flipped classroom movement, a new approach to teaching and learning. Flipped learning turns instruction upside down: simply put, it takes the attention away from the teacher and places it onto students. Under this approach, students attempt to study a topic by themselves at home (typically using a video assigned by their teacher) and then will apply the knowledge gained the next day in class by solving relevant problems and doing practical coursework.

Jonathan is a strong proponent of the mastery approach; that is, all of his students must achieve a minimum level of understanding by the end of each unit before being allowed to move on. The flipped classroom approach allows Jonathan to differentiate instruction among students; he can spend more time with those who are in need of extra help while students who have a good grasp of the topic can move on to the next lesson.
