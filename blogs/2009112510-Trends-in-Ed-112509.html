Trends in Ed 11.25.09

Jeannie Crowley

November 25, 2009

Author's note: I did my best to avoid today's blog trend of focusing on turkey-related technology information and stick to the basics today. You can search for gravy Tweets here if you really need a fix.
Is it possible to truly control information and charge for it on the Internet? Rupert Murdock believes it is possible but only without Google. Murdock has accused Google and other search engines of stealing his company's information and posting it for free on the Internet. He contends that this undermines his subscription-based publishing model and has discussed the option of pulling sites such as Wall Street Journal from search results. Murdock believes an ad-supported model used by blogs is not really profitable and will not explore that option. In his fight against Google, however, Murdock has left his flank open to bloggers. Once one blogger has a subscription to a source, no one else needs to pay because the information is referenced in his blog (and anyone's blog who links to his blog). This story about crowd sourcing, for example, was posted for free on CNET and cited the Wall Street Journal's paid article as its source. Once the information has been blogged, Facebooked, Tweeted, and re-Tweeted. . .who is going to go to the original source to pay?
