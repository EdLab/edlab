Should Classroom Design Take a Cue from Minimalism?

Kate Meersschaert

May 27, 2014

Image: Carnegie Mellon University While searching for tomorrow's High Five for NewLearningTimes.com I came-upon an exploration of this study by Carnegie Mellon University researchers (originally published in Psychological Science). The piece points to a correlation between highly decorated classrooms and highly distracted learners (in the primary grades - Kindergarteners were the focus of the study). From the Medical Xpress article listed above:

They found that children in highly decorated classrooms (bottom image) were more distracted, spent more time off-task and demonstrated smaller learning gains than when the decorations were removed (top image).
It is unclear whether or not these findings apply to other grade levels, regardless, it may be interesting as we choose (or not choose) what to include as a part of the design of the 4th floor space. Perhaps as we found during our modeling and prototyping sessions that a "white box" theater may be the best choice. What are your thoughts re: possible implications of this research glorifying minimalism in learning spaces?
