NYU's Online Cybersecurity Program Prepares Future Experts

Rebecca.Sullivan

February 21, 2019


Cyber security is a growing field, but because it involves responding to unexpected attacks and changing technology, it can be difficult to teach. Nasir Memon, founder of NYU’s Cyber Fellows program, is convinced that the skills needed to excel in stopping cyber attacks can’t be learned in the classroom. Instead, he and his colleagues have designed a virtual cyber range that students can enter in order to execute and respond to simulated cyber attacks. Through learning by doing, students gain the practice they need to face the uncertain future of this growing field.

NYU’s simulated learning environment gets students close to real-life scenarios from their first day of graduate work. However, their virtual cyber range may not be the only way or the best way to teach the skills needed to stop cyber crime. What is your reaction to the NYU Cyber Fellows program?
