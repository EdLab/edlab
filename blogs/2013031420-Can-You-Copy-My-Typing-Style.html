Can You Copy My Typing Style?

Janice Joo

March 14, 2013


MOOCs are facing an interesting conundrum. One of their primary selling points is that participants can take courses anywhere and at anytime. But when it comes to receiving an official certificate of completion, some MOOC providers are insisting that students go to a physical testing center to verify identification while completing an exam. This, of course, diminishes the convenience of online learning. In an effort to overcome this challenge, Coursera is experimenting with "keystroke biometrics" as an alternative ID verification route. Participants will be asked at the start of a course to show photo ID using a Webcam, followed up with a request to type a brief phrase in order to demonstrate keyboarding patterns. When students hand in assignments, they will be asked to type the same phrase to compare with that initial typing sample. Will keystroke biometrics serve as a reliable alternative to verify IDs?
