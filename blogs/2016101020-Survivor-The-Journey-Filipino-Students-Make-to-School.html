Survivor: The Journey Filipino Students Make to School

Jenny Abamu

October 10, 2016


While most students in America toss around in bed until they are almost late for school, students living in fishing villages in Sorsogon, Philippines travel up to 3 hours through rivers, up cliffs, and around sharp rocks to get to class. "During my first class, sometimes, half of it I am sleeping," says 14-year-old student, Michaela Garil. Yet, one NGO is working to change all of that with the creation of yellow school boats.

These boats act like public buses, picking up students from the fishing islands in the region and taking them to class. So far donations have allowed the nonprofit to build 1,045 boats that provide 11,942 students with transportation to and from school. The journey is free and safe for students, all relieving families of transportation expenses and reducing dropout rates.

The boats have significantly reduced travel time from three hours to 15 minutes. What other benefits might this program offer the community? Share your thoughts in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
