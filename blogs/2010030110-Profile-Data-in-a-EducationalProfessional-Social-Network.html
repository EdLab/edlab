Profile Data in a Educational/Professional Social Network

Jeannie Crowley

March 1, 2010

As we begin to develop the Teaching and Learning social learning management system, I am trying to determine what information is/is not appropriate to include in a university-affiliated social network. A few broad categories that could be included are:
Contact Info (Name, email, phone & website)
Education (This category could be moved above or below professional experience depending upon where you are in your career.)
Professional History
Publications and Works (Links to published articles and other work samples)
Self-Promotion (A category that users could post awards and other information . ..of course it would be renamed.)

One field that is always included in Facebook and other social networks is age. I wonder if this is something we should include in our network. There are a couple of questions to ask before allowing users to set their age:

  • How will this information be used?
  • Is this information important to the overall function of the social network?
  • Could this information potentially lead to ageism?
  • Even if it is optional, will only young members of the network choose to fill out this field? (Leading to an implied age if this field is blank.)
    • Age is simply one of a few categories that could cause more problems than it is worth. I'm not adamantly for or against the field, but I'd like to hear some feedback about it and the proposed categories.
