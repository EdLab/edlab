Palestinians And Israelis Find Peace Through Soccer

Nantwi

October 22, 2014


As recent events have reminded us, the Israeli-Palestinian conflict is one that affects both sides, especially young people. With the region experiencing a youth population boom, finding a long lasting solution to the conflict has gained greater importance. There are several programs in the region to foster understanding and empathy for the now 60-year-old conflict. One of these programs is the Peres Center for Peace, named and founded by former Israeli Prime Minister and Nobel laureate Simon Peres. The Center is using the world’s most popular sport to ensure that the next generation of Israelis and Palestinians has a genuine shot at peace.

As the most popular sport in the world, soccer appeals to almost every demographic. As such, the Center uses soccer’s broad appeal as a platform for Israeli and Palestinian children to build cross-cultural understanding. Despite geographical and political barriers, children and their families from both sides are able to engage in play and conversation on and off the field. Soccer is one of several educational programs the Center has or plans to have for Israeli and Palestinian children and their families. The hope is that the program will offer the children a solid platform on which to build a sustainable peace as they enter adulthood.
