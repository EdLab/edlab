World Food Programme

abagigah92

March 17, 2014

World famine has always been one of the major issues tackled by the United Nations (UN) since its inception. Every year, millions of people all around the world die of hunger. In 1961, the UN formed the World Food Programme to help distribute food to those in need. Overall, this program has been one of the most effective UN programs. It was responsible for feeding over 90 million people in 80 countries just last year alone. One of the strategies the program uses to distribute food is through local markets. In addition to ensuring the food reaches those who desperately need it, this method also improves the local economy. Rapid responses from the program have made it easier to control famine, especially when there is an increase in food prices. Over the last decade, deaths from famine have decrease from the millions to thousands. This is positive and encouraging news and now the big step is to make sure the food people are eating is good for their nutrition.
