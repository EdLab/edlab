Specialty in Core Subjects May Determine Child's Future Economic Status

Khalil Abubakar

June 2, 2013

A study by the University of Edinburg shows children who displayed aptitude in reading and math may reap socioeconomic benefits in the future. Psychological scientists Stuart Ritchie and Timothy Bates state abilities in the math and reading skills may impact an individual's social class three decades later. Data from the National Child Development Study, involving 17,000 individuals from England, Wales and Scotland over a span of 50 years, the Edinburg researchers discovered those with math and reading skills as children had better jobs, higher incomes, and better housing. In my opinion, I don't think this study is necessarily true because most students, especially in the U.S., don't do great in core classes but still go on to have great lives. I think economic success comes down to realizing and mastering your specialty. Earning any job or a degree is not strictly based on just math or reading, it's on other aspects of other subjects you learn during your lifetime.
