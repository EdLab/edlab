New Technology Gives Hope to Handwriting Enthusiast

Jenny Abamu

February 1, 2017


Nowadays classrooms are filled with laptops and iPads, but that trend might change as a tech company just released a new set of smooth, sleek, black notebooks. Not just another laptop, Montblanc’s journal is the notebook of the future, complete with paper and pen. However, this paper is augmented in that you can write down notes and transfer them to any digital device.

Many students claim that their memory is enhanced or their writing flows more freely when they do it by hand. However, written notes get lost, and very few professors or publishers accept handwritten submissions. With the value of handwriting lessons covering topics like cursive under attack, it is no surprise that students who are dependent on writing might find themselves at a disadvantage. That is why the Montblanc synthesis of technology and traditional writing is a welcome answer for those who value writing on paper. The pen is coded so that when pressure is exerted on paper, letters can be identified and transferred to other devices. Though the Montblanc notebook is currently marketed as a luxury item, future classrooms could include this type of paper for everyday use.

Experts have cited increased creativity, memory, comprehension, and literacy as handwriting’s benefits. However, others have rebutted the idea. They note that script enthusiasts’ panic about advancements in technology have been overblown since the introduction of the typewriter. What are your thoughts on augmented paper?
