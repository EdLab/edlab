High Five Clicks Analysis

Christine.Odenath

July 17, 2019

As the only NewLearningTimes.com stream that publishes new articles daily, I thought it might be nice to get some insight into High Five article engagement. In this report I seek to answer questions like...

What is the rate of engagement?

Can we improve performance by putting certain articles at the top or bottom of the list?

Does weekday published correlate with click volume?


Peep the full report here! It's full of pretty graphs and informative tables. It's also pretty long so here are the key takeaways:


What's the most successful High Five post of all February (when we started tracking link clicks) - May (when I finished this report)?

  • Apple Competes to Develop Edtech Tools and AI Reveals Secrets in Ancient Documents by @Melanie
  • Congrats Melanie! Now to be fair, High Five news articles are pooled by all pub team members throughout the week... soo... great teamwork, team!


How many people are reading high five?

  • Since 2017, the High Five stream has received 6.9K actions (clicks into high five and clicks on URLs)


Does the order of a URL (1-5) effect clicks?

  • URLs first in the list earn more clicks on average than URLs in the 2nd - 5th positions. 
  • Whether this is a function of beginning with the most interesting article, or whether people tend to click on the first article then clicks falter thereafter, it’s probably best practice to continue putting your most interesting article first in an effort to maintain audience engagement. 


Does Publish Date/Weekday Matter?

  • All weekday results should be interpreted with the caveat that daily tracking was inconsistent (see pp. 8). For example, there is only one article’s worth of data for Tuesday, but 10 or more articles’ worth of data for Sunday, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday. 
  • Of the 9 URLs that earned more than 10 clicks, 4 of them were published on Friday. 


Does Source Matter?

  • While the majority of High Five articles came from Business, Education, or Tech sources, URLs from science sources earned higher clicks, on average, than URLs from any other sources. 



Thought to Consider: A successful high five article will pull users away from NLT, with the hope that users will return daily for the same experience.
