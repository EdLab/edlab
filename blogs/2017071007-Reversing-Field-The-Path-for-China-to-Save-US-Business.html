Reversing Field. The Path for China to Save U.S. Business

dinosossi2

July 10, 2017


It's an unfortunately trope of global trade that there is a U.S.-China trade imbalance.

However, that may be about to trade due to e-commerce.

China's middle class is expected to expand to 600 million people over the next five years. And due to the global reach of the internet, U.S. businesses and retailers may be able to take advantage.

And the trends have already started.


In 2016, in Chinese e-commerce giant Alibaba's Tmall marketplace, American goods were ranked number two in terms of imported products.

Some of the most lucrative fields seem to be baby products, apparel, products for mothers, fresh foods, electronics, and supplements?

The tentative reason for these trends.

Stricter consumer rights laws that ensure safer products.

Perhaps the impacts of globalization may in fact help everyone: both sellers and consumers.
