Expeditions Pioneer Program

Sarah van den Berg

December 22, 2015


Expeditions Pioneer Program (EPP), is a Google for Education initiative that brings its virtual reality immersive field trips to schools. By donning a Google Cardboard viewer, the class can travel to a coral reef, Machu Picchu, or even the surface of Mars. Google Cardboard and virtual reality experiences have already generated much excitement but schools often don’t have the tools—say, a smartphone for every kid—to make this happen. Google makes this an actual possibility for schools by bringing kits with ASUS smartphones, teacher tablets, routers, and viewers to the classroom in the EPP. Google is currently offering this experience to schools for free, with the possibility of charging once they work out a reasonable cost structure.

Pros:

Expeditions Pioneer Program addresses many of the obstacles to virtual reality field trips by providing all the materials: the cardboard viewers, smartphones, and router that operates the program without an internet connection. Teachers can direct the tour from a tablet, drawing student attention to particular points of interest and pulling up editable notes in the students’ view. Local reviews of participating schools show that students and teachers are enraptured by the experience. For schools without the resources to make many field trips (and really, who has the resources to go to the moon?), the virtual reality field trips allow classrooms to visit over a hundred places, and counting. Google is also working with partnering organizations such as PBS and the Wildlife Conservation Society to add more trips and expand offerings to career exposure by following a day in the life of professionals and virtual college tours.

Cons:

Unfortunately, it’s hard to get a slot on the Expeditions Pioneer Program tour this year. The EPP van is visiting cities in the U.S., Brazil, Australia, New Zealand, and the U.K. Interested schools can sign up for a visit from EPP if the program is coming to their city. If not, they can only watch from afar.

Our Takeaway:

EPP offers stunning visuals, exciting places, and a thoughtfully designed teacher-student interaction. We’re excited to see where EPP goes and for the development of virtual reality field trips.
