Paying someone else to clean up your mess...

Jamison Judd

November 7, 2006

Wired is reporting today on a new startup that will search the web and remove information from sites that may be damaging to your reputation. From the information on the company's site it looks like you don't give them your user name and password... so they search the net for information about you and have it forcibly removed via threatening legal action. I'm wondering how they know what information is about you. Do you have to send them a picture or personal information so they can discern their client from all the other people on the web with the same name? It will be interesting to see if they can get information removed that their client has posted to sites like Flickr, Myspace or Facebook. I'll bet some users are going to wish they more carefully read those terribly long EULAs.
