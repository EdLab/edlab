Let Them Learn

Nantwi

December 21, 2014


For many girls across the world, education remains a distant dream. Cultural, religious, and economic factors often impact girls so they do not have access to the same educational opportunities as boys. When the Taliban failed in their attempt to execute young Pakistani girl Malala Yousafzai, they unknowingly started a global movement to give girls across the globe a chance at education. Yousafzai was targeted because she advocated for a right to an education after the Taliban prohibited girls from attending school in her village in Pakistan. Malala’s attempted execution brought the need to educate girls to the global forefront.

The need to educate children, especially young girls, is a pressing one. At least 34 million adolescent girls are not enrolled in school. In honor of Malala, the Malala Foundation was established to help continue Yousafzai’s fight for educational opportunities for all girls.

What can governments and international organizations do to ensure that all girls have access to education? Join the conversation on Vialogues.
