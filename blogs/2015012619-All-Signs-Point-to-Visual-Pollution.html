All Signs Point to Visual Pollution

Dana Haugh

January 26, 2015


Pittsburgh-based media company Industry Weapon recently unveiled an infographic stressing the importance of using digital signage on campuses to communicate with students (as it happens, the company specializes in digital-signage applications). Schools and universities are encouraged to use these visual, dynamic beacons of information in lieu of their static, print counterparts, claiming 96% of students will notice a digital sign immediately and are more likely to retain the information it displays. Using digital signage will enable campuses to ‘connect with their student body through a medium they use and love.’ But how do you determine which signs are worthy of an electronic glow? Are students really more likely to peruse bright, blinking cues or will digital signs turn into visual white noise?
