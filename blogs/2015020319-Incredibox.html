Incredibox

Alexandra Lederman

February 3, 2015


Incredibox is a visual site for creating, recording, and sharing music. This app is great for music teachers and can be used to introduce children to rhythm and music mechanisms. The user can drag and drop different beats, melodies, voices and effects in the shape of clothing onto an animated dude. There are four versions of Incredibox available to the user once the appropriate software is downloaded onto the computer. Each version of Incredibox begins with an introductory animated clip that builds upon the other versions. A greater variety of sounds are provided with each version. Once the user creates a song or mix, the user can record their creation and share it via social media.

Pros:

Incredibox is simple and straightforward to use. The visualizations of the beats, melodies, voices, and effects as pieces of clothing or outfits makes it easy to understand the combinations of the sounds. Inexperienced music creators can learn which sounds work best together by finding the right combo through a button located on the top right corner. The button changes in stages from transparent to opaque as the right sounds are combined together.

Cons:

Downloading the software to use Incredibox on the web was time consuming and a child would need adult assistance. The visuals and audio sounds may become repetitive and bore the user. The initial "how to" video is helpful, but how to mix the right sounds together is a trial and error process, which may frustrate users.

Our Takeaway:

Incredibox is an easy to use music-creation game. It uses a trial and error process to educate users about which combinations work with simple gamification. Incredibox is a great way for music theory educators and musically curious students to explore music mechanisms and rhythms.
