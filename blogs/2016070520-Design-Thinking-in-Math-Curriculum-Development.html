Design Thinking in Math Curriculum Development

Jenny Shen

July 5, 2016


Oftentimes curriculum development can feel like a stale and exhaustive process, and new ideas can be tough to come up with amidst all the small details that need to be addressed. Math in Common is bringing design thinking to the table to combat this, and math educators are noticing a difference.

Although design thinking itself is not exactly a novel concept, it has seemed to gain traction in curriculum development. Teachers and other education professionals come together to prototype new ideas in their field of expertise, and no idea is ignored in this brainstorming process. Many educators note that design thinking is beneficial for curriculum development because it pushes them to define the problem at hand and ask the necessary questions to find a solution.

Is design thinking an effective way to find the right types of lessons and curricula for students? Is it fundamentally different from other types of curriculum development practices? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
