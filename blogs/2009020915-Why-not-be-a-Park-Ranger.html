Why not be a Park Ranger?

Anthony Cocciolo

February 9, 2009

I came on this page on Simply Hired during my academic job search, which projects salaries for various job categories and organizations. It shows that the average salary at Teachers College is $33K USD. This is slightly above working as a Park Ranger and below a Veterans Service Representative. I wonder how they project this data? It seems like it would necessarily skew lower, because usually jobs that advertise how much they are paying are in the lower rung, where higher paying jobs usually don't advertise how much they are actually paying. It seems like they need some kind of upward estimator to account for this downward skew. I guess that the number may actually be correct though if they account for all the part time workers who work less than full time (it may also account for the enduring popularity of "free food"). They do say on the site that it "allows job seekers to get the information required to make important career decisions and negotiate salary ranges." In regards to TC, it doesn't seem like this page would help too many people. Anyways, it seems like an interesting use of scraping job sites to create on-demand statistics/models. (Or maybe I am just intrigued to wear those cool green shorts and big hat).
