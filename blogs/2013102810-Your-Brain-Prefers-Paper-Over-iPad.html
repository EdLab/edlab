Your Brain Prefers Paper Over iPad

Brian.Sweeting

October 28, 2013

This month Scientific American (@sciam) summarizes research on the effects and effectiveness of reading on tablets vs paper: Why the Brain Prefers Paper It's a fascinating read if you're interested in how the brain interprets text:

"Both anecdotally and in published studies, people report that when trying to locate a particular passage in a book, they often remember where in the text it appeared. Much as we might recall that we passed the red farmhouse near the start of a hiking trail before we started climbing uphill through the forest, we remember that we read about Mr. Darcy rebuffing Elizabeth Bennett at a dance on the bottom left corner of the left-hand page in one of the earlier chapters of Jane Austen's Pride and Prejudice."
