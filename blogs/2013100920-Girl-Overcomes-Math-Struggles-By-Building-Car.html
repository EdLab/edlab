Girl Overcomes Math Struggles By Building Car

jvazquez

October 9, 2013


Even though she isn't old enough to have a license, 14-year-old Kathryn went and bought herself a $450 Pontiac Fiero. It isn't much to look at, and whether it can even drive is yet to be determined, but Kathryn has every intention to fix it up herself and drive it out of the garage when she hits 16. Although she receives guidance and mentorship throughout the process, the motivation to complete the project is hers alone.

This project brings her closer to her scholastic nemesis: math. It allows Kathryn to immerse herself completely in a problem set and solve it from the inside out. When she gets stuck, she finds help for the more technical questions from other Fiero enthusiasts via online discussion forums. Kathryn wholly appreciates the learning opportunities that this project has afforded her. In fact, she feels schools should do more to support kids and their interests by providing similar interactive learning experiences.
