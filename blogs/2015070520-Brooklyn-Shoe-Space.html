Brooklyn Shoe Space

EdLab Studios

July 5, 2015


With American-made footwear accounting for less than 2% of the market today, shoemaking is more or less a lost art in this country; a statistic that concerns New York resident and decade-long shoemaker, Keiko Hirosue. Alongside an assortment of complementary artisan workshops occupying Williamsburgh, Brooklyn, Keikoâ€™s Brooklyn Shoe Space is dedicated to reviving the intricate craft while creating a community of designers, innovators, and mentors. The studio is lined with shelves of colorful foot-shaped forms for molding material known as lasts, tools, and yards of material and is equipped with second-hand sewing machines and other factory-grade gadgets dating back to as early as 1904. These rarities are made available to the interested public at large both through a membership program and educational workshops.

Monthly and hourly memberships offer students the "perfect place to meet like-minded people and to keep yourself motivated and connected while you create" while classes, including the 5 Day Intensive Shoemaking Workshop provide novices with their first, full-on start-to-finish experience. The course takes students from the basics of foot measurement, design, and patterns through choosing and purchasing materials, cutting leather, stitching, and fitting their one-of-a-kind creations to lasts before the final steps of outsole construction. On day five, students walk away with a completed pair of shoes as well as the skills and support system to continue a hobby or even career in shoemaking.
