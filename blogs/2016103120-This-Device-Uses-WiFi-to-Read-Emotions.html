This Device Uses WiFi to Read Emotions

saraehardman

October 31, 2016


Gone are the days when you can hide your feelings behind a poker face. Researchers at MIT have developed a technology that can read your emotions via WiFi signals. The device transmits a wireless signal that bounces off a person’s body and returns. With these signals, the device collects information on heartbeats and breathing rhythms, and then analyzes them. The device has an 87% accuracy rate reporting anger, sadness, joy, and pleasure.

Researchers think this technology could have several applications. For one, it could allow advertisers to relay ads customized to a specific person’s current emotional state. For another, it could connect with a smart house or smart room in the future to create changes in lighting or music. In addition, this technology could have many different learning applications and be particularly useful for children who have trouble understanding their own emotions and those of others.
