Be More Creative By Laughing

Brian.Sweeting

February 5, 2014

As soon as today's Story Pirates seminar wrapped up (I liked the Similes video the best), I stumbled upon a FastCo article summarizing new research in Psychological Science that explores how hilarity inspires creativity. Being in a positive mood can foster creativity because of...

cognitive flexibility: the ability to take old information and adapt it to a new context. In the case of chess, a cognitively flexible player will revise her strategy by responding to her opponent's moves throughout the game, rather than hold to the same linear plan.
I wonder if organizations like Story Pirates could contribute to and benefit from future research exploring whether there are differences in the cognitive flexibilities of kids vs adults and whether kids' learning abilities benefit more from laughter (they certainly seem like funnier humans). In any case, those videos were hilarious and made me want to write more. What do you think -- do you feel more creative when you're in a positive mood?
