Netposse & TLN: Connect with People

fanwar

October 15, 2010

* Private * Want to find out which professors or Edlab folks might share your interest in learning technologies, Cricket, or any other hobby/passion? TLN can help! We've released a prototype version of the Teaching and Learning Network (aka TLN, EdCircles). You can find the site at: http://dev-tln.tc.columbia.edu/tln The biggest feature is its integration with Netposse, a tool that helps users find others who share the same interests in a social network. In addition to this networking support, TLN also allows you to: - Create profile identities for yourself - Communicate with others using microblogging (bulletins). - Connect with people who you share interests with. We will be adding a lot of important features to TLN in the coming months. The goal of TLN is twofold: - Help users in an academic social network develop and leverage new relationships - Empower students while facilitating teachers in the instructional process. As you can see from the site, we haven't really branded TLN. So, in addition to feedback you have about the features and UI, any ideas on a name or tagline would be great. If you already created a Netposse account in the past, please try to sign in with CAS using the email you used for that account. We have preloaded the standalone Netposse biodata in to TLN with Netposse so that you won't have to retype your information again. Finally, thanks to Ankit, Haomin, Ketaki, Jamal, George, Jessica, Chingfu and others for leading different parts of this release!
