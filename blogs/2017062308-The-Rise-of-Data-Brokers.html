The Rise of Data Brokers

dinosossi2

June 23, 2017



In the digital era, more data is amassed about us than ever before.


No big secret.


But the more vexing issues are who has this data? And what do they know about us?

In a recent article, a globe trotting reporter dropped $50.00 to find out the answers to these pressing issues. I don’t want to fully spoil the read, but let’s just say it’s a tad anticlimactic...




In an increasingly digital world, the ability to opt out isn’t an option. We all need a certain base level of communications tools just to say that we are part of the digital conversation. Think email, a smartphone, and at least a minor social media footprint like Facebook or LinkedIn. 


Will there be an ability to ever truly drop out of the digital public sphere? Will the European right to be forgotten ever be extended from more sensationalistic events that we have unfortunately become a part of to the more mundane parts of social life that we just don’t want to share? Or that others want to share about us? Can we wrest control of our digital lives, and the data that constitutes who we are online, from nameless faceless data gatherers whose existence depends on the digital detritus of our very daily existence?


When all the world is a digital stage, can we ever close the curtain and disappear into the backstage darkness?
