Internationality of TC - A Long Tradition

Lu Wang

November 17, 2011

Recently a couple of historical dissertations were retrieved from the TC archive stacks. It can be seen that the tradition of international exchange and diversity of the TC community has been existing for a long time, even before globalization started booming in the modern poli-socio-economic context. The eldest dissertation contained in this archive list can be dated back to the early 1920s. The dissertation list contains: Chien-Hsun Li (1927)  Some phases of popular control of education in the United States Chai-Hsuan Chuang (1922)  Tendencies toward a democratic system of education in China Ching Sze Huang (1927)  Elementary supervision on a county basis by specialized agents in selected states You Kuang Chu (1933)  Some problems of a national system of education in China Henry Hsueh-chang (1923)  The measurement of composition ability Shu-Kuei Carol Chen (1933)  Honors and awards in American high schools Yuntin Cheng Li (1929)  A study of the organization of the American one-teacher school Chiu-Sam Tsang (1933)  Nationalism in school education in China since the opening of the twentieth century Ang Lanfen Lee (1925)  An experimental study of retention and its relation to intelligence Ying Cheng Kiang (1955)  The geography of higher education in China Thoong-sien Foo (1950)  Method in moral judgment: An intercultural analysis Chi-hsien Chu (1950)  A study of the development of Sun Yat-Sen's philosophical ideas You can go to PocketKnowledge to access the abstract and whole text of the above dissertations.
