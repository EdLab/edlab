TC Route Finder Release

Jessica Mezei

April 15, 2010

(Please Keep Private) Many of you have heard of or know about the TCRouteFinder (TCRF), a new interactive map tool which aims to help patrons and visitors of TC navigate around the College. We are excited to announce its internal release to the Edlab staff today. For those who may not be so familiar with what TCRouteFinder is below is a small refresher of it's background, people involved and current stage in the development process. Background: Last year as a part of TC101 the provost had requested that Edlab enhance the student experience when they first join the community. Edlab envisaged an idea of TC maps, wherein a new student can select his/her start and destination rooms to get the directions. TCRouteFinder in short would be google maps for TC. Team: Zhou Zhou, Shaoqing, Jess Mezei, Chandler kang, Pranav, Hui Soo Chae Status: We have completed a refined version of TCRouteFinder which we are initially opening only for Edlab so that we can get valuable feedback which we can use to enhance and fix any minor issues. After which within 15 days we would be scheduling another release where TCRouteFinder would be launched to the internal members of the library staff and thereafter to the wider audience of the TC community. We are really excited that TCRouteFinder can make a valuable contribution in improving the student experience at TC and reduce time and frustrations in navigating the maze of TC. We also need your opinion/findings in identifying any shortcomings you find in this tool which we should address before releasing it to wider audience. So please take the opportunity to test out the application linked below and comment here or approach any team members with feedback and any specific issues that you encounter.
