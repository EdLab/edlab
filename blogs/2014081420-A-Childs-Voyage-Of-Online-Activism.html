A Child’s Voyage Of Online Activism

Yue Fang

August 14, 2014


Historically, Easy Bake Ovens have discouraged boys from using the playful cookware, with their pink color and girls-only commercials. That is until one boy served as the indirect catalyst for change. That boy was 4-year-old Gavyn Boscio, brother of future TEDtalker and activist, McKenna Pope. At the age of 13, McKenna took it upon herself to encourage the ovens’ creator, Hasbro, to start producing gender-neutral cooking toys for the sake of her brother and other boys like him. She started an online petition , gathered 46,000 signatures in about 3 weeks, received interviews from national press, and was invited to Hasbro headquarters to see new unisex toy designs.

McKenna embarked on a voyage of activism and, thanks to social media, was widely heard and eventually made a difference in the world. But her journey wasn’t always easy and smooth as negative commenters nearly overpowered her. She rallied on, though, and gained character and wisdom from the experience.
