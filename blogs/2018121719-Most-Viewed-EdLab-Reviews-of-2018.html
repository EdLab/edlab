Most Viewed EdLab Reviews of 2018

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2018


2018 has been a year of free, interdisciplinary digital learning. Looking for an enriching fireside activity? These learning tools can help boost your media literacy, language and design skills, storytelling finesse, and even job prospects anytime, anywhere, and at no cost.

1. Bad News
Current events and fake news debates have a curious way of sneaking into holiday gatherings. Bad News builds media literacy by challenging players to create and spread fake news. It only takes 20 minutes to play, so share it with the whole family.

2. Beelinguapp
Beelinguapp helps users read in a foreign language. Aspiring linguists and world travelers might want to take advantage of this chance to hone their skills.

3. Lessons From the Screenplay
Some people are happy just watching films; others want to create them. Lessons From the Screenplay analyzes how videos are written and produced, offering viewers insights that can help them become filmmakers themselves.

4. TinkerCad
This online platform makes 3D design accessible to users of all ages and levels of experience. Tinker around with shapes, receive support from lessons, and come away with your very own 3D designs.

5. ImaginePhD
While this online platform is only useful to the small percentage of the population currently pursuing a PhD in the humanities or social sciences, the tool can provide much-needed support and encouragement. ImaginePhD offers graduate students free career exploration and planning, so they can come to the holiday dinner table with an answer to the famous question: "What are you going to do with that?"
