Trends in Online Educaton - 2008 Report

fanwar

June 26, 2009

Here is a report on overall trends in online education that I found useful from Sloan-C. Some highlights that were particularly interesting: - Over twenty percent of Higher Ed students take an online course, translating to about 3.9 million students. - The growth rate for online education (12.9 percent annually) far exceeds that for the education sector as a whole. - Online courses are primarily utilized by undergraduate students. - Public institutions, Associates degree granting institutions and for-profit institutions seem to be the leaders in valuing online educational programs. - It takes time to adopt and thrive with online education: most institutions that are leaders in their online offerings are also the ones that started very early. - Economic changes like recessions and higher fuel costs may drive enrollments in online courses. Whether or not online courses are effective or whether there are some online education strategies that work best, it is clear that adoption of online learning platforms is moving ahead at full speed.
