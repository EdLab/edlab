Rebranding Teaching

Kate Meersschaert

February 3, 2012

Hyperakt a Brooklyn-based design firm with a bent towards increasing social good through design, has set-out on a mission to counteract the often less-than-desirable view of the teaching profession. The firm was asked by WNYC's Studio 360 to develop a series of ads and promotional pieces (see screenshots below) aimed at creating a more scientific, sophisticated image for teachers and the field of education as a whole (see their full presentation to WNYC attached). What role do you think "branding" and design should play in education? In our projects, apps and mission here at the EdLab? New Learning Times? What historic "rebrandings" have had a major effect, either positive or negative, on other disciplines or brands? Think "New Coke", and the Democratic Party's 2010 logo update...
