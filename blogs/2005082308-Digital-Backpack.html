Digital Backpack

HSC

August 23, 2005

Check out this article in Yahoo Finance about Digital Backpacks. There are obviously lots of implications for education as people are able to think of additional features to load into the Digital Backpack. Currently, the product includes: 1) Merriam-Webster's (TM) Pocket Dictionary, 2) a choice of one SparkNotes(TM) Study Guide, 3) classic literature, 4) a free 30-day trial of MicrosoftÂ® Student Graphing Calculator, 5) specialized Web content such as HOMEWORK HUB from Scholastic.com, and 6) Web pages and learning modules created by educators. An interesting design activity might be to prototype a Digital Backpack for a current TC student and a hypothetical EdLab K-12 student. But we should buy one of these things are try it out first. Does anyone want to volunteer one of their kids for an informal experiment? At the very least, the Digital Backback will prevent a generation of humpbacked children.
