A Fishy Aquarium

jvazquez

December 21, 2014


When a child puts crayon to paper and dreams up fantastical scenes of underwater life, you know that the world extends beyond the sheet in front of them. Their imagination takes them to places unseen by most adults (tell me you’ve never had to struggle to interpret the meaning of a scribble). That’s why Japan’s Team Lab aquarium exhibit in Tokyo is the actual stuff dreams are made of.

At the exhibit, kids color various sea creatures and scan them in. That’s when the magic starts to happen. Soon after scanning, their creatures come to life as projections swimming through a giant, room-sized aquarium (complete with bubbly-water sound effects). When a child gets close to the fish, a piece of food appears and the fish gather around it. There are many moving parts of this activity-- drawing, scanning, playing with fish-- that seem to keep kids delighted every step of the way.

It’s clear that the exhibit is fun but is it also educative? Though little clue to this is given in the video, it lends itself to imagining the sort of scenarios that could benefit from paper-to-projection animations. What can you think of? Let us know in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
