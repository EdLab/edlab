How Your Social Media Use Could be Fueling Your Anxiety

dinosossi2

September 1, 2016


Youth who are constantly online are never truly away from their peers. Continual connection is beneficial for those who feel alone, but obsessing over photos of friends having fun when you are in the dumps can be deeply upsetting. A recent study surveyed over 400 adolescents regarding their fear of missing out (FoMO) on social engagements through their Facebook use.

The study found that today’s perpetually plugged in youth who engage in high levels of Facebook use have an increased need to belong that is also associated with a greater need for popularity. In addition, the study found that similar levels of increased Facebook use were also associated with greater FoMO. It appears that adolescent use of Facebook has both benefits and drawbacks for digital youth. Social media use can giveth access to our friends’ social lives but it can also taketh away our sense of enjoyment.

An overwhelming majority of people use social media in all of its many and shifting forms which makes studying this new form of communication important. Networking online via social media also impacts emotional well-being and social capital which are important in the emotionally tumultuous world of youth. Given the growth in social media use, educators need to know about youth social media habits to begin to understand the adolescents who are endlessly entranced by the soft glow of their screens.

Beyens, I., Frison, E., & Eggermont, S. (2016). "I don’t want to miss a thing": Adolescents’ fear of missing out and its relationship to adolescents’ social needs, Facebook use, and Facebook related stress. Computers in Human Behavior, 64, 1–8.
