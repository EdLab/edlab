Set Faces To Stun: Anticipation For Sci-Fi Museum Builds

jvazquez

August 6, 2014


It might be a little hard to recall the theory of general relativity from your high school physics midterm, but who can forget watching Captain Kirk and his Enterprise crew battle black holes and solar flares? Similarly, it may have been Marty McFly and his mad scientist friend Doc that sparked (or fed) your interests in quantum mechanics, and not your droll textbook. And there’s hope for the generations of sci-fi fans to come: the most memorable science lessons from TV and film will live on in a museum appropriately set against the backdrop of Tinseltown.

The Hollywood Science Fiction Museum promises to capitalize on the widespread popularity of the genre by using it to teach real science; that includes robotics, computers, space travel, and the various components of the filmmaking process. Though it’s not slated to open its doors until 2015, its website is chock full of conceptualized renderings of interactive exhibits, special halls like one for classic sci fi cars (the only place where you’ll see the DeLorean, KITT, and 3 Batmobiles in the same room), and a dedicated Learning Center.

What have you learned from your favorite sci-fi films and tv shows? Whovians, Trekkies, X-Philes, and others, share your insights in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
