Trends in Ed: Trimming Teacher Tenure

Fred Rossoff

July 29, 2011

The New York Times reported yesterday that this year, only “58 percent of teachers eligible for tenure received it.” This is as opposed, for instance, 5 years ago, when 99 percent of teachers received tenure. Of course, the vast majority of those who don't receive tenure aren't denied it, but rather kept on probation for another year. This is all in keeping with Bloomberg's “vow” to substantially reform the tenure system. Predictably, the teachers' union raised doubts about the quality of the evaluations, arguing that the system was unclear and inaccurate. Student achievement certainly seems to play a big role, as well as teacher observations by principals, although the teachers' union has argued that when those conflict, this misguided evaluation system goes with the data instead of observations. All told, it does look like we're transitioning into an education system with significantly less job security, although at this point the method seems to be “keep teachers we don't like on probation until they take the hint.”
