US Supreme Court Rules States Can Sell Violent Video Games to Children

Nantwi

June 27, 2011

The US Supreme Court ruled earlier today that states, in this case California, cannot ban the sale of violent video games to children as it is not the job of the federal government to regulate depictions of violence. The case, Brown v. Entertainment Merchants Association, was one of several the Court decided on its last day of the term. The case was a 7-2 decision with only Justices Clarence Thomas and Stephen Breyer dissenting. With educational video games gaining strength among some educators, I wonder what implications this decision will have on makers and consumers of video games in the classroom.
