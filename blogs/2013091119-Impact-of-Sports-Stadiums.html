Impact of Sports Stadiums

Bachir Diarrassouba

September 11, 2013

Sports are a big part of many societies across the world. At times, sports events receive more publicity than other more important happenings in that society. One aspect of sports that is becoming increasingly publicized is arenas and stadiums. A lot of money, usually taxpayer money, goes into building these stadiums. For instance, the Barclay Center in downtown Brooklyn is the newest arena/stadium to open up in New York metropolitan area in the past few years; CitiField, Yankee Stadium, Prudential Center and MetLife Stadium are the others. The construction of the Barclay Center alone was around one billion dollars. This trend is not just exclusive to the New York Metropolitan area as there are 13 cities in the United States that have four or more sports teams. That's billions of dollars going into building new massive arenas. The understanding is that these cities will earn the money back with tourist attraction. I just think that all this money should be used to help fund schools and give teachers a higher salary. The money used to build one stadium could potentially build hundreds of schools in just one city and help fund more important educational programs.
