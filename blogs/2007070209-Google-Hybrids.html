Google Hybrids

Minwoo You

July 2, 2007

Google announced their increasing interests in Hybrid Vehicles and displayed their specially converted vehicles. They went under a special conversion to give them 75 miles per gallon efficiency. Google achieved this endeavor through a partnership with A123Systems to obtain heavy duty extra batteries for the Toyota Prius and Ford Escape Hybrid. This allows the vehicles to drain power from the grid and store more energy into the batteries. Through this conversion, the Toyota Prius goes further for every gallon of gas it consumes. However, the more astonishing development is that they've developed a hybrid vehicle that can drain energy from the grid and return it, when there is a surplus of energy in the battery, back to the grid. This development allows the hybrid vehicles to power the grid at night when the power plants are at a “standby setting”. This will help owners save money because we spend billions a year to put the power plant on standby and maintain the frequency of system at 60 cycles per second. If all cars were Hybrids and we can give back some energy to help the power plant run on standby by the surplus energy of our hybrids, it would save owners thousands of dollars. Another beautiful thing about the Google Hybrid is that it can travel three to four miles on a kilowatt-hour and if that electricity came from natural gas that would mean that only a quarter of carbon dioxide will be released to the atmosphere compared to a gasoline powered car. Bottom line, we save money and save the environment.
