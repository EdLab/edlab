Arianna Choi

February 5, 2009

Is India's $10 Laptop Just a Hype? Last week, we heard about Indian higher education ministry's plans to roll out the world's cheapest laptop called the Sakshat (which means “before your eyes”) in an effort to narrow the nation's digital divide. But now, analysts and reporters are being cautious about its potential for success since it is difficult to build a laptop so cheap but which still has appropriate functions/specifications. For more on this skepticism: India's Sakshat = $20 wireless flash drive - ZDNet Education India's $10 Laptop is no OLPC Killer - Techtree.com, India

An Unconventional University Opens in Silicon Valley Brightest minds and leaders in technology and academics across all disciplines will gather at NASA's Silicon Valley campus for its first class of 30 graduate students this summer. The idea behind Singular University is to ask big questions about humanity in relations to the rapid of technology, and explore ways to ensure that tech improves humankind's problems instead of creating them. Singularity's founders unveiled the school's ambitions plans last Tuesday at the annual Technology, Entertainment and Design conference, known as TED.

Click Play, Watch What We Do at School Chicago public school district has an online news channel called CPS Right Now! which airs a series of videos about initiatives and projects the district is pursuing as well as profiles of new charter schools. With the increasingly heated debate in education sector about school choice, some parents might find this site helpful in their research and high-pressured “school shopping” some choose to do.
