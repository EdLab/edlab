sySTEMic Change Science Edition: A Blood Pounding Simulation!

Demetri Lales

January 9, 2012

Last week's Science Edition explored virtual dissection, and a website that allowed users to learn about the different parts of the human eye by using a virtual scalpel to dissect each part. This week we will a look at a simulation of blood pumping into the human heart on Explania.com. This simulation will allow students to understand how blood flows in and out of the human heart and when oxygen is transfered to the lungs. This simulation is completely two dimensional and very informative, a great resource to introduce younger students to the workings of the human heart. Potential for sySTEMic Change: Learning through simulation is a type of learning that professionals such as surgeons, pilots, engineers, etc. do to become more proficient in their fields. There is not much simulated learning done in primary and secondary education with science and computer labs been the only time for simulated learning. If we want to get more students interested in STEM fields, shouldn't we include more resources like the human heart simulation into the STEM curriculum? Would a student be more engaged in learning about the human heart through a traditional textbook or a simulation of the heart performing its main function? Thinking of ways to keep students better engaged in learning STEM subjects would potentially brighten the future of STEM education, and learning through simulation is a great way for students to learn and keep them interested in the subject. Let's keep simulating, everyone.
