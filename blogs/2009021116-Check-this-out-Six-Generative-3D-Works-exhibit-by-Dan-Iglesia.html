Check this out: Six Generative 3D Works exhibit by Dan Iglesia

Stephanie Hunt

February 11, 2009

Dan's work has been on exhibit since January 19, and I'm just now getting around to blogging to tell you to go take a look... Daniel Iglesia: Six Generative 3-D Works January 19 through March 5, 2009 Daniel Iglesia's computer programs explore the interaction of sound and video to create both interactive and generative works. His newest installation, presented as a Kasser Family Exhibition of Gottesman Libraries, exhibits new works that explore 2-D imagery culled from the web, 3-D (anaglyph) graphics, and responsive interaction to sounds and movements created by the viewer. Iglesia is a recipient of the 2008 EdLab Digital Art Residency (EDAR). EDAR supports the collaborative creation of new educational resources by connecting students and faculty with digital artists. Iglesia is currently a teaching fellow at the Columbia University Computer Music Center. Where: 1st Floor Kasser Family Exhibition Space, Gottesman Libraries, Teachers College, Columbia University All of the project files on display can be downloaded from PocketKnowledge Dan also documented work produced during his residency on his website.
