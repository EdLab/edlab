Forget Wallpaper. Try a Touchscreen Wall Instead

saraehardman

August 1, 2018


Researchers from Carnegie Mellon University and Disney have upgraded the ordinary wall for a high-tech, movement sensing, touch-activated one. What once simply separated rooms now has the potential to be turned into a futuristic canvas. Called Wall++, this new technology is relatively inexpensive and easy to create. The research team applied conductive paint in a diamond pattern on the wall that could connect to electrodes and transmit messages to other electronic devices. A layer of regular wall paint can coat the top, giving it the appearance of a normal wall surface.

The researchers think Wall++ could have many applications in different contexts, from homes to museums to classrooms. Currently, the sensors can be used as a control panel to complete tasks such as turning lights on or off, unlocking doors, or controlling music. It also can track movement within the room, which could make it a potential hit for life-size video games. Because it is so inexpensive to construct, only around $20 per square meter, the researchers see this catching on fast and developing even more functionality in the future.
