For Your Next Interview, Try a Little Character Acting

Meredith Powers

May 26, 2015


Whether you’re a small company in need of a front-end engineer, a media conglomerate in need of a copy editor, or even a school in need of a good teacher, one of the most important qualities to look for in new employees is their capacity for learning. Everyone wants someone who can absorb new information and rise to new challenges, but identifying that quality is difficult in a standard interview. Some creative companies are tackling the issue not through a litany of boilerplate "oddball" questions, but through role-playing exercises that show how a candidate takes—and responds to—feedback on their performance. This method can reveal unexpected insights into a candidate’s learning ability, but does it reliably net the best employee… or just the flashiest actor?
