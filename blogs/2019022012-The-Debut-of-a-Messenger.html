The Debut of a Messenger

jvazquez

February 20, 2019

Recently, some of you may have received a rare message from PocketKnowledge. I say rare because it is the very first mass email ever sent on PK’s behalf. If you counted yourself among the lucky recipients of the inaugural delivery, congratulations! If you aren’t on the list yet, do me  yourself a favor and login to PK for future notifications and announcements.


While the birth of a PK newsletter is indeed a joyous occasion, it’s really a cover for an even more remarkable achievement; that is, the launch of a new, in-house developed email communication system. We call it EdLab Messenger, a centralized service that will allow us to communicate with our users across all of our different platforms.


The true power of Messenger lies in the data it captures with every piece of communication we send out. This post will illustrate Messenger’s current and potential capacities to deliver actionable insights using the recent PK newsletter as an example.*


Targeted Recipients

One much-anticipated feature of Messenger is the ability to segment recipients so that we can send diverse, relevant content to defined groups. The ultimate goal with this is to increase our click through rates; or, to determine how successful certain content types were in luring people to the site.


For the PK newsletter, we created two segments: one for users with a TC/CU affiliation and one for non-TC/CU users.


In the future, we can define these segments even further; perhaps segmented based on department (if they are/were TC students) or based on demographic data we collect during the account creation process.


Delivery Statuses

In Messenger, email-related activities are known as “events.” The baseline event is sent emails.  Of those sent, we know how many were delivered and how many bounced. But perhaps the most exciting stat of all is how many of those delivered emails were opened. Upping the ante from there, we also know how many clicks we got per email campaign! With the power of these numbers combined, we can formulate each campaign’s open and click through rates, important numbers that allow us to measure campaign effectiveness.

Not-Messenger-Related-But-Still-Cool: Campaign Data

There’s an additional layer we add to our outreach messages in the form of campaign links. These are URLs addended with special fields that allow us to track traffic to our sites. They are completely optional but extremely recommended if you want that deep detail. Best yet, any EdLabber can create a campaign link! If you’re interested in tracking any of your work-related outreach efforts, come find me and I can show you how to create and track one. It’s a very simple process.


With campaign links, we can see which links are being clicked, how many times each link is clicked, when a link was clicked, and from where a link was clicked. Most revealingly, we can see what a user did on the site after clicking a campaign link.

TL;DR

From Messenger data we know…

  • Who is getting our emails

  • How many emails were sent, delivered, bounced, and opened

  • What time emails were opened

  • What time emails were clicked

  • How many links were clicked


From campaign link data we know…

  • What time links were clicked

  • What people went on to do after clicking a link

  • The location where links were clicked

  • How many links were clicked (it’s always nice to be able to cross reference)


Future Developments

The PK newsletters are only just the beginning! As we continue to improve the system, we will slowly add more services to the mix (i.e., the newsletters from NLT, TCR, and the Library) until all of our outreach communications are sent through Messenger.
