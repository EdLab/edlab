Plan B for Student Athletes

AbdulMalik

March 4, 2015

Coaches work tirelessly in instilling a "win at all coast" mentality into their players. Even though majority of college athletes have a goal of becoming professional, research shows they have little chance of achieving that goal. Perhaps it might be reasonable for college athletes to make alternative decisions with their career goals. This article suggests that the best alternative career for athletes is work at an investment firm.

According to experts, the competitive nature of athletes fits perfectly with the requirements of workers at investment firms. Working in investment firms requires commitment, time management, high tolerance levels and a "get it done mentality," all of which are qualities that each college athlete is required to have. For these reasons, several managing directors at investment firms have indicated their willingness to hire former student athletes. Will this be a reason for student athletes to consider majoring in finance or other business related fields?
