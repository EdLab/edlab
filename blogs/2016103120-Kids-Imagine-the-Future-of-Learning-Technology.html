Kids Imagine the Future of Learning Technology

Renae Lesser

October 31, 2016


In this Vialogue, a group of young students is asked to imagine what learning spaces will look like in the future. Through pictures and words these kids share the learning innovations of their wildest dreams. The coolest part? Emerging technology has already made many of these dreams attainable.

One student envisions a high tech classroom in which, "If you say, ‘I want to learn about earth’ a 3D picture of earth pops up and tell us what the earth is made of." This idea may sound far out but augmented reality classrooms like the one this girl is describing are already being developed. Another child dreams up a portal that will allow the class to take instant field trips to other continents and even outer space. While physical teleportation isn’t possible yet, a current Google initiative called Expeditions Pioneer Program transports students around the world using immersive virtual reality.

Even the girl who imagines a lunchroom machine that can cook every type of food at the push of a button, might be onto something. After all, there is already a 3D printed restaurant.

Of course not all of these creative predictions are yet within our grasp yet (we’ve been waiting a long time for that space rocket school bus), but what’s exciting about the future of learning is that the possibilities are endless. What do you think the learning spaces of the future will look like? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
