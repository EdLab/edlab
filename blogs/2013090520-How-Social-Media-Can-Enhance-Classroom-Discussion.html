How Social Media Can Enhance Classroom Discussion

Mable Yiu

September 5, 2013


Most teachers lead discussions by starting with an open question, then invite students share their ideas, opinions, and answers within the classroom space. However, this video shows a professor who uses an experimental approach for his in-class discussions. During his lectures, Professor Roloto will ask his students to discuss on Twitter, adding their comments in real-time. With their discussions online, he hopes it will expand to larger audiences around the world. That way, his students can learn from people with different mindsets and opinions, creating a diversity of thoughts on a single topic. His belief that social media should be incorporated into the classroom stems from the simple idea that social media is popular among so many people, so why wouldn’t teachers use it to their advantage?

Professor Roloto isn’t the only one using Twitter for teaching; other teachers are explaining why even kindergartners and elementary school students can benefit from using Twitter in the classroom. They note that learning is augmented when projected into the "real world" through social media and technology.
