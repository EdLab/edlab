How to Win an Election: A New Cafe Book Collection

Jennifer

February 8, 2016

Did you know that Italian master Foppa Vincenzo painted (circa 1464) "The Young Cicero Reading" as part of his commission to fresco the courtyard of the Banco Mediceo in Milan? How apropos the library poster for the new Everett Cafe book collection, How to Win an Election!, on display now through March 15th, the Ides of March -- a date that notoriously marks the anniversary of the assassination of Julius Caesar in 44BC! You'll follow some interesting threads, from the ideas presented in Cicero's own work, Commentariolum Petitionis, translated into English as How to Win an Election, to Kate Kenski's book about the Obama campaign, including how money, media, and message shaped it. As we watch the electoral process and gear up for the 2016 primary, we invite you to ponder the historical culture of power, modern culture of politics, and efficacy of the American media.
