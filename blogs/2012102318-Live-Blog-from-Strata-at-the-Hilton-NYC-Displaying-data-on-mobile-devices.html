Live Blog from Strata at the Hilton NYC: Displaying data on mobile devices

Janice Joo

October 23, 2012

Here we go... **Update 10/24/12 9:18AM: Photos and Summary (below) The seminar was a general introduction to displaying anything on mobile devices. I must admit I was expecting much more about the thought processes behind how data is visualized on mobile devices... but at least the obvious was stated: - Write or display less (remove clutter) - Use a legible font size in static visuals - If tiny font must be used in static visuals, make sure the "main point is legible at the very least" (- Zach, Qz.com) The few but poignant takeaways - A responsive interactive visualization for web that deconstructs into chunks on mobile. LOVE IT. - I talked to Luke (from WSJ) after, and he shared this idea inspired by online comic books: navigating a large image through animations that take the user through different parts of the image (also reminds me of Prezi). Based on last night, I wonder... How do we bring interactivity to our NLT visualizations, for mobile? What data do NLT readers REALLY want to explore? How do we "give a nod" to mobile users of NLT? How do we make clear the benefits of using a mobile publication (as opposed to desktop)?
