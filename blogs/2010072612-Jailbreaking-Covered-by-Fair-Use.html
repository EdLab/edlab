Jailbreaking Covered by Fair Use

Jeannie Crowley

July 26, 2010

Apple suffered a big hit today: jailbreaking iPhones is now protected under fair use. Apple tried to argue the act of jailbreaking an iPhone violated copyright, but the Register concluded that jailbreakers use a minimal amount of code to accomplish the task. Her final ruling:

"When one jailbreaks a smartphone in order to make the operating system on that phone interoperable with an independently created application that has not been approved by the maker of the smartphone or the maker of its operating system, the modifications that are made purely for the purpose of such interoperability are fair uses."
