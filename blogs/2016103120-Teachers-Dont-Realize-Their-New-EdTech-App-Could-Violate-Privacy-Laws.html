Teachers Don’t Realize Their New EdTech App Could Violate Privacy Laws

Jenny Abamu

October 31, 2016


" The California Student Privacy Badge makes it easy for educators to identify privacy compliant products for use in classrooms." (iKeepSafe Press Release)

Data Collection and Cyberattacks

Jaw-dropping cyber attacks on large corporations, politicians, and other segments of the population have called into question the safety of adopting new technological products for students. With many people feeling helpless and vulnerable to the vast technological changes sweeping our workplaces and schools, policy makers and nonprofits are collaborating to create safer technological environments for students.

Knowing Technology is Safe

iKeepSafe is one of the many new privacy quality assurance non-profits that have sprung up in response to this need for safety. The organization assesses websites, products, and apps to assure educators of their compliance with federal and local legislation on privacy. Their assessment focuses on transparency of privacy policies, control of collected information, choice of necessary and option information, security measures taken by companies, and education given to employees on data privacy and security. Companies outline these details on their iKeepSafe profiles and those meeting policy standards are awarded visible badges of compliance, an indicator that iKeepSafe encourages educators to be on the lookout for when selecting new education technology to bring into the classroom.

These privacy regulations highlight the structural adjustments needed for innovative changes to safely transform learning spaces. Though some claim this might slow down the already sloth-like rate of education reform, others applaud the effort as an important safeguard for students.
