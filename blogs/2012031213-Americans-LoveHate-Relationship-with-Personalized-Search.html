American's Love/Hate Relationship with Personalized Search

Kate Meersschaert

March 12, 2012

According this newly released Pew Internet and American Life Project report Americans are split over whether the benefits of personalized search outweighs the privacy risks. Survey results were based on telephone interviews conducted with 2,253 adults (18+) between January 20th and February 19th, 2012. The research overview summarizes the group's findings as follows, "Search engines remain popular–and users are more satisfied than ever with the quality of search results–but many are anxious about the collection of personal information by search engines and other websites." Specifically, 52% of those surveyed felt that "search results have gotten more relevant and useful over time" and yet over 73% are not ok with the collection of their user information to personalize these very same search results. Here is a link to all the questions asked in this study. Can we have our truly anonymous search cake and eat it too? Or, is it too much to ask, to have both a truly relevant, personalized search experience without paying the price of personal data collection?
