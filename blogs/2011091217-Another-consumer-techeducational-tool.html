Another consumer tech/educational tool?

Sharon Hsiao

September 12, 2011

It's always interesting to see how consumer technologies are being utilized in educational settings. For example, Evernote: note-taking, Twitter, Ning etc. All these tools were not originally designed for education, but have been endorsed and been used for education purposes. Fred Rossoff gave a more detail review on one of the recent studies on this trend: Research Digest: Social Networking and Education. There's a new tool, Mightybell, launched by the co-founder of Ning. It's another goal-based site, allowing users to do self-improvement and self-monitoring. The slick interface allow users to view personal analytics as well as to see the progress of others, who are also working toward the same goal and experience. Techcrunch described it as a site to help you “harness the power of baby steps". The tool encourages users to “think big and act incrementally". Isn't it another exciting example we'd like to see in learning and lifelong learning? Personalized learning experience, Open User Model for analytics, possibly Social, absolutely great Visualization.
