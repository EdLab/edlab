I want one.

Ian Toledo

January 6, 2006

In an eSchool News online article entitled Top 10 ed-tech stories of 2005, Part 2 "Nicholas Negroponte, founder of MIT's Media Lab, and his colleagues Joe Jacobson and Seymour Papert unveiled a prototype of the device in November. The Wi-Fi enabled laptops will run on an open-source operating system, such as Linux, and will be lime green in color, with a yellow hand crank for providing electricity, to make them appealing to children and to fend off potential thieves. They are expected to start shipping in February or March, and Negroponte said he hopes to sell 1 million of them to Brazil, Thailand, Egypt, and Nigeria." The yellow handcrank would be handy if there are no electrical outlets and for emergency purposes. May be it could be adapted to be portable so I could attach it to the stationary bike at the gym and store my own potential electric energy. I need more reasons to excersise. Also included in this article are the Google's book scanning attempt, Intelligent Design in the way of science and technology, and vSkool for displaced hurricane victims.
