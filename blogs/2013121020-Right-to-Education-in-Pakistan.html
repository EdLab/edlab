Right to Education in Pakistan

Bismark Appiah

December 10, 2013

Right to Education is the motto for children in Pakistan who are currently out of schools. This phrase “Right to Education” is the motto for the campaign to provide educational opportunities for all children across Pakistan. The slogan also reveals the failure of the Pakistani government to provide equal educational opportunities for all its citizens. Additionally, there is a dearth of resources in schools. In the Vialogues below, the Forum for Human Rights (FHR) reveals the difficulties children in Pakistan are facing to obtain a basic education. According to statistics from the video, about seven million children in Pakistan, who use to attend school, have dropped out. Additionally, 21, 000 schools do not even have school buildings. Classroom sessions are mostly held inside tents or outside an abandoned uncompleted building. This is the struggle that children in Pakistan are facing. Even worse, if a child is not in school, he or she is working to support their family. About 3.8 million children under the age 5-14 work in Pakistan.
