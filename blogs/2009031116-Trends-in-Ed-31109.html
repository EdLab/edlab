Trends in Ed, 3.11.09

jvazquez

March 11, 2009

More gaming in education's future, say futurists (and they would know) The DaVinci Institute is a forward-thinking company that sponsors a weekly “Night with a Futurist” panel. This week panelists discussed the topic, "Next Generation Learning" (Quotes are from this article). Michael Cushman Founder of LearnTheBook and a Senior Fellow at the DaVinci Institute

Cushman then went on to outline a number of the technological advances that may make the vision for a new type of learning possible. Increased and improved computing power including parallel processing, fail-safe designs, and higher speed wireless networks are all important to broad deployment. A better ability to make virtual worlds look and feel more like the real world will also enhance the overall user experience.
Ray Hutchins is the President of the SimGame Exchange and the Interactive Gaming and Simulation Alliance
Hutchins offered hope that as the first generation of game programmers moves towards middle-age with uncertain prospects in traditional gaming, they may start looking to training and learning as an option. Hutchins did note that the $30-50M it takes to develop a quality game remains a significant challenge to development in new arenas.
Chris Sitarzewskisis the Founder of HyperSites.com
Sitarzewski noted that significant adoption of these alternate realities is still “5 to 7 years away,” and cited as a key challenge “getting technology out to people” through wireless broadband, especially in the developing world.
