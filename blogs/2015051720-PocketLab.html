PocketLab

Dana Haugh

May 17, 2015


Self-proclaimed as the "Swiss army knife of science," PocketLab is a bite-sized science laboratory designed for on-the-fly experiments and discovery. This adventure-ready device is loaded with five highly useful sensors including a thermometer, barometer, accelerometer, magnetometer, and gyroscope. PocketLab is both durable and portable, standing up to even the most turbulent experiments while wirelessly streaming real-time data to your portable device. Users are no longer limited to single variable research and traditional time constraints; with PocketLab they can test multiple factors simultaneously in a single trial.

Pros:

PocketLab gives users the flexibility to perform multivariable experiments with one small device. This handheld tool eliminates the need to carry multiple, cumbersome instruments, many of which require connection to an electrical outlet in order to operate. PocketLab is completely cord-free, delivering real-time statistics straight to a phone, tablet, or laptop. PocketLab users also have the option of using the product's cloud server software to analyze, graph, and incorporate data into other experiments. Users can stream live measurements of acceleration, magnetic fields, altitude, pressure, force, and temperature right to their iPad or other device as well as share their findings with other experimenters around the world.

Cons:

The device is not available for shipment until the end of June 2015, so those hoping to use it in the classroom will have to wait until the start of the new school year. PocketLab is not waterproof, so underwater experiments are not recommended.

Our Takeaway:

PocketLab could be a valuable tool for science classrooms from K-12 to higher education. This device is small enough to fit on any object, durable enough to withstand rigorous experiments, and versatile enough complement a range of scientific curricula. This wireless device delivers immediate results directly to an experimenter's smartphone or tablet, ensuring exploratory trial and error are at the forefront of learning.
