What If Teachers Were Designers?

chingfu

December 16, 2013


Teaching can be one of the most creative professions. Teachers are expected to be versatile and resourceful at all times. They have to pitch sometimes dry course content in interesting ways, adjust materials and pedagogies to adapt to various learning needs, learn up-to-date technologies and apply them in constrained environments, and in some cases even find basic supplies and textbooks for their kids. While some enjoy these daily challenges, many more burn out within five years and leave the profession . Most in-between teachers are finding better approaches to address their daily surprises and needs in the classroom. One innovative solution for these educators doesn't come from traditional education consultants but from a less familiar group – design thinking advocates.

Design thinking is a systematic and creative process to generate ideas and design solutions to everyday problems. This approach encourages prototyping, testing and quick failure in order to find better solutions.

Karen, a veteran elementary teacher, finds the designer identity gives her a fresh angle to look at her daily work and helps her and her colleagues find new solutions to daily problems. It also makes her more optimistic about the future of education. What exactly is the magic of design thinking? Is it too good to be true?
