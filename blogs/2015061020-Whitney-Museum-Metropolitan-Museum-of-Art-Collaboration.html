Whitney Museum + Metropolitan Museum of Art Collaboration

Meredith Powers

June 10, 2015


"For our inaugural season at the Met Breuer, we have dug deeply into our own collection and created partnerships to stimulate new scholarship and explore themes that stretch across history, geography, and art forms. Great works of art can transcend both time and place, and our program will powerfully demonstrate that potential." (Sheena Wagstaff, via Met Museum Press Room.)

The Start of a New Chapter
Four years ago, the Whitney Museum of American Art announced its plan to move from their longtime home in the Breuer building on the Upper East Side to a new downtown location. At the same time, the Whitney and the Metropolitan Museum of Art embarked on a partnership, in which the Met will present exhibitions and educational programming at the Whitney’s old building for the next eight years, with the possibility of an extension. Now that the Whitney is comfortably ensconced in its new Renzo Piano-designed "laboratory for artists," the Met has stepped up to fill the Breuer building with its own take on arts and educational programming.

Contemporary Art Through the Lens of History
Starting in March 2016, the Breuer building will open with an exhibition on unfinished works, which will draw partially from the Met’s own collection, as well as from museums and private collections throughout the U.S. and Europe. The exhibition is an exploration of unfinished art, spanning time and media, and focuses on works that highlight incomplete, open-ended, or unresolved moments. "Unfinished: Thoughts Left Visible" raises questions about when works of art (including literature, film, and architecture) are finished, and if they ever are. The exhibition also highlights the remarkable ability of the Met to present a rich historical context for creative works, and foster deep appreciation and comprehension of creative practices.
