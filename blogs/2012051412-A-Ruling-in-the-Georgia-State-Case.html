A Ruling in the Georgia State Case

LauraCostello

May 14, 2012

Judge Orinda Evans has returned a detailed decision in the year-old fair use case which pitted several academic publishers including Cambridge, Oxford, and Sage against library-provided electronic resources at Georgia State. Publishers in the case reckoned that scanned electronic readings for a course amounted to a virtual “coursepack” and should be subject to the same licensing restrictions as bound physical course readings. The judge ruled in favor of the library and found that 95% of readings were in line with fair use and that the library had made a good faith effort to respect copyright in posting reserves. It is expected that the decision will be appealed, but the ruling is very positive for library e-reserves and upholds fair use expectations across the print to digital chasm.
