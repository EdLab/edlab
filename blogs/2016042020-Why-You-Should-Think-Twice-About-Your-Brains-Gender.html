Why You Should Think Twice About Your Brain's Gender

saraehardman

April 20, 2016


We’ve all heard it: men are better at math and maps, women are better at languages and memory recall. Whether you’ve accepted this as fact or not, this Vialogue features a new study that calls into question whether structural brain differences between the sexes actually exist.

After studying over 1,400 brains, researchers at Tel Aviv University have an answer to this question; they assert that there is no such thing as a male or female brain. Sure, there are brains that have more grey or white matter, and brains that have different patterns of connectivity than others, but this study suggests that these differences do not directly correlate to gender. In fact, researchers suggest that viewing specific brain differences as masculine or feminine qualities could be viewed as sexist.

The findings from this study demonstrate the need to abandon thinking about gender in a binary way in neuroscience, as it could lead to misinformation. What gender-specific stereotypes have you heard about the brain? Join the conversation on Vialogues.
