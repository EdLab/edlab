iPad Webinar

LauraCostello

October 21, 2011

(Please keep private) Yesterday Michelle and I attended a webinar around iPad use in libraries in preparation for our library's iPad circulation debut. We had hoped to find information about useful iPad apps for library patrons, copyright issues for library purchasing, and cataloging best practices. It was kind of a mixed bag, but it did put me in an iPad spirit and helped me understand the current role of tablets in libraries. Our copyright woes shouldn't be too crippling considering our debut fleet is only three souls strong, but we did learn about Apple volume purchasing which could allay institutional fears for greater adoption. The most interesting part for me was the descriptions of how tablets and readers are being used in academic libraries today. Virginia Tech has been a leader in tablet lending, the library manages a 144-iPad fleet for full-semester classroom lending and also has 25 iPads for circulation. The iPads appear in their catalog with cute personal names, but the cataloging is very brief. It would be nice to find an example of more descriptive cataloging for these resources, perhaps my further research should be towards e-readers since they have a longer history of inclusion in libraries. I would be nice to have something a little more robust and it will be interesting to manage metadata for such a changeable piece of equipment. Let me know if you have any ideas!
