Makerbot Madness

Meredith Powers

August 1, 2014

EdLab has a new 3D printer, the MakerBot Replicator 2! It's pretty simple to use: just download the free Makerware program and find something you like on Thingiverse. You can drag the .stl file to the Makerware window, where you can position and scale the object, and then save it to the printer's SD card. (The full instruction manual is attached if you want to really get to know your machine.) We're not quite sure what function MakerBot is going to serve in the library as of yet, but please poke around Thingiverse and find some useful or interesting things that we can potentially make! ... The creative possibilities are practically endless, and there are already a lot of interesting, fun, and useful things available to download, such as: An SLR camera and lens (Of course it's not going to have the quality of a regular SLR, but check out all the dreamy, softly-lit photos taken with it!) Bike hacks Adapters, docks, and other accessories Phone amplifiers Vacuum former Desk lamps Bottle & cap Buckles Frame pieces for a DIY drone ... and I could go on, but it's probably better if we all explore and think about some of the potential uses for this new machine!
