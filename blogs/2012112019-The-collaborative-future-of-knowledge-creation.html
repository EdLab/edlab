The collaborative future of knowledge creation

Laura Scheiber

November 20, 2012


According to Carnegie Mellon professor Adrien Treuille, the creation of knowledge has historically taken place at big universities and large corporations. But times are changing and Adrien predicts that future knowledge creation will shift to other settings, especially networks of people on the internet. In this vialogue, Adrien highlights curious and unexpected processes of knowledge creation, such as pre-teens participating in games aimed at protein folding (a pivotal challenge in biology today).

In addition to the knowledge sharing communities that the internet supports, current technologies allow us to filter through hundreds of thousands of people across the globe to find experts of esoteric tasks. Adrien advocates that we apply these technologies to encourage large groups of people with distinct areas of expertise to work together to tackle large-scale social problems. He highlights how gaming has been incorporated into the fold and could play a crucial role in this knowledge creation and problem-solving process.
