Get Lit on the Stage.

jvazquez

April 21, 2011

EdLab alumnae Adrienne Garber and Jess Mezei are making moves with their mobile app, Lit2Quit (developed to help people quit smoking). They are vying for a chance to "Make the Stage" at the ever-popular Internet Week by participating on a panel discussing the implications of mHealth. The panel is entitled, "Mobile apps: Rx for healthy living." They will be joined by another EdLab alum, Nabeel Ahmad. But! They haven't made it yet; they need to get enough votes to get to Internet Week. To cast your vote, follow this link: https://www.internetweekny.com/panels/171 (you will need to go through a painless registration process first) But hurry... voting is only open until tomorrow!

The Stage occurs at Internet Week HQ in Flatiron at 125 W. 18th Street, and is experienced by the conference attendees as well as streamed live over 40,000 times. To date, there have been almost 200 submissions for only 12 "Make the Stage" spots. The spots are in 6 categories (2 each) which include Advertising, Social Media, Entertainment, Mobile, StartUps, and Technology. One submission for the category will be chosen by the two experts and the other will be chosen by popular vote.
