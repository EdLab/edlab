Increasing Caregiver Empathy Using VR

Rebecca.Sullivan

April 28, 2019


Carrie Shaw became a caregiver when her mother was diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer’s. She found that creating tools that caregivers could use to experience the world the way her mother did enhanced their empathy and responsiveness. She began to imagine how to use technology to take medical staff inside the perspective of a patient with Alzheimer’s.

The Beatriz Lab is a virtual reality (VR) training tool that allows users to experience Beatriz’s transformation as her Alzheimer’s progresses. By seeing, hearing, and talking as Beatriz, users gain insight into the frustrations that come with decreased cognitive capacities. The creators envision the program being used by every type of Alzheimer’s caregiver, from doctor to food service worker. They hope that the virtual experience of living with Alzheimer’s will increase caretakers’ empathy and insight into how best to respond to patients’ needs.

Do you think this VR experience can serve as an effective training tool? Are there other situations or ailments that would be beneficial to experience in VR? Add your voice to the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
