Trends in Ed: Tech Savvy Teachers and Their Resumes

Janet Jang

January 30, 2012

There is no doubt that job searching has now migrated from the "classifieds" page in the paper to online postings. Therefore, the development of online resumes or resume websites have been booming across the interweb. This trend is not only for designers who need a platform to showcase their image-heavy portfolio, but for professionals of all careers, including teachers. Teachers are now being encouraged to create their own resume website, complete with a full resume, sample lesson plans, student testimonials and video clips of them teaching students. Here is an example of a teaching resume website of an elementary teacher. Not only does the teaching resume website include the past works and accomplishments, it also shows the potential employer that they are technologically literate and are able to use online tools within the classroom as well. Here is a chart of PBS's recent survey that shows how most teachers have access to computers in the classroom but lack the right level of technology to use them to its full potential. As the future of education become more and more tech-based, teachers have to be trained to deal with not just the content, but the up-to-date tools to deliver that content as well.
