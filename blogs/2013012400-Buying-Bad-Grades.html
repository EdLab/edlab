Buying Bad Grades

abagigah92

January 24, 2013

This article shows that many American college students do not study as much as they should mainly due to laziness. Many students blame professors for their failure instead of working hard to earn their grades and complain about having to do a lot work and participating in class. In a recent study from the University of California led by sociologist Laura Hamilton shows the less money parents pay for college education, the better the student's grades. In her work, Hamilton gathered information that proves that students with more financial support have a lower GPA than those doing work study to help pay for school. This proves the psychological effect students have as they feel money can buy grades. Although parents support in paying for education correlates with a lower GPA, Hamilton's study shows that students with financial support from parents have a higher chance of graduating than those without. Hamilton's study was to recommend parents to set guidelines and requirements for their kids in their academics in order to make sure they do not slack.
