Zoos Are Letting Apes Use iPads

Ryan.Allen

February 17, 2018


Orangutan Outreach is a nonprofit with a mission to protect endangered great apes. The organization helps educate the general public on how these endangered species are under threat because of human encroachment and other environmental problems. They have partnered with zoos across North America for their Apps for Apes project, which gives these animals access to tablets. The project simultaneously raises awareness for their cause while giving researchers valuable insight into how our close cousins learn and play. It turns out, they are a lot like us!

The trainers began by introducing the orangutans to simple memory games to get them used to working with the technology. After learning to properly use the tablets, the apes are able to choose apps to play or videos to watch. Similar to patterns in humans, the younger boys often gravitate towards videos of older, bigger males.
