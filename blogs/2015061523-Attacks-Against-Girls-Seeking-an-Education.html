Attacks Against Girls Seeking an Education

Bismark Appiah

June 15, 2015

According to the United Nations, attacks against girls seeking an education have increased tremendously since 2009. Many girls have lost their lives because they want an education. Across the globe, girls, parents, and even teachers who advocate female education are targets of violence by groups such as the Pakistani Taliban in Peshawar and Boko Haram of Northern Nigeria. These groups have killed and abducted a combined 432 female students. Girls who live in war zones are used for military communications work because of their ability to write, read and do math. Consequently, parents and guardians do not feel secured. Parents no longer see school as a safe environment for their children so they keep their children at home.
