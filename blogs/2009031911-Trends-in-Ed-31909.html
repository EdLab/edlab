Trends in Ed, 3.19.09

jvazquez

March 19, 2009

Chrome:Google as Social learning:The workplace The MASIE Center conducted a survey that assessed the use of social learning technologies in the workplace. The 1,069 respondents were self-selected from “around the globe.” The first question asked about technologies used in the workplace. The most used technologies were corporate collaboration portals (like SharePoint)-57%, peer coaching/teaching-52%, and media sharing (images, video)- 48%. Wikis and blogs followed close behind at 47% and 45%, respectively. At the bottom of the list but still high (in my opinion) was Twitter use at 12%. I wonder how Twitter is being used in these organizations…. The biggest barrier to social learning adoption is organizational culture. This makes sense: if the environment in an organization is such that it stifles creativity and implements control, then processes such as informal and collaborative learning through social technologies are not plausible. Interesting survey... I'd like to see what a retest would look like in a few years months! The science man cometh Eric Larson has taken home the grand prize (of $100,000!) from the Intel Science Talent Search with his project that classifies mathematical objects called “fusion categories”. Eric has been successfully making the rounds at science, math, and engineering fairs for years; I should know-- I am a girl who is hip to her science fair lit!
