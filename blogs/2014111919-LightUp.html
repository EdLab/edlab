LightUp

Dana Haugh

November 19, 2014


LightUp is an electronics construction kit that enables users to learn about electrical currents through trial and error. The basic kit comes with six component blocks and six wire blocks. Through different combinations, young experimenters aim to make the component blocks light up, buzz, or act as a switch. Additionally, users can log on to LightUpâ€™s learning app, an "interactive augmented-reality tutor," to learn more about the fundamentals of electricity and become inspired to create more complex combinations. LightUp encourages young learners to experiment and problem solve on the spot with little or no guidance.

Pros:

The exciting thing about LightUp is that there is no setup. As soon as users open the box they can start making circuits and experimenting with connections. The basic LightUp kit comes with twelve magnetic pieces (six connectors and six component blocks) but there is no end to how many parts can be added and functions performed. If users are stuck, they can open up the LightUp mobile app (available for iOS and Android) and take a picture of their circuit. The mobile app instantly shows the user where the circuit is broken and recommends ways to fix it. If they need inspiration, experimenters can log on to the blog and view other circuits and projects that LightUp users have created. All of the connections are magnetic, making it simple and fun to connect and disconnect each part.

Cons:

Right now, LightUp is only offering a basic (mini) kit for purchase, which contains only six component parts. This may not be an issue right away, but after completing all of the circuit combinations with the basic kit, young learners may become bored with the activity. The LightUp mini kit is $80, which may exclude it from most classrooms.

Our Takeaway:

LightUp is a great starter kit that could inspire young learners to explore their inner engineer. The kit is simple enough, but allows users to understand electrical currents and technology fundamentals in a safe environment. Larger kits will likely keep the attention of young learners for longer periods of time, but the mini kit provides a solid introduction into the world of technology.

The Bottom Line:

Mix and match to light it up!
