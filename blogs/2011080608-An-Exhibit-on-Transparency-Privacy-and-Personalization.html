An Exhibit on Transparency, Privacy, and Personalization

gnatriello

August 6, 2011

The recent posts by Alex and Julia make me think that we should consider a major exhibit on the third floor or the second floor with a goal of educating the college community about the many issues related to changing ideas about privacy, transparency, and personalization. There seem to be a large number of questions to be explored, including: 1 - The growing ease that individuals have with sharing their most personal information online 2 - The escalating efforts to monetize personal information 3 - The ability of the government to seize personal information from online providers, using means that they could not use to get the information directly from individuals 4 - The library community tradition of not collecting personal information on patrons 5 - The history of misuse of individual personal information by fascist governments 6 - The history of abuses of information on individuals by government agencies that took place on the Columbia campus in the sixties and seventies 7 - The growing movement to rely on "free" or "open" sources of information and the accompanying decline of commercial publishers that may leave governments as the last information provider standing What additional issues do you think we could explore in such an exhibit?
