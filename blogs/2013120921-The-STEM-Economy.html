The STEM Economy

rendy

December 9, 2013

The role of STEM employees plays an active role in today's knowledge based economy. Most STEM related occupations require at minimum a bachelor's degree. The author of this piece from the Brookings Institute makes an argument that policymakers, by focusing solely on those with bachelor's degree or higher, are missing out on a large pool of talented STEM workers who do not have a college degree. Since the Industrial Revolution, STEM jobs have increased from 10% to 20% as of 2010. As recently as 2011, there were 26 million jobs that required proficiency in a STEM related field. However, not all is doom and gloom for those still interested in STEM occupations but lack a bachelor degree. Half of all current STEM jobs do not require a bachelor's degree and the average salary for these jobs was $53,000. Most of these jobs are in manufacturing, healthcare and construction. Geographic location also plays a part in STEM jobs for those seeking one but lacking a bachelor's degree. The bulk of the aforementioned jobs are usually available in metropolitan areas. The federal government spends about $4.3 billion on STEM education but yet only one-fifth of that is for sub-bachelor education and twice as that for high STEM level careers. Maybe a shift in funding is needed to prepare non-college educated employers for jobs that are clearly available and could further boost our economy.
