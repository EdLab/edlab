Once Upon a School

Doug Beacom

February 19, 2009

In the process of kicking around ideas for a new series on After Ed (2.0...details coming at our seminar next week!) We've had a few education-focused organizations, people, activists and gurus on our radar. The list includes Once Upon a School, the TED wish of Dave Eggers, explained in short form here: After checking out the site, looking for possible opportunities in my 'hood, and considering writing up my current project with students on the LES, I'm also interested in where we would go with this--EdLab as a whole, and individually as fun, creative, world-changing education enthusiasts. I love the simplicity of the idea: put your skills and interests out there, make contact with your local teachers, approach the situation with humility and grace (I really love the volunteer guidelines..."Be willing to work on a project that the teacher and students really need to complete, such as editing or one-on-one tutoring, and not just on projects that seem splashy to you") and, perhaps most importantly, documenting and sharing-out your experience to inform and motivate other agents of change. Simple! Logistically approachable! Potential for HUGE impact! So the page is at about 200 projects now--is there somehow we could, as a lab, be involved? Is there somehow YOU could be involved? Can anyone here help us identify people already involved (including Dave Eggers?) for a project on AfterEd?
