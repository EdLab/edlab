TC R&W Project 8.18.2009: Digital Literacy (Continued)

Ting Yuan

August 18, 2009

I want to introduce Smilebox, which is a terrific tool for personalized online publishing. Compared with Sophie, which I blogged about before, Smilebox has several differences: (1) the system can "intelligently" display the photos/videos within the book. You can upload all the photos and videos at a time, select the background, and then the system will generate a designed book for you. You can further adjust the design and add texts; (2) While Smilebox doesn't have the function of getting feedback from readers, it offers more publishing formats (e.g., online book, online album, e-card, etc.) with various background choices. It can also be sent out via email or posted onto a blog. You can take a tour by clicking the yellow bar on this page.

The website says that over six million people have downloaded the software, which is free. The process of uploading/inserting videos, audios, and texts as well as linking to the web space makes personal publishing more creative and promising.
Smilebox, Inc., based in Redmond, Washington, was founded in 2005 by Andrew Wright. It is the leader in multimedia expressions technology, and it has raised $7 million in Series B financing. Bessemer Venture Partners led the round and was joined by existing investor Frazier Technology Ventures.
