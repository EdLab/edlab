Worth Motorcycle Company

EdLab Studios

February 24, 2016


On most days, Worth Motorcycle Company looks like an ordinary bike shop. Located in an old carriage house in Crown Heights, Brooklyn, it's home to an assortment of beautiful, vintage European motorcycles, carefully restored by founder Jeremy Malman and his team. But a few days a week, around the time school lets out, a group of kids from the neighborhood show up to work alongside Jeremy and volunteers, as he explains the parts to them and they get their hands dirty, reading manuals and learning the machinery together. The boys are part of a new after-school program that Jeremy is piloting to offer students at a local charter school the opportunity to engage in what is essentially therapy, except it happens around a bike. Informed by research he conducted as a doctoral student about adolescent antisocial behavior, Malman's philosophy is that students can develop people-skills, have trustworthy mentors to dialogue with, and process the troubles they may not otherwise have an outlet to share, in a setting that's laid back, cool, and professional.
