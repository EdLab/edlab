A Reminder to Pursue Your Passion

idrissa10

January 12, 2016

Our education system’s overemphasis on STEM has relegated the arts to the bottom of the academic chart. Today’s job market seems to have no place for those who have majored in the arts. Despite the uncertainty surrounding the arts, two NYU students are firmly banking their futures on it. Evan Bohan, a music major, and Jake Meile, a theater major, are gambling on the arts to ensure a wealthy and successful future. This NPR article focuses on how both these students hope to acquire that future. It also highlights how investing in the arts has helped the two students to become even better students. This is one of the positive outcomes of an arts based education that is seldom talked about. Everyone makes an investment in their future when they decide to attend college. However, a student should never feel like they should are choosing between their passions and what will land them a job.
