Economics & Education: Patent Trolling

Skanda Amarnath

July 26, 2011

This American Life did a great show on patent trolls. Many of us here, including myself, are interested in entrepreneurship and innovation. Yet if TAL's reporting is any indication, the rate of innovation and entrepreneurial endeavor will only stagnate further under the current patent system. TAL profiles the company Intellectual Ventures, that makes its revenue in large part by litigating against those companies that use technologies that fall under the patents IV hordes (and yes, hordes because they do not develop most of the patents they own). The sad fact is that IV makes their money by patent trolling, demanding that companies license their technologies with IV or else face patent litigation. What is worse is that IV is trolling on bad patents that never should have been patented in the first place. They are too general, require little R&D and hardly ever represented the cutting edge of innovation. Ideally, patents should only be given to those technologies that have already been developed and required very high fixed r&d costs relative to the marginal cost of each good. This is especially true for pharmaceutical drugs that have high social value. On the other hand, much of what goes on in the software industry is not high r&d relative to low marginal. The ratio is far more equal and thus not appropriate for patenting. While IV might claim to be creating a liquid market for patents, they are needlessly litigating on undeserving patents and stifling innovation in the process.
