ClassDojo

Dana Haugh

December 21, 2014


ClassDojo is a teaching application designed to encourage classroom participation and communication. Educators import their classroom roster into the application and award points or demerits to students based on their behavior. The application can motivate students to join classroom discussions, make quicker transitions between subject changes, and perform better overall. Teachers choose from a number of "good" or "bad" buttons and can assign them individually or as a group during a lesson with one simple touch. The application also offers integrated teacher to teacher or teacher to parent messaging and analysis for enhanced communication and transparency.

Pros:

The platform is bright, clean, and easy to use. The simple design and familiar navigation menu make this tool immediately accessible to even the most technologically challenged users. Educators can toggle dashboard views to reflect the classroom roster, a performance report, or the messaging function for outside communication. Teachers can easily preload existing student rosters onto the platform and begin rewarding or revoking points immediately; additionally, there is no limit to the number of customized behavior buttons an educator can create. All buttons have an associated image for quick reference and to ensure effective utilization, and all buttons can be awarded on an individual or classroom basis.

Cons:

There is nothing that sets this interface apart from the multitude of teaching aids already on the market. Admittedly, most platforms offer ClassDojo’s features in addition to a host of other useful teaching tools (zondle, for instance). For this reason, ClassDojo’s classroom application is limited. There’s much to be said for simplicity and intelligent design, but in a highly competitive landscape where teachers have the choice between using many, great individual tools or one good amalgamated tool, teachers will likely lean towards the latter.

Our Takeaway:

ClassDojo is best used in a K-6 classroom. The basic interface and funky graphics make this a great resource for elementary school teacher’s looking for a fun and simple way to encourage participation and good behavior in the classroom. Customizable behavior buttons and an integrated email system make this tool useful, but educators who are seeking multi-level functionality should steer clear.
