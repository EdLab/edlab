Center for Digital Research and Scholarship to host "Who Pays For Open Access?" Panel

Adrienne Garber

February 10, 2010

The Center for Digital Research and Scholarship (CDRS) at Columbia University will be hosting a panel discussion on Tuesday, March 9, 2010 regarding business-model options for open-access scholarly journals. Panelists include:

  • Mike Rossner, Executive Director of the Rockefeller University Press
  • Ivy Anderson, Director of Collection Development and Management for the California Digital Library
  • Wim van der Stelt, Executive Vice President for Business Development at Springer
    • For more information about this topic, visit the Scholarly Publication Program website. It provides resources to researchers, authors, and investigators for answering questions such as: (i) what is scholarly communication; (ii) who has to comply with NIH Public Access Policy; and (iii) how do I manage my copyrights. Panel Discussion Details:
  • Date: March 09, 2010
  • Time: 12:30 pm to 2:30 pm EST
  • Location: Alfred Lerner Hall, Room 555, Columbia University
    • For further information regarding this event, please contact Kathryn Pope by sending email to kp2002[at]columbia[dot]edu or by calling 212-851-2856.
