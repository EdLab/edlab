Accepting Defeat in Sports

AbdulMalik

February 9, 2014

Playing any kind of sport requires a certain level of sportsmanship. One major form of sportsmanship that remains vital is being able to accept that in most cases, one might win, lose or tie a game. However, as this article reveals, most youth and amateur athletes have a very hard time accepting defeat when they participate in sports. This is mainly due to the fact that they expect to win the game at all costs. Sometimes young athletes cheat and engage in certain acts that might be regarded as unethical by society just to win a game. When children lose, they are mostly disappointed and sometimes just decide to quit or ask for a "do over'. Personally, I think it might be of great help if coaches and parents help children better understand the dynamics of sports. That is, for children to comprehend the fact that in a game, one is likely to win, lose or tie. I think children will enjoy playing sports more if they understand how losing a game can actually motivate them to work harder for a better result next time out.
