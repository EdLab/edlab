The Copy Machine: A Threat to Privacy?

Jeannie Crowley

April 20, 2010

All digital copy machines built after 2002 have a hard drive that saves every document processed by the machine. Think about all of the personal documents scanned on public machines (like Kinko's) or private machines (like one owned by an insurance company). This CBS video shows how one man gained medical records, social security numbers and classified police files by buying old copy machines. The worst part? The machine makers want companies to pay $500 for the ability to wipe the hard drive.
