What is a Library Without Books?

Brian.Sweeting

April 22, 2015


The public outcry against the New York Public Library's plan to convert its stacks to service space is an indicator of our unease with a bookless future. For many bibliophiles, appreciating the practical convenience of digital documents doesn't assuage their fear of empty stacks. Even as libraries modernize to offer exciting new experiences—digital archives, maker spaces, programming classes, and virtual classrooms—it's hard to replace that feeling of settling down in the stacks for an afternoon with the comfort an old book.
