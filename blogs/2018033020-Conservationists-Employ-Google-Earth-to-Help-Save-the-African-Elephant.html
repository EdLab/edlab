Conservationists Employ Google Earth to Help Save the African Elephant

Ryan.Allen

March 30, 2018


The global ivory trade has devastated African elephant populations, and poachers continue to kill thousands of elephants each year. Rangers, conservationists, and law enforcement agencies have massive organized operations to fight this illegal trade, and building information on elephants and their environment is key to these protection efforts.

Up until recently, little was known about elephant movement and migration. However, through the recent incorporation of GPS into Google Earth technology, researchers have gained real-time tracking capabilities that can easily be shared across their network. Now, communities across the African continent can pitch in to help save this majestic species.

Increasing our understanding of how and why these herds move allows for greater protections against poachers. How can innovations like Google Earth be used in other cases of global conservation? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
