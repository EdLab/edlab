Teaching Robots to Do Chores

Rebecca.Sullivan

September 11, 2018


Watching TV may seem easy, but for a robot, itâ€™s a complex task that involves many steps. Researchers at MIT and the University of Toronto are developing a system to teach virtual agents how to complete chores in a virtual home by training them to complete tasks with step-by-step subtasks. For example, the instruction "watch TV" contains various subtasks: find the remote, walk to the remote, pick up the remote, point the remote at the TV, and press the power button.

The researchers found that the virtual agents could reliably carry out such complex tasks, and soon they plan to train the robots using videos instead of simulations so that the agents can be self-taught using Youtube.
