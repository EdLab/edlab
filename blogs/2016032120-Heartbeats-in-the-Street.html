Heartbeats in the Street

Rebecca Beeson

March 21, 2016


If you live in a city with an over stimulating and arrhythmic energy, the street is probably not a place you go to diffuse your stress. Nor is it a place where you might take time to pause and listen to what's happening in your body.

Pulse of the City is an art installation in Boston by artist George Zisiadis, intended to transform sidewalk culture from a place where people mindlessly rush through street traffic to a place for reflection. Crimson heartbeat monitors encourage participants to grasp the handles and enjoy a song that mimics their heartbeat’s rhythm. Pulse of the City is a small effort that could shape a much bigger movement to call attention to our everyday mental and physical health.

How can art installations that utilize modern technologies creatively advance our knowledge of mental and physical health? Tell us what you foresee in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
