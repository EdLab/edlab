VR Allows Seniors to Dine Around the World

Rebecca.Sullivan

August 13, 2018


Forty percent of adults over age 70 are malnourished or at risk of malnutrition, and Australian researchers hypothesize that loneliness often exacerbates this health problem. Inspired by video conferencing platforms, they have developed a device that uses virtual and augmented reality to transport an isolated kitchen table to an exotic shared dining location.

An immersive spherical screen allows remote individuals to share a meal virtually, and the display can be programmed to the background of the dinerâ€™s choice. Options include a rainforest, a picnic area, and a French restaurant. Do you think technology using virtual and augmented reality can effectively reduce loneliness among the homebound?
