A Retrospective

Nikita.Bogdanov

December 18, 2018


As Kai Ryssdal says, "Let's do the numbers!" But—much like the circa 1920 shot above, courtesy of PK, of the curriculum reading room on the fifth floor of Russell Hall—we're looking back to the whole of 2018.


Because the data are aggregated, and because this is intended as a review rather than as a study, the metrics and my presentation are simple. In fact, there are two main measures that capture usage across the EdLab site, Vialogues, TCR, the library site, New Learning Times, PK, Summon, Rhizr, and the Library App:

  • 122,399 total visits in 2018, as compared to 101,437 in 2017.
  • 262,615 total pageviews, as compared to 382,969 in 2017.

And a few site-specific visit totals.

  • EdLab site: 50,262 in 2018, as compared to 28,453 in 2017.
  • Vialogues: 26,302 in 2018, as compared to 28,452 in 2017.
  • TCR: 15,710 in 2018, as compared to 16,054 in 2017.
  • Library site: 15,559 in 2018, as compared to 14,550 in 2017.
  • PK: 3,868 in 2018.
  • Summon: 2,295 in 2018, as compared to 5,688 in 2017.
  • Rhizr: 1,642 in 2018, as compared to 582 in 2017.
  • Library app: 656 in 2018.

Stop by the front displays for more detail, to see more historical photographs of the library, and to gander at a few archived versions of our online platforms from as far back as 2006. Or, check out the PDF version.


With Thursday being my last day, this is my final contribution to the blog and to the front display project. Thanks so much for your engagement with the whiteboard and with the use data throughout this fall. I appreciated your feedback and questions and enjoyed our uncertain conversations about what it all meant.


All the best in the new year and moving forward.


Nikita
