Vacations Highlight School Segregation

Kevin.Camacho

May 22, 2017

Education is by its very nature supposed to be one of the few things where socioeconomic status and other inequalities don’t exist. However, there is widespread fear that inequalities in education continue to widen and along socioeconomic and racial lines. 


In this powerful piece for Time, author Bliss Broyard uses student’s vacation destinations to highlight the socioeconomic inequalities that persist in our schools. A lot of affluent school districts have been opening their schools to students from low-income families to achieve a diverse student body. We all know the benefits of a diverse student body and its importance in creating different viewpoints that enrich the academic experience. Broyard argues that efforts at desegregation have worsened the inequality gap and she believes Education Secretary Betsy DaVos’ fondness for school choice charter schools will only lead to more segregation.
