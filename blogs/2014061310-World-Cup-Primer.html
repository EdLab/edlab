World Cup Primer

Nantwi

June 13, 2014

The 2014 FIFA World Cup, soccer's showpiece event, kicked off yesterday in Brazil. While most ardent soccer fans wait anxiously every four years for the tournament, non-soccer fans usually just enjoy the sport during the month long tournament. Here is some basic info for non-soccer fans looking to enjoy the spectacle:

  • There have only been eight World Cup winners: Brazil (5), Italy (4), Germany (3), Argentina (2), Uruguay (2), France, England, and Spain. No European team has ever won the title in South America. Brazil are the pre-tournament favorites (Gonzalo might disagree).
  • There are 32 teams in the tournament split into eight groups of four teams. The 32 teams come from all six of FIFA's confederations (Africa, Asia, Europe, North and Central America, Oceania and South America).
  • Each team plays three games in the opening round. The opening round of games can end in a tie. There are three points for a win, one point for a tie and zero points for a loss. The top two teams in each group advance to the round of 16.
  • From the round of 16 onwards (the knockout rounds), every game must have a winner. If the game is tied after 90 minutes, it goes into two 15-minute periods known as extra time. If it is still deadlocked after extra time, the game is decided on penalty kicks.
