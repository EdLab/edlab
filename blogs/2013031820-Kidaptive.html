Kidaptive

LauraCostello

March 18, 2013


Kidaptive is working to produce interactive storytelling apps aimed at the preschool set. Developed in conjunction with Stanford University researchers, the apps apply adaptive technology to a static storyline that is revealed serially in "appisodes." Their first effort, Leo’s Pad, is a cross between a static animated television series and an interactive learning game. Kidaptive’s mission is to provide a home learning app for children ages 3-11 that can be managed by parents using a sophisticated data dashboard similar to those found in modern learning management systems.

Pros:

Leo’s Pad has a general science and invention theme and follows characters named after Leonardo Da Vinci, Marie Curie, and Galileo Galilei. The app is intended to be used with an adult and often asks for parent feedback and participation. Some of the tasks may be too challenging for younger children, so parental collaboration might be necessary to move the storyline forward. Kidaptive’s dashboard has not launched yet, but the goal is to increase parental involvement by providing educational data on time spent, progress, and developmental milestones.

Cons:

At least until the launch of the dashboard, the app seems primarily aimed at entertainment, with lush animation and a mostly static experience with little in-game freedom. Like much edutainment aimed at preschoolers, Leo’s Pad leads participants through the story and only occasionally asks for input. The app is a worthy replacement for static programming and it has earned accolades on parent review sites like Common Sense Media, but Leo’s Pad seems to skew to the fun side of the fun/learning spectrum.

Our Takeaway:

Limiting screen time is recommended for young children, but with increasing technology integration in schools and expanding potential for positive learning, digital comfort and learning before school will become essential. Kidaptive should work to integrate the dashboard with other learning apps, so Leo’s Pad might be a gateway to greater digital learning. Apps like Kidaptive’s and others that allow greater freedom and digital creativity, might soon be essential tools for school readiness.

The Bottom Line:

Kidaptive is working to bring pre-gaming to preschool.
