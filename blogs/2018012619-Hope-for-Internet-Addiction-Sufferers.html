Hope for Internet Addiction Sufferers

DebraLee

January 26, 2018


Internet addiction is associated with the destruction of social relations and linked to a host of psychopathologies: attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, depression, anxiety, paranoid ideation, hostility, interpersonal sensitivity, schizophrenia, and obsessive compulsive disorder. Treatments, such as cognitive behavioral therapy, are effective but none adopt a positive psychology intervention (PI).

This study explored whether positive psychology can enhance interpersonal compatibility, improve the quality of relationships, and reduce the severity of online use in internet addiction sufferers. As an intervention, PI emphasizes improved social communication by showing an interest in human abilities, such as happiness, pleasure, problem solving and optimism, rather than human disabilities and weaknesses.

Researchers recruited college-aged students with internet addiction. Participants were randomly assigned to either Group A, who received PIs, or Group B, who formed the control. Severity of internet use was measured through frequency of use, and addiction rate was calculated using a series of approved tests and scales over one academic semester.

The results showed that group-based positive psychology intervention significantly improved social adjustment, a key component of mental health, in people with addictions and that the more a person engaged in a constructive environment the greater the adjustment, leading to improved mental health. Researchers recommend that future studies explore treatments based on altering lifestyle since addiction is a disorder emanating from disturbed living practices.
