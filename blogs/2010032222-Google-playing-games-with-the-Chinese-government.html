Google playing games with the Chinese government?

Ellen Qian

March 22, 2010

Google declared to shut down www.google.cn earlier this year. The reasons are uncovered attacks and surveillance on google.cn.

Since Google has a strong competitor in China in baidu.com, it has met great obstacles to expand its market. However, most people thought the shutdown of google.cn will lead to another problem: the shutdown of gmail services in China.

In the past couple of months, most Google users have grown increasingly worried about their gmail accounts and busy storing important emails to other servers. However, when Google shutdown google.cn yesterday, the google.cn web page redirected to www.google.com.hk. On the new page, there is a welcome slogan which says "Welcome to the new home of Google in China". Is this a game that Google is playing with the Chinese government or just a temporary place to hold its web page? Is Google going to give up on the Chinese market or will it come back sometime in the future?

I think it will return to the Chinese market with new tools in the future because there is enormous potential in the Chinese market as there are billions of users. In a short time, it is not that easy for those users to find a replacement for Google. As a result, www.google.cn redirecting automatically to www.google.com.hk indicates its future activities.
