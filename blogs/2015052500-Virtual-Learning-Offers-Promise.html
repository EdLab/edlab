Virtual Learning Offers Promise

Henry Adjei

May 25, 2015

For many in developing countries, education stops at the secondary level. This is especially true in sub-Sahara Africa, where tertiary education stands at a startling 6%. The global average is 26%. There are many problems for this: lack of opportunities, and economic and social factors, among others. The African Virtual University (AVU) was established to provide educational opportunities for those eager to continue their education after secondary school. AVU claims it has trained over 43,000 students all across the continent since it was founded in 1997. Professors on AVU use a hybrid of apps such as Skype and WhatsApp with students. As true for most students who enroll non-accredited online courses, there is a very low completion rate. All the usual factors (lack of motivation, unpreparedness, lack of assistance, etc.,) plague students enrolled in ASU.
