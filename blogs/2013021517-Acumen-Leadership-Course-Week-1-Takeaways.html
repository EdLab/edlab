+Acumen Leadership Course: Week 1 Takeaways

Kate Meersschaert

February 15, 2013

Laura Scheiber and I completed the first session of this +Acumen online leadership course (original blog post from a few weeks ago that lays-out the details) and wanted to share our takeaways in relation to our work at EdLab and specifically, ideas to possibly integrate into future LaunchPad initiatives. Visit the +Acumen site here. We are excited to share our learning from this free opportunity from the world's largest social entrepreneurship-focused venture fund! +Acumen Session 1: What we liked: -Not a MOOC -No "gamification"... no gimmicks -Social, real-world interaction -Global -Encourages "meaningful" learning - face-to-face interaction paired on with curated online resources -In light of the challenges with creating online learning communities this seems like an interesting hybrid approach that fosters the "community of learning" -Communicate with others on existing social networks from Twitter to Facebook... we chose Vialogues & plan to share the Vialogue embedded below with the Facebook community to promote the tool as a potential future option. Challenges/Dislikes: -One of assignments in addition to watching the videos embedded below was to read this NYT's article, "The Service Patch" and Laura and I both felt that this article lacked focus and direct relevance. Vialogues of video resources selected for the first "lab" - Laura and I plan to share these resources with the +Acumen Group on FaceBook: More next week!
