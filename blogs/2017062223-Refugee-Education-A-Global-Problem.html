Refugee Education--A Global Problem

Derrick.Cordova

June 22, 2017

The Syrian refugee crisis has dominated all refugee-related news for the past couple of years. However, on the recently passed World Refugee Day, former UK Prime Minister Gordon Brown reminded all of us about other refugee crises and the right to education for all refugee children. Only 51% of refugee children are enrolled in primary school. The global average is 91%. Additionally, only 22% of refugee adolescents attend secondary school; the global average is 84%. Only an additional 1% of refugees youth matriculate in college.

 

Brown notes that over a million Syrian refugees are currently out of school and those lucky enough to be enrolled won’t complete high school. He laments that this may be the greatest education reversal in history. On the other side of the world in South Sudan, another refugee crisis has gone almost unnoticed. Close to a million refugees have fled South Sudan for safety in Uganda, where the government, despite its own share of problems, have been generous in their welcoming of refugees. They have provided access to health services, schools, and other social services.

 

According to Brown, the world needs to do more to help refugees. He notes that 86% of all refugees are hosted in developing countries. We all know developing countries have their own set of issues. It further strains their already limited resources and widens the inequality gap. Brown encourages governments and institutions to develop long-term educational planning strategies for refugees to ensure they receive their right to a proper education.
