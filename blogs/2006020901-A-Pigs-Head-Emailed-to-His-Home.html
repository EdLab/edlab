A Pig's Head [E]mailed to His Home

bhughes

February 9, 2006

Check out the NY Times article on a forthcoming online course/learning platform offered by Sony Pictures. Sony will be hosting a critical discussion of the History of Christianity, movie recommendations for Christians (with Cliff Notes), Jesus: The Man Behind the Marriage, and other interdisciplinary themes... This course, taught by the students themselves, is expected to be popular nationally and internationally--with a raucous emotional and intellectual impact. Several dozen articles will be required reading, as well as the optional attendance of an upcoming summer movie blockbuster. Guest lectures are expected by a handful of renowned personalities. Critics downplay the personal significance of the course. Sidney Sheinberg, the former president of MCA-Universal, which released "The Last Temptation of Christ," the 1988 film by Martin Scorsese, was skeptical that a Web site would satisfy those who found "The Da Vinci Code" insulting to their religion. Perhaps, Sidney, with all due respect, that is not the point.
