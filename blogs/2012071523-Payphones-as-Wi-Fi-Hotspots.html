Payphones as Wi-Fi Hotspots

Daniel Vergara

July 15, 2012

While browsing the web for the latest news, I came across this CNN article about payphones and Wi-Fi hotspots in New York City (NYC). As we all know, cellphones have become a must have gadget for almost every American and therefore, payphones have become almost useless though there are still countless payphones kiosks spread out all over the city. And to my surprise, as the article states, payphones might be making a comeback thanks to NYC's efforts to have a use for it. As part of an initial test, NYC has created Wi-Fi antennas at 10 payphone kiosks for them to be able to still serve a purpose. This is part of a broader plan by the city to make Wi-Fi readily available in as many places as possible. I believe that providing free Wi-Fi to the people using these kiosks is a great way to reuse payphones instead of wasting a great deal of money and time taking them down especially since most people have smart phones and thus the capability to access Wi-Fi. What do you think about this idea? Is it going to be worth it?
