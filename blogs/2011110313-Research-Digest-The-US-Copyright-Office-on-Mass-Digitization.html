Research Digest: The US Copyright Office on Mass Digitization

LauraCostello

November 3, 2011

Office of the Register of Copyrights (2011). Legal Issues in Mass Digitization: A Preliminary Analysis and Discussion Document. United States Copyright Office. Description: This document, meant to act as a baseline for legislative decisions around mass digitization, begins with an in-depth analysis of the Google Books disputes. The emphasis of the analysis is, not surprisingly, on the role of congress in the reasons behind the dismissal of the first case and its implications in the most recent case with HathiTrust. The document draws particular attention to the fact that mass digitization has been a series of bundled issues involving varying roles and involvement of copyright holders and that decisions made about the future of digitization need to consider the flow of information in a digital environment as well as the role that government should play in negotiating digital use. It considers the public/private digitization partnerships and the mass Europeana digitization movement as ways that digitization, especially of public domain resources, has richened the landscape of online information. Finally, the report goes on to summarize the main issues with orphan works and the general licensing models that have been considered. EdLab Relevance: Our digitization efforts have increased in scale and with hopes to monetize Pocket Knowledge, the Google Books dispute is particularly important. Legislation for these issues may take years and we luckily have scores of public domain archival holdings to keep our digitization efforts satisfied before we'll have to move towards orphan works.
