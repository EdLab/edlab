Thanks for attending the (gruelling) EdLab knowledge share

Sharath Gururaj

February 11, 2013

private please Today, we continued the EdLab developer knowledge share with a marathon session of over 6 hours. We discussed the following things. -- local development -- better use of tools like eclipse -- The logging framework -- The exception framework -- version incompatibilities in the various open source toolkits that we use -- The testing framework The meeting oscillated between intense technical debates and occasional but equally intense drowsiness. We were a couple of steps closer to repaying the technical debt that accumulates over prolonged periods of feature development. I sincerely thank Josh, Manav, Nara, Pranav, Yudan and Zhou for sitting through a gruelling day and hope that they will continue to be friends with me after today :) Some of the things I realized that could be improved: -- The session had serious scoping issues. Each topic can warrant a full session (if not multiple) by itself. -- Everyone was not on the same page when the knowledge share started -- Should do a better job of spelling out the agenda, scope and pre-requisites for the sessions Please let me know your thoughts on how it can be improved in the future, and thank you again for attending!
