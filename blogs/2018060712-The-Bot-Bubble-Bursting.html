The Bot Bubble Bursting?

Iyob.Gebremariam

June 7, 2018

Here's a great medium post on why bots haven't managed to replace GUIs as some have anticipated. 


https://medium.com/swlh/chatbots-were-the-next-big-thing-what-happened-5fc49dd6fa61


With recent leaps made in AI and NLP there was a lot of excitement around bots and if they can revolutionize how humans interact with computers. For app developers there would be no need to meticulously build UI if users can just tell their phones what to do.


Unfortunately as the article accurately explains bots are still far from human level competency. Especially when they get it wrong users are bound to demand UI they can control more easily.


On the other hand the author seems to think bots need to mimic human like conversations to fully replace UI. This is not necessarily true in my experience. I don't think people look for human level emotional depth to get things done. I think some would actually prefer the efficiency of blunt conversations with bots as long as they are understood correctly.
