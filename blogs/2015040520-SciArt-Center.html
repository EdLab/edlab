SciArt Center

EdLab Studios

April 5, 2015


Julia Buntaine studied cognitive neuroscience as an undergraduate student and fine art as a graduate student. Today, she’s a neuroscience-based artist in New York City, where she cofounded SciArt Center, an online platform and pop-up event organization that seeks to bridge the gap between artists and scientists. She explains that professions seek answers to similar fundamental questions: Who are we? Why are we here? Where are we going? Yet the opportunities for collaboration and exchange between artists and scientists are rare. This wasn’t always the case, and is due in part to the fact that the deep specialization of disciplines over the years has left less time for cross-disciplinary inquiry.

At a recent pop up event at St. Francis College, artist members of SciArt Center joined biology students in their science labs for an afternoon of cell-gazing, examining cells through a microscope and making art based on them. The purpose? In the words of Buntaine, "What I would love to see is for people like Einstein and Picasso to have known each other. . . I want the Einsteins and Picassos and everyone else today to know each other, because an interdisciplinary view of the world is just a more whole one."
