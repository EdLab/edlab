Boulevard AR Brings the Museum to You

Porsha.Monroe

January 17, 2019


Boulevard AR is an app designed to bring notable works of art closer to its users. After scanning the area around them with their tablet or phone, users may choose to mount a famous painting on a nearby wall, or they may place a virtual easel-supported painting on the ground.

After placing the painting, users interact with the piece, viewing it from multiple angles, flipping it around, or zooming in to take a closer look at specific details. They also explore the piece with the help of narration from the curator of the National Portrait Gallery in London, guiding them through highlights of the artist's life and key features of the pieces appearing on their tablet as though right before their eyes. This app costs $2.99.

Pros:

The ability to place art anywhere is incredibly engaging, creating a sense that works of art can live anywhere, whether it be in your living room, your office, or the middle of your local park. Select paintings gathered from museums, private collections, and arts institutions are featured, which art history buffs and students alike will appreciate.

Cons:

The selection of featured pieces is extremely limited, and without a wider variety of pieces to choose from, Boulevard AR leaves very little for users to explore after two or three uses. If Boulevard AR broadened its content library to include a variety of pieces created by artists from diverse backgrounds, it would offer a more comprehensive experience that could expand the reach of arts education.

Our Takeaway:

Art specific apps like Boulevard AR have the potential to bridge existing gaps, making historical art pieces accessible to any and everyone. Using AR is an innovative way to make this art accessible, removing the barriers that oftentimes exist in formal museum spaces.
