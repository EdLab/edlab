Game Based Learning

Youssef Ballo

January 9, 2015

Video game designers often use excellent methods to make sure people are enjoying the learning aspect of gaming. Game based learning (GBL) is defined as education outcomes and it's also a dynamic way to help educators evaluate learning. It uses competitive exercises so students can challenge themselves or each other to inspire them to learn. GBL intends to balance subject matter with game play and the ability of the player to apply that knowledge learned to real world situations. Games frequently have a visual element that involves players in learning activities through a scenario. GBL contains competition, engagement and reward. Once the learner starts a game, the person does not stop before the game is over. As a reward, players receive victory or points, and feedback to achieve their goals.
