PK Marketing & Outreach Evaluation (NOTES)

Jeff Frank

September 20, 2006

PK Marketing & Outreach Evaluation (NOTES) 1. Commercials, Phil, Posters, etc. a. Measuring student perceptions: Surveys; Measure ‘buzz' (How do students come to PK?); Usage (Who is using it and how often) 2. Orientation/Student Outreach a. Familiarity with PK: How often do students use tutorial, FAQ or ask for support; How often do staff at Library receive questions (What types of questions are they asked); How often do students recommend product to other students and faculty (Measured in 1a) b. Usage, hits… 3. Faculty Outreach a. Faculty usage: How frequently do they upload; Integration with class b. Faculty hits I. Automatic measures: Usage, hits, etc. II. Surveys: Online on PK or in Library III. Make it a topic at a faculty meeting IV. Student Senate integration V. Polling random students in library and at other areas of school Additional Outreach Ideas 1. Integration with online courses; strong effort to online professors, etc. 2. Early adaptor faculty members pushing ideas in class 3. When someone turns on computer in library, they are given PK automatically–this can alternate with Library Homepage 4. Doing research into what Departments currently do and make their current work easier. For example, in the Philosophy Department, we need to share papers in our dissertation writing seminar (this can be done on PK easier than through email); likewise, we send our colloquium speakers' paper to the department via email–it would be easier to do this in PK 5. Outreach to other universities; contact chairs of history of education departments and let them know about our archives 6. Instead of handing out USB drives–when they are requested by patrons–we should have library staff trained to hand out PK card. Likewise, requests for information for our archives can be dealt with in a different way thanks to PK. 7. Merchandise at front desk: Have folders available, give out bookmarks, etc.
