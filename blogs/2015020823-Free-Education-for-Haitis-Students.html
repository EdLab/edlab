Free Education for Haiti's Students

Bismark Appiah

February 8, 2015

Haiti has not been the same country after the devastating earthquake in 2010. Almost everything in the country was destroyed. Many of the country's citizens had to live in tents for months. Children did not attend school because school buildings were damaged. This is one event that will forever be apart of Haiti's history. Though it will remind them of the pain and suffering, it will forever remain a scare in the heart of the survivors and those who witness the tragedy. Sine the disaster, other countries and organizations around the globe have made efforts to help amend the wounds suffered by Haitians. In terms of education, as highlighted, the World Bank and Haiti's Minister of Economy and Finance recently signed a grant of US $24 million from the Global Partnership of Education (GPE). This grant is intended to provide education for 230,000 children across Haiti. Furthermore, the grant calls for tuition waivers and other assistance. Approximately 80% of primary and secondary schools in Haiti are private. This project will benefit a lot of families because children will receive free education and meals, among others.
