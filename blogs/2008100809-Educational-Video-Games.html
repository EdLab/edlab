Educational Video Games

Nantwi

October 8, 2008

A recent article by the NY Times noted that there is an increasing number of authors, teachers, librarians and publishers are using video games to draw children to reading. It seems video games are becoming another arena to foster learning for educators and many schools are exploring how to incorporate video games in the classroom. As expected, there are a lot of critics who question the effectiveness of video games as a teaching tool. They fear that students might skip the reading and use video games as the sole means of learning. Others suggest that video games detract from the experience of being wholly immersed in a book.

On the flip side, some researchers argue that even when children don't read much text, they are picking up skills that can help them thrive in a visually oriented digital world and some educational experts suggest that video games still stimulate reading in blogs and strategy guides for players. The findings in the article will help the video game project that I'm working on with Jess Mezei, Zhou Zhou and Jessica Pang. We are researching and examining the kinds of games that fall into the educational games category with the hopes of developing an EdLab educational video game.
