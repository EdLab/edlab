"Nice Watch"

Elysee Dougan

December 6, 2013

Imagine carrying luggage or doing something which includes both hands and then someone important texts or calls you. How will you know? Unless you constantly check your phone or you're a psychic, you won't know someone texted you. Now imagine the same scenario, but instead you are wearing a little something called a smart-watch. Most smart-watches can sync with a mobile device, preferably a smartphone. These watches can detect notifications, messages, calls or emails that are sent to your phone and send you a message on your watch via Bluetooth. Pebble, one of the best smart-watches, is said to be scratch and water resistant and has a battery life of about a week in a single charge, and most importantly, can tell time. Advances like this in technology opens door to many other creative inventions.
