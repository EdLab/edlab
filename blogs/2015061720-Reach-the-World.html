Reach the World

Alexandra Lederman

June 17, 2015


"Reach the World makes the benefits of travel accessible to classrooms, inspiring students to become curious, confident global citizens." (via Reach the World site)

Traveling Around the World in a Classroom
Reach the World provides a digital platform for classrooms and students to explore the world together. This nonprofit was created to solve two major problems in underserved communities: unequal access to technology and the narrowing of curriculums. The interactive website virtually connects classrooms to travellers around the world to help develop knowledge and thinking skills for responsible global citizens. Reach the World also provides direct programming to school, afterschool, and summer school sites in New York City.

The Roles Within RTW
The interaction on RTW revolves around four key roles: educators, student interns, travelers, and editors. Educators include any teacher from around the world looking to bring the world to their classroom. The program is centered in NYC public schools, so RTW classrooms in this area are also assigned student interns. These interns train to become global educators in public school settings and receive a stipend. Travelers are volunteer college students studying abroad, who share their personal experiences while mentoring and inspiring disadvantaged youth. Editors volunteer on the web, editing travelers' blogs while fact checking and helping to integrate content.

Collaboration is Key
Reach the World not only promotes collaboration among classrooms, educators, and travelers, but also collaborates with other programs to provide the best content and service. RTW partners with the Fulbright Program, Benjamin A Gilman International Scholarship Program, and study abroad programs at universities and colleges. The NY Department of Education granted RTW vendor status, which has allowed the organization to partner with BELL, Citizen Schools, and the YMCA.
