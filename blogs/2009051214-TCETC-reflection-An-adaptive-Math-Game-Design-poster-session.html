TCETC reflection: An adaptive Math Game Design (poster session)

Ting Yuan

May 12, 2009

Math Game.TingYuan.TCETC
View more presentations from tingyuan.
Sunday, May 10, 2009 3:45 PM to 5:15 PM Implications for Math Game Design through Mobile Applications (Ting, Faisal) The slide shows an initial prototype of an adaptive math game current developed by Faisal and I. Through this game, we want to let students grasp concepts of spatial reasoning, mathematical decision making, and multi-digit calculation through previous knowledge of single-digit calculation. Students' math skills will be recognized by the system when they pick and complete the first calculation, then the complexity of the next calculation will be adaptively judged and presented by the system. In general, we got positive feedback from the audience, including people from the Games Research Lab at TC and a game designer from California. Their further suggestions include: (1) make specific meaning for lower graders (e.g., counting); (2) rethink about the "magic" tree presenting choices of numbers; (3) more forms of "challenges" for higher graders (e.g., moving blocks of numbers within the labyrinth). For the next step, we plan to (1) further revise the content of the game for lower graders; (2) try to use Scratch to make a prototype of the game. Please let us know if you have any thoughts, feedback, comments and suggestions for our current version.
