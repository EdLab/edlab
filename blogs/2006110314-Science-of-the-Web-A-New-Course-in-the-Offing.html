Science of the Web - A New Course in the Offing

Stephen Asunka

November 3, 2006

Scientific American reports that a Web Science Research Initiative, headed by Berners-Lee at M.I.T. and Wendy Hall at Southampton's School of Electronics and Computer Science in England, is to start a new branch of science called the science of the Web, because "vast emergent properties are beginning to arise on the Web, and no one is studying how they have blossomed or what they may mean for society." I think they have a point here, especially with the emergence of what they call a Semantic Web i.e. "the global effort to allow computers to recognize not just what online documents are, but what kinds of information they contain and what it might mean." See complete article for the questions they hope to answer with this initiative.
