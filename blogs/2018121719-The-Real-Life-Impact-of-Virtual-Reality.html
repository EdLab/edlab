The Real-Life Impact of Virtual Reality

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2018


Virtual and augmented reality seem to transport us into alternate universes, but 2018 research indicated their real-world relevance. VR and AR are still in their infancy, but initial studies suggest their promise for improving learning in fields as varied as medicine, education, ethics, and sports. Wondering what the new year might bring? These five studies from 2018 offer insight into what the future may hold through virtual and augmented reality.

1. VR-Robotics Takes Snowboarding Training Into the Living Room
This VR experience hopes to rid the world of the scraping whoosh of novice snowboarders butt-sliding down the mountain, taking the snow with them. Practice the key moves in your living room, then hit the slopes for a fun-filled, injury-free day.

2. VR Makes Medical Procedures Seem Less Painful
This study found that children immersed in a VR game while having their blood drawn experienced reduced pain and anxiety. The researchers hope similar tools can be used to distract patients from more serious procedures.

3. Making the Right Choice is Still Hard in VR
In an effort to grapple with ethical decision-making in self-driving cars, this study gauged participantsâ€™ responses to different road situations in virtual reality. It turns out, choosing who to run over and who to spare is a tough call, even in VR.

4. AR Promotes Perspective-Taking in History Class
3D visualizations of historic sites offer students immersive, meaningful experiences of the past. Researchers hope virtual environments can promote historical empathy and help students fit their personal story into the drama of humanity.

5. What Stroke Victims Can Learn Through VR Rehabilitation
Virtual Reality can help stroke victims regain motor function and may make rehabilitation more accessible. However, more research is required to fully understand the promise of VR in this field.
