iPhone App Developer... And 6th Grader.

Janet Jang

November 15, 2011

Thomas Suarez is a 6th grade software developer of the hilarious iPhone game: Bustin Jieber (A Justin Bieber Whack-a-Mole game). In order to encourage more of his peers to make the transition from being an app-user to an app-developer, he started an after-school app club at his school. He talks about how there just aren't enough resources and programs out there for students like him to learn about programming and app making. Maybe the lack of focused STEM education in the early years influences what happens to those students later in life, like in college. The decline in the number of engineering, biology, and computer science degrees being granted can be from 1) Overall loss of interest by students? or 2) Too harsh grading policies? I think it's students like Thomas that tells us that more deliberate and focused technology education is needed in primary and secondary schools. Calculus and American Literature is important, no doubt - however, not only do schools need to figure out how to teach this new generation of students, but exactly what to teach them as well.
