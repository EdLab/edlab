Hacking Video

Jihii Jolly

January 20, 2015

(private) Hi everyone! This past weekend I participated in a video journalism hackathon that some friends put together at Conde Nast. This was the second hacking journo event put together by this crew (the first being last year at MIT Media Lab). Basically, after a series of lightning talks you were assigned to a team of 6-7 folks, including journalists, producers and developers, and set to brainstorm a product that solved some sort of video (creation, consumption or dissemination) problem, and then build it by Sunday. Interestingly, annotated video was a huge topic of interest during brainstorming and a good opportunity to show Vialogues off to our teammates (Diana & I both participated on separate teams). Journo/producers were really into the use case of time-stamped commenting for things like keeping track of fact-checking notes on a video or giving freelancers production feedback. A lot of pretty neat projects came out of the event. My team worked on tmp.RTr.ly.io (not a real site, lol), which basically evaluates the "temperature" of your video by running sentiment analysis around tweets that mention it, and produces a list of top words mentioned along with color and emoji. The problem it was trying to solve was: how can we create cheap/free metadata around video? (Code isn't public yet but I can share it when it is!)
