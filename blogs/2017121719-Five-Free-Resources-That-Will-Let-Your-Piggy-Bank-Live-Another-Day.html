Five Free Resources That Will Let Your Piggy Bank Live Another Day

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2017


While you might expect new educational technology to be exclusively for those with cash to spare, our team has discovered many resources you can use for free. Here are five favorite free products we reviewed this year:

1. I Am Woman: Watch Me Code
Inspired by a Wonder Woman resurgence, this Made w/ Code project features a three-level coding game that can inspire girls and women to fight against gender stereotypes in the tech industry.

2. Nutrition Labels Aren't So Intimidating With This App
Fooducate helps users learn about the nutritional information of different foods simply by scanning a supermarket barcode. This app recognizes that learning to eat healthier is a lot easier when you actually know what’s in the food you’re consuming.

3. A Podcast App Just For Kids
Leela Kids has specially curated content for kid listeners. Divided by topic and age group, this app provides an exciting variety of podcast episodes for kids.

4. Autocoach
Autocoach instructs parents on how to teach their teenagers to drive and prepare them for their license test. It comes complete with driving regulations in all 50 states.

5. New Google Game Teaches Kids How to Be Internet Awesome
Interland is an internet safety game developed by Google to teach kids how to make smart decisions online in a fun way.
