Trends in Ed 11.30.2010: US Students Looking at Colleges Overseas

Nantwi

November 30, 2010

With tuition skyrocketing at some of the nation's top higher education institutions, many students are looking to colleges abroad as a cheaper alternative. The top 20 colleges in the nation, according to US News and Report, costs about $34,000 annually on average while the annual cost for a foreign student at Oxford is around $20,000. According to UCAS, the de-facto company for managing applications to colleges in the UK, more than 3,000 American undergraduate level students applied to colleges in the UK. Though only about half of those were accepted, since 2006 there has been a 27% increase in undergraduate applications from American students. Like most transatlantic relocations, students applying to study in the UK must deal with a change in time zone, weather, and culture, though those brave enough to weather these changes can save as much as $80,000 during their the length of their four year stay in the UK. Though tuition costs is the primarily reason students seek colleges in the UK, industry analysts warn that students should also take into consideration their career options after graduation since many American companies do not recruit overseas.
