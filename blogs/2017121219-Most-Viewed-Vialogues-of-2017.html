Most Viewed Vialogues of 2017

Brian.Sweeting

December 12, 2017


Throughout the past year, our vialogues have highlighted new ways of learning, teaching, and creating in both educational and real-world environments. Here’s a collection of our most popular features this year.

1. An Augmented Reality Lego Set Helps MIT Design Healthier Cities
MIT Media Lab members explain how they hope their new process will help them analyze the impact of design on wellness.

2. Bob Ubell at the EdLab Seminar
The Vice Dean Emeritus of Online Learning at NYU Tandon School of Engineering shares his work on moving the classroom to cyberspace.

3. The Art of Interactive Design
Interaction designer Adiel Fernandez explains how his innovative Collective Wisdom exhibit in the Smith Learning Theater gathers and shares academic knowledge through a new medium.

4. Why the Next Step for Medical School is Virtual Reality
Case Western Reserve Medical School’s partnership with Microsoft Hololens demonstrates how interactive holograms of the human body can revolutionize the medical school classroom.

5. This Supermarket is the First to Use Augmented Reality
This supermarket in Milan uses digital mirrors to interact with shoppers and help them make more informed food decisions based on sustainability and nutritional value.
