EdLab Review: Top Hat Monocle

LauraCostello

March 16, 2012

THM is a BYOT adapted clicker that allows a number of in and out of class interactions through their course portal Monocle Cat.* Teachers are able to sub in created content and students are able to participate in tests, quizzes, and interactive models through their device. Pros: It seems like the system has a lot of created content around math and science topics and teachers are able to request custom content that Monocle will create for free (at least on the teacher side.) It seems like many professors are using it to good effect in very large university lectures. The BYOT flexibility and seamless quizzing with after-class analytics make it a good way to check in on your students when you don't have time to see them individually. It also serves as a de facto attendance-keeping system for enormous lectures, though most professors seem to use this for their own stats rather than participation credits. Cons: THM has a really interesting business model, teachers are able to create free accounts on their site but students have to purchase an access code to their class through the site or through the school's book store. Teachers might feel frustrated at the walled garden of content created by the site, it might be more comfortable to allow users to create and share content with other educators. EdLab Relevance: Clickers have been around for awhile, but the BYOT model is a real advantage for many classrooms today. While the clicker model has limitations, particularly in the kinds of questions teacher can assign to students, surely some participation in mega lectures is better than no participation. Our Takeaway: Top Hat Monocle is an adaptive update on the road to course participation models that are more interactive. *
