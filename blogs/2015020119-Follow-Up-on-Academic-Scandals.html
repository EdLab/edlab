Follow Up on Academic Scandals

AbdulMalik

February 1, 2015

In a recent blog post, I summarized a statement by two former University of North Carolina-Chapel Hill student athletes who criticized the entire athletic department for depriving student athletes of the opportunity to acquire a meaningful education. A recent policy proposed by the authorities at the University of California is meant to ensure that coaches should play active roles in ensuring academic success for student athletes. The policy is very essential because until now there was no financial incentive for coaches to ensure that student athletes were excelling in the classroom. I believe this is a great initiative that will also help reduce "classroom failure" tremendously. However, will the bonuses at stake for coaches ensure more academic success or will it increase the level of academic scandals?
