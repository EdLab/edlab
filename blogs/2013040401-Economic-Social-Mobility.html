Economic Social Mobility

Khalil Abubakar

April 4, 2013

In my sociology class, my professor discussed economic social mobility in America. Social mobility is the movement of an individual from a lower to higher ranking in society or vice versa. In this case how education, your parents or people you know can help you climb the economic leader. For example, if you have a job and you decided to get a degree to help better your wages, you could go from lower class to middle class. Also if your parents are wealthy and in the upper middle class, its obvious you might do better than them or be in the same status as your parents. Knowing people above your economic status is also important because you might have an education and still be in the middle class but when you know a richer person, he or she might give you a better job, which might help you move to upper middle class. This lesson really taught me a lot because an education just guarantees you a job but working harder and keeping in touch with successful people might help you achieve greater things.
