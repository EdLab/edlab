Solitary is the New Expulsion

Dana Haugh

January 20, 2015


Think selling drugs in between classes will get you kicked out of school? Think again. One Colorado superintendent is locking rule-breakers in solitary confinement in lieu of expulsion to decrease dropout rates and retain the district’s accreditation. About 40 students have been placed in "solitary" during the last 10 years, effectively lowering dropouts from 13% to just under 2% last year. Recently, parents have started complaining to the board, demanding the schools remove the doors on the 4x6 rooms and monitor students more closely. Now, these young offenders will be exercised at least twice a day and can eat lunch with the other, law-abiding students.
