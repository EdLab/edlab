Potential Vialogues Research Opportunities

Sharon Hsiao

October 30, 2013

I just read this new meta-review study on Video-based learning. I thought it could be a quick reference for us to think about advancing Vialogues research. Giannakos, M. N. (2013), Exploring the video-based learning research: A review of the literature. British Journal of Educational Technology, 44: E191â€”E195. doi: 10.1111/bjet.12070

  • the number of papers (about video-based learning) has significantly increased during the last years;
  • the focus of the recent studies is on empirical quantitative and mixed studies;
  • there is a lack on studies on secondary education;
  • there is a swift move from social science domains to more applied and technological domains;
  • research with mobile devices has significantly increased during the last years;
  • the video-based learning research was swift to more asynchronous and noninteractive systems.
    • It is a bit surprising to see the trend of more asynchronous and non-interactive systems. There's no doubt that conducting research in this stream in video-based learning should be continued. Perhaps a more theoretical based study, secondary education, or novelty in mobile devices of video-based learning studies are the directions to go.
