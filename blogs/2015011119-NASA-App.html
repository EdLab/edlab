NASA App

Alexandra Lederman

January 11, 2015


The NASA App introduces science lovers of all ages to frequently updated news content and facts about space exploration. There are nine main categories for users to explore and search on the home page including news content, images, videos, mission information, satellite tracking, tweets and Third Rock Radio & TV. This app will increase users' curiosity while providing easily searchable content. The NASA app can be downloaded through the Apple Store for use on iPhones and iPads or through Google Play for Android.

Pros:

The app is visually appealing and hooks the user into exploring and searching the content. Constant updates of NASA news, tweets, images, and videos keep the user in the loop of the newest discoveries and findings. Users can control their feeds based on subject. The app is linked so that users can easily share content via email and social media. The dynamic site content including photographs, video, and text lets a wide range of users explore the app in the medium most comfortable to them. For instance, the NASA videos category is made up of short, easy to understand, and visually captivating clips great for a younger audience.

Cons:

Much of the written text and vocabulary might be hard for younger users to read and understand. The sheer amount of content may also be overwhelming for some users.

Our Takeaway:

The NASA App is great for users looking for regularly updated NASA and space news. The app is interactive and has content that will keep users engaged. The photographs and videos are eye-catching and easy to understand due to the smaller amount of text and the short length of the videos. Educators and students can use the NASA app to instigate interest and exploration and also to support deeper understanding. This app is recommended for older elementary students and higher.
