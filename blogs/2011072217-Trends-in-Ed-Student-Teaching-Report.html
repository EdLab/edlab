Trends in Ed: Student Teaching Report!

Fred Rossoff

July 22, 2011

Yesterday the National Council on Teacher Quality, released its report evaluating the quality of student-teaching programs nationwide. Student-teaching, for those of you not in the know, is like an internship for prospective teachers, where, as part of their education, they go out and get experience teaching in a classroom. Already the (fairly critical) report has taken a lot of flack, particularly by those institutions who feel they have received poor ratings for essentially arbitrary reasons. The report argues that many student-teaching programs fail to monitor or supervise student performance, and furthermore fail to account for the needs of schools today. Instead, they train too many elementary school teachers, creating a glut on the market in that particular sector, and a shortage of trained teachers for other roles. The National Council on Teacher Quality is, it appears, part of the Gates-funded push to introduce more market and test based reforms on the education sector. I don't see Teachers College mentioned, so it must have done pretty well!
