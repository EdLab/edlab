End of Computer Labs?

gnatriello

May 29, 2008

An article in The Chronicle reports on a virtual computer lab developed at North Carolina State University. The lab provides computer lab-like facilities using a bank of servers that students connect to over the network. A follow-up piece provides additional detail and some reader reactions. The implication for our work at the library is that we might want to consider such virtual service delivery mechanisms as a way to provide more powerful resources for students and faculty. To the NC example I would add the possibility of locating any equipment (servers, etc.) with an external service provider so that we can maintain better service with no use of campus space.
