Sports and Education

Bismark Appiah

January 8, 2014

One of the greatest things about sports is its ability to bring people from all walks of life together. As an athlete, I can attest to this testament. Non-athletes and fans might view sports as a leisure activity or watch for entertainment purposes. However, sports, especially youth sports, can teach young people skills and practices that can serve them well in school, their personal lives and subsequent professional life. Sports teach young people commitment, punctuality, discipline, teamwork, and perseverance, among others. These “soft skills” help young people prepare for life in the work world. One of the knocks on young people in so far as employers are concerned is their lack of work experience. In this op-ed, the author notes that encouraging young people to participate in sports will help build these “soft skills” that will serve them well in their search for employment. With global youth employment (16-24 year olds) around 75 million, new strategies and ideas needs to be implemented. I believe creating different sports programs in developing countries, where youth unemployment is extremely high, will be beneficial for youth because they will be able learn these soft skills. By learning these skills, youth might be able to land employment. Additionally, sports can also help promote school attendance and enhance young people's confidence.
