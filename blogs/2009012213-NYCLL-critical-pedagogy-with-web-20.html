NYCLL: critical pedagogy with web 2.0

Doug Beacom

January 22, 2009

re: http://nycll.wordpress.com/ In addition to AfterEd videos, I'll be plugging along on my MA project for the International Education Development department. My concentration is in 'literacy, language and technology'--so I've designed a project that will work with students outside of classroom parameters, but with the technologies they actively use in and out of the classroom. You might agree, there could be a lot of overlap with AfterEd and EdLab on this project. Students will be have a series of workshops on: the meaning of linguistic landscape (reflecting on the connection between text in public spaces and the power relations of that space), using google maps, media formatting, ethics and safety and sharing/reflection. My goal is to learn equally from the students and their skills with web 2.0 tools while at the same time trying to scaffold and facilitate a project where they feel comfortable to think and act outside the box, critically reflect on their surroundings and produce high-quality media. An introduction page and short video can be found here: http://nycll.wordpress.com/ A google document page is published at: http://docs.google.com/Doc?id=ddc354b6_47469mdphj this includes notes for the lead-up to this project as well as production notes and links. I'd love any comments, notes, suggestions or inquiries! ddb2113@columbia.edu
