Trends in Ed, 2.11.10

jvazquez

February 11, 2010

The talking pen Say hello to India's first talking pen: The Multimedia Print Reader (MPR). It features a built-in camera (which detects the code), speakers, microphone for recording, 2GB flash memory, USB connectivity for downloading audio files and a Lithium Ion battery. When the pen is placed over a book with MPR codes, it activates preloaded audio files so that the user can listen to the text as they read it. "The tool will foster a self-learning process, specially in the rural areas, encouraging the students to work independently in the absence of teachers, this would heighten results even in a poor student-teacher ratio [in India]." source This pen will be useful not only to burgeoning readers, but also those with visual and/or learning disorders. What I found interesting was that the MPR-enabled books are published at no additional printing cost. Currently it's being sold at Rs 7000 (conversion plz?) throughout India. Additional source
