Trends in Ed, 2.13.09

jvazquez

February 13, 2009

Happy Friday the 13th! #Education Do I blog about Twitter too much? I may have a slight obsession. Regardless, a new PEW study reveals that as of December 2008, 11% of adults who use the internet admitted that they microblogged to report their status or to keep up with friends. This is up from the 9% who reported microblogging in November, and the 6% reporting in May. So what does this have to do with education? The report goes on to say that 19% of online adults ages 18-24 have used or are using Twitter (and the like). Twitter and ed actually make pretty good bedfellows: Keep up to date with ed news at twitter.com/education, send reminders to students, or use it to keep in touch with fellow educators. The Internet of Things The “Internet of Things” is a very interesting concept. It's a wireless network that connects objects, much in the same way that the World Wide Web connects addressable web pages, except that it connects addressable inanimate objects. Mir:ror is an example of this new technology. A simple wave of an object in front of a USB-attached mirror will automatically open up apps and programs. The mirror reacts to a colorful “ztamp" (RFID tag) that has been placed on the object. This user codes each ztamp with instructions. I recommend you PhD students code several ztamps to open up your dissertation and stick one on everything–maybe some of you could use the motivation! iPhone App-a-Day WritingPad allows you to forego typing in favor of tracing letters on the soft keyboard. It takes some getting used to (for example, spelling the word “look” might turn out as “lok”)but so far users have enjoyed how well it works. It's free at the App store!
