Unmasking the Decline of American Higher Ed Institutions

Janice Joo

January 31, 2013


According to the Times Higher Education World University Rankings, 76 American higher education institutions sit in one of the top 200 spots, globally. It may seem like American schools are doing well overall, but a closer look at each school will reveal that more than two-thirds of these schools have individually dropped in rank, with more dramatic declines down the list. Six other institutions have maintained their spots, and 20 have risen in the charts.
