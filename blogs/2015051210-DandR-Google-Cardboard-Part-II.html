D&R: Google Cardboard Part II

LauraCostello

May 12, 2015

Today we're going to finish construction and get started doing app research! The questions are reprinted below from Meredith's post on our first Cardboard D&R: ACTIVITY #1: Investigation! - Inspect the materials, is this technology sustainable? scalable? globally shareable? - Observe the construction process, what is the age/knowledge range needed to make this? - Where and how could this technology be used for learning? Our decorative supplies today are a little limited, but there's plenty of inspired Cardboard creations out there to help you turn Cardboard into a platform for art as well as VR! Next week, we'll explore apps to use with Google Cardboard! We came up with a list of apps that we thought fell into the educational sphere (related to literature, history, arts, culture, science, technology, etc.) but we don't want to color your perceptions or narrow the field too much. Other learning spaces? ACTIVITY #3: Use & Post-Discussion! - Did you look at a lot of apps, or did you focus on just a few? - Do any apps stand out as particularly great or terrible? - Which apps could be used in a classroom? What about informal learning spaces? Why? - What are some drawbacks to using this technology? - Are there any apps that we should cover for EdLab Review?
