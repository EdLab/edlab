Fertility and Education

abagigah92

November 20, 2015

As populations continue to grow elsewhere, the birth rate in Africa has seen a drastic decrease. From 1990 to 2010, the birthrate dropped from 6.2 birth per woman to 4.9. Many believe this change will help the continent's economy by enhancing schooling and other development goals. This has caused a split among development scholars. Those who believe that the decrease in birth rate will improve the overall economy argue that fertility decline enhances education, which will spur growth. They believe it will decrease the incidents of pregnancy dropouts, class sizes, and number of children in households. On the other hand, opponents argue that there is not enough correlation to conclude that the drop of fertility in Africa will improve the education system and economy. The most important factor to improve Africa's schools and economy is the right infrastructure and social structures.
