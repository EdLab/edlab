Walking In Their Shoes

Nantwi

September 14, 2014


Though our globalized world has made it easier for individuals from all corners of the earth to connect with one another, our differences sometimes serve as barriers or sources of conflicts. The effort to foster empathy and build cross-cultural understanding lies at the heart of the Global Lives Project. The project is an initiative that draws photographers, educators, designers and filmmakers, among others, to capture and explore the everyday lives of individuals across the world.

Global Lives captures simple everyday moments such as eating breakfast or spending time with family members to highlight the fact that despite our obvious differences, there are many things that bind us together as humans. By capturing individuals doing everyday things, the project shows that the human experience simply has no boundaries. The collection of videos serves as a platform for educators and others to use as a starting point in conversations about culture, history and politics. By capturing the human experience, the initiative aims to open a dialogue about our differences with the hope that it will lead to "empathy and cross-cultural understanding".
