Meet ER-D2 the Humanoid Disaster Response Robot

DebraLee

January 8, 2018


ER-D2 can search through crumbled buildings and is designed for recoveries during natural disasters, like earthquakes, hurricanes and tsunamis. The robot is strong, nimble and can sustain buckets of rain without exploding, as this video shows. But itâ€™s still a prototype and a way of achieving optimal functionality, when it becomes fully useful.

By then ER-D2 will enjoy three-dimensional movement enabling it to climb stairs and vertical ladders and transition between ladders and steps. It will also be able to move in narrow spaces, over pipes, absorb contact while moving through debris, and get up after a fall. For now ER-D2 can walk at 1.2 miles per hour, step over an eight inch pipe, clamber over debris, progress through an inch of rain for 20 minutes, and climb up and down a vertical ladder.
