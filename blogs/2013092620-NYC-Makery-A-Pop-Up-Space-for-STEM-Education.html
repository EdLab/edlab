NYC Makery, A Pop-Up Space for STEM Education

Mable Yiu

September 26, 2013


The NYC Makery defines itself as a "pop-up Makerspace, a movable venue where youth and adults are encouraged to be curious, to tinker, to experiment, and to make with technology." The Makery was formed not only to help students do well, but also to inspire them to become interested in STEM subjects. The founders are working on finding a permanent space for people to visit, but for now, they will continue to "pop up" in different communities to influence as many kids as possible.

This video introduces current projects happening in the Makery. One day, students may create their own video game; another day they may experiment with a 3-D printer. By combining technology and creativity, the students not only learn from their teachers and peers, but also on their own when creating different tools. Giving them these experiences may inspire them to continue to build and design things on their own, because, believe it or not, learning can be fun.
