Born to Learn

Rebecca Beeson

February 14, 2016


The ideas of Inquiry-based learning and fostering creativity in school have moved to the forefront of education discourse. And now children have more resources than ever to stimulate creative skills and transform their ideas from concepts to creations. Students today are traversing the world with LEDs as nightlights, 3D printers in their libraries, and coding classes taught alongside geometry. Programs outside of school day learning provide kids with MakerSpaces and extracurricular activities that train them how to adapt materials in their tech world. When has it ever before been true that children could watch the stream of code illuminating their video game sidebar while chasing the zombie they designed?

After watching this Vialogue on the potential of young minds, what other ways do you think we can foster creativity?.

Excerpts from the discussion:

@02:04 elisha.brazil: We have evolved to learn by looking at things from different perspectives and making connections between things, and we do that through play. So important when thinking of fostering learning.
