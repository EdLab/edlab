The Locker-Less School

Dana Haugh

December 19, 2014


Lockers may soon become a thing of the past. Secondary schools in North Carolina have already stopped issuing these lockable closets to students, claiming they cause unnecessary hallway congestion and safety violations. As many classes no longer require physical textbooks, school administrators are leveraging the shift to digital materials to make the case for widespread locker removal. But some parents are concerned about the potential for weighty backpacks if a student happens to land a teacher who insists on using physical materials. Add a laptop to the equation and congested hallways could resurface in the form of hundreds of students slowly crawling along, burdened by their heavy technology.
