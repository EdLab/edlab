Learning Chemistry in Virtual Reality

saraehardman

July 10, 2017


Virtual reality (VR) lets you travel to the world’s greatest sites and to the most imaginative environments, and now it even lets you explore the smallest particles in existence. MEL Chemistry VR is a virtual reality chemistry class where kids interact with molecules and atoms in an immersive way. When kids put on the VR headset, they are transported to a futuristic classroom, where they can then zoom into objects on the molecular level. Like other cutting-edge startups, MEL is making VR into an educational experience.

Currently, MEL offers over 100 lessons in K–12 chemistry, and they plan to add more in the next year. One lesson lets kids disassemble atoms, count the protons, electrons, and neutrons in them, and reconstruct them. Through this type of virtual lesson, kids can interact with atoms in an entirely new way. They can see the individual particles and manipulate them in various experiments. MEL seems to make chemistry a sandbox experience, where users learn by play.
