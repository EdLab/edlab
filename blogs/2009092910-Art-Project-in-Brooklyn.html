Art Project in Brooklyn

Erin Murphy

September 29, 2009

Artists Jennifer Fisher and Christian Cerrito created a really interesting art project in Brooklyn. It was a part of the Art Under the Bridge Festival in DUMBO last weekend. You can read more about the project at this blog post. Basically the concept for the project is to "to stop and reconsider their surroundings, to explore new paths, and to embrace the unexpected." I think it would be interesting to use this project in conjunction with an iPhone app or Google maps. Each of the arrows would correspond to a tag on a Google map. As you are looking at the arrows in the "real world" you could click on the arrows on the map and either watch a video, read a story or look at pictures. I think this could be a really interesting way to explore a city. In particular I was thinking of New Orleans pre and post Katrina. Various arrows around the city could correspond to an oral history project re-telling residents stories after Katrina hit.
