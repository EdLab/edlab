Youtopia

christophergu

July 23, 2014


Youtopia is a classroom management tool that uses digital rewards to increase student engagement and involvement. Instructors sign up their classes and then assign points for completing routine tasks and bigger challenges. Users earn badges and rewards that they can proudly display on their profile pages. Youtopia helps students to curate an online resume of achievements and accomplishments.

Pros:

Youtopia allows instructors to create activities that are aligned with their curriculum and teaching goals. Students earn points and badges as they complete tasks and challenges. Youtopia also introduces an element of friendly competition with a class leaderboard. Instructors receive detailed reports about student involvement and they can easily monitor user engagement in real time. By adding the layer of gamification to the classroom experience, Youtopia enables instructors to creatively involve and engage their students to meet and even exceed their learning goals.

Cons:

Youtopia offers three different pricing options. The Starter plan is free for up to 50 students. Instructors can only set one challenge and will only receive simple data reports. The Pro plan, which costs $10 per month for 150 students, allows instructors to manage up to 5 different groups (classes) and set unlimited activities and goals. They also receive more detailed reports. The Organization plan, which costs $1 per month per user, allows instructors to assign unlimited challenges, goals, and activities. While this plan allows for unlimited users; in reality, this number will be bound by the budget of the participating organization.

Our Takeaway:

Youtopia has many applications outside of the classroom setting as well. For example, charities can use the tool to track fundraiser progress. Volunteer organizations can use Youtopia to monitor participation hours. Major organizations, such as The Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, The MacArthur Foundation, and The California Department of Education have already joined the Youtopia community.

The Bottom Line:

Youtopia uses gamification to increase student engagement and boost positive behaviors.
