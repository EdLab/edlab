Yahoo & University Libraries Working Together, What about TC?

HSC

October 6, 2005

Read this article in the Times about Yahoo's book digitization effort. What's interesting is that they are working with University libraries and corporations (e.g., HP, Adobe) to digitize books in the public domain (these books don't require copyright permission). Does anyone know how many books in the Gottesman Libraries are in the public domain (according to the article, nearly 15% of all libraries collections are out of copyright)? Is there an opportunity for us to connect with Yahoo? I think it makes sense for our own digitization efforts to build on/contribute to the work of others. The project appears like it is going to involve libraries throughout the world collaborating to create a massive digitial collection. The Uni. of Toronto (which is also using D-Space for their digital repository) is already part of the effort. This would have some interesting consequences for the TC library (more resources and space), DocDel, and GLaDAS.
