Trends in Ed: Publishing After Elsevier

LauraCostello

February 2, 2012

The Research Works Act Elsevier boycott, covered here by Joanna and on Shelfless by Julia, has continued to grow steadily and now lists 3,073 names. Prominent in the wake of this disenchantment is the peer review site Faculty of 1000. They have announced plans to create an open access program that flips the traditional publishing model. F1000 Research will focus on post-publication review, after an editorial “sanity check” the article will be immediately published and reviewed after publication for sound method. The thought is that this model of publication more accurately mirrors the pace and function of digital information. The site will also allow revisions so publications can evolve as new information becomes available and users will be encouraged to publish raw data alongside more traditional publications. Open access publications still encounter visibility and accountability issues, but the bluntness of the Research Works Act seems to have raised awareness around profits and fairness in math and science publishing and has left authors looking for alternatives.
