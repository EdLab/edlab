Lessons from the Instagram Founders

Kate Meersschaert

April 10, 2012

Billion-dollar baggage...?! Now that Facebook owns Instagram... creating what some fear will become "FaceTagram" with all the privacy concerns and "big brother-like" baggage of their new parent company. Incubation @ the "Patch"... Regardless of what side of the privacy divide you straddle, there are valuable lessons to be learned from Instagram's founders, Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger. Both are relatively recent Stanford grads, who incubated their now $1 Billion-dollar startup at Dogpatch Labs Palo Alto, CA campus. Fellowships & Real-world Experience... Pre-Dogpatch both were involved in the startup world at Stanford, and received Mayfield Fellowships. Among other things, this unique fellowship experience allowed Krieger & Systrom to first study startup case studies, then do an "externship/apprenticeship" at a startup and finally write their own case on the experience. Learn more about this year's class of fellows (and invest in their eventual startups ASAP)! Advice for "Starting"... The duo returned to their alma mater in 2011 to deliver the talk embedded below to Stanford's Technology Venture Program - the group at Stanford that hosts the Mayfield fellowship and creates free ECorner resources to support entrepreneurship. Listed below are just some of the many myths that Krieger & Systrom debunk in the vialogue below: Myth #1: You can learn to be an entrepreneur from a book, blog or talk. Myth #2: Startups only come from computer science students. Myth #3: Finding the solution to the problem is the hardest part. Myth #4: Work for months building a robust product in secrecy, then launch to the world. Myth #5: Start a bidding war among VCs with a slick pitch deck.
