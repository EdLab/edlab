Another Study Shows Exercise Could Protect Students from Stress

Sharon Hsiao

March 11, 2013

We all perceive that exercise is good for us. But how exactly is it good for us, academically? Researchers tested children who wore wrist-accelerometers to measure their physical activity and the Trier Social Stress Test for Children was measured daily. The results showed that: children with lower levels of daytime physical activity have higher hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenocortical axis (HPAA) activity in response to stress. These findings may offer insight into the pathways of PA on physical and mental well-being. I find it interesting. What would such a survey say about working adults? Office workers barely move, but wrists do. What are more accurate measures to make the association between physical and mental health? If the results are similar across age groups, should we at least exercise in the day time? (I'd be happy to do that) Here is the original article and news piece.
