Using a Social Robot for Student Learning

Jenny Shen

April 6, 2016


Move over, Tickle-Me-Elmoâ€”Tega and other complex, social robots are entering the spotlight, presenting students with the opportunity to learn in new ways. Tega, who is fluffy and red with a blue mohawk running across its head, looks like a character out of a Disney animated movie. This might have been the goal since the creators modeled it after principles of animation.

Tega can be used as an educational aide in the classroom; it prompts students to sort blocks by colors and exclaims in excitement when interacting with kids. The robot is programmed through an Android smartphone, and powered through its electromechanical system.

How can robots be used organically and productively in a classroom space? What is different about a social robot that might make it more compelling for educators to use? Join the discussion below on on Vialogues.
