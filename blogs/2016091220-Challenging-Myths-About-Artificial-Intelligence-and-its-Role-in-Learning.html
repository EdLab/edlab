Challenging Myths About Artificial Intelligence and its Role in Learning

Jenny Abamu

September 12, 2016


Artificial intelligence (AI) is a term that often takes peopleâ€™s imagination directly to Spielberg films, where we exist in a world with flying cars and robots waging war against humans. However, this vialogue places AI in a much more relatable context. Dr. Rand Hindi, CEO of Snips, and Professor Rose Luckin of London Knowledge Lab make a sensible case for embracing AI and its use in learning environments.

AI is defined as "an autonomous behavior in an artificial agent" which is really anything that can be programmed to respond to you. And it's already been embraced for learning purposes: developers program personalized learning modules that track and respond to users based on their interests and interactions. These systems mimic how the human brain naturally selects things that are important to remember, rather than retaining everything we ever learn.

Where does AI take us after personalized learning? What possibilities does it create for how we learn and process information? Join the AI debate on Vialogues.
