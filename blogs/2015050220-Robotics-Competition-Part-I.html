Robotics Competition Part I

EdLab Studios

May 2, 2015


Hands go flying to rearrange colored wires on the blinking electrical board, chains are tightened and wheels rotated like a pit crew, the last "i’s" are dotted and "t’s" crossed on entry essays for awards, and programmers work rapid fire to rewrite lines of code. It’s down to the last 48 hours of the build period and the Harlem Knights aren’t sure if their human sized robot will be up to this year’s NYC First Robotics Competition challenge. Under the guidance of head coach Joel Binanci, the group of about 40 students from Frederick Douglass Academy have been plugging away diligently in preparation for the New York City FIRST Robotics Competition held at the Javits Center from March 13-15th. The six weeks of non-stop design, construction, and coding all come down to their performance in "Recycle Rush" where two alliances of three robots compete on a 27 by 54 foot field, scoring points by stacking totes and recycling bins and tossing out litter. Sixty-six robotics teams from New York, the tri-state area, Brazil, China, Turkey and the United Kingdom will compete. Only one will advance to the global competition.

Tune into Part Two to find out how the Harlem Knights fare on the robotics battlefield.
