Day 1 at EdLab: Is there such a thing as too many questions?

carmen.cortez

February 1, 2019

Hello Team!

I’m a newbie on the Publishing team and excited to form part of a team of learners, thinkers and doers. To kick things off, I want to give a very special shout out to both George and Rob for withstanding a seemingly unending stream of questions from me this morning. Maybe this will also serve as a cautionary note for anyone that tends to invite others to ask away because I take this offer VERY seriously. I’m fascinated by the art of asking questions and maintain a database of questions for every occasion. Here are a couple of my favorites:

  • Interview Question: What do you not do that if you did do would significantly improve your performance at work?
  • Vision Board Questions: What do you appreciate about EdLab? Why do you want to be here and what do you hope for in the future?

Questions are a way for me to connect with others and understand how to best advance our mission and support the team. I’m genuinely looking forward to getting to know everyone and will be reaching out over the next few days to connect. I want to learn more about you (anything from hobbies to crazy ideas) and your preferred working styles to help inform how I can best support you and our work at EdLab. I’ll start off by sharing a bit about myself and some of my preferred work styles:

About Me:

  • Place of Birth: I was born in Arequipa, Peru. If you are planning to visit Peru anytime soon, I have some great recommendations. Please come find me!
  • Favorite Spot in NY: Whenever I need to recharge, I head straight to Battery Park. It offers a beautiful view of the New York Harbor and a stretch of 25 acres to explore.
  • My Areas of Passion: A deep-rooted passion for the study of developmental education within community colleges emerged as a result of my work at Year Up, a national workforce training program, where I had the opportunity to support a joint effort between Year Up and Miami Dade Community College designed to help bridge the opportunity gap. My work immersed me in the study of developmental education and the limitations of placement policies, revealing the critical role that community colleges play as a gateway to higher education and the workforce. This realization instilled a sense of urgency surrounding the need to remove obstacles to student success and increase student outcomes across academic and broader goals.

Working Style Preferences:

  • I appreciate when colleagues... Set a tone of joy (CHECK! :)), have fun and are clear communicators.
  • I dislike when colleagues... Don’t come to the table with a malleable mindset and don’t believe that we all come to this with a unique and significant perspective, which means no single perspective and experience is “right”.
  • When I feel stressed out at work, what I need most from my colleagues is... a sense of immediate and medium terms next steps – what needs to be done in order to relieve team stress and willingness to collaborate to get there. Additionally, a sense of camaraderie means the world to me. In general, my bias toward action shows up most when I'm under pressure.
  • To feel balanced in my work, it is important for me to... (1) have alone writing time to process, read and reflect (2) a sounding board to collaborate on deliverables with clear touch points early on to integrate all feedback in a shoulder-to-shoulder way (3) some in-person time to get deep on big questions and thought partners who want to think about the big picture/where we are going.  
  • What I need most from my team is... Solidarity and openness for collaboration.
  • I prefer to communicate .... In person? On the phone? Via text? In writing? Through writing and then talking through themes. Brainstorming in-person is something I greatly cherish.
  • I best receive feedback (both positive and constructive) in these ways... When it is framed within (a) how do we best serve our mission? (b) is shoulder-to-shoulder “let’s do this together” tone and (c) has some associated action items as experimental ways to improve. I also appreciate when iterations are modeled or there are examples of changes to make rather than just a critique.
  • The best experience I’ve had with a team... is one where everyone honors their commitments and comes through for one another.
  • My hope for this team is that we have ... (1) clarity around our individual and collective why, (2) trust one another in manner that reflects spontaneous interactions that are not propelled by mandated mechanisms, (3) a growing understanding of our roles, strengths and needs so that we can reach our goals as a team.

I also welcome all questions and appreciate any opportunity to brainstorm ideas. You will find in me someone to partner with on projects and think through problems and ideas. I love ideas in all varying stages, especially those that are still in early draft form. As we all know, the perfect is the enemy of good.


Thank you all for welcoming me to EdLab!

Cheers,

Carmen
