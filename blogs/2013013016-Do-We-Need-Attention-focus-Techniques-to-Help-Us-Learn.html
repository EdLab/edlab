Do We Need Attention-focus Techniques to Help Us Learn?

Sharon Hsiao

January 30, 2013

How often do you pause a movie/video, and think, in order to memorize something? It's a common technique that people use to learn a language. In fact, I do that to learn English too. I read this paper, Ogan, A., Aleven, V., and Jones, C. (2008), Pause, predict, and ponder: use of narrative videos to improve cultural discussion and learning . In Proceedings of the Twenty-Sixth Annual SIGCHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems. CHI 2008. ACM, New York, NY, pp. 155-162. The authors designed and studied a pause-predict- ponder model in order to help students with their attention-focusing techniques. The results showed that such techniques helped students to reason better and potentially increased learning of declarative knowledge components. With appropriate techniques that are relatively easy to implement and can make progress in a difficult and ill-defined domain, such as cultural discussions. It strikes me that, isn't it Vialouges? (Except, Vialouges allows users to pause voluntarily not automatically, and we didn't ask users to "predict". We don't really implement an attention-focus technique or a strategy per-se. We rely on moderations and volunteering in the dialogues. But there are so many alternatives with promising results in learning from discussions. Maybe, it's time to think about more AI techniques in Vialogues?!)
