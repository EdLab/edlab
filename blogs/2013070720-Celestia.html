Celestia

Demetri Lales

July 7, 2013


Celestia is a desktop application that allows users to travel through space virtually and discover the many objects found there. Users are shown a realistic visual representation of different planets and their moons and can view their actual orbits. Celestia also shows many other space objects such as asteroids, comets, spacecraft, stars, and galaxies. The app lets users select which types of space objects are displayed. Students can learn their names, orbital paths, and locations. It is possible to see how objects move in space by manipulating the passing of time in the visualization. Celestia has a separate site called Celestia Motherlode that provides downloadable add-ons for the app and activities for educators to use with their students.

Pros:

Celestia allows users to fully explore space and control time. Time can be sped up and slowed down to show the movement of planets. The app also lets users see the planets and sun in real time to determine which parts of the planets are experiencing daylight. The app’s default is set to show the objects in space at time of access but users can set the time and date to view the positions of space objects throughout the year. For example, with the real positioning of space objects, users can actually see the position of the Hubble telescope in the earth’s orbit.

Cons:

The controls are confusing and it takes time to master travel through the virtual space. It can be difficult to rotate a planet to a desirable position. It would have been beneficial to be able to rotate around a planet to show the light and dark sides of a planet. There are extra add-ons to the app which are only available for Windows that are a bit more complicated.

Our Takeaway:

Celestia lets users see and interact with space and everything that it has to offer. This app could be a great tool to teach the sciences and pique a student’s interest in astronomy. The extra resources the app provides simulate what it is like to travel in space give students a firsthand look at the experience. Teachers can use Celestia as a tool to show some concepts in astronomy and earth science to their students. Celestia is a great virtual model of space and time that gives users full control.

The Bottom Line:

Celestia lets users travel through space and time.
