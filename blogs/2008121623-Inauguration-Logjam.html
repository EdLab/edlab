Inauguration Logjam

Nantwi

December 16, 2008

According to CTIA-the Wireless Association, the DC area can expect a logjam cellphones on the day of Barack Obama's inauguration as president. Thousands are expected to gather at the National Mall on January 20th to see Obama's historic oath as our latest president. Analysts are predicting a large number of activity through mobile phones, e.g. phones calls, text messages, photo and video sharing, and as such deliveries of messages and calls will have long waits. In preparation for the expected logjam, Sprint and Verizon, two of the biggest carriers, have already began spending millions to add capacity to their cell sites prior to inauguration day. The former has even gone as far as using resources normally reserves for natural disasters. Sprint is using COWs (Cell on Wheels) and COLTs (Cell on Light Truck), vehicles that use satellites and microwave technology and usually serve as mobile cell towers when disasters knock out regular towers. The COLT should be able to carry about 1,500 extra callers though only 60 calls can go through at the same time.

The logjam could pose a potential security risk for the hundreds of security officers and emergency officers who rely on their cell phones. The news is not all that bad. First responders have a special access code that will enable them to make calls should the need arise. Sprint has urged its users to text instead of call as data consumes less data and will get through quicker.
