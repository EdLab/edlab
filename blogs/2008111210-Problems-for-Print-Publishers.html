Problems for Print Publishers

Jeff Frank

November 12, 2008

This blog post offers a fairly detailed analysis of the problems that The New York Times is sure to face in the near future. What is striking about this entry is that papers like the times--see, in particular the Christian Science Monitor--are finding that printing is just too expensive. And yet, online advertising is not the revenue generator that many hoped. More, sites like craigslist have drastically cut into revue that papers may have raised through classifieds. So, what model will work moving forward? Might a low-cost online subscription model–like the one used at the Record–help? And, if this practice does take hold, will proponents of Open Source publishing be forced to rethink their assumptions? For example, if people begin realizing that things online are not free–writers have to be paid, copyeditors need to be paid, production staff need to be paid, content hosting and management are not free–even though they have been free(on the hope that advertising would generate more revenue than they are), will they begin appreciating why small publishers must charge a small fee for access.
