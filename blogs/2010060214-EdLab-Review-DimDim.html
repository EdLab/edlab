EdLab Review: DimDim

Chandler Kang

June 2, 2010

Edlab review: DimDim A web based tool for sharing information http://www.dimdim.com/
DimDim is a web-based platform that enables distant conferencing, meeting, and chatting. You can share knowledge expressed with all types of modalities with others.
Price: Prices vary depending on the number of employees Up until 50 employees: Monthly cost $25, Annual cost $228
How it works: Once you log in, a sharing option page appears. It has several sharing options such as a computer screen, a white board, a presentation, webpages, and a webcam chat. If you choose a “computer screen sharing” option for a meeting, you can invite people via email to your sharing page and set the levels of privacy. After setting up the meeting, you can also manage the meeting and participants in the meeting. For instance, upon choosing a white board, you can share the white board on your computer screen. In the presentation option, you can choose your own ppt or pdf file from your computer. Throughout a meeting, you can record all interactions and behaviors that happened on your computer screen and in webpage sharing by typing an address.
Prop points: DimDim enables a user to host or attend live meetings, demos and webinars using just a web browser. It is possible to give presentations, share web pages, whiteboards, voice, video — even record a user's events - with no software to install and with no hassles. Dimdim is for everyone who wants to meet using just a browser. It saves time, money and travel expenses. There are training sessions for users almost everyday. Final score: DimDim is aiming to provide users with easier and more accessible communication as well as a knowledge sharing environment. It covers all types of modalities based on web environment, so then users do not have to install specific software to communicate each other. It is easy to use and has user centered graphic interfaces.
