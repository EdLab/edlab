The Digital Media Industry and YOU

Kate Meersschaert

February 9, 2012

University of Pennsylvania Professor, Joseph Turow's recent release, The Daily You, explores the behemoth industry that has grown-up around your online data. More specifically, Turow explores how these advertising and media companies gather and profit off of this information. Turow describes both the power and the need for caution in this comment featured on The Atlantic site: To recognize such immense power is not to deny the spaces and opportunities for freedom, participation, and creativity that several scholars identify in the new environment. But it does make clear that the ruthlessly commercial logic and obscure social profiling practices of the new media-buying system are what dominate the emerging digital world. As we work towards more interconnected applications and data-based projects here at the EdLab, it seems increasingly important to both harness the power of personal data to make tools better and more powerful tools, while carefully guarding individual's privacy and right to fully control their online personhood. Do you feel that data will be the most valuable commodity of the coming years? If so, what will the human cost be for mining this data "gold?"
