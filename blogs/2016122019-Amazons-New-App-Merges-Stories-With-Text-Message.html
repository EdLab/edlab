Amazon’s New App Merges Stories With Text Message

saraehardman

December 20, 2016


Amazon Rapids, launched in November 2016, is Amazon’s new reading app for kids. Instead of normal storytelling, Amazon Rapids tells stories through text transcripts. Users scroll through each message, and the story unfolds through a conversation between two cartoon characters. The app is geared toward kids 12 and under; users can subscribe for $2.99 per month after a free trial.

Pros:

Amazon Rapids is, to put it simply, a fun way to read. Each story is told in a dialogue entered into a text messaging format. Each message pops up next to the character’s image, and someone’s typing dots appear before users scroll to the next message. Characters even share selfies at times to give a more realistic digital texture to the pieces. This format lends itself easily to making the stories funny and entertaining.

In addition, Amazon Rapids has a built in glossary system. When kids come across words they don’t know, they can press the word and a definition pops up. That word is automatically saved to a user’s glossary, which grows throughout each story and can be accessed at any tie so kids can return to the same tricky words again and again. Also, each user creates a profile on Amazon Rapids, and the app displays stories within the child’s age range and interests. The app is set up similarly to Netflix, with the homepage showing recommendations targeted at the individual, then stories separated into genres.

Cons:

The format of Amazon Rapids does not easily allow non-fiction pieces; in fact, there are no strictly factual or information articles. Because of this, children who are interested in nonfiction might not find a story that keeps their attention. In addition, the stories are mostly comical, which means that children who use this app will not necessarily be exposed to tales that provide rich emotional experiences. The app could benefit from content that ranges from funny to serious.

Our Takeaway:

Amazon Rapids is a great app that provides a new format to encourage children to read. Kids will likely be captivated by the text message stories, and the built-in glossary system ensures that they learn new vocabulary as they go. Although I don’t recommend replacing your books with Amazon Rapids, I certainly recommend the app as a fun reading activity for any child.
