Coursera and U.S Government to Create a Global Network

Bismark Appiah

November 6, 2013

Kate recently blogged about Coursera two year anniversary and included an infographic on the scope and impact of the platform up to date. After looking closing at the infographic, I noticed that of the top 10 user base countries, none were from developing world. I believe MOOCs have the potential to reach and change the lives of students in developing countries. The US State Department recently formed a partnership with Coursera and other MOOCs with an emphasis on reaching those in developing nations, where access is often an issue. The partnership will surely increase the five million people already using Coursera. To present this idea of Global Network, Coursera and the U.S government are working to create "learning hubs" around the world to provide Internet access to students for free and make them engage in discussions with local teachers and/or facilitators. This might also explain why the top 10 user base countries included no developing countries. There is a lack of access to Internet connection in most developing countries, especially in rural areas. The lack of Internet access and the definition of a new stage in the evaluation of MOOCs are some of the reasons for the learning hubs. Universities and institutes of learning from all corners (e.g. Trinidad & Tobago, Kenya) of the world will serve as the centers for the learning hubs.
