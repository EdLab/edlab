Make Your Own Brain Science

Brian.Sweeting

December 16, 2013

Even though the future has disappointed us in failing to deliver flying cars and jetpacks (for now), it IS pretty cool that in 2013 you can cheaply measure your own brain signals. From my favorite PSFK is a blurb on OpenBCI, an open-source platform that allows users to access their brain waves as data. It remains to be seen how accurate the converter is and what kind of programs the user community will be capable of building, but by expanding access to this kind of data to more researchers, it seems like there is huge potential for advancements in so many fields. If you could measure brain waves in the context of learning about learning, what would you want to know?
