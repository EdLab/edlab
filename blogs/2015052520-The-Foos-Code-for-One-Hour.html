The Foos: Code for One Hour

Ting Yuan

May 25, 2015


The Foos: Code for an Hour is a free early childhood iOS app created for the Hour of Code, a nationwide initiative by Computer Science Education Week and Code.org to introduce millions of students to computer science and computer programming. The app's core concepts include pattern recognition, problem solving, sequencing, spatial visualization, algorithmic thinking, debugging programs, loops and conditionals. It includes 24 levels and three distinct "toy box" areas for creative visual coding. Its development was inspired by beginning programming languages such as Scratch from MIT and Alice from Carnegie Mellon University.

Pros:

The app is designed as a series of coding challenges situated in a cartoon game world. It contains elements of childrenâ€™s familiar games such as Super Mario where the game character picks up coins along the way. The app interface is intuitive with a visual block palette supported by icons set at the bottom of the screen. Young players can decide how they want to move forward in the game by selecting and dragging different character movements on the palette. The challenges revealed on each level can be solved in different ways, with the most efficient outcome earning three full stars.

Cons:

The transition from learning about combining different movements to repeated loops could be improved with the addition of an animated modeling video to help to guide young players in their independent programming journeys with the app.

Our Takeaway:

The Foos is a fun tool for introducing children as young as four-years-old to coding. Teachers could add it to a variety of classroom-centered activities in early childhood settings, and parents may consider it as a digital toy that could help their children to build an initial understanding of coding through gaming. The Foos is a gateway to introduce younger children to the Hour of Code and help build a base for more flexible tools like Scratch Jr.
