Citizen Scientists: Play a Game and Map the Brain

Karen Bryner

September 5, 2013


How long does it take a scientist to map the brain? It’s hard to say, but enlisting citizen scientists through online gaming is accelerating the process. It takes a neuroscientist around 50 hours to map one cell, or one neuron; there are more than 80 billion neurons in one human brain. To accomplish the mammoth task of mapping the entire human brain, a lab at MIT has created EyeWire, an online game that maps neural networks, to engage individuals outside of the trained science community for crowdsourcing help.

The entertainment factor EyeWire provides is tapping into the estimated 3 billion hours—or 342,000 years worth of hours—individuals world-wide spend playing online games each week. Within 5 months, the international gaming community, through individual work and team competitions, was able to map new neural networks and cells previously unknown.
