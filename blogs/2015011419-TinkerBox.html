TinkerBox

Alexandra Lederman

January 14, 2015


Tinkerbox is a free physics puzzle game designed to teach basic engineering concepts while providing interesting science facts. Users can exercise their imaginations by building bold and unusual machines. Tinkerbox starts the user with lessons, where written explanations and visualizations about the tools and puzzles are provided. The Lab, aka puzzle mode, is physics-based and uses mechanical concepts, allowing users to solve problems creatively. Once users have finished the lessons, they can experiment in the lab. Tinkerbox is recommended for early elementary users and is available for free on iPhones and iPads.

Pros

The lesson level of Tinkerbox pairs together written and visual explanations of tools, physics concepts, and mechanical strategies. This pairing ultimately allows users to understand the tools and concepts of the puzzle in different ways. The lab level provides predetermined goals. When users complete a lab, they are introduced to the steel mill, space station, and earth and beyond levels that let them think and build creatively without predetermined goals.

Cons

The music is repetitive and the first lesson section moves slowly. There are awkward aspects to the game, specifically when trying to angle tools to other objects. The mechanical design of the puzzles may not captivate everyone.

Bottom Line

Tinkerbox is an older game, but delivers with visuals and written facts. The app has a seamless way of teaching physics without making the concepts too difficult to understand. Tinkerbox helps scaffold learning so early elementary students can build creatively while learning physics and mechanics in a digital format.
