Penumbra Foundation

EdLab Studios

October 25, 2015


It requires only a fraction of a second to take a picture on an iPhone and share it with someone halfway around the world. An alternative photography process from the 19th century involves dozens of steps and can take an entire day to get right. So why do it? Located in the historic so-called "Photo District" of NYC, The Penumbra Foundation offers educational opportunities where inquisitive artists and students can learn a variety of alternative processes, test them out, and decide for themselves. Engaging with the chemical process of an albumen or kallitype from start to finish, through every chemical wash and coat of egg white, provides a greater understanding of the foundations of photography and what forces are at work when we click a button. Students are experiencing history; and this doing generates deeper appreciation and understanding of the nuances of the craft over time like no documentary or museum visit ever could. What's more, modern artists find their voice and breathe new life into their photography through previously unknown methods. As any student will posit, these processes are anything but obsolete; a legacy worth revisiting as a means of enhanced personal and artistic expression.
