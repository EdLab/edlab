D&R Recap: Enhancing Library Reports

Jessica Mezei

April 20, 2010

What kinds of data should we be collecting to understand how library resources and sites are used? Respective percentages of user hits on the various categories of the library website during the academic year. Fall '09 Report Today during the D&R meeting we held brainstorming sessions around the data collection and reporting services the Edlab provides to different sectors of the library. The featured reports today included the Teacher's College Record Report (TCRR) and The Library Website Report (LWR). The session was comprised of focussed discussions addressing questions such as, (a)What would you like to know? in relation to collecting data and reporting results from these sites, (b)What are the current reports comprised of?, and (c)How can we connect a and b moving forward to with the reports? (a) What to Collect? Ideas from what data to collect for these sites included having specific questions to use data collected to answer (not just a broad summary), including optional surveys about user satisfaction, visualizations of ratings that represent the most popular things on each site, user data, new feature effects and the reports should reflect a longitudinal trend approach. The Library Website Report

  • Survey of user satisfaction
  • Focus on what is uses beyond library searches and library services the 'other stuff' on the site
  • Popular search trends- create resources on them Advertisements and affect on enrollment or popularity
  • Longitudinal approach: trends over time What do twitter and facebook do? how many people connect using them are they useful?
    • Teachers College Record Report
  • Trackable additions to website: purpose of the design of website connected to data
  • New features and how they interact? (the voice, discussion board)
  • User information connected to subscription data who uses it and how, activity by role
  • Link information to newsletters
  • Financial data
  • Optional survey user satisfaction
  • Emails to direct traffic to the site
  • Search feature what people look for rating (what's worth looking at)
    • (c)How to enhance reports? We split into two teams, to discuss each report as it currently exists and then potential ways to change or develop such reports. This discussion though were independent from each other seemed to touch upon similar aspect for future areas of data collection. These themes included transparency and user personalization as future directions or areas to improve upon. For more specific information suggested toward each click below. Reports could be published to Edlab tab or site. -Data could be accessible with permissions on access, drop down menus to select specific pieces of data so can have more specialized reports (have a long list of all data that can be collected but a page that people can pick and choose what is relevant to their question) -Have tab on edlab site that makes reports public -Have D&R meetings or seminars that summarize and share out this data to get feedback and always have a cycle for improvement and current questions to be addressed. - Use data for more scholarly publications - looking at longitudinal trends of the library or specific data that is new or could suggest trend. - Website/Cost breakdowns
