Trends in Ed: Ed Schools Moving To The Practical?

Skanda Amarnath

July 26, 2011

It looks like some education schools are shifting away from classroom instruction on education theory and more towards actual practice working in the classroom. At the crux of the problem is that "there is little research into what kind of training is most likely to produce a successful teacher, a fact that social scientists are now working to remedy through long-term study." The article is a bit long but well worth reading for its analysis of how education schools are debating whether a practical approach to teaching will yield better teachers and student outcomes. Essentially this boils down to the rise of cheaper, more vocational-like teacher training programs that are more affordable but also less content-rich. This seems likely to be an intermediate solution for those that would be going to a comparably-priced teaching graduate school, but hardly a substitute for a Teacher's College.
