Solar Beats

Alexandra Lederman

September 20, 2015


Solar Beats is a visualization and sound box of our solar system's orbital frequency. Designed by Luke Twyman, an illustrator and graphic designer, this celestial music box isn't scientifically accurate, but can be a great way to introduce science, music, and graphic design to students. Users can also manipulate the sounds of this celestial music box, giving students a glimpse of digital music production. This simple website would be great for younger elementary students

Pros:

The Solar Beats website is easy to use based on its simple design and few options. It's visually pleasing, which could increase students' interest in science and astronomy, especially for those students who are STEM reluctant but drawn to art. Educators could also use this website to spark interest in design and digital music production. The easy and minimal manipulation tools seem like a good way to whet students' appetites for science without overwhelming them. The educator would be able to let the students explore this site with little instruction.

Cons:

The minimal design and tools available to manipulate the sounds could be seen as a limitation. Educators may find it hard to use this site for extended lessons and might need to present it as part of a suite of tools meant to spark creativity and interest at the intersection between science and art.

Our Takeaway:

Solar Beats is the perfect site to intrigue students in a number of disciplines. From digital music production and design, to astronomy, physics, and general science, this site can inspire students with different interests. Solar Beats' simplicity also allows the teacher to take a more hands off approach and let students explore individually.
