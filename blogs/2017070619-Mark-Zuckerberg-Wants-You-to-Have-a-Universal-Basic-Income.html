Mark Zuckerberg Wants You to Have a Universal Basic Income

dinosossi2

July 6, 2017


As Occupy Wall Street brought to mass attention, and Bernie Sanders’ strong showing in the Democratic Party process for presidential nominee further emphasized, income inequality is becoming a substantial problem.


And who’s to the rescue?


Facebook impresario Mark Zuckerberg.


To remedy this difficult situation, Zuck is advocating for universal basic income (UBI). Simply put, UBI represents a minimum level of income for all citizens to ensure that they can have a basic standard of living. 


In a recent visit to Alaska, Zuck gave an example of the state’s Permanent Fund Dividend. Oil revenue fuels a fund that is divided among residents. 


Alaska's plan sounds great. And as an additional perk, you can also see Russia from your house!


But the million (maybe billion?) dollar question is who is going to pay? 


Publicly traded companies like Facebook aren’t in the habit of handing out money. And you won’t see UBI being debated at an annual board meeting anytime soon


What financial sources could work? Perhaps transaction taxes on financial trades? How about some kind of levy on large tech companies with high revenues, but relatively low numbers of employees?


However, at least in the U.S., given the current moves to dismantle various parts of the administrative state, building the kind of cross-aisle political goodwill or the type of political infrastructure necessary to handle this type of national fund doesn’t seem likely. 


The best bet for you UBI advocates?
