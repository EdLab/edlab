Education Design Studio, Inc.

Kate Meersschaert

June 11, 2014


One of the first non-resident edtech incubators
Unlike leading edtech incubators such as ImagineK12, Penn’s Graduate School of Education has recently launched (Fall 2013) a 6-month-long edtech incubator program with a distinct difference, no "residence" requirement. While local, Philadelphia-based startups such as apprenticeship-focused, Apprennet and AutismExpressed, a company focused on developing digital literacy for autistic youth, share Philly-based Education Design Studio, Inc. (EDSi) office space, all other participants are spread across the country. While there are some in-person mentorship and advising meetings, most interaction takes place digitally. This distinction allows working, married professionals unable or unwilling to uproot their families an exciting opportunity. With over $2.1Million dollars in funding, EDSi is relatively small in comparison to other verticals and funds, but, in the world of edtech, it has a reasonable pool of funding and provides each participating startup with $10,000. Chosen from recent winners of the Milken-Penn Graduate School of Education Business Plan Competition, participants include (in addition to AutismExpressed and Apprennet mentioned above) Scrible, Raise Labs, and apidapter.

Will EDSi help cultivate a stronger Philly-based edtech ecosystem?
As this Technical.ly Philly article points out, the vision for the fund is in line with Penn’s Executive Director of Academic Innovation, Bobbi Kurshan’s (read her New Learning Times Profile), hopes for creating a larger hub of East Coast educational entrepreneurship in Philly. However, unlike New York (disclaimer the NLT editorial office is located here), which is home to the nation’s largest school district, Philly is ranked #14. While this is large in comparison to all 50 states, it is by no means a direct competitor to Los Angeles Unified or even Chicago in terms of access to students, educators and administrators. However, if Kurshan and EDSi succeed in their "hybrid incubator/seed fund" mission, Philly just might rival the Valley’s edtech presence and network of resources. The fact that the fund has no residency requirement is in itself a distinctive, exciting point-of-difference that just might cause Philadelphia to rebrand itself as, the "City of Edtech Love."
