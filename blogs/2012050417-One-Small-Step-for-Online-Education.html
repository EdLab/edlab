One Small Step for Online Education

Fred Rossoff

May 4, 2012

This week, Harvard and MIT announced that they were teaming up to create a platform (and content) for free online courses. This new non-profit organization, called edX, has already received $60 million in funding, and will make its courses freely available, although a certain level of monetization seems to be in the works. Although at first glance this seems to be a significant step forward in the ongoing transition to 21st century learning, but the inevitable question arises. If, as with the Already existing MITx and Stanford online courses, the relevant institutions will not be granting any kind of degree or credential in return for work in these courses, then how much of any impact can it really have?
