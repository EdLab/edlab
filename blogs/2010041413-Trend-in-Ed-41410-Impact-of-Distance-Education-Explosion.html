Trend in Ed 4.14.10, Impact of Distance Education Explosion

Jeannie Crowley

April 14, 2010

Trend: Online Education
Related Posts:: The 2008 Report, Perception of Online Degrees and How to Teach an Online Course

Online education enrollment continues to increase rapidly, especially at community colleges. According to a recent report by the Instructional Technology Council, online enrollment increased 22% over the previous year. What are some of the not-so-obvious impacts of this rapid growth?

  • New technical requirements for staff: As online education continues to grow, it is possible that universities will push harder for the staff to be more technically proficient. A major element of Seton Hall's new technology initiative is to make sure the staff is using online learning tools. I don't believe educators have been laid off for a lack of technical ability, it has been happening in other sectors.
  • Support staff layoffs: The University of Texas recently laid off twenty-three employees as it closed its on-campus support for online learning. A representative from the university noted that online courses are easier to create and in-person support is unnecessary.
  • Preparing students for online education: Many student may not be prepared to interact and succeed in an online-only degree program. Common Sense Media has created a curriculum to prepare young students for online interactions by teaching them how to protect their intellectual property, understand their changing identities in different online environments and participate respectfully in online groups.
