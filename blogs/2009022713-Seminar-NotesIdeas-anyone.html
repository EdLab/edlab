Seminar Notes...Ideas anyone?

Skye MacLeod

February 27, 2009

Here are the notes that Doug and I took during the seminar on Wednesday. Any comments or suggestions would be greatly appreciated! How's the pitch? Does it make sense? What sense do you make of it? Stephanie: really wanted there to be some kind of abstract: before the goals---'we're working on a new series called 'etc etc'---this works with these kinds of groups to produce a video that _______. ex--from Skye's email: "I am currently working on a documentary project that will explore non-traditional and non-formal learning environments in NYC, and I thought the Lower East Side Ecology Center would fit well into the doc. Presently we are in pre-production for the project, and I was hoping you might have a few minutes to chat about the Lower East Side Ecology Center's educational program. I have cc'd my colleague Doug Beacom, who is also working on the project." Gonzalo: It is important to choose the stories that will accurately and would best represent the organizations that we highlight. Make sure there's transparency so that the organization feels a part of the production process. How will you cover explicit "challenges" or "problems" that may arise? Usage? would it be linked to a youtube channel with smaller, rougher videos, supplement the whole/main story? this could be confusing in terms of knowing where the video lives--- Ways to engage audience during PRE/POST production? what are other ways to engage audience beyond comments and video? facebook apps? NING? something more social. Ways to publish the video? one 20-30 min shot? shorter, 5-6 min chunks? seems like its very focused on going from community to community--going to one, then another maybe look at themes-- ex. 'how are human rights taught?'---for 20' episode, then include AdLab and few other approaches. if shorter videos...how do we keep the audience engaged on that story? make it really compelling and not boring for viewers-- Cinema Veritae or VO approach? what do we feel is most important? Expert Interview to tie-together content? how should this show break down? episodic? Gonzalo: I would highly recommend a narrator to give unity to the story; it will be a way to tie the different groups together. Adam: Think about a protocol to employ so that the different sections look and feel similar, even though they are different contexts. Jess: The participatory aspect of the project is really great. I think it's a good idea to involve the students as much as you can in the production process. Gonzalo: Here are a few ideas I have as a protocol, or structure for telling you story-- 1. Location: first establish the context in which the educational setting exists. Why here? Where is it? History of location? 2. Introduce the problem: what is the problem that this educational program addresses? How has the problem existed in the past? In the present? Why are you interested in this problem? 3. Introduce the character(s): who are we watching? Where is/are she/they from? How is/are she/they involved in the the setting? How is/are she/they coping with the problem? 4. Show the process of overcoming the problem: (Students working in the project, teacher's experience) Ting: Important to decide the story first, then choose cases. How will this video be used? as a teacher? as an academic? as an organizational leader? as students? for documentary film makers? educators--people/professionals in the field of education--looking for cutting edge/peripheral activities going on-- also, (re: LES ecology) art/technology issues could be overlapping--DIY kits demonstrating energy harvesting, composting, etc. hooking up with those types of people/projects could be a really good way to get kids into exploring those ideas for themselves. **which gets back to the THEMATIC focus--do we put out a call Adam: It would be great if you could have a video response to the segments, or allow students/teachers to post. Students could post videos recommending new experiences, teachers could post videos for pedagogical ideas.
