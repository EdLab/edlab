Playing With Feelings (An Exercise in Empathy)

jvazquez

February 25, 2014


You can't learn empathy from a textbook, sure, but who would think it could spring from wooden blocks? Twenty One Toys is a Canadian-based startup dedicated to teaching kids 21st century skills (namely empathy and resilience) through a different kind of play. Their first creation is a game in which teams of two interact with each other through the use of interlocking wooden shapes, blindfolded. They must rely on verbal cues and listening skills in order to reach their objective.

The game itself is simple but remarkably effective and allows kids (and even adults) to broach a potentially complicated subject in a straightforward and fun way. The company envisions toys as tools for social change. Play, they say, is the catalyst that will re-introduce creativity, empathy, and collaboration into classrooms and workplaces alike.
