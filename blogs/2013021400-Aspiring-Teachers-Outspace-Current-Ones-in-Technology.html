Aspiring Teachers Outspace Current Ones in Technology

Francisco.Mendoza

February 14, 2013

According to a new report, a lot of educators are using the internet to develop and enhance their skills. Many principals expect teachers to be doing this, as well as using technology to contact both students and parents. Plenty of teachers see technology as a " teaching tool " more than anything else, though I do not fully agree with that just yet. It seems that the teachers examined on how they use technology in the classroom gave less-than-desirable results. Educators in low income schools wish for more technology but unfortunately because of budgeting issues, it was not possible. I personally think that teachers using social media and online resources to better their skill sets is a good thing. I'm not sure if using it with their students is a smart idea though. As technology continues to become ever present in education, I will always believe that receiving help from an actual teacher is better, such as having one-on-one time with them. This article highlights a survey that shows how aspiring teachers outpace current ones in technology.
