Students Get to Play CEO For a Day

Mable Yiu

July 28, 2013


Having business and marketing skills is important for any person, regardless of your position within an organization. But when can you start building these skills? Most people don’t have the opportunity until they are in college or even later. This is where Project Empower comes in. As an after-school program designed to teach business concepts to elementary school students, Project Empower allows kids to learn marketing, sales, and entrepreneurship at an early age. This video highlights students of the After-School All-Stars at St. John’s University working in groups to pitch their business plans for a chocolate product to a panel of judges, or "investors." Throughout the learning process, the students were able to work with CEOs, professors, and university students. At the end, a winning group was announced, and the students were able to meet the chocolatier, Timothy Maguire, himself.

Not only did the students get to "play CEO" for a day and participate in a real-world situation, they also learned vital communication and collaboration skills.
