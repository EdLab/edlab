LinkedIn Economic Graph & EdNode

zhou

July 28, 2016

I encountered the following video where a LinkedIn staff talked about the Economic Graph project at LinkedIn. After seeing this video and some digging about the EG project, it turns out EG is very similar to our own almost-forgotten project -- EdNode!

LinkedIn Staff Software Engineer Srinath Shankar on Making the Economic Graph a Reality from LinkedIn
The basic idea of EG starts from today's increasing trend that more and more professionals are becoming freelancers. To my understanding, EG aims at connecting these talents by various assets - education, skills, experiences, interests, location, and so on. This will create lots of opportunities for economic growth because it's a lot easier for talents to gather together to make great things happen. EdNode has a similar vision, except that the talents are in the education domain. And we have had that vision ever since 2011. But for this reason or that, EdNode never really soared. And I feel sorry about that. I am hoping we can bring EdNode back to life some time. I have been working on the EdLab Super Search (official name TBD) project which allows people to search multiple databases at once based on the related attributes we define by ourselves for each resource. For example, the following mental map shows how we recommend resources from Vialogues, NLT, and TCR to Ryan who has used Vialogues and NLT before but not TCR. Also, we recommend Zeph to Ryan as well because they participated in the same Vialogues discussions. I hope now you understand what EdLab Super Search is, and you should be able to see EdNode is the natural continuation to the Super Search because without the Super Search we cannot make really good recommendations to people. And now it's the perfect time for EdNode because of the increasing popularity of MOOC - more and more instructors and students are on their own and they need to be connected! Please let me know what you think.
