Gotham Girls Roller Derby

EdLab Studios

May 22, 2016


The best place to build up confidence might be where you can get knocked down. And bruised. And separate a shoulder or two. The aptly named "crashpad" located in East Willamsburg is the site of the Gotham Girls Roller Derby, the only non-profit, all-female, skater-operated derby league in the city that bears its name. Since 2003, GGRD and its diverse collection of world champion skaters have worked hard to win bouts but also cultivate a community of strong, competitive women. The Gotham Girls Junior Derby imparts these lessons in leadership, good sportswomanship, and a lifelong commitment to fitness to the next generation of empowered women. Under the tutelage of GGRD all-stars, girls ages 8-11 and 12-17 attend weekly practices and scrimmages on the same flat track as their role models. Juniors build skating, sliding, and blocking skills in a safe and supportive environment where they can find a place on the team regardless of body type or experience level. But no roller derby queen would be complete without a roller derby name. In the spirit of tradition, each girl takes on a roller derby moniker and newfound attitude to match, unlocking a fierceness she'll carry with her through life.
