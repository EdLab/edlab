U.S. Economy Remains in Neutral

abagigah92

March 7, 2013

The biggest financial issue that once faced this nation has been over for about three years but the bigger questions remains: how long will it take the United States to play the role of the world's big Engine? The recession caused lots of negative turning points not only in the United States but for the global economy. This caused many companies to lose profit, thus leading to the bigger problem of unemployment. Despite the recovery, it has been very difficult to find jobs. The Feds president, Richard Fisher said “it is not possible to create jobs through monetary policy alone.” Although it is clear that the U.S remains the economic engine of the world, our nation's economy is stuck in neutral and has not reach its full potential. This is because lawmakers are not working in a way conducive to economic growth. If the federal government do not fix the debt problem, the Feds cannot really impact the economy as much to decrease unemployment. The Feds have played their part by keeping inflation down and buying bonds so that borrowing cost and interest remains low in an effort to encourage investors to invest and employ workers.
