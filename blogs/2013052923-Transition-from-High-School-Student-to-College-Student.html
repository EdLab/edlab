Transition from High School Student to College Student

Oumar Soumahoro

May 29, 2013

The high amount of high school dropouts from predominantly urban areas can be attributed to the fact that most students do not find college relevant to their lives. In other words, they have no clue of which direction to take their futures should they complete high school. Mentoring and internships is one of the best, if not the best way, to get high school students to focus on certain subject areas or interests and find out what they want to major in once in college. As a college bound student, deciding on a major in college would not be an easy task. My internship here at the EdLab has reawakened an earlier passion I have for Web Design and Programming, my potential majors in college.
