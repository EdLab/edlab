What Percentage of Individuals Contacted Clicked the NLT Link?

Unique Individual Clicks by State:


Global Insights:

Right about now you’re thinking, “So the most clicks came from Texas (18), Florida (6), Virginia (6), California (5), New York (5)... but didn’t we also target a greater volume of authors in those states?”


Astute observation! Let’s look at click rate by state. We’ll take the individuals who clicked in each state and divide those link clickers by the total individuals contacted per state.







Click Rate (Unique Individuals) by State:


Note: this^^ is a percentage scale (lightest green = 7%, darkest green = 100%)


Yes, the one person we contacted in Kansas seems to have clicked the link. SCORE!

Missouri?? We didn’t even CONTACT anyone in Missouri. Let's address this point….

  1. Metabase is only so specific with their data. For example, Metabase wouldn't tell us that Elvis clicked the link, it would just tell us someone near Graceland clicked the link. Breaking down the metrics by state is a proxy for narrowing who our individual link clickers are. With that said, people travel.   
  2. As you’ll see shortly, some of these individuals were SUPER jazzed about NLT. One author may have forwarded George’s email to 20 of their friends who all clicked the link. This would mean our click rate is much smaller than calculated… BUT, given the high volume of clicks from a geographically diverse audience, we can’t be mad about the word of mouth publicity.





Did anyone actually email you back?


Of 208 authors contacted, and 93 individual link clickers, 15 authors (7%) emailed responses to George. Iowa, Kentucky, Alabama, and Tennessee had the greatest response rates. Midwestern folks are so darn nice.


  • Iowa: 50% email response rate; 1 actual email
  • Kentucky: 50% email response rate; 1 actual email
  • Alabama: 33% email response rate; 1 actual email
  • Tennessee: 33% email response rate; 1 actual email
