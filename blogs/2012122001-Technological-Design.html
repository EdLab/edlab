Technological Design

abagigah92

December 20, 2012

As the forms and types of technology we use become increasingly part of our everyday lives, particularly those with the sleek designs like Apple's products or Androids, John Maeda, an artist, graphic designer, computer scientist and the President of Rhode Island School of Design, implores us to question whether we are making true technological advancement. He sees technological advancement as stuck in a vicious cycle, without any interaction with the consumers. He explains that we are stuck in a loop of “text, images, audio, video” only with slight modifications in form. He emphasizes the importance of form and content in the design process as a general rule. Depending on how a word is presented, it can invoke contrasting emotions. Maeda's message is simple: create technology that interacts with people. This, he explains, cannot be achieved by simply disregarding “old” technology and embracing “new” technology but rather proposes a unity of both old and new ideas to make “what is good.” To sum up, Maeda explains that beneficial technological advancement will happen only if leaders consider the fields of Art and Design in the creation process. The ways in which technology responds to people is as essential as its form and content.
