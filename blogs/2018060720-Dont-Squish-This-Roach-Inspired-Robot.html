Don’t Squish This Roach-Inspired Robot!

Melanie.Hering

June 7, 2018


Inspired by the seemingly invincible, nimble cockroach, researchers in the Harvard Microbiotics Laboratory have developed a centimeter-scale robot that can run at high speeds, jump, climb, carry twice its weight, and fall from great heights without sustaining damage. Complete with actuators that mimic the joints of real insects, the Harvard Ambulatory Microrobot, or HAMR, is highly maneuverable, and can run about 10.5 body-lengths per second.

With its versatile functions, HAMR holds potential for many different applications. The fact that so many functions are programmed into such a small bot means HAMR holds promise for work in a variety of challenging environments. Some of these include biological studies, search and rescue missions, and inspections in places unreachable or unsafe for humans.
