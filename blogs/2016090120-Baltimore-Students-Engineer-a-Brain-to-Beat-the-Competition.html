Baltimore Students Engineer a Brain to Beat the Competition

saraehardman

September 1, 2016


A group of middle school students tested their engineering and artistic skills in the annual Kinetic Sculpture Race in Baltimore this past spring. The race requires teams to build a custom, human-powered work of art and pilot it through a 15-mile course. For their submission, the students created a gigantic brain on wheels, complete with puppets representing common daydreams and insecurities found in middle school.

The students worked after school for eight months building their vehicle. Their goal was not to win, they said, but rather to express themselves creatively and overcome obstacles as a team. Their advisor, a science teacher at the school, said one of the main takeaways from competing in the race was the idea that kids can have fun learning engineering. "We need kids out there that can solve problems," he said. "This is a context for kids to learn the skills for solving challenging problems."
