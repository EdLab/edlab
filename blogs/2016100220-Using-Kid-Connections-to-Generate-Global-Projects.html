Using Kid Connections to Generate Global Projects

saraehardman

October 2, 2016


""This entire world will change in a generation. We believe that if you connect the young global citizens who inhabit it, they will turn it into one of unprecedented imagination, understanding, respect, and discovery." (About Kidnected World)

Global Connections

Kidnected World’s name says it all: this non-profit organization is dedicated to connecting kids around the globe so that they can learn, grow, and create with those from different cultures. The company wants children to not only share their experiences, but also create projects that help disadvantaged communities.

Funding Projects

In their first initiative, an app called the Wonderment, Kidnected World encourages children to brainstorm programs that would impact a community in the world. If an idea is both valuable and feasible, the company will seek donors to make it become a reality. So far, the Wonderment has seen several projects come to fruition, including a mobile library in Guatemala, a series of mime classes for deaf students in Peru, and a cultural exchange between children in the Q’eros tribe of Peru and students in Costa Rica.

Collaborative Creativity

Along with these projects, the Wonderment provides a platform for students around the globe to share creative, collaborative experiences with one another. On their website and app, children and educators from dozens of countries around the world brainstorm ideas for projects, answer challenging questions, and hold informal conversations about their different cultures.

Kidnected World believes that cultures should be preserved and shared, and that technology expands rather than dilutes communities. This organization plans to create other initiatives in addition to the Wonderment, each acting as a platform for children to share their ideas and experiences with others around the globe.
