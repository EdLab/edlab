What is Quantum Computing?

Ryan.Allen

February 2, 2018


Scientists have been able to continuously shrink down computing technology throughout the years. For instance, the smartphone you are holding has more computing power than the craft that reached the moon in 1969. But we are now approaching a physical limit on how small we can construct circuitry. The fix to this limitation is through quantum computing, which works on the subatomic level where physics operates differently.

The foundation of current computing technology is in the form of a bit, which stores data in either a 1 or a 0. All the digital innovations that we enjoy today have been built upon this binary. Now IBM has developed a method of storing data on a single atom called a qubit. The qubit, which can simultaneously be a 0 or 1, is 100,000 times more powerful compared to the traditional bit. This means that calculations and computing speeds that are unimaginable now can become a reality through this subatomic innovation.
