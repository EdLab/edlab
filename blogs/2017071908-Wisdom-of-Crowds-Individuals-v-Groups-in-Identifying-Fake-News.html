Wisdom of Crowds? Individuals v. Groups in Identifying Fake News

dinosossi2

July 19, 2017


James Surowiecki's iconic book The Wisdom of Crowds argued on behalf of how groups of people can often contribute to our collective knowledge base and decision making better than any one individual alone.

However, when it comes to identifying fake news, that may not necessarily be true.Â 

A Columbia marketing professor believes that social presence deeply impacts our perception and evaluation of fake news. In short, when we are members of a crowd, we tend to believe that others in our presence will fact check a particular item.


In psychological terms, this tendency has been deemed to be the bystander effect. In simpler terms, in previous studies in psychology, a person within a group will tend to avoid engaging in an important task because they think someone else will do that important task.
