PISA International Student Rankings

rendy

December 3, 2013

The Program for International Student Assessment (PISA) recently revealed its annual rankings of international student progress. The rankings were from test scores that included more than half a million students aged 15-16, representing 65 different countries and representing 80% of the global economy. Students from East Asian countries and territories (e.g. Singapore, Hong Kong) performed well, occupying seven out the first 10 top scores in Math, Reading and Science: Shanghai had 613 followed by Singapore (573) and Hong Kong (561). The United Kingdom was ranked 24th and the United States was ranked 36th, well below the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) averages. Since the launch of the exams more than a decade ago, the scores of most countries have decreased or remained the same. According to the OECD's deputy director of education, Andreas Schleicher, the reason for Shanghai's high scores on the exam is the drive and confidence of the students to fulfill their potential. Most US students don't concentrate a lot on their education because of the distractions such as sports and video games.
