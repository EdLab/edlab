Check out this chapter on self-directed learning by Dr. Stephen D. Brookfield in the International handbook of education for the changing world of work. The excerpt below highlights an important area of inquiry for the Institute for Self-Directed Learning: "...in self-directed learning all decisions about how and what to learn, and how or whether to consult external resources, rest with the learner. In the context of a self-directed learning effort, it is quite possible for there to be periods in which the learner decides it is most effective to place himself/herself temporarily under the control of an expert" (Brookfield, 2009, p. 2615) . Specifically, what can "experts" (teacher, mentor) do to encourage effective self-directed learning practices?
