Galileo Innovation Camps

Carmen James

August 24, 2014


"We think the world needs innovators. We also think kids who learn to explore and fail without fear—the essence of innovation—are happier, more creative and more confident when faced with life’s challenges. They don’t always get this incredible boost in other settings, but thousands of them have been getting it at Galileo since 2002." (from Galileo Website)

Summertime STEM
Galileo Innovation Camps are STEM oriented summer camps for Pre-K through 8th grade students in the Bay Area. Founded by Stanford University graduate Glen Clipp in 2002, the camp’s curriculum draws heavily on the Stanford d.School’s innovation method. There are currently over 40 locations and the camp continues to grow. Older students are able to select from 18 majors including Chefology (cooking), 3D Design and Printing, Digital Photography, and Minecraft Universe.

An Interdisciplinary Mission
Curriculum developers use the d.school’s design method to create a rigorous program that aims to cultivate "fearlessness and creative action" and inspire young people to make the world a better place. The inspiration for the camp’s mission comes from none other than Galileo himself. The camp instructors are carefully selected to support the diverse teaching demands of the camp program. Each week of the camp is organized around a different theme, and the curriculum of each day mixes art, science, outdoor, and indoor experiences. Written on the walls and peppered throughout the speech of the instructors are goals such as collaboration and courage, essential qualities for future innovators.
