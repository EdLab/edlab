Ice Hockey in Harlem

EdLab Studios

February 3, 2015


People in Harlem don’t play hockey. At least, that’s what the common conception was when Wharton Business School grad, Dave Wilk, first suggested the idea of starting a hockey program in an inner-city environment to a rather dubious executive director of an after school program located up on 119th street. Thirty years later, Ice Hockey in Harlem continues to prove the exception to the rule; providing both athletic and educational opportunities to boys and girls living in the Harlem community. Students gain the skills to skate and master a sport that’s decidedly foreign and fundamentally challenging while building confidence, grit, and tenacity over the course of a season. Students gravitate towards and discover a newfound love for subjects like geography, history, and math by using hockey as a central theme through IHIH’s after school programs. As stated in their motto, "education is the goal." The reality is palpable personal growth both on and off the ice.
