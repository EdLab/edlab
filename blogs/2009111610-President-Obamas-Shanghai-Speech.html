President Obama's Shanghai Speech

Ting Yuan

November 16, 2009

President Obama gave a speech yesterday (Nov. 16th in China) to an audience of college and post-college students at the Shanghai Science & Technology Museum. Remarks including the Q&A session can be viewed here. In his speech, he briefed the historical cooperation between the two countries and announced further educational exchanges between the U.S. and China -- the United States will dramatically expand the number of our students who study in China to 100,000. In the Q & A session, students asked interesting (and critical) questions in different areas, including the U.S.'s military strategy towards Afghanistan, the U.S.'s arm sales to Taiwan, President Obama's Nobel Prize winning experience, the possible further collaboration between Chicago and Shanghai (as sister cities), keys to success, etc. Here are a few highlights: Regarding the global warming issue, the two countries should collaborate more, because the United States and China are the world's two largest emitters of greenhouse gases, of carbon that is causing the planet to warm. Regarding the Shanghai-Chicago future cooperation -- They can better learn from each other, including the energy-efficiency technology for construction. Regarding success -- The people who I meet now that I find most inspiring and successful are people who are not only willing to work very hard but are constantly trying to improve themselves and to think in new ways, and not just accept the conventional wisdom.
