Application of Rhythm to Education

Evans.Asamoah

December 31, 2017

Learning something requires focus and time. The higher the difficulty of the material, the higher amount of focus and time you must spend. There has been a lot of talk about making education relevant to students, especially considering the platforms that are popular with students. This article focuses on the unique teaching style of Erica Buddington, an award-winning educator who uses culturally relevant pedagogy to connect with and engage her students.  For instance, she remixed one of the most currently popular songs, “Bodak Yellow” by rapper Cardi B into a geography lesson. Her idea was very successful and led to her sixth-grade students easily mastering the material. Buddington has remixed more popular songs in to teach other subjects. I understand the difficulty of learning certain things all too well having just completed my first semester in college. As a result, I try to find different ways to make the material easier to understand and memorize. I believe that Buddington's form of teaching is a great idea because when a rhythm is attached to anything, it is incredibly easy to remember. Furthermore, the songs are mainstream and students hear it regularly. Therefore, connecting the songs to a lesson means students connect the song to the lesson when it plays.
