Moment.me

LauraCostello

November 28, 2012


Moment.me is a cross-platform social aggregator that combines tweets, videos, updates, and photos from an event or moment into a single multimedia album. The system plugs into G+, Facebook, Twitter, Flickr, and Instagram and extracts content which it then assembles into "moments" based on location and time. At this point, it’s primarily been used for parties and concerts, but its accessibility and easy community make it an intriguing idea for online education.

Pros: Unlike many similar products, Moment.me can run abundantly on a tiny user base. It creates moments from users’ friends and followers even if those connections are not actively using the system. It also breaks the online social bubble by creating moments and augmenting users’ moments with content sourced from public profiles. This scrappiness allows Moment.me to bypass the "ghost town" phase of most social startups. It’s well-used and fruitful feel is echoed in the moments themselves; because of the variety of sourced content, moments have a unique panoramic feel that can’t be matched by most standalone social networks.

Cons: Moment.me respects individual privacy settings for each network, but there is still enough crowdsourced content to make one feel a bit squicky. Privacy line-walking is a strength for the system, but the weird window into strangers’ lives should inspire users to ensure their own privacy settings are up to snuff. The system is hoping to link out to social profiles so users can find and friend strangers with whom they’ve shared experiences.

Our Takeaway: Though social sites like Facebook, Twitter, and G+ continue to add new ways to share content, it’s unclear that there will ever be a single preferred social stream. Moment.me is a solution for this temporary or permanent problem and its ability to unite strangers around experiences might become more important as we increasingly interact online with people we never meet in person. Online education could especially benefit from this type of personalization. Moment.me could create communities around shared online learning experiences like MOOCs and increasing the human element of these often faceless communities might help to improve retention.

The Bottom Line: Moment.me is occasionally creepy for a good cause.
