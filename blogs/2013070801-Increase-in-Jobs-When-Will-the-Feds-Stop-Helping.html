Increase in Jobs, When Will the Feds Stop Helping?

Carmel Addae

July 8, 2013

The increase in jobs these past couple of months did not affect the overall unemployment rate. The unemployment rate still remains at 7.6%. About 195,000 jobs were created in June. In April and May, there were 190,000 jobs being created as well. The labor markets were stronger than assumed and due to the increase of jobs, the economy is doing better now. These jobs are too much that people think the Federal Reserve should pull back efforts to boost the economy. Other people would like it if the Feds pulled back because they think that the increase in jobs are not really having a positive impact on the economy right now. Interest rates are still really low to the point investors are taking risks that can hurt the economy in the long term. Overall our country's overall gross domestic product (GDP) growth remains really weak.
