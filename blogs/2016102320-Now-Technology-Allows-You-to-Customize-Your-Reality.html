Now Technology Allows You to Customize Your Reality

Jenny Abamu

October 23, 2016


Students in the MIT Media Lab’s Fluid Interface group are working to develop new technology to allow us to control the way objects can connect and respond to our actions through the Reality Editor.

"Imagine you can sit at your workspace and every object around you is not set. If you want, you can connect a knob to a light or two lights and control them," says MIT researcher Valentin Heun as he describes the Reality Editor. This prototype is built with an Open Hybrid platform, allowing invisible capabilities to become visible when you want to edit them. This technology allows users to customize the functionality of everything they come into contact with, changing objects from the designer’s vision to their own.

This hyper-connective, super responsive technology offers a new set of options for customizing learning. Imagine how much more accessible learning spaces could become for students with hearing and visual impairments, or how interactive objects could help autistic individuals better navigate new spaces.
