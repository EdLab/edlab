Tiny Computers Help Researchers Understand the Global Bee Die-Off Disaster

Ryan.Allen

April 16, 2018


Bees are an incredibly important part of the planet’s ecology and the human food chain. In recent decades though, the global bee population has been plummeting. The cause is likely related to pollution, pesticides, and global warming, but scientists still aren’t quite sure of the reason. Without understanding exactly why bee colonies are collapsing, proper solutions can’t be implemented to stop this ecological disaster.

Paulo de Souza, a researcher from the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation in Australia, has devised a way to monitor bees by attaching micro-sensors to their backs. He also installed tiny computers inside beehives that record movement through sensors. With this data, de Souza and his team have been able to visualize and measure potential threats, gaining key insights into stopping global extinction.
