Data Literacy Comes to Life for Teacher Trainees

dinosossi2

February 14, 2016


Classroom teachers are overwhelmed by a variety of assessment data. They are typically expected to improve their practice using this information. Instead, they are usually left to their own devices to analyze and interpret this data, and make modifications to their teaching. Despite such expectations, little is known about developing these data-related aptitudes, skills, and knowledge during pre-service teacher training.

A recent study of 61 pre-service teachers focused on the impact of a data literacy intervention aimed at improving their practice. It was conducted in a collaborative, technology-focused school environment. The study examined teacher self-reported attitudes and beliefs regarding data literacy.

The researchers found that the intervention helped improve participant knowledge and skills regarding data literacy. Pre-service participants reported moderately higher self-efficacy with data, and moderately stronger beliefs that assessment is both valid and makes their students more accountable. Participants also reported that using data tools—such as Microsoft Excel—was among the most useful parts of the intervention.

This study is significant because it supports the training of pre-service teachers in using data to improve assessment, professional practice, and student achievement. Collecting larger sample sizes for both pre-service and in-service teachers in future studies would provide better direction for developing improved data literacy interventions for educators.

Reeves, T. D., & Honig, S. L. (2015). A classroom data literacy intervention for pre-service teachers. Teaching and Teacher Education, 50, 90–101.
