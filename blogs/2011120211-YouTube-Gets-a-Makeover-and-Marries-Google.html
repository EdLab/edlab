YouTube Gets a Makeover and Marries Google+

Kate Meersschaert

December 2, 2011

While searching YouTube to create my daily dose of Vialogues this morning I noticed that all the people in my circles on Google+ and any videos they have shared using Google+ were listed prominently on my logged-in homepage. Wow. I actually felt more compelled to view these videos as they are "recommended" by peers who I trust, or at least know enough to add to a circle on Google+ (see screenshot below). The other big feature of this redesign is channels. Channels you you create and customize (see left side of screenshot below). In keeping with an increasing move towards personalized, customizable media, YouTube joins the ranks of responsive, media platforms like Pandora.com and Hulu, allowing users to create free streams of content that are tailored to their interests and other user's behavior. From a design and UI experience, I would say that the new YouTube is easier to navigate, friendlier feeling and less cluttered. Ads seem to be given a backseat to content which is a refreshing change. I would be very curious to know their long-term marketing strategy re: emphasizing content over ad partners. I hope this is a future trend for all media providers! As we move forward with Vialogues updates and new features, perhaps we should study the YouTube model and find-out a way to meaningfully integrate a channel-based feature to Vialogues.
