Team Drafts Only College Graduates

AbdulMalik

May 21, 2014

The NFL draft comes with a lot of hoopla and fanfare among fans, commentators and the general public. Most of the top picks in this year's draft were underclassmen. This is nothing new to professional sports as more and more student athletes are leaving college early to pursue professional careers. This is especially true for college basketball, where the term “one and done” (for standout freshmen who leave after first year) is fast becoming part of the sports lexicon. Though most teams draft purely on talent and ability, the Philadelphia Eagles, as highlighted in this Wall Street Journal article, have one criteria all their drafts must meet: they must be college graduates at the time of the draft. Statistics show that professional football teams with the most college graduates tend, on average, to perform better than those with less graduates. The success is not limited to just the team but also the individuals as college graduates tend to earn more in the course of their careers and adapt to professional life much easier. Can this trend catch on with other teams and in other sports, i.e. basketball and soccer? What are the social implications of drafting only college graduates?
