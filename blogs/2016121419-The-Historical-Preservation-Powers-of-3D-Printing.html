The Historical Preservation Powers of 3D Printing

saraehardman

December 14, 2016


Among the casualties of war can be ancient artifacts that, once destroyed, can never be replaced. This Vialogue shows historians particularly concerned with Syrian art and artifacts that are at real danger of being permanently erased by war. Yet, using 3D scanning and printing, researchers have developed a method to preserve 3,000-year-old clay tablets rescued from Syria.

First, the historians scan the tablets and reproduce them digitally on a computer. Then, they use those digital scans to 3D print exact replicas of the clay tablets. Because the digital scan can be sent via email, historians can 3D print the clay tablet from any part of the world and study it. This method is not only useful for preservation purposes, but also for purposes of cross-world study and collaboration.

Why is it important for artifacts to be preserved, and is technology such as 3D printing the best way to do so? How might 3D scanning and printing affect the future of museums and historical studies? Join the discussion on on Vialogues.
