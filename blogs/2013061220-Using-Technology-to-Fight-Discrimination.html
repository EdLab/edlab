Using Technology to Fight Discrimination

Laura Scheiber

June 12, 2013


How do you talk to young adults about difficult subjects like racism and discrimination? Are there proactive ways to facilitate dialogue? This video demonstrates how a social studies teacher incorporates digital tools into his lesson plans to encourage students to think critically about discrimination. Students then conduct internet research and build concept maps to organize their findings and personal reflections. Particularly compelling are the ways in which technology can encourage interactive and collaborative learning about nuanced subjects.

Have you used digital tools in the classroom to facilitate learning around tough controversial topics? Share your thoughts and experiences on Vialogues.
