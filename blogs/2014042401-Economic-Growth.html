Economic Growth

abagigah92

April 24, 2014

Economic growth is an increase in the capacity of an economy to produce goods and services, compared from one period of time to another. Economic growth can be measured in nominal terms, which include inflation, or in real terms, which are adjusted for inflation. Growth is important indicator of how well a country is developing. Usually, a growth in economy triggers growth in GDP. Economic growth creates the resources to support essential, quality-of-life improvements in health, education, and infrastructure. These are factors that can reduce poverty and improve the standard of living for that country's citizens. The World Bank has a mission to end extreme poverty by helping the world's poorest nation improve their economies. It is doing so by executing studies, providing technical assistance, and disbursing loans at the behest of developing countries.
