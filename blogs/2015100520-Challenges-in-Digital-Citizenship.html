Challenges in Digital Citizenship

dinosossi2

October 5, 2015


Digital technology is constantly changing. As a result, the social, political and legal mores that govern these technologies, as well as the spaces they inhabit and the people who negotiate this shifting terrain, also change.

LeeAnn Lindsey is a technology infusion and professional development coordinator who thinks a lot about digital citizenship. Digital citizenship focuses on the use of technology to spur participation in government, politics, and civil society—heady, important stuff.

Mistakes are an inevitable, even welcome, part of learning. According to Lindsey, when students make mistakes with technology, they can miscommunicate and even harm. Lindsey believes these mistakes become increasingly difficult to repair due to the accelerated pace of technological change. Just when we think we have caught up, we are behind once again.

Lindsey returns to the idea of communication as a possible solution. Students receive mixed messages about digital technology. Friends, family and teachers often have competing notions of appropriate behavior. Although we may not be on "the same page" as Lindsey says, she believes that’s okay; as long as we acknowledge differences and communicate, we can find a way forward.
