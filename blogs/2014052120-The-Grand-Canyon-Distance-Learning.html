The Grand Canyon Distance Learning

EdLab Studios

May 21, 2014


For casual tourists and school classes alike, Grand Canyon National Park is more than one of the seven natural wonders of the world; it is the perfect outdoor classroom. Ecosystems ranging from those resembling the deserts of Mexico to the boreal forests of Canada, thousands of years of human history, and billions of years of earth's history reveal themselves to visitors here as evidence of a changing planet, of delicately interwoven webs of life, of important and abiding human connection to one of the most special places on earth. Rangers in the park's Environmental Education branch witness daily the transformative learning experiences had by students participating in their field trips. Believing that the learning opportunities here are too powerful to be limited by geography or finances, these rangers have crafted a Distance Learning program that allows students all over the country to see the canyon up-close, examine its fossils, artifacts, and food webs for themselves, and interact with Environmental Education rangers through enhanced video conferencing technology. While it may not equal the Real Thing, students get a chance for personal and dynamic exploration of this environment-as-classroom; all they need is an internet connection and a little room to dance.
