Chineasy

Demetri Lales

September 22, 2013


Chineasy teaches users to understand and remember Chinese characters and phrases by associating them with a mnemonic picture. For example, the character for fire has flames drawn around it. The hope is that when users encounter these Chinese characters elsewhere, they remember the image from Chineasy to help them remember what the character means. Using images to illustrate an English translation is an interesting method for learning a new language. The site also provides an in depth explanation of why the character is shaped the way it is, the history behind the character, and whether it represents a combination of two or more characters.

Pros:

The site provides a novel way to learn Chinese characters. Each character includes information like translation, pinyin pronunciation, usage frequency, character style, a description of the character, and options to share the character image. This gives users a chance to learn all the details about a Chinese character. This time investment may help students remember the characterâ€™s English translation. The Chinese characters are organized by radicals, compounds, and phrases and each section builds upon the previous one.

Cons:

At the moment the learning content on the site is scarce, but more lessons are added periodically. While it provides an interesting way to learn Chinese characters, it would be beneficial to provide some type of assessment to see if users can recognize the characters without the illustration.

Our Takeaway:

The story behind the creation of Chineasy is intriguing. The creator ShaoLan, unable to find a learning resource to satisfy her needs, created her own, visual way to learn Chinese characters. She compares the Chinese language to Lego blocks because combining some characters can form a new word or phrase with a different meaning. In order to learn Chinese phrases users need to know what each individual character means. Chineasy applies its learning method very well but could benefit from more interactive features. It would be beneficial to include a functionality to test student learning. The site is still growing and users may find new content added periodically.

The Bottom Line:

Chineasy builds your Chinese language learning.
