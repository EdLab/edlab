Digital Promise Reports Good Advice for Schools, and for Marketers

mason0

November 17, 2014

Last week Digital Promise, a non-profit started by USG back in 2011 under section 802 of the Higher Education Opportunity Act, released a report titled Improving Ed-Tech Purchasing. The 25-page pamphlet is a digest of the more intimidatingly titled Fostering Market Efficiency in K-12 Ed-tech Procurement, a September report to DigiProm from the venerable Johns Hopkins University School of Education. The report (both the short and long version) culminate in potential courses of action schools can take to codify their edtech uptake. Interesting that the answer to chronic bureaucratic inefficiency would be a new set of rules, but the authors' tack harks the zeitgeist. Instead of talking about departmentalizing, the report suggests specific methods of planning for edtech adoption, from identification of the school's needs thru piloting to full-scale implementation. The guidelines are there, and they're adaptable. From the Johns Hopkins report - Figure 19. Ed-tech procurement Operational Framework with recommended changes. This might sound like business planning, and it pretty much is. The report's "Operational Framework" is actually a concise disentanglement of educators' concerns starting with what drives them to look for tech solutions in the first place. It's interesting to think about where we as a lab have the greatest potential in this ecosystem to support learning, even as our target audience tends to be more inclusive than the traditional definition of "educators". For instance, there is something instructive about the distinction between discovery of edtech products and their evaluation (the third and fourth blocks in the figure above). They are discrete steps on the product adoption journey, individual problems deserving individual solutions. The pedagogues in the audience might right now be thinking of reducing cognitive load for the problem solver. What are we doing to make it easier to be a learner, or an educator?
