Google Insight and Higher Ed Traffic

Jeannie Crowley

September 28, 2009

Google Insight Beta allows users to compare search volume patterns across specific regions, categories, time frames and properties. At first glance, Insight may seem very similar to Google Analytics. While Analytics allows users to track specific site data, Insight allows the users to track search data. This can be very helpful for web developers concerned with search engine optimization. One very cool feature of Insight is the explanation of peak traffic for some sites. In the image below, the graph showing searches for the term "Rutgers" spiked several times in 2006-2007. Google Insight shows the accompanying stories that explain these search spikes (usually football related). It is important to note that none of the links for the traffic spikes lead users to the Rutgers site. Why is this important? If developers of higher education sites understand what users are looking for (and when they are looking for it), they could use this data to direct traffic to their sites. Assuming Rutgers utilizes proper metadata on their site, if Rutgers University posted football results on their homepage immediately after a big game all of the traffic that usually goes to random news sites could be directed to Rutgers University. Higher education developers could use the search data from Google Insight to tailor the content of the site to funnel traffic in their direction. However, one big change that universities would have to make is to realize that the university homepages should be a dynamic space for information that will change throughout the year.
