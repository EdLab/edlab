Apps of the Future

saraehardman

December 18, 2016


Many new apps this year dove into the future with virtual reality, augmented reality, and artificial intelligence. Hold on to your seat as the EdLab Review Delorean zooms us past the five most futuristic apps we encountered in 2016.

1. Lifeliqe
Lifeliqe provides users with the most detailed 3D images of cells, sharks, and Matisse paintings they’ve likely ever seen. It also includes a wild AR component that brings science to your surroundings.

2. Expeditions Pioneer Program
This Google initiative brings cardboard VR headsets to classrooms so students can virtually travel around the world without ever leaving school.

3. Skyview
Point this AR app to the night sky and it will tell you everything about the constellations and celestial objects it sees, as well as how distant each is from earth. It’s the perfect companion for stargazing.

4. WordsEye
WordsEye is not the type of AI bot looking to take over the world. Instead, it helps you create art by recognizing your words and transforming them into visual images.

5. Sensay
An informative conversation is just a quick chat with this AI bot that connects you with another human based on what kind of advice, inspiration, or how-to knowledge you seek.
