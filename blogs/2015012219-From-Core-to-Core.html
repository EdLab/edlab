From Core to Core

Dana Haugh

January 22, 2015


Classrooms around the nation have felt the effects of the Common Core Standards implementation, and its clear that not everyone is happy about the adjustments. But one 5th grade teacher in a Washington D.C. elementary school is commending the changes, specifically those regarding the reading instruction curriculum. Ms. Wertheimer, who has been teaching for the past 17 years, believes the move toward more complex reading materials has challenged her students and kept their interest regardless of individual learning levels. Plus, after close-reading sessions and collaborative group work, students are given time to read books of their choosing. Oftentimes, these titles are at or below a student’s reading level, but that’s okay—it provides a nice counterbalance to the tough stuff.
