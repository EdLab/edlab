MLK on Jazz

Jamal Bagigah

August 12, 2010

While researching web resources for the Let Freedom Swing Project, I came across a speech Martin Luther King Jr. gave at the Berlin Jazz Festival in 1964. In the speech, Dr. King talks about the impact of jazz on peoples lives and how it relates to democracy. In the speech, MLK said "much of the power of our Freedom Movement in the United States has come from this music. It has strengthened us with its sweet rhythms when courage began to fail. It has calmed us with its rich harmonies when spirits were down."
