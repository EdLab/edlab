The Conch Shell is More than Meets the Eye

csd2126

July 10, 2017


As sharp-toothed predators and storms are no matches for the toughness of the conch shell, scientists have become interested in understanding what gives this particular type of shell its strength. Its unique geometry has a three-tiered structure with layers of grains running in opposite directions, making it difficult to break; a crack would need to travel through a sort of maze. Researchers at MIT have been able to show that the superior strength of the conch shell could be recreated using engineered materials.

Understanding how this structure works in nature allows scientists to solve engineering design problems. Using simple additive manufacturing techniques, these sturdy materials could be used to create anything from bike helmets to buildings.

The feat of re-engineering the conch shell has been to recreate not only the structure but also its performance. How else could a material similar to the structure of the conch shell be used to solve engineering design problems? Join the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
