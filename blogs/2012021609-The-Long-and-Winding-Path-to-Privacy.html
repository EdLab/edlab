The Long and Winding Path to Privacy

Kate Meersschaert

February 16, 2012

In the wake of the Path privacy scandal, AllThingsD reports that Apple has changed their privacy tune, requiring apps in the App Store to explicitly request permission to access user's private information. As a result, Path may have blazed a path to greater data collection transparency, the days of shifting, often hidden, privacy practices may be over. Personal data-reliant companies ranging from Twitter to Foursquare have all responded with more transparent and clearly stated policies. Google, it seems, was ahead of the pack with their national print and online campaign to both implicitly share the reason their service needs your data, announce a shift in their privacy policies and make an explicit call for greater awareness of internet security and the need to more closely guard your private data. As an aside, Path was apparently not on the right path as the March 2012 issue of Fast Company magazine proclaimed under the heading "On the Right Path." This seems especially poignant to me as other larger, companies seem to have gotten away with similar practices for months.
