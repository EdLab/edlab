About the J-Reader (and J-Reader Store) Project

bhughes

December 7, 2008

The next two weeks are an important two weeks for the publishing team. Jeff, Pravin, Anthony, and I will be pulling together a complete RFP for the development of the new software platform hosting Teachers College Record, NSSE, and possibly a dozen more journals in the next few years. We've been assembling the pieces of this RFP all fall, including specifications for the core publishing component, Bricolage, as well as specifications for the "front end" (what you see and use on the web). One of the remaining details is how we will build the user management part of the system, and integrate it with an e-commerce system (potentially something like the open source Magento). We think the final "platform" product — for which my code name is "J-Reader" — is an innovative journal platform, balancing quality and access in a new way. Our goal will be to use it to attract new and existing journals to the college, and in particular, journals who see value in a low-cost publishing model. That is, we are not interested in "open access" (because we think "open" journals with low revenue will not be able to attract a wider readership beyond a narrow academic audience) or totally custom (and therefore expensive) platforms. We think a middle-of-the-road solution is a viable one. After all, for the price of a meal you can get access to TCR all year! (read more for how to get involved) We have a lot of documentation lying around, so let one of us know if you'd like to take a look. For example, Pravin can share his wireframes so you can give us feedback on the updated look and organization of the site. Anthony is finishing up the project narrative that explains our needs and goals. And, if you take a look at these docs, Jeff can discuss our publishing model and future goals. Anyhow, just a heads-up. Thanks for helping if you can.
