Vision 2025

Nantwi

October 11, 2015


There is a constant argument from government officials and industry leaders about a dearth of skilled workers in today’s labor market. The talent is simply not meeting the demand. This has challenged public education systems across the country to rethink their mission and goals for students. If one of the goals of public education is to prepare students for the labor market, then our public schools have to align those goals with the proper curriculum. San Francisco’s education system is taking the lead on reimagining public education with an ambitious 10-year initiative dubbed Vision 2025.

Though it is one of the highest achieving urban school systems in California, San Francisco public schools also have one of the largest achievement gaps in the state. One of Vision 2025’s primary aims is to close this achievement gap by motivating and preparing students for jobs within their own communities. As the tech capital of the world, San Francisco is strategically placed to partner with major companies such as Google and Facebook to provide educational and employment opportunities for students. The curriculum for Vision 2025 will be built around generating student interest in coding, design, and other technologies.
