CSCW 2013 - Trend Makers and Trend Spotters

Sharon Hsiao

February 23, 2013

Trend Makers and Trend Spotters in a Mobile Application Sha, Xiaolan; Quercia, Daniele; Dell'Amico, Matteo; Michiardi, Pietro CSCW 2013, 16th ACM Conference on Computer Supported Cooperative Work and Social Computing, February 23-27, 2013, San Antonio, Texas, USA This paper considers a new mobile social-networking application with which users share pictures of “cool” things they find in the real-world to spot new trends. Unlike YouTube, Twitter, or Foursquare, the focus is on analyzing when current items on their sites become trends. So this app takes into account activities, geographical information, demographic features and all interactions. Apart from regular users, they define trend makers and trend spotters. Trends is different from popularities, so time is considered in the TrendScore metric. For instance, each week, they sort the items by the TrendScore in descending order. The results show that:

Trend Spotters prefer voting more than uploading, and when they vote, they do so in very diverse categories. By contrast, Trend Makers tend to have special care in producing quality content and “stay focused” — they upload and vote items in very specific categories. Successful trend spotters are early adopters who are attracted by diverse items, while successful trend makers attract diverse social relations (they tend to be followed by users from different social clusters).
Maybe New Learning Times (NLT) will start collecting readers' feedback (ratings or even the content uploaded by themselves) sometime soon? We may consider such a method to identify trends from the crowd.
