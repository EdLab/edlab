Human-Centered Design With Social Impact

DebraLee

March 18, 2018


"We’re pissed-off optimists. We know the world is full of injustice but we believe design can push us closer to equity." (Greater Good Studio)

Serving Vulnerable, Underserved Populations

Greater Good Studio (GGS) knows its market: non-profits, foundations, government agencies, and social enterprises that work with vulnerable or underserved populations and can execute program or organizational change. Knowledge and expertise are essential to GGS’s human-centered design process, which involves a diverse team of curious researchers, designers, and project managers with backgrounds in product, graphic, service, and space design. Simply put, GGS designs solutions, usually programs or services, that contain multiple elements underpinned by ambition.

Starting With the Ideal State

At the heart of the Greater Good Studio’s approach is behavior change. Clients are encouraged to articulate who they hope to impact and how they would like a life or an action to be different. For example, a client sentiment like: "I want to expand my program’s reach" might become "I want young women across the country to access our services." Starting with the ideal state rather than the problem or the solution is a philosophy that works for the GGS team. The proof is the myriad things they have successfully designed: apps, clothing, field trips, games, software, spaces, websites, and workshops.
