Learn by Mind Mapping

Arianna Choi

March 30, 2009

What does the Washington Post website have that the New York Times, Facebook, Amazon.com, Wikipedia, or Google doesn't have? Well, let me rephrase it to be more specific: how is the Washington Post site better than others at helping visitors learn? Evri is a widget service that helps expediting the web search process by accomplishing three important things very well: help visualize what you're learning, give a list of options for your self-directed movement, and doesn't pop dozens of new windows. It helped me explore the Texas science curriculum debate issue and, most impressively, helped me stay focused on it as I was making connections! Branch out or dig deep, it can all be done. What a great self-directed learning tool. In the case of the Washington Post website, Evri widget: 1) Shows related keywords in a simple mind map format; 2) Shows a list of link and headlines to related news articles either within washingtonpost.com or the entire web; and 3) Allows further exploration of the related topics without launching another webpage. Evri.com has an even better way of consolidating then organizing. This is what I got after typing in John Adams.
