Data Visualization Course is Available in EdLab TC and Columbia

Sharon Hsiao

February 13, 2013

I started teaching Data Visualization this spring. It's a graduate level elective course offered by Columbia School of Arts and Sciences' Quantitative Methods in the Social Sciences program. The course is focused on applied techniques to data visualization, visualization designs and analyses. I prepare 12 topics and compliment it with lab exercises and assignment practices, sort of giving a sampler of every element in data science curriculum. Visualization is a red-hot technology, but it's just a tip of the iceberg of,statistics + data mining + cognition + data wrangling + cartography + machine learning + presentation skills and color theory.

Introduction, Data Visualization, Value, Data, Image Models Vision & Colors, R-ggplot2 Social Visualization & Interactive Visualization, JavaScript & D3 Principles & Design Mapping & Cartography, Mash-ups Perception & Attention Graph & (Social) Networks Time Series & Layering Big Data, Data Journalism, Infographics, Analytics, Dashboards Multi-dimensional Data Visualization Machine Learning in Visualization Text Analysis
I just collected my first assignment back and their project proposal. I've already had a glimpse. Very impressive and very excited about it. You are welcome to check out my course website, follow my course tweets and blogs. Even audit a class or two :) But, without going down two blocks to attend my lecture, you can also check out the course on our mSchool - DV101. I found that almost each topic can be broken down into smaller classes such as Machine Learning in Visualization, Time series, Interactive Visualization, and Text Analysis. They can also be stand-alone mini-courses. So, stay tuned.
