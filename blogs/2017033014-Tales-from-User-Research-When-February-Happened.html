Tales from User Research: When February Happened

jvazquez

March 30, 2017


I may be a month late but February was worth the wait!


Quick Stats

Total users: 36966

Signups: 1029 Â +7% from January


Of the 791 vialogues created...

Returning users created 517 vialogues (44% private, 56% public)

New users created 274 vialogues (23% private, 77% public)


Most were open to everyone.

  • 501 were public (63%)
  • 290 were private (37%)


And a whopping majority outsourced their video sources.

  • 573 were from YouTube (72%)
  • 212 were from Amazon (27%)
  • 6 were from Vimeo (0.8%)


There was a lot to talk about.

  • Returning users in public vialogues using YouTube videos had the most to say (10.12 comments/vialogue on average).
  • In close second were new users in private Amazon-sourced discussions (10 comments/vialogue on average).


Standouts

This month deserves a big spotlight on discussions, because there were a lot of good ones.


First we had an active discussion on bringing humor into the classroom in the vialogue, Expecting the Positive. Notice the role the moderator plays here. He gives his participants a bunch of prompts in one comment, adds thoughts throughout, and occasionally replies to people. Could his attentiveness have raised the temperature of the discussion?


Next we have a series of very specific mods.

  • In Global Inequality, the mod not only quantifies his participation expectation, but also gives students several different angles to take in their comments.
  • In Southern Words, we see again the use of comment requirements (all notably at 00:00). While it doesnât exactly seem to have panned out in her favor, it was a noble attempt at hosting a dynamic conversation.
  • Finally, in Edgar Allen Poe Biography, we see a mod who displays more leniency in her comment rule. She also provides a set of example comment starters students could use (i.e., Something that surprised me wasâ¦, Whyâ¦). Though it looks like her students have yet to take to the vialogue, sheâs set them up for a thought-provoking activity.


As proof positive that Vialogues blurs boundaries between pedagogies, an intrepid mod hosted an unprecedented active reading discussion in Social Inequality. By grouping timecodes into one comment, each learner was able to keep a clear record of their observations and the mod could easily grasp participation at a glance. Points for creativity! Â
