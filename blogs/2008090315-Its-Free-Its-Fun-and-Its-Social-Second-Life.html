It's Free, It's Fun, and It's Social: Second Life

Frank Webster

September 3, 2008

Twenty years ago many corporations relocated their r&d ventures to North Carolina's "Research Triangle" to take advantage of the low cost of living and conservative labor laws. Today they might consider a virtual reality. "It's free, it's fun and it's social," is how Robert Curet, the owner of Little Wonder Studio in Burbank, CA, describes the advantages of development and marketing in Second Life in this article in BusinessWeek Business, and Startups, in Second Life. Not only can he design a prototype in Second Life in half the time it would take him in his conventional shop, but he can interact with colleagues and clients as well.
