Attack on Pakistani School

Carmel Addae

December 29, 2014

The Taliban in Pakistan, in their zest for revenge, recently attacked a school run by that country's army. The end result was grim: 148 people dead, including 133 students. An additional 250 were wounded. The attack really struck a chord with many Pakistanis, who have been used to terrorist attacks in recent years. The backlash could give the government and armed forces the power to wage a successful attack against the Taliban. How can the government insure that all other schools in the country remain safe from Taliban attacks?
