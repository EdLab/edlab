Luck Be An Astronaut: Designing and Playtesting Mars Games

veronicablack

May 2, 2017

Over the past few weeks the Design Team has been gearing up for the Mars event in the Learning Theater. Researching, prototyping, and building Mars Space Ships, Crew badges, and Space Card Games. While in the development stage of the Mars design process our team was asked to think about an activity the Mars Crew (participants) could play as a learning activity during the event. The game would provide knowledge about Mars, support collaborations among the crew members, be fun and easy to learn, and to simulate what real Mars Crew might have to face on a trip to the red planet.    


Designing an educational but enjoyable games has been a fun challenge for our team. We were inspired by the Kickstarter funded game “Exploding Kittens” and a similarly popular card game, “Fluxx”.

Exploding Kittens is a fast paced, silly game that is easy to learning, and adaptable for our Mars needs. Looney Labs Fluxx Cards Games is a series of “card games with ever-changing rules” but with simple mechanics, Draw One, Play One. We play tested both games -- for science! We found that “Exploding Kittens” had more opportunities to include factoids and create collaboration among the crew members.

In initial testing we tried to make the antagonistic nature of the game more collaborative. Rather than try to defeat your opponents, we made a “Lucky Astronaut” card so people on the same team could complete “Mission Matches” together. Each “Mission Match” has a useful factoid taken from the book “How We’ll Live on Mars” by Stephen Petranek. Crew-mates can also rescue each other from “Critical Failures” by using a “Diffuse” card. Just like in space, there is still a high likelihood of death though!

The game we developed is called Lucky Astronaut. The Objective is to collect the most Mission Matches with the fewest or no deadly Critical Failures. The Crew can play the game as many times as necessary in the time allotted and average the scores together to determine the success of their mission as they journey to Mars.
