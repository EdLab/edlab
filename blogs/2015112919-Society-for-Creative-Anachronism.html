Society for Creative Anachronism

EdLab Studios

November 29, 2015


The first time you attend an SCA event, chockablock with armor-clad knights, bardic circles, and men claiming to be kings, it's likely you’ll have some questions. Namely who are these people, what are they doing, and what are they wearing? "Dressing up in old clothes from back then--it seems funny the first time around, a little odd" says local Chatelain, Lady Sofya, "but it's about bringing forward possibilities." The SCA, or Society for Creative Anachronism encourages its 60,000 worldwide members to do exactly that. Since its inception in 1966, participants and volunteers in the non-profit historical reenactment group have explored their pre-17th century passions through gatherings, performances, combat training, arts and science classes, and even a two week long battle called the Pennsic Wars. After choosing a persona from the middle ages to embody, years are spent not only studying and teaching but truly experiencing medieval European culture--what it was like to wield a sword, make lace by hand, map the heavens using an astrolabe, or attend court within a kingdom. And for many members, the skills learned translate into everyday life; inspiring them to exercise chivalry and forge onward in their endless quest for knowledge. Events such as the annual Medieval Festival at the Cloisters offers the opportunity for members to share with they’ve learned with the greater community, breathing new life into the organization and assuring that these traditions live on.
