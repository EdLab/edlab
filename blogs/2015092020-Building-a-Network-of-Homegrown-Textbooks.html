Building a Network of Homegrown Textbooks

Jenny Shen

September 20, 2015


A school in Italy is making sure that everyone is a part of the educational content creation process. Forgoing the usual system of purchasing textbooks from big publishing companies and curriculum through mass-produced networks, ITIS Majorana di Brindisi invests its money in developing and printing its own textbooks. Its national network of teachers works together to create teaching content amidst evolving curricula and lessons in schools across the country.

But it’s not just this textbook creation process that makes ITIS Majorana di Brindisi unique. The school also provides an array of technology options to its students so that they can create interactive and multimedia books in class. The classrooms are set up with wheeled chairs that students use to circulate around the room so that they can shuffle their groups around with ease.

ITIS Majorana di Brindisi emphasizes the rejection of a traditional curriculum model in favor of one that allows students and teachers to produce their own books, individualize their learning, and keep the curriculum from becoming stagnant through their "Book in Progress" program.
