Price Ceiling Effect

abagigah92

May 16, 2013

Government interference comes into play when a price is set below the equilibrium price. Equilibrium is the point whereby the demand meets supply. This is called a price ceiling; a price ceiling sets the highest amount charged of any goods or services. Price ceiling mostly occurs in rent controls, oil industries or in the market of organ transplants. Price ceilings are set to help consumers on the merit of equity because the equilibrium price of such goods and services are too high. Usually, since the price is low, it creates a scenario where the demand is higher than the supply, which causes a shortage. The sale of organs is a very controversial topic but the government has stopped the sale of it in any form. This is because organs are too expensive and might only favor the rich. The government believes everyone should have the equal chance of survival whether the individual is rich or not. Since no one can buy or sell organs, the price is at zero. This creates a lot of demand for organs because it is free but the biggest problem is the market is not efficient because organs cost thousands of dollars. Furthermore, price ceiling in rent control has the same effect as in organ transplant. The government setting a low price will only favor buyers that can afford to pay for that low price. Buyers willing to be more are left out of the situation. This is what happens in New York City, landlords cannot charge a lot of money for rent, so most of the times the apartments are not well kept.
