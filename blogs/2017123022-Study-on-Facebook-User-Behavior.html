Study on Facebook User Behavior

idrissa10

December 30, 2017

In an effort to combat growing criticism about depression on its platform, Facebook recently conducted a study to debunk the allegations. The research was conducted primarily because former Facebook employees grow increasingly worried about the platform and its effect on society. The researchers found that using and interacting with Facebook is healthier than just scrolling through the news feed. This prevents a user from comparing themselves to their friends. The two experiments at Michigan University and Carnegie Mellon University. The researchers in Michigan asked participants to scroll their newsfeed. Participants reported that they felt worse off emotionally than when they began the experiment. The test at Carnegie Mellon invited participants to interact with their Facebook friends while browsing the site. Participants were more cheerful and exuberant than those at Michigan. The researchers, therefore, concluded that communicating and interacting with friends on Facebook is better than just scrolling. Though this is an interesting experiment, it was conducted and sponsored by Facebook. As a result, do you trust the findings?
