Can’t Attend Class? Send Your Robot

jvazquez

April 19, 2013


Third grader Miranda Garcia suffers from Lupus, an immune-weakening disease that often prohibits her from attending school. Like many students in her situation, she was confined to home schooling until one day, when a 4-foot, 4-wheeled solution rolled into her life.

The VGo is a remote-controlled stand-in that lets Miranda go to class while remaining safely at home. With the robot, she is able to once again connect to her peers (who display commendable amounts of maturity and support regarding the situation) and feel as though she is a part of the team. Her mother reports that her reading levels are up since getting the robot.

Miranda is the first student in her San Antonio school district to test drive the VGo, but will unlikely be the last, as 30 other school districts in the country are piloting the robot program.

Says her classmate, Esther Rose, "When she’s on the screen, it makes me feel happy ‘cause she’s here at school."
