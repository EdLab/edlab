“The Circle” v. The Public Square: Facebook’s Approach to Urban Design

dinosossi2

July 12, 2017


Georges Seurat, eat your heart out.


In response to the recent opening of Apple’s Circle headquarters in Cupertino, CA, Facebook responded by unveiling its own plans for a redesign of its Menlo Park campus.


Think arms race, but change weapons to green space and trees...


Given its reinvigorated attempts to promote community in response to the fallout from the recent presidential election, community is also the aim of Facebook's revitalized campus.


Facebook is planning to include additional community services, improved transit support, and additional housing. And in contrast to Apple’s sealed off garden, Facebook’s campus is intended to be open to the public.


This is all good in theory.


However, nightmare stories of traffic congestion in the Bay area and outrageous housing prices may end up reinforcing exclusion by race and socioeconomic status. 


Maybe some gardens end up being walled off even if they are meant to do otherwise...
