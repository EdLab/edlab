Trends in Ed, 4.22.09

Arianna Choi

April 22, 2009

Green School Design Today is Earth Day. I have a paper cup on my desk right now and this cup containing 10% post-consumer recycled fiber is boldly staring at me. It's trying to say something to me or maybe it's just my conscience questioning whether I'm doing enough.

Earth Day, celebrated April 22, is a day designed to inspire awareness and appreciation for the Earth's environment. It was founded by U.S. Senator Gaylord Nelson as an environmental teach-in in 1970 and is celebrated in many countries every year. — Wikipedia
As far as blogging about Trends in Ed goes, here is a story to share. Green School in Bali, Indonesia serves students from preschool years through grade eight. The school's emphasis on holistic, sustainable education makes it uniquely relevant to us today. To learn more watch the CNN report on how the ‘buildings' were built with bamboo below— plus, there's no air conditioner and the school community eats what it grows. Green School, it's as simple, straight, and true as this. Students Design School for Year 2050 Geometry students from Mountlake Terrace High School, in Mountlake Terrace, Washington, participated in a school design challenge posed by their math teacher. This project-based learning unit was done seven years ago, but I bet you — especially AfterEd's School ReDesign crew — would be impressed to find out how much these students had prepared for their final presentations.
