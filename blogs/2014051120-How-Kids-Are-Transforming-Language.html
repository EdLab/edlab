How Kids Are Transforming Language

Carmen James

May 11, 2014


Stephen Fry and Jonathan Ross, in this laugh-out loud piece, discuss the peculiar side of language. Where do words come from? Why do they change so much? How are words invented?

Ross begins with a quote, "Shabby tools are always deteriorating" and they are not able to stand the "test of time" (A quote from T.S. Eliot's "Burnt Norton" (1935). The two men agree that Eliot’s point in some ways seems true, but Ross worries that we are losing hold of our language and that it is rapidly falling into decline because of the way young people communicate with each other using technology.
