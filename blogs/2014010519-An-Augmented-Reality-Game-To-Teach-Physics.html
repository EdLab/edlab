An Augmented Reality Game To Teach Physics

jvazquez

January 5, 2014


iPads in education have gotten a lot of press this past year. From the notorious (two words: L.A. rollout) to the reputable (iPads and autism research, for example), few can be surprised at the amount of attention that tablet technology has captured. Part of that attention is due in part to those watching the growing trend of augmented reality apps.

By just switching on an iPad, college students in Quebec have a charged particle beam in their hands and are ready to gingerly split the walls of a mysterious ancient Sumerian chest with it. So goes the premise of this augmented reality game for physics students. Parallel, the name of the game, cleverly teaches students how charged particles travel in electric and magnetic fields with its mission-based formula.
