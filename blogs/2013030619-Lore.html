Lore

LauraCostello

March 6, 2013


Lore is a learning management system that embodies the idea that real education comes from connections and communication among people. Online learning is often characterized as a lonely endeavor, but Lore takes cues from popular social media sites to bring community to its online courses. The central discussion roll, robust multimedia sharing options, and individual student profiles form the core of Lore social interactions in class. Peter Thiel is a fan of the platform and used it to teach his Stanford course last year. He cited a shift in student dynamics as a major benefit to the platform and Lore seems to deliver a course space primed for digital learners.

Pros:

The beauty of Lore is that its spare and share-ready interface could easily be used as either a standalone LMS or the social-digital component of a blended course. Perhaps this is the Thiel cash component, but Lore also offers surprisingly comprehensive customer service for an education startup. Instructors opting in to the system are lined up with a "Lore Concierge" who acts as a personal touchpoint for any questions or ideas.

Cons:

Lore has a post-timeline sensibility that may not appeal to all learners. Despite recent efforts to improve metadata attached to individual posts, the Lore course is a constantly evolving beast rather than a static and thorough record of activity.

Our Takeaway:

Far from resting on its worthy build, Lore seems to be reaching out to the design and learning community in the form of a wide-ranging, tumblr-esque external blog called exp.lore. This casual exploration and development of peripheral disciplines that are definitely tied to the evolution of education, like animation and design philosophy, is an indication that Lore may be looking beyond the zenith of the social LMS. Lore is intriguingly committed to riffing on the idea of communication in learning and seems willing to innovate.

The Bottom Line:

Lore is an LMS developed for learners who are lost without a "like" button.
