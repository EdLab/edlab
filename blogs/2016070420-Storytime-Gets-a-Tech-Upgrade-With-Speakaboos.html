Storytime Gets a Tech Upgrade With Speakaboos

Jenny Shen

July 4, 2016


The bedtime story experience has evolved rapidly with the advent of multimedia storytelling apps. Speakaboos, a reading app for elementary school students, makes it an even more interactive experience. With several elements of embedded technology, Speakaboos brings new life to the experience of learning how to read.

Pros:

Speakaboos has a library of over 100 stories, featuring everything from dinosaurs and robots to familiar Sesame Street characters and classics like Jack and the Beanstalk. Children can either read these stories aloud themselves, or choose to have the app read it to them with playful interactions on each page. Some stories offer a Sing to Me feature, which allows users to play the video and follow along to the unique music and lyrics of the story.

Speakaboos showcases fun and colorful illustrations that make each story age-appropriate and easy to follow. The way the app highlights each word as it reads to the child, and the way it allows users to click a word to hear its pronunciation, make the reading experience productive and engaging. And the interactivity of each page allows young readers to extend their learning and challenge themselves beyond the given text.

Cons:

Although Speakaboos quickly highlights the different points of interaction on each page, the marking disappears almost immediately, with only the link-select cursor showing up when you hover to indicate an interaction point. For young readers, this may not be a clear enough clue and they could miss potential opportunities to play with the text.

Speakaboos offers a free 30-day trial for all new users, but after that trial ends, it will cost $7.99 a month or $69.99 annually. For schools and libraries, there are bulk pricing options, but the cost might be a bit steep for individual families, especially if voracious readers manage to get through the majority of the library in a few months.

Our Takeaway:

It's about time that the learning-to-read experience feature unique tech elements that add interactivity and scaffolding for elementary school students. Speakaboos does a great job providing a variety of stories to make storytime both exciting and exploratory.
