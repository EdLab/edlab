Library Services, Summer 2017: Making Compulsory Library Instruction Compelling

Allen.Foresta

March 13, 2018

{{unknown}}
{{unknown}}
    Some examples of areas of research with which students sought our help:

  • Assistance learning how to search online journals for articles on classroom leadership, 6-12 schools, and alternative grade configuration.
  • Assistance with investigating barriers to homeless children attending and excelling in school in NYC, and evaluating the extent to which existing legislation and policy protects homeless students; determining how NYC's laws and policies can best adhere to human rights recommendations for supporting homeless children's educational pursuits; and finding detailed information/ data evaluating programs for homeless children (e.g., transportation programs, tutoring programs, food programs, individual school initiatives to install laundry machines or open study spaces year-round).
  • Full text navigation.
  • "Deep dive" in motor learning.
  • Seeking phenomenological studies on teacher's experience teaching with secular spirituality or mindfulness.
  • Guidance on doing a literature review regarding occupational therapy within the field of art education.
  • Immigrant integration and English language challenges.
  • Literature review on outdoor education.
  • Novice teachers and spatial theory.
  • Literature review on the benefits of breastfeeding.
  • Assistance with a text analysis to determine what text features make one text more complex than others; will require sample texts of different complexity levels to start with, probably primary and college level readings, purposefully selected to meet the reading requirements of a particular grade/level.
  • The impact of technology in social skills development in children and adolescents.
During Summer 2017, Services staff provided assistance to our users in the following categories:
  •  Senior librarians and services associates handled 1,122 in-person and email queries during the Summer A and B terms.
  • Senior librarians responded to 296 queries of various kind--reference and information questions, requests for one-on-one research consultations, inquiries concerning library hours and operations, questions and problems pertaining to room reservations, circulation issues--that came to us via the library's email Support Request service.
  • Senior librarians and services associates delivered 20 research consultations to individuals or small groups.
  • Librarians and associates presented 4 course-specific library information sessions, either in the classroom or in library spaces, for a t0tal of 48 attendees.
