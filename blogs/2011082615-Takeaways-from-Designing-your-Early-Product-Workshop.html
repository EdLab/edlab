Takeaways from Designing your Early Product Workshop

Kate Meersschaert

August 26, 2011

While slightly disappointing as a "workshop," last night's event at General Assembly turned-into a great Q&A session with an eloquent entrepreneur, San Kim. As I mentioned briefly in my post announcing this event, San is the founder of Easel and co-creator of ShowMe, an app that allows educators to create their own interactive tutorials in the Khan Academy style. Listed below are thoughts & resources that San shared with the group that I thought would be valuable to explore as we examine and refine our development of new tools. San's thoughts on early product design... -Build fast & iterate quickly b4 you run out of money. -Do whatever it takes to prove-out your concept... including: Survey/interviews, paper or wire-frame prototypes. -Important to ask the right questions early-on. -As an entrepreneur you need to design experiments to proof your concept. -Have to take survey results w/ a grain of salt - people will tell you what you want to hear. -If I could go back I would build a fake vid to test the tool before investing in tech or use a landing-page test beta invite (here is a great ex of this!). -Get non-binding letters of intent from potential investors/large accounts to test the willingness of people to invest their time & $ in the product. -Dangerous to start building against competitors - people don't always know what they need or want (very Apple!). -Need to see the "boulders on the road" - what are the insanely obvious things about the tool that you as a creator/developer might overlook. -Don't reinvent the wheel every time - freely borrow from other existent tools that are successful! Resources: Keynotopia.com Patterntap.com Dribbble.com Yahoo design elements Lucidchart.com (free) Google human interface guidelines Usertesting.com crowd-sourced usability testing. Adobe ideas Balsamiq.com Books: The Design of Everyday Things Don't Make Me Think (2 free chapters on UI/UX testing available online as a pdf)
