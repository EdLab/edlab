Library Website Report (3/20/19 - 8/20/19)

Christine.Odenath

September 24, 2019



In case the 2 images above don't load, you can view the report as a google doc here, or download a PDF version of the report from the research Rhizr. I've also provided some highlights & key takeaways below:


Highlights:

  • Total visits: 62,812 (44,116 off campus | 18,696 on campus) 
  • Pageviews: 150,704 pageviews
  • Returning visits: 43,378 (28,880 off campus | 14,498 on campus) 
  • Average # actions per visit: 4.6 (170 max actions in one visit)
  • Average visit duration: 4 min 27s
  • Total searches: 55,841 (41,052 off campus | 14,789 on campus)
  • Room Reservations Created: 4,615


Key Takeaways (Site Traffic):


> In cases of both new and returning website visitors, off-campus visits are more common (~70% of all visits).


> Library visits drop off on weekends and between the hours of 1am-6am, which suggests site users maintain decent work-life balances. 


> A bit more than half of all visits resulted from direct navigation to the site. Indirect routes to the library website (via search engine or website referrals) suggest users were purposeful in their routes to the library website.


Key Takeaways (Search):


> Nearly 80% of all on-site searches took place off-campus.


> Of the 14K searches that utilized the library’s categorizations to organize their search, over half (55%) selected Articles, 25% selected books, and 14% selected journals. 


> Top search terms indicate that users are more likely to search for specific titles than broad learning topics. Also given the repetition of search for the Counseling Adults book, it may be useful to put more emphasis on course reserves. Also, i feel like course reserves should land on “My Classes” rather than “my requests” 


Key Takeaways (Header/Footer Discovery): 


> In terms of site navigation, the “Resources” page was the most viewed (7.2k pageviews), with 56% of Resource page traffic ultimately navigating to the E-Journals and Databases page. This insight should be combined with search behavior insights to gather notes on site UX. 


> 60% of traffic through the “About” page went to the Room Reservations Guidelines page, suggesting design could be improved to more directly guide users to Room Reservations.
