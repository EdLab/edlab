Trends in Ed, 11.17.2010 : Education in Obesity

Angela Lee

November 17, 2010

This Trends in Ed is to the latest education controversy: obesity. The Obama government has been putting in a strong effort on educating and making America a healthier country. First lady Michelle Obama even started a campaign named Let's Move to advocate a healthier lifestyle for all Americans. But what does it mean when someone is obese? What is fat and what is skinny? I grew up in Taiwan, a country obsessed with dieting. and where the concept of being fat is taken entirely to another level. I always worry about my weight before I return home to Taiwan and I am always teased about being fat when I am home. Truth is, by America standards, I am nowhere near fat. I often wonder how I feel confident, beautiful, and fit while I'm in America, but feel “obese” when I return home. The video above shows a response to the Obama administration's recommendations that all children receive a BMI status check. The first lady unveiled her Childhood Obesity Action Plan earlier this year and it includes plans such as building more sidewalks, curbing time with digital media and removing deep fryers out of schools. I believe everyone is born with a different body structure due to genes, DNA, among others, and for America to fight the “obesity” problem, education with about which foods are healthy and which ones are not should be the first step. I want to share this already popular video of Jamie Oliver on Ted talking about healthy eating. This video serve the purpose as a great educational tool for people to understand the difference between looking fat (obese) and having a healthy eating habit. What do you think?
