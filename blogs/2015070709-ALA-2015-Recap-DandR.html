ALA 2015 Recap D&R

Meredith Powers

July 7, 2015

ALA 2015: We did it! We thought about it! We picked out the most relevant stuff! Part 1: The Future of Digital Books Part 2: Book & News Displays Activity #1 - Design a Display Questions to answer on the blog 1. What do you want patrons to get out of the book and news collections in the cafe? 2. How would you design the display (style, format, frequency of updates, etc.) to achieve that goal? 3. Anything else you’d think would be a good use of the cafe collection space? Things to consider, maybe! What’s the point of the cafe book display? Maybe you want to: - get patrons interested in the library’s resources - start a conversation, get patrons talking to each other or to us - get patrons interested in something specific - something else entirely—you choose! How will you achieve that? You might want to think about some practical questions, like: - How frequently should we update the displays? - What medium would you use to create the displays? (posters, screens, etc.) - Do you want consistency from display to display? - How important is the collection vs. the display? Part 3: Automating Workflows Part 4: Data Driven Collection Development Activity #2 - Curating Collections 1. How would you determine if a resource is successful over a period of time? 2. What metrics would you use to determine which materials should be weeded or discarded? 3. Interpret real data! Look at two spreadsheets: Items brought in by patrons and Items curated by librarians. (Note: “Items brought in patrons” means that these are items we purchased because of patron demand—they picked the items. “Items curated by librarians” means just that—books we purchased based on our own librarians’ recommendations and planning.) - Draw some conclusions about this data, or pose some hypotheses that might help you frame research questions about collection development, our library’s collection, patrons, etc. (The file size was too large to attach the slides, but I'll link them in the comments!)
