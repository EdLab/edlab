Yahoo buys Summly

abagigah92

March 28, 2013

Summly, a start-up company in London was the idea of young entrepreneur Nick D'Aloisio. Summly is a mobile application that gives a new approach on reading news articles. The approach is to make it easier to read news article and other content on smart phones. Nick started to work on Summly when he was 15. Summly redefines the news on mobile by generating summaries of hundreds of sources which makes reading on the go fun and easy. Moreover, Summly allows you to select your favorite topics which could vary from ESPN to the Wall Street Journal. After selecting the topics, Summly has a system of algorithms which generates the concise summaries to read. Much like Summly, New Learning Times (NLT) is taking the same approach to push learning forward by making it possible and easier to access news articles on mobiles. While it is hard to keep up with the landscape of learning, Summly and NLT provide readers with the opportunity to access knowledge on mobiles anytime, especially in our ever growing fast paced world where access to news has to be current and available at all times. Yahoo's purchase of Summly makes it the fifth small acquisition it has made in the last five months. The acquisitions by Yahoo are seen as a big step toward an effort to provide more services for mobile phones and tablets. Marrisa Mayer, the CEO of Yahoo, believes that Yahoo's recent focus on technology shows an increasing importance in the area that she believed the Internet company had been neglecting.
