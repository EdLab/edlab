Research Digest: Mobile Learning Matrix

Lu Wang

February 9, 2012

Park, Y. (2011). A Pedagogical Framework for Mobile Learning: Categorizing Educational Applications of Mobile Technologies into Four Types. International Review of Research in Open and Distance Learning: 12(2).pp. 78-102. In this research paper, the author introduced a matrix describing four different types of learning processes based on transactional distance theory. Transactional distance refers to a psychological separation between the learner and the instructor. Compared to e-learning which has limited flexibility of device accessibility, mobile learning has higher portability, individuality and interactivity. It seems that the trend that m-learning is leading to is ubiquitous learning (u-learning), which “involves learning in an environment where all students have access to a variety of digital devices and services, including computers connected to the internet and mobile computing devices.” This chart shows the flow of e-learning, m-learning, and u-learning. The author further developed a pedagogical framework consisting of four types of mobile learning activities, namely high transactional distance and socialized m-learning activity (HS), high transactional distance and individualized m-learning activity (HI), low transactional distance and socialized m-learning activity (LS), and low transactional distance and individualized m-learning activity (LI). Under each category, the author listed examples of educational technology products currently in use, so as to illustrate more clearly how this pedagogical framework is applied in real-world learning environment. EdLab Relevance: This study is highly aligned with the EdLab foundations specified in a recent EdLab seminar. As EdLab is gearing toward adapting teaching and learning environment to the age of information technology, the insights of this paper direct a clearer way forward. The relevance of the article is also shown in its theoretical basis, which can be further adapted and incorporated in the mSchool platform.
