Electronic Heart Glove

Elysee Dougan

March 4, 2014

A pacemaker is a device that is placed in one's chest or abdomen to help regulate abnormal heart beat by sending electronic pulses to the heart. People suffering from arrhythmias usually have this device implanted. Recently, scientists have created a new device that is worn around the heart like a glove that sends pulses to the heart and can keep a heart beating regularly for an indefinite amount of time. The membrane has a “spider-web-like network of sensors and electrodes” which monitors the heart and delivers shocks that will help maintain a healthy heart rate if the heart starts to fail or show problems. Although this device has not been tested on an actual human, it should be available for humans in the next 10-15 years. A prototype was tested on a rabbit's heart and is shown to work outside of the rabbit's body in a nutrient and oxygen rich solution. Amazingly this product was made by a 3D printer. This new invention will replace the pacemaker.
