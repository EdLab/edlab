I would like to contribute by writing about Professor Rose Luckin from University College London (UCL). Back in 2011, I had the pleasure to meet with Professor Luckin. We talked about the work of the London Knowledge Lab and discussed ideas for an international collaborative EdLab Design Event.


Although we never identified a project to partner on I followed Professor Luckin's work in A.I. in education and learning analytics.  I was pleasantly surprised when her book, "Machine Learning and Human Intelligence," was featured in Quartz (which I found on Melanie's High Five a few months back). 


According to Professor Luckin, A.I. should compel schools to expand their narrow understandings of intelligence. She identifies seven kinds of intelligences important for learners:


Interdisciplinary academic intelligence

Social intelligence

Meta-knowing

Metacognition

Meta subjective intelligence

Meta contextual intelligence

Accurate perceived self-efficacy


So what does this mean for education?
