A Standard By Any Other Name…

Dana Haugh

November 3, 2014


Mentioning the phrase "Common Core Standards" in the education world is likely to elicit strong and sometimes aggressive comments regarding the NGA’s bold attempt to standardize K-12 education across the country. Those outside the education sector with no clear idea of the term’s meaning or application simply know it as being a "Very Bad Idea." The overwhelmingly negative response to the Common Core initiative has made those three words irrefutably, and perhaps unfairly, associated with everything wrong in education today. However, one study found that by simply removing the words "Common Core" from the uniform standards initiative, the majority of respondents voted in favor of its blanket implementation. Has toxic branding prevented widespread appeal? Or is it just a Very Bad Idea?
