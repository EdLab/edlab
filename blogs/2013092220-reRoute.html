reRoute

EdLab Studios

September 22, 2013


Organized by the New Economics Institute, this inaugural convergence brings together youth and student leaders from all over North America to develop a new way of thinking about our economic system and to build and develop a solidarity economy together. Workshops, panels and walking tours of the neighborhood economies showcase real life successes and failures. The three-day gathering creates a platform for individuals and groups to share experiences, strategies, and tools for building an alternative economy. By discussing topics such as fossil fuel divestments, creating a just and sustainable food system, and reforming the economics curriculum, this grassroots-style education aims to change attitudes and opinions through communities all around the country so that learners and citizens can work together for a more democratic society.
