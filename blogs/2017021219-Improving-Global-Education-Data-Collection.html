Improving Global Education Data Collection

Nantwi

February 12, 2017


One of the great promises of the technology revolution is the potential for data to change how we address complicated and pressing global problems. As the United Nation works to achieve its Sustainable Development Goals for education by 2030, its Institute for Statistics is driving the organizationâ€™s efforts to improve data collection, especially in developing countries, where education data is often missing or inaccurately reported.

Since data comes from different sources like governments, non-profits, and the private sector, the Institute works with national governments to reconcile all the different data sets. They also work with statisticians and data practitioners to find and use the most appropriate data that can inform policy decisions. Better data collection can lead governments and NGOs to answer lingering questions around student enrollment, expenditures, and learning outcomes.

What are some ways to improve data collection in developing nations? How do practitioners create specific indicators for countries since each has their own unique schooling system or culture? Join the conversation on Vialogues.
