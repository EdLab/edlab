The changing face of America's youth

Carmel Addae

July 14, 2011

The changing face of America's youth According to this recent article on CNN, there is a growing shift in the youth population of our nation. The youth population of America is now more diverse than at any time with the Hispanic youth population rapidly growing. The two states where this change has been obvious are California and New Mexico. White children are the minority when compared with all the races or ethnic groups. The number of white children dropped by 4.3 million during the past decade while the number of Hispanic and Asian children increased by 5.5 million with Hispanics making a large bulk of that number. The changing demographics have yet to transfer to education. Hispanic children are the lowest ranked children in terms of graduating high school and college acceptance rates. What does this all translate to? Like other aspects of American life, the youth demographics was bound to change since soon enough America will be a minority majority country. I think this speaks to the diversity of our ever-changing nation. Though there is a big shift in terms of the demographics, it seems the growing segments of the population are still behind in terms of education, and careers, among others.
