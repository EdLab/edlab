Fossil Rocks Are In The Ground Tonight

Nantwi

July 20, 2014


A lot of the current discourse in K-12 circles revolves around improving student outcomes in science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM). The performance of US students on recent global assessment exams has led to an outcry that our schools and education policies are failing to engage students deeply in the STEM subjects. How can we buck this trend?

Like most of the general public, students are very much in the know about pop culture. Educators can leverage this platform to generate student interest in learning about STEM. This is the primary goal of Fossil Rock Anthem, an educational music video that aims to teach students about natural science (e.g., fossils, rocks, soil). The video samples the popular song, "Party Rock Anthem" by hip-hop group LMFAO. Using the song’s catchy and up-tempo rhythm, the song’s lyrics are displayed in text throughout the video for students to grasp the content.

Is this an effective teaching strategy? What are some other ways to generate student interest in STEM? Join the conversation on Vialogues.
