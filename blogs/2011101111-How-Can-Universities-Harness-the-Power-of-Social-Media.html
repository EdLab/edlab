How Can Universities Harness the Power of Social Media?

Kate Meersschaert

October 11, 2011

Teachers on Twitter, farmers on Facebook, tweens on Tumblr... social media has permeated all areas of modern existence. Like email many decades earlier, social media is a way of life for humans the world-over. For institutions of higher education, it is not a question of will you have a social media presence, but HOW will you create a presence on the social web. The social media race has become almost "evolve-or-die" with those late to the social media game risking missed "tweets" and "likes" from both existent, former and prospective students, parents and even donors. Not only for promotion, social media is a viable way for professors to communicate with students, creating a vibrant "backchannel" for digital natives, who are used to getting the news from their smart phones and media feeds. This recent Mashable article lays-out 6 strategies for maximizing the efficiency and effectiveness of social media campaigns for higher ed. The basic tenets include the following: 1) Develop a Strategy and Set Goals - this can include identifying your target audience(s) and establishing a schedule for posting regularly. 2) Pick and Choose Your Platforms - decide who your audience from the above step and then select your platforms accordingly. For example, not every institution needs a Tumblr, which tends to skew young, but perhaps your alumni would benefit from a Ning site or a YouTube channel for potential and existent students. 3) Empower and Support Individual Departments - Don't be afraid to allow each department to have their own social media presence (as long as they are all coordinated!). Here is a great example of this working effectively at BU. 4) Put Guidelines in Place - Be sure all social media falls-under established university-wide social media guidelines and best-practice. Here are some a great examples and resource for establishing these parameters: Princeton University's social media guidelines and Eduguru 5) Develop a Consistent Voice Across Platforms - Identify and stay true to your university's unique "brand." 6) Communicate Across Campus - Perhaps you can develop a regular social media cross-campus meeting? Be sure to avoid the social media "silo" effect!
