The Domino Project

Kate Meersschaert

May 29, 2011

I know Seth Godin's work has popped-up in other recent EdLab posts and I am currently reading his classic, Tribes, so I was excited to discover his innovative publishing effort in partnership with Amazon, The Domino Project . Basically, the project takes the "domino effect" of one good idea leading to another to the publishing realm. Emphasizing a "bottom up" approach to publishing rather than the classic "top down" model that has been in practice for centuries. This consumer driven take gives power to the end user to help guide innovation and co-determine content. Here are the group's core principles as outlined on their site: -Exceptionally high quality ideas, created without regard for what bookstores and middlemen want. -Ideas packaged with cogency and urgency in mind, not a word wasted, no filler. Permission at the heart of the model. Ideas for our readers, not more readers for our ideas. -Virality first. An idea that requires a direct sale won't thrive in a world where the most powerful ideas spread from hand to hand. Create content that works best when spread, and then package it so it's easy to spread. -Reward the sneezers who stand up and spread these ideas. -No patience for obsolete institutions. Bestseller lists are not worth compromising for. -Speed triumphs. Rapid time to market, rapid evolution, rapid response to reader feedback. -Format agnostic. Imagine if similar concepts were at the core of education reform and curriculum development? If we replace "readers" with "students" we can create a powerful guiding light for education reform AND the future of publishing.
