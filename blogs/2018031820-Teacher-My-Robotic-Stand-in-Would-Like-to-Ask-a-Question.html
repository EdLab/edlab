Teacher, My Robotic Stand-in Would Like to Ask a Question

Melanie.Hering

March 18, 2018


While the advent of online university courses has provided important opportunities to a demographic that lacks access to college campuses, the impersonal aspect of remote learning is something that drives many students away. With online learning comes an expressed concern by students and instructors alike that staring inactively at a screen can lead to a lack of engagement.

However, Michigan State University has recently developed a new way to inject excitement into online learning through the implementation of telepresent robots, or "robot-learning." A twist on the traditional videoconference, these courses allow students to experience a class through the perspective of a robot stand-inâ€“a machine with a mounted video screen that students can position anywhere in the class and adjust to better interact with their instructor. Research reveals that this method increases interaction and engagement with remote students and improves the sense of connection between these students and their instructor.
