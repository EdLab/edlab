College Marketing on Facebook and the Presence of Teachers College

Ting Yuan

June 23, 2009

A recent eSchool News reported how social networking has changed the way campuses compete for prospective students and gave an exemplar case of Texas A & M's marketing on Facebook. According to Diane C. McDonald, associate director of marketing at Texas A&M University, “the campus has transferred about 80 percent of its marketing budget to online advertising, while cutting back on print ads” and it is the time for universities to have “online evangelists”. Furthermore, a "non-authoritative" Facebook presence can be embedded/linked to many other websites such as Flickr and LinkedIn, which can reach thousands of people. Texas A & M links the Facebook page to its websites and photo, video, and iTunes collections. The page also includes a discussion board with job and other information postings. Do You Wonder? is a website collecting students and schools' video works. Here is a super interesting clip shown on the website (made by students): In order to know other schools' marketing status on Facebook, I have looked at a few pages on Facebook, and to my surprise, Teachers College doesn't have a formal page on Facebook yet. The current version is very rough. Here are some statistics from my "investigation": Teachers College, Columbia University, 32 members (It shows up first after searching "Teachers College") Teachers College - Columbia University, 1235 fans, 1270 members (It cannot be found on the first result page after searching "Teachers College".
