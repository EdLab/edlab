CMU Sphinx: Open Source Speech Recognition

zhou

January 20, 2012

I encountered CMU Sphinx while searching for some other information. As an open source project for speech recognition, it looks fantastic and highly promising for education. CMU Sphinx is developed at the Carnegie Mellon University. The project includes a series of speech recognizers (Sphinx 2 - 4) and an acoustic model trainer (SphinxTrain). So potentially it can not only recognize speeches but also "teach" itself to recognize a particular person's voice better and better overtime. Another great thing about this tool is it can accommodate different language models such as English, Russian, German, or even Mandarin Chinese. But of course the accuracy of these models may vary. I'm interested in learning about any project ideas using CMU Sphinx at the EdLab.
