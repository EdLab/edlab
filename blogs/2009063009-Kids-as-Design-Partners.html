Kids as Design Partners

Arianna Choi

June 30, 2009

At the University of Maryland's Human-Computer Interaction Lab, researchers and kids work together to design new tech tools for children. The RA being interviewed has it right: kids are experts at being kids, so who better to ask for ideas? Where better to go for observing and understanding the needs for redesign? This video reminded me of how important it is to challenge the power dynamics of any organizational situation in order to promote innovation and free thinking. My favorite part? It's at 1:40 when a child says, We work with the National Park Service a lot. Some of the games on their websites are sort of cheesy, so we help to fix them up and stuff.
