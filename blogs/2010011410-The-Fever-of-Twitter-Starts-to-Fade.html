The Fever of Twitter Starts to Fade?

Ting Yuan

January 14, 2010

As reported by Mashable, Twitter's user number has dropped down to 23.6 million U.S. unique visitors, a loss of nearly 24 percent, since its peak at 29.2 million users in July, 2009. This serious decrease poses a big contrast of its 752 percent increase in 2008 (as given in a report by Quantcast, a web analytics firm): Possible reasons are suggested, such as that unlike Facebook, people don't have that much to say and to follow on this web. How do you like using Twitter? Personally, I like Twitter's "following" and quick publishing function and use it to follow big figures in my interested field (including a professor at TC). It turns to be subscriptions to multiple sources of easy-to-read and up-to-date academic highlights, where information about reports, news, and conferences, as well as their abbreviated opinions are published. I also try to post related thoughts/news on my own page for both sharing and archival purpose. However, this naturally brewed academic sharing phenomenon on Twitter seems unnoticed and at least hasn't been openly marketed by Twitter to attract the participation of graduate students.
