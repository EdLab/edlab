Startup Spotlight: Contactually

Kate Meersschaert

February 8, 2012

The Details and Back-story Zvi Band, Contactually's DC-based founder (#dctech) hopes to help companies improve their CRM process through fully optimizing email interactions. The startup describes the benefits of their service as follows, "Contactually synchronizes with your GMail account and contacts, and will help you connect with new contacts you email. Track how you met them, remind you to follow up, follow on Twitter, and more." Band was inspired to create Contactually after realizing that he was all but ignoring his classic CRM options and instead focusing all his customer relations and management on email. Unique Point of Difference Contactually offers a well-designed, streamlined dashboard for analyzing who, what and when you reach-out to clients and contacts via email. This graphical representation of your inbox is pulled automatically without any additional app or software installation. This mining of your existent email account is a nice way to painlessly gather data, generate reminders to follow-up and distinguish who your lead contacts are, ultimately leading to an optimized email interaction. As "phone time" becomes all but obsolete for businesses to fully function, this startup's proposition seems poised to become even more valuable. EdLab Relevance and Takeaway As we continue to develop our proprietary social network (EdNode) and think about additional value to add to apps like ResearchBroker and SurveySidekick, it seems that "email optimization" could be an interesting model to mine. At the very least, we could think about how to tap-into or recreate the Google experience of a unified data share and opportunity for user interaction analysis across all apps. Sounds like DataDashboard...
