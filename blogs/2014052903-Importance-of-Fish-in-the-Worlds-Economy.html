Importance of Fish in the World's Economy

abagigah92

May 29, 2014

A recent UN report notes that fish has become a very important factor in the growth of a nation's economy as it provides more jobs than ever before. The sale of fish has continuously increased in recent years. In 2012 alone, 158 million tons of fish was sold. This is 10 million more than it was in 2010. The growing demand in fish provides an opportunity for job creation, one of the crucial barometers to economic growth. The fish industry contributed to 60 million jobs, most of it in Asia and Africa.

However, in order to sustain the growth, policymakers have urged all to take care of the environment. According to FAO Director-General JosÃ© Graziano da Silva, the health of our planet as well as the health and future food security depends on how we treat the blue world.

Sustainable prosperity is very important in order to keep creating new jobs and providing a source of income for families around the world through fishing. Fish production used for food has grew from about 70 percent in the 1980s to a record high of more than 85 percent in 2012. This means we have to ensure that our economic goals has to be in line with our duty to protect marine life and our environment.
