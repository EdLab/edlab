Motion Math

Fred Rossoff

May 7, 2014


Motion Math is a suite of mobile app games designed for elementary school math learners. Each game involves solving math puzzles through game mechanics, such as maneuvering balls into the proper slot or guiding a bird to fly over the correct answer.

Pros:

The visuals and game mechanics are fun and attractive. Primary color birds and fish swoop up and down the screen, responding mostly to screen-tilting. Young students will find these apps a pain-free way to do some learning.

Cons:

Although each app marries math and traditional gaming elements, the focus seems to be more on creating an engaging surface than on ensuring the usefulness of the learning tool. The subjects are confined to very basic math, and there doesn’t seem to be much room or scaffolding for learning progression.

Our Takeaway:

Educational games occupy an uncomfortable space between learning and fun, and although Motion Math apps are easy to dive into, they’re no different in essence. However, Motion Math has committed to developing a platform to assess how helpful its tools actually are in the learning process, so that we should soon have a better sense of how useful the app is in practice.

The Bottom Line:

Like most educational games, it’s better than twiddling your thumbs, but worse than doing your homework.
