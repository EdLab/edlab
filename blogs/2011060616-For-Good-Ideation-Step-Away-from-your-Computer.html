For Good Ideation... Step Away from your Computer

Kate Meersschaert

June 6, 2011

I was inspired by Joyce's last post to share a few recent revelations and ideas around "unsticking" yourself when ideating for a project... 1) Break your usual media routine. Check-out someone else's RSS feed. I am often amazed at what resources, like the dailybeast.com, I have been missing. 2) Look outside of your usual inspiration outlets. We are habit-driven creatures and this can inherently lead us to come to the same conclusions over and over. I recently went to see Complexions perform and found myself flooded with great project ideas completely unrelated to dance. I also, couldn't access any of my devices in the theater so I was forced to use analogue memory devices... like... gasp, a piece of paper and pen. This lead to a whole new way of looking at the problem & lots of helpful doodles. 3) Get outside (Or at least walk to the coffee maker). As the prolific inventor (140 patents) Charles F. Kettering once said, "I have never heard of anyone stumbling on to something sitting down." Take a new route to work or home, get lost. I discovered a "do tank" in my neighborhood this way, but most importantly the meta idea here is to keep moving when you are stuck and the movement itself can lead to new ideas... This must have been what what Aristotle was thinking when he founded the Peripatetic school of teaching.
