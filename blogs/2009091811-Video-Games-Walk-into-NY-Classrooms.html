Video Games Walk into NY Classrooms

Ting Yuan

September 18, 2009

As Jess blogged about in her previous post, Quest to Learn, located at East 23rd Street, is a gaming-oriented NYC public school. As reported by the Metropolis Magazine, the school is the first American public school with a curriculum built around gaming principles and it will formally start to give students video-game-based classes (such as Little Big Planet and Civilization) this October. New York is the third state in the U.S. (after Virginia and Florida, see a previous post here) which has started to apply integrated gaming courses in public classrooms.

Civilization IV
It would be very interesting to follow this school to see how students' learn curves with different types of games. Furthermore, if the computers being used by students can be installed with software such as iShowU to track students' video activities, it will be very helpful for data collection.
