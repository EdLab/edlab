E-Cigarettes' Use Rise Among Students

Bismark Appiah

September 8, 2013

According to recent statistics, there has been an increase in the use of e-cigarettes by middle and high school students. E-Cigarettes are battery-powered devices that deliver nicotine vaporized to form an aerosol mist. E-cigarettes come in different flavors, and the different kinds of flavors are proving the catalyst for its use among students. We are all aware of the perilous diseases that smoking brings to the human body. In 2012, about 1.8 million middle and high school students said they had tried e-cigarettes. This huge amount indicates that students' attention is now being fixated on e-cigarettes rather than traditional cigarettes. In one way, it is good that students are using e-cigarettes as an alternative for traditional cigarettes; however, in the other way it brings a host of other issues. E-cigarettes is good for students because researchers believe that it is a healthy alternative to smoking, but they have yet to confirm it because there are still some unanswered questions about e-cigarette health effects. On the other hand, e-cigarettes are bad for students because it might serve as a path to smoking. The rise in youth usage of e-cigarettes is "unacceptable." And I believe the FDA need to act quickly to stop the selling of e-cigarettes to prevent it from becoming yet another health crisis among young people.
