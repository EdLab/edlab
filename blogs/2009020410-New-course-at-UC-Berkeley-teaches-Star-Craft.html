New course at UC Berkeley teaches Star Craft

Xuyang Shi

February 4, 2009

There is an amazing course being offered at UC Berkeley that aims to teach students how to enjoy the "Art of competitive StarCraft." The prerequisites include a practical knowledge of StarCraft strategy and suggested reading includes “The Art of War” by Sun Tzu and “Crazy as Me” by Lim Yo Hwan. UC Berkeley students who are interested in real-time strategy games and the competitive gaming landscape are encouraged to participate in this class. This course will teach in-depth about the theory of how war is conducted within the confines of the game Starcraft. There will be lecture on various aspects of the game: from the viewpoint of pure theory to the more computational aspects of how exactly battles are conducted. Calculus and Differential Equations are highly recommended for understanding of the course. Furthermore, the class will take the theoretical into the practical world by analyzing games and replays to reinforce decision-making skills and advanced Starcraft theory. Would you like to take this course? Unfortunately, there are only 60 seats available. Reference: Brian Crecente, http://kotaku.com/people/crecente/posts/
