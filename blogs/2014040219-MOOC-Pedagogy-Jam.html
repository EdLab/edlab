MOOC Pedagogy Jam

chingfu

April 2, 2014

MOOC Pedagogy Jam is an asynchronous online discussion that invites educators from across the globe to join a conversation about effective pedagogy for MOOCs at a schedule time (in this case, 4/1,1-5 pm). I found this Jam approach interesting and spent some time in the conversations yesterday. The asynchronous Jam format was good, but the discussion platform wasn't working well because of the single thread format. Here are my takeaways from the Competencies for Teaching Online: Describing Effective Digital Pedagogy discussions, and you can see more discussion summaries here (a great idea and supplement for such Jam discussion format, log-in required).

  1. Participants were eager to see more learner-directed, instead of facilitator-directed MOOCs, and discussed pedagogical arrangements for this shift (more choices of assessment approaches/deliverables, choices of topics, choices of learning activities etc.).
  2. Educators were also very interested in learning how to create and manage learning groups on a massive scale.
  3. Great question: "How do we create meaningful assignments (that foster student learning, engagement, motivation, etc.) and are also practically assessable (i.e., provide meaningful feedback, but also don't require the instructor to spend his/her entire life grading/evaluating)?" Solutions to this problem seem to be crucial for moving MOOCs forward.
