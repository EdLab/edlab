Economics & Education: Is this really fiscally responsible?

Skanda Amarnath

July 18, 2011

As Eric Hanushek writes in Education Next, California's legislature is cutting down on the length of the school year, a horrible idea as far as educational policy goes. Yet what's worse is that this is a terrible fiscal trick as well. Never mind the obvious effects less time in the classroom have on student achievement, is this really going to save California a lot of money? More students will have to be held back and more will likely involve themselves in costly social ills that ultimately require government spending anyways. This has been the nature of most fiscal debates; it is as if cutting spending costs no one. Odds are that these spending cuts will not cut spending as much as politicians hope and will likely lead to lower quality government services. In nearly all fiscal discussions, there is a failure to clarify how spending cuts translate to fewer government services and possibly increased efficiency (less waste, fraud, abuse, etc).
