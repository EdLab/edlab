The Revolution will be Parent Powered... and on Film

Kate Meersschaert

February 21, 2012

Thanks to new "Parent Trigger" laws, it might be possible for children's legal caregivers to one-up teachers, administrators and even unions to legally take-over a failing school. As the New York Times explores in this article, the parent-powered revolution may actually become a reality for students at this school in Adelanto, California, as the district decides today whether or not they will become the first to be fully governed by parents. In the meantime, the movie may precede a nationwide movement, thanks to a film highlighted in the aforementioned NYT's article, "Won't Back Down," is slated to be released this September by 20th Century Fox.
