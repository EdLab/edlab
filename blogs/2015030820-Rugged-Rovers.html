Rugged Rovers

Ting Yuan

March 8, 2015


Rugged Rovers is a free engineering-themed app for iPad and iPhone developed by the Science Museum in London. Rugged Rovers provides young users with a gaming space to build and test a space roverâ€™s driving abilities on the simulated rough terrain of Mars. Users design their very own space rovers around a motor and equip them with tires, before testing them out along an obstacle course to compete against other rovers to reach the furthest distance.

Pros:

The game interface is simple and self-explanatory. Users start by drawing the shape of a rover around its motor and adding wheels to their rover. Specifically, the drawing tools and resizable wheels let users test a variety of designs on the challenging terrain. Actions like jet hover and speed boost give users the ability to help their rovers overcome terrain obstacles. The game also features guiding messages about how to solve common rover challenges. In the testing stage, a self-designed rover will encounter different designs, some of which are made by real-life Mars rover engineers, as a way for users to compare and reflect on their own design choices.

Cons:

The current game does not provide the option of group play. On mobile devices, users can only participate as a single player; they have to go to the museum site to join a group play on a big screen. The addition of social options to the game might inspire collaborative science learning opportunities in and out of school settings, including the use of the app as a conversation platform to promote online peer learning.

Our Takeaway:

Rugged Rovers brings fun to science learning, and could serve as an introductory tool for elementary students beginning to think about the crossroads of science and design. The tool can be applied to both whole-class and individualized learning scenarios in classroom.
