Ashley Bittner

Nantwi

January 24, 2019


Ashley Bittner is a principal at Owl Ventures, a Bay Area venture capital fund that invests in the world’s leading education technology companies. She previously served as a White House appointee where she worked in the Office of Innovation and Improvement to support the development and scaling of innovative approaches to improving student outcomes across the country.

How did your education and previous professional experience shape your current work at Owl Ventures?

I became passionate about innovation in education from my experience teaching in the Bronx through Teach for America. I taught 7th and 8th grade Social Studies at a traditional public school and struggled to meaningfully meet the individual needs of over 130 students. I hoped that technology could improve outcomes and access for students across the education system. After I completed Teach for America, I pursued my MBA at The Wharton School and my MPA at the Harvard Kennedy School. I focused on social entrepreneurship and investing at Wharton, and social and urban policy at HKS. While in graduate school, I explored a variety of roles that all touched on an aspect of innovation in education: interning at NewSchools Venture Fund, at a charter school, at an education technology company, and at an education consultancy. I then joined the Boston Consulting Group in DC with the hopes of refining an analytical toolkit to bring to the education sector in the near future. I was asked to join the Obama Administration’s Office of Innovation, where I learned the government’s approach to scaling innovative practices and tiered-evidence systems. These experiences inform my perspective as an investor at Owl Ventures.

How do you decide which edtech companies to invest in?

Owl invests in companies that are addressing significant education challenges that translate to large market opportunities. We invest in companies that have demonstrated product-market fit, a path to scale, and evidence of effectiveness that will give the company a competitive advantage.

What broad trends do you think will have the most impact on learning in the years ahead?

We see many major technology trends being applied to the education sector. We see companies leveraging AI, VR/AR, distributed workforces, etc., to improve outcomes across the education spectrum.

What, if any, are upcoming or future plans for Owl Ventures?

Owl will continue to invest in education entrepreneurs across the education spectrum, from early learning to K-12 to post-secondary to career mobility, in both the US and abroad.
