How Digital Literacy Empowers Young Student Activists

csd2126

March 26, 2017


Social justice movements have increasingly turned to social media to organize. Hashtags such as #icantbreathe, #nodapl, and #bringbackourgirls have created increased awareness around issues affecting communities across the U.S. and internationally. Two researcher-educators explored digital literacy as a site for activist work with tween-aged students in New York. The two-year collaboration between these teachers aimed to promote inquiry, enhance student learning, and generate action toward social justice.

The teachers built on students' existing interests in equity, activism, and technology to create a community of learners advocating for social change. They purposefully selected a set of digital tools that enhanced student collaboration. Tools like VoiceThread and Glogster allowed students to share questions or insights on their topics of interest.

Students were interested in topics ranging from #blacklivesmatter to religious intolerance. Many of the issues students chose to engage with were directly linked to their personal experiences living in New York. Because both teachers encouraged students to work across a variety of modalities, many projects used some form of technology access through tablets or computers.

Recommendations from this project include:

  • Communicate with parents, students, and administrators about how digital tools can support student learning,
  • Think about technologies as a way of knowing and processing content; and
  • Try one tool at a time to fully leverage each platform.

The teachers found that their inquiry-based approach allowed students to move beyond looking for the right answer and begin to better understand how inequities impact society.

Price-Dennis, D., & Carrion, S. (2017). Leveraging digital literacies for equity and social justice. Language Arts, 94(3), 190.
