EdLab Review: Paul's Notes

Fred Rossoff

December 22, 2011

In the EdLab Review we often discuss slick new education technology solutions, but this week I want to talk about a much less polished resource whose sole virtue is that it gets the job done. Paul's Online Math Notes is an online resource created by Paul Dawkins, a math professor at Lamar University. The topics covered range in difficulty from algebra to the higher levels of calculus. Pros: What impresses me about the website is that it is a very simply solution to a very simple job (teaching math). There are no registrations or forms to fill out (although there is a forum you can become a member of to ask other math learners questions). You just click on what you want to learn (the categories organized by course, and then subdivided by subject), and immediately you're reading a straightforward discussion of the material, buttressed quite frequently by practice problems that the website will walk you through. Cons: Of course this service might benefit (as would any like it) from a more rigorous practice and evaluation module, but that's nowhere near a deal-breaker. EdLab Relevance: I think this illustrates how simple it would be to get learning resources online and freely available. A relatively primitive platform will do the trick, as long as the content is well presented. Our Takeaway: I heartily recommend that math learners (and those curious about what online learning done right looks like) visit Paul's Notes.
