Brooklyn Skillshare Shoot

Molly Riordan

October 12, 2009

This Saturday, Skye, Erin, and I were lucky enough to attend and tape the first (hopefully annual) Brooklyn Skillshare. It was a high-energy day of workshops led by volunteers with knowledge to share, including our own Patrick Carey who taught a fun DIY Electronics session. From kombucha brewing and jewelry-casting, to "upcycling" and three-color screenprinting, well over a hundred participants gleaned the knowledge and the inspiration to try out dozens of DIY techniques for themselves. Skye is in the midst of sifting through the hours of footage for an eventual AfterEd series. In the meantime check out the Flickr Set to see what it looks like when learning goes to Gowanus. If you know of any similar events going on in the area, post them here! It'd be great to profile and learn from the different ways knowledge-sharing happens beyond the classroom.
