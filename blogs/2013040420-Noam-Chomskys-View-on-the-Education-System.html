Noam Chomsky’s View on the Education System

Laura Scheiber

April 4, 2013


The education system in the US is facing a conundrum, according to Noam Chomsky. He perceives the institution as an indoctrination of the young, training them to be "obedient and conformist." The problem, he points out, is that such practices stifle innovation and progress. This conflict in values comes to a head in higher education because the type of students most likely to have done well in the education system and therefore accepted into college, have been trained to maintain the status quo. Yet many areas of study in higher education, such as the sciences and engineering, require students "to think outside of the box" if they are to move their field forward. Considering this contradiction in priorities, something must change.
