NASA's Newest Space Station Robots Were Inspired by Star Wars

Ryan.Allen

August 27, 2018


Remember when Luke was training with his lightsaber and the little floating ball on the Millennium Falcon? NASAâ€™s newest robots geared for work aboard the International Space Station in 2019 were inspired by this sci-fi tech. These Astrobees have been equipped with tiny fans that allow them to whiz around zero-gravity environments, assisting with research, ship diagnostics, and even basic housekeeping.

NASA scientists believe that Astrobees and future iterations of these robotic personal assistants will be key for human survival during future missions to Mars or even further in deep space. At such incredible distances, communication to mission control will be hobbled, so the bots will provide a multitude of automated functions using their cameras, lasers, or mechanical arms, among other tools. These Astrobees may become more comparable to R2D2 as the technology advances.

NASA is once again pushing the limits of our imaginations through ingenuity, engineering, and genius. Will these kinds of robots completely replace astronauts in the future? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
