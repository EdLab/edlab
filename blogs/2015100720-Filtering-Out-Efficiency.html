Filtering Out Efficiency

Jenny Shen

October 7, 2015


Even though the Children's Internet Protection Act (CIPA) requires school districts to block certain websites on school computers, there are a lot of misconceptions about what exactly must be blocked. Some administrators actually end up "over-filtering" web content. Although what constitutes over-filtering is up for debate, teachers are arguing that filtering is actually impeding their teaching methods. Some teachers spend hours downloading YouTube videos outside of school in order to have the videos ready to show during class.

Teachers who advocate for less administrative control over web content in schools also point out that it is a school's responsibility to teach students how to use technology responsibly. One such teacher noted that with the presidential debates going on, most of the discussions taking place are happening on social media, and teachers can leverage that in a social studies classroom—but not when those sites are being blocked. When districts go out of their way to "protect" students from certain online content, at what point does it become a hindrance from learning rather than a means of support?
