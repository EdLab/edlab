Princeton U. targets female graduate students with "family friendly" benefits

Samantha Schoeller

April 5, 2007

In what could be a slowly growing trend at well-endowed U.S. universities, Princeton U. expanded benefits for graduate students with children. Highlights from a Chronicle article include:

  • Three months of paid leave from research and teaching to birth mothers
  • An extra semester for primary caregivers, regardless of gender, to complete studies
  • The opportunity for birth or adoptive parents to apply for extensions of academic deadlines and/or financial grants and to participate in new mortgage programs
The “family-friendly” benefits are mostly designed to keep female graduate students from giving up on academia. A recent survey at Berkeley showed that doctoral students are increasingly worried about the work-family balance: 46% of first-year female doctoral students aimed to pursue careers at research universities, but only 31% maintained that interest a year later. Mary Ann Mason and Marc Goulden at Berkeley also report that though women earn doctorates at a much greater rate today than in the 1970s, the percentage of tenured male faculty and the percentage of tenured women faculty is about the same as it was 30 years ago. Additionally, their article Do Babies Matter?, (Mason & Goulden argue they do!), notes that the gap between male and female salaries has actually increased during the past few decades.
