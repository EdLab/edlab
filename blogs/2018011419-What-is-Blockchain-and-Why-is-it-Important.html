What is Blockchain and Why is it Important?

Ryan.Allen

January 14, 2018


Youâ€™ve probably heard of Bitcoin. This cryptocurrency has no central bank or nation controlling its flow or creation. Instead, network users authenticate and validate each individual transaction. The technology behind this innovation is called Blockchain. How does it work?

Blockchain is a kind of public ledger that stores information across a network of computers, decentralizing the entire system through wide distribution. No one entity or organization owns the system and anyone can use it or monitor every transaction. For instance, every Bitcoin transaction can be seen on so-called Bitcoin explorer websites.

Each user has a small amount of information called blocks stored chronologically on their computer. Through advanced encryption, the information cannot be duplicated or counterfeited because everyone else in the network can see the records. With this decentralized distribution, Blockchain systems are highly resilient to attacks or breaches.
