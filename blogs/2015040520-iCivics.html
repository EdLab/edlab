iCivics

Alexandra Lederman

April 5, 2015


iCivics is a non-profit organization dedicated to reinvigorating civic learning through interactive and engaging learning resources. (From the iCivics website.)

Civic Learning
iCivics aims to educate the next generation of Americans through civics learning role playing games. This nonprofit company was founded and is run by Justice Sandra Day O’Connor. Resources are developed by former educators and are aided by a volunteer network of State Chairs and State Coordinators. iCivics partners with technology companies, game developers, and civic organizations.

Education through Gamification
The materials available for students and educators include Common Core aligned and curriculum supported digital interactives, games, webquests, and lesson plans. The games have clear learning objectives, are seamlessly integrated into the lesson plan, and place students in different roles with real-world problems, issues, and scenarios.

iCivics provides educators with small group or classroom-wide learning along with individual instruction. A clearly written and visually enhanced Teachers Guide is available through iCivics. Students do not need email addresses to sign up and participate in iCivics. Teachers can view each student’s progress or post discussion topics to dive deeper into a topic. They can also encourage students to contribute to impact projects by donating their iCivics game points to community service projects proposed and organized by other young people. Teachers can the use iCivics games in class as a group activity, where the teacher is the moderator and the students take turns answering. Group games are a great way for teachers to provide test preparation.

The innovative and creative iCivics’ approach towards active participation, collaboration, and civic learning landed the organization a grant from the MacArthur Foundation.
