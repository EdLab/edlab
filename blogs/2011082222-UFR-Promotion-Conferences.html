UFR Promotion: Conferences

Nantwi

August 22, 2011

As the UFR team ready to implement the next phase of promoting the curriculum, one of our plans is submitting proposals and hopefully presenting various components of UFR at conferences. Two such conferences are Preparing Sound P-16 Financial Education Settings (June 4-5, 2012 in Normal, IL) and the 7th Annual Economics Teaching Conference (October 27-28, 2011 in New Orleans). The former asks applicants to submit proposals in five different categories: (1) defining and creating a sound financial education environment, (2) financial literacy teacher preparation and awareness, (3) interdisciplinary relationships, and (4) connecting with commuter. The latter asks for paper presentations in a host of categories including computer based instruction, faculty advising, global economic issues, economics through literature and teaching the one-semester economics course, among others. Are there any other effective ways of promoting the curriculum, especially with the debt and unstable stock market prominent in the news.
