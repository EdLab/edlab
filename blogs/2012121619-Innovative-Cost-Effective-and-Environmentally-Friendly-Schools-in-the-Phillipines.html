Innovative, Cost-Effective, and Environmentally Friendly Schools in the Phillipines

Laura Scheiber

December 16, 2012


A major problem in many countries across the globe is lack of funding to build schools. Equally problematic is the formidable accumulation of garbage that wrecks havoc on the environment. While these two issues at first glance seem unrelated, in the Philippines, a pilot project is addressing both problems by building schools entirely out of recycled materials. The instigators of the project were looking for a cheap way to build thousands of classrooms on a tight budget, and using recyclable materials allowed them to build a classroom at half the usual cost. An extra bonus is that the building can hold its own against the elements, including gale-force winds. Vialogues participants were inspired by this innovative project and commented:
