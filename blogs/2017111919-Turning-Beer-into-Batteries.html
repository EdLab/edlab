Turning Beer into Batteries

Ryan.Allen

November 19, 2017


It’s surprisingly difficult to dispose of wastewater from the beer brewing process. The liquids cannot simply be flushed into the sewer because large quantities will disrupt the microbes used to break down organic waste. Researchers from the University of Colorado Boulder have devised a solution by recycling this beer sludge into energy for batteries.

Partnering with Avery Brewing, university researchers have discovered that leftover wastewater provides an ideal foundation for bio-manufactured batteries. Fungal spores are combined with the beer leftovers and put through a chemical transformation process. This new formulation becomes the raw material for batteries.

The partnership has also spawned a private sector start-up, Emergy, that is focused on getting this clean innovation to the market. What do you think of this new environmentally friendly public–private partnership? Join the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
