Neil deGrasse Tyson urges kids to question answers

jvazquez

July 28, 2013


If science had its version of rock stars, Neil deGrasse Tyson would be in the Hall of Fame. He’s a physicist by trade but may be more well known throughout certain online communities as the ultimate source of witty science-related one-liners. He even has his own meme! He’s able to reach younger people in a way that has eluded many science educators before him; and when he speaks, they listen.

That’s why when Neil Tyson says that kids need to up their science literacy skills, it doesn’t fall on deaf ears. Kids are naturally inquisitive, he explains. They explore and question. They bang pots and observe. But as they grow, somewhere along the way they lose their inherent inclination to investigate, gather information, and challenge "facts." Without this analytic thinking, kids are susceptible to believe everything without question. But as a role model and leading science authority, Tyson serves as a beacon for all those who would otherwise be lost in a sea of gullibility.
