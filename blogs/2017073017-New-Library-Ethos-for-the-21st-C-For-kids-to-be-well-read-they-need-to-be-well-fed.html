New Library Ethos for the 21st C: For kids to be well-read, they need to be well-fed.

dinosossi2

July 30, 2017


Institutions need to continue to be responsive to their constituents to remain relevant.

Even libraries.

In an interesting response to the post-2008 economic malaise, U.S. libraries are starting to offer food for their patrons.

Over the recent past, library-based food programs have been funded by the U.S. Department of Agriculture. During 2016, almost four millions kids received 179 million meals.

As a former K–12 teacher in Canada, the U.K., and the U.S., I have experienced first-hand that poverty is a recurring stigma that makes developing a healthy student identity difficult.

But as one librarian states, it goes even deeper than that.

“For kids to be well-read, they need to be well-fed.”
