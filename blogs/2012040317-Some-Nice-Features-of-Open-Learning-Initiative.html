Some Nice Features of Open Learning Initiative

Yudan Li

April 3, 2012

I was playing around Carnegie Mellon's Open Learning Initiative. It is site well-designed to support online teaching and learning. I was most impressed by its features as it were carefully devised for course creating and managing. The site provides a "Course Builder" utility to instructors who plan to create a course. Apart from the course creation options that are common to most learning sites, the Course Builder does provide some outstanding features. Basically, a course can be created in either "simple" or "advanced" mode. If created under "advanced" mode, the editor will have greater freedom in defining and organizing the content of the course. The Course Builder has a powerful GUI utility that displays the course content using an expandable tree-like view. For each entry in the view, there are buttons that allow users to move them up or down. The site also gives great attention to course managing experience. In the managing section, instructors can access student roster, schedule assignments, make assessment plan or even assign teaching assistants. Take the assessment plan for example, the system provides to the instructor a list of possible assessment options. They include "Attempts", "Scoring Model", "Late Submit", "Late Start" and even "Grace Period". Another amazing feature is the content sharing utility. Basically, the site provides GUIs for course creators to go to other courses and pick up components. It's also possible to copy a course. In doing so, users can choose to copy the course exactly in full or elect to change the scheduling information. Give it a shot yourself: here.
