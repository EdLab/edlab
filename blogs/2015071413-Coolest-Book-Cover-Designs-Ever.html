Coolest Book Cover Designs Ever

Panisuan Chasinga

July 14, 2015

I've stumbled upon this great gallery 50 Coolest Book Cover Designs Ever and I'd like to share a few of my favorites, as a designer and reader. George Orwell's 1984. Designed by Shepard Fairey If you have seen the famous Red-blue graffiti-like portrait of Obama's Change campaign, that's one of this controversial street artist's work. Great match of artist and book. Against Happiness by Eric G. Wilson. Designed by Jennifer Carrow I'm a fan of minimal design. This downward curve resembles a sad face (or mouth) on top of the bright yellowish radiance. The Brief History of the Dead by Kevin Brockmeier's. Designed by Archie Ferguson I dig this photograph, it feels really creepy and suggesting "ghost" and "history". The Psychopath Test by Jon Ronson. Designed by Alex Merto Great play on the paperness of the book. Are you a predator or a victim? The Stranger by Albert Camus. Designed by Helen Yentus If you've read this classic piece, you'll know why this cover is so fascinating. The Humbling by Philip Roth. Designed by Milton Glaser The work of a legendary graphic artist who's behind the equally legendary "I <3 NY" typeface that saved the city. I like how he treated the typeface as punctured into the book and seeing the light ray through them. It's ingeniousity. Survivor by Chuck Palaniak. Designed by Rodrigo Corral As radical as the author himself, this design is downright simple and, well, semi-interactive. Would you like to make a plane now? The Books About Dead Philosophers by Simon Critchley. Designed by John Gall. For some reason I really into this cover. A "dead" book inside a book! Very metaphorical, and very thrilling. Just by looking at it I felt thrilled. Like seeing a Magritte's painting. There are many more, sure to check them out and leave a comment to let me know which is your most fav of all!
