Wonderkind’s Seek and Find Apps

Rebecca Rubenstein

July 15, 2015


Wonderkind apps are all about the quality of the experience. This Berlin based company started in 2010. Wonderkind ideas start in watercolor on paper and are then transferred to the screen and animated. The seek and find apps take the form of interactive activity books and introduce children to a wide variety of places such as the circus, zoo, firehouse, airport, farm, and forest. The apps are ideal for ages 2 to 5.

Pros:

Tapping on a figure in each scene initiates an action and related sound that is sometimes unexpected and at times humorous - a winged dragon breathes fire outside the airport, a monkey climbs a lighting fixture at the circus while frogs play piano and dance. And tapping on all the animals at the zoo, you can hear all the animal sounds and see elephants spray each other. For a nice overview of the scenes and to zero in on the action, users can also bring up a screen of thumbnails. The "My Little Town" seek and find includes similar elements and is considered a picture book with morning, afternoon and evening scenes.

Cons:

These apps are between $3.00 and $4.00 each and there are additional costs in some of the seek and finds to unlock all of the scenes. A few of the apps can be purchased from the app store in bundles of three or four at a time.

Our Takeaway:

Along with the seek and find apps, Wonderkind also offers apps that help children learn the alphabet, create music, or play a card game based on the classic, Memory. This company’s passion for discovery shines through in all of the work. The hand drawn elements and animations seamlessly work together to create immersive spaces that are fun to explore and could spark curiosity and learning for preschool-age students.
