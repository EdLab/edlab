Museum of Food and Drink

EdLab Studios

March 22, 2016


"Food is fundamental to human existence. I can't think of anything more fundamental," says Program Director of MOFAD, Emma Boast as she stands in front of the massive Smell-Synth exhibit. As part of the current exhibition "Flavor: Making It or Faking It," the contraption accomplishes what every interactive exhibit and education program at the Museum of Food and Drink does: inspires curiosity and a deeper appreciation of what we eat and why. Located on the border of Williamsburg and Greenpoint amidst a long history of food manufacturing, breweries, and a burgeoning food culture, the 5,000 square foot MOFAD lab serves as a test-kitchen for the concept of a food museum and the intersection of a variety of different disciplines. Visitors explore the history, culture, production, economics, science, and technology surrounding food through sensory experiences.
