AI Bots Could Help Humans Collaborate Better

saraehardman

June 20, 2017


As robots continue to play bigger roles in our lives, researchers are constantly studying how humans and robots collaborate with each other in different scenarios. One such situation is in collaborative problem solving. A recent study aimed to answer the following question: what happens when we randomly put artificially intelligent (AI) bots into an online group problem solving task?

Over 4,000 people participated in this study. Groups of 20 people each were given a problem to solve called the color coordination game; they solved it collaboratively on an online message platform. AI bots were distributed in different groups. These AI bots emitted various levels of noise, which the authors define as meaningless or incorrect information. In other words, one group of bots emitted no incorrect information, another group emitted some, and the final group emitted a lot.

The researchers found that some levels of AI noise actually helped speed the problem solving abilities of the group. While the bots that emitted either a lot or no meaningless information did not significantly improve the dynamics of the groups, the bots that emitted some meaningless information decreased both the number of internal conflicts among the human participants and the time it took for them to solve the problem. In fact, this effect was more significant the more challenging the problem was, with bots decreasing the solution time by 56% on more difficult problems.

This research demonstrates that when a simple AI bot is programmed correctly, it might improve human problem solving. Having an AI bot emit some levels of meaningless information not only improved human-bot interaction, but also human-human interaction, which in turn improved the overall quality of collaboration. This could have significant impact on various ways we use robots in the future.
