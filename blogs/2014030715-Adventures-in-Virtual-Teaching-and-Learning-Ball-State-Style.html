Adventures in Virtual Teaching & Learning: Ball State Style

Kate Meersschaert

March 7, 2014

***Private! Dr. Stephen Gasior is a biology professor, who for the past 5+ years has experimented with teaching and learning on "metaverse" platforms like SecondLife (see a Vialogue of Dr. Gasior lecturing on this topic below). Dr. Gasior is the subject of an upcoming Profile and NL Sector piece focused on one of his custom-built "worlds," Virtual Islands for Better Education (VIBE). When I reached-out to him for the NL Sector he offered to give me a virtual tour of this virtual world (so meta, right?!). I agreed, listed below are my takeaways and a few screenshots of the experience (yes, my avatar name is Ziegl - no judging! ;)). Ball State's Virtual Campus The tour started in SecondLife on Ball State's virtual campus - I was surprised by how easy it was to create an avatar and navigate this world. However, the 1:1 tutorial from Dr. Gasior was probably the main reason for this. I would imagine the general learning classroom or remote learner might have a bit more of a challenge. The VIBE of VIBE We then used the Singularity viewer to log-into a separate "hyper grid", Virtual Islands for Better Education (VIBE), which serves as a hub for global educators to share and teach across a variety of subjects, including biology, archeology & public health. It was a bit confusing that this is a separate "grid" but the Singularity player made it relatively easy to navigate. Apparently this "world" can be shut down to outside participants when younger "classes" visit to secure the space. Interior shot: Inner Space... VIBE Style The VIBE biology world included a giant cell that you could enter, interact with and access other learner's "notecards" attached to elements. This was the most exciting part of the whole experience. I really feel that by being able to "enter" a cell and manipulate each part, learner memory would be facilitated and concepts further reinforced.
