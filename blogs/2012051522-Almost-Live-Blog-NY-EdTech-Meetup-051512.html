(Almost) Live Blog: NY EdTech Meetup 05.15.12

Kate Meersschaert

May 15, 2012

***Private Please! Listed below are my thoughts & take-aways from tonight's EdTech Meetup & panel discussion: The setting: The event was hosted at the Hatchery (on 7th & 37th), which is a tech incubator that officially opens next week! As such, the space was still very much "under-construction", lacked AC and adequate space for the 50+ attendees. Additionally, the video screens, which were only used to display #'s and panelist @'s were set on 3-D mode and all text was blurred. "Secret", unadvertised sponsor & host... The College Board was the "secret" sponsor and two of the group's biz dev. members where the host and emcee... this seemed a bit disingenuous as the group hid their involvement & they seem to be the founders of this particular meet-up group. Somehow this was a big turn-off, especially when they did a pitch for the group as an access point for making higher-ed more accessible. Really?! The SAT's & ACT? On a positive note, the group did mention the founding of an EdTech Council (placeholder URL) that will have an advisory board that helps to move the edtech space forward. However, based on this meet up, I am not sure it is a totally impartial or well-managed endeavor. Take-aways from each of the panelists: Brad Mcllquham - Director of Academics - Knewton -"Early warning systems" built-into the analytics (did not elaborate). -Trying to say that they are an "incubator" -40 plus programmers. -Upcoming partnership with Pearson... Sannier? -Wearing a partner startup's tee... Wistia. Harris Goodman - Late Night Labs -Focus on community colleges... Partnership w ASU & soon PSU. -Higher-ed market is more accessible than K-12. -Community colleges are big users & more open to innovation. -NYC is a great talent pool - why they are here & not Silicon Valley. -Needs-based assessment vs licensing with schools. -Want to work with Big pharma to gift labs to schools in need. -WATCH Late Night Lab's Seminar here! Jeremy Friedman - Co-founder, Schoology: -Focus on K-12, free to teachers, self-curated content. -DOE is almost impossible to penetrate. -Great inspiration from NYC but Midwest biggest growth opportunity. -Should not try to get rid of teachers, but instead just augment them -Scale of being a platform vs app. -Read my StartupSpotlight on Schoology here. Joseph Cohen - Co-founder, LORE (formerly CourseKit) -Wants to connect educators & students around content. -Consumer platform that is free to join & doesn't target institutions. -"Normals" ppl who are not tech-saavy... new term. -Feels that Udacity & MOOC's are "fluff." -Importance of personalization -Surprised by how little bandwidth & infrastructure is present in US schools. -Not always best to name our product for what it is now - better to name for what it could be! Great point! Final take-aways... -LORE seems like an interesting course creation/LMS platform to explore for a review, Startup Spotlight & Seminar related to the mSchool. -The emcee (from the College Board) was the dullest public speaker I have ever encountered... and did not direct questions to specific panelists, instead, he just threw questions at them and expected the panel to sort-out who would answer & in what order. Needless to say, this was not the best approach. -Panel discussions can be hard for audience members to sit-through if there are not opportunities for audience participation interspersed at regular intervals. Additionally, once again, questions form a moderator should be directed at specific panelists and not the whole panel at once. -The space really matters... it is fun to have an informal, edgy feeling, but it needs to be comfortable and conducive to sitting for long periods of time. -All technology needs to be pristine, more screens are not better if they are all blurry! -Finally, the EdLab's demo night is going to rock the EdTech world! For additional conversations about this event on Twitter: #edtechnyc NOT the ideal space...
