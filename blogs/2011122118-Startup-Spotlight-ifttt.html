Startup Spotlight: ifttt

Kate Meersschaert

December 21, 2011

The Details & Back-story Short-hand for "if this then that," ifttt was started in December of 2010 as an open-source, graphical, event-driven programming "recipe" generator that helps users to "put the internet to work for them." Tasks are generated by selecting icons in a logical "if, then" order. For example, if Kindle makes more free e-books available, then email me a notification. View the examples below to see how ingeniously simple these "recipes" really can be. In addition to event-driven programming, the founder, Linden Tibbets, was inspired by laws of cause and effect as well as, Jane Fulton Suri's book, Thoughtless Acts?: Observations on Intuitive Design and the impact of small actions on our everyday lives. Unique Point of Difference ifttt is uniquely simple, fun-to use and graphically appealing. Not only is the value proposition extensible and infinitely adaptable, but the streamlined, UX really stands-out in a market of often convoluted "efficiency" tools and programs. For example, I love and avidly use OmniFocus, but at times I just want to set a reminder & forget about it... much as the founders aptly catch-phrased their platform to "make the internet work for you." EdLab Takeaway Simplistic, fun & open-source. These are great rallying cries for the start of any project. Sometimes seemingly menial tasks and need states we take for granted can be harnessed and turned-into a meaningful tool. In this case, something as potentially complicated as event-driven programming can become as simple as a well written 2-ingredient recipe in the right hands.
