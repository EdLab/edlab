Let This 11-year-old Create Your Next Password

csd2126

April 9, 2017


Eleven-year-old Mira has created a complex system to create secure passwords. For a small price, anyone can buy one of Miraâ€™s uncrackable passwords from her website Diceware Passwords. As Mira says "it sounds crazy to buy a password, but using a terrible password, such as 12345, is even crazier." With online accounts being hacked more frequently, this young entrepreneur saw a market for creating secure passwords.

To craft her passwords, Mira uses a system called diceware. She rolls dice 5 times for each password she creates and looks up the numbers to find corresponding words in the diceware dictionary. The diceware dictionary is a word list where 5 number sequences match unique words that are both easy to spell and remember. Mira then sends the passwords by postal mail to maintain their security.
