Augmented Reality Glasses Guide the Blind

Rebecca.Sullivan

February 14, 2019


Researchers at CalTech are adapting the Hololens to create a virtual navigation system for the blind. Using Hololens’ augmented reality (AR) capacity, the team developed glasses for the blind that overlay sound on top of physical objects. When a user turns toward an object in the room, the glasses will say the name of the object out loud. The researchers tested the product by having blind subjects navigate their way to a second-floor office following the device’s audio prompts.

The researchers envision these glasses being provided as guides for the blind in public buildings and large spaces. What other functionalities could this or other VR and AR products have? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
