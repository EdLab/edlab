Recap: ArtsEdTech Meetup @ Empire Hotel

Brian.Sweeting

May 7, 2014

Last night Mason and I attended the #ArtsEdTech Networking Happy Hour at the Empire Hotel and had some riveting conversations with individuals doing cool things in arts. Here are a few highlights from my three favorite conversations:

  • Autismate is one of the few mobile applications addressing special needs students. By providing users with customizable visualizations of every day objects, the app helps improve daily communication and social and behavioral skills.
  • Teachers at Third Street Music School have been inspired by educators like James Frankel, an associate professor here at TC, to integrate advanced collaborative music making programs into their theory curriculum, and to support organizations like Music First that ensure artists are being paid fairly for their creations.
  • Baruch College is working on a grant from the Rubin Museum to encourage viable ways for faculty to integrate museum exhibits into their curriculum. Their collaboration site, Ahead21, offers instructional resources for teachers and supports an impressive network of arts educators in NY.

Mason has some fun things to share too, like banana xylophones. Take it away.
