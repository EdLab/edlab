Startup Spotlight: Remind101

Kate Meersschaert

March 7, 2012

The Details and Back-story Remind101 was started at Michigan State University in 2009 by Brett and David Kopf. The brothers started the text-message-based startup after feeling overwhelmed by assignments and work, and realizing that a simple text message from an instructor could help them keep track of multiple deadlines. Remind101 lets instructors send a text message to a pre-loaded list of students and their parents and easily track these interactions. Unique Point of Difference Simplicity. The platform, app, and user experience are incredibly simple. There are no metrics, just a powerfully useful tool for teachers to more fully connect with students using something as simple and ubiquitous as a mobile phone. Also, unlike Facebook or another form of social media, Remind101 automatically "anonymizes" all numbers so teachers never see student #'s and vice versa. Being able to easily harness technology that most students have–a phone–to remind them about important in-class or extracurricular activities seems like a low barrier way to unite learners and instruction. EdLab Relevance and Takeaway Sometimes the most useful tools are the simplest and closest to home or, in the case of this app, your pocket. Which of our current apps could utilize additional mobile interactions? As we develop New Learning Times content and structure, mobile will be of central importance. As such, what additional interactions beyond consuming content can we imagine?
