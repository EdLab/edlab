Great videos from Teachers Tube-- tomatoes that defy gravity!

Jessica Pang

February 28, 2008

Having reviewed over 300 videos from Teachertube.com, I probably have seen over 100 videos on the water cycle, 3 states of matter and different rock types (although I still don't know how to spell them). While there are a lot of repeated topics, I do find some very interesting and surprisingly good videos from Teacher Tube. 1. Tomatoes that “defy gravity”! A student interviewed scientists about growing plants in Space. This topic is really advance. Which direction will the roots/ leaves go if there's no gravity? http://www.teachertube.com/view_video.php?viewkey=2ad376f8597d28cbd372 2. Estrogen Mimickers! This is an NBC clip that talks about how estrogen mimickers in cleaning agents are transforming normal fishes to transsexual fishes. The cupid is Nonylephenol. Europe has banned it, USA is still allowing it, and P&G claims they have stopped using it in all their detergents (but still in cosmetics). The most horrifying fact is when human absorbed these chemical estrogen mimickers, they WILL add to our natural estrogens. http://www.teachertube.com/view_video.php?viewkey=d0ed3033a3281d865ccb There are a lot of people/ groups that submitted more than one video, but I think the best series goes to the Ontario Science Centre ~!!!
