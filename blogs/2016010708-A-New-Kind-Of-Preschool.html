A New Kind Of Preschool

ABARRY

January 7, 2016

Preschool is the most playful but also one of the most important stages of our educational journey. For many children, preschool is their first experience interacting with teachers and peers. It's their first opportunity at learning. A preschool in Seattle has developed a new system to help preschoolers learn and experience something new. The program integrates the concept of experiencing and exploring nature with learning that usually occurs indoors. The students spend four hours a day, regardless of the weather, at the University of Washington Botanic Gardens. A typical day starts at 9 am where teachers help students zip up their waterproof suits for a fun and adventurous day. Students go on walks with their teachers, eat lunch, and sing songs, among a host of other activities, under a peaceful place. I like this concept of allowing students to explore nature and interact with teachers and classmates. Additionally, education should not be solely classroom based. A mixture with the outdoors provide students with the opportunity to explore other things outside the traditional classroom.
