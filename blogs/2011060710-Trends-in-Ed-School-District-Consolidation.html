Trends in Ed: School District Consolidation

Skanda Amarnath

June 7, 2011

About a year ago, my home state of New Jersey decided to back off from what I saw as necessary school district consolidation. There are enough districts in New Jersey that have ridiculously low enrollment and disproportionately large bureaucracies, a problem that certainly plagues other states as well. Unfortunately, consolidation efforts were deemed a legal nightmare. Currently the state government now simply encourages resource sharing between districts. Now 7 districts in Vermont are about to vote on merging into one district. If the vote passes, it is sure to be a promising sign for those in favor of consolidation. Otherwise, the way Texas and most other states have been managing costs in a time of short-term budget shortfalls will continue to prevail.
