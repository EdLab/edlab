Bill Gates and the Problem of Great Teachers

bhughes

March 10, 2009

At around 8 minutes, Our Friend Bill begins his discussion of how to support education in the 21C. Quickly, he narrows his focus to "great teaching" as the most important factor in educational achievement, and then talks about the problem of finding/creating it. Worth watching! Importantly, for us here at EdLab who tend to focus less on teaching (and more on learning), note his very practical approach (much like his focus on mosquito netting to help solve the malaria problem). This is not bad, just interesting. The picture he paints of the excellent teacher (who runs around keeping everyone's attention) illustrates the problem in a nutshell: Really, we need to find and train millions of people to entertain disinterested kids? For what future exactly? Hey, I'm just highlighting the problem as I see it. Special treat: At around 13 minutes, he points to data that suggests masters degrees don't affect the quality of teaching. Thanks Hugo!
