[4:15] Social Technology and Education - cont'd

jvazquez

August 14, 2009

Social networking lessons from the Obama campaign — Leonard Lin -Was impressed by Obama's tech policy -Blue State Digital -“By the time I came on board, the election was 80% over.“ -When the media covers the Obama online presence, they paint it as an overnight phenomenon but it had been in the works since 2004 -Communications tools don't get socially interesting until they get technologically boring — Clay Shirky, Here Comes Everybody -The killer app pf the Obama campaign was …… email! ---It had the biggest impact on getting people involved and active. ---You need critical mass for a tool to be effective: 1B+ emails, 12 M subscribers; 1 in 5 of the people who voted for him received the emails -The actual social networking tools didn't really have much impact on the campaign -The real groups were mailing lists ---Also, affinity groups -YouTube- ---David Plouffe's strategy updates- Sept 17, 2008; He was in his real office, making human engagement -“Social” is not enough. Case in point: McCain Space ---You have to have a goal, a direction. ---Instrument well- test ---Iterate ---Remember: Not everything will launch, not everything will be successful
