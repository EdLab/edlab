This Collaborative Musical Instrument Teaches Physics, Math, and Music

saraehardman

June 27, 2017


A creative, collaborative, tactile musical instrument geared toward young children is slated for release at the end of this year. Musicon is a new instrument that combines design and music to inspire children to learn by experimenting. Children manipulate the contraptionâ€™s sounds by pressing buttons on its cylindrical shell and choosing a tempo by which the shell turns.

The designers of Musicon spent 18 months prototyping it, receiving feedback along the way from education and music specialists. The instrument is intended to give children a taste of physics, math, and coding as they play with creating new songs and rhythms. Because it provides a tactile experience that tests fine motor skills, it is also intended to be a useful tool for young children with special needs, such as those on the autism spectrum. Musicon started as a Kickstarter campaign and will be available for purchase in November of this year.
