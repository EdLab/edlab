How LA Makerspace Brings STEAM Learning to Everyone

Ryan.Allen

December 4, 2017


"Making more makers." -LA Makerspace-

Technology Access for All

LA Makerspace is a Los Angeles-based nonprofit organization geared toward providing access to science, technology, engineering, the arts and mathematics (STEAM) education and training for 21st century careers. Founded in 2012 by technology expert Tara "Tiger" Brown, filmmaker Jeff Geoffray, and science educator Ariel Levi Simons, the organization blends elements from each of the founders. Its courses center on robotics, coding, stopmotion animation, 3D printing, and other creative outlets. LA Makerspace is partnered with 160 library branches in the Los Angeles Public Library System and has taught over 7,000 kids in the past year.

All of the programming for children is free. The founders hope that their organization can bring the general public the same educational and technological resources found in more affluent private schools.

Outside fundraising is crucial for the operations. LA Makerspace has already received sponsorship from Google Rise for its Coding Crew program. Currently, funds are being raised for a new maker center in the Koreatown LA Library branch.

Building a Maker Community

The goal for LA Makerspace goes beyond introducing kids to project-based learning. They also focus on encouraging others to pass along the knowledge and creativity gained through maker programs. For instance, kids are not only taught how to code in their classes; they are also encouraged and coached on how to teach these new skills to friends and family.

Even educators can build new skills through the program. The organization hosts STEAM curriculum courses for local librarians that can be transferred back to their local branches. All of the curriculum is also open-sourced and freely available for anyone to download.
