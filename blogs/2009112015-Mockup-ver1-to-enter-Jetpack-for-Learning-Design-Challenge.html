Mockup ver.1 to enter 'Jetpack for Learning Design Challenge'

Arianna Choi

November 20, 2009

Do you remember the very first time you had to do a research project? What do you think of our idea? Jeannie, Jess, Jo, and I are looking forward to submit our idea to Mozilla Firefox's Jetpack for Learning Design Challenge. The submission requirement for the first round is very simple: 1) describe your concept (no defined format; no particular word limit) and 2) provide a link to your mockup. By Wednesday, Nov 25th, we are going to finalize our concept and submit our Firefox add-on idea, temporarily called "Smart Researcher." Designed for grades 4-8 students and teachers, [Smart Researcher] is a social online resource evaluator. It allows students to select and rate any part of online resource into three categories: Red (bad reference), Yellow (not sure), and Green (good to use). For each rating, the rater can write up to 140 characters to explain why s/he chose that particular color. The metadata (i.e. url address, selected text, given rating and short-text explanation) will be automatically aggregated and be shared with the teacher (who sets up this private network) and other classmates on [www.smartresearcher.com] to discuss the basic principles and process of doing research. This tool should offer practical help to each student as s/he independently screens for appropriate references and cross-checks the validity of content. Best of all, sharing this 'reference rating/filtering' effort will also help them to collaboratively reflect on their research skills. Most ideal scenario I can think of is if a teacher assigns the same research topic to everyone in class. Take a look at our first stab at it. Only four slides! Go full-screen to read the text. ... and please grab any one of us for feedback. Time is ticking and we want to stand out from thousands(?) of entries :)
