Higher Education in New York Prisons

AbdulMalik

January 10, 2016

The goal of the prison system has changed over the years. Inmates who need to be rehabilitated tend to face more punishment than expected in the prison system. We rarely hear anything about education when it comes to discussions about prisons.

This proposal by New York Governor Andrew Cuomo has sparked a nationwide debate on whether funding education in prisons is a detriment or an advantage to society. Some state prisons already offer a few college courses but this program will expand it and make credits obtained in prison transferrable to schools outside prison. Those serving life sentences will not qualify for this program. The Manhattan District Attorney assured the public that this program will help reduce crimes tremendously. He explained that several studies have shown that highly educated inmates are less likely to commit crimes after they are released. This initiative is based on the assumption that each individual should be allowed to redeem themselves. How will higher education in prison possibly deter individuals from committing crimes?
