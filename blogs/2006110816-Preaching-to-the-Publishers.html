Preaching to the Publishers

Frank Webster

November 8, 2006

I was once asked what librarians would want from an institutional TCR site. Andrew K. Pace, who writes the monthly "Technically Speaking" column for American Libraries magazine, blogs about the importance of standards like NISO-MI, SUSHI and COUNTER to facilitate gathering usage statistics and federated searching: Preaching to the Publishers "...If libraries did not have to expend so much effort to collect statistics, they might actually have some time to discern what they mean." And: "...It's all well and good to make publisher websites nicer landing places, but such effort without a means to push content out to users or to make the content available for federated search are futile."
