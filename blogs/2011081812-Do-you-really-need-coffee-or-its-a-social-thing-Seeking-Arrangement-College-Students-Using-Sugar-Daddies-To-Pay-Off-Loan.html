"Do you really need coffee or it's a social thing" - Seeking Arrangement: College Students Using 'Sugar Daddies' To Pay Off Loan

Angela Lee

August 18, 2011

Continuing from the last series of "Do you really need coffee or it's a social thing" on pursuing a higher degree and Kevin's post on the "The Long and Winding Road to a PhD (Warning: depressing)", it's clear that not everyone can afford to purse a higher education. The Huffington Post recently published an article on a service called Seeking arrangements, which many college students use. I would like to ask my fellow Edlabbers' opinion on what you think about the site. This site has caused quite some controversial noise recently as it is matching college students in debt to meet older, wealthy individuals who can provide and even pay off their student loans. The founder of seeking arrangements is a MIT graduate who "got the idea for Seeking Arrangement more than 20 years ago, while in college at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Watching from the sidelines as his beautiful dorm mates pursued significantly older, moneyed men, Wade fantasized about someday becoming one such man. After business school at MIT and stints at General Electric and Microsoft, Wade dabbled in various start-ups before finally creating his own." Read the full article on seeking arrangements here.
