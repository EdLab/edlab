D&R: Characteristics of Online Readability

Brian.Sweeting

September 30, 2014

*Private* Inspired by this study on forgetting online content, and this short story on finding your voice, I've created some activities to help us identify best practices for improving the readability of our online content. Read through first Article A and then Article B. Article A Article B In your group, discuss:

  • Which one was easier to read?
  • What did you take away from each article?
  • What was different about each?
Based on your group discussion of the articles and the resources below, create a list of at least 5 suggestions for improving the readability of online content. These can be include guidelines for formatting, style, voice, tone, grammar, and punctuation. Please list your suggestions below in the comments. Resources: 8 Guidelines For Better Readability On The Web 7 Best Practices for Improving Your Website's Usability A Man Who Knew The Value Of The Human Voice George Orwell's Brilliant Guide to Writing Well The Readability Test Tool
