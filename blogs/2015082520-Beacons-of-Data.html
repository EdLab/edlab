Beacons of Data

Rebecca Beeson

August 25, 2015


The information age is all about connectivity, easy access to an outlet, and wifi signals. It's hardly shocking that wifi is now an assumed amenity in our cafes, flights, and AirBnb abodes, but where might you find your next connection? A light bulb might not be your first guess, yet residents of Plainville, Connecticut will soon be able to surf their Facebook and food blogs via the nearest street lamp. It may make for an interesting scene to observe people soaking up hotspots on the street corner, but what does this mean for wifi's ubiquitous existence and our expectations? In the future, where won't we be able to connect?
