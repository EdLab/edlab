On Annotating

Carmen James

September 30, 2014

(private) I came across this quote and it reminded me of Craig Tashman's of Liquid Text presentation and the importance of finding fluid ways of annotating and organizing information. It is a helpful reminder, especially when browsing and reading so many things in one day, that we have to find ways to organize and creatively keep track of our notes. "They say an elephant never forgets. Well, you are not an elephant. Take notes, constantly. Save interesting thoughts, quotations, films, technologiesâ€¦the medium doesn't matter, so long as it inspires you. When you're stumped, go to your notes like a wizard to his spellbook. Mash those thoughts together. Extend them in every direction until they meet." - Aaron Koblin, head of the Data Arts Team in Google's Creative Lab How do you keep track of your notes, quotes, annotations, etc? I heavily use gmail drafts. I am constantly creating new drafts for notes I want to remember or things I need to do. I would say this is not the best strategy. I believe the better we can organize our notes, the better writer we can be. I look forward to sharing resources to support better writing via better note taking.
