Content Wanted: Lorem Ipsum Need Not Apply

jvazquez

April 1, 2015

Content is no longer the red-headed stepchild of design! Rise up, words! In our time of agile, get-it-done development, wires and prototypes often pass muster without so much as a word of intelligible language. It's all filler; fake page headers sitting on top of a pile of mushy body text. Even when real words are used, they are superficial at best. If you don't develop a narrative from the beginning, how can you accurately evaluate your site in those early pre-alpha stages? How will you know if your website meets your content needs? It's a point well made in Christopher Detzi's Content Conundrum. He uses the following example to underscore why genuine content — written during the development process — is so important. The image on the left is chock full of filler content; the image on the right is the same page with all the necessary information on it. Notice how the CTA gets pushed below the fold in the second instance. If UI designers had hold of this copy during their design process, they may have decided to change the layout of the page to inspire more clicks on that CTA. Writing site copy in conjunction with UI can inform design. Sometimes images speak louder than words, so a designer might choose to reinterpret text as a visual instead. The writer/designer collab is further emphasized in Dr. Bronwyn Jones's influential and highly shared 2007 article, "Better Writing Through Design." So next time you're prototyping — be it for a website, a game, a course — resist the urge to use placeholder and filler text. See if adding some thoughtful content improves your design at all. If it doesn't, the least you could do is use something better than the regular, boring ol' ipsum.
