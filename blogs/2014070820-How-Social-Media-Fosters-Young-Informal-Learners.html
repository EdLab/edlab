How Social Media Fosters Young Informal Learners

Carmen James

July 8, 2014


Mimi Ito, research director of the Digital Media and Learning Hub at the University of California Humanities Research Institute (UCHRI), looks at how social media spaces provide opportunities to foster kids’ intellectual development, their civic engagement and their personal development. Friendship-driven social media use, for example, is critical for kids growing up today. She differentiates friendship-driven social media use from kids who are "messing around" or "geeking out." Students who are messing around are on the first tier of discovery, inquiry, and understanding. Students who are geeking out are on the second tier; these kids have strong "interest-driven orientations" and are using digital environments to explore and develop technical kinds of media literacy.

Ito believes all three kinds of media use are important and yet today we lack understanding of how, as adults, we can support kids’ social media use. For example, students who are geeking out are hungry for information, and the more we can support them in this critical stage of inquiry and development of skill formation the better for their future and our society as they are well poised to make important scientific discoveries.

What do you think about these three spheres of social media use? How can we encourage more kids to geek out? Share your thoughts in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
