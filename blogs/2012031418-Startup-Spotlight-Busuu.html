Startup Spotlight: Busuu

Kate Meersschaert

March 14, 2012

The Details and Back-story Busuu.com's two founders, Adrian & Bernhard feel that they have, "personally suffered from the traditional way to learn a new language" which they describe as, "expensive, difficult and boring." To overcome these limitations they have created a "freemium", online language learning community that is focused on immersive real-world experience. How is this real-world relevance possible in an online learning space? The answer lies in a recent partnership with the UK newspaper The Guardian & Macmillan Education. This partnership allows users to read news articles in the language they are studying and instantly look up words they are unsure of in the integrated Macmillan-powered dictionary. Busuu.com also launched a bold promotional campaign (see the vialogue embedded below) to serve as both a viral marketing effort and to help raise awareness for endangered languages like "Busuu." Unique Point of Difference Social editing of assignments... Users are able to earn points for completing writing assignments around articles from The Guardian, which are then edited by user volunteers in a kind of wiki-style social editing experiment. According to this TechCrunch piece, over 25,000 writing exercises have been edited by volunteers in 7 different languages so far. EdLab Relevance Social editing of user generated content within a learning environment is an interesting and engaging way to create a "community" around platform content. As we explore new ways to make mSchool content more social, this might be an interesting feature to explore further. Takeaway Partnerships work when they enhance the features already present or provide unique new opportunities for discovery and engagement and Busuu.com is a great example of this.
