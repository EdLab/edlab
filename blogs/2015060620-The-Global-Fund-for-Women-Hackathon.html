The Global Fund for Women Hackathon

Nantwi

June 6, 2015


The absence of women in the tech industry isn't a new topic of conversation. In the U.S. there's much discussion over the systemic causes of the gender divide in Silicon Valley, but the disparity is even more troubling on a global scale. Girls in developing countries are often denied a right to a basic education. Access to technology is a far-fetched dream; however, increasing access to technology provides girls with a chance to address issues that directly affect them.

Earlier this year, the Global Fund for Women hosted a weekend long hackathon for girls in five international cities: New York City; Oakland; Taipei; Trivandum, India; and Porto Alegre, Brazil to do just that. The goal of the hackathon is to bring more young women into the conversation, in hopes that they will be able to highlight and address important issues that affect not just girls, but everyone.
