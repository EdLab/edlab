EdLab Review: Biblion

LauraCostello

October 23, 2011

Biblion: The Boundless Library is the New York Public Library's archival site and app for their 1939-40 World's Fair Collection. Pros: The site for the World's Fair is well-curated, consisting of several topical nodes which each contain pages of photos, clippings, and documents as well as a running explanation of the historical value of the presented documents. The collection itself is vast and extremely interesting. Most of it is press photography and published news, but the background correspondence is quite fun. The app is very pretty, with nice photo piles and a good timeline feature. The color choices, which look a little strange on the web, make perfect sense on the app. Cons: The nodes are selected well, but arranged poorly for web viewing. The archival documents line the left side of the page and the thin explanation runs down the right. This gives a rather squished together effect that ruins the delightful mystery of the offerings. It's a bit scrapbooky and over-explained for my archival tastes, but the care and knowledge with which it was selected is evident everywhere. EdLab Relevance I've been thinking a lot about archival representations in brainstorming around Pocket Knowledge. I really think that archives have benefited heartily from the media-heavy web of the past few years. No longer a niche product seen by few scholars, modern archival hubs have a real audience and a real platform for sharing. One of the things I like best about Biblion is the clarity of the content and scope on the homepage. Our Takeaway: Biblion is a pretty app and a well selected website, it's a good example of the possibilities of digital archival representation.
