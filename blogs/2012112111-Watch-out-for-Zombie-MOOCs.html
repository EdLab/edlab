Watch-out for Zombie MOOCs!

Kate Meersschaert

November 21, 2012

powered by Fotopedia

(Check-out Fotopedia site used above for auto-embedding and attribution!) Larry Cuban, no not this Cuban, has very definite views re: education and edtech. As Edsurge reported this AM, the latter Cuban's recent post on his blog re: "Zombie education" is worth reading. He outlines the dangers of perpetuating "living dead" ideas in Ed that won't seem to die. In particular, his piece focuses on "cyber-charter schools" and new technologies for K-12. Definitely a must-read! Night of the living MOOC? What really caught my eye more than this post on Zombie ideas in ed... is Cuban's recent piece on MOOCs. Not only does Cuban feel that they are mostly hyperbole, but he dropped-out of the Stanford AI course that I took, loved and received a certificate from. Read more about my experience with this MOOC here. Our Takeaways... While I don't entirely agree with Cuban re: MOOCs... I do think that we should assess and apply more scientific rigor before EVERYONE jumps-on the MOOC bandwagon. I predict if we don't use a bit of restraint and wait for more results and testing... ineffective MOOCs, like cyber-charter schools will never die. Regardless, we should consider his warnings as we develop mSchool!
