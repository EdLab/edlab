With Common Core, Fewer Topics but Covered More Rigorously

Bismark Appiah

September 4, 2013

One of the biggest mistakes in early education is to not fully understand some part of a specific topic you've learned. For instance, a kindergarten student should be able to fully grasp the basic concept of counting numbers before making him or her understand it thoroughly. The reason is because the Common Core Standards, which has been introduced and adopted by many states, including New York State, tends to reduce the amount of topics in a specific subject a teacher will cover with their class during a school term. My concern is the impact this will bring to our children's education.

If the new mathematics standards adopted by New York and 44 other states work as intended, then children, especially in the lower elementary grades, will learn less math this year.
Nonetheless, I think the common core will improve students' learning and the way they study. However, I am also worried that the new standards might be overwhelming for some students. If you take a look at the new mathematics standards for kindergarten students, they are now supposed to orally learn how to count from 1-100 and write all of the numbers to 20. Previously, the kindergarten students were expected to orally count 1 to 20 and write 1 to 10. You can see that there has been a great amount of change, and although this change will help the students learn a lot, I also think that it puts them in a difficult position in terms of quickly understanding what they have learned.
