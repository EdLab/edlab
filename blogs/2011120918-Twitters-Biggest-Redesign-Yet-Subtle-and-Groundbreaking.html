Twitter's Biggest Redesign Yet... Subtle & Groundbreaking?!

Kate Meersschaert

December 9, 2011

Twitter has redesigned & simplified their site in the largest redesign for the micro-blogging platform since their founding in 2006. In the context of the workshop we attended and live-blogged last night, I wonder how these new designs will effect the sphere of Twitter "influencer's"... influence? Will the new design facilitate greater sharing between people who already follow each other? Or will the new design place an emphasis on "discovery" and allow people to find new people to follow? Regardless, the impact of theses changes and the questions posed above await answers, but one thing is clear; the new features and design elements including redesigned navigation buttons & tabs, more prominently displayed photos & videos within tweets and the ability to easily embed tweets into blogs and other platforms will have an impact on user behavior. Just how much an an impact remains to be seen. What do you think of Twitter's new elements which affect not only the site but all mobile platforms and Tweet Deck? My take is that these changes are deceptively subtle, yet potentially groundbreaking... the ability to embed Tweets is a major development that I am excited to track and the more graphically displayed and user friendly "deck" should increase user engagement and encourage exploration. Only time and thousands of loyal Twitterers' Tweets will tell.
