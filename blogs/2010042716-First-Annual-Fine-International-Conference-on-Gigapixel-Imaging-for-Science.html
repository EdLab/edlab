First Annual Fine International Conference on Gigapixel Imaging for Science

Adrienne Garber

April 27, 2010

The CREATE Lab at Carnegie Mellon University's Robotics Institute, together with The Fine Foundation, will host the first-ever Fine International Conference on Gigapixel Imaging for Science, on November 11â€”13, 2010 on the campus of Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh, PA. The conference will explore innovative use of gigapan technology in the classroom, the field and the laboratory. Keynote speakers include:

  • Mark Bauman, Executive Vice President TV & Film at National Geographic
  • Alan Eustace, Senior Vice President, Engineering & Research, Google
  • Pete Worden, Director of NASA Ames Research Center
    • For those of you unfamiliar with this technology, GigaPan consists of three technological developments: (1) a robotic camera mount for capturing very high-resolution (gigapixel and up) panoramic images using a standard digital camera; (2) custom software for constructing very high-resolution gigapixel panoramas; and (3) a new type of website for exploring, sharing and commenting on gigapixel panoramas and the detail our users will discover within them. One of the most famous GigaPan photographs has been viewed over 12 million times by people in 210 countries.
