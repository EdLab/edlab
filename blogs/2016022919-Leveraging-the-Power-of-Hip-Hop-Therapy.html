Leveraging the Power of Hip Hop Therapy

dinosossi2

February 29, 2016


Ian Levy is a hip hop educator with a dream. He counsels students at New Visions Charter High School in the Bronx. Ian leverages the liberating power of music to appeal to the emotional lives of teens who are burdened with challenges from deep within. Hip hop therapy aims to help youth make sense of the rough terrain of their difficult environment through music.

Have you used technology, like musical production tools, to cope with your educational and personal problems? How can we help youth in impoverished areas acquire digital tools to help them create their own worlds? Tell us about a piece of art that you have created that helped you cope with life in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion:

@00:13 Beckieb: Hip hop can seem like expression distanced from education. I believe it can be nested with the Humanities as an art form. There is structure, rhythm, history, and vocabulary that makes hip hop a discourse with society and the past. It also incorporate[s] a performance element to it [that] is different from other forms of expression.
