BBC open source production : Digital revolution

Frederik G. Pferdt

July 14, 2009

The British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC) is producing an open source documentary series about the "digital revolution". They are planning to show how the web has changed our society and will collaborate with the internet community by showing the users some material to comment on and allowing them to make proposals to develop the plot. At the end there will be a documentation about how the web has changed our lives over the last 20 years. It will be on air starting in spring 2010. Right now the users are able to comment on blog entry by Aleks Krotoski, who will present the series. "Digital revolution launch": The inventor of the Web, Sir Tim Berners-Lee, with neuroscientist Baroness Susan Greenfield, technology journalist Bill Thompson, and presenter Aleks Krotoski at the launch of open source documentary "digital revolution". Debate covers policing and security of the internet, privacy, and the potential of the web when it reaches the developing world to help human rights.
