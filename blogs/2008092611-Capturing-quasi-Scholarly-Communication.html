Capturing (quasi-)Scholarly Communication

Anthony Cocciolo

September 26, 2008

I've been interested in new ways of capturing the communication that goes on in F2F contexts (e.g., Meety), and I thought I would try out something for my presentation to the CCTE Colloquium.

Participatory Culture and Web 2.0 in Higher Education
View SlideShare presentation or Upload your own. (tags: learning_environments repository)
During the presentation, I placed my iPhone and used the .99 cent recorder device to record the audio of my presentation. I placed the phone on the table next to me. If I had enough forethought when I got dressed in the morning (which I clearly and regularly do not), I would have worn a shirt with a pocket in the front and placed it in there. Before I did any of this, I cleared off a bunch of music on my phone to make room for what could be a long audio file. The audio file turned out to be only about 45MB, so this was totally needless. Afterwards, I transferred the audio file to my desktop and edited it with Windows Movie Maker (the only editing I did was remove the beginning where the absent members of the class were being criticized for not being thereâ€¦ hoping that it would get back to them somehow). The file started out as an AIFF, then a WMA, and then I finally converted it to an MP3. I uploaded the Powerpoints to SlideShare, and linked the Audio file to the slides. The interface for matching up the audio with the slides is actually really slick (it is a very smooth flash interface that allows you to listen to the audio and map it onto the slides). Et voila, a relatively accurate and durable capture of the presentation, without all the needless information (like the drabness of the classroom in Grace Dodge, my fidgeting, etc.) The audio quality would be much better with a clip-on microphone attached to the iPhone, but it seems to be useable without it.
