Avast Ye!

LauraCostello

September 28, 2011

A new website has sprung from the primordial internet soup of e-textbook piracy. Library Pirate aims to create a community around student file sharing in an effort to circumvent what the creators see as corruption in academic publishing. Their strategy is twofold, users can “rent a pirate” by purchasing a gift card for a semester e-textbook rental. Pirates from the site will rent the textbook using the gift card, strip the DRM and return the open pdf to the student for use and sharing. The second strategy is equally innovative, one of the site contributors created a book production app that uses a digital camera for scanning. Users can photograph pages of their physical textbooks and the app will crop, sharpen images and generate a finished, readable pdf. While I can't professionally advocate blatant piracy, the DIY moxie of these folks is pretty inspiring. I'll have to give the app a try for DocDel scanning (in a legit fair-use way, of course!) The site is getting a lot of press in academic and e-publishing circles and that's not necessarily a good thing for a pirate's den, but I hope the minds behind the project go on sticking it to publishers, especially if the bi-products are usable strategies and technologies for law-abiding libraries.
