Reflections on our Judicial System

Francisco.Mendoza

May 17, 2017

{{unknown}}
{{unknown}}
I am now in the second week of the course. After reading this article, I realized how the current judicial and penal system in our country is so unfair to many. The article did a good job highlighting the differences in sentencing criminals between how the U.S. and Europe. For instance, while a lot of states in America still have death sentences, it is abolished in almost all of Europe. Additionally, in some European countries, prisoners are provided actual jobs and even vacation time; a dream and pure fantasy for all those incarcerated in American prisons. Overall, I was glad to be exposed to a different point of view when it comes to sentencing and treatment of prisoners. I am very surprised and shocked at how inmates in Europe are treated. It puts our judicial and penal system as well as treatment of prisoners to shame. The article, in addition to being informative, was also very thought provoking. Moments like these I wish I could have taken the class in-person so I could participate in what I am sure will be interesting arguments about prisoner treatment. For instance, I could imagine a scenario where students debate the merits and disadvantages of both the European and American system of sentencing and prisoner treatment. It would be great if MIT OpenCourseWare could offer a discussion platform for learners who are taking this class. The debate should extend beyond the physical classroom.
