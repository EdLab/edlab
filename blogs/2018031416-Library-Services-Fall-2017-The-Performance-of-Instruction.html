Library Services, Fall 2017: The Performance of Instruction

Allen.Foresta

March 14, 2018

The following are representative topics and areas of research on which librarians consulted with students:

  • The history of mathematics curriculum in New York City, present state of mathematics education, and mathematics curriculum impact on communities of color.
  • Critical summary of research on assessment and intervention in reading.
  • The connection between Mexican teachers and Teachers College in the early twentieth century.
  • Funding for girls' education in secondary and tertiary levels in India.
  • Trends in nursing curriculum development, specifically internationalism (more foreign students), change in health policy (hospital to community based), access and affordability of nursing education, and human resources (creating a happy workforce).
  • How students with disabilities perceive themselves in regards to physical activity.
  • Empirical papers on how children with traumatic brain injuries (TBI) receive bilingual education.
  • Peace education in India, primarily in K-12 context.
  • Primary source materials on the history of teacher education in Singapore; additionally, autobiographies written by early career American educators and autographies written by teachers in Singapore.
  • Research on Helen M. Walker, TC mathematics educator:  biographical information and writings.
  • Visual art education in private/independent schools.
  • Topics in argumentative discourse, nature and practices of science, evolution of ideas, critical thinking.
  • African Immigrant student populations (middle school and high school) in the U.S and New York; personal narratives and experiences of African Immigrant students and their families as they work to navigate the public school system; how current curricula implement diversity, social justice, equity and human rights so that classrooms can promote an inclusive learning environment for African Immigrant students.
  • Resources and practices to assist middle school students with the art of debate.
  • The history of biology education in America; specifically, how we have credentialed and educated high school science teachers between 1880 and 2000.
  • Phonological analysis of loanword adaptation from English to Taiwanese Mandarin.
  • How cross-cultural and/or trans-cultural students learn, and how this knowledge might inform the way we teach them;what motivates cross-culture and trans-culture students in school learning.
During Fall 2017, Services staff delivered assistance and support to our users in the following categories:
  • Senior Librarians and Services Associates responded to a total of 1,749 in-person and phone queries.
  • We responded to 773 email research- and information-related support requests.
  • Librarians met with students for 78 one-on-one research consultations.
  • Services staff provided 17 course-specific library information sessions for a total number of 277 attendees.
