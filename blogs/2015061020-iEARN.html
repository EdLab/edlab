iEARN

Alexandra Lederman

June 10, 2015


"iEARN (International Education and Resource Network) is the world's largest non-profit global network that enables teachers and youth to use the Internet and other technologies to collaborate on projects that enhance learning and make a difference in the world." (from iEARN site)

By the World, for the World

iEARN is focused on empowering teachers and students to work together online using new technologies to collaborate on a worldwide project, improving the quality of our planet's life. The organization is a safe and structured environment made up of 30,000 schools and youth organizations in over 140 countries. Projects connect studentsâ€™ learning with local and global issues while meeting specific curriculum needs. Students create, research, and share with peers around the world using digital technologies, enabling participants to become global citizens.

Globally Local

iEARN facilitates conferences, summits, and projects all over the world regardless of national borders. The annual conferences and meetings focus on a particular country and provide a space for educators to meet, share, and collaborate. The youth summit places a spotlight on the exceptional students involved in the organization. iEARN provides two forums, one for teachers and the other for students. The online forums allow teachers and students to meet, make announcements, share ideas or initial project ideas, find partner schools, and discuss logistics. iEarn facilitates globally local projects, such as Folk Tales & Storytelling, Plant a Tulip Day, and Medicine in My Backyard.
