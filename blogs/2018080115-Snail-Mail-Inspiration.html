Snail Mail Inspiration

JasmineSK

August 1, 2018

If you are feeling inspired by the July/August Staff Picks, Dear Summer: Letters in Literature, here are some websites that facilitate and encourage letter writing. Postcrossing is an international project in which one exchanges postcards with strangers around the world. Sendsomething.net is a place to connect with other people looking for pen pals. InCoWriMo and Lettermo are annual challenges to send a piece of mail every day during the month of February. I have tried all of these with great success, but please share any other correspondence sites in the comments below! If you are interested in going beyond letters or postcards, redditgifts hosts regularly scheduled themed gift exchanges. Snail mail my email  was a community art project where volunteers would transform emails into handwritten letters and mail them to their intended recipient. The project lasted 6 years (2011-2017) and over 29,000 letters were mailed all across the world. In 2014, my children used the project to send a house warming card to their grandparents who had recently relocated.                         There are also some great books filled with writing prompts and decorative ideas that are available to purchase and I've listed below:

  • Good Mail Day: A Primer for Making Eye-Popping Postal Art by Jennie Hinchcliff
  • Happy Mail: Keep in touch with cool & stylish handmade snail mail! by Eunice Moyle
  • Snail Mail: Rediscovering the Art and Craft of Handmade Correspondence by Michelle Mackintosh
  • Write Back Soon!: Adventures in Letter Writing by Karen Benke
If these resources inspire you, I would love to see the results. Please share your creative correspondence adventures in the comments below!
