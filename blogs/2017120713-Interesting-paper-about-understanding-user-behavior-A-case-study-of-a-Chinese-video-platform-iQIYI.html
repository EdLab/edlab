Interesting paper about understanding user behavior: A case study of a Chinese video platform (iQIYI)

SeungYeon.Lee

December 7, 2017

http://uxpamagazine.org/tracking-user-behavior/


I found this interesting paper about a model to understand user behavior and the application to data from a Chinese video platform called iQIYI.

Basically, they track user actions in a similar way we are doing using Piwik and use those information to refine their product design.


Their case study was interesting. They analyzed data from the Bubble area within the iQIYI app.

The following is a piece of describing the app, extracted from the original paper.


“The target users of Bubble are fans.  The fans can follow their idols, express their feelings towards the idols, make friends, and communicate with their idols online. Sometimes celebrities are invited to Bubble to interact with their fans, for example, when introducing a new movie or TV show. We wanted a better understanding of whether or not we were providing a good video viewing and commenting experience for users and if there were any improvements we could make.


In the Bubble area of the app, users often watch videos and comment on them. As a business, we consider comments on videos to be high value behavior since commenting is often associated with users spending more time in the app and being more engaged. “


They found that users are less engaged with commenting on the video than saving, sharing and downloading videos in their first iteration of analysis. And they described steps of how they improved their design to encourage more commenting actions by analyzing user action tracking data at each iteration.


A part I liked from this paper was that they considered values of different behaviors. This is also what I have been thinking about user behaviors in Vialogues.

Major actions I have been considering for Vialogues are "Video Play/Pause", "Post Comments", "Expand Reply", "Reply Comment", and different video completion rate such as "Video Watch for Seconds", "Video Watch 50%" and "Video Watch 95%" etc.

Among those actions, I think that the order of values could be: Watch a video < Browse others' comments and replies < Post Comments < Post Replies (in increasing order).


So far, my preliminary analysis suggests that there are three big distinct behavior patterns: (1) Mixture of watching a video for secs and posting comments (not reply), (2) Only watching videos and (3) Replying focused behavior.

I am working on further analyses to understand more details about user behavior.

For our research for Vialogues, one thing we need to think about is what is our goal and the ideal behavior we would like all users to reach. I believe one of the goals would be to support active discussion among different users. So I think it is important to understand current situation of usages based on user behavior (e.g., distribution of different behavior patterns) and think about how we can improve our product to encourage more active discussion/communication among users, which is actually what I am currently working on.
