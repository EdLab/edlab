Words of Encouragement?

idrissa10

May 31, 2016

A school in Pennsylvania decided they wanted to send their graduating students off with some encouraging words from Adolf Hitler, Joseph Stalin, and ISIS leader Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi in the school's yearbook. This runs counter to the traditional inspirational quotes from guys like Mahatma Gandhi, Martin Luther King, and Mark Twain. Al-Baghdad quote says, “Be just: the unjust never prosper. Be valiant. Keep your word, even to your enemies.” Though these are wise words, there has been a backlash from students and parents due to the violent history of these individuals. Our constitution guarantees free speech; even hate speech is protected under the constitution. The school might have been trying to encourage students to learn and engage with individuals they might not agree with. Do you agree with the school deciding to share quotes from these notorious leaders?
