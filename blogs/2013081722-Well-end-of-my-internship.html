Well, end of my internship!

Sakar Khattar

August 17, 2013

It's said that all good things must come to an end. Well, so did my internship at Edlab last week. The whole summer went by in such a rush, that I didn't realize how quickly the time to finish up came, until it was staring me right in the face. It was quite an incredible experience, and I am thankful to have been given this opportunity. I personally grew a lot as a programmer, worked on my weaknesses, had my set of highs and lows, both frustrating periods of inefficiency and super productive bursts of time. More than anything else, I felt connected. Connected to everyone around me at Edlab. I met some amazingly wonderful people, and I struggle to think of another place I could meet so many amazing people in one place. I'm thankful for everyone being patient with me, taking time out from their schedule to help me out whenever I had trouble. I hope I was able to repay those times with some meaningful contribution through my work. I have definitely grown in both technical skill and professional behavior with extremely valuable feedback I got. I also had the pleasure of having some of the most awesome people as my fellow interns. I honestly don't know what to write for them, I've been thinking for a while, but I can't come up with words suitable enough. I guess I don't need to say anything, they'll understand. And that would tell you a lot about how wonderful we all were as a group. One of my regrets is that I left in a rush, and didn't get to say goodbye to some people. Maybe it's a good thing I didn't get time to think much about goodbyes, it would have been much harder had I had time to ponder over it. On the plus side, as I said to some of you, I'll keep visiting whenever I'm in New York. Thank you everyone. It's been wonderful knowing you, I promise to keep in touch, and it's been a bright, sunny summer in new york! Best Regards, Sakar
