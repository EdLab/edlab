Corporations Shift from Charity to Investment to Accelerate Technology in Africa

Jenny Abamu

November 17, 2016


Googleâ€™s new project, Digify Africa, is part of an effort to digitally transform countries in Africa. It is a youth-focused digital training program that aims to reach 1 million youth across the continent through partnerships with Livity Africa, The Rockefeller Foundation, and the British Council. Since its start in South Africa in 2014, it has already expanded to Kenya and Nigeria. The program offers tracks of free one- and two-day courses focusing on the role of digital media and learning skills for blogs and websites, as well as face-to-face 10-week courses covering Google analytics, AdWords, SEO, and a lot more.

With youth unemployment up to 35% in some participating countries, the prospects of opening up new learning and job markets is welcome by many, yet this initiative has not been without critics. Some fear "digital colonialism" or structural obstacles that might make investors wary. However, as the Digify Africa Blog points out when African youth gain knowledge and technical skills, they are equipped to create solutions to address local community problems.
