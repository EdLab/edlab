Labfundr

Ryan.Allen

November 12, 2017


"Find, fund, and follow science you care about." ( Labfundr)

Crowdfunding Scientists

Crowdfunding sites like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, and GoFundMe have revolutionized the fundraising process. But, Labfundr’s founder and CEO Eric Fisher, a self-described "entrepreneur and recovering biochemist," could not find research-specific campaign listings on those popular sites. So, he launched his own platform with a strict focus on progressing scientific achievement. Through this Canadian-based startup, scientists can launch campaigns for research projects, travel expenses, lab equipment, program outreach, events, or other initiatives.

Labfundr doesn’t have any specific fundraising limits. But their guidelines suggest researchers first set modest budgetary goals. Like on the other platforms, fundraisers can choose to keep all the donations, even if the final goal hasn’t been met, or they can send back the money collected if the project cannot work with a smaller budget.

Public Accountability

Labfundr’s platform does not let just anyone launch campaigns. There is a vetting process for fundraisers to ensure projects meet ethical standards and have realistic outcomes. This accountability helps to increase legitimacy and foster trust with the general public. Right now, the service is focused on researchers at Canadian institutions.

Unlike the other crowdfunding platforms, Labfundr limits rewards for backers. Campaigns can send small tokens of appreciation, but the site hopes that scientific progress is the real reward for their community. The guidelines suggest that the most successful projects are the ones that are actively engaging with the audience via discussions, emails, and even videos.
