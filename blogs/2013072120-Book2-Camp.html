Book2 Camp

Rebecca Beck

July 21, 2013


The Unconference
On February 10, 2013 librarians, booksellers, and people who work in print and digital publishing gathered in NYC for Book2 Camp, an unconference strategically scheduled the day before the Tools of Change Publishing Conference, helping to bring in esteemed authors and publishing leaders. After everyone proposed and decided upon discussion topics, an exciting day of imagining the future of books ensued. A central theme that emerged throughout the day was ‘what would happen if people didn’t profit from writing?’ With the industry of publishing changing rapidly and the way that people read and obtain books constantly evolving, the future of books is unknown.

The Next Incarnation of the The Book
Many book lovers and individuals working with books have strong ideas and opinions about the future of books and Book2 Camp brings these people together. Book2 Camp describes itself as a series of free meetings that discusses, debates and brainstorms what the next incarnation of the book will be. The leaders of Book2 Camp, Chris Kubica and Ami Greko, have successfully organized meetings that bring together bright and interesting people to dive into deep discussions about the future of books and all of the issues surrounding books in today’s world. Topics discussed have included ‘Rebuilding the Ecosystem: Imagining New Models for Content and Media Companies’ and ‘Middle Core: What Problems Do Average Readers Have?’ The participants break into smaller groups to discuss different aspects of books, writing and publishing. Intimate and fiercely intelligent conversations ensue. Innovative and ‘out of the box’ thinking is encouraged, and Book2 Camp stresses that "there are no wrong answers." Book2 Camp meetings look to the future at all of the possibilities and opportunities that exist for books and publishing.
