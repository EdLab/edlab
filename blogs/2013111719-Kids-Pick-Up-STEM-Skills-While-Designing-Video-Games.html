Kids Pick Up STEM Skills While Designing Video Games

chingfu

November 17, 2013


Kids learning through video games is not a new phenomenon; educators have been exploring ways to integrate video games into their teaching for some time. However, kids writing their own video games is an arguably less common learning approach that is gaining popularity among kids and teachers. What do kids learn from writing their own video games? How can video game design and production be linked to academic learning? What are some challenges? Are there any similarities between authoring video games and traditional kid-friendly activities, like playing baseball?

In this video, ten-year-old Rhys shares his game design experiences and helps answer the above questions. Thanks to the rapid advancement of technologies and new media, kids today have easy access to powerful game design programs such as Gamestar Mechanic and Scratch. As producers of videos games, they have new channels to express themselves and new forms to release their creativity. Furthermore, the authentic audience from online game design communities helps learners improve their design skills through feedback loops and motivates them to continue working on their projects.
