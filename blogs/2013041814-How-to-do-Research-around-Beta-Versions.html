How to do Research around "Beta" Versions

Janice Joo

April 18, 2013

Is there a standard way of getting users to check out a beta site? Well, I just got an email to check out something on the abc.com site... and it directed me to what seemed to be a beta site (http://beta.abc.go.com...). It seemed like a sneaky way to get me to interact with a new interface -- through an email newsletter! I looked for that same video clip through the regular abc.com website and got directed to this: After a little more exploration on abc's website, it turns out the entire microsite for one of their shows, "The View", has the beta prefix in the url. So maybe it's not a true testing site. BUT in any case, this got me thinking about how to get users to test a new feature (for our research purposes) without telling them it's "new", thereby avoiding a biased response... (P.S. the video was about Bhangra dance classes at Alvin Ailey... in which one of our very own EdLab'ers is currently a dance student...) http://beta.abc.go.com/shows/the-view/video/PL5554876/_m_VD55296947
