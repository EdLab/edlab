Intelligent Survey Sidekick, Maybe

Sharon Hsiao

August 20, 2012

To provide a personalized survey design platform, there are several approaches we could also consider to make it more artificially intelligent. (Which I assume it's the key for the “sidekick”). Most of the users would think they have “ok-enough” knowledge to design a survey, given the truth is usually otherwise. Survey Sidekick should be an easy to use tool for users to go ahead and design their surveys and obtain assistance down the road. However, to provide the assistance by showing the acclamation point as the sidekick is one technique. My assumption is that it may be less intrusive, but may be less effective too. We could provide more proactive in situ assistance when the user is designing the survey, i.e. detecting the missing survey goals; reminding the importance of creating sub-goals; pre-calculating the ideal sample size based on the targeted effect size; there should be a neat way to show the suggestions and diagnoses. For example, showing the progress chart to identify the missing points, worth reminding points etc., and a survey summary would be nice to help users understand their designs. Utility wise, the tutorials is really good. It could provide thorough information for surveyors. A search tool, visual aids could be great support here to help go through all the text.
