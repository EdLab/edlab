Education Reforms in Sierra Leone

Bismark Appiah

June 4, 2014

Sierra Leone is still reeling from the aftermath of a decades long civil war that ended in 2002. For the most part, schools, families, and prominent buildings were destroyed. This calamity brought sadness and made life difficult for survivors such as Alpha Lamin, a farmer who takes care of 15 children. The sad part about Alpha Lamin's job is he lives in a small compound with no electricity or running water. Alpha Lamin's goal is educate all the 15 children. A few of the children belong to his two brothers, who died during the war.

During the war, the destruction of school buildings meant children couldn't attend school. The current government has managed to rebuild some new schools and enrollment is high, especially among females. This might due to the fact that female students attend school for free. Though Sierra Leone's education system, like most of the country, has come a long way since the war, there are still major issues. Learn more in the video below.
