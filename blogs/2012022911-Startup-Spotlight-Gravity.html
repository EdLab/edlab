Startup Spotlight: Gravity

Kate Meersschaert

February 29, 2012

The Details and Back-story Gravity is on a mission to help personalize the Internet. Using semantic technology, including proprietary ontology and natural language processing algorithms, the Gravity team is able to create personal user interest graphs, analyze content, and generate recommendations based on this data. Companies are then able to use this data to personalize each unique visitors' experience with their site based on what elements they share, use, and interact with. Learn more about Gravity's unique "interest graphing" process here. Founded in 2009 by the former Chief Operating Officer of MySpace, Amit Kapur, and based in Santa Monica, CA (not Silicon Valley!), the group has been hailed by CNET reporter Rafe Needleman as, "one of the most interesting machine learning and data mining experiments I've seen." Unique Point of Difference A million points of light... well, in this case, data. Not only does Gravity pull rich data from their "interest graphs," but they provide a suite of tools to allow you to quickly and dynamically make design, editorial and content creation changes based on this info. Data is one thing, design-driven implementation and real-time updates take these findings out of the theoretical and into the practical. EdLab Relevance and Takeaway As we continue to develop a data analytics "dashboard" to track info across our tools, it is important to remember both the value on the front end, as well as the value of the data being collected on the back end. Both seem to work in harmony here and provide valuable lessons for any new data-driven development. It is one thing to discover the data... how your app allows you to instantly and automatically connect these data dots to real change is the special sauce.
