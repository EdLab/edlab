Trends in Ed: Collaborative Creation Websites

Felix Chang

June 19, 2012

Social networks and crowdsourcing work: Facebook now has more than 900 million users and a study in Nature found that Wikipedia is as reliable as Encyclopedia Britannica. Online communities eliminate distance factors and allow people to create and learn together, separately. Many Eyes by IBM applies these principles to data visualization. Users can upload sets of data, create visualizations for pre-existing data, as well as comment on and rate the work of others. If you like doing the work, it's there for you to do. If you don't like doing the work, there are others there to do it for you (symbiosis, eh?). For those into cinematography, audio production, or writing, Hitrecord.org is a great collaborative site where users can upload anything they've created, from video snippets to soundtracks to scripts. Community members then mash these source materials into snazzy short videos or films. Say you've finished an interactive visualization or video and want to share it. Visualizing.org features a visualizing player that allows you to directly embed your interactive projects and infographics (including HTML5, Java, Flash, video formats) on your site or blog, just like you would with an image or screenshot.
