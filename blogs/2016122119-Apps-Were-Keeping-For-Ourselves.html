Apps We're Keeping For Ourselves

saraehardman

December 21, 2016


We’ve toggled and clicked on and swiped a lot of apps this year, and while many of them have tremendous educational potential, there’s only a handful that we want to keep on our phones or computers for use in our daily lives. Here’s our top five.

1. Field Trip
Walk around your city and learn about its hidden history with this augmented reality app.

2. Google’s Art and Culture App
This app acts as a knowledgeable companion while visiting your favorite museums in person and can virtually take you to museums half the world away.

3. Flexcil
This app merges all the charm of a physical notebook with the convenience of a digital one.

4. The Most Dangerous Writing App
Need help getting over that writer’s block? This app helps you get in the writing flow by forcing you to type without stopping—or everything will be deleted.

5. Tinycards
From the creators of Duolingo comes a new app that tests your knowledge with flashcards. From languages to countries to movies, this app is the perfect long-trip companion for trivia lovers.
