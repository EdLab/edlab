Still Waiting For An Edtech Revolution

Christina Terracino

September 9, 2015


Although it's been predicted time and again that our education system is ready for a revolution, practices have remained static for. . . a long time. A typical classroom still has a single teacher lecturing in front of the class. Every supposed revolution seeks to alter this paradigm—whether by replacing teachers with technological medians or hoping that the internet will provide an unknown breakthrough in instructional delivery.

The good news for teachers is that they are not so easily replaced—not even by the coolest app. Despite the web being the most gigantic repository of information, translating that information into student knowledge is still best facilitated by teachers. Their roles as motivators and guides are essential to students' excellence.

In this video, the most successful learning occurs as a social activity. The internet is a poor replacement for teachers, because it is devoid of the benefits of real life social interaction. Perhaps the edtech revolution won't happen when teachers are replaced, but when they are empowered to use new technologies that are seamlessly integrated in meaningful ways.
