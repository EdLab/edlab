Open Textbook Network Is Trying to Make Textbooks Free

Ryan.Allen

March 1, 2018


"We believe in the power of open education to transform higher education." (Open Textbook Network)

Open Textbook Library

Textbooks are incredibly expensive, costing individual students thousands of dollars each year. The Open Textbook Network is trying to curb this costly aspect of education with the Open Textbook Library, an online resource of high quality, peer-reviewed academic textbooks that are freely available for anyone to use. The textbooks are licensed under Creative Commons and can be edited or modified to fit the needs of individual instructors. These free books can be read online, downloaded in various formats, or even printed out.

The library covers a range of topics, from humanities to mathematics, with resources suitable to both basic introductory courses and more specialized, advanced studies. Professors and administrators even provide ratings to give prospective instructors a better idea of book quality.

Larger Movement

The Open Textbook Network is a nonprofit within the Center for Open Education, a research institution at the College of Education and Human Development, University of Minnesota. In addition to operating the library, the center also organizes a larger network of institutions advocating for the open source textbook movement that now consists of universities and libraries from across the United States.

Institutional membership costs $5,000, which is matched dollar-for-dollar by funding from the William and Flora Hewlett Foundation. Members have access to organizational staff who help promote, train, and support conversion to a free textbook system. Professors from the network also have additional support for publishing open textbooks and can submit them to the library.
