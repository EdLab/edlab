Proper Diet and Learning

Frank Obeng

December 28, 2014

It seems a never-ending cycle that students in our inner cities are constantly under performing. Most indicators point to students' socio-economic background as the biggest indicator of academic achievement. However, a report by the nonprofit organization No Kid Hunger found that 73 percent of students come to school hungry. How is relevant to inner city students? Most inner city schools tend to be overcrowded so students, especially those in large schools, tend to skip breakfast and eat lunch at the wrong time due to their school schedule. For instance, some students have lunch at 10am while some have at 3pm.

The result is a lack of proper diet and students eating junk food they brought from home or school vending machine. Studies show that bad eating habits have negative effects on academic performances. Although students might often complain about food in the cafeteria, it provides them with a balanced diet that is good for their brains.
