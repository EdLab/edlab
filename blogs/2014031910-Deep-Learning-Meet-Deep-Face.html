Deep Learning Meet Deep Face

Kate Meersschaert

March 19, 2014

Facebook/Deep Face Facebook has taken facial recognition and "deep learning" a step further than the competition with, Deep Face. Facial recognition software has been in the works for a while, however, this newest iteration is able to recognize and "straighten" images of faces that depict side or partial views. This development is particularly interesting as Facebook obviously has access to millions of tagged facial images. How accurate are these algorithms? A human can correctly ID the same face twice when presented with multiple images with 97.53% accuracy, Deep Face is not far behind with a success rate of 97.25%. (For more on read this piece from GigaOm & this one from MIT tech Review.) Facing Deep Questions Do you feel that deep learning has a place in education? Will it make us more trackable and less safe? Or, will this technology evolve to make our lives easier and more secure?
