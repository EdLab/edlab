Strategies for Raising IQ

fanwar

April 16, 2009

For some reason, I've been seeing a lot of articles like this NYTimes one on raising IQs. There seems to be a lot of momentum building both within the research communities and the general public about strategies for improving intelligence (beyond genetic engineering). This article is particularly interesting because of the equity issues it raises around intelligence levels. One a related note, I came across the following article in the Developmental Science journal talking about working memory, its role in intelligence and strategies to improve it. Could be a useful direction to consider what tools or games might challenge WM and thereby impact intelligence. I couldn't get it from Eric, but you could probably download it off of CU libraries if you have access. Joni Holmes, S. E. G. D. L. D. (2009). Adaptive training leads to sustained enhancement of poor working memory in children. Developmental Science, 9999(9999). Perhaps you could build in adaptive WM exercises into other learning tools so as to exercise WM (to support problem solving skills) while also building declarative knowledge and mental models of the subject matter.
