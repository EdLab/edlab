NLT Analytics Updates

Sharon Hsiao

July 21, 2013

***Keep private please We submitted a NLT poster in April for Columbia Data Science symposium (poster is currently hung on the back of oval). I've done some analytics update since then and here are some of the findings: 1) We have grown our viewers from 350+ to ~1000. But, the article view per session only marginally increased for desktop users (0.48->0.70). On the other hand, EdLabbers doubled the views per session. This could be intern-effect. We manage to read at least 1 article per session now. But not the non-edlabbers. Non-edlabbers' behavior are not changing much over the past three months. But, us Edlabbers we clicked more (why? we already know where to find what we want, why in a sudden, we start clicking?). Again, this can almost prove that, the increase of article views are the intern-effect. (They are relatively new users come to the site and actively access NLT). 2) Break down the article views by device and user categories (EdLab vs. Non-EdLab). Unfortunately, most of the article views are attributed by us, dedicated EdLabbers' views which I personally think is not a bad thing. (We are just at the first step of step 1, if we can't make an impact on our own staff, how do we make an impact on the rest of the world?) However, an interesting thing that might deserve some attention is, look at the red arrow in Mobile article view figure (top). Non-edlabbers have a spike reading point around 1pm while, most of us are probably having lunch, and not reading NLT. Perhaps some marketing strategies, or publication timings, can target that period in the day. 3) In the following analysis, it shows the aggregated article view % by every hour and device of all users. No surprises for evening commute hours and before bed time, mobile device achieves relatively higher article view percentage. Compared to the same analysis of what we did in April, no special behavior patterns were found. However, the one consistent thing is that, no particular article view by mobile in morning commute.
