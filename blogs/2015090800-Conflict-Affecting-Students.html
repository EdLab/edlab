Conflict Affecting Students

Youssouf Bamba

September 8, 2015

The images of refugees crossing international water to escape conflict in Syria and other countries are currently dominating global news. Most of the refugees are flocking to European countries such Greece and Germany. A large portion of the refugees are children of school age. According to a UN report, more then 13 million children from the Middle East and North Africa have been displaced and driven out of school due to conflict. The consequences of children missing out on school due to conflict could have long lasting effects on the region. As our world continues to rely more heavily on technical skills, it is extremely important for students to get a strong educational start. An entire generation is growing up without access to any form of education. This “lost generation” could impact the economic and political development of the region, especially as countries such as Qatar, are already preparing for a future where there is less dependency on oil for economic growth.
