Young Makers Sign Up To Be Hacker Scouts

chingfu

November 6, 2013


We're living in the age of cloud computing, where everything seems accessible through a single touch on a shiny screen. While the cloud brings convenience to our lives, some miss the excitement of being able to touch, grab, cut or manipulate tangible objects and create something that is working on the ground. The maker movement responds to such needs, and a variety of youth maker programs have sprung up around the country in recent years.

Hacker Scouts is one such program with an emphasis on kids learning technical skills and exploring creativity through the study of STEAMâ€”Science, Technology, Engineering, Art and Math. Beyond making, Hacker Scouts wants to take back hacking from its negative associations and defines it as "the improvement and modification of technology. Hacking is how we progress." From this standpoint of hacking and not merely making, it aims to hack young people's ideas of conservation and sustainability through making and repurposing materials, reshaping people's concepts of learning, and transforming the look of education.

In this video, you will see young kids working on a Hackerling Circuit, which is a series of projects that includes soldering a circuit board, loading a program, fabricating a case, and sewing a bag for the whole thing to fit into.
