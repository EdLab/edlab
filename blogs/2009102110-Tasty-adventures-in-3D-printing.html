Tasty adventures in 3D printing

Gus Andrews

October 21, 2009

Now that we have a super-cool die cutter, some of you may have been getting ideas about things that could be done with it, or its even-cooler big sisters, the 3D printers now hitting the market. As it turns out, the French Culinary Institute got one of these little lovelies, and they're asking for suggestions of how to make food with it! Any food that goes through it needs to be a nearly homogeneous emulsion -- so ground meat is too big. They've had some success with celery gel and bacon fat. Ideas?
