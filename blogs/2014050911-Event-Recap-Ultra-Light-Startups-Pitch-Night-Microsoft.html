Event Recap: Ultra Light Startups Pitch Night @ Microsoft

Kate Meersschaert

May 9, 2014

Tweets by @CuriousBrooklyn Last night I attended Ultra Light Startups pitch event at the NEW Microsoft headquarters off of Times Square (here is my original post). While the setting was new (and very beautifully designed) the pitch format was classic Ultra Light with a panel of venture capitalists offering feedback to presenting startups, followed-by the audience voting on the top three presenters and top 2 panelists. (see full list of presenters/panelists listed below) Overall, the event was a fascinating chance to step inside the mind of a VC and the classic, startup pitch process. Some presenters failed to meet the high expectations of the panel, while others, like the two winners, Shareswell and LessonFace. Takeaways for EdLab future events: -Possible integration of audience voting at future events -Panel format (each panelist needs a mic - passing one mic was awkward) -Value in printed event program listing all presenters/panelists (needed @'s) -Sponsors who offer prizes to winners Listed below are the panelists and presenters: Investor Panelists Murat Aktihanoglu - Founder at ER Accelerator Somak Chattopadhyay - Managing Partner at Armory Square Ventures Brian Cohen - Chairman at New York Angels Pankaj Jain - Venture Partner at 500 Startups Startup Pitches AdhereTech - Josh Stein RentHackr - Zeb Dropkin Shareswell - Emily Washkowitz Now In Store - Charles Brun Unlockable - Zach Goodman Lessonface - Claire Cunningham Lifedots - Rafael de Haro Weekdone - Juri Kalundi
