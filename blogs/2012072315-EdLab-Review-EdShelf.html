EdLab Review: EdShelf

Kate Meersschaert

July 23, 2012

EdShelf is a directory of web-based tools and applications created to help teachers "decrease the friction of using and procuring effective technology into the classroom." By providing a central location to rate, discover, share, and save new learning technologies, the founders are well on the way to their goal. From Evernote to Audacity, the site features a wide range of applications for teaching and learning. Logged in users are even able to store favorites for later, check out what their colleagues are using, and leave detailed product reviews and ratings. Pros: Design. The site is beautifully designed to highlight a host of useful features (ratings, categories and embedded videos etc.) and is incredibly intuitive to navigate. Embedded video tutorials is a great additional functionality (where applicable) for each product page. Finally, being able to search by grade and platform is a huge boon to users. Cons: Categories. I felt there were almost too many choices in the initial "category" dropdown menu when you search for tools. Surely there must be a way to condense these into smaller groupings? EdLab Relevance: As we work to develop our own database of adaptive-learning-related resources, it is important hit home the value of simplicity and effective visual design. Really letting the work of each application's design team ring true through effective logo display is key here. Just listing edtech is not enough; you need engaging, effective displays of this information that highlights both features and functionality in a standardized way. Our Take-away: Great reliable resource for finding and rating educational technology. Note: Please check out my Startup Spotlight on EdShelf from earlier this spring.
