Advice from Mayor Bloomberg

Christopher Zamora

May 29, 2013

Mayor Bloomberg recently gave some advice on a radio show when he stated that students who are not doing so well in high school don't have to attend college. They can seek another career like becoming a plumber. Becoming a plumber is a better economic bet for many young students than obtaining an undergraduate degree. According to the mayor, if you compare a plumber to going to Harvard College, "being a plumber, actually for average person, probably would be a better deal." The mayor's advice can change the course for many students, if they happen to take advice from someone as rich as him. The mayor also states that students who pursue a career in plumbing or a related field will be saving a lot of money, instead of investing on continuing to study and going to college. What are your thoughts on Mayor Bloomberg's advice? Shouldn't he be supporting young students to continue their education by going to college?
