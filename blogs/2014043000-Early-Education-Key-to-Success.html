Early Education Key to Success

rendy

April 30, 2014

During a recent meeting at Ohio State University and Central Ohio Technical College, different issues came up and one of them was improving the students' knowledge in STEM. Despite the Department of Education noting that 16 percent of high school students are interested in STEM fields, it has been indicated that there will be a 14 percent increase in STEM jobs between 2010-2020. The focus of the talk centered on generating student interest in STEM at an early age. Some panelists noted that it might take time to see improvement in STEM among students.

I agree with the sentiment that it takes a generation to change. We can start a new program to help the economy or education but won't see progress until the years go by. A prime example is the Common Core standards. It may take years before we see the full effects of the standards' impact on students and the nation as a whole.
