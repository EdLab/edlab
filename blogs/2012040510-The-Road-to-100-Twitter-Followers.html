The Road to 100... Twitter Followers

Kate Meersschaert

April 5, 2012

I know 100 isn't necessarily "Kloutworthy," but growing the @TCRecord follower base from 50-odd to 100+ in just under 2 months feels like a minor accomplishment. The following are my thoughts on Twitter "best-practices" and it would be great if you could add your own Twitter tips and wisdom in the comments below. Twitter Tips for Institutional and App-based Accounts: Editorial... -Try to never repeat content! -Work-out a framework and rotation for content before starting to Tweet - for example... feature only original content from, about or by your institution and peers. -Test new ideas... the framework listed above is important... but don't be afraid to try new strategies! Using Google Analytics... -Remember that people are constantly checking their Twitter feeds... but most likely this corresponds to meal-times: morning, afternoon and evening. -Also, best not to only Tweet at one time of day. Check-out your site's analytics diplayed by hour to see when spikes in site visits occur. -Try tweeting based on most popular search terms on your listed in your analytics account. Opinion... -Try to stay-away from opinion if you are Tweeting for an institution or app, try to deliver unbiased tasty facts linked to content. For example: “Read the Review:" instead of, “Read the Amazing Review:...” Re-tweets, "follows" and @'s... -Only re-tweet people and institutions you want as followers and be sure what you are re-tweeting is furthering your mission and adding to your work. -Also, only follow accounts you would want as followers and do so selectively... that being said, nothing re-starts a stalled Twitter following like strategically following a few like-minded accounts who might be interested in your content. -To be efficient only Tweet @handles with over 1K followers. -When you feature a company's site, product or even a publisher's book review tweet it @ the group most invested in its success... the co. or publisher. That way, they can re-tweet for you. ###'s... -Search for ALL #hashtags on Twitter to “vet” before using... could have a majorly negative association you are unaware of! -Finally, be prepared that your hashtag could get “hijacked” as McDonald's learned the hard way... I am excited to share thoughts and additions to this "best-practice" list!
