Combining Interdisciplinary Approaches and Video Game Technology for Physical Therapy Innovation

Ryan.Allen

February 11, 2018


The systems used in physical therapy and for studies related to human body movement are incredibly expensive, costing over $100,000. Researchers from the University of Missouri have teamed up to create a cheaper system that costs just around $150. By utilizing common video game components, the Xbox Kinect, this new system is portable and has similar capabilities of much more expensive traditional technology.

The design and implementation of this cheaper alternative system brought together an impressive interdisciplinary group from across the university. Physical therapists, athletic trainers, surgeons, engineers, and students in related fields have been crucial in developing and utilizing this new innovation. Trainers and researchers have already begun to to take advantage of the system to pinpoint problems with joints, discover possible abnormalities or injuries, conduct rehabilitation sessions, and to spot potential risks.

Because it’s built on readily available video game technology, this innovation can be reasonably scaled to provide wider access across the sector. What are some other areas where video game technology has been integrated into educational innovations? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
