Games For Change Festival

Youssef Ballo

May 13, 2014

Most teachers have historically relied on textbooks to cover their curriculum. But times may be changing. According to an educator, “being a teacher you have to be unconventional.” Video games are being increasingly used in classrooms because it's a much more dynamic environment than reading a textbook, listening to a lecture or self-study. Some educators note that one way to enable students interest in educational games is by bringing them to theGames For Change Festival (GCF). The festival, a three-day conference, showcases video games that have a social impact. GCF was founded in 2004 and it's New York City's largest gaming event. The festival has different activities such as playing amazing digital games, and also play live- action games commissioned specifically for particular events. All these activities are great experiences for festival gamers.
