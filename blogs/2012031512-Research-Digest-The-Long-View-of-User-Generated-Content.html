Research Digest: The Long View of User-Generated Content

LauraCostello

March 15, 2012

Lampert, C. & Chun, S. K. (2012). Strategic planning for sustaining user-generated content in digital collections. The Journal of Library Innovation, 2(2), 74-93. A few short years ago, it wasn't uncommon to witness naked librarian fear at the prospect of user-generated content. Through a period of growth, it's become clear that UGC doesn't wreck libraries or destroy the stately reputation of well-sourced information, but the mere integration of this uncontrollable content is not the end of the story. As we plan for the future role of UGC in library resources, we'll have to consider how we should curate these resources and what their place should be in light of our more traditional collections. The article recommends adapting tech project management techniques across traditional and UGC collections as well as bringing classic collection management ideas to new types of acquisitions. How do we give users a voice in our holdings while providing the most appropriate, informative content? The essential question of collection management should begin to be augmented by ideas about the best platforms and ways to utilize existing and new systems toward our content and service delivery goals. The role of a collection manager and that of a moderator are not so different. EdLab Relevance: It's a long held belief, even mentioned in our seminar yesterday, that user-generated content is inherently problematic. The barrier between the type of materials libraries now acquire and the wilds of the Internet is quickly dissolving, no longer is the librarian king of a secure fortress of information. Database grooming and moderation are going to become more important roles for librarians as our resources migrate towards UGC and open-source. In this quickly growing area, bibliographic description fails us. The time is nigh for a system of description and an ILS that demonstrates the flexibility and havestability of modern metadata.
