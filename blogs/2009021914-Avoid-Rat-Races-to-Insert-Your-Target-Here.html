Avoid Rat Races to (Insert Your Target Here)

Arianna Choi

February 19, 2009

Today, EdLab's Development and Research Team brainstormed what features might be useful for users if we launched a mobile version of the Gottesman Libraries website. Lots of ideas came up including one that tells how many and which desks are available. Take the case of Anna (an imaginary character, ah hem): she would like to check whether there is an empty desk at the library before she heads out of her room, just like she checks Laundry View to check whether there is an empty washer/dryer before she heads out with a bag full of dirty clothes. Ting liked the Laundry View site so I thought I'd share with more people. Besides checking available machines real-time, there have been other cases of applications such as ‘Smart Parking' which guides drivers to open spaces. You might have seen it at airport parking lots, but to take one step further San Francisco began a trial last year installing wireless parking sensors to the city's metered spaces to alert drivers via street signs or cellphones when a spot becomes available. Putting feasibility or marketability aside, I'd like to see Disney World take on this idea and allow visitors the real-time access to information on how long the wait is for each ride — so that we can design our own route as we roam around the gigantic theme parks. The world's largest recreational resort already lets you know how long the wait is before you get in line. Why not take it up a notch and let us maximize our time and excitement.
