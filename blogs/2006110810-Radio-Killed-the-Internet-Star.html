Radio Killed the Internet Star

Dan Mallinger

November 8, 2006

Reuters reports that Google is positioning itself to start their adSense game on the radio as well, showing that helping people and companies link together is profitable on and off the web. What's far more interesting is that everyone else seems to have forgotten about radio. But when vacuum tubes lost their popular market, companies willing to ride out the decline stage of the cycle were able to happily charge exorbitant prices for the same old good. Is Google positioning itself in a similar boat? Or are they betting that radio will always exist in one form or the other and that pre-existing companies will be far more apt to move into the next evolution of radio?
