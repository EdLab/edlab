Monmouthpedia - A one town version of Wikipedia

Josh Lopez

January 5, 2012

Techdirt has an interesting article about Monmouthpedia, a micro-wiki devoted entirely to the town of Monmouth in Wales. The idea is to allow the residents of Monmouth to create wiki pages for anything relevant to their hometown without having to justify the significance of each article to the rest of the world. The project will also include QR codes posted around the city, kind of like a virtual guided tour. I can see the potential for some issues here. Suppose a historical landmark posts a QR code to the Monmouthpedia page for the location, but the article contains incorrect information. This reminds me of some of the points Mark Moran from Finding Dulcinea brought up in yesterday's seminar. Specifically the point about author credibility. If one of the single biggest problems with online resources is determining the reliability of the source, what happens when the subject becomes the source (assuming the majority of Monmouthpedia users will either be residents or visitors to the town)? I think in some sense this could lead to more well-informed entries in the wiki, but it also provides more opportunity to promote personal agendas and makes the consequences of online vandalism far more severe. I also wonder what this would look like in other locations, NewYorkCityPedia, FukushimaPedia, NorthKoreaPedia (wouldn't that be something).
