Economics & Education: Regulating Distance Education

Skanda Amarnath

April 21, 2011

Inside Higher Ed has an interesting piece on the regulation of distance learning for higher education. While regulation should be forward-looking and the for-profit school fiasco has probably increased concerns over the supposed quality of distance learning courses, I am skeptical whether this necessary until I see more evidence that regulation can address gaping holes in the existing system. A commenter on MR put it quite nicely: "In this case, the government is preemptively legislating against what may or may not become a problematic industry in the future. In doing so, they are reducing the future consumer's choice set, and raising prices for a class of goods that was supposed to induce cost-savings. Within the (diminished) number of online education providers that make it to market, their opportunities to try new things to meet the mysterious consumer demand is truncated. There are many innovations that will never happen because they won't conform to regulations. But since the regulations are being made ex nihilo, they aren't solving any sort of existing problem!" - ck
