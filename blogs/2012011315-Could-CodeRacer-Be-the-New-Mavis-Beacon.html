Could CodeRacer Be the New Mavis Beacon?

Kate Meersschaert

January 13, 2012

Treehouse, who Janet recently profiled, hopes, through their new live-action Facebook hosted game, CodeRacer to teach budding coders the basics necessary to build websites using CSS and HTML. Pairing classic racing game elements with video tutorials, badges, surprise code "bombs" you can throw at other racers, and the chance for advanced practitioners to hone their speed and accuracy, the Treehouse team wants to engage both beginners and experienced programmers in this new multi-player, competitive gaming experience. It is interesting to draw a parallel between CodeRacer and one of the original game-based tutorial programs introduced in the late 1980's, Mavis Beacon Teaches Typing. This early software-based tutorial allowed you to practice and gain new typing skills by controlling a car and winning challenges based on your speed & accuracy. What sets Beacon apart from CodeRacer, beyond advanced design etc., is that teachers were often able to use this program as either a "reward" if you finished your work early, or as an integral part of a typing class. CodeRacer, on the other hand, can only be used as a tutorial at home, owing to the fact that many schools ban Facebook access on school grounds. EdLab Takeaway & Relevance... What would a fun, effective in-school game-based tutorial to help teach coding look and behave like? Are there any models currently in use? I know that this is the Year of Code thanks to Codecademny... Lets take this idea beyond social media platforms and help teachers to use these concepts in more engaging ways in-class. On the flip-side, these out-of school tutorials are great way to to help educators to "flip" a CS classroom... Learn more about CodeRacer in this FoundersSpace article.
