Learn In Any Language

Meredith Powers

June 2, 2015


Early next year, users of the free language-learning app Duolingo will be able to learn Klingon, a fictional alien language from Star Trek, alongside more typical offerings like Swedish, Spanish, or French. If this seems odd, it’s worth noting that one of Duolingo’s goals is to offer courses for every language—fictional and dying languages included. Still, the decision to roll out Klingon before other languages (like Chinese) is a bit harder to understand. While developing courses for written languages not based on the Roman alphabet (again, like Chinese) is admittedly difficult, one of the ways language courses are developed is through a bilingual language incubator, which allows people fluent in two languages to collaborate on creating coursework. In this case, a few bilingual Klingon-English speakers have contributed enough to make Klingon for English speakers a reality, and others are eager to add languages from J.R.R. Tolkien and George R.R. Martin to the mix. But are there really more English speakers fluent in Klingon, Dothraki, and Elvish than in Chinese, Hungarian, or Ukrainian?
