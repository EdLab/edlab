Adaptive Exhibit on Future Technologies: Updates and A Call for Help!

Ting Yuan

August 19, 2010

*Please keep this post private* Our slides shown in the meeting:

Adaptive exhibit 8.19.ppt
Thanks to everyone (who are part of the exhibits group) for your helpful feedback. Luke and I have developed our current ideas for the tech exhibit: 1. The video as the first center piece in the exhibit will be showing how a child interacts with his/her robot pet and how he/she through the robot gets connected to different contexts of advanced technology use: such as SixthSense technologies, 3D avatar distance call, and the robot's data collection/synthesization/reporting of the child's out-of-school informal learning from birth; 2. The second center piece of the exhibit will be a transformable robot pet which can transform itself to a giant robot (as a protector/playmate/friend of the child)and adaptively any portable machine-based device. 3. Smaller video screens will be showing silent background videos which will add to the futuristic look of the space. What we need: 1. We need a child who can play a role in our video. Ideally, he/she can be 8-10 years old. Please let me or Luke know if you might know a potential candidate. 2. We need help to build or borrow a (giant) "robot". Anyone who might have connections at Columbia (e.g., the Columbia's Robotics Lab, Computer Science Department), please contact us, the sooner the better.
