Trends in Ed: News from the Future

Fred Rossoff

May 6, 2011

Minding the Campus, the higher education news and discussion website based out of the Manhattan Institute, has up this week a very witty essay on the future of education. The author, Jane Shaw, has written a short summary of the history of the past ten years in higher education from the perspective of a journalist in the year 2020. The general trend seems to be the increasing irrelevance of traditional higher education institutions. With online classes, free-lance professors, and the online “Bill Gates University,” the cost and trouble of a traditional education does not seem to pay off. The story ends with Harvard and Yale, desperate from declining enrollment, merging to become “Ha-Ya.” But how did we get to that point? It seems that the rising cost of traditional college education, as compared to the cheap online credit alternatives, cause high school students to abandon traditional universities in droves. A general free market in this online education “disaggregates” the traditional bundle of services, so that students begin to take any class they want, from any professor they want, in a free market system. This optimism about the free market may be a result of the Manhattan Institute's conservative bias. From what we know of online or non-traditional education today, there seem to be plenty of scammers and bad deals already operating in that world.
