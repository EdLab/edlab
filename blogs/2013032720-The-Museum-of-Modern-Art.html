The Museum of Modern Art

EdLab Studios

March 27, 2013


The Museum of Modern Art's "Art Labs" are interactive spaces that allow children and adults alike to explore how modern and contemporary artists have used and imagined such basics as shape, color, lines, or the human form. Billed as a "gateway to the collection," each MoMA Art Lab includes opportunities for hands-on experimentation, play, and art-making. The current Art Lab is called "People," and focuses on artistic representation of the human form; visitors have the chance to draw on walls, paint digitally, put on shadow-puppet shows, and more. In the Art Lab, participants can try or challenge the ideas of great artists, go beyond to learn for themselves about visual experience and representation, and enjoy art in an organic, self-directed way.
