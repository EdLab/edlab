Crazy Gears

Ting Yuan

June 1, 2015


Crazy Gears is an iOS app developed by Seven Academy for children ages five and above. The game uses machine components such as chains, rods, and pulleys to teach young players the basic physics of motion, gravity, and tension. The app provides over 60 gear puzzles at different levels, and automatically saves the in-app play at any level so players can enter and exit the app without losing their work.

Pros:

The game features a child-friendly design, with bright colors and succinct touch-screen interaction options. The game promotes logical thinking through the process of constructing a working machine. There are no rules or direct instructions, children learn through active gameplay and experimentation as they progress through the different levels. There are no examples for how to connect two wheels, so players must tinker with the tools to discover how the movement of one wheel might make a second wheel move. The app does provide a handbook for parents and teachers, which explains the scientific vocabularies and concepts incorporated at each level.

Cons:

The game auto saves play instantly, but there are no options for individualized evaluation. Because of the free-flowing nature of the game, app data cannot clearly convey young players' concept strengths and weaknesses. Until evaluation and adaptation are built into the system, the app might work better as a low-stakes addition to early physics lesson or an at-home app with parental supervision.

Our Takeaway:

Crazy Gears offers a unique hands-on experience to children and a digital lab where users can explore and experiment with various machine components. It's a novel and fun way to introduce physics to children and encourage their interest in science. Teachers in both early childhood and elementary classrooms could use it for both teacher demonstration and student exploration.
