Gender and Bullying: Five Studies From 2017

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2017


This year bullying and stereotyping were repeatedly held up in mainstream media as primary culprits driving women out of the tech industry and discouraging curiosity in girls. Researchers conducted reviews, trials, studies, and interviews in an effort to understand both the workings and nuance of these oppressive cultures that consistently stack up against women. Here are five studies you may have missed.

1. Six-year-old Girls Already Have Gendered Stereotypes
Despite the force of gender stereotypes, this study found that after just 20 minutes of a programming and robotics activity, girls showed more interest and confidence in technology.

2. Women of Color Fight Against Present Representations of Their Future Selves
This research examines the way historical narratives about women of color in STEM are taken up by high school students.

3. Player Rank is a Primary Motivator in Cyberbullying
In a surprise for researchers, females and LGBT gamers did not, as expected, report more overall cyberbullying than males or heterosexual players. They did, however, experience more cyber-victimization.

4. The Digital First Impression
Digital first impressions are found to form quickly with minimal cues and through gendered perceptions in this study.

5. The Fraught Field of Game Design
The value of women in the gaming industry has long been overlooked. This research identifies a culture in need of change.
