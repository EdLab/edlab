Online Course Ratings Ruffle Feathers

Kate Meersschaert

September 30, 2011

For the first time, an off-shoot of the famed, if controversial U.S. News “Best” rankings for educational institutions list will be compiled for online offerings. This ground-breaking ranking will solely focus on baccalaureate degree programs and master's degrees in subjects ranging from business, computer information technology, education, engineering and nursing. For-profits and nonprofits will both be considered as long as the programs offer at least 80% of coursework online. Compiling an online institutional ranking may seem innocent enough, but not if you are Capella University. The for-profit online institution has decided not to participate in the ranking citing inequities in the survey sent to all participants. From InsideHigherEd's post, "Deb Bushway, Capella's interim president, wrote an article for the Huffington Post in which she said the questions from U.S. News focus too much on how prepared students are when they enroll, instead of what they learn before graduating." This seems like a relevant concern and sounds similar to the inequities cited by experts regarding the fairness of community college placement exams. The ranking system is not finalized yet and the results should be available by the end of year after being delayed from an initially proposed October release.
