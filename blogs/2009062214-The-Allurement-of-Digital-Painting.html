The Allurement of Digital Painting

Ting Yuan

June 22, 2009

One more allurement of getting an Iphone comes from the Brushes, an amazing application I noticed over the weekend, which is speedy and easy to use. Check out the amazing works created through Brushes. An artist named Jorge Colombo also used this Iphone application to create the cover below for The New Yorker (June 1, 2009): I believe Brushes' finger painting will bring a revolution to the art world (i.e., art creation and art exhibition) as well as art education for both children and adults. University professors (including Teachers College professors) in the field of art education were strongly against digital painting which traditionally involved Windows paint tools and drawing software developed on the platform of computer screen. They argued that computerized painting couldn't provide the natural feeling of doing sketch and it gave too much convenience for learners to draw shapes and lines. Now these drawbacks have been removed by Brushes. High-resolution drawings/paintings are available. Painters can do zoom-in and zoom-out to work on the details. Users can paint or draw any time they want without setting up the tools and cleaning up afterward. I'm very excited about Brushes and want to try it as soon as I can!!
