Gottesman Libraries Traffic Tracker!

Stephen Asunka

October 1, 2007

How many people come into the library each day? Which sections of the library are mostly patronized? Do patrons visit the stacks anymore? Are the collaboration spaces really serving their purpose? Do we need a cafÃ© in the library? ... To obtain data that can help in answering questions of this nature, the knowledge center is proposing the implementation of a library walk-in traffic analysis and reporting technology. How will the technology work? Currently, the physical estate of the library can be divided into the following sections: 1. Main Entrance, 2. Everett CafÃ©, 3. 1st floor - Main, 4. 2nd floor - Main (including the digital depot), 5. 2nd floor - Stacks 6. 3rd floor - Main 7. 3rd floor - Stacks 8. 4th floor - Stacks 9. 5th floor - Stacks a) We will install wireless digital counters (available at http://www.walkerwirelessco.com/) on the front doorways leading to each of these sections. These detect and count people (unobtrusively) as they walk through the doorways, and can automatically transmit data to a receiver. A booster will be added to enable the signal pass through floors. b) A wireless receiver/server will be placed in a strategic location of the library and programmed to collect data from the individual counters c) A PC hooked to the receiver will have the requisite software installed that will automate the collection and reporting of traffic data from each of the wireless counters. d) Traffic data will be reported daily, weekly, monthly, and also by semester basis a) Data will be charted to show i. The relative patronage patterns and trends of the various sections of the library ii. The rate of activity vs. time of day for each section iii. The rate of activity vs. day of week for each section We are seeking people's views and opinions on how best this technology can be implemented, or even if it is worth the time and effort in the first place.
