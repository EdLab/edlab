Supporting Self-Directed Learning With the Thinkering Studio

Toland.Lawrence

April 23, 2019


Birmingham Covington School in Bloomfield Hills, Michigan is committed to supporting student inquiry and research. To encourage this, the school offers an elective called the Thinkering Studio, which allows students to select their own learning project and manage themselves throughout the process. Ideas for projects range from learning to play the ukulele to creating a Youtube channel for DIY videos.

The teacher doesn’t tell students what to do but acts as a facilitator by offering resources and asking questions to help students clarify their thinking. Students don’t have to go to the teacher for help and are encouraged to reach out to other students for support in their projects. There are also peer critique groups each week where students can provide feedback on each other’s work. Through this process, the program designers hope to set students up for lifelong self-directed learning.

What are the advantages of this self-directed approach to learning? What would you have done if you’d had the opportunity to pursue your own project in middle school? What skills are students learning through this process? How might it impact their approach to work and learning in the future? Add your thoughts to the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
