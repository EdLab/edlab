Augmented Reality in the Library

Kaitlin.Kehnemuyi

July 7, 2017

University of Texas at San Antonio has started collecting children's books that utilize augmented reality (think of modern day pop-up books that can read aloud). This collection will allow students at UT to experiment and play with this new technology to understand how it can benefit their future students when they start teaching. It is a small collection but they are hoping to provide feedback to publishers on how to grow this genre. 


It also highlights how the library can be used as a place to experiment and grow your tech skills in lower stakes environment. An anthropologist found that a reason professors don't adopt new teaching methods is a fear of looking stupid in front of students. Which I think is something we all fear with technology. The library is a place to experiment and fail and grow with other people who are also learning.
