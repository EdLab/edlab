Using Design Thinking To Re-imagine A School

Carmen James

April 29, 2014


Design firm IDEO has joined forces with Riverdale Country School in New York City to create a Design Thinking Toolkit for Educators. The over 80-page document has since been translated into countless languages and downloaded thousands of times across the globe. Building off the success of the toolkit and addressing the need for educators to get their hands dirty prototyping ideas, IDEO and Riverdale partnered to organize a design thinking workshop for faculty, students, and administrators.

"We really need to figure out processes to tackle hard problems in school," says Dominic Randolph, head of Riverdale Country School. "If we realize [things] are designed, we realize they can be redesigned," shares Sandy Speicher, Director of Learning at IDEO. She continues, "How do we activate teachers to be part of that process?" The video is filled with interviews and ideas so if you are thinking about holding a design thinking workshop for teachers or just want to learn more, this is an excellent place to start.

When there are no limits, when you have permission to fail and be creative, what would you design for the future of education? Share your experiences in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
