Flaws of College Admissions

Abdulrahman Sanda

January 24, 2016

How can we collaborate to fix the broken system of college admissions? The admission of students to selective American colleges has gradually become an issue of great importance. Applying to colleges can be a very tedious if not given the proper guide. Colleges tend to mislead students about their chances of acceptance. Students should be given a more realistic sense of their acceptance chances. This NY Times op-ed sheds more light on the broken admissions system and why the time for reform is now. It asks very interesting questions such as how can we make the admission process candid to every student? This would not only alter the way colleges admit students but tackle the way society perceive children who apply to prestigious schools. What are your thoughts on this? Is the admissions system really broken?
