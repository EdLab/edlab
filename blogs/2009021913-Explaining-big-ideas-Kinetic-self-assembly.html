Explaining big ideas: Kinetic self-assembly

Stephanie Hunt

February 19, 2009

I once had to figure out how to present physical self-assembly - research done at MIT by Saul Griffith - in an innovation-themed exhibit. Gadzooks, I still remember how daunting the subject matter seemed, and I never did feel satisfied with the display solution I came up with. Saul and his colleagues at Howtoons did create the cool breakfast cereal-themed explanation of self assembly that I've linked to below... It's a great start, but I still wanted more... Well, fast forward a few years. I just ran across this awesome idea for a self assembly toy, created by Eric Rosenbaum of the MIT Media Lab's Lifelong Kindergarten Group. Sounds like Eric was inspired by Saul's work too, and he whipped this little demo up on the laser cutter to test out. Check it out!
