It's a Financial Issue

Bismark Appiah

February 23, 2014

Here is another Vialogues on my continuing topic about global education. This video describes improvement in Tanzania's education system. Tanzania is increasingly focusing resources on improving its secondary schools. In that case, I assume that Tanzania's primary schools are doing quite well. One great aspect about this project is that girls are now eligible to attend secondary schools in Tanzania. Even though families are happy that their children are receiving an education, they are also worried about enrollment fees. The cost of attending school is $40 per child, but most families live on $2 per day. As a result of financial constraints, parents often struggle to keep their children in school. Schools continue to be established, but a major problem is how students can remain or attend if they can't afford it. This is a major issue for the Tanzanian government.
