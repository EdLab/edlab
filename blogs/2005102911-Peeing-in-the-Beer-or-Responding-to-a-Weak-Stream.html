Peeing in the Beer or Responding to a Weak Stream?

gnatriello

October 29, 2005

In Web 2.0 Cracks Start to Show Xeni Jardin comments on the emerging problems with Web 2.0 approaches and observes that "When you invite the whole world to your party, inevitably someone pees in the beer." The piece goes on to highlight two quite different problems associated with the openness of 2.0 strategies. One problem is deliberately destructive behavior such as splogs; a second problem is the mediocre (or worse) performance of those who seek to make honest contributions. This second problem is highlighted in Nick Carr's recent entry on The Amorality of Web 2.0 in which he critiques Wikipedia. It seems important to distinguish between deliberate negative behavior and honest, but poor, performance, though some seem to heap equal criticism on both. For those of us concerned with using 2.0 approaches to achieve educational goals the implications are that we need to defend applications against intensionally destructive behavior and that we need to design applications that elevate performance for all those willing to engage in sincere participation. These are both substantial challenges.
