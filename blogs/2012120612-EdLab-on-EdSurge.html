EdLab on EdSurge!

Kate Meersschaert

December 6, 2012

EdLab featured on the main page of EdSurge.com! I am thrilled to announce that Elizabeth Woyke, an EdSurge, FastCo., Inc. and Time reporter, here">has written a piece on EdLab, Demo night and our unique vision for helping to support educational entrepreneurship and the Teachers College community. Please feel-free to share your thoughts, tweet, re-post, Like, comment and share this piece! She answers the "Why?!" One of my favorite quotes re: WHY we held demo night and the event's potential to uniquely support the NY Edtech community:

New York City is home to thousands of teachers, dozens of edtech start-ups and a handful of graduate education programs. Yet, in spite of their common interests, the groups don't always communicate. Now one organization, based at Columbia University's Teachers College, is kick-starting a dialogue.
Well said Elizabeth! I am so glad that the event was well-received and viewed as a potential resource to foster dialogue and development. Here is her take on who attended:
EdLab's new role was clear Tuesday night as the organization hosted its first start-up Demo Night. About 100 people trekked uptown to Columbia's campus to hear three local edtech start-ups (Amphetomobile, BiblioNasium and Late Nite Labs) discuss their products and answer questions. The mix of faculty, students, teachers and entrepreneurs represented a more diverse crowd than the business types who typically attend New York edtech Meetups and networking events.
Here's to living up to this great press with more events like this in the near future! Again, a special thank you to the fantastic startups who presented and to the EdLab team for making this event possible!
