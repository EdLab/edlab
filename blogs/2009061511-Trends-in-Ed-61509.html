Trends in Ed 6.15.09

Calli Kosch

June 15, 2009

Student Performance Art: Chalking to the High Line Yesterday my sister and I went to check out the new High Line Park, which she informed me was a "big deal" to New Yorkers. That was made clear to me once we placed ourselves at the end of a line of about 50 visitors awaiting entry in the Meatpacking district. The High Line really does, in my opinion, live up to the interest surrounding its development. Our visit became my inspiration for a project that the Critter crew is working on, where we will set up kiosks in the library to receive feedback on the tool from TC frequenters. High Line Chalk Shoes Art Project Faisal and I have been on the lookout for videos to have our survey participants discuss on Critter. I found this one: The video details a performance art project with the help of Julia Mandle's chalk shoe idea, which she hopes will cause onlookers to "pause" and think about the innovative spaces in the city. Students walked to the High Line in chalk shoes and in doing so created a chalked-out path to the park. Other cities have utilized Mandle's art as well, with the common goal of youth making a statement about urban living--to celebrate the meaning and beauty of city spaces.
