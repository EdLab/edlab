Higher visual memory recall in more violent games

Jessica Pang

April 7, 2008

Now that the AERA conference + our podcast experiment is over, work is back to normal. I have been looking at a few literature on gaming and cognitive development. Actually, my assignment is to look at new stuff in the gaming industry, and how it can enhance education, but I cant help myself and gear towards the cognitive psych side of things. A 2007 research has suggested that violent games are linked with higher visual memory recall than non-violent games. See link. At the same time, I have just found out that a new Math educational game named Evolver has been created for middle schoolers. This is a full-scale (meaning not those mini online games), RPG shooting game. I have just completed the tutorial part of the game before the AERA conference. There's about 50 keys combo you need to learn before you can go out and shoot prime number monsters and such. Personally I think if I have the cognitive ability to master that 50+ keys combo and understand all those instructions + command, I would not need the game to succeed in Middle school level math. But on the other hand, maybe I should resume playing high-tech games more, they do enhance your eye-motor coordination right?! See link.
