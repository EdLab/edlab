Consumer Wages

abagigah92

February 19, 2015

New York State has taken measures to maintain sustainable lifestyle for the citizens by increasing the minimum wage to $8.75/hr. In classical economics, increasing minimum wage always leads to an increase in unemployment. This is because if employers are paying higher wages for labor, they will be less willing to hire workers. This is mainly caused by budget constraint, which limits how much employers can spend on wages. The constraints on wages limit the amount of people they can employ. However, some economists believe that a strong minimum wage will benefit everyone in the economy. A strong minimum wage should adjust to inflation and increase in living standards. The increase in wages supports working families that rely on low-income jobs to make ends meet. It also gives consumers spending power to buy goods and services that will boost the economy. Raising the minimum wage is a key strategy to improve the Gross Domestic Product (GDP). Consumer spending makes up about 70% of the nation's GDP. The more consumers spend, the happier producers are because they will make more profit in the long run. Since the minimum wage is not being lifted that high, there will be no unemployment issues because of minimum wage. People in the lower income bracket are going to spend the money back into the economy unlike high earners, who tend to save. This will help create new jobs and lift millions of Americans out of poverty.
