Generating Electricity from Fire

saraehardman

January 19, 2017


Benjamin Franklin got it from lightening, Thomas Edison wielded it with the light bulb, but this group is harnessing electricity from fire. Students from the University of Washington have created a simple device that creates electricity from open flames. Called Spark, the device can generate enough electricity to charge a cell phone. The device is easy to use: people simply place it above a fire, then plug in their phone to the attached cord.

The creators of this student startup, called JikoPower, think these devices will have the most impact in developing countries, where electricity might be rare but open flames for cooking are not. With over 600 million cellphone users in developing countries, Spark could make a huge difference in the world. The students also believe that Spark could be useful while camping or during emergency situations and power outages.
