Protecting Your Online Info

Oumar Soumahoro

December 22, 2014

With the Internet being a big part of our lives today, we are pushed to create personal login information in order to keep out the public's eyes from the different activities we involve ourselves in. These login information include a password consider to be the key to the lock of our personal information. People became more vulnerable to cyber crimes over the past years, as very efficient software have been developed by hackers to get a hold of Internet users' personal and private information. Hacking became easy due to the fact that internet users make their series of passwords easy to remember, since they have to keep track of over a dozen sometimes. However, the easiest it is for one to remember his/her password, the easier it is for hackers to crack. Having been a victim of cyber hacks, I learned to make my passwords as hard as possible for myself to remember, and make impossible for someone else to guess it. To avoid being victims of internet crimes, the article suggest that users should make their password very random and hard to remember because it will reflect on how safe an account is from hackers' sight. To keep of your different password, it will be safer to keep them in an area where no one has access. Moreover, one can solidify their privacy with Dashlane, which proved itself to be an easy way to synchronize your different passwords with your online accounts.
