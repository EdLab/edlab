Your chance to see who stole your laptop!

Adam Mitchinson

October 8, 2008

I was reading yesterday the University of Washington have just released a free, open-source laptop protection tool that helps track your laptop if it is stolen! Great! Called Adoena after the Roman goddess of safe returns, the program allows the machine to sporadically send its IP address and other useful information to a free online storage database. Even better, if you have a Mac, the program will use the internal camera to send a picture of the thief to the same database! The program is being billed as a deterrent and is expected to reduce crime as thieves are unsure of which laptop is protected and which is not. The rationale is much like a similar product designed for cars, but I'm not so sure it applies as well to laptops. Whilst it may be a slight deterrent, are we underestimating criminals computer knowledge? Surely there is a simple way to remove the program before accessing the internet? And even if we are able to track the laptop, what are the chances it will actually be recovered? Either way an interesting idea and another example of free, open-source software being put to a good social use.
