Hewlett Foundation offers Prize for Software that Automates Grading of Student Essays

Kate Meersschaert

January 11, 2012

The William and Flora Hewlett Foundation has announced a 100K prize to developers who create software that can, "reliably automate the grading of essays for state tests." The competition will occur in two phases, the first of which involves existent assessment companies demonstrating competance in the field, and the second open to individuals to create software that demonstrates an ability to grade student essays as well as a human. The goal of this contest--which will be hosted on Kaggle--is to help free-up time and resources to encourage institutions and educators to include more essays and not rely solely on multiple-choice-based assessment in standardized testing. Currently the time & money needed to pay humans to hand-grade these additional sections is prohibitive to wider adoption & implementation. Barbara Chow, Education Program Director at the Hewlett Foundation, explains: "Rapid and accurate automated essay scoring will encourage states to include more writing in their state assessments.
