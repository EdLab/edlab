This Robot Can Read Your Mind

saraehardman

April 13, 2017


Forget the button pushing, switch flipping, and mouse clicking; researchers at MIT have created a robot that is controlled directly by the human brain. Baxter has one job: to correctly sort spray paint cans and spools of wire into separate bins. And he has only one method for completing the task: reading a humanâ€™s brain impulses.

The human controller is hooked up to EEG equipment, which is used to record error-related signals that pop up in the brain. When Baxter holds an object over an incorrect bin, he is able to read these signals, understand the mistake, and change which bin he puts the object in. Although this is a relatively simple task for a robot to complete given current advances in robotics, the fact that Baxter relies entirely on human brain activity to make the decision is a huge advancement in robotic control.
