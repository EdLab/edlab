Trends in Ed: White House Could Now Help Students With Debt

Demetri Lales

October 26, 2011

According to this article from CNN, the White House will offer two measures to alleviate students with large loan debts. The first offer would push the start date for a special repayment program forward. Starting in 2014, graduate students enrolled will be charged 10% of the monthly discretionary income to pay off loans, compared to the 15% being charged now. Also effective in 2014, debt forgiveness will begin after 20 years, compared to the 25 years it is now. This sounds like it would be a good start to helping those with large student debt. The second offer is the student loan consolidation, which is where the government will combined a graduate's loans into a single direct loan, and give them a small interest rate break of 0.5%. Its good to see the President address this issue especially now since this is one of the reasons we have the Occupy Wall Street demonstrations all over the country, and the fact that there's the growing trend of college tuition skyrocketing every year.
