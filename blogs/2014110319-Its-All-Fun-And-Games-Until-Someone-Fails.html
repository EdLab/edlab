It’s All Fun And Games Until Someone Fails

Brian.Sweeting

November 3, 2014


There are a lot of positive things to be said about using video games as a form of assessment: they’re thorough and adaptable, able to evaluate different skills, and most students actually enjoy them. But before teachers replace pop quizzes with Super Mario, there are still some significant questions to be asked. Like are they shortening kids’ attention spans? Are they capable of delivering the type of feedback necessary for growth? And most importantly, will they adequately prepare students for real life disappointments and failures?
