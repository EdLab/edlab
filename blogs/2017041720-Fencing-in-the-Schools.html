Fencing in the Schools

EdLab Studios

April 17, 2017


As a silver medalist in the 2008 Beijing Olympics, Tim Morehouse has learned a few life lessons along the way. "If being a champion was easy, everyone would do it," he states. "And you have to understand that being a champion involves setbacks and challenges and difficulties." These lessons are just some of the foundational beliefs that he teaches at Fencing in the Schools, which runs as an after-school program in Harlem near where he was raised. The benefits of bringing fencing to communities that typically are not exposed to this sport are reflected in the attitudes of the students. In addition to an aerobic workout, Tim believes fencing heightens perseverance, patience, accountability and focus. It's easy to see how the skills practiced and acquired in striving to become a fencing champion can transfer over into any career path that Fencing in the Schools students may choose to pursue.
