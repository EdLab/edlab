Catch Happier Employees With Honey

csd2126

June 15, 2017


Internal company websites have long been places to house outdated HR documents, expired alerts, or never-ending contact lists. Honey is a tool that simplifies internal communications for companies to keep employees up to date on the latest company communications.

Learning on the Job

Honey provides a corporate communication tool that groups related resources, project files, and messages. Rather than digging through extensive lists of links, employees join groups that are relevant to them to access only the resources they need. Within groups, users can see newsfeeds of the latest information to stay current on information relevant to the topics or groups theyâ€™ve selected. This self-directed approach to learning within an organization differs from many of the intranet or enterprise social networks companies typically use, as employees can curate the information most relevant to them.

Beyond internal news, employees also have access to relevant industry news. Staying on top of current industry news can be an added burden, but by integrating these resources into the platform, Honey is able to provide a broader tool that evolves with companies.

Making Workplaces Flatter

The Honey platform levels typical HR structures so that anyone within a company can communicate with one another. This democratic approach to corporate communications allows all employees to post, share, or message groups or each other. Honey lists several case studies where companies have expressed that this flat structure allowed a corporate culture to naturally evolve.

Similar corporate communications tools, such as Slack, have likewise grown in popularity. The development of new tools for connecting employees, teams, and workplaces could signal a shift in workplace communications, especially when employees are expected to consume more information than ever before while also filtering out information needed to learn on the job.
