New VR-Robotics System Takes Snowboarding Training Off the Slopes and Into the Living Room

saraehardman

February 11, 2018


With Olympians making snowboarding look incredibly cool, it’s no wonder that the sport has become more popular in recent years. That said, it’s also one of the sports most likely to cause injuries for beginners. In fact, 67% of all snowboarding injuries happen within the first week of someone taking up the sport, and more than half of those are due to falling.

One cutting-edge research team tackled this problem by creating a VR-robotics system to train beginning snowboarders indoors before they hit the slopes. The system consists of a platform that mimicks the movement of a snowboard going downhill as well as an HTC Vive playing a custom snowboarding VR program. Synced, the platform and VR headset create an immersive environment requiring actual movement and decision-making on the part of the user.

The system helps people practice two of the most common moves in snowboarding: the roll and yaw. The research team believes this combination of VR and robotics builds muscle memory in users so that when they hit the real slopes, they are more equipped to handle the sport and less likely to get injured.

Hament, B., Cater, A., & Oh, P. (2017). Coupling virtual reality and motion platforms for snowboard training. 14th International Conference on Ubiquitous Robots and Ambient Intelligence. Jeju, South Korea: IEEE.
