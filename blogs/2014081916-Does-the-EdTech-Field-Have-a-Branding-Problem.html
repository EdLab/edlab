Does the EdTech Field Have a Branding Problem?

Kate Meersschaert

August 19, 2014

While researching edtech companies to feature in upcoming EdLab GroundBREAKERS, Seminars and Demo Nights I came across this NYT's article (also featured as a Hi5 pick earlier this month) focused on how TERRIBLE and terribly nondescript educational tech co. names have become. Take for example, QLovi, Noodle, Grockit and others. This naming issue is potentially interesting for a few reasons... 1) should the end learning goal be more clearly highlighted in a name? 2) Is this a similar issue to that of the Common Core branding debate? What are your thoughts? Do names like LORE and 2Tor (now 2U) do these company's a disservice? Does a name really matter, if so, why? If not, why not?
