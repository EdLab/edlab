IDEAS CITY Festival

EdLab Studios

July 26, 2015


Italo Calvino's 1972 book, Invisible Cities, has for decades inspired architects and artists to visualize cities in a new way. The book's unconventional structure and form was a natural fit for a group of people trying to re-imagine New York City, more specifically, its creative communities and street fair culture, and thus, was the theme of this year's IDEAS CITY festival. The biennial festival is a three-day event hosted by the New Museum, featuring talks, panels, workshops and performances that culminate in a street festival unlike any other seen in New York City. Tens of thousands of people gather in the Bowery neighborhood's alleys and parks to explore questions of transparency and surveillance, citizenship and representation, participation and dissent, and visibility in an increasingly crowded cityâ€”all this through participatory showcases by small businesses, community organizations and artists. Contrary to most street festivals, however, no traffic is blocked and everything is environmentally friendly. Garbage clean-up, in fact, is an artistic event in and of itself over the course of the day. Harnessing the voices and stories of the people who live and work in the neighborhood, the festival is complemented by a series of unconventional walking tours to re-examine the city, be it through the perspective of a pigeon, old-timer residents, or internet infrastructure.
