"Problems With Education"

Bismark Appiah

May 12, 2013

I thought about sharing the Vialogue below, which although very funny, offers an accurate assessment of the problems in education. For instance, in high school, a lot of students spend time studying to memorize the information rather than comprehending. The actor in the video makes a great point when he states that students memorize the material just for exams. But the question is, what happens after you take the exam? Do you still remember the materials that you've learned? And I guess it is because of the bar that has been set by the Board of Education. In New York State, high school students are required to take the Regents exams for most of their courses. And the truth of the matter is we sometimes struggle to make comparisons between what we learn and the real world. For most students in New York, all that matters is passing the Regents and moving on to another elective course or the next challenging course, which will be an Advanced Placement (AP) course. Please feel free to share your thoughts and laugh a little while you at it.
