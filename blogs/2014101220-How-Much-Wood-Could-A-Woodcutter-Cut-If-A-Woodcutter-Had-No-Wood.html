How Much Wood Could A Woodcutter Cut If A Woodcutter Had No Wood?

jvazquez

October 12, 2014


Without ever laying hands on a real bench saw, female students in India can now learn the tools of the trade thanks to haptic technology. Think of it like a hybrid simulation: students use different machines that replicate the actions of power tools while a virtual display reacts in kind with their movements. All the while, the machines realistically respond to whatever digital material is being cut/sanded/drilled etc.: vibrations, resistance, pressure, itâ€™s all there! This project comes out of AMMACHI Labs at Amrita University.

In this case, could haptic technology act as a reasonable substitute for the real thing? In any case could it act as a substitute? Let us know what you think in the comments of the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
