EdLab Review: EBSCOhosting E-books and Audiobooks

LauraCostello

July 22, 2011

You may recognize EBSCOhost from their ubiquitous suite of databases, but now they're also peddling e-books (formerly of NetLibrary) and audiobooks with new patron driven acquisitions functions. Pros: The EBSCO structure looks familiar and users who have patronized the databases will be able to navigate the e-books quite easily. The changing landscape of book ownership is very evident here; libraries are able to buy, subscribe or offer selected dummy records in their catalog for patron driven purchasing. E-books are auto-purchased when patrons spend more than 10 minutes reading, view more than 10 pages, download the e-book or make any kind of copy. E-book purchases can also be customized by number of allowed simultaneous users. The database is also huge, utilizing the former NetLibrary's cache of 300,000+ e-books and audiobooks. Cons: The interface is familiar and friendly, but like its librarian forebears, favors organization over style. Things are working pretty well right now, but EBSCO is still rolling out features like patron driven acquisitions. Though they have their finger on the pulse of current library needs, there's some uncertainty about the integration of these features. EdLab Relevance: As collection development becomes more focused on electronic resources, arrangements like this will become more common. Library acquisitions have become more subtle than merely purchasing a book and owning it forever. Our future collections will be more flexible and less permanent. Our Takeaway: EBSCOhost e-books and audiobooks will be an exciting addition to their database service and the option for patron driven acquisitions is very relevant.
