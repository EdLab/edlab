NYC Makery

Dana Haugh

December 21, 2014


At The Makery youth and adults are encouraged to be curious, to tinker, to experiment, and to make with technology. We are a place where communities can gather to play with the creative power of digital design and fabrication, electronics, physical computing, engineering, art and coding. (via NYC Makery)

Technology Playground
NYC Makery is a pop-up makerspace that teaches hands-on workshops in digital fabrication, 3D printing, robotics, computing, game design, circuits, and more. Workshops are led by instructors with technology and engineering backgrounds and take place in a variety of environments, some of which have included historic houses, a bodega, storefronts, and warehouses. Workshops range from $50-$300 and all Makery participants take home the physical and digital artifacts that they create. The Makery gives kids and adults a space to freely explore, tinker, build, imagine, fail, and succeed. Aspiring tinkerers choose from a list of workshop topics on the "Makery Menu," but those with more elaborate ideas need not worry – the Makery also offers private, customizable workshops to satisfy every creator’s whim.

A Moving Makery
The NYC Makery has taken over and transformed dozens of spaces to create unique and inspiring landscapes. Officially located at "Somewhere, NYC," NYCMakery embraces the idea that people can make anything, anywhere, anytime, any way. The organization aspires to one day open an official headquarters, but for now they are content to explore the NYC landscape, giving new creators the opportunity to construct light-up postcards in their corner Laundromat or build a sensor-triggered camera in the front of a bodega.
