Where is the Unity?

Bismark Appiah

March 3, 2013

It seems that the idea of bipartisan in the US Congress and other branches of government is slowly fading because of the heated economical, social and political climate. Nowhere has this being more obvious than on fiscal issues. Last year, the G.O.P and President Obama were able to come to an agreement to prevent the nation from going over the “fiscal cliff.” The fiscal cliff brought to light the conflicting and sometimes harsh nature of our divided government. Now another major fiscal issue is looming and it centers on government spending. This NY Times article discusses how the Republican party, is essentially banking on facing the President and the Democrats on fiscal issues than on social issues such as same sex marriage. With the majority of Americans (and state governments) favoring same sex marriage in some form, and other social issues, Republican leaders are hoping their showdown with the President on government spending will rally the base with an eye towards next year's midterm elections. As a citizen, what do you make of the constant fighting in Washington about fiscal issues? Are politicians putting their views and ideas ahead of making effective policy for all Americans?
