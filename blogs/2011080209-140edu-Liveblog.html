#140edu Liveblog

Kevin M. Gong

August 2, 2011

Liveblog of the #140edu conference. 9:28am - Jack Hidary describing students at a public school where 9th and 10th graders are required to write 15 page theses every 10 weeks and present to an external panel. 9:34am - Jack Hidary explains how a group of students learned about science by building paper planes in front of the head of NASA, got feedback, and re-built their planes to fly further. 9:45 - The audience is challenged to make a flying object out of 5 business-card pieces of balsa paper! 9:58 - Louis Wool injects some pragmatism and perspective. "People resist change, and there is a huge pushback against early adopters." 10:12 - Rebecca Levey, a public school teacher, highlights that parents get almost no information from their kids on what they are learning in schools and the projects they are working on. In order to have more parental engagement. parents need to be informed about what happens in the classroom. 10:27 - Christian Long discusses the importance of teachers and parents to encourage experimentation in children. 10:47 - Lisa Nielsen advocates for the importance of connecting schools to the real world. For example, banning electronics (laptops, cell phones, etc) is counterintuitive to how students learn outside the classroom. Most tools students learn in school are not actually used in real life. 11:39 - Cynthia Lawson emphasizes the importance of "slowness" : being able to turn off distractions and disconnect. 11:56 - Mel Rosenberg notes that 5 year olds incessantly ask questions, but when we move them into the classroom, they are forced to answer questions.
