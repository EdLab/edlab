Electrifying Democracy

dinosossi2

June 16, 2016


The Norwegian Museum of Technology encourages patrons to think about technology’s role in democracy through deeply immersive experiences. The museum provokes its audience with an overarching question: Is it possible to control technology in a democracy?

Hot button issues are addressed through technologies with mixed effects. A 3D printer makes production available for the masses, but what about printing a gun? Drones conveniently fly Amazon’s wares to our front door, but what about enlisting them to perform predatory acts? Robots are convenient assistants, but aren’t they also dehumanizing? The questions are challenging and the answers are as elusive as they are vital.

What are your views regarding technology’s impact on democracy? Do you think technology invigorates the body politic? Tell us about your perspectives on technology and democracy in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion:

@03:26 AimeeLee: The International Spy Museum in Washington DC engages visitors by creating experiences in which they spy on one another. Maybe this museum could do something similar to emphasize internet, surveillance, and privacy issues through (non-intrusive) experiences with patrons?
