Competition Reveals World’s Most Creative Kids

jvazquez

March 10, 2014


Destination Imagination (DI) allows kids and young adults to cultivate their creativity through a series of fun challenges. Working in self-assigned groups, students can choose to tackle one of 7 challenges in the following areas: technical, structural, scientific, fine arts, improvisational, service learning, and early learning. Curious learners are then given a stage-setting scenario and different objectives that they must meet, tailored to their area of choice. For example, students choosing the structural path could be presented with a scenario like this one, from the DI site: "Two forces. One structure. Will your team rally to prevent destruction OR crumble under the pressure?" Subsequently, one of their challenges is to build a structure that can withstand two simultaneous forces.

What begins as a spirited attempt at solving a problem among group members can soon escalate into an all-out battle between countries. DI is a creativity competition that has local, national, and global tiers. But while the competitive aspect of DI is a draw for its participants, one young man from Mesquite, Texas proves you don’t have to take home the grand prize to feel like a champion. "If we lose," he says with a shrug, "we still tried our hardest. If we try our hardest, we’re all winners."

DI attracts bright (and even bored) kids in need of a challenge. It even boasts a host of former-participants-turned-celebrities. Find out who by visiting the ongoing DI-scussion on Vialogues.
