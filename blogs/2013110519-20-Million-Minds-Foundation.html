20 Million Minds Foundation

Mable Yiu

November 5, 2013


Affordability in Higher Education
The 20 Million Minds Foundation (20MM) is focused on creating greater affordability in higher education. 20MM is actively engaged in creating and developing OERs (open educational resources) to help reduce the cost of textbooks and other educational materials for students. Founder Dr. Gary Michelson started 20MM in 2010 to help reduce the majority of studentsâ€™ expenses that come from textbooks, with the goal of someday replacing textbooks entirely with more affordable and high-quality OERs. 20MM partners with a number of organizations including Google Education, OpenStudy, and BenchPrep, to sponsor programs related to lowering textbook costs.

Custom Textbooks and Courses
20MM gives faculty control of the content their students use through the Twenty Million Minds Mix. This tool is designed to help faculty create custom textbooks for their students. With access to the open source Connexions library (learn more about OpenStax & Connexions), faculty can combine resources from the library with their own files and article web pages to create textbooks in digital or print form. Not only does the Twenty Million Minds Mix help make textbooks more personalized for the teacher, it also creates an affordable way for students to learn more effectively. Teachers can also choose to share their courses with others or see what others have curated by using Twenty Million Minds Mix Co-op.

Will OERs Replace Textbooks Entirely?
20MM may be the answer to providing a cheaper, yet still high quality, alternative to the traditional textbooks students have been using in higher education for generations. By enabling teachers to customize their own content to share with their classes and other faculty digitally, the need to pay for excessively heavy and costly textbooks may someday disappear entirely.
