Let the Competition Begin!

Demetri Lales

January 25, 2012

Nothing motivates people to do their best more than a competition. But when you add the subject of engineering into the mix, then it becomes a potentially innovative competition. The TEAMS program is an annual high school competition that challenges middle and high school students to use their math and science knowledge to engineer a solution to real everyday problems, or a simulated problem. This year, students will get ready to try and solve the major issues of the medical and health care industry. An engineering competition where students try their hand at solving real problems is a great way to build their engineering skills. Potential for sySTEMic Change: Integrating competitions like this into K-12 education is a great way to reinforce STEM education, challenge students, and maybe even solve some real problems in any industry that adults can't solve. Building engineering skills through these types of competitions can benefit young students in several ways. Students will build their planning and problem solving abilities, and give them a chance to see how real professionals work in their field. These potential benefits are not being addressed much in K-12 schools today, so implementing these types of competitions will be a new and engaging way to show students how to start thinking about the big problems in today's world by using their STEM skills. Let's keep competing, everyone.
