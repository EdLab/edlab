Shoeing Bush

Nantwi

December 15, 2008

The now infamous shoeing of President Bush by Iraqi cameraman Muntadar al-Zaidi has deep religious and cultural meaning. As highlighted by a BBC article, it is an insult in Arab culture to show the bottom of one's shoe to another and crossing your ankle over your knee is considered a no-no. In Islam, shoes are ritually deemed unclean. One cannot enter a mosque with shoes as they are usually left outside or carried inside with the soles of the shoes pressed together so it is not visible. The shoeing of Bush junior is yet another tale in an ongoing battle between shoes, Iraq and the Bush family. During Saddam Hussein's reign, the entrance to Rashid Hotel in Baghdad had a mosaic of Bush senior on the floor; therefore visitors had to step on the likeness of the former president as a sign for revenging the war crimes he supposedly committed in Kuwait. All across the Arab world, posters of Bush has seen the bottom of a shoe while Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice has been given the first name Kundara, which means shoe. Like Bush's legacy, time will determine the significance of Zaidi's actions. Some may see it as a disrespectful gesture to a man who liberated Iraq from the shackles of a ruthless dictator while others may see it as a symbolic gesture to a man who started an unpopular war that has caused the deaths of thousands of innocent civilians.
