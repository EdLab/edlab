Optimizing Thumbnail Images on Vialogues

James Manlapid

July 30, 2013

** PRIVATE ** Some of you may notice that Vialogues is quite slow loading the home, explore and play pages. If you go to http://www.webpagetest.org/ and plug in Vialogues.com, it will fail almost every test. Two tests which failed are Compress Images and Progressive JPEGs which directly relate to thumbnails of videos on almost every Vialogue page. How can we easily improve web performance based on this information? For instance, the thumbnail in this attachment is 75 x 55 px. The actual image that is stored in Amazon is 1568 x 1176 px. Here's the catch, your browser actually downloads the HUGE image just to display the small thumbnail. So we're wasting about 700 KB of bandwidth just to display a very small thumbnail which should be about 5-10 KB. We should take note that images account for 85.5% of all bytes downloaded and 50% of requests when your browser accesses Vialogues.
