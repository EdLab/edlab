Tired of Being Wired

Brian.Sweeting

September 29, 2013


A new study finds that a majority of teens are growing tired of their online lives and crave more peer-to-peer interactions. Not only do teens admit that they’re addicted to their social networks, but they also express a desire disconnect: 49% of 13-17 year olds interviewed said they prefer in-person communication to electronic. 44% said that using social media distracts them from the friends they’re with, and become frustrated when their friends pay more attention to gadgets than people. Will digital burnout have implications for connected classrooms? After an initial rush to embrace online classes, education institutions and policy makers (like San Jose State University and The State of California ) are now pausing to re-assess the consequences of digital-only courses. As they do this, they should consider a host of concerns including self-efficacy, dropout rates, and students’ attitudes towards in-person interactions. At this point, the benefits of digital learning are well-recognized, but institutions should be wary of losing the benefits of in-class instruction -- otherwise it’s all FaceTime without any real face time.
