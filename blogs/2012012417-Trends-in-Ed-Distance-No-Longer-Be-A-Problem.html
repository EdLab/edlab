Trends in Ed: Distance No Longer Be A Problem

Lu Wang

January 24, 2012

Online tutoring may not sound like a new idea. However, with the development of technologies and their increasing use in the education sector, the idea of "sharing a screen, if not a classroom" is becoming more and more popular in classrooms. Tested to be a real help for students from low-performing classrooms, those online tutoring softwares are very likely to be ideal alternatives of face-to-face tutoring, which is highly restricted to distance and time.

These days, a boosting number of collaborations appear between grass-root NGOs (non-governmental organization) and distant learning software companies. Organizations such as Innovations for Learning, Everybody Wins! New York, and New York Cares are using online tutoring softwares to help improve the reading skills in low-performing schools in Chicago, New York, Detroit, Miami and Washington. Increasing literacy scores have been observed and reported. It can be predicted that a wider usage of similar softwares in primary and secondary classrooms will appear in the near future.
