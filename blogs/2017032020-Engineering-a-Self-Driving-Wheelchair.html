Engineering a Self-Driving Wheelchair

csd2126

March 20, 2017


During a visit to an assisted-living facility, an engineer from MIT noticed that there werenâ€™t enough staff members to allow patients to move freely around the facility. This observation led lead researcher Daniela Rus to the insight that self-driving wheelchairs could greatly reduce the limits placed on individuals with physical disabilities.

The wheelchairs use similar technology to that found in self-driving cars. The chairs work in several phases. Prior to going into service, the vehicle drives around a closed space to create a map. Once the map has been programmed, the user can select where on the map to go. Finally, sensors allow the chair to avoid new obstacles. The engineers in the Vialogue explain that they want to develop the future of robots by making machines that are increasingly more capable.

If successful, self-driving wheelchairs could increase the independence of those with limited mobility. How do you think empathy for these individuals impacted the design-thinking process? What lessons can designers learn from this story? Join the discussion and share your thoughts on Vialogues.
