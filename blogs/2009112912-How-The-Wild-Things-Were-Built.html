How The Wild Things Were Built

Gus Andrews

November 29, 2009

All I can say about this cool, super-detailed explanation of how the Where The Wild Things Are costumes were built is, can we pleeeease pleeease pleeeeease get a machine that mills three-foot-diameter foam shapes for puppet heads?! If we do, I promise to make us a new sit-on bear for the library's second floor :D WTWTA was definitely a must-see for those of us into puppet craft, and I want to see it again now that I've read this article. Also for puppet fans, you probably all know this by now, but Henson's still got it. I really feel the Muppets -- the ones from The Muppet Shows, specifically -- come into their own in the medium of YouTube. The short variety-parody format the various Muppet Shows always had on TV -- which never seemed to be sustainable past a season or two -- translates extremely well to viral video, as is evidenced by the near-7-million views the Bohemian Rhapsody video has gotten in the past week. Having a different channel for each Muppet is also a stroke of genius.
