One Month is Netflix for MOOCs

Ryan.Allen

May 6, 2018


"Who says you need 10 years to become a good programmer? You can get surprisingly good at something with just 20 hours of focused, dedicated work." (One Month)

Programming in 30 Days

Doesn’t it take years and thousands of dollars to learn how to code? Not according to One Month, an NYC-based startup that promises to teach students basic programming skills in just one month’s time. One Month offers MOOC classes on coding languages like Python or Ruby and others related to new tech innovations, such as the Bitcoin & Ethereum course.

The creators of One Month, Mattan Griffel and Chris Castiglione, both teach at Columbia Business School; and while they don’t guarantee that users will become experts in just a month, they do promise that students will learn the basics. These foundational lessons should prepare people for future success as they gain more expertise and experience.

Netflix for MOOCs

One Month’s intensive 30-day bootcamp courses cost around $200 per class. Additionally, there is a membership fee that costs around $29 a month, which includes unlimited Learning Library access and $100 off each bootcamp course. The Learning Library contains a multitude of self-directed learning resources, while the the bootcamp courses provide more traditional, structured learning and are taught by One Month’s founders.

All of the classes can help to build foundational skills for students. Before registering for a course, users can view the syllabus, class activities, and outcomes to help gauge expectations for these short sessions. Further, One Month also offers group packages, which could encourage teams or businesses to skill-build together.
