Roots & Shoots Celebration of Service

EdLab Studios

July 21, 2013


Now in its 22nd year, the Jane Goodall Institute Roots & Shoots program - a worldwide network of youth leaders doing service projects to improve the future for their communities, animals, and the environment - teamed up with NYU's Wallerstein Collaborative for Urban Environmental Education to present an afternoon of workshops, a service project, and a speech from a youth representative to the United Nations, all oriented toward public environmental education. The afternoon showed that working for sustainability isn't just necessary, but fun and easy for everyone, with conservation workshops on urban ecosystems and journaling, home gardening, make-your-own batteries, water use, solar conservation, and more. Celebrants took home their useful creations, a stronger appreciation for their role in crafting a better future, and the joy of having been a part of something useful.
