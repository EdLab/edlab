Professional Dev Seminar Ext - Bus Factor

ryanwu

February 21, 2019

Most of us all have been in a situation that we use some workaround in the development process that will create technical debt. Another common issue we might encounter is the "hit by a bus" problem which happens when one of the talented team members suddenly become unavailable. In short, it's people's single point of failure.


To prevent this issue, we have to allocate enough time for the team to share knowledge and tackle the debt. Most of the time, it doesn't look like an essential activity because we can always find some "more important" task to do. But according to my experience, it is one of the most critical plans.


Please comment down below if you're curious about how we handle it.

Bus Factor

Will a project fail if one of its members is hit by a bus? The bus factor is a measurement of the risk resulting from information and capabilities not being shared among team members, from the phrase "in case they get hit by a bus". It is also known as the bread truck scenario, lottery factor, truck factor,[1] bus/truck number, or lorry factor.


Workaround noun COMPUTING

A method for overcoming a problem or limitation in a program or system.


Technical Debt

Technical debt (also known as design debt[1] or code debt) is a concept in software development that reflects the implied cost of additional rework caused by choosing an easy solution now instead of using a better approach that would take longer.
