From the Morton Schindel Animation Cels Collection: Strega Nona (1975), by Tomie dePaola

Rebecca Hyams

June 28, 2012

In exploring materials in the PocketKnowledge Viewer I ran across the Morton Schindel Animation Cells Collection. I enjoy animation, so I clicked into the collection and read a little bit about it. Schindel founded Weston Woods Studios, which produced animated movie versions of popular children's books. What jumped out at me was the mention of Strega Nona. I remember watching that in the library in elementary school, sitting on the carpet in the nook where most of the picture books and simple chapter books were. (In other news, remember when reading chapter books were a big deal when you were 6 or 7?) [caption id="attachment_706" align="aligncenter" width="300" caption="from Strega Nona (1975), by Tomie dePaola"][/caption] Does anyone else have memories of watching Strega Nona, even if they're as vague as mine? As soon as I saw the name in the collection description and the image of the cell I remembered it right away. I think I'm going to explore this collection further and post some of my findings. Consider it a new post series.
