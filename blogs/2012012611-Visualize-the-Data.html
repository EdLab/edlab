Visualize the Data!

Demetri Lales

January 26, 2012

With the rise in demand for data analysts, and professionals who can use data to make predictions, it may be beneficial to start teaching the concept of data visualization. This isn't a new concept, but what is more widely available today as opposed to a few years ago, is the increasing amount of free resources that let students present data in a aesthetically pleasing way that also makes sense. In many K-12 math classes, students learn the basics about data and how to present them in a graph, usually two dimensional and unchanging. Educators can challenge students by having them create graphs that include the time variable. Motion Chart from Google is a free resource that allows users to do this, and use computer programming to make and customize the graph. Another way for students to practice their data visualization skills is to present their data in an infographic. There are plenty of free resources on how to create them as well. Potential for sySTEMic Change: With the increase of data being used in many industries, there is also a rising need to present data in a way that professionals from all disciplines can easily understand. Educating students about data visualization is a good way of combining teaching art and mathematical principles. Finding new ways of better visualizing data can potentially lead to new discoveries. In the Vialogue below, we can see how JoAnn Kuchera-Morin demos a device that shows data in a whole new way which led to some discoveries. This is an example of a benefit to teaching students more about data visualization. Let's keep visualizing everyone.
