D&R ideas: The EdLab Educational Entrepreneurship Series

jvazquez

July 27, 2010

**Please keep private** As part of the D&R seminar today, we asked participants to imagine an ideal EdLab-produced learning/professional development experience for educational entrepreneurs. They were further pressed to imagine that the experience would take place within 2 weeks. Could it be done? Here are the ideas of the five groups in attendance. The italicized sections are questions I had for that group. If any member of the group could enlighten me, that would be awesome :) Also, if I got anything wrong, please don't hesitate to correct me! Group 1: A new version of the EdLab Venture Competition

  • Utilize the alumni network (via online collaboration tools)
  • Create specific categories for entrants. This would encourage participation rather than limit it
    • -- Can you elaborate on this point? Did you have specific categories in mind? Why do you think this style would nurture creativity? --Also, I think I missed how exactly this differs from the previous EVC... Group 2: "Failure [to adopt]" Conference
  • First we would do a case study of EdLab products that would enlighten as to why they aren't being adopted.
  • Then, we would come up with interventions and implement them.
  • At the end of this research process, we will present our findings (in the form of "lessons learned" at the Failure [to adopt] Conference
  • The underlying goal of this study/conference is to establish EdLab as a credible educational entrepreneurship resource.
    • --What would the length of this experience be? -- Also, would it only be EdLab presenting? If so, maybe as it evolves we could invite others to present at our failure conference... I'd pay to see that! Group 3: Online courses and face-to-face workshops to help people start a business
  • This would be a paid experience but "learners would get their money's worth"
    • -- What would make this a worthwhile investment?
  • Strategy: Release at least one course as soon as possible to see if it garners interest
  • Create a series of face-to-face workshops that would appeal to entrepreneurs. Invite "big names" (i.e. Phoenix Wang) to give the workshops within their specialty (marketing, PR, getting funding, etc)
    • Group 4: A series of workshops
  • Help bring educators and developers together via workshops
    • -- How will you reach these groups?
  • Start small (so, start at TC) and exceed expectations. Grow from here.
    • Group 5: Reality TV
  • Focus on "the idea"
  • The reality TV show would showcase how ideas can make a difference
  • The show could also link people with ideas to people who can help execute them
    • --I may have misinterpreted this. As a side note, another cool show would be to connect aspiring entrepreneurs with whatever they are lacking ($, office space, mentorship...).. it could be a gaaame show!
  • Inspirational TV show that features current educational entrepreneurs.
  • Create a website full of resources
    • -- Like what?
