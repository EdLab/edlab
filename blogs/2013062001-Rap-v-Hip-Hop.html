Rap v. Hip-Hop

Carmel Addae

June 20, 2013

During Tuesday's D&R meeting, when Fred asked George what was the difference between rap and hip-hop in reference to the two of the group names, I was reminded of an mSchool course I started several summers ago. The course was about the history and cultural impact of hip-hop as an art form though many argue for it as a lifestyle. I want to finish the course this summer and wanted to know what, if any, categories and activities I should include for the course. So far, the course looks at the history of hip-hop from its inception in the South Bronx in the late 1970s to its current global status as one of the more popular music genres. Watch the video below from Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five, aptly titled the Message, in which they “rap” about all the ails of their community. Though the song was originally released in 1982, many of the themes it touched on are still prevalent in urban America, underlying hip-hop's societal and cultural influence.
