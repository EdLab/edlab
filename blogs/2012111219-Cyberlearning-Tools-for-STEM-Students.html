Cyberlearning Tools for STEM Students

Laura Scheiber

November 12, 2012


Cyberlearning, or computer-mediated learning, offers a number of advantages that can enhance or, in some cases, replace the traditional classroom. Consider, for example, that certain cyberlearning tools transcend time and space, allowing students to learn anytime and anywhere. Others, such as game-based learning and augmented reality tools, can help bridge learning that occurs in the classroom with informal learning that happens in everyday experiences.

The National Science Foundation sponsored a video that explored how cyberlearning tools are currently supporting STEM students. Considering that women only make up 25% of the STEM workforce in the US, of particular interest in this vialogue conversation is how some of these tools encouraged girls to engage in STEM-related subjects:
