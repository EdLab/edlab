Trends in Ed, 2.6.08

jvazquez

February 6, 2009

Happy Friday! Here, have a google doodle: Celebrate the weekend by playing with one of these interesting sites/products… Ultralearn allows users to create interactive rich media mashups that combine audio, text, graphics, video, and animation for training, learning and knowledge sharing. For the budding screenwriters out there you might find zhura to be a good time. It provides a forum to write screenplays publicly, privately, and collaboratively. You can also see what other writers in the community are working on. Got writer's block? The site also offers tips and tricks. Google Latitude allows users to broadcast their locations to friends and family based on the coordinates of their cell phone. Users can track who they follow (this already sounds creepy) and call, email, or IM them from within the app. Latitude works on BlackBerry, Windows Mobile, and Nokia S60s. Geobroadcasting: because the insane accuracy of your tweets just isn't enough. Flatword offers free textbooks online as well as softcovers, audiobooks, and self-print options. I think the catch is that instructors have to sign up for the site and request the book for their course before students can read it. Sign up is free, though. Once the book is added to the instructor's course, s/he can feel free to modify it–take out chapters that aren't covered, reorder the chapters, or add personalized exercises.
