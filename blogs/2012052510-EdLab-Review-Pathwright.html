EdLab Review: Pathwright

LauraCostello

May 25, 2012

Online course management systems are all the rage right now, but many of the fancy ones are proprietary. The institutional set tends to look towards Moodle, D2L, or Blackboard for branded production and school-level organization, but these systems tend not to be thrilling in design or execution. Pathwright aims to shake 1998 off the LMS and offers school level organization, solid design, and administrative elements like fee collection. Pros: The UI is cool and gorgeous in both front-facing and build modes, especially in contrast to something like Moodle. Pathwright replaces the html hatch by creating course elements that are more rigid, but ensures that the few options they do offer are the ones users need. It's easy and intuitive to create a course and the whole thing drums up in a wrapper that's minimally customizable, but clean and chic. Cons: The school-level organization is great for branding, but there's no centralized hub for all Pathwright courses, so it's a bit difficult to see it in its natural habitat. (Here's one in action, if you're interested) The thing runs pretty well, though I did experience some buggage in the slick, swoopy course creation pane. The design might be slightly over ambitious, but that's a good con in my opinion. EdLab Relevance: For the mSchool courses we're developing this summer, it might be good to explore some of these free, newfangled management systems. Nfomedia, instructure, and rcampus seem to have similar freemium builds and offbeat outlooks. Our Takeaway: Moodle's really functional and migratable, but it's always good to know the neighbors.
