Trends in Ed: 10.26.2010: Junior High School Class on Diversity

Nantwi

October 26, 2010

When students at Jericho Middle School started school earlier this fall, they were anticipating the usual junior high courseload: history, English, and algebra, among others. However, on the docket was an unexpected yearlong course: diversity. Called Seedlings, after the national program SEED (Seeking Educational Equity and Diversity), the class is divided into seventh and eighth grade sessions for around 30 students and runs 45 minutes per. The course description “will have you actively thinking about everything from food through language in a way you may never have before as we learn about what unites and divides all of us, and why.” The design of the course itself also divert from the normal junior high school course, where there is the usual emphasis on quizzes and homework. In Seedlings, students are neither quizzed nor tested on any specific topic and most of the class time is devoted to general discussions on subjects such as privilege, discrimination and course readings. Students will be graded at the end semester on their class participation and how well they challenged themselves when they submit their self-evaluations. The class comes at a time when bullying and other forms of intolerance have taken the national spotlight. Earlier today, the Obama administration announced a nationwide effort to combat bullying in schools.
