EdLab Review: Chromatik

LauraCostello

April 6, 2012

Chromatik is an iPad app and desktop application meant to be a one stop for music education. Its primary function is to distribute, collect, and share annotations on sheet music, but it also allows users to listen to instructor uploaded reference audio and record and share their own practice sessions. Pros: Chromatik is a great idea and quite clearly built with love. Everyone on the team is a musician and the app, though new, has already partnered with several schools including Juliard, UCLA, and the Los Angeles Unified School District. The app is focused and everything from the design touch of a slightly disheveled stack of sheet music to the unobtrusive recording and annotation tools speak to the care and passion of the team. Cons: I was unable to rustle up much about Chromatik's business strategy, but its tie-in with the iPad means that it might not see much integration in non-wealthy schools for awhile. EdLab Relevance: Perhaps the most interesting thing about Chromatik is the way it deals with copyrighted sheet music. Sheet music use deals are pretty unique and seem to almost lay the groundwork for the multi-user digital book negotiations of today. A system for managing the distribution and collection of digital “copies” of copyrighted music material seems like a simple idea, but Chromatik is really pioneering this in a way that's useful to schools. Our Takeaway: Chromatik combines a great concept with a dedicated build team.
