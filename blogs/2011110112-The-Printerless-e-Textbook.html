The Printerless e-Textbook

LauraCostello

November 1, 2011

In response to a digital access survey of librarians, Ebrary has begun a push towards greater online access. They're in the process of developing a mobile app and in the meantime, have made it possible for users to permanently download a (60-page max) pdf of their e-books or temporarily borrow the whole thing through Adobe Digital Editions. Downloaders of the pdf-ilk will be able to read on any device but the Adobe Digital Editions, like most awesome e-book advancements, are debuting without Kindle support. Ebrary is still rolling out this feature and at press time it hasn't hit our catalog yet so I still have a couple of questions about how it'll work. I'm guessing the full-book offline viewing will only be for MUPOs (multiple user purchase option) and those purchased with single-user viewing will only be eligible for the 60-page copy. I'm also guessing that Ebrary, since it requires users to register before viewing online, will ensure that users are not able to download more than one sample. My final guess is that downloading will become one of our DDA triggers, akin to printing from the text. This development puts textbook-like functionality in an existing library subscription service. We've been focusing on building an e-book collection through Ebrary, so this functionality should be especially good for TC.
