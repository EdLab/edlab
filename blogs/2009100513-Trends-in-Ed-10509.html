Trends in Ed, 10.5.09

Arianna Choi

October 5, 2009

Justice Sandra Day O'Connor's New Job The Washington Post reported yesterday (Educational? You Be the Judge) that the former Justice has a new job as a computer game advocate. More specifically, we're talking about an online game designed to teach school children about the judicial branch. The nation's first female justice has been spearheading a project called Our Courts, which develops educational games for 7th and 8th graders to learn about government. The Post wrote:

"One of the reasons we started a public education system was to create citizens who will participate in our government," [she said in an interview]...She lamented the fact that the No Child Left Behind law has shifted schools' focus from inculcating civic values to jacking up math test scores.
