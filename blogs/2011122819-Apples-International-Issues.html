Apple's International Issues

Oumar Soumahoro

December 28, 2011

While browsing the international edition of France 24 a few days ago, I came across this article that highlights some legal and moral issues Apple is facing outside the United States. Well known as a revolution of the 21st century for its innovation and cutting edge technology, it comes out in Italy that Apple misled consumers as part of a technique to increase sales. As a result, Apple was fined 900 000 euros (about $1.2 million) for misleading customers on assistance service and guarantees for its product, said Italy's anti-trust authority. Apple had given "unclear information on payments for additional assistance offered to consumers" and the company had not "fully implemented the two-year guarantee by the producer," it added. Can apple be anymore trusted by Europe and other foreign countries? Is Apple also losing status as a global giant?
