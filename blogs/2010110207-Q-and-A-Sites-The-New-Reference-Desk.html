Q and A Sites: The New Reference Desk?

gnatriello

November 2, 2010

Sites devoted to Questions and Answers seem to be generating more interest lately. See, for example, this piece in Businessweek. A more recent piece on Quora led me to check out the site. There were certainly a number of areas where the questions and answers were interesting and enlightening, but my sense is that it is difficult to determine the quality of the answers outside of one's own area of expertise. This raises of the issue of how to generate quality content from a user base, something that we are encountering with our Pressible project. Still, the Q & A format is an interesting one, and it is easy to see how this might be an appropriate genre for online communities. I would be interested in thinking more about how a Q & A site for a community could serve as a new type of reference desk. How might we organize something that would promote quality content and make both the quality and the provenance of information apparent? What kind of scaffolding might be used to promote quality content from a community in a Q & A format?
