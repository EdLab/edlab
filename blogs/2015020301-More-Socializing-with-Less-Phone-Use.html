More Socializing with Less Phone Use

Khalil Abubakar

February 3, 2015

The average American spends a decent amount of their time on phones messaging, video chatting or browsing the web. In other parts of the world, individuals socialized more on a face-to-face level. Students in Singapore won 30,000 to create an app that prevents people from using their phones. The app, Apple Tree, works when two or more people bring their phones together. This immobilizes the phones. The longer they are joined together, the more apples grow on the trees, which discourages them from using their phones and enhances more socializing.
