Let's learn something from our blogging behavior

Janice Joo

January 9, 2013

Just wondering... is there a dashboard for the EdLab blog? If not, maybe we should set it up. Can we somehow keep simple metrics on the type of content we are blogging about, as a loose measure of what the team is thinking about, interested in, or even commenting on? EdLab is a learning organization, and we are focused on gathering analytics for the learning that goes on with our products' users, but what about us? Aren't we all learning too? I personally want to know what I tend to blog about or comment on, and have the data to back it up, without having to wait for Hui Soo to make an empirical statement about my tendencies... So here's something simple we could do to set ourselves up for it. Any other ideas would be helpful: Take tagging seriously. We need to update our "Group" list with more meaningful tags. We should, at the very least, have the top 3 categories from our EdLab priority-list as tags: mSchool, LaunchPad, and NLT. Tags like "EdLab Review" and "Research Digest" now fit under NLT... Should they remain? Should they be visually displayed as an indented section? Overall, if there's a quick way to rethink this tagging system, using the existing structure (for now), then we might be able to learn something from our own blogging activity... Thoughts?
