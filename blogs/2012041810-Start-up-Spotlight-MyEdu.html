Start-up Spotlight: MyEdu

Kate Meersschaert

April 18, 2012

The Details & Back-story: MyEdu is on a mission to help college students and their parents get a better “return on education." It promises to help students save up to 20% on college fees that would otherwise be incurred by using traditional channels to plan their courses, select a major, and buy textbooks. The free online service provides dashboard-like reports based on data from universities that includes professors' ratings, student course grades, updated course schedules, GPA calculators and, among other data points, degree requirements. One of three founders, Christopher Chilek, describes MyEdu's "special sauce" as a, "unique combination of data, network, and applications [that] provide the support students need to be successful in life." Unique point of Difference: The depth & breadth of data available to users from participating universities, paired with an easy-to-use dashboard-style navigation. Additionally, the ability to query for courses and professors based on your learning style (users can respond to items that are ranked on a sliding scale) adds another dimension to the search experience. For example, for the item, “When it comes to class attendance I...” users can choose an answer on the spectrum from “Go only when I need to” and “Go to every class. MyEdu also allows users to see student grades for each professor's section. EdLab Relevance: Pundit! As we continue to imagine new incarnations and organizations for our intelligent course recommendation platform, it is interesting to note how MyEdu uses a dashboard and "friendly," easy-to-interpret graphing to organize the data they present to users. Our Takeaway: MyEdu provides the data and dashboard to help college students more easily navigate their course management experience.
