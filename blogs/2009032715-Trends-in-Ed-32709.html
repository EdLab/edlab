Trends in Ed, 3.27.09

Arianna Choi

March 27, 2009

Dot-Gov Tries Online Town Hall Meeting The White House has been on YouTube for a while now, but yesterday marked another initiative for change. The trial run of Open for Questions in the White House was held yesterday; it's an online town hall meeting where online participants are asked to submit questions for the president real-time and also vote on which questions he should answer. Click here for more on the President's online town hall meeting. More on Democratic-izing YouTube: YouTubeEDU The google empire holds a very high expectation of its capability or it may be that they just don't stop thinking about how to make searching easier. Most of us know how much www.google.com/scholar can save some sweat and pain, now for more visual sources, simply type www.youtube.com/edu where you'll find videos from its college and university partners. Columbia University and its smaller colleges/divisions are up, but Teachers College is yet to be found on its directory (wink, wink). iPhone-App-a-Day This video doesn't showcase nor review a particular application, but sure shows the potential for more innovations to come.
