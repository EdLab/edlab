Solar Solutions for Impoverished Communities

Nantwi

January 21, 2015


Electricity and access to light are a part of everyday life for individuals in developed countries; however, this is not the case for individuals in impoverished and remote communities across the world—they often lack access to light. An estimated 1.3 million individuals across the world live without electricity. Anna Sidana is on a mission to change this. Sidana founded One Million Lights, a non-profit endeavor that aims to deliver a million solar powered lights to families in remote villages across the globe.

A lack of access to electricity means students are unable to study at home. For these individuals, kerosene lit lamps provide their only access to light; however, there are a host of problems associated with kerosene lamps, including respiratory issues (especially among children), fires and fire burns, and kerosene costs, which can add up to half of a family’s meager income. To date, One Million Lights has distributed over 33,000 solar powered lights to families in over 27 countries.
