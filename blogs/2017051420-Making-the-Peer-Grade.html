Making the (Peer) Grade

csd2126

May 14, 2017


Peergrade is a free online platform that lets students partake in the evaluation process by facilitating peer assessment sessions between students. (About Peergrade)

Bringing students into the feedback fold

The idea for Peergrade occurred to founder David Kofoed Wind when the master's course he was teaching surged from 20 students to a class of 130. No longer able to provide individualized feedback, David brought students into the evaluation process. Reflecting its origins, feedback is the central ingredient of this platform. Peergrade is based on the idea that students get much more feedback (ten times more, so they claim) from each other than a single teacher could provide.

Saving Time and Improving Learning Outcomes

Peergrade’s assessment platform improves students’ learning experiences, saves teachers time on grading, and provides data insights into students’ performance on assessments. Teachers first create an assignment and an evaluation rubric in the platform. Next, students upload their individual or group work. Their work is then automatically distributed amongst their peers. Students provide feedback anonymously, which is delivered back to the assignment owner. Peergrade cites research stating that anonymity allows for better feedback, but this feature can also be disabled, allowing students to see who is reviewing their work. Peergrade explains that not only do students get more feedback through this system, but that students learn by assessing each other's work.

Far from being a way to offload teacher tasks to students, this platform offers a suite of tools that gives teachers greater insight into their students' performance that goes beyond grades. Teachers are able to see how their evaluation measures fare with students and identify areas of improvement in their course.
