How do you transform an industry?

jvazquez

November 21, 2011

Start with a brilliant, creative, and supportive yet altogether modest leader. Enter Dennis Hong. Dennis leads RoMeLA, or the Robotics and Mechanisms Lab, at Virginia Tech. Dennis and his team of students are all about robots. Humanoids, to be exact. You can find their creations crushing the competition at RoboCup or gearing up to save lives. But let's get back to that leader. Hong is lucky enough to work for an institution that gives him the freedom to explore new ideas and directions without placing restrictions on where he can take these ideas. In turn, Hong passes this sense freedom onto his students. They are a group that embraces brainstorming and, better yet, the crazy ideas that come from those ideation sessions. "Creativity is the ability to connect things that are not at all related," says Hong. My favorite part of this article was when it refers to the students who work at RoMeLa as the rockstars on campus. I believe it! When you have such an intricate vetting process that lets only the best and brightest in, others are going to want to be a part of that legacy. I think the takeaway here is that while not all labs build game-changing robots (nor should they, lest we succumb to a swift robot rebellion), they can all learn a lesson from the way Dennis Hong runs his revered lab: Encourage the crazy, do everything you can to be supportive, and let the students lead.
