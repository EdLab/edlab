Jeff Bezos: The Smartest Guy in the Room

dinosossi2

June 29, 2017


 


The latest squabble about charging Amazon an internet tax, is really beside the point and much too late.

 

With its domestic footprint stretching across the globe through its eponymous warehouses and, now, with its acquisition of Whole Foods, Amazon is paying real estate tax and being charged through state and provincial sales tax. In short, it’s paying its share.

 

Given their earlier struggles with Amazon Fresh, it looks like Amazon has readily embodied the tired adage if you can’t beat ‘em, join ‘em.

 



As you imagine, executive teams at large scale food sellers are loosening their ties and rolling up their sleeves wondering how they can avoid being the latest road kill for the fast moving Amazon juggernaut. Seeing its influence on intelligent personal assistants (Alexa), eReaders (Kindle), newspapers (the Washington Post), booksellers (Amazon books), movie studios (Amazon Studios), and online television (Amazon Prime), they better be worried.

 



Also, given the current administration’s past tension with Amazon, it won’t be a surprise if some kind of antitrust legislation on the way.
