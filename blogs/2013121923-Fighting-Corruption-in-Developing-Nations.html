Fighting Corruption in Developing Nations

abagigah92

December 19, 2013

According to the president of the World Bank Jim Yong Kim, corruption is the number one public enemy in developing nations. President Kim calls on private sectors, world partners and leaders to become part of a fight against corruption. In developing nations, government officials and business managers are taking big advantage of what amounts to a loose system. A lack of organization and accountability has made it easier for officials to manipulate the system for their own personal needs. President Kim states that every dollar that a corrupt official or a corrupt business person puts in their pocket is a dollar stolen from a pregnant woman who needs health, from a young child who deserves an education or from communities that need water, roads, and schools. Every dollar is important in the fight against extreme poverty. Some of the solutions needed to end corruption in developing nations are building organized state systems, and auditing systems that check government officials and the accounts of private enterprises. Moreover, it is also important to build institutions with great integrity and values. The World Bank wants to improve the way we share and apply knowledge about building institutions with greater integrity; need to empower citizens with information and tools to make their governments more effective and accountable and to build a global movement to end corruption.
