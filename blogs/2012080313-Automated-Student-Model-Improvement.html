Automated Student Model Improvement

Sharon Hsiao

August 3, 2012

This paper got the Best Paper Award in this year EDM conference. Automated Student Model Improvement. Kenneth R. Koedinger, Elizabeth A. McLaughlin and John C. Stamper Pages 17-24 The basic story is: Student models are critical to effective personalized learning. Intelligent tutors are currently the best-known strategy for e-learning due to the well-constructed cognitive models. Unfortunately, it is an expensive and time intensive process that is fundamentally driven by human experts. So the idea of this paper is to use educational data mining and machine learning techniques to optimize the process of building a student model automatically. They applied Learning Factors Analysis (LFA) algorithm to automatically finds better student models by searching through a space of KC models. The process utilizes decomposing & composing so-called “Knowledge Components” (to represent pieces of knowledge) from existing proposed student models in an online free data repository (DataShop). This datashop contains a lot of datasets including a huge variety of domains from math to second language learning. The study results showed that the proposed approach helped in error detection, instructional re-design, etc. EdLab takeaway: The data dashboard project is more than the front end dashboard, I think it's more like the datamart, datacenter. It's the engine of the student model server, all distributed applications, and more importantly the personalization (services) server. To continue providing "in-time" services, we certainly need to somehow automate the improvement process. So, i think this should also be listed as one of the scalability issues down the road.
