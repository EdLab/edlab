A Robot That You Eat

Ryan.Allen

November 13, 2017


Every year thousands of Americans swallow small items, such as corrosive button batteries. While the "why" factor remains ambiguous there, these various foreign objects can get stuck in the digestive tract and cause internal damage. Oftentimes, the only option for removal is to perform risky surgery. A team led by MIT researchers has designed an edible origami robot prototype that can remove these items from inside the human body.

This ingestible robot is made from dried pig intestines and can be delivered through a small ice capsule. Once eaten, the ice melts, the robot unfolds itself, and it can then perform its tasks. The robot can be controlled from the outside to remove foreign objects, patch wounds, or deliver medicine to specific internal locations.

The team hopes that the next upgrade to the robot will allow it to function without a human operator. Would you rather risk surgery or eat this new foldable robot? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues
