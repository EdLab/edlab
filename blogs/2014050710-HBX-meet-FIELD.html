HBX meet FIELD

Kate Meersschaert

May 7, 2014

Stock Market as Stage Show Harvard Business School relatively recently announced their new HBX program for online learning (here is my recent post re: the news) and the famed institution is, at the same time, exploring more in-person, collaborative learning opportunities as well. FIELD, is just one of these immersive, in-person experiences that HBS hopes will more clearly align their existing curriculum with real-world work. Involving role playing, stock market simulation and a healthy does of theater, the program hopes to give initiates the opportunity to pitch, test and evolve ideas outside the classroom. In-person vs. Online Debate Why is this notable? This dual focus on both enhancing virtual learning experiences, while simultaneously supporting the development of more meaningful face-to-face learning is a unique signal of the dual "hybrid" focus many learning-focused groups are starting to explore. From Acumen Fund's Acumen+ leadership courses to the rapid growth of often online/in-person Coding Bootcamps, there is growing interest in creating not only meaningful online learning experiences, but also in fostering the chance for potentially game-changing, in-person human interaction. What are your thoughts on the push & pull of in-person vs. online learning? Who is on the right path?
