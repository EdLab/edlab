EdLab Review: LearningJar

Kate Meersschaert

August 6, 2012

LearningJar, currently in beta launch mode and a winner of the 2012 SXSW LAUNCHedu contest (along with BloomBoard), is on a mission to help users gain new skills without additional schooling. Instead, the platform offers informal skill-based "pathways" or collections of resources to help users gain mastery and specific challenges to test those skills. For example, a pathway entitled "Market on Pinterest" (see screenshot below) includes four video resources collected in that pathway (one of which is embedded as a vialogue below) and a dedicated challenge which, in this case, is to set-up a Pinterest page for your business. Users are then able to display their new skills in a dedicated portfolio for other users to see. The site also lists certain careers and the suggested pathways necessary to be successful in the given field. Pros: Easy to navigate, lightweight learning experience. Perfect for informal knowledge acquisition and armchair learners who want to gain new skills but may not have the time or resources to commit to a more intensive online program. Cons: The ability to customize additional resources for learning is limited. Also, the courses seemed a bit too lightweight for those who want more serious, intensive feedback. Instead, the system is extremely hands-off. EdLab Relevance: As we develop our own course delivery system, it is important to remember that fine balance between lightweight, low barrier-to-entry, and a measurable educational experience. Our Takeaway: Lightweight learning at its most simple.
