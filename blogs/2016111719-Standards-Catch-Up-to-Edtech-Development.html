Standards Catch Up to Edtech Development

Jenny Abamu

November 17, 2016


"The CWiC Framework supports postsecondary decision-makers in effectively navigating the market of courseware solutions. It is designed to help you make better-informed adoption and implementation decisions." (About CWiC)

Up to Standards

Nowadays it seems like everyone has a digital solution to classroom problems that educators must adopt. However, the arduous task of comparing products to evaluate their effectiveness can also turn into a full-time job. Education technology has run far ahead of policy and standards, but researchers and experts are catching up by implementing new laws and frameworks to guide its adoption and development. The Courseware in Context Framework (CWiC) was created by a grant from the Bill and Melinda Gates foundation to help higher education institutions make more informed decisions when adopting digital courseware.

Diving into the Framework

The extensive, research-backed framework is broken down into four parts: product taxonomy, research collection, institutional-level implementation guide, and a course-level implementation guide. Each of these categories is matched with specific CWiC tools to help educators make quality evaluations of courseware. They look at important indicators like depth of interaction, adaptivity, and scaffolding, things educators might not think of until they have already begun to implement the tool. The framework is free and has been tested in four different institutions for its effectiveness.

Looking Ahead

The process of getting designers and evaluators to understand and properly implement the framework will require time and resources. However, with billions of dollars flowing into educational technology and rapid innovations taking place, frameworks such as CWiC can provide part of the foundation for useful evaluations.
