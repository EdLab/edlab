Trends in Ed, 9.17.09

jvazquez

September 17, 2009

“Creating a culture that's not afraid to fail” In a recent post, Beth Kanter explored why organizations are so quick to drop social media practices–some reasons being that they set unrealistic outcomes, did not plan a proper methodology, and consequently obtained poor results. Kanter urges people to value the learning experience that failure presents. In response to her question, "How do you create an organizational culture that is not afraid to fail?” she presents the following answers: (1) Must come from the top: reward learning (2) Unpack the fear of failure through internal discussions (3) Make learning the norm (4) Emphasize what works (5) Start small, early, and reiterate Is the EdLab afraid to fail? I don't think so. In fact, one of our mantras is “fail faster.” We have several social media projects going on at the moment. If we had been afraid of failure, we would never have taken the necessary risks. Still, the answers provided serve as a reminder for any organization experiencing a change in its culture.
