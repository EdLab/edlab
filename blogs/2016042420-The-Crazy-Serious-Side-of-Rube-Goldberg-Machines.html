The Crazy Serious Side of Rube Goldberg Machines

saraehardman

April 24, 2016


Zip, zing, and zap—these are just some of the noises you will hear at the annual Rube Goldberg Machine College National Competition. This may be the only DIY tournament that judges teams on their silliness just as much as on their technological innovation.

Inspired by the wacky inventions of cartoonist Rube Goldberg, these machines all have a simple task. At this competition, it was to erase a blackboard. Yet, as the college students in this vialogue demonstrate, a Rube Goldberg machine is about so much more than its conclusion. It’s about putting together an intricate system of moving parts that function in a chain reaction. The machines can have as many as seventy-five steps, and if even one goes wrong, the whole process halts.

Although these contraptions are as funny as they are complex, students are serious about making them. Some worked as many as 800 hours to perfect their creations. As the students presented their machines, it’s clear that all the hard work paid off. It’s likely that no other contraptions made of dominoes, marbles, and miniature catapults will keep you on your toes more.

How can making a Rube Goldberg machine get students excited about mechanics? What sorts of funny and complex devices have your students made? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
