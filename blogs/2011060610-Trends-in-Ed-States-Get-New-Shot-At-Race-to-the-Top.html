Trends in Ed: States Get New Shot At Race to the Top

Nantwi

June 6, 2011

The Obama administration announced nine states will receive another shot at its much-publicized Race to the Top program. The nine states, Arizona, California, Colorado, Illinois, Kentucky, Louisiana, Pennsylvania, New Jersey and South Carolina, all came up short for last year's awards and as such must submit new applications. The big difference between this year's competition from previous ones is the amount of money being doled out. Last year's winners shared around $4 billion in earnings while this year's competition is only for $200 million among the nine competing states. Last year's winners were Delaware, the District of Columbia, Florida, Georgia, Hawaii, Maryland, Massachusetts, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Rhode Island and Tennessee. In addition to the competition among the nine states, there is an alternate 50 state wide competition for $500 million to raise the quality of child care programs and families' access to such programs. The award for each state in this year's competition is expected to be in the region of $20-$50 million depending on the state's population and other determining factors.
