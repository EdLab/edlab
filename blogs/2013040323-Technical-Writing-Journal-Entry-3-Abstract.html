Technical Writing Journal Entry 3: Abstract

Sebastian Sackey

April 3, 2013

In today's class, we focused on how to write an abstract. This was something completely foreign to me; as a matter of fact, before today I had no idea what an abstract in writing was. However, after paying close attention to my professor's explanation, I have some sort of comprehension. When writing a lab paper, an abstract is very closely related to a synopsis. As defined by my English professor it is a "self contained statement describing a larger work". In other words an abstract contains all the information that the reader needs to get a general understanding of a paper. It is made up of the introduction, objective, discussion, procedures (methods), results, and the conclusion. It is very concise, made up of only six to seven sentences. This means that brevity plays a very important role, especially since certain papers can be lengthy. A writing skill which our professor emphasized was been able to compress several sentences into one concise statement that still held the same idea. This skill would greatly benefit us in writing our abstracts. I'm still trying to get the hang of being concise in my writing, but since taking this class I think I have become more aware of certain writing habits I have that unnecessarily stretches my writing.
