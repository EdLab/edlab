Digital vs. Analogue: Editing in Perpetuity

Kate Meersschaert

January 3, 2012

As Nicholas Carr points-out in this Wall Street Journal post, some books now, are literally, thanks to digital publishing, never done being written or in this case, re-written. Previously, like an artist, your work (book) would hang untouched on a gallery or museum wall (library) for all eternity, without the opportunity for change. Now, thanks to the advent of e-books, this parallel can no longer strictly be drawn. What does this new definition of publishing and the endlessly editable book mean for education? Now, thanks to printing on demand and digital publishing, being "published" and teaching students about this industry takes on a completely new meaning. Your work can now live in one of four formats: 1) on a blog 2) self-published as an e-book 3) released by a traditional publishing house as both an e-book and traditional print and finally 4) solely as a physical book. in this new age of infinitely editable how will the aforementioned options inform student's experiences with the "printed" word and expand and/or inform their view of what it means to be an author? When "books" are re-written en masse will history also become infinitely rewriteable? Only time, future, experience will tell...
