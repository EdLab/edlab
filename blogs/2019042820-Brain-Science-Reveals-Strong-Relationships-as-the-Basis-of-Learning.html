Brain Science Reveals Strong Relationships as the Basis of Learning

Rebecca.Sullivan

April 28, 2019


Recent neuroscience findings highlight the malleability of the human brain. Relationships and experiences drive the neurological development that is a necessary precondition for learning. This means that trauma can impede children’s ability to learn, but also indicates the potential positive effect that school relationships and experiences can have on enabling and promoting brain development.

When teachers intentionally combine social-emotional, affective, and cognitive development, connections form in children’s developing brains that accelerate their ability to problem-solve, think critically, and learn complex skills. Healthy relationships, this study indicates, are an essential prerequisite for learning and development.

In your opinion, what value do different models and types of education place on healthy relationships? Are such relationships typically seen as a prerequisite for learning? Do you agree that teachers have the responsibility to intentionally cultivate such relationships in the classroom? Contribute to the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
