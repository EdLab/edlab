Computers Can Now Detect Student Frustration

Janice Joo

May 6, 2013


Researchers in the Netherlands have developed a program that monitors students’ facial expressions while they are working on math problems. The program identifies parameters related to a student’s mood, such as happy, angry, or surprised, and subsequently presents math problems of appropriate difficulty. Though impressive advancements have been made in using facial cues in the past decade, research on facial cue recognition is still in the early stages.
