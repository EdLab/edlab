Shaking Up Chemistry With Mixed Reality

saraehardman

May 15, 2017


From the HoloLens to Magic Leap, mixed reality is changing the learning landscape in a significant way, and yet another new cool app has been added to the mix. MyLab, created by Lucas Rizzotto, is a new mixed reality chemistry app that lets you interact with the periodic table like never before. Not to be confused with augmented or virtual reality, mixed reality uses a transparent headset to overlay graphics on a personâ€™s surroundings. Although it is currently used less frequently than either augmented or virtual reality, mixed reality is expected to far surpass them in use in the coming years.

Designed for high school students, MyLab allows users to view the periodic table superimposed in the air in front of them by the device. They can then interact with individual atoms, drag them around the environment, study their structure, and combine them to make molecules. "Education today for the most part is passive," says Rizzotto. "[MyLab] turns education into more of a sandbox experience." Through mixed reality headsets, this sandbox experience can occur either individually or collaboratively, potentially making the chemistry classroom the most cutting edge it has ever been.
