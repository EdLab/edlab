Your Next Pet Will Be a Social Robot

saraehardman

November 27, 2018


We met Siri long ago, we recently welcomed Alexa and Google Homes into our lives, and we might soon adopt a cute, personality-filled, pet-like social robot called Vector. The latest product from Anki, the company that created Cozmo, Vector is a small bot that can fit in your hand and might open up an entirely new form of human-robot interaction. Instead of solely providing information, Vector’s main characteristic is its lovable personality. When it recognizes its owner, it acts excited and eager; when you pet its backpack, it relaxes; and when it hears a noise, it turns in that direction to check out the source.

Professional animators programmed Vector’s reactions and expressions, ensuring that every movement conveyed its personality and made it seem simply alive. Unlike a typical pet, Vector can also answer basic questions like a digital assistant, such as the day’s weather, the nutritional content of an avocado, and the square root of 256. Vector can also take pictures, which you can find on its connected app. Oh, also, just like a pet, Vector is always on and always searching for familiar faces and objects.

Anki funded Vector using Kickstarter, and officially released the product in October 2018. Each Vector costs $249. What are your thoughts on this new type of pet-like social robot? Who do you think would use Vector, and for what purposes? Answer these and more on Vialogues.
