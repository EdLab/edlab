BYOC--Bring Your Own Cardboard

Karen Bryner

April 25, 2007

A different kind of field trip. This Saturday night Displace Me, an overnight simulation of living in a camp for internally displace persons (IDP), will take place in 15 cities across the US. This is an effort to educate people about the conditions of the 1.5 million northern Ugandans displaced due to war that has been going on for 21 years. Food (saltine crackers) and water (1.5 liters per person) will be collected before the event and redistributed (likely in an uneven fashion if it is to parallel IDP camps) later that night. People will spend the night in cardboard structures they will make themselves. We've been talking about different ways of learning here at the EdLab. We've mentioned video simulations, Second Life experiences, and we have used media to promote discussion about democracy. The group organizing this event certainly puts a lot of value on experiential learning. Perhaps the EdLab could do something along these lines of physical simulation. Certainly, it has the power to be an effective way to teach. (And, yes, those under 18 are required to bring a signed minor release form to Saturday's event.)
