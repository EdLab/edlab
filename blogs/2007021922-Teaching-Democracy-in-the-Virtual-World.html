Teaching Democracy in the Virtual World

Anthony Cocciolo

February 19, 2007

For Prof. Kinzer's course on virtual worlds, one of our assignments was to build a quiz in SecondLife using the Linden scripting language. I used the Teaching the Levees project as the context, and used it to create a very simple 5 question quiz. The answers to all the questions can be found in the draft curriculum guide. You can teleport to the quiz by clicking this link, which will take you to the quiz's little corner on the TC Island. Although this was a simple project, it made me think more broadly about the use of virtual worlds for such curricular projects. I wonder how many years it will be before projects of similar scope will not only commission a website, blog, and resource sharing tool, but also a presence in one virtual world or another. If we use the Horizon report as guide, it may be 2-3 years. In the case of commissioning a blogger, I wonder if there might become a point where someone may be hired to roam a virtual world and engage in “democratic dialogue”. Today, however, its affect would be limited since virtual world users are not representative of the US population (obvious limitations include those with high speed connections, good computers, and impetus and interest in using a virtual world).
