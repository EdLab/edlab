Tired of Handwriting Letters? This Machine Will Do It For You

saraehardman

April 2, 2017


There’s nothing quite like a personal, handwritten letter, but Bond wants to change that. The four-year-old company developed a machine that can duplicate anyone’s handwriting to craft penned letters, which the company then sends to the desired recipients. Clients simply type or text their message to the company, and the pen-yielding 3D printer takes care of the rest.

"Our goal is to make it really easy to send beautiful experiences to people," says Bond founder and CEO Sonny Caberwal. While some customers might use the company’s services to send birthday cards to their moms or letters revealing their love to admired ones, most use it to distribute handwritten letters in bulk, such as in forging business relations.

The technology is incredibly impressive, there’s no denying that. But the question must be asked: is something lost in human-to-machine translation? Is a handwritten letter truly the same whether it comes from a person or a bot? Finally, a question that will bother any lover of pen and paper (like myself): what could this technology mean for the future of handwriting?
