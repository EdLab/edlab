New Learning Times - October Usage Report

Nikita.Bogdanov

November 19, 2018

The big screens near the door have recently been displaying some usage data from New Learning Times, for the fall generally and for October specifically. I've compiled this data into a more detailed report and invite you to give it a read if you're interested.

The key takeaways for me were as follows:

  1. We need to conduct a more focused study of dropping pageviews, sessions, visitors, and other basic metrics to understand and counteract the dynamics behind the dominant decline trend.
  2. It may be worthwhile to reconsider the design of article pages so as to encourage visitors to continue engaging with the site after they read the article on their landing page, especially because most of the traffic that comes to the site is referred from search engines and because most of this traffic lands on an article page rather than on the homepage. Relatedly, it may be interesting to experiment with the algorithm the curates the article selections on the homepage.
  3. While direct entry dominates referral for on-campus users, there are several underutilized referral channels for off-campus users, who arrive at the site by a variety of paths. Options include expanding the newsletter, improving SEO, and continuing to advertise on Flipboard, all of which are likely to bring more traffic to the site.

Let me know if you have any questions or other commentary.
