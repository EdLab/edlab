The Battle is Won by Apple, but the War is Not Over....

Alex Park

July 25, 2011

And..... Poof! It's gone. Amazon has taken the Kindle Store button out of its iOS Apps. If you recall, in February, Apple updated its Terms & Conditions of the Apple Store, saying that if you have a link to a store outside the app, you must have an option to purchase the content inside the app as well so that Apple could get 30% cut on the purchases. I also blogged about it earlier. Well, it seems that Apple has won the battle. The Kindle Store button is gone. So is the Barnes & Noble's store button, Kobo's store button, the WSJ's store button... It seems that this may cause more users to use the iBooks app, since it will be more convenient to buy books through that method. But the war is not over. Even now, all it takes for users to buy a Kindle book is simply type amazon.com into Mobile Safari. Also, the outlook is that this will actually hasten the transition of these e-reader giants to move towards an HTML5 solution, away from the Big Brother's watchful eyes. Even Apple doesn't have the power to control what their users use their mobile browser for... or can they? They have done more ridiculous things... The next move in this war will make this very interesting. Source: ZD Net
