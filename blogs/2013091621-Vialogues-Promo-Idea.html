Vialogues Promo Idea

Nantwi

September 16, 2013

One of the ways we've been promoting the grant projects (UFR and YoungArts) is joining and posting about them in active teacher online forums. We initially identified about 15-20 of these forums and quickly found Edmodo to be the most active and vibrant of these communities. We discovered that educators on Edmodo usually use it as a platform to request or ask for resources to better help them teach a particular topic or lesson and sometimes current events. As such, we felt this was a good platform to try a promotional experiment with Vialogues on how educators can incorporate current events into their classrooms. Brian S. and I created the Vialogue below on the conflict in Syria with guiding questions, comments and resources on how to teach the conflict and posted it in the Social Studies community on Edmodo. We spread out the amount of posts to three times on the first day: morning, afternoon and evening. (See graphic below). The 10am post certainly had more clicks, which was shocking as I figured the evening post would get more clicks as that is when educators are home and “free”. Our posts in Edmodo generated 142 total clicks over several days and several educators shared the video on their Twitter accounts. We want to continue this experiment again this week with a current event (right that is today's shooting at the Navy Yards in Washington, D.C.) and see if we can draw some comparisons and if this is an effective strategy to continue, especially in terms of user accounts and participation.
