Save CUNY

Abdulrahman Sanda

February 11, 2016

City University of New York's (CUNY) motto of "vital importance as a vehicle for the upward mobility of the disadvantaged in the City of New York" is currently being challenged by students who can't afford to enroll due tuition increase. The " Rational Tuition Plan, a law signed by Governor Cuomo, authorizes the CUNY Board of Trustees "to increase the resident undergraduate tuition rate by no more than three hundred dollars per academic year". This has affected the economic stability of students living in New York. The cost of living in New York has risen about 23% from 2009 to 2014. The rise of CUNY tuition have outpaced the income and the price of living necessities. Once upon a time, CUNY tuition was free. The rapid increase of CUNY tuition have affected college affordability among low income individuals. New York State needs to fund CUNY by increasing public investment and cover all mandatory costs. What policies or laws can be implemented to help freeze CUNY Tuition?
