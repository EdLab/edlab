China Institute

EdLab Studios

March 8, 2016


In the spirit of co-founder, John Dewey's ideology, China Institute inspires cross-cultural understanding through doing, through experience, and developing emotional connections. Opening its doors in 1926, China Institute is the oldest bicultural organization dedicated to advancing understanding of Chinese culture in the country and continues to do so today in its new downtown location. In addition to a myriad of talks and lectures, language and business courses, culinary experiences, and gallery exhibitions that run year round, the organization offers family programming including annual Chinese New Year festivities. Brimming with experiential and immersive learning opportunities directed towards the city's children, the day of celebration blends performance with hands-on workshops. Attendees participate in the meaning behind age-old traditions like the elaborate lion dance, dumpling making, and paper lanterns. The event draws in over 300 visitors from the neighborhood, hoping to rediscover their roots, educate their children, or even connect with a culture entirely outside of their own.
