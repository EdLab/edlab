"The GovLab’s mission is to improve people’s lives by changing the way we govern." (About GovLab)

Solving the World’s Problems

Climate change, mass migration, and poverty are examples of problems so mammoth in scale that no single actor can solve them. GovLab examines the ways technology can help institutions work collaboratively and openly to solve pervasive public problems.

Free Information

GovLab believes in the saving power of data. By expanding the availability of information for public institutions and providing them access tools, GovLab helps organizations use data to address problems. It builds and tests prototypes for collaboration; trains entrepreneurs to take up public interest challenges and disseminate innovative programs; and connects technologists, designers, and entrepreneurs to develop research. In short, GovLab fosters information sharing and lets the data undergird the work.

Runaway Roster of Projects

Collaboration is embedded in GovLab’s DNA, and it acts as a hub for a roster of public interest projects. For example, Data Driven Criminal Justice uses info to decrease incarceration and increase public safety. Information for Impact frees non-profit sector data. Legisletters uncovers Congressional oversight processes. NHS - Open Data improves health care, and Orgpedia fosters crowdsourcing to provide high-quality corporate data. These are a few of the partnerships proving that GovLab uses data in new ways to solve old problems plaguing the world.
