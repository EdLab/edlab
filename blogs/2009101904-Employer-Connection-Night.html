Employer Connection Night

Pranay Dharmale

October 19, 2009

I recently attended 'Employer Connection Night' organized by Center of Career Education at Columbia University. It presented the students of the CS, EE, CE departments with an opportunity to interact with employers from biggies like GE, Goldman Sachs, Reservoir, Advanced Acoustic Concepts, etc. in a Mock interview. I was interviewed by Richard Lethin, President of Reservoir Labs. He scrutinized my resume and suggested necessary changes. The recommended updates were to give an interviewer just what he/she need to see -- key technologies I know, the projects I've worked on, my learning experience, my objective(this should align with the employers need) and last but not the least varied interests besides academics that would qualify me as a "fit" employee (as Richard quoted). Moreover, I was taught to maintain a "selling-pitch" during the interview. And, the most interesting tip I got was "to be a good Listener -- let the interviewer speak". It helps us to grasp the particular key qualities that the employer is looking for. This was an overall good learning experience for me.
