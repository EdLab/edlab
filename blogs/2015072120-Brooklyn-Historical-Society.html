Brooklyn Historical Society

EdLab Studios

July 21, 2015


Spanning nearly 300 acres along the East River, the Brooklyn Navy Yard represents over 400 years of rich heritage and thriving industry. Three and a half years ago, BLDG 92 was opened as a gateway to this historical treasure; allowing the public to experience the neighborhood landmark through exhibitions, school visits, and educational programming. Since September of 2014, BLDG 92 has partnered with the Brooklyn Historical Society to bring this learning one step further through their third collaborative but first STEM-based tour: Seeing Green. The tour explores sustainable architecture and design using BLDG 92, an adaptive reuse project in itself that has achieved LEED platinum status, as a tangible template for the kinds of challenges the urban jungle presents in relation to energy consumption and storm-water management as well as the creative engineering employed to overcome them. In classic Brooklyn Historical Society style, Seeing Green helps to bridge the gap between a pre-Civil War shipyard of the past, the burgeoning environmental friendly practices of the present, and the sustainable innovations that will maintain the fragile balance between man and nature in the future.
