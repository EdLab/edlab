Ikea, Meet Amazon. Amazon, Meet Ikea

dinosossi2

July 3, 2017


We’re midway through 2017 and apparently Ikea just heard of Amazon.


Fact is stranger than fiction. And apparently the Swedish furniture behemoth had never had a presence on Amazon.


Hey, but why would they? Ikea is only selling goods in an increasingly e-commerce world. Why use the biggest e-platform in the world?!? (My apologies, of course, to Alibaba users who could make a strong counteragent…)




But it does look like Ikea is finally making its bridge into the Amazon.


Or is it?


The corporate press release making the statement, well, didn’t actually make the statement. Despite this opaque message, the underlying theme is quite clear.


Even well-established, beloved furniture vendors that go so far as to also sell the tastiest meatballs this side of Sweden (full disclosure, I LOVE their meatballs!) have to eventually give in to Amazon.


Meatballs aside, this leads to some interesting questions:


Will Ikea pay a premium to have its results trend higher in Amazon’s recommendations algorithms?

What other major proprietors will have to relent and join Amazon to sell their wares?

How will this impact current furniture vendors on Amazon, especially smaller niche players?

And most importantly, can I get those meatballs delivered to a home address?
