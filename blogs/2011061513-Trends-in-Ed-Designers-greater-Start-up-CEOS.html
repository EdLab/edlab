Trends in Ed: Designers -----> Start up CEOS

Angela Lee

June 15, 2011

] Inspired by today's seminar on One Fabrication Lab per Child I would like to talk about designers and startups in today's Trends in Ed. When talking about startups, people mostly think of software engineers or technology exports, not designers. Enrique Allen- the founder of The Designer Fund sees it differently. He explained "I realized I should be helping designers become more startup-ey, those are the people, who, if they become leaders of a company, will model that 'designery' behavior that from its inception, right in the company's DNA." He further claims "what we're hoping to do is shift the paradigm of what design is. Design encompasses systems now, not just 'making things look pretty.'" "Designers have traditionally been paid a lot of money to make what people want; meanwhile, most startups fail because they make things that people don't actually want! But designers are trained in methods of getting to these 'aha' moments about customers, products, and use cases. And that's a great opportunity for designers to make a foundational contribution in a startup venture." Umm....Merging designers and techies...sounds like an idea that should already exist, The Designer Fund is bringing the concept to be more doable.
