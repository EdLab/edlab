Gamifying Life Skills

dinosossi2

March 16, 2016


Video games are as much a part of childhood as candy bars and bubble gum, just less sticky. Like a diet with too many sweets, video games often have little substance. In educational settings, gaming enthusiasm goes to waste, floating away like a kite lost among the clouds on a windy day. Can we reel in this excitement by substituting video game content to make games a vital part of educational experiences?

Some educators think seamlessly folding interpersonal experiences within game mechanics can make social gaming the next educational frontier. Researchers at the University of Wisconsin-Madison use Crystals of Kaydor, a role-playing video game involving aliens, to develop student empathy.
