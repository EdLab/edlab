NYC Small High Schools Demand Sports

AbdulMalik

May 28, 2014

For most schools in New York City, sports remain a vital tool for the development of their students. This articlesheds more light on how New York City fails to provide adequate infrastructure and funds for small high school sports teams. Apparently, the lack of funds from the Board of Ed means there are less teams in schools or no sports programs at all. The absence of sports in schools might lead to inappropriate acts such as fighting and draw students towards street gangs.

Most of the affected high schools intend to file a complaint to the US Education Department's civil rights office, charging that the city is violating the 1964 Civil Rights Act in its allocation of funds to support high school sports. As a student athlete, I know the important role that playing high school sports played in my development as a student and young man. The same opportunities should be afforded to all students, regardless of the size of their schools.
