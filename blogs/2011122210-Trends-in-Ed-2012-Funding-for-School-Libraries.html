Trends in Ed: 2012 Funding for School Libraries

LauraCostello

December 22, 2011

The Omnibus Appropriations Bill, passed over the weekend, allocates $28.6 million in 2012 to be used towards school libraries and literacy programs. This news comes on the heels of a decision by the Department of Education in May to end its Improving Literacy Though School Libraries program in FY 2012 and is great news for school libraries that depend on federal grants for programming and materials. It's nice to see that library programs are still a priority in government fund allocation. The bill specifies that half of the funding should go to school libraries and half to literacy initiatives, but I hope that the Department of Education will pay particular attention to technology programming and information literacy in their award decisions. The now defunct Improving Literacy Though School Libraries program concentrated on up to date media resources, hardware, and internet resource maintenance and these types of services are still badly needed in underfunded school media centers.
