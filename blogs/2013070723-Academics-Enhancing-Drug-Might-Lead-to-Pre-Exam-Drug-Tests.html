Academics Enhancing Drug Might Lead to Pre Exam Drug Tests

Khalil Abubakar

July 7, 2013

Neuroscientists have suggested that students should take pre exam drug tests to test for Ritalin before they are allowed to take tests. Ritalin is a drug used to treat patients with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) and it enhances students' academic performance similar to what steroids do for the athletes. The British Psychological Society have launched an investigation and realized the demand for the drug has risen. A report also by the Academy of Medical Sciences suggested a 10 percent increase in memory can raise a student one grade band at A-levels or into a different degree class. New York Senator Chuck Schumer has called upon administrators to restrict students from obtaining the drug without a prescription. Nobody seems to know much about the drug which enhances academic performance, which can be obtained so easily from the pharmacy. In the future, I think people will find it more difficult to get drugs related to treating ADHD without a prescription and I can see pre exams drug become a real possibility in the future.
