Playing With Art In The Museum

chingfu

January 14, 2014


Museums are one of the most important informal and lifelong learning spaces open to the public. Within museums, visitors self-direct their explorations, interact with objects of interest, and gain new knowledge from carefully designed immersive experiences. Advanced technologies have made exhibitions and objects much more accessible and playable in many science and culture-themed museums. However, paintings, sculptures, and fragile vases in art museums remain largely out of touch for most visitors.

This is not the case at the Cleveland Museum of Art . At its Gallery One, visitors will find the artistic pieces accessible through the gallery's interactive software. For instance, they can make a face or strike a pose to make connections and engage with the sculptures in the museum through Sculpture Lens, a computer program that integrates facial and motion recognition technology for an innovative art viewing experience. At Global Lens, the visitors can build virtual vases with their choices of materials, techniques, and shapes and get an estimated price of their digital creations. This vase-creating activity gives them a better understanding of the historical vase trade between Asia and Europe.
