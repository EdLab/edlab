Inspiration for your bio: the deeper Personal Narrative

ZKL

May 18, 2017

A bio can be a daunting task, but the process of writing out your personal narrative may be an important part of affecting self-perception, and potential happiness.


A recent NY Times article "Writing Your Way to Happiness" enumerates the many studies that show how writing about the self can affect personal satisfaction, expectation and success. It can also illluminate truths about the self that may be helpful in making life decisions, influence self-esteem and even health.


The concept is based on the idea that we all have a personal narrative that shapes our view of the world and ourselves. But sometimes our inner voice doesn’t get it completely right. Some researchers believe that by writing and then editing our own stories, we can change our perceptions of ourselves and identify obstacles that stand in the way of better health.

Social Psychologist James Pennebaker, who has led many of these studies, believes that there is a link between our words and our deepest emotional state even when we are only using 120 characters or making a Facebook status update. His book The Secret Life of Pronouns  "traces the discovery of the links between function words and social and psychological states." 


While a personal narrative is not the same as a biography, one can influence the other. What does your bio say about your own assessment of your self? What story do you want to tell verses what is objectively true and how can you bring the two closer together?
