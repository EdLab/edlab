Explore the Internet of Yore and More with the Internet Archive

Ryan.Allen

January 14, 2018


Imagine if the Library of Alexandria had made copies of its collection and stored it in other locations. We may still have works from Homer, Aristotle, or other great thinkers that have been lost in time. With a mission to protect all of human knowledge, the Internet Archive, a San Francisco-based nonprofit, attempts to record every website on the Internet.

The organization has been running a tool since 1996 that takes a snapshot of every webpage roughly six times a year and records the results in their Wayback Machine. These records are searchable and freely available for anyone to explore. You might even be able find your now-defunct embarrassing GeoCities page. "If you type in a URL, it shows you all of the past versions, and you can surf the web as it was," says Brewster Kahle, the organization's founder.
