Big History Project

Carmen James

October 26, 2014


"Big History examines our past, explains our present, and imagines our future. It's a story about us. An idea that arose from a desire to go beyond specialized and self-contained fields of study to grasp history as a whole. This growing, multi-disciplinary approach is focused on high school students, yet designed for anyone seeking answers to the big questions about the history of our Universe." (via Big History Project site)

History as Science
What was once a college-level history class taught by David Christian (and attended by Bill Gates) has now become an online course available to high school students and teachers for free. The Big History Project looks at the question of history through the lens of science and is based on the notion that understanding humanity requires a working knowledge of the whole universe. The course is divided into seven thresholds of life on earth, starting with the big bang and ending with the modern revolution.

Curriculum Ready
The Big History Project represents the work of teachers, researchers, and scientists developing a perspective on knowledge that will support lifelong learners across the globe. The website offers an open source curriculum for teachers of grades 7-10 and encourages parents and students to explore too. There are also many extension activities to explore including videos, reading activities, and quizzes. In addition, users who pass the exam for each threshold are rewarded with a "Big Historian" sticker.

Supporters
The Big History Project emerged from the Big History Institute, led by David Christian at Macquarie University in Sydney, Australia. Working closely with Christian is Bob Bain at The University of Michigan. Bain is in charge of the curriculum design and ensures that all of the Big History Resources meet US educational standards.
