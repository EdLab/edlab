Bloomberg's Earphones Issue

Christopher Zamora

March 6, 2013

Mayor Bloomberg wants to warn teens about the effects listening to music very loud on their earphones can have on their health. In this article from the Wall Street Journal, a concerned Mayor Bloomberg warned:

The federal Centers for Disease Control and Prevention says excessive noise has caused permanent hearing damage in 1 out of 8 children and teens and about 1 in 6 adults under age 70. Problems can include hearing loss – especially trouble hearing high frequencies or following conversations in noisy situations – and tinnitus, an internal ringing, whooshing or buzzing in the ears.
He wants to advocate this campaign through social media and other forms of media marketing to warn teens about future hearing loss and have already helped raised $70,000 for this campaign. Their goal is to raise $250,000. I agree with Mayor Bloomberg about warning teens about the threat earphones pose to their hearing in later years. But I feel many enjoy listening to music in a high volume simply because they won't hear others around them, especially on crowded and loud subway cars. What are you thoughts on Mayor Bloomberg's campaign on earphones? Will it bring about any changes?
