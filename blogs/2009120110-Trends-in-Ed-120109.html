Trends in Ed, 12.01.09

Ting Yuan

December 1, 2009

Latinos and Blacks as the Major Mobile Hyperusers according to Recent Research According to a recent Pew Internet and American Life Project, about one third of American teens have gone online using a handheld device. And this trend is increasing with youths' activities in texting, emailing, listening to online music, and instant messaging. However, the research also shows that the largest ethnic groups of these hyperusers are Lations and Blacks, instead of mainstream teens (see the results below). The research results indicate students' informal and self-directed learning increasingly happen on a mobile screen. The results also offer school administrators and teachers new alternatives to consider how to improve students' academic performance and how to blend students' mobile activities with their learning experience. Memoov: An Animation Making Site Launched in September 2009, Memoov is an animation making site for children to make their own animated video stories. It offers multiple backgrounds, characters, and accessories, as well as audio recording and choices of visual and audio effects. MeMoov is a promising and motivating tool to engage children in speaking, writing, and designing. The created video can be further shared on Youtube, Facebook, MySpace, or be sent to mobile devices.
