Stanford Students Engage in Design Thinking

Laura Scheiber

February 27, 2013


Have you ever heard of the term, "design thinking"? Ever wonder what it looks like in practice? In this video, a group of Stanford University students engaged in design thinking to develop a new product for their fellow classmates.

These playful design students tackled the challenge of students who ride their bikes to class and wish to carry a cup of joe along the way. The video showcases the core principles of design thinking, including understanding a problem or unmet need from the user perspective (in this case riding a bike while carrying a cup of coffee), prototyping ideas (designing a drink holder specifically for a bike), user testing (asking students with bikes to test out their models) and iterative design based on user feedback. In the end, the students came up with a unique product that they believed addressed an unmet need of their fellow classmates. After watching their design process showcased in the video, do you think design thinking was a useful approach in their pursuit of problem solving?
