Trends in Ed 12.16.09

Jeannie Crowley

December 16, 2009

The National Institutes of Health is starting to enforce its open access policy. This means that any "any publication funded by NIH research had to be sent into the agency's archive within a year of publication". This move stems from Obama's open government initiatives and an attempt to make research paid for by public funds available to the general public.

The government is considering expanding this policy to other departments but the focus is mostly on scientific research. However, if this trend continues, it could impact the research done at universities that are funded by government grants. There is the possibility, in the future, that private institutions will no longer be able to keep their findings (if funded by public monies) hidden behind subscriptions and buried in paid-only access archives. Could this potentially have an impact on the grant-seeking behaviors or publishing habits of a university?
