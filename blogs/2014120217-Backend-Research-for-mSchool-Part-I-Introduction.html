Backend Research for mSchool. Part I: Introduction

tzaffi

December 2, 2014

****PRIVATE**** Currently, a top priority for the D & R team is bringing mSchool from its present "alpha" version to a "beta" version. In doing so, we want to ensure that the application will be performant and scalable. In plain words, mSchool is currently too darned slow and unable to handle a large number of simultaneous users. We want to design it so that every action on the website takes a blink of an eye regardless of how many users are logged in. No one should have to "twiddle their thumbs" after clicking on a teacher's module, or adding a new block to their own course, or for any reason whatsoever. We also want to choose a technology stack that allows for easy additions of new features and modifications of old ones. Over the last couple of months, we've closely analyzed mSchool's performance bottlenecks and looked at a lot of different technology options to use for mSchool. I'll be summarizing the results of this analysis in some upcoming blogs: Part I: this post Part II: Explanation of mSchool's Back-End Node.js technology Part III: Databases.
