App Inventor For Computing Education in K-12

chingfu

September 18, 2013

App Inventor is a visual programming platform for creating applications for Android-based mobile devices. Previously developed by Google, it is now maintained and developed by MIT. A couple of months ago, I messed around with this programming language to see its potential for a course I taught this past summer. I wrote a couple of simple games/apps (based on learning resources available here) and was excited to see them running on my Android mobile device. I think it is a great tool for teaching programming and computational thinking to middle and high school students. Recently I am looking for studies about App Inventor for Research Digest (RD). I ran into this paper with some preliminary findings that confirm my own experiences and observations about App Inventor. Here are some findings from this study (the design and data are not solid so I didn't pitch this article for RD). Problem-Driven Learning: "The use of blocks instead of a written programming language would enable students to getting bogged down in syntax problems." Accessible & Powerful: "Students progressed quickly from writing “Hello Kitty” to developing apps using database, interactive maps, client server communication, and other advanced concepts." Relevance/Motivational Potential: "The relevance of programming a phone, something important in students' lives, should also work well as a motivator." Another popular kid-friendly programming language Scratch has similar visual and programming platform design. Regrettably, its mobile potential has been blocked by App Store (see this story: Apple Rejects Kid-Friendly Programming App)
