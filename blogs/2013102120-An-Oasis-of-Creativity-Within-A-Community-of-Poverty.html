An Oasis of Creativity Within A Community of Poverty

Karen Bryner

October 21, 2013


To counter the elimination of the arts from the California curriculum, Inner-City Arts provides art education to inner-city Los Angeles students. Elementary and middle school students attend classes during the school day at Inner-City Arts’ creativity-inducing space, participating in music, visual arts, and drama lessons by professional artists. Developing creativity in and providing a wide range of creative experiences for students is of paramount importance to the organization. Demonstrating the importance of participation in the arts for academic achievement, long-term research shows that students participating in this program increase standardized test scores: 25% in math, 18% in reading, and 8% in English proficiency . Additionally, as stated in the video, 96% of Inner-City Arts’ students go on to college while 75% of their non-attending peers do not graduate from high school.
