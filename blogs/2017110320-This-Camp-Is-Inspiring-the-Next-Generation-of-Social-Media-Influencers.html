This Camp Is Inspiring the Next Generation of Social Media Influencers

saraehardman

November 3, 2017


Have you ever wanted to become famous on social media? For anyone who has, there are programs aimed at teaching the skills required to become the next big thing online. One such program is the 10-day immersive Creator Camp in Los Angeles that caters specifically to teens and young adults. This overnight camp teaches young people skills such as directing, lighting, marketing, and networking to prepare them to be the internetâ€™s next social media influencers.

Being a social media influencer means that you have collected a large enough viewership base to get offers from companies to feature sponsored content and advertisements. Essentially, you are using your status as a social media star to influence viewers to buy certain products. And while this might be an elusive goal, if reached, it can be a lucrative one. YouTubers with seven million followers can make as much as $300,000 per ad campaign. True, seven million is a difficult number to achieve, yet even 100,000 Instagram followers could land you $5,000 per sponsored post.

Would you send your child, or yourself, to a social media influencer camp? What might be the benefits or the negative repercussions of a camp like this? Answer these and more questions on Vialogues.
