Engaging Yet Expensive Science Education

Rebecca.Sullivan

November 12, 2018


Blue Apprentice wants kids to fall in love with science. By integrating elementary school science standards in an intergalactic space adventure, the creators hope to enhance learning and increase interest in subjects such as life science, engineering and tech, and physical science. The user begins the adventure as an isolated alien child with no knowledge of science. By exploring, and with some mentoring from a few flying squirrel friends, the user gradually gains the science skills needed to save the universe.

Pros:

Blue Apprentice gives about equal weight to education and fun in this learning adventure. In order to progress as an apprentice, the user must answer questions incorporating content from the previous level. The science content is also genuinely game-based: for instance, the user learns about solid, liquid, and gas states when space creature friends mysteriously seem to disappear as they approach a fire-witch. In the beginning levels, the game is quite prescriptive; as the user progresses, the game incorporates more free exploration. While the scientific concepts lack deep explanation, the game offers a fun supplement to classroom learning.

Cons:

While Blue Apprentice offers engaging, light learning, its content may not be worth its price. The single-user app runs about $100 a year. And while the game does include valuable science concepts, it could not serve as a stand-alone curriculum. It does, as its creators suggest, reinforce science standards in an engaging way, but it comes with a steep price tag for a review tool.

Our Takeaway:

Blue Apprentice offers educational fun, but it is a pricey luxury more than a pedagogical necessity.
