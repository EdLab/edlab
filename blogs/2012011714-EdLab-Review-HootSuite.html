EdLab Review: HootSuite

Janet Jang

January 17, 2012

HootSuite announced today that it reached its three millionth user. Hootsuite is a social media management tool that's been around since late 2008. Rather than being a social network, it allows you to connect to multiple social networks from one web dashboard. The main focus of HootSuite is to help organizations use the social web to launch marketing campaigns, identify and grow audience, and distribute targeted messages across multiple channels. Pros: The user can create teams that can collaboratively schedule updates to Twitter, Facebook, Linkedin, WordPress and other social networks via web, desktop or mobile platforms. They also create custom analytic reports that track campaign results and industry trends to help groups adjust their tactics with the rapidly changing times. On their blog, they publish case studies using real companies as examples and analyze their social media data. Cons: There are not many free version tools and the updating seemed a bit lagging and slow. It also lacks a full capability RSS reader (even though they do have an option for it). There's also not enough space on the dashboard to show more than 4 columns at the same time, so a new tab has to be created. Only one single tab is functional at a time, unlike a web browser. EdLab Relevance: As EdLab continues to increase its social media presence, it'll be a useful tool to facilitate communication and organization within this area. Maybe this can inspire us to create a social media/community managing team of our own.
