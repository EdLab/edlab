Open Video Conference

Melanie Hibbert

October 4, 2010

This past Saturday I attended the Open Video Conference in Manhattan, described as "a multi-day summit of thought leaders in business, academia, art, and activism exploring the future of online video." I only attended a fraction of the sessions, but a few take-aways include: 1. HTML5 is the direction online video is headed. 2. Several higher education institutions including Penn State, USC School of Journalism, University of Virginia, and MIT have adopted the open-source platform Kaltura as their video content management system. 3. Susan Crawford, a law professor who has worked for the FCC and on other government policy committees, describes this fall as a "tipping point" regarding net neutrality and copyright/intellectual property laws (one example includes the recent COICA act).
