Plan B Can Benefit Students' Future

Christopher Zamora

July 15, 2013

Last month, the Obama Administration gave access to emergency contraception across the country. It allowed the best known pill, Pill B One-Step, to become a prescription only to those 17 years and older that could be bought over the counter. This emergency contraception is a big help for many students who have had unprotected intercourse. Plan B is a pill that decrease the chances of pregnancy once you take the pill within a 3-5 day period. Sometimes the pill could work up to seven days but the sooner, the better. This Plan B pill helps many students continue to reach their future by preventing pregnancy. I believe with this pill being eligible to use for students of aged 17 and older is important. No one is perfect and everyone makes mistakes, I believe having a child at a young age can really make education a lot tougher knowing your going to have a bigger responsibility.
