Can You Learn To Play Guitar From A Video Game?

jvazquez

November 19, 2013


The next installment of music-based console games has arrived. At first glance, you might categorize Rocksmith with its musical predecessors, but look deeper and you’ll see how different it really is. You won’t find some plastic toy resembling a guitar in this game’s box. Instead, you are expected to plug in your real-life electric guitar (or hookup to a pickup if you have an acoustic) to start rocking out. In fact, you can learn your instrument’s fundamentals in the time it would take you to bake a frozen pizza.

Prior to beginning a session, players tune their guitars with the help of onscreen feedback. Game play may seem familiar to those who have played Guitar Hero or Rock Band; once the music starts, notes begin to fall towards you, indicating which ones you should strum or pick. As you do better, the note and chord progressions become more challenging; if the computer senses that you are struggling, it’ll ease up.

When it comes to learning the basics, would you go with the video game or the tried-and-true guitar teacher method? Does the element of "fun" weigh on that decision at all? Head over to Vialogues to add your thoughts to the ongoing conversation.
