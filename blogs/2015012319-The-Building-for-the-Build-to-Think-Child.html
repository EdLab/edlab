The Building for the Build-to-Think Child

Carmen James

January 23, 2015


Get the inside scoop on this compelling school-meets-design-lab. The Nueva School is an independent school that partners with Stanford's d.School and is making waves in the education world. Nueva School seeks to "champion the build-to-think child" according to Kim Howard-Saxe who discusses the schoolâ€™s 3500 square foot innovation lab and the ways the lab supports using tried and true methods of learning.

Kim has taught for 14 years at Nueva School in addition to teaching as a lecturer at Stanford. She has taught and built K-8 project-based curricula; she consistently relies on the methodologies of design thinking; and as director of the Design Thinking, Design Engineering, and Entrepreneurship programs at Nueva School she is constantly rethinking and re-imaging teaching, learning, and space. Among her many her projects, she has led an award winning robotics curriculum and spearheaded an engineering curriculum for students in 4th-6th grades.

While many people think of design thinking as a theory, the Nueva School sees it as a practice. Concepts like mindsets take on real meaning in the life and work of students and teachers through innovative implementation practices spearheaded by leaders like Kim.
