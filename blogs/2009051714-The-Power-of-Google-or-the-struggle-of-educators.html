The Power of Google — or the struggle of educators?

Frederik G. Pferdt

May 17, 2009

This is a video, produced in the module about human perception of new media by Derrick De Kerckhove. They describe:

We tried to imagine the future of internet, web applications and devices scenarios in the next years. To explain our theories, we had imagine a future where Google will confirm its supremacy in internet innovation, and it will be the major innovator in applications and devices, till the final human integration in technology.“

A really interesting scenario which — in my opinion — shows at least one direction the internet revolution is "heading" (literally) and the "old“ applications like messenger, Skype and Facebook and even hardware like iPhones will be improved or replaced by even more innovative tools. One of my future scenarios — (definitely not reaching 2030, but maybe the next 5-7 years) consists of a device attached to the body which shows you real live streams (like status updates on Facebook) of every person in your network and you have the chance to project every information gathered with a human-computer-interaction tool input device for multipointing interaction in 3D (like iPoint presenter or the Sixth Sense Projection Technology). The challenging and struggling question for educators remains what are learning scenarios, which should be designed and developed now, to prepare learners for such environments and what skills and competences will be relevant and needed then? Educators! Get up and let's brainstorm... Otherwise the web-education gap will increase even more!
