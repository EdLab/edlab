The Secret Art of Book Hacking Comes to Manhattan

Kate Meersschaert

March 6, 2015

(Image source) March 13-27th (opening talk March 13th) "Magic Books: The Secret Art of Book Hacking" exhibition will make it's magical appearance at the Italian Cultural Institute, 686 Park Avenue. What will this exhibition entail exactly? No-one knows, but, you can rest assured that magic, innovations in multi-media texts, medieval book art and more will be on display. From the BoingBoing.net announcement listed above:

As a matter of fact, like most technological artifacts, a book can be hacked. By altering its structure and content, magical things can happen: volumes with movable parts, pages providing cinematic experiences, images appearing and disappearing, non-linear structures to predict the future, pop-up magic shows.
EdLab Relevance: As we continue to consider our role as a world-class library and soon space for a future-focused learning theater it may be useful to examine the art and science of the most basic building block of learning, the book. Also, as we are constantly striving to "hack education" it is only fitting that we also explore hacking the book... will you attend?
