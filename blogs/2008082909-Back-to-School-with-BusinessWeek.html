Back to School with BusinessWeek

Frank Webster

August 29, 2008

In BusinessWeek's Back to School issue: Universities Try Out New Digital Devices documents the reappearance of all the usual suspects (iPods, clickers, Facebook, video, Internet2, Sakai) on prominent college and university campuses. Cloud Computing is catching on big time:

Thousands of universities are turning to Microsoft (MSFT) and Google (GOOG) to replace often clunky and outdated in-house e-mail systems.
And this reader's Comment with a vivid simile illustrating the importance of the teacher caught my attention:
Technology in the classroom is like a split rail fence. One post is hardware the other is software. Suppliers ask the teacher to walk the rail...well if we want them to be world class gymnasts buy the right equipment and get them content and training. Taping content does nothing to validate that learning occured. Another example of wasted resources on technology.
- Paul Rutledge, Aug 29, 2008 12:44 PM GMT Also noteworthy: Students Will Help Save Apple
[Despite the economic turn-down and Apple's pricey products] a recent survey by Student Monitor, a New Jersey outfit that tracks the buying habits of college students, found that 13% of all undergrads expect to buy a new notebook this fall. Of those, 43% say they plan to get a MacBook or MacBook Pro, nearly double those who said they expected to get a Dell notebook, and seven times as many as those who plan to buy from HP...[aided by] the perception that Windows Vista is a buggy mess.
