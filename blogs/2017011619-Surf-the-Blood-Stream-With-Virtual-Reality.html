Surf the Blood Stream With Virtual Reality

Jenny Abamu

January 16, 2017


"We are determined to find the best way to teach using virtual reality, and will continue to iterate on our approach as we develop more content." (About The Body VR)

Ride on the Magic School Bus

You donâ€™t need a magic school bus to travel into the human body. Now scientists are cruising through blood streams and building DNA parts thanks to virtual reality. The Body VR is making that possible through medical education, training, and entertainment virtual reality (VR) content. Their ambitious goal is to develop a full curriculum covering the human body by 2020.

Virtual Reality Transforms Education

The most exciting part of this virtual reality company is that it is both educational and fun. The entertainment portion of the app allows users to surf through the bloodstream, identify body parts, and even attack viruses in the body. In the medical education portion, the anatomy viewer allows doctors to view 3D volumetric parts in virtual reality. They'll be able to examine the body and highlight specific tissues; this is a huge upgrade from previous 2D colorless photos. Through simulations in the medical training portion, they can compare virtual body parts to traditional MRI scans and perform virtual operations.

It will take time for the Body VR to be ready to enter classrooms as the company is currently seeking partners in curriculum development and content creation. Though the VR industry is still finding its path, organizations like Body VR are doing what it takes to move the sector from entertainment to education and build effective learning tools.
