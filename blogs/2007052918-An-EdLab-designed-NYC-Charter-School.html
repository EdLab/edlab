An EdLab-designed NYC Charter School?

Neil Eckardt

May 29, 2007

Perhaps the warm weather has taken a hold of my imagination, but recently I've been thinking about the idea of an EdLab-designed NYC Charter School. This could be interesting to explore by thinking about the design of the facility (and its library), the use of technology, the theme of the school, the allocation of resources, etc. Potentially such a project might provide: (1) an internal contribution to the EdLab Reimagining Schooling project. Initial designs and actual proposals might emerge. (2) external deliverables to the growing number of charter schools in NYC. Recently I came across this announcement of the New York legislature's decision to raise the cap on public charters. New York City was guaranteed an additional 50 charters, which Chancellor Joel Klein hopes to open in the next three to five years. (3) a template for a real-world, TC-centric NYC charter school. (4) a way to position (and market) the lab at the intersection of education technology and innovative school design. I'm thinking out loud here, but I wondered if this might be a project worth talking about. I don't think charters are going away anytime soon. The support they have acquired spans the political spectrum - and for good reason, since they promote K-12 institutional freedom, while promising to be more accountable (because of market forces) than traditional public schools. Over time, I suspect that charters will provide fertile ground for emergent education technologies. Does that make them of interest to EdLab development projects? Or have the blue skies taken too strong a hold of me?
