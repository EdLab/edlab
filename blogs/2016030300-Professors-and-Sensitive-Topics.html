Professors and Sensitive Topics

Abdulrahman Sanda

March 3, 2016

Should professors shun sensitive topics in order to avoid tension within their classrooms ? A recent law passed by certain states allow students to carry concealed guns on school premises. To my understanding, a classroom is a platform where discussions are held in order to learn and explore opposing viewpoints. It's an environment for students and educators to share different ideas and perspectives. Discussions about sensitive issues should be encouraged because it allows individuals to understand the true nature of certain issues. In the op-ed, Don't Let Campus Carry Destroy Classroom Discussions, Judith McDaniel, a professor at the University of Arizona, highlights major problems faced by professors who discuss sensitive topics, especially the threat they now face because of the gun law. "Professors shouldn't shy away from sensitive topics" she says. "Teaching means taking risks and encouraging others to do the same as we search for the deeper meaning, the more elusive truths, the harder lesson." A proper understanding of this concept will allow students to further their experience within their learning environment. How can we encourage professors/educators to discuss sensitive topics without concern for their safety ?
