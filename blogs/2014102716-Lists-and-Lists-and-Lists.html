Lists and Lists and Lists

Carmen James

October 27, 2014

I am a fan of lists and advice. Aren't we all. We all must have our reasons, but for me, it's often a reminder of what I'm after. Sometimes I vociferously disagree with the lists, sometimes I wholeheartedly agree and think “I thought of all of those things before, why didn't I write it down in a list and publish it?” If the list is something we've been thinking about, it's a good way to get a refresher and renew one's project of saying writing or staying in shape. If its something we've just started or are hoping to start, the list offers a good beginning point. Sometimes I wonder, what comes after the list? In a recent post on what makes a creative person, the article professed there are 18 things that all creative people do (the lists of what make a creative person are one of many): The list got me thinking. I do many/ most of those things. Am I creative? To put it another way, just because I do those things, does that mean I am creative? To get to the heart of my question, what happens after the list? I realized I have to remember that the list cannot promise to be more than it is: a list. (Though in marketing they often want you to think it is more). In fact, a list merely some things the author were salient points on the topic, but must always be viewed as jumping off point for further discussion (with others or with oneself). I was reminded of this in a recent discussion about top changes in 2013 in Education Technology. The list was interesting, not everything, and in the end proved a provocative instigator for discussion.
