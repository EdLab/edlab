Dispensing With Literacy Problems

Rebecca Beeson

July 26, 2015


In 2015 you can find vending machines that dispense: Diet Coke, m&m's, sandwiches, sushi, cupcakes, iPhones, guitar strings, bike parts, and even salad. So wouldn't it be nice to also find a book?

Existing research confirms that increased access to books will make students more likely to read for pleasure. So corporate responsibility model Jet Blue has launched a pilot program to slap down vending machines full of books throughout Southeast Washington, DC. Whatever their motivations, the airline is hopeful that their venture will take off, and if it does it might be a model for schools, nonprofits, and other benevolent businesses. But it's too early to tell if boxes of books will be a sell for kids, especially when Doritos and Gatorade are competing for their money.
