The End of Bookstores?

gnatriello

August 13, 2010

For at least the last decade observers have been suggesting that bookstores are attractive environments that engage people and that they might offer models for libraries to emulate if they want to increase patron traffic. Now an article in the NY Times discusses the changes taking place as bookstores struggle to adjust to changing customer habits as a result of the growth of ebook sales and the decline in interest in print books. What might this mean for libraries? Does a possible decline in interest in bookstores mean a similar fate for libraries? Or will the decline in bookstores create a new opportunity for libraries to engage patrons as libraries become one of the last places where it will be possible to browse among physical books? What might this mean for our own stacks? Do you see a future where the stack tower is used for other things like the toys being moved into Barnes and Noble stores? Or might the stacks be re-invigorated as places to stay in touch with physical books? What would you like to see done to our own eight floors of book stacks? Should they be changed to house other things or should we invest in them to make the cosier places to engage physical books?
