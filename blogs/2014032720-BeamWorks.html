BeamWorks

EdLab Studios

March 27, 2014


BeamWorks is part after-school program, part apprenticeship in which high school students work with a master designer to create a large work to be employed in "real life," in the community. By transforming the space known as Beam Center into a fully functional garage workshop, students gain the experience of designing art while working with a professional artist and handling the same heavy duty tools. This particular piece is a large-scale sculpture that balances, rotates and has integrated technology representing the student artists who helped build it. BeamWorks seeks to prepare young people for a post-school world of work, and thus steps away from the abstractions of school. In pairing the students with a working artist, they get to see an example of somebody who loves their job while making a living in a non-traditional way.
