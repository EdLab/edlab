You Don’t Have to be a Genius to Begin Using Smart Paper

Jenny Abamu

December 20, 2016


Paper, the number one symbol of traditional learning, is now getting smart. The simple white paper you used to scribble down notes when your phone died can control your lights, create virtual animations, and digitally compose music. Developers at Disney are having a field day discovering the bountiful learning and entertainment applications for smart paper.

To create smart paper, you simply draw or print an antenna on the paper and attach it to an RFID sticker. These stickers use the same technology that is in a credit card chip, and they turn an ordinary piece of paper into a remote that responds to your swipes, slides, taps, or covering of the sticker. Interacting with your computer, the paper creates virtual responses to your physical actions. Disney has already taken this to the classroom with smart worksheets that allow student touch responses on paper to instantly show results on teachers’ computers. They are also working on pop-up books that trigger sound effects through the paper.

Now that you can program paper, taking notes will never be same again. What learning applications can you imagine for smart paper? Tell us about your new learning expectations in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
