AngelScholars Turns Scholarship-Hunting into a Crowdsourced Effort

Jenny Shen

July 5, 2016


"AngelScholars is a crowdfunding platform for education, created for a new generation of donors seeking a more personal engagement with students." (About AngelScholars)

The Crowdsourcing Revolution

As crowdsourcing sites like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, and GoFundMe have grown in popularity, they've also spurred more specialized platforms for raising money. AngelScholars is one such special interest crowdsourcing community, focused on providing student scholarships. Donors or angels create or fund scholarships with specific requirements for a targeted applicant pool. Students between the ages of 13 and 25 can apply for them directly through the site. One example of a currently available scholarship is Learn a Language, which is aimed toward lower-income female students to help them purchase live or online language classes.

Community-Centered Funding

AngelScholars emphasizes shared experiences over one-time payments, putting the spotlight on opportunities for community-centered funding opportunities for students of all backgrounds. Many of the posted scholarships thus far are technically one-time offers, but the organization pushes for developing an angel-scholar connection that goes beyond just the scholarship, to further enhance the scholarâ€™s educational experience.

Although AngelScholars is a new and mostly unique form of educational fundraising, it raises some questions about the existence of such platforms. For example, because concerns can arise about the legitimacy of those applying for certain scholarships, the site maintains some safeguards and regulations. Applicants are therefore required to submit verification information like W2 forms or transcripts to prove their eligibility. Furthermore, these platforms are frequently criticized for being a band-aid solution for underfunded initiatives and programs. With the rising costs of college tuition and extracurricular programs, perhaps putting the onus on crowdfunding ventures rather than educational policy may be shifting the spotlight in an unproductive way. Despite these potential issues, AngelScholars offers a well-intentioned funding option for students all over the country.
