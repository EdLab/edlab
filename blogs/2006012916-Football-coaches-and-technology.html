Football coaches & technology

Jeff Frank

January 29, 2006

The cover story of the New York Times Sports Sunday reminded me that high school and college football coaches — unlike some other teachers in schools and colleges — are not afraid of new technology. XOS recently rolled out a virtual reality platform for football coaches. The University of Michigan and Arizona State already use similar technology. In addition, many high school coaches are taking advantage of a handheld digital scout . This is just one of the many new technologies that will soon be in many high schools.
