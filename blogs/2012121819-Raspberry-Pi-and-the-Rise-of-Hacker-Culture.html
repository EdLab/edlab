Raspberry Pi and the Rise of Hacker Culture

jvazquez

December 18, 2012


The Raspberry Pi is a tiny computer with a virtuous goal: to help kids learn how to program. So many young people are adept at using technology, but so few understand what makes their beloved gadgets tick. The Pi seeks to inspire kids to put down their devices and open them up to discover the magic behind computer science.

Perhaps "magic" is a bit much, but with many K-12 schools lacking robust IT classes, it is not too far off to imagine a generation bereft of technological skills. To prevent this from happening, there are those who propose introducing pRogramming as the fourth ‘R’ to the reading, writing, and arithmetic trio. Proponents argue that learning programming goes beyond coding; it also allows students to practice creative problem solving, logical thinking, and, often times, perseverance in the face of failure.

The Raspberry Pi is poised to disrupt how and when young people learn computer programming. It promises to spark interest in an ever expanding field by allowing students to easily see and manipulate the inner workings of a device previously seen as mysterious.
