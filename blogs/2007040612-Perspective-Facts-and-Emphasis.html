Perspective, Facts and Emphasis

Jeff Frank

April 6, 2007

Henry Nau has written an insightful article about Foreign Policy that reminded me of the seminar that Neil gave last week. I tried to read Nau's article through the lens of education and not Foreign Policy, and I believe that his insights still stand. In education, “You are dealing not just with natural facts, such as disasters and disease, but also with social facts such as human beings who change their minds and behave creatively.” As this is the case, we simplify experience; we work with models that help us make sense of a particular aspect of a realty that we cannot comprehend in its totality. Of this process of simplification and selection Nau writes, “Are these positions just partisan? Possibly, but I'd wager they are also the product of different perspectives about what causes things to happen in international affairs.” In all, I found this an engaging article that develops many of the points made in the seminar and subsequent discussion.
