Exposing New Learning Times Through RSS (Really Simple Syndication) Feeds

James Manlapid

June 18, 2013

During the D&R seminar today, Hui Soo said that only 12 or so websites link New Learning Times. One of the easiest ways for articles on the web to gain exposure is through RSS feeds.

RSS feeds benefit publishers by letting them syndicate content automatically. A standardized XML file format allows the information to be published once and viewed by many different programs. They benefit readers who want to subscribe to timely updates from favorite websites or to aggregate feeds from many sites into one place.
For example, a top 10 mobile news app called Flipboard publishes content almost entirely on RSS feeds. Websites that publish news articles apply their feed on Flipboard. Once a site publishes an article, Flipboard in turn publishes it as well. Why should we be interested in RSS feeds? Statistics from this article states that:
In May 2011, Flipboard CEO Mike McCue said the app's users were generating more than 8 million “flips” – something approximating pageviews – per day, and as of April 2013, Flipboard reported 6 billion monthly “flips” and 53 million total users. Its iPhone app grew even faster, generating 1 million downloads in its first week in December 2011.
A "flip" is basically a swipe on a mobile screen that retrieves another random article. At 8 million flips a day, a New Learning Times article can appear thousands of times! We don't have to stop there, we can publish for Google news, Yahoo news and any other big name. There are many other publishing websites that utilize feeds.
