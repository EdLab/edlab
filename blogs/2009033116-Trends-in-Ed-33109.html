Trends in Ed, 3.31.09

jvazquez

March 31, 2009

Want to talk ethics? Game on. Teachers College student Karen Schrier and professor Charles K. Kinzer have attempted to teach ethics in the classroom using a digital game. Their article, "Using Digital Games to Develop Ethical Teachers," was published in the book, Digital Simulations for Improving Education: Learning Through Artificial Teaching Environments. In the article, the authors suggest other ways in which future games and simulations can teach educators about ethics. Schrier and Kinzer hope to see ethics being taught to educators via meaningful learning environments. Story from this post Richard Branson: ur elevator pichez. give me dem. Billionaire genius? I think a true genius dances dangerously close to the “crazy” line. Enter: Richard Branson. He's slated to start a new production, PitchTV, that will air on his Virgin Atlantic flights. It will feature the video elevator pitches of entrepreneurs who are promoting their businesses. Users will vote on which pitch they like the best and the top five will be shown on the in-flight entertainment system. Viewers will be able to get in contact with the pitchers and Branson promises that, each year, he will give a “special prize” to his favorite idea. Indirect networking.
