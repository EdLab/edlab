For Manav

Nara Kasbergen

November 1, 2012

At lunch, Sharath tried to sum up his impressions of my country, Holland, and he basically boiled it down to a few words: football (soccer), orange, bicycles, and tall people. Then Manav challenged me to come up with 5 words to describe America. I couldn't come up with them off the bat, so he encouraged me to think on it and blog about it. Well, challenge accepted! Here are the five I came up with: * corpocracy (okay, clearly I have been reading Cloud Atlas, but I think it's very relevant -- most of the power in this country is in the hands of corporations, and money and politics are inseparable, and consumerism is a cornerstone of society) * Christianity (you might argue for freedom of religion and yes, we do allow people to practice all faiths, but the country is still founded on and continues to operate under strongly Christian values) * bigness (my very erudite AP US History teacher loved to use this word and I think it applies well, because of the physical size of the country, the sense of cultural superiority, and everyone always wanting "bigger and better") * diversity (mainly in the ethnic/cultural sense, but also regional, ideological, etc) * sports (Manav suggested football is still relevant, but I'd argue more for sports in general; there do seem to be regional differences in allegiance to specific sports, but much of life does revolve around the sports 'cycle', a la http://xkcd.com/1107/)
