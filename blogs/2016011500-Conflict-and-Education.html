Conflict and Education

Abdulrahman Sanda

January 15, 2016

For many developing nations, internal conflict has stopped economic growth. It also prohibits children from attending school, which stops the very future of the nation from further development. When a country is mired in war and chaos, obtaining an education wouldn't be an incentive. A report by UNICEF states that "roughly 109 million children of primary and secondary school age in these [conflict] countries, almost one in four, are not getting an education". This leads to a wide range of issues. It greatly reflects how childhood education during war is regarded as a long term crisis that must be purged. How can we support children's education during war time?
