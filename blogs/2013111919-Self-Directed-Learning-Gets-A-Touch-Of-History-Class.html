Self-Directed Learning Gets A Touch Of [History] Class

jvazquez

November 19, 2013


Middle school history teacher Mary Perrine is a self-proclaimed hater of textbooks. So, she applied for and received a grant that gives her students more control over their own learning; they are able to choose what and how they want to learn. After Mrs. Perrine introduces a topic, she works with her students to identify learning targets. Her students then dig deeper into the subject to find an area of interest for a project. She calls these "Power Projects."

The response from parents has been overwhelmingly positive. Says Mrs. Perrine, "Every parent I have talked to has said ‘This is the best thing. Kids love school for the first time.’" Most importantly, the feedback from students has been inspiring. In fact, Mrs. Perrine had students switching into her class just so they could participate in this unique learning experience. Perhaps most satisfying of all is the fact that students put forth extra effort on their projects, working on them through the night.
