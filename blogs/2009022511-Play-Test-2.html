Play Test 2

zhou

February 25, 2009

To respond to Hui Soo's question in Jessica Mezei's previous post: We selected five games from the list we have analyzed for people to play and analyze. They are not necessarily "good" games for learning. But in our opinions they are typical to represent the population of existing “educational games”. Some of the games are very simple while others are comparatively sophisticated. Some involve science while others involve humanity. We hope by playing and analyzing one particular game then hearing others' analyses of other games, our colleagues will have an impression on the status quo of current educational game design. Combining Jessica Hammer's talk with the game analysis experience, people will also have deeper understanding on educational game design and analysis. In addition, our game research group will compare the colleagues' opinions with the results we got form our analysis on those games. The second game to be looked at is called Waste of Space which tries to teach space physics in the form of a single-player 2-D aircraft shooting game. The game simulates the physical conditions in the outer space where no gravity or friction exists. The player must go through all stages and eliminate all enemies while trying to dodge enemies' projectiles and suicidal spaceships.
