Widbook

Demetri Lales

June 30, 2013


Widbook is an online platform that allows aspiring authors to write ebooks and collaborate with other writers. Authors can invite other users to co-author or request to collaborate on any ebook project in the system. Collaborations can be something as small as adding an extra paragraph or writing several chapters to the story. Widbook is a highly social writing platform. Users can follow each other, "favorite" a book, share an ebook on social networks like Twitter or Facebook, and integrate multimedia like photos and videos into their ebooks.

Pros:

Through social media and Widbook’s social elements, writers can inform other users that they have written a new chapter. Users have many options to customize their ebook including designing the cover and integrating multimedia. The platform also makes it easy for users to upload a story that they have written outside of the site. Users can also follow each other, get updates on the activities of their network, and leave comments on ebooks through Widbook’s social network.

Cons:

The platform gives students a space to create written work and collaborate with each other, but it does not provide learning tips or writing instruction. If used in the classroom, it would be up to teachers to correct any grammar errors and intervene if necessary.

Our Takeaway:

Literature educators could use Widbook with their students to bring collaboration to the writing process. Student authors are able to flex their creative muscles to construct an ebook that can include text, images, and videos. Writing together could encourage peer learning in the writing process. Many companies require their employees to collaborate on written projects like contracts and proposals. Widbook gives teachers a platform to teach this collaborative writing strategy. Collaborative writing encourages students to learn new ways of writing from one another. Widbook makes story writing social.

The Bottom Line:

Widbook allows students to write together.
