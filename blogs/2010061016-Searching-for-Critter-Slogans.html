Searching for Critter Slogans

Max Sklar

June 10, 2010

Hi everyone, this is my first week at EdLab and my first blog post. I've spent a little bit of time looking at Critter and trying to get a perspective on what it does from an outsider's point of view. I think that Critter can be applied to a wide variety of projects, and I hope that we can come up with a slogan that evokes these uses and personalizes it for someone being introduced to Critter for the first time. As opposed to existing video systems, Critters helps manage the noise of free-for-all text posts. On Critter, you can effectively annotate a video and have a valuable discussion about each timestamped moment. It can used for labeling chapters in a video or presentation, but it can also be used for more compelling exercises. Maybe we'd want to look at every point a certain character or historical figure appears, or every time a certain catch phrase is used. Clues in a murder mystery or symbols in a Hitchcock film can be identified. Students can ask questions about certain parts of a video (whether it's instructional or fictional), and the next person can see the answers in real time. If you want to supplement a video with your own knowledge, or argue against it's point of view, Critter can be used to place your content side-by-side with the original video. All off these examples point to Critter as a tool for collaboration, conversation, and critique. We're looking for a slogan that fits this and gives people an intuitive sense of what to do with it. If you have anything, please post it below. Also check out this promo created last year.
