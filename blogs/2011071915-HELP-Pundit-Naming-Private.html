HELP! Pundit Naming (Private)

Kate Meersschaert

July 19, 2011

(Keep Private!) As many of you may know, Pundit, the EdLab's innovative entry into the world of course recommendation, will launch v 1.0 on August 15th. With the launch date rapidly approaching we need to make sure that the name name fits the tool's functionality & the EdLab mission. Please let me know if you feel that Pundit is a good name for this product, & if not, please provide your best try at an alternative! What is the first thing you think of when you hear the word "Pundit"? Let's unite our creative naming minds! Speaking of minds... Keep in mind that Pundit is pulling profile information from EdNode and that in the future, the tool may eventually be featured under one big mSchool umbrella as one of many in an "mSchool" suite of tools. For example, we would have mSchool: Vialogues, mSchool: EdNode etc. Here is the first paragraph of Pundit's Wikipedia entry: A pundit is someone who offers to mass media his or her opinion or commentary on a particular subject area (most typically political analysis, the social sciences or sport) on which they are "knowledgeable." The term has been increasingly applied to popular media personalities.[1] In certain cases, it may be used in a derogatory manner as well, as the political equivalent of "ideologue." In contrast, here is the Webster's Dictionary definition: 1 an expert in a particular subject or field who is frequently called on to give opinions about it to the public : a globe-trotting financial pundit.
