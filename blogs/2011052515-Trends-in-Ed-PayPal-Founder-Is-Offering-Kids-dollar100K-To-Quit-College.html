Trends in Ed: PayPal Founder Is Offering Kids $100K To Quit College

Angela Lee

May 25, 2011

We all know the stories of former college dropouts such as Mark Zuckerberg , Steve Jobs , and Bill Gates, all of whom have created successful business ventures. Who is next? Paypal founder Peter Thiel is hoping to answer that as he is offering students $100k to quit college. He wants these students "getting two years of mentoring from a network of tech and entrepreneurial experts and $100,000 to start a business." I just don't to want to report this article in today's Trends in Ed , but also ponder on what will eventually happen to the "book smart" community. It's hard to ignore the changing paradigms in education. How will these changes affect communities such as the Asian-American community, who have been known to religiously cram in order to score high on exams such as the SATs in order to get into the Ivy League colleges? I am mentioning this because earlier today I read an interesting article which spoke heavily about Asian Americans' affiliations with America's elite schools.
