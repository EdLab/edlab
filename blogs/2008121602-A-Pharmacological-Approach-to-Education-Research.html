A Pharmacological Approach to Education Research

HSC

December 16, 2008

This piece in Esquire provides an interesting overview of the Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab (J-PAL) at MIT. The lab's purpose is to "apply the tools of pharmacology--randomized testing and double-blind trials--‚Äù shape education policy. Randomized trials are difficult to conduct and not always possible. But the projects described on the J-PAL website show that is possible. For example, check out the findings from the study, "Peer Effects, Diversity, and College Roommates." The article got me to think about randomized trials we might do in the Teachers College context. For example, what might we do with Survey Sidekick once the beta version is ready? What kinds of experiments might we organize around the student tutoring network that Pravin has discussed? Is there something we might do with the Educational video sharing + content management system tool that Zhou has proposed? I can imagine others around DocDel, TCR, After Ed content, and even the new TC Portal Anthony has blogged about. Are there others people can think of?
