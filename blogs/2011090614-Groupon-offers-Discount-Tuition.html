Groupon offers Discount Tuition

Kate Meersschaert

September 6, 2011

For the past few years Groupon has become the go-to group-buying power behind cheap sushi deals, discount yoga classes and now... graduate-level courses?! Today, Groupon is debuting a deal that is the first of its kind, in which a private educational institution, in this case National Louis University in Chicago, will offer an almost 60% discount ($2,232 to $950) for a single, graduate-level intro to education course through the site. Julie Mossler, Groupon's Communications director describes the ground-breaking deal in this Mashable article, “National Louis joins an innovative list of merchants who were the first in their industry to experiment with collective buying power, and do it on Groupon.” Mashable goes-on to point-out that this new initiative was created to generate interest in a graduate-level teaching program. There is no mention of acceptance into the program as a pre-requisite for purchase, instead only a bachelor's degree and a Groupon account are required... and a willingness to spend almost a thousand dollars to try-out a new field, course and university. Would you buy a course on Groupon? Do you think Teacher's College should consider offering students a discount to try one of their courses through a site like Groupon? As EdLab continues to explore EdFluences and mSchool course development should we consider Groupon if we decide to charge for content? Perhaps for PocketKnowledge access? Do you feel this is a "desperate" move to gain new prospective students, or a promising sign of collective buying power in the Ed space? What other Ed-related services could be offered? Text-books? Tutoring?
