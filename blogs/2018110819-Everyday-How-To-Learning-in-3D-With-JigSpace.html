Everyday How-To Learning in 3D With JigSpace

Porsha.Monroe

November 8, 2018


JigSpace, an Australia-based software company, developed a self-titled iOS app designed to allow the sharing of knowledge using 3D augmented reality. The company believes that while we learn how to do most things in 2D, we actually learn better in 3D. The JigSpace app democratizes learning, allowing anyone to create and share interactive 3D instructions for just about anything.

Pros:

Users can explore 3D Jigs for categories ranging from machinery to general science, to history, to how-to tutorials. While the content seems to be geared toward late teens and adults, topics are broad enough to attract a wide age range of learners. Upon opening the JigSpace app, selecting a topic, and focusing the phone camera on a flat surface, a three-dimensional object appears along with a step-by-step explanatory guide. Users can interact with the object, rotating it, zooming in and out as needed, and moving through the guided steps at their own pace.

Cons:

While the topic range varies widely, the biggest drawback for JigSpace is the limited number of Jigs currently available to users. The idea of creating 3D instructions for anything and everything is appealing, however, it can be difficult to produce enough instructional experiences to create a comprehensive library of topics. JigSpace developers plan to release a Jig Workshop function that will allow more users to easily create and share Jigs, which will likely increase the number of Jigs available in the JigSpace library.

Our Takeaway:

For a tool that promises a valuable AR educational experience for users, JigSpace fulfills expectations. The depth and breadth of available content still has some way to go, but JigSpace makes on-the-go learning engaging and accessible while providing some practical ways to increase usersâ€™ general knowledge.
