Measuring User Engagement

Sharon Hsiao

August 5, 2013

The traditional way to gauge user engagement is to measure the click-through and the time spent on the site. This is the same story for any online educational system. We look at how many activities students have participated, attempted to do, problems solved and how much time they spent on the system. Yahoo! Labs researcher Mounia Lalmas studies user engagement and shares several interesting results in her findings. I found this very fascinating: an alternative user engagement measurement - absence time!

Assumption: if users find a site interesting, engaging or useful, they will return to it sooner. Returning relevant results is important, but is not the only criterion to keep users engaged with the search application. (Clicking lower in the ranking suggests a more careful choice from the user, while clicking at the bottom is a sign that the overall ranking is of low quality.) More clicks is not always good, but no click is bad. (results suggest that on average, clicks after the fifth one reflect a poorer user experience; users cannot find the information they are looking for) Clicking fast on a result is a good sign.
My takeaways As we improve the search function for our products, we should not neglect that 1) search isn't everything. We do seek information by so many other sources: social networks, social media, and exploration, etc., 2) more clicks is not necessarily good! It only counts when users click them right!
