Trends in Ed, 5.5.09

jvazquez

May 5, 2009

Happy Cinco de Mayo! World's largest margarita, anyone? Moving on. From one thing to another... From “The Internet of Things” — the wireless network of connected objects–comes Pachube (pronounced “Patch bay”–I know, it feels wrong to me, too), a service that lets you tag real time sensor data from objects, devices, buildings, and environments. Pachube's website has a multitude of suggestions for use, including connecting home-automation devices, collecting sensor data of your electricity output (by connecting to your electricity meter), creating a game in which players react to changes in the physical word while playing virtually, and using the sensor data to collect geographic-specific info to inform the social researcher of potential areas to recruit in or study further.
