Teaching Toddlers Through the Screen

Sarah van den Berg

January 11, 2016


Research shows that toddlers learn better from a real person than a video. But what might the difference be when there is a real, live person in the video?

This research team video chatted with toddlers to teach them new things and followed up with an assessment on what they learned. Empirical evidence shows that toddlers can learn from a real person interacting with them through video chat, unlike watching passive media. Beyond being adorable, this has implications for increasing access to meaningful educational experiences for our youngest learners, such as those in remote areas.

Have you video chatted with a toddler? Are you an early elementary teacher with ideas for how video chats can be used with toddlers in preschools? Tell us about your experiences in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
