Blending Teacher Education

Ting Yuan

April 14, 2015


Are web-based modules effective for increasing pre-service teachers’ knowledge and competence? Do participants successfully meet online learning objectives in online teacher education courses? Do teachers like web-based modules better or think they are more effective than traditional classroom instruction? A recent mixed-methods study examined these questions using effectiveness data and opinion surveys from a blended face-to-face early childhood teacher education course in a higher education setting.

There were 19 undergraduates enrolled in the course which featured research-based intervention practices in inclusive settings. Three web modules were selected to blend with face-to-face instruction, representing research on effective professional development regarding embedded intervention, assistive technology, and tiered instruction. Each module was implemented with both in-class instruction and after-class voluntary participation. Module-related online and offline activities included exploration of video and audio content, written activities completed in and outside of class, and small and whole group discussions in class.

The study revealed that students completed 64-85% of the standard written activities required online. Students deemed the web-based modules effective for increasing knowledge and competence, especially for their understanding of specific evidence-based teaching practices. In addition, students found videos related to real-life practice examples and dilemmas to be the most useful resources in their blended learning experiences.

The findings of the study provide a unique perspective for understanding pre-service teachers’ learning from practice in action. Although they may not be able to serve as a substitute for quality face-to-face practicum placements, web modules embedded with videos that convey stories of teaching and family involvement may help pre-service teachers enhance their field experiences and further understand children and families by learning from various real-life lessons.

Hollingsworth, H. L., & Lim, C-I. (2014). Instruction via web-based modules in early childhood personnel preparation: A mixed-methods study of effectiveness and learner perspectives. Early Childhood Education Journal, 43(2), 77-88.
