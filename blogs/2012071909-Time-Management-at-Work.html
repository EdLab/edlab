Time Management at Work

Oumar Soumahoro

July 19, 2012

I came across this article on Forbes.com that notes that certain employees not only waste their time at work, but their employers' money as well. According to the article, employees spend more time socializing than working. According to the survey ran to determine the breakdown of workers' timing in their daily jobs, employees spend a majority of their time either socializing on Facebook, Twitter and shopping on sites like Amazon. To maximize the productivity of their company, the article also discussed numerous ways to eliminate this problem, which seems rampant at most work places. It suggested for companies to keep track of every computer activity online, as the Internet was defined as being the major cause of workplace slacking. Though I agree with most of the recommendations to curb workplace slacking, it seems to me that the question of privacy was rejected. What happen to individual's privacy if their online activities are being followed every minute of the day? How do you think companies should balance their goals and workplace culture while ensuring the privacy of their employees?
