UMass Amherst Has Team Spirit

jvazquez

November 19, 2013


At UMass Amherst, active learning is a key pedagogical concept. Several newly built classrooms on the campus boast amenities that support team-based learning (TBL). The rooms are equipped with circular tables that seat a maximum of 9 students and have up to 3 such tables per room. Each table has an enough power outlets for all team members and its own large monitor to aid in group work and class share-outs. Surrounding the class are all-you-can-draw whiteboards.

Students like their new classroom setup because it allows them to have more hands-on experience with the material. Professors like it because they are able to interact with small groups of students, as opposed to 30 individual students.

The university plans to roll out several more TBL-friendly classrooms. Given the attributes already included, are there any changes you would make to subsequent rooms? Smaller tables? Different seating? Is all that tech really necessary? Add your thoughts to the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
