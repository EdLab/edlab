EdLab Educational Workshops?

Dan Mallinger

December 13, 2005

There's a fantastic format for academic collaboration in the Dahlem Workshops. I wonder if a similar setup can't be designed for education which takes advantage of electronic resources to link geographically distinct individuals? It'd be great to see educational research grow from this kind of collaboration: In keeping with the overall goal of Dahlem Konferenzen – to promote an interdisciplinary exchange of ideas and cooperation in research among international scientists – the Dahlem Workshop Report series was established to disseminate the results and ideas of each workshop to the scientific community at large. The series differs from other conference proceedings in several ways. Problem-oriented themes of the workshop as well as state-of-the-art summaries on the key topics are presented, with an emphasis given to the perceptions, open questions, and existing controversies in the field. Perspectives taken at the workshop are distilled in the group reports, where the identification of future research directions are highlighted. An extensive review and editorial procedure ensures a cohesive volume. There's a list of publications from the workshop listed if you scroll down the page. I'm still finishing up "Genetic and Cultural Evolution of Cooperation" but it's very unique and innovative.
