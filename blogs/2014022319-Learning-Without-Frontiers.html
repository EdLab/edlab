Learning Without Frontiers

Kate Meersschaert

February 23, 2014


"Learning without Frontiers is the Davos of the global education industry not because it attracts the leaders of today, but because it attracts the leaders of tomorrow." (Quote from the Learning without Frontiers site)

Fostering a New Learning Frontier
Founded in 2004, Learning without Frontiers is a UK-based multidisciplinary, international group with an open platform dedicated to learning innovation. More than just a working group, summit or conference, Learning Without Frontiers is a global convener of big thinkers in the learning landscape. From Sir Ken Robinson to leaders in the technology world, the group is a melting-pot of backgrounds and ideologies, all focused on helping to create a learning revolution. Through an assortment (game-based learning, hand-held devices etc.) of virtual communities paired with in-person conferences and "talks," Learning without Frontiers is able to operate as a conduit for change. The founder of Learning without Frontiers, Graham Brown-Martin (Martin is now at EdLabs UK), explains the group’s function within the larger learning landscape (see full interview in this this vialogue), "Our role in this is to bring-together thinkers from across the spectrum… bring in new ideas from different fields that are related to learning." He goes on to say, "This is not a traditional conference about education as it is today, but a conference about learning as it is tomorrow."

Helping set the Digital Learning Agenda
In addition to provocative "conversation starters" like Robinson, Learning without Frontiers has also attracted innovative corporate partners ranging from NESTA to Nintendo. Regardless of background and mission, these partners help to move the group further towards their goal setting the agenda regarding how and why mobile technologies, gaming, social media, open source development and more are used in a learning context. It is this ability to support a "vital dialogue" around digital learning that just might be Learning without Frontiers’ most lasting contribution. Since 2011, Learning without Frontiers has been a part of the Closer Still Media group. Closer Still hosts massive educational technology conferences (among other verticals) in Great Britain and throughout Europe. Through this partnership, Learning without Frontiers aims to reach an even larger audience and become an even bigger part of the guiding vision of educators who embrace the outer edges of the digital learning frontier.
