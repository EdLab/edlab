Job Skills That Are Always In Demand

Jeffrey Hado

August 5, 2011

Workers will find it much easier switching career paths with certain skills that are always in demand. "Everyone needs to be open to the idea of updating their skills through classes or getting a degree," says Andrea Kay, a Cincinnati-based career expert. A good start before you pursue a degree will be to have these vital transferable skills. #1 Organization Skills - Staying focused while doing multiple tasks - Neatly arranging and organizing stuff on your desk - Putting everything in place, even during the busiest part of the day You may be more ready than you think to switch to a career that values organizational skills. When tackling administrative and clinical tasks as a medical assistant these skills might come in handy, as well as when working in a doctor's office or assisting a physicians during exams. Attaining a certificate or a degree in medical assistance is a great step. The average earnings are around $26, 760 #2 People Skills - Enjoy meeting new people - Able to start off a conversation with anybody Being able to work well with different people and having good communication skills might get you a position helping companies recruit and attain the best and brightest workers. A bachelor's degree in human resource or business administration would be a good first step. The average earnings are $57, 830 # 3 Creative Skills - Brainstorming - Being able to see potential where others see problems Coming up with new ideas is a big part of graphic design. Having a sense of style or graphic design with new computer software will benefit a person who is willing to switch into this career. Getting a bachelor's degree is often required. The average earnings are around $48,140
