Tech & the Future of Higher Ed

Anthony Cocciolo

February 26, 2008

In this article (via CUNI login or non-CUNI login (requires EDUCAUSE account)), Franke describes three visions of the future, and what its impact could be on higher education. The first is the Friedman version, which is the pro-globalization position that most people are familiar with. The second version is the Heinberg version, where the world runs out of oil and we move into the digital dark age. In this scenario, "the survivors' grandchildren are entertained by stores of a great civilization of the recent past in which people flew in metal birds and got everything they wanted by pressing buttons". The last scenario is the Kurzweil version, which features high-technology and rapid change . Which do you think is the scenario that higher ed should be most concerned with? I tend to take the Friedman position, which the author notes is the most conservative of the three.
