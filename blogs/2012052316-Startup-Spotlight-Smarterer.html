Startup Spotlight: Smarterer

Kate Meersschaert

May 23, 2012

Details & Back-story: Boston-based Smarterer combines crowd-sourced test questions, the same scoring system used for NCAA tourney "trees" (Glicko Ratings) and robust badge-based credentialing. Smarterer tests are user-created, user-rated, and focused on the acquisition of digital, social, and technical skills. For example, you can take tests and receive badges that call out your Excel acumen, Powerpoint prowess and even, on the social front, your Foursquare finesse. Started by a group of three veteran entrepreneurs including Dave Balter, Jennifer Fremont-Smith and Michael Kowalchik, the platform, which launched in 2010, remains in Beta and, as of last month, over 5 Million questions were answered. Unique Points of Difference: The platform provides a set of tools for the development of unique, embeddable test-based widgets. This not only allows Smarterer to live within a company's native ecosystem, but it provides a built-in promotional element for the group. EdLab Relevance: Testing and gamification in the online space is a hot topic here at the EdLab as we develop and test new ways to engage learners. Will the badge economy help invigorate and incentivize learning, or are badges just a cheap, meaningless credential? This is a question we endeavor to explore further... Our Takeaway: Smarterer is a smart way to gain and test technical and social skills that will help you to thrive in an increasingly digital world.
