Will Siri Forever Change Natural-Language-based Tech?

Kate Meersschaert

October 10, 2011

Siri, the natural-language-based voice recognition software developed by Apple will be launched in the company's long-awaited iPhone 4S. Shannon Cross, a research analyst, is quoted in today's AllThingsD.com post describing why Siri is so game-changing to the tech field: We believe the use of natural language and potentially the ability to distinguish between voices could one day change the way we interact with electronic devices and provide a substantial technology advantage to Apple. Quite simply, we have not seen a demonstration of comparable AI in any other consumer system. What is the relevance for education? Siri, like many other field-changing Apple innovations (iPod, iPad, App Store to name a few), may allow educators to experience a truly "smart" classroom experience. Imagine a Smart Board that, instead of relying on complicated controls was guided by an instructor's voice. Similarly, voice-enabled, tablets (Siri is rumored to make-it onto the iPad 3) could assist learners who are other-abled or possibly aid in language acquisition programs. Imagine, learning a new language with a smart "assistant" built-into your phone or tablet. What do you think this new technology will allow the educational technology of the future to accomplish? Why is this significant and different from existent voice recognition software?
