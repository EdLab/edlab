Can video games really keep children fit?

Daniel Vergara

March 10, 2011

This article shows that Wii fit games and most active games tend to achieve the same amount of physical fatigue as outdoor activities. Have we reached the stage where children will rather be home playing video games than outside with their friends? Though, it seems as if both types of activities offer their share of physical rewards, I prefer outdoor activities as it offers both a physical reward as well as a social one. Spending the majority of your leisure time at home playing Wii or other video games can lead to social isolation, which often comes with its fair share of psychological and emotional problems. Though there is growing interest and great potential in educational games, (e.g. Budget Hero, which I found rather useful), I wouldn't want to spend the bulk of my time at home playing it or any other game. There needs to be a right balance between how much time to devote to games at home and engaging in outdoor activities.
