The Final Frontier in 2018

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2018


These five vialogues document new discoveries and technological developments from 2018 that point to where the field of space exploration is headed.

1. NASA's Newest Space Station Robots Were Inspired by Star Wars
NASA’s new robots designed to work aboard the International Space Station were inspired by sci-fi. These Astrobees have been equipped with tiny fans that allow them to whiz around zero-gravity environments, assisting with research, ship diagnostics, and even basic housekeeping.

2. NASA Uses Old Data to Reveal New Secrets of Europa
In the 1990s, the Galileo satellite explored the Jupiter system, obtaining data with its camera and other sensors. Through this mission, scientists confirmed that the moon held vast oceans under its icy surface, but there were other readings that couldn’t be understood until now.

3. Mini Satellites are the Future of Deep Space Exploration
When thinking of NASA, most people envision the organization’s iconic spacecraft, giant machines responsible for transporting astronauts and collecting important data. However, one of their latest creations for the Mars mission proves that downsizing also has its advantages.

4. NASA Hopes to Find Martians in 2020
NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory is gearing up for a new mission to Mars in 2020. Using knowledge and parts from a previous mission, this next rover will focus on areas that scientists suspect could have once supported life.

5. The New Space Race Is a Battle of Billionaires
The old Space Race pitted the United States against the Soviet Union in a battle rooted in military supremacy. The new Space Race is guided by profit, as billionaires like Elon Musk and Jeff Bezos have taken the lead from NASA and are competing for the lucrative captain’s chair in the exploration of the final frontier.
