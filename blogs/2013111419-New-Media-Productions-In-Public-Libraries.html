New Media Productions In Public Libraries

chingfu

November 14, 2013


What is a library for you? A place to borrow books? A room for quiet reading? Or an information center where you can find out the latest social events in your community? As technologies continue to digitize our intellectual and social lives, libraries face challenges to create new learning and social experiences. Many libraries have started to provide digital services such as lending e-books or e-readers to their patrons as a response to the digital movement.

While most of the innovations of library services focus on new information consuming experiences, some libraries take a different approach. YOUmedia is an innovative space that helps urban youth learn new media production at five Chicago Public Library locations. Its design is based on three new social and learning approaches -- hanging out, messing around, and geeking out-- that were documented in Ito et. al's (2008) ethnographic studies . At YOUmedia centers, local teens can exercise their technical curiosities through digital projects or pursue more professional skills with cutting-edge software, all while being assisted by program mentors. Currently the program provides a variety of experiences, including: graphic design classes, maker labs, animation and video production studios, and a library of games.
