Trends in Ed, 4.27.09

jvazquez

April 27, 2009

E-Learning's metamorphosis into social learning While flashy interactive multimedia courseware is entertaining, some educators (including blogger Jane Hart) feel that it is missing a key component: interactions with other people. Social learning affords the opportunity for informal learning. Hart recommends a learning platform that allows users to store and share information and a chance to work collaboratively with other learners. Check out the really informative presentation she created on social learning (from her blog).

The future of e-learning is social learning
View more presentations from Jane Hart.
Speaking of sharing information, blogger gsiemens challenged his readers to take a look at the traditional information cycle and compare it modern information dissemination. He provided this diagram of the traditional info cycle: In this cycle, info is created as a result of research and then shared through conferences and/or discussions. The info then goes through a validation process and, if found valuable, is shared with a wider audience through journals, books, and conferences. If the info shows moxy, it may be recreated in other publications. Advancements in technology have not changed these steps drastically, but the process is definitely different. Information is created regardless of research and is disseminated via crowd sourcing–digg links, social bookmarking, and product/service review sites (like yelp). Info can be shared through blogs, journals, podcasts, wikis, youtube… you get it. Mashups are an example of how it can be recreated. The trends highlighted in this post point to familiar themes of a more participatory and democratic learning experience aided by, but not contingent upon, web 2.0 technologies.
