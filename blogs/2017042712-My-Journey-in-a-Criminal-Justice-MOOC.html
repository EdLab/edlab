My Journey in a Criminal Justice MOOC

Francisco.Mendoza

April 27, 2017

I have decided to enroll in a course on MIT Courseware called Race, Crime, and Citizenship in American Law. The course aligns my interest in criminal justice and the current contested political debate about policing, especially in inner city neighborhoods. I’ve had a lifelong interest in law enforcement. I will enroll for in criminal justice focused degree program in the Fall. This will be my first time taking an online course. I am interested in learning about how criminal penal codes are written, why they were made in the first place, and how it can be changed or adjusted given current public opinion and political debate surrounding racial and economic inequalities in the criminal justice system. I learn best when something is vividly explained to me. This makes it easier for me to comprehend the material. Additionally, based on the content, I am also a visual learner. For instance, when I took math in high school, I learned best when I saw how my teacher broke down the formulas on the board. During my time in community college, I constantly used YouTube and other platforms to supplement the materials I was learning in class.  I’m excited about this class and all that I hope to learn. I will be sharing my journey so please check back for constant updates.
