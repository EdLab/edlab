Google as a Disruptive Force (in education?)

gnatriello

November 6, 2005

This article on Google as a disruptive force generally raises some interesting questions as to how Google (or similar services) might have a disruptive impact in education. One obvious impact that hits close to home is the effect on academic libraries once Google's major effort to digitize massive numbers of books reaches some critical mass. Libraries that are still oriented to collection building will need to find new identities and reasons for being. A more destabilizing effect might come from an effort to catalog a listing of the world's courses. If colleges and universities operate by assembling courses into degrees, then any effort to develop a global catalog of educational offerings that can be searched by individuals who might then assemble their own (presumably higher quaility or at least more individually tailored) degrees could be a threat. It seems to me that we have enough experience with music online to understand how albums (degrees) might hold up under a regime in which individual songs (courses) are readily available via searching, downloading, and burning. So what will the local course catalog need to look like for an institution (say our own institution) to be better than the global catalog?
