Archival Excellence

Kate Meersschaert

August 2, 2011

Inspired by today's EdLab Development & Research meeting on new thinking regarding Pocket Knowledge promotion and possible pivoting, I wanted to share The Brooklyn Historical Society's Map of the Month Project. The project is an attempt to better catalogue & bring the vast holdings of the society archives to the public in fresh and engaging ways. Of note for EdLab archival efforts, this BHS project was funded through the Council on Library and Information Resources Hidden Collections Program. As described in today's BHS e-newsletter: Every month, BHS showcases a different map from our collection, from property maps of the 1820s to subway maps of the 1940s. The maps are posted on our blog the first Monday of every month and emailed to eNews subscribers the first Tuesday of every month. An example of one of these newly digitized maps was included in the post and what really struck me upon viewing was the remarkable speed of the image download, quality of the scan and beauty of the image. As we move forward with archival initiatives like Pocket Knowledge, the BHS UI/UX experience is a great inspiration. How interesting to offer subscription-based e-newsletters & or tweets in addition to the exciting new Pocket Knowledge archival content blog features that Laura will debut soon! This and other maps are searchable through NYU's cooperative catalogue BobCat & inventoried on Emma.
