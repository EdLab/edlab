Bravemind: Virtual Reality Treatment For PTSD

jvazquez

October 12, 2014


Brave Mind is a virtual reality environment for returning soldiers suffering the intrusive thoughts, nightmares, and flashbacks of PTSD. It was developed out of the University of Southern California’s Institute for Creative Technologies, a research lab affiliated with the US Army, among many others. A common treatment for PTSD is exposure therapy. Brave Mind combines this therapy with controllable, simulated combat sequences. For example, a veteran could find himself walking down a road in Iraq, a scene that triggers the memory of a roadside bombing. But here, the vet can relive the trauma in the presence of a clinician and learn to confront and process emotional responses in a therapeutic way.

A natural tendency for survivors of trauma is to avoid reliving the triggering experience, regardless of whether they’re in a therapist’s office or at the beach. Brave Mind’s immersive, manageable environment allows patients to gently face their fears, one stimulus at a time. It’s a game you can win only by learning how to handle your stress.
