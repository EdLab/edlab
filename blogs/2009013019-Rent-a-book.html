Rent-a-book

Adam Mitchinson

January 30, 2009

Yet another semester is well underway which means, amongst other things, most of us will have recently looked at our debit/credit/loan accounts in bemusement at the speed and ease with which we are able to offload thousands of dollars in the name of education. One such expense that never fails to leave me feeling a little cheated is buying books. The book market has always baffled me - publishers set high prices to try and recoup costs (understandably); retailers add their cut, most offering the 'cheaper' 'buy it used' option which still has an amazing markup. A new service called Chegg offers a service which may help ease this financial burden by offering a text book rental service. Advertising that it has already saved its users close to $20 million to date (there is a counter on the site that is continually rising), Chegg works the same as many other popular rental services that are cashing on peoples reluctance to shell out in order to own something (Netflix anyone?). However, the service has one added benefit - for every rental, it plants a tree! Whether a crafty marketing ploy or a genuine social good, it seems that this service really does benefit everyone - the poor student, the owners' business and the environment! Beat that!
