Trends in Ed, 8.19.09

jvazquez

August 19, 2009

Last week's “Social Tech in Ed” conference really drove home to me the excitement that social media tools are bringing to the classroom. I heard stories from some of the speakers who tried teaching with the tools and ended up learning a lesson themselves; mainly, that these are powerful devices and can serve to really enhance a student's learning experience…. if they are used correctly. But you could sense something in the room. It wasn't a resistance, really…. it was more like… uneasiness. You could tell people in the audience were interested but their questions reflected a sort of, “But, how can I pull that off?” Hopefully the conference got them moving in the right direction. The video below, from socialnomics.net highlights the “Social Media Revolution” and alludes to its importance in education. It is a video depiction of years worth of research on the subject. A 2009 Dept of Ed study revealed that, on average, online students outperformed those receiving face-to-face instruction 3.
