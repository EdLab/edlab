Bob Ubell at the EdLab Seminar

EdLab Studios

April 9, 2017


On the heels of the publication of his new book Going Online: Perspectives on Digital Learning, Robert Ubell shared insights from his work related to the anonymity, analytics, writing, and reflection opportunities that present themselves when education moves from classroom to cyberspace. He is Vice Dean Emeritus of Online Learning, at NYU Tandon School of Engineering.

Watch the full seminar on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion

@04:36 sklee: I agree that the visibility within physical classrooms can become a cause for prejudices, but at the same time, I think it's important that we turn this visibility into an opportunity to address those prejudices.

@40:00 chingfu: It really depends on the contexts and goals of the communication to decide which mode of communication is more efficient and effective than the other. Combining both modes of speech and writing communications in different learning contexts would be ideaI.

@53:33 gnatriello: Opportunities for reflection seem to be related to the opportunity to control time in a learning setting. Online learning typically provides greater flexibility in terms of time.
