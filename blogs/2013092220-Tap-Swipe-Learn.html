Tap, Swipe, Learn?

jvazquez

September 22, 2013


Need to find a good educational app to help your young child learn math/science/reading/etc.? Chances are you’ve checked the app store and felt immediately overwhelmed by the countless options. Sure, you can sort by a few modestly helpful filters, but how will you know that the app you’ve selected isn’t just another waste of money and time?

It can be particularly difficult to discern whether or not all that tapping and swiping is adding anything to your child’s learning experience. Vanderbilt senior Charlotte Duncan has teamed up with associate professor of psychology Georgene Troseth to determine to what extent randomly attacking screens impedes learning. More specifically, they are looking at the role that self-regulation plays in the mobile learning experience. Based on her research, Charlotte has developed a children’s app that embodies many of the elements of effective learning interventions.
