Community Building Through Challenge-Based Learning

Jenny Shen

July 31, 2016


When educators talk about problem-based learning, they may not be thinking about presenting kids with the challenge of tackling a serious disease like lupus. After all, there are doctors and scientists all over the world researching potential cures for these diseases. However, at a school in Culver City, California, the students are thinking about lupus and tasked to "positively and measurably impact the community with lupus" through whatâ€™s called challenge-based learning. They think about the challenge and how it affects the community, and then decide how to develop a solution that might address it.

Two students at the school, for example, decided to create a lupus relief kit, a box filled with items that might help someone recently diagnosed with the disease. The box contains things like aloe vera, sunscreen, and a journal. Through this relief kit, the students hoped to provide practical resources that those coping with lupus might appreciate, and after the students delivered the kits, they certainly seemed to see the extent of their impact.
