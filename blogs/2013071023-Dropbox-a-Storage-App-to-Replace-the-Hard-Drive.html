Dropbox a Storage App to Replace the Hard Drive

Khalil Abubakar

July 10, 2013

We are all aware of iCloud, Apple's app that syncs all saved files from all its products in one place. This type of file storage is safe and accessible anywhere with an internet connection. This time, a new app by Dropbox do it on a scale capable of replacing hard drives. Dropbox is built on sync API, which syncs all new activities and files from apps and saves emails, contacts and even game states across all types of devices. The API also let apps save and protect user data added or changed while their device is offline. Dropbox also has a drop in button which allows apps to extract files from it. All this can be done offline or online with the users data protected. In the future, storage apps like Dropbox will help protect data from hackers and all viruses or hard drive damage and also all our files will be accessible anywhere and could be extracted or synced to any device.
