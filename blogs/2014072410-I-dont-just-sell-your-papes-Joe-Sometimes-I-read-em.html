I don't just sell your papes, Joe. Sometimes I read 'em.

Brian.Sweeting

July 24, 2014

*Private* It may seem like an outdated practice (if you haven't heard, the 90s are back), but newsletters are still a reliable means of bringing readers back to your site. Our weekly NLT newsletter is responsible for anywhere from 3-10% of weekly visits, but on an otherwise slow week it's a dependable source of traffic, and it occasionally results in new signups as subscribers forward it to friends. I'm always open to thoughts on editorial improvements, and wanted to share this piece on newsletter tips from Fast Company. Some takeaways from the successes of the popular TinyLetter platform: 1. The most popular formats are: digests, curation, single topics, and personal stories. 2. Readers prefer humor and informality. 3. Commit to a regular schedule that delivers frequently without sacrificing quality. IYHO what is the NLT newsletter doing well and what could it improve?

The hardest part of newslettering is building an audience, because of the aforementioned "email is sacred" thing. "Start with your inner circle," Kiefer Lee suggests. For most people that means sharing it on Twitter, and putting a link in your Twitter bio. "If your newsletter is good, people will share it. It will grow over time," she says.
