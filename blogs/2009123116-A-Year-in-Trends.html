A Year in Trends

jvazquez

December 31, 2009

Trends in Ed has survived its Freshman year! We've brought you a slew of new tools, services, products, and ideas that have made a splash in the education sector. In this special installment you will find Trends' 10 hottest topics of 2009 and speculations of their impact in the coming year. 10. It *is* easy being green

This past year gave us the opportunity to see what initiatives schools have taken to make themselves leaders and educators in the green movement. Seedlings of sustainability had been planted everywhere from California to Bali. Green tech, also a hot topic this year, has given rise to products like the solar powered eBook. With these ideas as predecessors, green innovations face the challenge of having to push the bar higher in 2010. I'm looking forward to seeing what develops!
9. Virtual schools
There are 90,000 full time students in virtual charter schools in the U.S. With evidence of school districts paying more attention to this alternative to the brick-and-mortar standard, I predict the virtual school stock will only grow in 2010.
8. Educational/serious games
The marriage of gaming and education has enjoyed a pretty sweet matrimony, especially this year. For one, smell was introduced to games via the scent delivery system (SDS). The video game industry is expected to rake in $42 billion in 2010… educational/serious games will likely claim a decent slice of that pie.
7. Future of publishing
From creating your own magazine to your own storybook, online publishing is an attractive alternative to the standard publishing practice. Even the way in which people publish and read information is getting a makeover by making it easier to share, comment on, and annotate within the digital copy. These are some of the affordances that many say are missing from eReaders and tablets.
6. eBooks, eReaders, and tablets
Hey look at that–I made a segue! From personal to classroom use, the buzz around these items was undeniable. With more capabilities and lower prices in their future, expect even more buzz next year.
5. Privacy and security
A lot of hubbub was had over Facebook's changing privacy settings. Open access policies (see here and here) also got people talking. The privacy/transparency issue is one that will likely make headlines in 2010.
4. University tech use
The wired campus was another hot issue this year. In one study we learned that over half of the students using an LMS were happy with the experience while in another we found that less teachers used tech in the classroom in 2009 than they did in 2008. Interesting stats. Maybe we will see more congruence this year.
3. Personal branding
With an increased online presence, individuals, businesses, and institutions have become more aware of the breadcrumbs that they leave behind. Given this, knowledge of your digital DNA is more important than ever!
2. Social learning platforms
Meet the LMS's kid sister. Social learning platforms throw more into the learning mix with their ability to let students collaborate, share info, and even offer the chance for informal learning opportunities. Now, more sociability does not necessarily equate to more learning… but it certainly is a fresh take on an old standby!
1. Social networking + social media = social learning
Like it or not, this is the number one trend that I guarantee we will see more of in 2010. See you next year!
