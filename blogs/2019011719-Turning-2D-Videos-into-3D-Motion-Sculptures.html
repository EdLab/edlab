Turning 2D Videos into 3D Motion Sculptures

Melanie.Hering

January 17, 2019


Studying your opponent's moves on video is a tactic used by many athletes, but what if you could learn how to improve your tactics through 3D visuals? Researchers from MITâ€™s Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL) might be able to make this fantasy a reality with their MoScup program.

Short for "motion sculpture," MoSculp is a system that takes 2D videos and turns them into 3-D-printed visuals that show how a human body moves through space. MoSculp can capture different scenarios featuring athletes, people involved in animated conversation, and performing artists to allow students and professionals the opportunity to compare movements and understand different techniques.

What other learning opportunities could this new technology provide? How might it be utilized by sports professionals as well as those in other fields? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
