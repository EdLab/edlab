EdLab Review: So.Cl

Kate Meersschaert

June 4, 2012

So.Cl (pronounced "social") has emerged from its year-long beta testing phase ready to help enhance college students' "social search" experience. Combining social media with research-focused search and discovery, the site, which is a Microsoft FUSE project and is modeled after a Pinterest-style "board" of resources and Google+ hangouts, is one of several recent search and discovery tools aimed at the ed space (check-out Learni.st). Pros: So.Cl is easy to use for the social media-savvy higher ed audience. Although the platform feels like a hybrid of many social network-based experiences, it still manages to add something new to the research space with a modern look, feel and suite of fun functionalities like "video parties" and interest-based peer introductions. Cons: From an initial test, I am not sure how much functional newness this platform brings to the table. The fact that it is specialized to higher ed student research needs is unique, but it seems like this might just be one more window to have open while a student is studying instead of a game-changing form of communication and knowledge dissemination. EdLab Relevance: Adding social elements to search is nothing new, but making this search experience especially relevant, "fun", and tailored to a niche-market of higher ed-level students is a novel, fresh approach. Sometimes it is more valuable to develop products for a small segment of a population instead of trying to dominate a broader user experience. Finally, the fun factor is often overlooked when creating products with a research focus, and this tool is a great reminder that all research-related product development doesn't have to have to feel "serious." Our Takeaway: So.Cl has promise and only time will tell whether the platform brings enough to the table so that students are willing to engage with it in addition to their existing social media assortment.
