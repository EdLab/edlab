A 10th-Grader's Documentary On Genetically Modified Foods

chingfu

July 23, 2014


Documentaries play an important role in raising public awareness on issues of food production and health. From Food Inc to Fed Up, they offer powerful visual narratives to inspire audiences to learn and think about food processing and how it deeply impacts daily life. While informative, documentaries present value-laden perspectives, and it is important that learners understand the nature of documentary films as they receive new information from this persuasive medium.

Having students produce, not just consume, documentary films, is one of the most effective approaches to teach them how to use this medium for public good. In this mini-documentary, 10th grade student producer Andrew Demeter, who is one of the first prize winners for the C-SPAN 2014 StudentCam documentary competition, addresses issues surrounding genetically modified foods and provides a great example of documentary production for social good.
