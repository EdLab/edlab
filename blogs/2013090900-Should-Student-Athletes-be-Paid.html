Should Student Athletes be Paid?

Francisco.Mendoza

September 9, 2013

The start of the college year also means the start of college football, one of the two major sports for NCAA, the governing body for collegiate sports. One of the longstanding questions the NCAA has to deal with is the issue of whether college athletes should be paid. Every year, the NCAA makes over a billion dollars on revenue generated from TV and sponsorship deals that comes mainly from basketball and football. Athletes in those sports, and all other college sports, do not receive any money from these revenues. I do not think student athletes deserve any kind of payment for their performances on the field as the university usually covers their tuition. That should be more than enough. Additionally, most student athletes are still amateur athletes and those fortunate enough to make it as professionals end up making millions. What are your thoughts on this topic?
