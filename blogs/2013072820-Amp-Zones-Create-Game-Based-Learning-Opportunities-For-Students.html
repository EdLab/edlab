Amp Zones Create Game-Based Learning Opportunities For Students

Mable Yiu

July 28, 2013


At Shadow Mountain High School in Arizona, teachers and students have the ability to game in the media center using "amp zones" to support their curriculum. These zones feature large-screen monitors with high-tech software and internet connections that provide access to lifelike simulations and educational games. The purpose of these amp zones is to facilitate game-based learning with the belief that if students are more interested in what they are learning, they will perform better. Because of the engaging aspect of the amp zones, students are motivated to compete in games that are challenging and conceptual, many at a college-level difficulty. Teachers find that students aren’t leaving the amp zones even when class is over; instead, they continue by inviting friends to join during their lunchtime.
