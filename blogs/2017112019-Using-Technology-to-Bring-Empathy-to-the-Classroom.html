Using Technology to Bring Empathy to the Classroom

DebraLee

November 20, 2017


In Harper Lee’s novel To Kill a Mockingbird, Atticus Finch tries to teach his outspoken six-year-old daughter, Scout, a little trick: "You never really understand a person until you consider things from his point of view, until you climb inside of his skin and walk around in it." The little trick, of course, is empathy, and it’s easier said than done. With this video from Common Sense Education, teachers can learn ways to apply the wealth of digital tools available online (like PenPal Schools, Digital Classroom, Skype and GarageBand, to name a few) to teach kids empathy.

Building social and emotional learning skills has traditionally required face-to-face interaction, meaningful discussion, and reflection. But technology takes the process a step further supporting learning with hands-on strategies that help students build empathy in digital spaces and become responsible digital citizens. This video explores three specific approaches: how to model and practice digital citizenship, how to introduce kids to different perspectives and people from different backgrounds, and how to help kids turn empathy into action.

Classrooms are complex and diverse environments and, like the community in To Kill a Mockingbird, they are also ideal spaces for students to take an inward look at themselves and a closer look at their peers.
