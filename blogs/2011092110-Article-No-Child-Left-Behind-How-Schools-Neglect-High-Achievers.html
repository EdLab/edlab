Article: No Child Left Behind? How Schools Neglect High Achievers

Julia Martin

September 21, 2011

"Part of the problem high-achieving students face is that many schools are so focused on achieving proficiency on standardized tests that they forget about the students seeking to truly excel. Depending on the test, proficiency may require only answering 50 to 70 percent of the questions correctly. Students, including those that are academically gifted, are applauded for hitting that relatively low mark, which can show them that nothing greater is expected and thus accidentally encourage them to stop pushing themselves." -full article here This article goes on to propose reinstating some of the special supports for gifted students to continue to help them excel. I wonder how they'd manage it without reverting to tracking like we had in the through the mid-nineties.
