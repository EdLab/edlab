Vialogues Wins of the Week, 01.15.14

Kate Meersschaert

January 15, 2014

***Private! Please find our weekly Vialogues.com "wins" and developments below! As always, please let us know if you find any additional vialogues mentions on the world wide internets! ;) User acquisition/creation/visits: New Users - 121 Vialogues - 63 Visits - 4,186 When the Martians invaded EdLab MaRS education advisor Joe Wilson writes about their recent trip to EdLab and plugs Vialogues via GroundBREAKERS Learn to love the video Educator/blogger Henry Coll reflects upon his experience with Vialogues saying, "I don't think I would use it for my students since we emphasize speaking over writing. The experience of watching the Vialogue video did make me realize how quickly an audience can go from engaged to bored within the same video. Style of presentation is an important component in keeping an audience interested, so in selecting videos I will now also look for appropriate length and style along with content." He lives up to his name! High school french teacher Mr. Lovely posted Vialogues as a resource for students on his site. We're like the whetstone of edtech Blogger Wendy used Vialogues for the first time and noted that while its graphics "were a little too small for me and crowded", the overall purpose of our site came through for her and she was happy to be able to put another tool in her box. Resolved: Vialogues is the best thing since the alphabet Educator Sue Donovan used Vialogues to spark a debate among her students over Bloomberg's ban on oversized soft drinks. She had a positive experience, saying it "was a neat way to introduce a video and have students comment on the content." Twitter:
