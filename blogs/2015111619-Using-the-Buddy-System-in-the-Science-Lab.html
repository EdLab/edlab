Using the Buddy System in the Science Lab

Jenny Shen

November 16, 2015


In science education, labs are often hailed as the "practical" way of engaging students in the classroom. But handing students a set of instructions and a beaker or two won’t necessarily help them learn, which is why finding ways to add structure and guidance to the process—while still giving students the space to explore and investigate—is so important.

LabBuddy is a product that is meant to do just that. It is comprised of ExperD, which allows students to design experiments and visually arrange tasks for the lab, and WebLM, which is an interactive lab manual that gives teachers the option of adding additional questions to prompt students in their work. Both of these options enrich student engagement and deepen learning.

In the growing field of educational technology, LabBuddy brings to the fore questions about what works and what doesn’t in supplementing student learning with technology. Does adding tech only complicate an often already confusing lab environment? Or does it help students with updated versions of lab manuals and worksheets that clarify key concepts?
