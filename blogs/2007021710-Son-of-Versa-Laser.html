Son of Versa Laser

gnatriello

February 17, 2007

For those who are skeptical of my interest in the Versa Laser, check out this artifact from the future. This raises the more general question of the opportunities for networked information that might be deployed as a service to create artifacts at a distance. For our own purposes, these would be educational artifacts. An obvious example that is already within our grasp would be a networked service to create high quality assessments to guide individual learning and/or the work of educators. The assessments might be paper-based at first and then gradually become more multi-dimensional as the technology permits. This would be a way to deploy state-the-art assessment expertise to support more powerful learning. Of course, once the technology in the artifact from the future is available, we could also distribute prizes and awards to learners as well.
