The Artist With a Robot Assistant

saraehardman

July 8, 2018


Barnaby Furnas goes to his New York studio each day and paints like any artist, except for one small detail: he uses a robot to help his process. Created by art and engineering company Artmatr, Barnaby’s artistic assistant is named Sozo, which means imagination or creation in Japanese. With its paintbrush tip, Sozo marks the canvas according to Barnaby’s instructions, either filling in patches of paint or drawing new lines. Barnaby says this provides him with more security as an artist because Sozo can help correct mistakes, or avoid them altogether.

Barnaby works with Sozo at one of Artmatr’s New York studios, where artists and engineers regularly come together to rethink art in the era of digital technology. They consider their studio space an art and technology playground, and use it to play with new ideas to reimagine one of the oldest and least high-tech art forms: painting.

Will Sozo become a common tool for artists? How do you think art is changing in the digital revolution? Join the conversation on Vialogues.
