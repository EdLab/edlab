The Strong National Museum of Play

EdLab Studios

October 31, 2016


Rochester, New York is home to The Strong National Museum of Play, a colorful and whimsical 282,000 square foot interactive museum devoted entirely to play. This unique institution houses the National Toy Hall of Fame, the World Video Game Hall of Fame, an indoor butterfly garden, a deep archive and library of play, and its very own on-site preschool. Every year, 550,000 visitors from all over the world come to The Strong to explore the cultural history of play through collections-based exhibits of playthings used throughout time. Guests also experience play firsthand through dynamic exhibits like a 20-foot virtual bubble wall and a treasure trove of arcade games from past decades. The underground vaults below The Strong contain vast archives of more than 72,000 play-related artifacts, including centuries' worth of dolls, board games, and multiple iterations of classic toys. Scholars can apply to access these extensive holdings to research play’s impact on culture and the ways that play can illuminate important social themes of an era. Visitors to The Strong have the opportunity to explore and discover new ways to play and learn while experiencing what is arguably the essence of play: fun!
