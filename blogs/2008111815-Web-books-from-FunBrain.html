Web books from FunBrain

Ting Yuan

November 18, 2008

FunBrain, ranked as the no. 1 site for online educational games for K-8 students (math, grammar, science, spell, history), is featured with engaging arcade-style and flash games. Somehow overwhelmed by the quantity of games there, I curiously had a look at the reading section. To my surprise, the web books listed there are displayed very vividly and pretty interesting to read. For example, students reading Diary of a Wimpy Kid can browse any page they want by clicking the calendar. I think, with an increasing trend of online reading, school teachers can use this kind of web books to introduce a new reading concept to students. I am not sure about the economical, technical and IP aspects of designing such books, and is there a software can help to make these books. If the above problems can be solved, maybe to build up an open and high-quality collection of selected children's books online will come true, moving even further than the Google Book.
