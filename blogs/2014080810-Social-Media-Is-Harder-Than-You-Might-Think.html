Social Media Is Harder Than You Might Think

Brian.Sweeting

August 8, 2014

*Private* Warning: Contains shameless humble brags. I thought I was having a pretty good social media week (Forbes regularly tells me this is important to millennials) and was feeling fine about it, until I checked my stats on the revamped analytics.twitter. What I thought was a personal record for retweets didn't really translate into any meaningful engagement with NLT content. This was especially evident for the following Tweet from the Scientific American conference on Tuesday, which was even mentioned on their blog post: The skinny:

  • I have 1,227 Twitter followers.
  • The Tweet made 562 impressions (number of times users saw the Tweet).
  • 9 people clicked on the Tweet to expand the details.
  • 5 people retweeted it.
  • 1 person clicked on the link.
  • Overall Twitter scored this Tweet with an engagement rate of 4.8%, one of my highest scores ever.
Regarding that one person who clicked on the link: I can't see how long they spent on NLT, but because I created a campaign, I know that they didn't sign up for an account. This confirms my long-held suspicion that people don't actually read what they retweet. Of course, building influential social media relationships and effective campaigns takes time, and you never know what's going to be popular, and I suppose it's better to have a social media presence than not (because: millennials). But actually getting people to engage with your content isn't as easy as it seems.
