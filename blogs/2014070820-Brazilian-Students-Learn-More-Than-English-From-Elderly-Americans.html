Brazilian Students Learn More Than English From Elderly Americans

jvazquez

July 8, 2014


A language school in Brazil has figured out a way to connect two groups with specific needs: students who need to practice their speaking skills and elderly citizens who want someone to talk with. CNA English School in Sao Paulo calls this exercise "Speaking Exchange." There’s an added bonus, too. In addition to using English with native speakers, students also nurture interpersonal skills as they form relationships with their newfound friends.

The human element of this learning experience makes it particularly unique. The school recognizes that many of its students can’t afford to immerse themselves in a native English speaking culture to attain fluency so they’ve come up with the next best thing. In practicing with the Americans, the Brazilian students can learn the subtle nuances of conversational English, like vernacular and slang. It’s hard to concretize these concepts by reading a textbook or from having hokey conversations with classmates ("Do you like the party?" "Yes, I like the party.").

Language learners, could you benefit from a speaking exchange? If you’ve done something similar, was it helpful? Share your experience in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
