EdLab Review: Disney Digital Books

Chandler Kang

March 8, 2010

Disney Digital Books Tags: online book, digital book, story telling, reading
http://disneydigitalbooks.go.com/homepage.html Overall: 1.8 Design score: 1.6 out of 5 Pedagogy score: 2.0 out of 5 [The Good] Great visual and sound effects and rich content resources beats other trivial weak points. There is a voice reader that traces words while it reads and a dictionary function that can help users stay in reading activities and learn the meaning of words. [The Bad] Almost every activity is presented within a website and thus there are less opportunities for users to express their own ideas and actively participate on the site. In addition, interaction with other users is also limited. [The Bottom Line] The site is aiming to create fun reading activities. The site is a natural task based, and therefore lacks learning activities and assessment tools. It still provides a lot of natural resources that learners get benefit from to enhance reading skills.
