Open Science Framework Gives Researchers a Free Collaboration Platform

Ryan.Allen

June 6, 2018


"A scholarly commons to connect the entire research cycle" (Open Science Framework)

Collaborative Science

Research today doesnâ€™t have to be conducted by scientists locked together in a small laboratory; instead, it can now be done in conjunction with teams of researchers across the world. To facilitate these efforts, Open Science Framework (OSF) is an open source hub for science and knowledge-building. The platform offers a free space for researchers to collaborate and share research.

Through OSF, users can easily create and manage projects with others across the world throughout the entire lifecycle of any research. Team members can easily track progress or make updates to projects. Tracking features also allow users to see who is accessing or using your data or findings, a way to measure impact beyond citations.

The platform offers seamless workflow integration with Dropbox, Google Drive, GitHub, and figshare. Additionally, OSF works in tandem with JASP, a freeware statistical software. The software is a tool for basic statistical analysis, such as descriptives, t-tests, and even regression. Users can upload data and use OSF to share results, citations, or analysis. The statistical result tables even come completely formatted and can be simply pasted into Word.

Mission-Driven Nonprofit

OSF is part of the Center for Open Science, a nonprofit research center located in Charlottesville, Virginia. The organization's goal is to promote the open science movement, enhance research integrity, and promote the replication of studies. They believe that scientists should not simply be trusted under their authority as scientists, but rather that scientific claims should be replicated, tested, and challenged.

Researchers are under considerable pressures to publish or perish. Paradoxically, good research can take years to conduct. The center created their free OSF platform to boost efficacy in the research process and to help standardize scientific practices. Currently, there are over 45,000 researchers using the site, with almost 75,000 research projects.
