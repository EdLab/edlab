Home Journey 6.08.2009: Teaching the Levees and Its Potential Influence in China

Ting Yuan

June 15, 2009

I got a great opportunity to meet with Professor Limin Xie at Shanghai Normal University (SNU) last week during my journey home. Professor Liming Xie, one of the most famous scholars in the field of education in China, is the leading Professor in Curriculum & Teaching and Director of Institute of K-12 Subject Education at SNU. One of the main purposes of the meeting is to "interview" Prof. Xie about the current teacher education programs in Shanghai (which I will post separately). Before our interview, I introduced myself and our work at the EdLab briefly, including our current development of online learning tools and previous projects such as the Teaching the Levees Project triggered by the Hurricane Katrina. Prof. Xie is very interested in the activities/projects developed by the EdLab and especially asked many questions regarding the Teaching the Levees curricula book. As he mentioned, the curricula book will shed light on the current curricular development for China's 2008 Sichuan Earthquake, one of the most damaging catastrophes in the recent years in China. Up to now, there is no high-quality curriculum developed for the Sichuan Earthquake. He and his colleagues in SNU are actually working on that. Thus, the resources from the EdLab will play an important role in helping the development of the curricula book in China.
