Currix: your lesson library on the go.

Sharon Hsiao

September 13, 2011

Currix is a marketplace for educational content: teachers will be able to monetize the lessons, activities, logos and more that they upload there. Some other comparable sites, such as We Are Teachers, Teachers Pay Teachers. According to this article, Teachers Pay Teachers paid $1 million to teachers. Similar mechanism could work for students for notes sharing, such as ShareNotes, etc. Users get to upload and download the content into their personal library. These tools remind me an old study: [Authoring intelligent tutoring systems: An analysis of the state of the art (Murray, 1999)], which demonstrated that it takes roughly 300 hours of development time per hour of online instruction for ITS authoring tools. Modern approach may use crowdsourcing to cut down the content creation process, and in effect shorten the developing time period. Next questions is, how about the quality? Peer review, social mechanism, such as rating may filter good and bad content. There are solutions around these questions, but never a product that takes care of all. Some pros from the examples above, which can be taken away for Edfluences. include the personal library on the go, itemize the "content creation" such as activity, logo, game, instead of the whole lesson. I'd even add one more, teaching instances (could be examples to explain concepts; ideas...)
