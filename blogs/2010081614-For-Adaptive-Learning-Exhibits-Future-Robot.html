For Adaptive Learning Exhibits - Future Robot

Ting Yuan

August 16, 2010

*Please keep this post private* Luke and I have been developing material for our "Exhibit on Future Technologies" which is one of the four exhibits Edlabbers are working on for the the incoming September event. Our exhibit will be centered on a robot pet who is a friend, assistant, protector, and tutor for individuals: In the future, everyone will have such a robot pet. It will help us connect to other future technologies, such as the future SixthSense digital world, and it will collect information composed or presented by individuals and help record individual information into a global information system. Our robot pet idea came from the famous Japanese cartoon character named Doraemon first shown in a Japanese manga series in the late 1960s. With the above said, Luke and I are interested in creating a short video which highlights the future robot learning and extends the ideas in Prof. Sandra Okita's research on robot and children. Prof. Okita once gave a talk at the Edlab and she was featured in a recent New York Times article which includes a video on robot and children. The video to be developed is probably a 30-60 second commercial-style clip on how one child (5-10 years old) interacts with his/her robot dog in the future world. Maybe we should try to borrow a "robot dog" from Prof. Okita for the filming? Any thoughts?
