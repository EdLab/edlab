The Science of Productivity

Jenny Shen

September 20, 2015


Blogs and lifestyle magazines are constantly pumping out articles with lists of ways to "hack life" to increase productivity and successfully multitask. AsapSCIENCE, a YouTube channel that creates videos on interesting science facts and phenomena through colorful and dynamic graphics, sought to delve into the real science behind some of those tips to confirm or refute them.

For example, the video talks about the Zeigarnik effect, which is a psychological tendency to feel ill at ease when an initiated task is left incomplete. Though starting a task might seem like an obvious first step, this can be capitalized on because there will be a looming and inherent need to get the work done.

The video also discusses how some of the most successful people in their fields, such as musicians, achieve productivityâ€”they practice deliberately, not necessarily for longer periods of time. This has been scientifically proven to be more effective, because people tend to do better by working through smaller, more manageable time periods and tasks. Some people also use accountability charts, which have one column for time span and another for the activities accomplished during those times.
