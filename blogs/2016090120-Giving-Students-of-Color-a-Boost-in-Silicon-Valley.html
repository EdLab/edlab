Giving Students of Color a Boost in Silicon Valley

saraehardman

September 1, 2016


"CODE2040 creates access, awareness, and opportunities for top Black and Latino/a engineering talent to ensure their leadership in the innovation economy." (About CODE2040)

Closing the Gap

CODE2040 has one mission: to close the achievement, skill, and wealth gaps in the U.S. that Blacks and Latino/as face. As jobs in technology are growing at a faster rate than in any other field in the country, this organization prepares members from underrepresented minority groups for careers as software engineers or computer programmers for top tech industries.

From Student to Tech Professional

The Fellows Program at CODE2040 works with highly talented Black and Latino/a college and graduate school students to place them in summer internship programs at top tech companies, and in 2013, 90% of those students received return offers from these companies. The success rate may be due in part to the other educational experiences the Fellows Program offers its students, which includes entrepreneurial workshops and a speaker series, as well as one-on-one mentoring with tech professionals. The program is designed to accelerate minority studentsâ€™ success so they have a better chance of becoming the next generation of tech leaders.

Reciprocal Efforts

CODE2040 also works with businesses and entrepreneurs to establish diverse and inclusive entrepreneurial environments at their companies. For CODE2040, closing the achievement gap in the tech industry is a two-way street. At the same time it helps students climb their way up, it also helps organizations restructure so that these students can make it to the top.
