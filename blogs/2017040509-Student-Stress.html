Student Stress

Evans.Asamoah

April 5, 2017

One of the things I’ve always being curious about is stress. What is it exactly? What are some coping mechanisms?  As a student athlete in my final year of high school, stress has been inevitable. Whether it is taking the SATs, applying for colleges, running for my school’s track team or playing soccer, stress has been a constant year around. Loosely defined, stress is when an individual encounters certain challenges. Stress is harmful physically and mentally. Rising blood pressure, tense muscles, and slow digestive system are all common symptoms of stress. Though stress has negative physical and mental effects, it is also welcomed. Without stress, my life will be non-active and boring. In a way, all the stress I’ve encountered this year has also been good. I know all the effort I put into school and sports will pay off when I matriculate in college in a few months. 

I turn to sports, my church and mentors as my support systems to deal with stress. I know a lot of my peers don’t have the same support system when dealing with stress. I am thinking about studying psychology in college and reducing stress in students is an area I want to further investigate.
