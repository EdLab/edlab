Infographics are Dead, Webgraphics are IN

Megha Agarwala

January 29, 2013

According to this Venturebeat article , Infographics are dead. A webgraphic is essentially an interactive infographic that lets users play around with information and discover new information as they move along. Even though the idea sounds familiar, it is only now that the web is seeing an increase in webgraphics as opposed to infographics. This can be attributed to developments in technology such as responsive design, HTML5, CSS, jQuery and increased hardware improvements. Pros of webgraphics: - Ability to narrate the information story through series of clicks and information discovery. - Coding the webgraphic provides ability to embed website URL's to redirect a user to potentially your website. Cons: - Doesnt work well on social networks since webgraphics sit on a seperate web page, so they dont embed well into social networks. To summarize, the time for infographics is over. So what we should be seeing is more webgraphics in this year. Some examples of webgraphics mentioned in the article: Daily Dose of Water Future of CarSharing Other than webgraphics, the future of infographics will also include: Real time Data Visualization and cross over between information design and web development.
