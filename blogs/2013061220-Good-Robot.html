Good Robot

jvazquez

June 12, 2013


For children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) the simplest things, like responding to the direction of a pointed finger, can be a challenge. To help such children develop social skills, researchers at Vanderbilt University have created an adaptive environment around the NAO robot-- a diminutive humanoid robot developed in France and available commercially.

This video showcases the incredible achievements made as a result of the interactions between NAO and young Aiden, a 3-year-old diagnosed with ASD. Researchers watch as Aiden dons an electrode-laden baseball cap and responds to the various cues that the robot throws at him. "Aiden, look!" NAO says while pointing in one direction. Amazingly, Aiden’s gaze follows NAO’s extended fingers. The hope is that Aiden’s progress with NAO will translate to successful human interactions down the road.

To think that a robot could help a child develop social skills is nothing short of remarkable. What other developmental or behavioral disorders could technology ameliorate? Leave your ideas in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
