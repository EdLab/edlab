Documenting Design: The Major League Baseball Logo

HSC

November 12, 2008

Check out this story on the design of the Major League Baseball Logo. I found the piece to be an interesting read in light of the discussions we've had about the design approach in the EdLab. Some highlights from the article: - The batter in the logo can be viewed as being either right-handed or left-handed. - The type underneath the logo is Helvetica. - The logo, which turns 40 in 2009, has never been redesigned. - The logo was developed in one afternoon. - Major League Baseball accepted the design with no modifications. - Be sure to document your work. MLB has never acknowledged the true designer behind the logo. And here is my favorite interchange between the interviewer (UNIWATCH) and designer (Jerry Dior): UW: So many other sports leagues have copied your approach for their logos. What do you think of that? JD: I think it's great. Like they say, imitation is the sincerest form of flattery. I started something, and I didn't even realize it! UW: So you don't resent it, like they're stealing your ideas or anything? JD: Not at all. They're not stealing it -- they're carrying on the idea. I feel good about it.
