Learning Gets Touchy-Feely With Haptic Technology

jvazquez

February 2, 2014


How can our sense of touch heighten the learning experience? More specifically, how can we leverage touch (haptic) technology to make strides in areas such as dentistry, art, medicine, and other tactile fields of study? While still a young territory, it is already redefining experiential learning through realistic simulations: surgeons can practice meticulous procedures virtually by way of vibrations that reproduce what it feels like to manipulate human flesh; young military recruits-in-training can brace against bullet-like blows that hit their Kevlar vests without being put in harmâ€™s way.

In this TED talk, Associate Professor Katherine Kuchenbecker (responsible for both the surgery and military projects just mentioned) from the engineering school at UPenn explains how haptic interfaces might combine with the sense of touch to usher in an entirely new way of learning.
