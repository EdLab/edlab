Mystery Skype: A Geography Game

chingfu

January 20, 2014


Casual videoconference technologies, such as FaceTime or Skype, connect people from across the globe in real-time visually and aurally and have made real-life story sharing much easier. They are also a popular means for educators to invite guest speakers to share expertise with students remotely. Recently, an interesting twist of this video call technology with gaming elements, Mystery Skype , has created exciting geographic learning opportunities for kids around the world.

Mystery Skype is a game involving two classrooms from different areas of the world that Skype each other and try to guess the location of the other classroom through strategic questions and clues. The learning of geography is well integrated in the core of the game mechanics. Before the Skype session starts, teachers and students think about how to present the geographic features of their communities as questions and clues. Then they must be ready with maps and other tools to help them quickly filter out unlikely locations and narrow down possible places where their opponentâ€™s classroom might be located as the game starts. Real people in real-time seems like a magic combination to excite kids to learn geography.
