Conference Notes: From Wearables to Shareables

Kate Meersschaert

November 5, 2014

Jo, Mason and I are at Baruch College for the conference "From Wearables to Shareables" Speakers for first panel: Robert Genovese, VP Marketing, Kenneth Cole -Nikki Nash, Director of Digital Marketing, Ready Set Rocket -Forest Young, Creative Director, Interbrand -Gareth Price, Technical Director, Ready Set Rocket -moderator: Ana Valuenzuela, Associate Professor of Marketing, Head of Baruch Consumer Research Lab, Baruch College -The great brands are creating "experiences" -Wearables as a gentle consumer nudge -Fight against "Glasshole" reputation and towards how to use wearables to be a better person -As devices become more intimate, advertising becomes more intrusive -Low click-through banner ads - Wearables are more intimate -Need to "give back" via ad -Interoperability of devices - the really interesting things happen when devices talk to each other (much like early internet) -Device to device "communication" most exciting to wearable developers -Haptic or multi-sensorial experiences are the future - ex: vibratory updates -Whimsical Wearables -Ex: Dorothy -subcutaneous & digestible wearables around the corner
