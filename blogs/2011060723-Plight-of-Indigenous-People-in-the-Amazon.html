Plight of Indigenous People in the Amazon

Nantwi

June 7, 2011

As someone with a deep interest in various cultures, I've been keenly following researchers and anthropologists who are claiming to have found a previously secret tribe deep in the Amazon. The Brazilian government, much to the ire of NGOs dedicated to preserving the livelihoods of indigenous tribes, recently decided to build a hydroelectric power plant in the Amazon arguing the plant was in the long term energy interest of Brazil while opponents argued it will essentially lead to displacement for many indigenous tribes. The video below offers rare footage of one of these unknown tribes in the Amazon with the hope that those interested in their cause might be compelled to help if they knew of their existence. How can governments and businesses protect its interest while maintaining the history, culture and lives of indigenous people?
