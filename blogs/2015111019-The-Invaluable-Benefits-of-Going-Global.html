The Invaluable Benefits of Going Global

Jenny Shen

November 10, 2015


When someone mentions "study abroad programs" most people think about a college semester spent in a foreign country, usually with the hopes of experiencing a different culture and lifestyle. However, the Institute of International Education is trying to expand that view.

The Institute argues that because of the expanding globalization of communities and networks, it is more necessary than ever before for learners to connect on an international level. Careers and companies often have international ties, and to keep up with this, students should experience as much as they can on global scale.

But what about the financial burden? Many students cannot afford to travel to another country for a semester. That’s where the Institute of International Education’s "Generation Study Abroad" program comes in. This initiative is a five-year plan to double the number of students studying abroad by 2020 by providing scholarships, study abroad advisors, and other forms of support at various universities across the U.S.

Is this worthwhile initiative enough to help students take a semester in Italy? Croatia? New Zealand? What is the value of studying abroad, and is it enough to invest resources into programs like Generation Study Abroad? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
