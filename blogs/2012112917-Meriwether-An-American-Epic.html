Meriwether: An American Epic

Josh Lopez

November 29, 2012

Sortasoft, the company I interned with before coming to Edlab, has just posted their latest project on Kickstarter and has already been added to their list of staff picks. The project is a video game called Meriwether, an RPG based on the Lewis and Clark Expedition. I'm sure some of us remember another video game about crossing North America and I think this one will be just as much fun. But more importantly, this game has some serious educational takeaways. Playing the game involves immersing yourself in the world of the game (like any other good RPG), but because Meriwether is based on historical people, places, and events the world you are immersing yourself in is the real world. Along the way you'll discover new animals (no one really knew what was living out there yet; some people thought there might be prehistoric animals still roaming around), create medical remedies for sick and wounded soldiers (see how well you survive with only 1800's medical knowledge), encounter Native Americans (not all of whom are friendly), and hunt (everyone's favorite). I'm always on the lookout for educational games that don't suck and I think Meriwether is going to be a big score for the good guys.
