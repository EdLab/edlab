Trends in Ed, 2.20.09

Arianna Choi

February 20, 2009

today's news is a tribute to those working on mobile apps have a great weekend! 95% of iPhone Users Ditch Apps After 1 Month A slew of new applications appear on the iTunes store everyday and many of them are available for free. Clearly some of them are good while some others are not, but what do we know about user behavior in regards to just how much users like an app? Pinch Media, a marketing & advertising company specializing in serving iPhone developers, found that just 20% of users return to using a free application after it is downloaded. This figure plummets to 5% after one month, meaning that an average user will keep only one app for every twenty apps downloaded. Apple Rejects South Park Apple rejected a new South Park game for iPhone/iTouch citing that the application contains inappropriate contents. It is not surprising that an app gets rejected based on the Apple iTunes Store's rules, some raise eyebrows on the fact that the store sells seasons 11 and 12 of the comedy TV show with TV-MA (i.e. mature audiences) rating. So as it stands, you can watch Kenny, Kyle, Cartman and Stan but can't do so in the virtual life in the world of South Park.
