Education and Entrepreneurship Program at Penn

ABARRY

January 26, 2016

Succeeding as an entrepreneur is very demanding, it requires more than the concepts learned in the classroom. One of the primary reasons some ventures fail is unpreparedness. What if you fail to succeed? What are the alternative routes to take and overcome this setback? The University of Pennsylvaniaâ€™s Graduate School of Education has developed a new program, Education Entrepreneurship program. The program blends education, business, and entrepreneurship without leaving the classroom. Students take specific courses where they design, construct, test and launch new models that connect ideas and best practices in education with business principles and market-based solutions. This hopefully will enable entrepreneurs to obtain the knowledge and skills to conceptualize, design and execute successful education ventures and launch innovative outcomes.
