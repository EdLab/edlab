Bursting Media Bubbles

EdLabStudios

September 16, 2018


The United States of America didn’t become politically polarized overnight. A growing trend of ever-narrowing echo chambers and media bubbles has widened the gap, reducing understanding across political parties and dividing a nation. Founded by Annafi Wahed The Flip Side  is a daily news digest with the mission to bridge the gap between liberals and conservatives by providing curated, concise summaries of political analysis from both conservative and liberal media. Ms. Wahed presented The Flip Side and the concept of bursting media bubbles through exploring new perspectives at the EdLab in August 2018.Watch the full seminar on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion:

@03:30 Sabarish Raghupathy: Social media platforms perpetuate media bubbles .

@05:30 Thomas Anderson: It does not help that our President thinks the media is the enemy of the people.

@18:00 Brian Sweeting: Yesterday I was curious how Fox News was covering the Manafort and Cohen news, and they were primarily focused on the suspected murderer of Maggie Tibbetts.
