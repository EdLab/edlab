VideoNote, Critter and More...

Arianna Choi

September 24, 2009

VideoNote is a platform for communicating, educating and collaborating with long-format video â€” could this be Critter's think-alike friend? We have seen a variety of asynchronous online tools for video-centered discussions such as VoiceThread, Viddler and Wimba but so far VideoNote seems to share more similarities with Critter compared to other tools we've internally reviewed. The most visible similarities are that participant comments are linked to specific video time and that there are different options to display comments (e.g. sort by video time, date posted or popularity). More importantly, both solutions aim to enhance the effectiveness of online education. Improving the practice of teaching and the process of learning looks to be VideoNote's primary interest as well. The overarching difference, on the other hand, is that VideoNote focuses more on cognitive support by providing the option to navigate between instructor's notes, presentation slides and participant comments while watching the video. (Oh but, it doesn't support threaded discussions!) Critter, as explained in the beta design proposal, is currently being developed to focus more on collaboration using existing social networks and also on expanding assessment support such as analytics, surveys and polls. All this is based on a brief review which means the Critter team will continue to do a more in-depth comparative review. If you know any interesting model or have suggestions for making a cross-comparison of video-centered learning tools, please shoot away. Comparing technical features/functions is quiet straight forward, but what else do we want to know?
