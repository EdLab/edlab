A Programmable Robot Named Robby

csd2126

March 19, 2017


The goal of Avishkaar Box is to provide ideas, experiences, material (hands-on, virtual or anything under the sky) that makes parents first understand the areas of interest of their children and then provide them a path to achieve the same. (Avishkaar Box)

Parents work for their children to play

Avishkaar Box is a New Delhi-based startup that creates robotics kits for children including Robby and Bonny, gender-specific robots that can be easily programmed by children. The company was founded by Tarun and Nandini Bhalla, a husband and wife team, to encourage their young daughter to develop a love for STEM. Their vision for Avishkaar Box is to create a platform "that will allow parents not only from India but from rest of the world to go through this very important journey of achieving the dreams that they have for their children." The boxes are designed for children 5–12 to engage them in STEM activities and don’t appear to include instructions targeted at parents.

Meet Robby and Bonny

The robots can be programmed to move, blink lights, and play sounds. Simple sequences to control Robby and Bonny’s movements can be programmed through an accompanying Android app. The drag-and-drop programming interface aims to cater to the company’s young user base. A desktop programming language extends the capabilities of the robots so children can continue to learn. Hardware components such as screens, carts, and motors allow kids to extend their robots.

STEM-based toys and kits for kids are becoming increasingly available across the world. While Avishkaar Box’s approach is not the most flashy or novel, the two different programming languages (app-based and desktop) that can be used to control the robot may extend the toy’s use.
