The Oberlin Project: Reinventing a Community for Future Scarcity

Melanie Hibbert

November 21, 2011

The proposed Oberlin Project aims to join town and university resources to create a resilient community for a post-fossil-fuel era. "When asked to describe the project, Mr. Orr conjures a picture of life in Oberlin–a city of 8,000 residents and students, 40 miles outside of Cleveland–many years into the future: The town and the college would be powered by renewable energy, with a smattering of new and renovated green buildings at the town's core–the first among them paid for by the college. A 'greenbelt' of farms would pump food, wood, and fiber into the city, while a steady stream of money from the college and small businesses, like restaurants and furniture manufacturers, would flow back out to the farms." The project proposes that the college, the local community college, the local vocational school, and the city's elementary and secondary schools would alter their programs to prepare students to live in the future: "a world short on oil, wracked by unstable weather, relying increasingly on sustainable design, regional industries, and local know-how. The town itself would be a laboratory for a new way of life." More at the Chronicle of Higher Education.
