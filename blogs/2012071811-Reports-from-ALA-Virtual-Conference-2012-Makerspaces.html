Reports from ALA Virtual Conference 2012: Makerspaces

Rebekah Wallin

July 18, 2012

Makerspaces are a hot topic these days. EdLab has talked about making and Maker Faire before, but the maker-movement is continuing grow, and more schools and libraries are wanting to make learning resources available for making things. Basically, a makerspace (aka hackerspace) is a shared space with tools where people with common interests can learn, teach, socialize, and collaborate on making things. This morning at the ALA Virtual Conference: Mapping Transformations, Travis Good delivered a keynote on "What's a Makerspace and Why Should Libraries Care?" Thanks to exciting new tools, people are now more empowered to make things than ever before. Good did a great job explaining some of the smart tools and materials that are available to makers, like micro controllers, sensors, actuators, CNC mill, 3D printing, laser cutter, etc. He also gave some incredible examples of things that people have made.
