CFP: Learning Analytics and Knowledge 2013 (LAK) Workshop & Poster

Sharon Hsiao

January 28, 2013

Every morning I open my mailbox and it's always flooded with CFPs (Call For Papers). This is one of those that I read carefully. Learning analytics is a new emerging area as we all learned from Dr. Siemens's seminar the other day. There are some interesting tracks that might be relevant to us this year at the LAK conference WAVe: Workshop on Analytics on Video-based Learning and International Workshop on Teaching Analytics. I read a lot papers from CSCL, CSCW, LAK, ITS, UMAP, AIED, and EDM areas. I think LAK falls nicely into the mid place of bridging learning science and artificial intelligence. There's a big overlap of the committee members from those communities. The reason is simple, the new sub-domain, LAK, really just requires both AI and Learning Science to work together and move forward. From AI perspective, it's usually expecting more innovative algorithmic methods. In LS, innovative theoretical frameworks will be appreciated. For areas in between these two? I think we are still figuring out the uniqueness of it as more innovative evaluations are always welcomed. I think it'd be nice to join their discussions for our DataDashboard project. Workshops and posters could be a good place to start.
