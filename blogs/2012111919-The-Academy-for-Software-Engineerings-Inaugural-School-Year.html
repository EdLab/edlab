The Academy for Software Engineering’s Inaugural School Year

jvazquez

November 19, 2012

Ask any developer you know how young they were when they broke their first computer just so they could put it back together, and I bet you’d find their interest in technology is deep-rooted. The problem is, there are few outlets that allow young people to exercise these interests outside of after-school clubs. So, many students are hopping from an extracurricular hobby to a full-blown four-year career path with little in the middle to ease this transition.

That’s why New York City’s new public school, the Academy For Software Engineering (AFSE), is a major win for the city, its students, and a burgeoning tech industry in the area. With the need for skilled developers on the rise (the Bureau of Labor Statistics projects an increase of 30% in job opportunities this decade), AFSE is answering the call many employers have been making.

Aside from the obvious competencies that students of software engineering pick up, they also learn the valuable skill of creative problem solving. Add to that, as computational geometer Hugo Boyer points out in the video, "computers are an extension of your brain, so the younger you get to play with them, the easier it is for you to get them to do tricks for you."

With Silicon Alley merely a stone’s throw away from the school, eager new talent and relieved employers are no doubt welcoming future opportunities to collaborate.
