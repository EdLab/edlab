Learning Ace

LauraCostello

November 24, 2013


Learning Ace is a tool for higher education students to search, share, and organize research materials in an innovative way. The site is based around Ace Learning Company’s adaptive resource search and hinges on private relationships with publishers to provide micro-subscriptions to some materials students discover. The platform, incubated through Pearson Catalyst, presents a modern search and discovery system in line with the way students structure digital research.

Pros:

Students searching through Learning Ace can organize their found resources into "learning paths" that unite like items in chronological groups. Users accustomed to autosave technologies like Google Docs will appreciate Learning Ace’s memory for learning paths and the fact that search keywords stay in the search bar from session to session. The site’s design evokes mass-transit maps and this metaphor carries over into the hierarchical grouping and many intersections of learning paths in research. Resources on the site are also heavily networked, with most content coming from class notes and sources that are easy to share with other students, teaching assistants, and instructors.

Cons:

The site’s design is well-organized, but there are still a few quirks in the search. For instance, the single search bar implores users to write a question, but the search is based on keywords rather than natural language. The search results also leave something to be desired; the titles in search are often not descriptive and further information consists of a snippet-view of search keywords pulled from the full text, making it hard to know what will appear when you click.

Our Takeaway:

Learning Ace is an intriguing model of research that creates a network and a narrative. In traditional analog and digital research, the strategy of search is often lost and the results are presented as single citations rather than a group of interconnected resources. Online research in Learning Ace results in a citation list that is more highly visualized and retaining the record of the search method might enable researchers to look back on their research to create strategies for more efficient searches in the future.

The Bottom Line:

Learning Ace is a model for the future of connected research.
