Rain, Rain, Go Away: Scientists Might Soon Decide the Weather

Rebecca.Sullivan

October 4, 2018


Scientists around the world are experimenting with ways to mitigate the effects of climate change by controlling the weather. For example, cloud seeding adds particles to clouds, forcing them to rain or snow. With cloud seeding, scientists can control where and when it rains. This technology has already been used for a variety of purposes: it has helped increase rainfall in Wyoming, ensured sun for the Beijing Olympic games, and been used by couples who want sun on their wedding day.

At Harvard, researchers are attempting to cool the planet off by creating clouds. This could simulate the effect of volcanic eruptions, when sulfurous clouds block the sunâ€™s rays, leading to temperature drops. While these new technologies might help reduce the effects of global warming, tampering with nature also might have unforeseen consequences.
