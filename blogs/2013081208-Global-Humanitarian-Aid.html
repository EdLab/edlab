Global Humanitarian Aid

abagigah92

August 12, 2013

Historical trends show an increase in global humanitarian aid across the world. Particularly in the 90's and early 2000's in Africa, South America, and Eastern Europe due to disasters and complex emergencies like civil wars and genocides This requires coordination in order to improve response and provide aid in an efficient and effective manner. In Kurke and Olsen's article Knowledge creation and reliable decision making in complex emergencies, they examine some of the conflicts between humanitarian aid workers on the field and decision makers and how that influences collaboration provided around the world. Challenges such as dissemination and misinterpretation of information, lack of effective information system, mistrust among different actors and organizations, lack of inclusion of local staff in management position and the centralization of decision making was addressed. These challenges hinders the decision making process, particularly when deciding what situation or aid is the most important.
