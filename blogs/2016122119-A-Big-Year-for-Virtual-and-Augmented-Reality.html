A Big Year for Virtual and Augmented Reality

saraehardman

December 21, 2016


Virtual and augmented realities are rapidly evolving technologies. Their educational potential is growing at a tremendous rate, and the research is just trying to keep up. Here are the research articles from the field that we found most compelling this past year.

1. Testing an Autism Therapy with Virtual Reality
Children on the autism spectrum underwent a therapy to improve social skills that centered around virtual reality and Second Life.

2. Public Speaking Is No Easier in Virtual Reality

3. What is it Like to Teach Virtual Students?
College teaching assistants get a taste of a virtual classroom full of avatar students during their teacher training. They reported to the real world to share their experiences.

4. When Will the Workplace Be Ready for Augmented Reality?
Construction workers test the social applications of an augmented reality app by bringing it to the site along with their hammers and nails.

5. But How Does Augmented Reality Really Make You Feel?
When history meets field trips meets augmented reality, the results demonstrate how futuristic technologies can better connect us to the past.
