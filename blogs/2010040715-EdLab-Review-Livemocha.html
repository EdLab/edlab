EdLab Review: Livemocha

Chandler Kang

April 7, 2010

Livemocha Tags: english learning, speaking, listening, active learning
http://www.livemocha.com/sihp
Overall: 3.95 Design score: 4.1 of 5 Pedagogy score: 3.8 out of 5 [The Good] Learning tools are intuitive and simple. Contents are well organized. Also, depending on a situation where a conversation happens, they provide a "culture note," that is a tip for helping language learners who live in other cultures better understand expressions in contents. This also plays a contextual role in that learners can retrieve their learning easily later in practical situations. Learning task itself can become a social activity since it is in sync with a Facebook account. [The Bad] It is not a necessarily a bad part, but adaptivity in assessment is lacking because the difficulty level in testing does not change depending on a learner's performance. [The Bottom Line] LiveMocha is a language-learning tool that combines fun self-study lessons, motivational tools and community to help users learn the language of their choice. Unlike CDs and books that contain stale academic content, LiveMocha brings to learners useful everyday language from actual native speakers. Read Full Review
