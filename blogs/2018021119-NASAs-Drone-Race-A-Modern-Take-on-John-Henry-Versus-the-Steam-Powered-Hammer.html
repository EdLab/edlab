NASA’s Drone Race: A Modern Take on John Henry Versus the Steam-Powered Hammer

Ryan.Allen

February 11, 2018


People have had anxieties about being replaced by machines dating back to the earliest days of industrialization, famously captured in the American folktale John Henry, The Steel Driving Man. In a modern day twist of this classic, NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory invited Ken Loo, a world-class drone racer, to challenge their autonomously piloted drone in an obstacle course.

The researchers prepared for this race between man and machine for over two years. Their AI-powered drone utilized state-of-the-art cameras to track its position and mapping software to memorize the course. In the end, the human racer prevailed in terms of speed, finishing the course on average two seconds faster than the AI, but the machine won in terms of course accuracy. Soon, though, the technology will become so advanced that humans will not stand a chance in either category.

Like in the tale of John Henry, humans won this match, but will ultimately lose out to mechanization. In what other areas do you think humans still hold advantages over machines? Are there any jobs or tasks where you think they will never replace us? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
