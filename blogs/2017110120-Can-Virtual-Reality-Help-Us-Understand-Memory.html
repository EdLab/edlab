Can Virtual Reality Help Us Understand Memory?

saraehardman

November 1, 2017


Nanthia Suthana is exploring the waters of an entirely new virtual reality (VR) application: collecting data on memory formation in patients with brain implants. To put it lightly, memory is a complex phenomenon that researchers are still trying to understand, but innovative studies such as Suthana’s offer a unique point of view on the topic. "If we can understand what’s happening in the brain when these memories are recalled or formed," Suthana says of her research, "then potentially we can develop therapies for patients who have memory disorders such as Alzheimer’s disease."

Suthana is not alone in her pursuit of neuroscientific VR research; other research centers, such as Neuroscape at UCSF, are also diving into the field, but she is the first to do so with patients who have brain implants. In particular, her research topic revolves around patients who have suffered memory loss due to seizures, such as the infamous patient H.M.. VR allows Suthana to create a more real-world environment for patients to conduct memory tasks than many lab experiments afford, which may lead to more accurate data.
