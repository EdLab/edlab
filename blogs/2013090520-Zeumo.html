Zeumo

LauraCostello

September 5, 2013


Zeumo is a digital hub for middle and high school students that combines social networking, electronic messaging, and school news. The administrative functions of the app include direct messaging between teachers and students, announcements through a news feed, connecting students with school counselors, and school-wide text messaging. Zeumo’s "chime in" feature lets students weigh in on new school policies and issues. The app integrates students’ personal Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram feeds into the interface so they can access all their contacts and responsibilities in a single app. The system is free for students and runs on web, Android, and iPhone.

Pros:

The app simplifies school administration by providing several ways to get in touch with mobile-connected students like text message alerts and dashboard notifications. Zeumo also does some social good by bringing in select nonprofit organizations to provide volunteer opportunities for students and publicize social interest events. Zeumo contributes to novel school data through a daily "pulse check" of student moods. This feature might help administrators and teachers gauge the effectiveness of events, policies, and school initiatives.

Cons:

Zeumo bills itself as a resource to simplify students’ lives, but even if students are also using it to corral their other social networks, it seems like it could increase the information load.

Our Takeaway:

Instead of rejecting social networks, as many school policies do, Zeumo integrates them into the interface. This embrace of mobile generation communication sets Zeumo apart from similar administrative apps. Products like Chalk, Edmodo, and Chalkable have emerged recently with missions to increase the social culture in schools, simplify administrative tasks, and open communication with students. Schools seem attracted to the increased efficiency and possible rich data offered through these platforms. It’s still unclear whether students will take to these systems with the same fervor, but the possibility of a digitally enabled school is nonetheless exciting.

The Bottom Line:

Zeumo is either the antidote to or the embodiment of the school social network.
