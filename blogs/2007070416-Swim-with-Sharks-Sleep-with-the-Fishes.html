Swim with Sharks, Sleep with the Fishes

gnatriello

July 4, 2007

We have had several good discussions of whether it is wise to place more than a few demo video clips on iTunes despite the popularity of iTunes as a distribution platform. Now that world wide distribution of information is easier and less expensive than ever, all sorts of institutions seem to be unable to resist turning their content over to distributors with little concern about ultimate control of either their content or their future. Two recent articles highlight some of the issues. In the NY Times an article titled Universal in Dispute With Apple Over iTunes explores the tensions between the music industry and Apple. In Businessweek an article titled Welcome to Planet Apple explains how Apple relies on a network of relationships to advance its own agenda, often to the detriment of others in that network.
