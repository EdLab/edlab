Proof That Some Social Media in Class Isn't Terrible

dinosossi2

July 7, 2016


A classroom full of students on laptops or phones might appear to be severely distracted. But what might also be happening is a worthwhile digital discussion. When students use social media to talk to each other about the lecture, it's called backchanneling, and it might actually be productive. A recent study compared two college classes to find out.

Authors of the study found that discussion among classmates and instructors was widely adopted. Instructors used backchannels to capture, integrate, and summarize student ideas, needs, and questions inside and outside class. This increased student engagement and met their expectations for communication. Overall this practice increased classroom collaboration.

Students who chatted on social media reported higher levels of participation, engagement, and satisfaction, and achieved higher grade averages. Not only was social media not a distraction overall, it also provided a better learning experience. Before stubbornly sticking to an all-out cell phone ban, instructors should consider that integrating mobile devices into the classroom for backchanneling can keep students engaged.
