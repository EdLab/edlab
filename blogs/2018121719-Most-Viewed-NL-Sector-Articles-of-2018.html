Most Viewed NL Sector Articles of 2018

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2018


This year we highlighted organizations that are using technology to elevate issues of literacy, representation, and diversity in learning. Take a look back at the most viewed NL Sector articles of the year.

1. Storyline Online
Storyline Online is a childrenâ€™s literacy website that provides free storytelling videos and resources for parents and teachers. New and classic picture books are read by well-known actors and actresses to bring stories to life and foster a love of reading in children.

2. Juni Learning
Started by two female Stanford graduates frustrated with the inaccessibility of computer science education, Juni Learning pairs students ages 5-18 with instructors for online coding classes. Students learn programming fundamentals, logic, and math, and have the opportunity to use their knowledge to create a game, website, app, or larger portfolio.

3. 500 Women Scientists
In a field still globally dominated by men, 500 Women Scientists aims to advocate for women working in STEM and diversify the voices telling the stories of science. They facilitate networking opportunities within the community and for those looking to hire or talk to an expert.

4. Our World in Data
Our World in Data is an online publication dedicated to compiling and highlighting publicly available data in order to illustrate changes happening in the world. Using datasets, visualizations, and blog posts, the site publishes on a range of topics like economics, violence, and human rights.

5. RoboKind
RoboKind is a company that aims to create more diverse, inclusive education for students who have traditionally been disadvantaged in education systems. Their two products, Robots4Autism and Robots4STEM, utilize humanoid robots to give all students opportunities to pursue their tech-related passions.
