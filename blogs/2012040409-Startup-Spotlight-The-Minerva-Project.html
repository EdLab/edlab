Startup Spotlight: The Minerva Project

Kate Meersschaert

April 4, 2012

The Details & Back-story: Ben Nelson, The Minerva Project CEO, knows a thing or two about school... photos. The former SnapFish CEO has raised a record-breaking amount of SEED funding ($25 Million) from Benchmark Capital. The Minerva Project has lofty aspirations to reinvent higher education by enlisting the world's leading professors to teach exclusive online courses, select students based solely on academic performance, and eschew traditional "101" level courses because, as Nelson describes in this VentureBeat article, “it's ridiculous for students to pay us money to learn stuff that they can go online and learn for free.” Instead, Minerva Project students will pay $20,000 a year to virtually attend, "the first elite American University to be launched in a century." Unique Point of Difference: Customer service and your own personal PR team after graduation. The Project promises to offer grads a team that will provide, "personal publicity and active participation in career management." This is a ground-breaking offer. What current university, beyond offering alumni networking opportunities, also promises to provide PR to recent grads? EdLab Relevance: As we continue to rethink what "getting educated" could mean, it is interesting to watch, in addition to ubiquitous, free online options, a model that while living online, has a substantial fee-structure attached. Takeaway: Could the future of "elite" higher education be relatively low-cost, online, and distributed across a network of students in far-flung locales? If The Minerva Project succeeds, for students who are selected and can afford the price, the answer may be yes.
