Keeping History Alive With Technology

saraehardman

July 7, 2016


An innovative 3D technology is giving new life to Holocaust survivors’ stories. Housed at the University of Southern California’s Institute for Creative Technologies, this cutting edge technology records 3D videos that put Holocaust survivors in the room with you. Not only are the videos three-dimensional, they are also interactive. Audience members can pose questions to the survivors, and language recognition algorithms find corresponding answers from their interviews. This makes it seems like the conversations are happening in real time, even though the videos have been prerecorded.

Instead of reading or watching stories about the Holocaust, people can use this technology to converse with survivors, and through their words, better understand its enormity. As Holocaust survivors age, technology like this can keep their stories alive long after they’re gone. The creators hope these 3D projections will find a permanent home in museums and classrooms.
