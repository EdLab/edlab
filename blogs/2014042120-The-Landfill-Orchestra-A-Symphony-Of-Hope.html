The Landfill Orchestra: A Symphony Of Hope

Carmen James

April 21, 2014


It is not an overstatement to say that the story of the Landfill Orchestra is no less than a Shakespearean saga of heart and soul trumping all other obstacles; everyone has something to learn and appreciate.

This video captures the incredible story of students in Paraguay who built and learned to play their own instruments from items found in a landfill. The carefully crafted and colorful instruments create the sounds of real instruments like violins and oboes. The children, directed by the dedicated Favio Hernán Chávez Morán, play symphonies. You will not believe your eyes and ears as you watch this demonstration of the unlimited power of the human imagination.

"For me music is the smile of the soul," shares Luis Szarán, Director of "Sonidas de la Tierra." Morán and Szarán worked together to bring self-made music to the poorest area of Paraguay built around a landfill. The two believed that the experience of instrument making and playing complex and timeless music would empower the community and lead to social transformation. The video, interspersed with the music of children, shows the process of making the instruments and the items they used. The video also shares accounts from children in the orchestra on how the experience has changed their lives, kept them out of drugs, and taught them to appreciate themselves.
