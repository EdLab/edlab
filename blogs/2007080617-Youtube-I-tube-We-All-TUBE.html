Youtube, I-tube, We All TUBE

Sonia Lee

August 6, 2007

Our generation is controlled by the cyber world. We are all involved in the world of facebook, myspace, and celebrity gossip blogs. We love to read about our friends, Hollywood stars and search the web for funny new videos. Lately, though, I've realized that being stuck without internet access basically leaves you an outcast from the whole world! Internet has even replaced television. Episodes and series of our favorite shows are now available online. Whether you are looking for a comedy clip or symptoms to an incurable disease, a Google search will most likely have what you are looking for.

So when you are looking for video clips (music videos, and video posts) there are unlimited sites that allow you to access practically anything you are looking for. And one of the most popular and relatively new video sharing website is Youtube. Created in 2005 by former PayPal employees, youtube uses adobe flash to display videos which has revolutionized video blogging and is viewed by over a hundred million viewers a day!

I'm guessing you can see where the problems will arise. Celebrities and large broadcasting corporations are attacking (suing) free websites such as Youtube for copyright infringements. Youtube is great for marketing, yet people still believe that it infringes legal rights. Many people are divided on the subject of Youtube, but I can't help loving the witty blogs of Hank singing Accio Deathly Hallows, silly prank calls impersonating Christopher Walken, and the adorable little potter puppet pals that make me snicker quietly at my desk every time I watch it.

So I can't help but be defensive of the little things that give me joy. My standpoint I assume is obvious. For those of you who are against Youtube all I can say is (puts up right hand) “Don't Hate!”
