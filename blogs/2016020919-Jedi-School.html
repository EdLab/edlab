Jedi School

EdLab Studios

February 9, 2016


Not so long ago, in a studio not too far from here, Grandmaster Raab Rashi began the training of a group of young Jedi. Or Sith. Really, the decision is theirs as long as they check with their parents. The newest addition to Sword Class NYC's curriculum, Lightsaber Fitness for Kids and Youth, approaches the Star Wars brand of combat not from a cinematic, but rather from an historical perspective. As it turns out, the silver-screen phenomenon is based upon real techniques and choreography borrowed from German Longsword, Kendo, Samurai, and Iaido practices. Master Raab has dissected iconic Dark Side/Light Side duels to construct a class that takes 5 to 12 year olds through body movement and footwork, proper form, drawing of the sword, ascending and descending cuts, strikes, and powerful combinations that build week to week. But it’s not all just slaying opponents and the inevitable temptation to make awesome sound effects. Through embracing the Jedi Code, which itself draws from long-standing bushido and chivalric ideals, students come to understand and demonstrate reflection, leadership, and self-discipline. As Rashi states with all the wisdom of Yoda, "when you have control over the sword, what you’re really doing is controlling yourself. If you can control yourself, you can control your environment."

Music: "Electrifying Landscape" by Airglow
