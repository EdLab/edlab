EdLab Review: ISSUU

Kate Meersschaert

December 12, 2011

33 million readers consume digital media ranging from reports to magazines from ISSUU every month. Quickly growing into one of the world's largest virtual publishing and distribution platforms ISSUU has created a free, simple and powerful way for brands, groups and individuals to easily "publish" and share "print" material like brochures and magazines across devices. With "standard" and "pro" options anyone can create content easily and affordably through the platform. Pros: ISSUU is free, easy to use and the UI is beautiful. Users can upload almost any file-type ranging from a PDF to a Word doc. All publications are embeddable on your site and user/reader stats are available through your dashboard as well. The first reads I have given to publications like this Portugese magazine on educational technology are stunning and the authentically magazine-like layout is intuitive & feels just like navigating print pubs. Additionally, social media features are easily built-into each publications main page on the ISSUU site (see screenshot below). Cons: There doesn't seem to be a way to hyperlink to content within each publication. This would pose potential navigation issues for the developers, but this additional connectivity feels like a logical next step. EdLab Relevance: As we consider design, feature-set and functionality of NLTs it is great to consider this model of knowledge dissemination that is free, beautifully laid-out and device agnostic. Perhaps NTLs might even have a presence on ISSUU as a "publication?" Our Takeaway: ISSUU sets the standard for virtual self-publishing.
