Right Conditions Reduces Poverty

abagigah92

June 22, 2014

It is well documented that trade is much more efficient in open markets than in closed markets. However, when it comes to many developing countries, trade needs to be practiced in the right setting in order to achieve economical growth. Trade can reduce poverty and promote growth only with a strong market sector that is supported by a sound education system and a reliable government. These three major components are very important to sustainability and development in developing countries.

A research project evaluated 30 African countries since 1981 and concluded that those countries have a deep market sector. However, it is not supported by a sound educational system and the government is corrupt and unstable. Due to these conditions, it is hard to achieve success in the international trade market. On the contrary, imagine a nation that has a sound economy, education and governance. It will allow resources to be used efficiently. A developed financial market allows consumers, banks and investors to have confidence in doing business. Moreover, a sound educational system will help provide much of the skills needed to deal with the challenges and job opportunities from the financial sector. A strong government will provide trade policies that will make trade easier, faster and efficient.
