Charters Adopt Common Application Systems

abagigah92

September 30, 2013

The process of applying for charter schools has become a long and tedious process for most parents as they must go through several online applications. It is a long process searching and waiting for deadlines for lotteries specific to each school. There have been some recent changes among administrators across the country in hopes of making the application process easier for parents. A small but growing number of school districts, as well as charter school organizations, have rolled out new programs such as universal enrollment systems and common applications to centralize and streamline the process. This was organized to combat the confusion and make applying to charters easier and more transparent. Among the efforts, Newark and District of Columbia school systems are making plans to implement universal enrollment systems for their district-run and charter schools for the 2014-15 school year. Moreover, Denver started a centralized enrollment program called SchoolChoice that started in 2010 and New Orleans is also taking the same initiative for a universal enrollment system called OneApp.
