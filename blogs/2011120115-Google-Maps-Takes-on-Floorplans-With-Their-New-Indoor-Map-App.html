Google Maps Takes on Floorplans With Their New Indoor Map App

jvazquez

December 1, 2011

The newest innovation in cartography is turning buildings inside out! From the people who brought you the ability to search for an address and see the house you grew up in (down to the dent in the siding that you made while playing baseball in the front yard) comes an even MORE detailed look at your searchable destinations. Google has created a mapping system that allows you to orient yourself to the inside of a building. Indoor mapping! With this app, available on Google Maps 6.0 for Android, you are able to see exactly where you are within a building and how you can get to where you need to go. As you walk up and down different levels, the map auto-updates to display the new information. So futuristic! It's the juiced up version of the standing maps/directories found within malls and large campuses. The Mall of America in Minneapolis. Notice the blue locator dot! Indoor mapping only works if indoor map data is available. Currently, Google has partnered with major retailers in USA and Japan, namely: Mall of America, IKEA, The Home Depot, select Macy's and Bloomingdale's, Daimaru, Takashimaya and Mitsukoshi locations
