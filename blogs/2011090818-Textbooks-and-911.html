Textbooks & 9/11

Kate Meersschaert

September 8, 2011

In honor of the 10th anniversary of September 11th, the New York Times created a powerful, interactive site: The Reckoning: America and the World a Decade after 9/11. Visually arresting, full of facts and touching in the inclusion of personal accounts of the attacks, this feature is a beautiful example of form and function working together to serve the greater good... in this case, a robust, humanistic exploration of one of the nation's darkest moments. As a part of this exploration an article is included on the evolution of the portrayal of these attacks in textbooks both in the US and abroad. For example, the article cites the change in US school texts as moving from an emphasis on "patriotism" and "heroism" to more graphic images of the event in recent years. Explore 9/11 through the lens of vialogues by searching for #9/11Anniversary. What other resources have you found helpful in examining and making sense (if this is possible) of these events?
