Visiting the J-School

Jihii Jolly

June 29, 2015

(private) Last week, Diana, Jackie & I went along with some other TC folks to tour the Journalism School facilities. Lots of inspiration for the learning theatre there, as well as studio set-ups. The purpose of the visit was to familiarize neighboring Columbia communities with the broadcast tech offerings (TV and radio) the J-school is capable of and the fact that, for a reasonable fee, we could use them. The school is fitted with fiber optic cables and various networks use their broadcast facilities for shows, and they also do lots of high profile events that are livestreamed (i.e. Pulitzer Prize announcements). Some features of note: -The Brown Institute for Innovation, which also houses the Tow Center for Digital Journalism, both of which exist in a newly renovated space pictured below that sports a 10k (!) projector and is used as a classroom, as well as for collaborative working and events -Apple TV in all classrooms to make airplay easy -soft walls to cancel echo, which is great for sound at events -big windows with lots of natural light that can be controlled by heavy drapes if darkness is required -10 small editing suites, each equipped with 1 computer, which students can use to post-up storyboards, play sound out loud, and work on documentary uninterrupted (...having 1-2 such spaces would be very useful for EdLab Studios considering the sound/light distractions that come with an open workplace) Photos!
