Trends in Ed, 11.02.09

Arianna Choi

November 2, 2009

On iPhone, eBooks Outdo Games The most popular iPhone application? It used to be games until eBook apps overtook the games category since September of this year. New research from mobile application analytics service provider Flurry tracked the number of iPhone apps submitted to the iTunes App Store per month in order to predict which sector of Media & Entertainment might shake things up next. According to Flurry's research since the inception of the store in August 2008, there have been more apps released in the games category than any other. But in September, a new category - Books - surpassed the number of game apps released. This indicates that one out of every five new iPhone apps in October has been a book. The surge in eBook apps, however, needs to take into account that these counts are based on the number of new "unique" apps - meaning if a publisher/developer releases several (or many) book titles, each and every title gets counted as an unique app. For those interested in loyalty by application category, here's the result of a pulse taken this past summer on app retention across Apple, Android, Blackberry, and JavaME platforms.
