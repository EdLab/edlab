The Good, The Bad And The Ugly: The iOS 8.3 Update

Evans Frimpong

April 12, 2015

I recently made the bold switch from a Samsung Galaxy to the iPhone. My first experiences so far with the iPhone have been very good. I am impressed with its speed and many features. After going through the early stages of setup, I updated to Apple's new iOS 8.3 software. Beside the program's usual bug fixes and patches, more creativity and options were made available for the average Apple user. Apple introduced a diverse way of expression through its new “colorways” of emojis. This new feature allows users to cycle through different shades of emojis to fit their respective skin colors. Apple showed a sign of racial inclusion but their intentions of using iOS 8.3 as a platform to include human diversity went astray. Hours after the update, some were using the new emojis rightfully to racially identify themselves while others used it for racist and insensitive remarks on social media. On the good and positive side, the new update allows iPhone users to make phone calls via WiFi when signal strength is weak. There is a new CarPlay wireless feature that allows your iPhone or iPad to connect to a head unit without a lightning cable. Apple also updated their privacy settings by allowing users to filter messages they receive from unknown iMessage accounts. Even Siri gets more freedom and can make speakerphone calls. You can also opt out from entering your password when downloading free apps and use the Touch ID to speed things up.
