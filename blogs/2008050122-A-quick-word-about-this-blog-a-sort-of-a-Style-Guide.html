A quick word about this blog... a sort-of a Style Guide

bhughes

May 1, 2008

For all you newcomers to the lab (and anyone else who might have forgotten), I want to say a quick note about this blog's public/private feature, as well as some notes on style. As you may have realized, your blog always starts out life as "private." This means everyone in the lab can read it, but no one on the outside -- a great feature to encourage everyone to post to their heart's content without worrying too much about attaining perfection. However, I am always on the lookout to make your blog public, since we have hundreds of people from around the world who subscribe. To keep our blog fresh, I try to do this a few times per week â€” and look forward to diverse ideas on educational issues. Please be aware that there are several reasons I may not make your blog public. For examples, read more...

  • I've been asked by another manager to keep it private
  • It has already been blogged about before
  • You didn't embed your links with html
  • You didn't use this kind of "blockquote" tag for quoted text
  • There are too many grammatical mistakes
  • You didn't editorialize enough
  • You didn't provide a good link to a blog you stole from
  • You still haven't written a bio (sign in, click on yourself, click on the "edit" tab, and click on the "personal information" tab)
  • You've violated the general feel-good spirit of the EdLab blog
    • But, in the end, your contributions are always appreciated, so keep blogging!
