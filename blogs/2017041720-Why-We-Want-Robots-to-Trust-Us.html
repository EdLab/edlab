Why We Want Robots to Trust Us

saraehardman

April 17, 2017


We’ve long asked whether we, as humans, can trust robots. But we’ve neglected to ask the counterpart question: can robots trust us? The human-robot interaction lab at Tufts University is exploring this question. Matthias Sheutz, the program’s director, believes experimentation is important as robots become more integrated into our daily lives, including as household, classroom, or service-oriented companions.

"The overarching goal in our lab is to make robots natural and safe for humans," says Sheutz. "We want them to blend into human societies and act in a way that is consistent with our moral and normative expectations." Part of blending into human societies involves forming relationships and loyalties with particular people. This is impossible without trust. And if robots don’t learn to trust, the ideas of ownership and allegiance between human and bot couldn’t exist.

This brings us to some serious philosophical questions: Why is it important for robots to integrate seamlessly into human societies? Should humans trust robots as much as robots trust humans? In what circumstances is it important for a robot to trust a human? Help us ponder these questions on Vialogues.
