Will Kooaba's 'Shortcut' Kill the QR Code?

Kate Meersschaert

February 10, 2012

Do you QR? A vast majority of the population does not, as illustrated by the infographic below, many people feel confused by these increasingly ubiquitous, scannable designs. One company may help to make them completely obsolete, Kooaba. Kooaba's mission, as stated on their site is to, "unlock the information captured in images using sophisticated image recognition technology" and the group, founded in 2006, has done just that. Using a smartphone camera, Shortcut allows users to simply take a picture of text or even an image (with the Shortcut symbol present) and instantly be connected to similar content online. Will this "shortcut" between image capture and information discovery become the newest way to experience the printed world? Advertising with scannable images and text could forever replace the pixelated, black and white QR code. How might a teacher adapt this visual image recognition technology for the classroom? Could smartphone cameras become the search engines of the future?
