Nelsan Ellis RIP. And the Tendency of Art to Preconceive Social Reality

dinosossi2

July 9, 2017


Fans of True Blood are sad at the unexpected passing of actor Nelsan Ellis.

Ellis portrayed the flamboyantly gay short order cook Lafayette on the camp television series chronicling the adventures of Louisiana vampires.

Executive producer Alan Ball, who is openly gay, has been up front with LGBTQI issues across his highly successful career including Six Feet Under (Michael C. Hall as David Fisher) and American Beauty (Chris Cooper as Colonel Frank Fitts).


One of the most important implications of the narratives of Lafayette and David, especially given their relation to weekly episodic television, is that their sexual orientation, although presumably different than that of many viewers, becomes normalized over time.

And luckily this kind of non-traditional inclusion becomes less important over time.

Think of the initial uproar regarding Ellen DeGeneres coming out on Ellen.

This later paved the way for a show like Will & Grace (Eric McCormack as Will) featuring a gay lead character and Modern Family (with Jesse Tyler Ferguson and Eric Stonestreet as the couple Mitchell Pritchett and Cameron Tucker respectively), among others.

Unfortunately, social mores take an incredibly (and excruciatingly…) long time to shift to their rightfully inclusive positions. Luckily television can play a small role on this.

Thanks Nelsan Ellis with forwarding the broader mission of accepting all members of the LGBTQI community and playing your role with real gusto and style.
