Teachers: Born or Made?

Dana Haugh

November 3, 2014


What is the difference between a competent teacher and an excellent teacher? Is there something unteachable in those who can capture the minds and hearts of students? Or is excellence simply another skill that can be obtained with years of study and practice? The debate between what is innate and what can be acquired has persisted over the years, and most believe that great teaching is a skill that, essentially, can be taught. But isn’t there something more to teaching than just accurate recitations and point-driven lectures? Perhaps passion holds the key to excellence.
