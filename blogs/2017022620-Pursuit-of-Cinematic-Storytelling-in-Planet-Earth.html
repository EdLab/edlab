Pursuit of Cinematic Storytelling in Planet Earth

advisser

February 26, 2017

Vox Observatory's latest video on the BBC's Planet Earth II series explores some of the new techniques in visual storytelling which have recently been afforded to filmmakers through the introduction of relatively inexpensive technological developments.

The video explores the utilisation of dynamic camera movement in the pursuit of a "cinematic" and emotionally engaging experience - allowing the audience to feel both immersed in, and connected to, the world and story they are witnessing.

There is a clear emphasis here on how new technologies are able to drive unique storytelling and give the filmmakers unparalleled access to the "characters" of their films, not least through the implementation of inexpensive 3-axis gimbals, which were used extensively on Planet Earth II.

The BBC's Natural History Unit has always pushed the boundaries of new cinematographic technologies in order to create stunning imagery, and draw audiences into their worlds. And whilst the cinematography in and of itself is truly breathtaking, it is also worth remembering that, as Executive Producer Mike Gunton puts it:

No technology will ever replace the ability to be able to tell a story that grips, and fascinates, and emotionally connects with an audience.
