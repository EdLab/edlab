The First Step To Solving Education Problems? Rethink the Problems!

jvazquez

November 4, 2012


@00:34 bhughes: "unbundling." ah, yet another neologism for analysis...

@2:29 Katezvdp: Energy & enthusiasm is contagious! Pair it with wonder, openness and realistic problem-solving & you have a recipe for potential school reform success!

Based in New Orleans, 4.0 schools is "a community of curious people committed to solving the toughest problems in education." Its community of problem solvers is wide and varied: educators mix with technicians, designers, engineers, and business leaders to come up with creative solutions to education’s most plaguing problems. 4.0 schools goes beyond imagining a new educational landscape, though; thought leaders are encouraged to put their ideas to paper and develop working prototypes that can be placed in front of a key audience (i.e., teachers, school administrators, and/or students).

4.0 schools aims to equip leaders with a new mindset, a new way of thinking about problems. They attempt to do so through a series of classes they call "essentials." Essentials teach 3 primary skills: empathy, unbundling, and prototyping. The more ambitious of the bunch might be interested in their fellowship program, a 4-week-long program intended for people who have developed an idea and are looking to launch it.

Combining entrepreneurship and education is a clever way to encourage creative problem solving within an industry that can certainly use more outside-the-box thinking. Curiosity is the driving force behind the world’s most significant innovations. It is this motivation that the team behind 4.0 schools attempts to instill in its students.
