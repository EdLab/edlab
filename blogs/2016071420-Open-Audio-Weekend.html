Open Audio Weekend

EdLab Studios

July 14, 2016


Between podcasts, oral history collections, and radio news reports, the web is a treasure trove of information. The problem is, no one can find it. Search engines fail to locate recorded audio without full-text transcripts or detailed meta-data while those who are deaf or hard of hearing miss out on this information entirely. Together We Listen is a collaboration between New York Public Library and The Moth, dedicated to promoting audio accessibility on the web. Through the help of Pop-Up Archiveâ€™s speech-to-text services, countless hours of audio from live Moth performances and NYPL's Community Oral History Project were processed into comprehensive computer-generated transcripts. But those only go so far. Human intervention is required to make corrections and fill in gaps where computers fail. Open Audio Weekend is a two-day hackathon that brings representatives from data journalism, programming, data analysis, audio production, digital humanities, design, and the heritage sector into one room to develop innovative solutions and reinvent the way we access and interact with our ever-growing cache of audible documentation from an array of sources. Motivated by the themes of listening, discoverability, accessibility, and engagement this collection of audio stakeholders put their heads together to develop prototypes and proofs of concept that attendees and participating organizations can continue to work on and draw inspiration from even after the weekend comes to a close.
