[11:15] Social Technology and Education - cont'd

jvazquez

August 14, 2009

Fuse Fly is a social network for homeschooled kids (age 13+) and parents. It connects geographically removed students. Some features that students can look forward to are: profiles, messaging, chat, groups, image galleries, forums and more. Other features:

  • Sections for parents and teens
  • Safety features: Chat filter, demerit system, automated user suspension, student and adult moderators, family center

They chose to use Elgg because...
  • It is Open Source. Open source is adaptable: it can be modified to fit your needs, you own your data, easy point of entry
  • They already use moodle, php, ubuntu, mysql
  • Because Elgg stood out
  • Active community, written in PHP, built-in features fit our needs, easily extendable
They have learned lessons on...
  • Automatic content filtering
-Initially was a primary tool to keep site clean from unwanted data -It is hard to police this. So, they created the student moderator program. These students volunteer to st on the site and monitor for inappropriate content. They sit in on biweekly meetings, too, in order to learn about new features. They can ban users, flag content. They have a special status on the site. The “cool kids” :P
  • Privacy vs monitoring
-Finding balance- separation of parent and student areas -Limited access to demerit content -Notifications only for significant issues
  • Documentation for users
-Increase in users led to increase in support -Created videos and documentation -To be released at the end of August 2009 And finally...
  • Inspiration and application
-It is about socialization… not entirely education
