"Cheating is the pedagogy of the internet"

bhughes

June 8, 2006

I ran accross this fun and informative lecture by Jon Ippolito while researching cheating. It's the online version of a lecture he gave on campus a couple years ago while I was taking a course at Columbia... He discusses various tensions between cultural production (in general) and the current "culture" of intellectual property law... and the related subject of intellectual honesty. Watch and be introduced to such goodies as: The Net-Art Diagram The Unreliable Archivist Network Auralization for Gnutella For me, one interesting part of this presentation is how Jon is all over the map in terms of ideas and criticisms, but how he pulls it all together to support his project (The Pool). I'm not sure any of his work here (and related writing) counts as well-researched material, but you can't say the guy isn't passionate. (In other words, he's a bit of a "radical.") I've been trying to take some of the ideas he touches on here and push them forward a bit. The law stuff is great, but perhaps insurmountable for a lowly aesthetic philosopher. In lieu of that, I'm working on the cheating/academic honesty angle. i.e., What kind of conceptualization of education do we need to make room for new technologies that accelerate cheating? (Maybe it would turn out to be a conceptualization we always needed?)
