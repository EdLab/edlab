Showing Your Cards

Jenny Shen

December 16, 2015


There has always been a market for card games for children in the toy industry, with many of them focused on fantasy worlds and fictional characters. Second Avenue Learning wanted to take advantage of that by introducing an educational element into the mix. TARGETS features characters based on the periodic table of elements and allows for game play both through the physical cards as well as an augmented reality setting.

The card game offers students the opportunity to learn about atomic and molecular structures, and the augmented reality provides them the chance to solve science problems in a 3D world. For example, students may be prompted to create water molecules in both forms of game play. Users can play individually or with others, and the device tracks activity and progress over time.

Would you be interested in learning science through card play? Is an augmented reality world more engaging than traditional forms of science instruction? Chime in on Vialogues.
