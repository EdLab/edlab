Home Journey 6.4.2009: The ArtLab of MOCA Shanghai and the EdLab Space Design

Ting Yuan

June 5, 2009

Much influenced by contemporary artists who won their names in the western world (e.g., Bing Xu, Weiwei Ai, Guoqiang Cai.), the burgeoning Chinese contemporary art has just started to draw attention in China in the recent years. The Museum of Contemporary Art Shanghai (MOCA Shanghai), established in 2006, is the first non-profit, independently-operated contemporary art institution in Shanghai. Though it is about 1/10 the size of MOMA, the government grants the institute this piece of land within the People's Park which is next to Shanghai government as the center of the city. The ArtLab of MOCA Shanghai (see the pictures below) is an art and design project that transforms the third floor of MOCA Shanghai into a "live" exhibition. Through contemporary artworks, music, design, and inspired cuisine and cocktails, people can get together here to eat, to relax, and to chat -- Can the ArtLab give us some enlightenment in designing our EdLab space? Do we want to create some innovative art works/decorations/signs on the wall in addition to our ideas about painting guiding maps on the floor as presented at the EdLab seminar?
