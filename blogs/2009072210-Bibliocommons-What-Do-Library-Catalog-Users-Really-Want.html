Bibliocommons: What Do Library Catalog Users Really Want?

Frank Webster

July 22, 2009

BiblioCommons is a new social discovery system for libraries that replaces all user-facing OPAC functionality, allowing for faceted searching and easier user commenting and tagging and is expected to be implemented by half of Canada's public libraries: BiblioCommons Emerges: “Revolutionary” Social Discovery System for Libraries Meanwhile, the company is so busy in the field that their web site is just one page of user comments and a search box for their pilot project at the Oakville Public Library (Oakville, Ontario, Canada): Bibliocommons This article from RSS4LIB by Martha Whitehead, Queens University, and Steve Toub, BiblioCommons, reports on an Ontario Council of University Libraries study of the academic research process: User-Generated Content and Social Discovery in the Academic Library Catalogue: Findings from User Research -- Access 2008 Catalogs are solitary experiences, but learning and research are social activities.
