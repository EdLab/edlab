Event Recap: #SharkTankEDU #ELL Edition

Kate Meersschaert

July 25, 2014

PRIVATE!!!!***** Last night Carmen, Chris, Mason and I ventured down to @ThoughtWorksNYC for the next installment of InnovateNYC's #SharkTankEDU series. Past #SharkTankEDU events have been great. With EXCELLENT panelists and more or less, interesting companies as presenters. However, this particular edition left us all cold. Why? Here are my takeaways of things to avoid for future EdLab events:

  • Be sure the panelists (in this case ELL educators) are ALL prepared to ask questions. A panel during which only 3 of the 6 participant educators participate is awkward.
  • Limit the time for demos! The event was 2 hours long. With only three presenters. This was WAY too long for each presenter. EdLab models this well at our Demo Nights and guests have expressed thanks (thanks Laura).
  • Open Q&A up to the audience, it is odd to sit through a presentation, but, to not be able to interact or ask questions. Instead, only panelists are encouraged to participate.
On a positive note, it was great to have a chance to share our NLT review of @EllevationED (see below) and tease the community with our upcoming coverage of Leksi founder, Danelson Rosa's GroundBREAKERS interview that will be featured on Vialogues. Also, it is always fun to see "Friend of EdLab" and DOE veteran, Preeti Birla (the evening's organizer). #SharkTankEdu Tweets
