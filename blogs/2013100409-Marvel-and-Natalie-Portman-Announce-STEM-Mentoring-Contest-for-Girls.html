Marvel and Natalie Portman Announce STEM Mentoring Contest for Girls

Nara Kasbergen

October 4, 2013

I've never been a huge fan of Natalie Portman, but my appreciation for her did increase after her portrayal of Jane Foster in Thor, which famously passed the Bechdel test in its opening scene (while most mainstream movies fail to pass the Bechdel test at all). Now, Ms. Portman has once again won a lot of points with me after partnering with Marvel to offer a STEM mentoring contest for high school girls. The website offers girls resources to "find a woman working in a STEM job that they find interesting in their area, and also get tips on how to interview and introduce themselves". The grand prize recipients (a trip to LA to see the new Thor: The Dark World on opening day) will be selected based on entrants' submitting an " unedited five minute video in which they talk about their love of science, their experience interviewing their mentor, and what steps they might take to embark on a STEM career". I think this is a very cool idea and, while maybe not groundbreakingly innovative, certainly a step in the right direction, and I hope that other movie producers and actors will be inspired to hold similar contests in the future.
