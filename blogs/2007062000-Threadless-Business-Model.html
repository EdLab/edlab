Threadless Business Model

HSC

June 20, 2007

Threadless, an online t-shirt retailer, relies on Web-savvy designers and an active online community to drive its business. Specifically, they allow customers to rank t-shirt designs submitted by artists and then print only the winners. The shirts cost as little as $4 each to make and sell for $15 and up. So far, Threadless has sold out every shirt it has printed. The print-on-demand model seems to work for t-shirts but not other products (e..g, wallpaper, ties). Does anyone have any explanations for why this approach works for some things but not others? Read more about the Threadless business model and the incentives it offers to artists and customers (e.g., winning artists get $2,000 in cash and merchandise).
