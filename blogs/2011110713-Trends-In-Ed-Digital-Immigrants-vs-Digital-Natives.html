Trends In Ed: Digital Immigrants vs. Digital Natives

Janet Jang

November 7, 2011

This past weekend, my cousin visited NYC to do research for her thesis on education policy in urban schools. We grabbed lunch and ended up talking a lot about this idea of “digital immigrants vs. digital natives”. Many researchers and educators have debated about the validity of differentiating teachers and students in this way. They say the word “immigrant” makes teachers sound antiquated and unreliable with technology in the classroom (The debate on stigmatizing “immigrants” is a WHOLE other can of worms). Here are a couple of articles and a lecture unpacking this issue: How To Best Educate Our Digital Natives In Classroom of Future, Stagnant Scores Are You A Digital Native or a Digital Immigrant? Vialogue: Digital Immigrants vs. Digital Natives My only thought about this — Is this really going to be an issue anymore? I mean, as more young “digital native” teachers enter the nation's school system, there really won't be many immigrants left anymore. Instead of continuing to argue about these categories and labels, I think we should focus on how these new digital native teachers can better use the technology they already know as effective tools in the classrooms. After all, just because you know how to drive doesn't necessarily make you a “good” driver.
