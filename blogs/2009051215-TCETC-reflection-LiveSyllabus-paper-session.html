TCETC reflection: LiveSyllabus (paper session)

Ting Yuan

May 12, 2009

Presentation can be viewed at: http://prezi.com/59327/ Live Syllabus: Building an Intelligent Networked Course Syllabus Tool (Hui Soo, Faisal, Huning, Pravin, Adam, Ting) LiveSyllabus, is an "intelligent" syllabus building tool that a few of us are designing and developing. It has a number of unique functions and features: (1) an "intelligent" database agent which can "recognize" all the available electronic readings stored in the end database of the library, which will help reduce the current manual work of librarians; (2) incorporating class schedules into Google Calendar; (3) suggestion of related recent publications within a similar topic; (4) convenience of inputing and researching available electronic readings; (5) convenience of using previous syllabi; (5) realtime feedback and course improvement, etc. The presentation on Sunday afternoon went very well, and we got the following feedback and questions from the audience (5 out of over 20): 1. For students, there should be a list of non-repeated readings from all classes he/she is taking? 2. Would you expand the access to instructors outside of Columbia (what are the restrictions for them?) 3. Would you use Amazon's recommendation system? 4. Issues of sharing syllabi within the system (Is every syllabus searchable by instructors? Would professors be comfortable sharing their syllabi with other instructors? ) 5. Would you give options to tag the readings in addition to letting the system recognize readings based on titles. You are more than welcomed to let us know your further thoughts/comments/suggestions!
