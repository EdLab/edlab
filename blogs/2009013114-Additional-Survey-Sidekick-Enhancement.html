Additional Survey Sidekick Enhancement

Huning Dai

January 31, 2009

After joining Edlab, my first assigned task assigned was to improve a specific aspect of Survey Sidekick: - Ticket #3 (assigned defect): Floating tool for editing questions is difficult to use - fix it in place on the screen. If you have used Survey Sidekick before, you know that once your survey becomes longer, the floating tools panel is pretty annoying. In order to achieve this, there are two subtasks: 1. Keep the tool panel in the right of the screen. 2. Implement scrollbar in the mainbar. That's to say, instead of letting the tool panel fly around, users can now use the scrollbar when the survey becomes longer. I was assigned this task on Thursday, and finished near the end of the day on Friday. The reason it took so long was because: 1. The code was not written by me. Reading and modifying someone else's code is far more complicated than writing one's own code. 2. There is not enough documentations regarding Survey Sidekick. Whenever I am looking for a variable or function, I had to read the entire code. It reminds us again that we should keep documentation of large projects. To compare the before and after. check out these two links: Before: http://surveysidekick.com/edit.php After: http://jay.tc.columbia.edu/survey-hd2210/htdocs/edit.php If you have any suggestions, please don't hesitate to tell me or leave a comment to this post.
