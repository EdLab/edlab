Debt Ceiling

abagigah92

October 7, 2013

The history of the U.S. debt ceiling began in 1917 with the Second Liberty Bond Act, which help financed the United States' entry into World War I. The US Treasury borrowed money to pay for legislation from Congress. The limit to borrow is determined by Congress and increasing the limit is what is known as the debt ceiling. Since then Congress has raised the statutory limit on the amount of U.S national debt about 110 times within the last decade alone. The debt ceiling is important because it helps the U.S pay its bills since we spend more than we generate. Borrowing is important in order to avoid defaults that can destroy not only the U.S market, but also the entire global. Recently, President Obama have spoken about the need for Congress to increase the debt ceiling in order for the country to pay its bills and avoid default. A default will result in having a bad credit that will increase interest rates, prices and programs being shut because of shortage of money.
