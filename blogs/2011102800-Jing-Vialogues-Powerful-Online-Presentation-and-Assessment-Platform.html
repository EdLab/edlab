Jing + Vialogues = Powerful Online Presentation and Assessment Platform

chingfu

October 28, 2011

This past summer I had an opportunity to teach a course about social studies and new technology in the social studies program at Teachers College. At the end of this course, I conducted an experiment to screencast students' presentations via Jing and put these screencasts on Vialogues. Although this experiment was not successful in my summer course (it's a last-minute proposal and students had been overwhelmed with all course assignments), I think there are some strengths in this model if implemented properly: 1). In a typical one-hundred-minute TC course with fifteen students, each student will only get less than seven minutes to present and gather feedback on his/her class presentation. This model will help each presenter to get additional feedback on his/her presentation on Vialogues. 2). These Vialogues allow students who are absent from course presentations to participate completely online (which is the case for one of my students). As I reflected on this experiment recently, I realized that this model can work well with online education programs: instructors can ask students to upload their presentation screencasts to Vialogues and require students to provide comments on each other's presentations. In addition, these screencast Vialogues can serve as a new format of online course assessment/assignment, which is much more interactive than research papers and discussion forum assignments in most online courses. This seems to be another direction we could consider for Vialogues outreach efforts.
