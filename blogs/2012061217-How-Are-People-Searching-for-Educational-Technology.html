How Are People Searching for Educational Technology?

Greg Schrank

June 12, 2012

After having gone over potential marketing ideas this morning for Research Broker, I was interested in how people are searching for all things education technology related. I plugged a number of related terms into Google Insights to get an idea of how people are searching. Here are the results I got (click to open image in a separate window): We can clearly see "education technology" is far and away the most popular term (the rankings are relative on a scale of 100). Not terribly surprising, but interesting in the sense that "ed tech", a term I hear a lot around here, ranks fourth among the keywords I sampled. Plugging these same terms into Google AdWords Keyword Generator we get the following results:
"Education technology" and "education tech" have the highest searches/month, and have the highest competition (in Google AdWords, higher competition means you'll generally pay more for ads with these keywords). However, "educational technology" and "ed tech" still get over 100k+ global monthly searches and have less competition, meaning it's easier (relatively speaking) to get ranked higher on Google result pages than these other terms. As we go forward and continue to think about inbound marketing strategies, this is something we should consider. Meet the customers where they are! PS: Both Google tools featured are free, though you'll have to sign up for AdWords.
