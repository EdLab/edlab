Grading Obama's College Scorecard Site

Brian.Sweeting

September 24, 2015


A record-breaking Twitter profile, the first presidential AMA on Reddit, the most social media-savvy campaign in U.S. politics—President Obama has some impressive online accomplishments. So is his new college comparison site one of them? Collegescorecard.ed.gov is a freely available database of basic stats like graduation rates and annual costs for a long (but not comprehensive) list of U.S. colleges. It's supposed to help students and parents make university-level decisions, but it's been criticized as nothing more than a glorified data dump. Obama is good at perfecting his personal brand online, but overseeing agency sites (cough, healthcare.gov, cough) may not be earning him many likes. This current project has the president looking a little two thousand and late.
