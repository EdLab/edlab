Economics & Education: Shut Up John Stossel

Skanda Amarnath

June 30, 2011

Fred and I have made it clear before on our respective blogs why higher education, despite rising prices, is NOT a bubble. But John Stossel's different. He has to be a contrarian. Always. Yet more to the point, he's so keen on making some libertarian point in every issue, that he fails to realize there is no libertarian point to be made here. Federal subsidization is not the reason for rising tuition costs. Private institutions are major players in higher ed, so don't whine about a lack of competition. More to the point, ending subsidization of college will not do squat to keep out the slackers who ultimately drop out. It will only keep out the poorest who do not have access to financing. Are we to say to hell with equal opportunity? As a former libertarian and maybe even contrarian, I must say both are easy modes of thinking. It's harder and more rewarding to think seriously about the details than to have a S.O.P. when approaching any public issue.
