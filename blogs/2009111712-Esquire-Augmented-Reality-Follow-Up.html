Esquire Augmented Reality Follow-Up

Jeannie Crowley

November 17, 2009

In an earlier post I mentioned the new augmented reality version of Esquire magazine. I wasn't quite sure what the magazine or the interactions would look like. I found a YouTube video that provides more details. According to the video and the official site you need the following to access the Esquire augmented experience:

  • A webcam.
  • Software from the site.
  • An Internet connection.
  • The magazine.
    • After watching the video I wondered why anybody would want to engage in the clunky behavior of holding a magazine up to a webcam to access special features. Other than a one-time novelty factor, I can't imagine this having any real impact on a person's consumption of print vs. digital media. Thoughts?
