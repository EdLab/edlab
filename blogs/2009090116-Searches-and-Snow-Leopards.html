Searches and Snow Leopards

Jeannie Crowley

September 1, 2009

Every once in a while I receive search results that remind me what it is like to be a novice searcher. Concerned about the compatibility of Warcraft and the newest Mac OSX, I typed "warcraft snow leopard" into the search window. According to the top five sites:

Snow leopards are a specific subrace of cats that have adapted to the cold climate of the mountainous landscape of Dun Morogh. They sometimes threaten isolated dwarf outposts in the region.


I quickly changed my search to "warcraft snow leopard OSX" and received the desired information (instead of a description of a Warcraft animal). This may seem like a silly incident but many novice users would get stuck after that initial unhelpful set of results. When I was a teacher, I witnessed my students muddling through searches with queries such as "when did he die". Inexperienced users of all ages often expect the search engine just to know who they mean by "he" or to know what information they really want. My search error reminded me of the importance of teaching children not only how to critically analyze digital information but how to effectively find it in the first place.
