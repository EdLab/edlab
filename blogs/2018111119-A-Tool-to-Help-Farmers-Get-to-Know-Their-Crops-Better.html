A Tool to Help Farmers Get to Know Their Crops Better

Melanie.Hering

November 11, 2018


Farming is no easy profession; not only is it demanding in terms of lifestyle, it is unpredictable due to its dependence on the whims of the weather and local climate. Arable Labs, a small company funded by the National Science Foundation, has been working to create technology that might ease some of that difficulty, especially when it comes to being aware of what crops most need.

Arable Labsâ€™ new device is composed of sensors that assess weather and crop health, and the system uses machine learning to auto-correct national weather data so that it reflects more local conditions. The system determines how much fertilizer is being consumed, measures leaf temperature and moisture content, gauges sunlight, and gives disease predictions, all to help farmers make more informed decisions that won't risk wasting resources.
