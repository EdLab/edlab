3-D Printing , Simple as Paper Printing ?

Kevin.Camacho

July 5, 2017

My interest in 3-D printing was peaked when I worked with Alvaro to create hooks for the Quuppa system several months ago.  3-D printers have designs that are manually programmed into the printer. Each finished design is manually removed before starting a new print, which consumes some time. Schools and businesses usually have a trained person that handles all prints. This can be very expensive.
 Is 3-D printing therefore a cheaper alternative?

 

New Valence Robotics (NVBOTS), a startup out of MIT, has developed a 3-D printer “equipped with cloud-based queuing and automatic part removal, making print jobs quicker and easier for multiple users, and dropping the cost per part.” Though the printer, NV Pro, just launched this past April, it is being used in over 100 schools and businesses. According to their research, they have saved over 165,000 labor hours of printing.

 

 

Is this an option to consider for the library and other printing services at TC?
