El Sistema Music Education in LA

Kate Meersschaert

October 4, 2011

The Los Angeles Philharmonic is leading the charge in creating a new masters in music education program in the style of the global music education movement, El Sistema. Bard College and Longy School of Music will partner in the endeavor to grant the official degree and their first official event under the umbrella name "Take a Stand" will be a conference held in LA at the end of the year. For more specifics on the program, this piece from the NYT's explores this new program fully. From the NYT's: El Sistema aims to use the teaching of classical music to improve the lives of poor children and to help underprivileged neighborhoods. It has involved some 400,000 young people in Venezuela and spread to a number of countries, capturing the imagination of leading performers, teachers and executives in classical music. In light of our recent work with the Rock & Roll Forever Foundation, Young Arts and EdLab team interest in creating new apps for students around new media and music education, how can we best leverage technology to help teach young people about music?
