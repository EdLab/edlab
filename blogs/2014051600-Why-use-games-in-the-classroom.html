Why use games in the classroom?

Youssef Ballo

May 16, 2014

Most people might see video games as a waste of time. However, games can and have been used to in a number of areas such as learning, military planning, finance, and even urban planning. It is easier to run a few educational games to see which strategies work than to spend time and money implementing bad ideas. Most classroom instruction is composed of lectures. You see the occasional audio with and visual support. There are many advantages to use games as opposed to standard lectures. Educational games can be interactive and embraced in many different learning factors at once. It also allows students to learn by enjoying the game instead of just listening and taking notes. One way educators can replace lectures with video games is to translate the knowledge and views to a computer programmer and then develop a game system around that idea. If professors do not implement that game well into the classroom, it can be more of a distraction than a benefit.
