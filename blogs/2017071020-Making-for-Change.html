Making for Change

csd2126

July 10, 2017


Although Emily Pilloton was originally trained as an architect, she has applied the understandings of architecture and design to create meaningful change in the classroom. As the founder of Project H design, Emily provides students with the space and tools to bring their ideas to life. No matter the range of experiences students come to the Project H space with, Emily hopes that they leave with a sense of accomplishment.

Maker-based learning expands the types of experiences that are offered to students within school walls. A motto of Project H is that "we are never bored and we are never done." While Emily believes she is personally neither of those things, she hopes to instill this same motto in students. Through their work in making, Emily hopes students gain a sense that creating an artifact is just one experience that they can continue to build upon.
