First Impressions Matter: The Importance of Great Visual Design

Panisuan Chasinga

February 5, 2015

This article by Peep Laja is probably one of the most helpful articles on UX/UI I've read in months. According to it, visual impact has way more greater and longer-lasting influences on users' impressions about your site and product. Here are a few key takeaways from the article: Users love (or dislike) your site within the first 17-50 ms Yes, it's milliseconds with an 'm'. Google has recently confirmed this in their own research. And that jiffy first impressions can have eave a long-lasting influence on their future trust and engagement (or prejudice) to the product. Their study revealed that websites with low visual complexity and high prototypicality were perceived as highly appealing. Eyetracking study identifies key elements It takes 2.6 seconds for a user's eyes to land on that area of a website that influences their first impression most. The better the impression, the longer users stayed on the page and be more forgiving. Key elements that drew most interest from viewers are as follows:

  • The logo
  • Main nav menu
  • Search box
  • Main image
  • Written content
  • Bottom of the site
    • First impressions are 94% design related British research shows that the look and feel of a website is actually the more important driver of first impressions than content. 94% of all feedback the test participants gave was about design versus 6% about the actual content. Inspiration drives better first impression User want to imagine and feel, not think. It's probably better to use inspirational and captivating photo than to give complex textual information. To sum things up, we humans are emotional and impulsive, and our intuition reacts quicker to things like color, proportion, scale and size. Intuition is what makes us all able to be in love after all, regardless of who or what the subject is about. More can be read on the actual blog here.
