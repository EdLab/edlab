NLT Profile Questions

Christine.Odenath

March 27, 2019

I'm writing seeking for advice/input from the EdLab community. I've been publishing Profile articles on New Learning Times and am wondering if we're asking our interviewees all the right questions.


As avid readers of NLT Profiles, you probably don't need this context, but entertain me as I provide the following background information on Profiles:

  • The purpose of Profiles is to glean insight and inspiration from leaders in our fields of interest (learning, learning tech, education).
  • Profiles feature entrepreneurs, CEOs, founders, or other types of leaders in these fields of interest.
  • Profiles average 500-700 words in length, brief enough that you could read on your subway commute.
  • We publish a new Profile each week, every Mondays at 12pm ET.
  • We have a template that highlights five questions to guide the Profile (listed below). We can modify these questions as we see fit, but this is where I was hoping for some input.


Are there any questions you feel are missing from our template, or would like to see us ask our Profile candidates?


For example, I've easily surfaced interviews with a few profile candidates that directly address question #1. Given the essential nature of this question for understanding a person being profiled, I wonder if there's a way we can reframe question #1 to get at something deeper or more interesting (for the sake of the readers and the interviewee, who may be sick of talking about that one experience that sparked their current work)...


New Learning Times Profile Questions: 

 

1) How did your educational trajectory and past professional experience shape your current work?

 

2) How do you hope your work will change the learning landscape?

 

3) What broad trends do you think will have the most impact on learning in the years ahead?

 

4) What are you currently working on & what is your next big project?

 

5) Who are the most interesting people you are following on Twitter (if you have a Twitter account)? Who are three people you recommend we feature in New Learning Times?
