One Hospital’s Newest Staff Member is a Robot

saraehardman

October 10, 2016


A humanoid robot is the newest staff member at a hospital in Belgium. The bot, named Pepper, can show patients to their rooms, hold conversations in 19 languages, and offer bedside comfort. In its first week on the job, Pepper was in the maternity ward, and patients had an overall positive reaction to it. "It’s another way of making contact, and maybe it’s reassuring for some people," said one patient. "The baby was reassured."

Perhaps one reason why Pepper is so appealing is that unlike some humanoid robots, Pepper was obviously not built to look too human. In fact, the small bot is genuinely cute and was programmed with patients’ needs in mind. Even slower-moving patients are able to keep up with Pepper’s easy pace, and children seem to find the robot particularly fun to interact with. The hospital is not planning on replacing human caretakers with robots, but maybe bots like Pepper could give patients a new hospital experience, one that will put a smile on their face.
