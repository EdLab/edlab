Starbucks, Wal-Mart Offering Classes - for College Credit

Carmel Addae

July 1, 2013

Wal-Mart and other fortune 500 companies are tired of colleges not providing the skilled workers they need and as such they've decided to offer their own college level classes. Some schools have already started offering these classes. The City University of Seattle offers classes called Barista Basics and Barista 101 for Starbucks workers. A student earns one and a half credit if they take each of the companies barista classes. Also, they receive three credits for a more higher level management class. It is now up to other colleges to accept these credits; Mary Beth, director ACE College and university partnership, believed that 2,000 college institutions will consider these credits. Hamburger University is where people aspiring to become managers at McDonald's spend part of their time every year to learn how to sharpen their business and leadership skills. The employees can earn about 23 to 27 credits in this school towards their associates and bachelor's degrees. Wal-Mart courses help its learners in time management, phone skills and managing diversity. In addition, they acquire certain skills which puts them ahead of other colleagues. There are tuition discounts for the employees as well.
