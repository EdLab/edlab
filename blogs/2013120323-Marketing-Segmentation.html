Marketing Segmentation

Elysee Dougan

December 3, 2013

Today in class, I learned about Marketing Segmentation. This technique gives companies the ability to market goods to different categories of people who share the same needs or wants as one another. Another way to think about this concept is simply breaking down a larger target market into many smaller target markets that share the same wants and needs for your product. There're different types of marketing segmentation and it is important to know which form of segmentation to use: demographic (age, race, gender, marital status, occupation, education or income), geographic(certain communities, state, region, country or group of countries), psycho-graphic (individual lifestyle), behavioral (behaviors, patterns, price sensitivity, brand loyalty and benefits they look for), business segmentation (geographic, customer type and behavior-based strategies which seems to be sort of a mix between them all).
