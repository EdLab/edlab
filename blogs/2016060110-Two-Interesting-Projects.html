Two Interesting Projects

saraehardman

June 1, 2016

Recently, I've come across two interesting projects happening around the world: 1) Researchers at Stanford have created humanoid robots to explore an abandoned shipwreck off the coast of France. The ship—one of King Louis XIV's fleet—has been neglected because it's so difficult to reach, until now. Using computers, the researchers can control their scuba-diver-shaped robots, send them to the depths of ocean, and collect video and pictures from the once-forgotten wreck. Check out the Vialogue and add your comments! 2) A team of Dutch scientists had a mission: to create a new Rembrandt painting. Using computers and laser technology, the team scanned every painting ever created by Rembrandt in order to add all the pieces together and find an average. After, they used a special 3D printer that could create layers that resembled brushstrokes to make the new painting. They unveiled it at a museum in the Netherlands shortly after. Watch the Vialogue and see whether you think their "new" Rembrandt painting looks believable! Also, comments always appreciated.
