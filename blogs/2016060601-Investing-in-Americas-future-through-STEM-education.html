Investing in America's future through STEM education

abagigah92

June 6, 2016

In this op-ed, Dean Kamen (founder of FIRST) and John R. Allen (Co-director at the Center for 21st Century Security and Intelligence) argues that we must invest more in STEM education as it will increasingly continue to play a prominent role in student success. Kamen and Allen argues that in too many of today’s neighborhoods, students are spending countless hours in the hopes of becoming the next Lebron James or Cam Newtown. This has led to a culture where children are not spending enough time studying or engaging with the STEM subjects. As we all know, US students are ranked the lowest in science and math among the world’s advanced economies. This deficit extends to our labor market. There are millions of jobs in the STEM fields that remains unfilled due to lack of qualified candidates. In their recommendations, they suggest that the government goes through with the funding it set out for STEM in the Every Student Succeeds Act. For instance, they note that a stipend of $5,000 per school roughly translates to an annual total cost in the region of the low hundreds of millions of dollar. This is a tiny amount that could shape the future of our country in the coming years.
