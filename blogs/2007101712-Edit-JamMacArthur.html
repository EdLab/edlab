Edit Jam/MacArthur

bhughes

October 17, 2007

Hi everyone. I've attached the proposal for the Edit Jam project. Please take a look and feel free to offer feedback (sorry, only positive feedback allowed at this point). We'll probably make this pitch again, to other grant partners and institutions, so let us know what you think. Here's the brief description: Our project, "The Edit Jam Methodology: Creating Digital Environments to Explore the Future of Learning," will evaluate certain learning environments and multimedia products as vehicles for bringing new technologies into schools. A series of six events plus a conference will constitute the Edit Jam project, allowing multiple constituencies of New York City students, teachers, and artists to participate. (read on) To support diverse learning opportunities and creative processes, we will use our nascent Edit Jam model. Edit Jam events support collaborative production, where participants explore ideas in a dynamic flow of media, technologies, and energies. The educational focus of each Edit Jam allows for participants to achieve new modes of expression, interactivity, and connectivity through art and technology. At each event, high school students and teachers work alongside expert participants–artists and technologists–as they experiment with media and technology. Each participant will create digital multimedia narrative products as they respond to information about current and future developments of the education sector. These products are central to the learning experience, as well as a major component of the research, as they are made available online after each event. In addition to assessing the effectiveness of Edit Jams as learning environments, we are investigating how these products support further engagement and extend the learning experience.
