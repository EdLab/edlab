Mozilla Open Badges Project

Kate Meersschaert

April 19, 2012

The Details... Mozilla has run the "Open Badges" project since 2010... but now, they offer even more! Fully turnkey badge management and development is now available through their "Open Badges" software. Check-it-out here. How to? The software, which is currently in beta launch, allows organizations with Django-like skills to create, manage and display badges to enhance everything from a blog to a complex LMS. Check-out the on-boarding documentation here. From organizations like Pixar and NASA to the blogger down the hall (almost) anyone can get skin in the "badging" game. The group is planning to launch a "light" version for those of us without hardcore programming skills that is TBA... learn more here. EdLab Relevance... How could the EdLab use this software here at the lab? For the winners of LaunchPad Demo Nights? On EdNode... ResearchBroker? Add your thoughts below! As Mozilla states on the "Open Badges" site..."Learning today happens everywhere." Now we have the tools to encourage and reward that learning within our ecosystem and applications. One last EdLab-relevant thought, badges that users create are stored in "backpacks"... sounds like PocketKnowledge has a badged-out buddy!
