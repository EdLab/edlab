You Might Be Awkward, But This Robot Is Not

saraehardman

February 26, 2017


When we think of robots, we think futuristic, efficient, and . . . awkward? Perhaps the last word doesn’t come to mind often, but if you think about it, robots just aren’t aware of human etiquette. A team of Stanford scientists, however, is changing that with their new socially aware robot, Jackrabbot.

Jackrabbot is learning how to do something that no robot before has ever done; it’s learning how to make on-the-fly decisions about right-of-way and personal space while navigating crowded environments. In other words, Jackrabbot is learning how not to be awkward. Instead of following a fixed set of rules, this hat and tie-donning bot is programmed with machine learning to master rules as they arise. Yet this technology has potential well beyond Jackrabbot. In particular, the research team thinks this technology could significantly improve self-driving cars and caretaking robots.

What does it mean for a robot to learn how to do something rather than be programmed to do it? How could human-robot relationships change if robots are taught to be socially aware? Where else could this technology be beneficial? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
