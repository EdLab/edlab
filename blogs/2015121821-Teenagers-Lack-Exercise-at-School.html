Teenagers Lack Exercise at School

AbdulMalik

December 18, 2015

Most schools are mandated to set aside some time for recess in order for students to remain physically active. Physical activity ranges from simply walking to class to engaging in more vigorous forms of exercise. Researchers conducted a study to figure out whether students remain physically active as they should in order to maintain a healthy life. The way this research was conducted is very interesting when you consider how difficult it is to identify which group of teenagers to focus on. With the help of a GPS tracking device, researchers found that teenagers were physically active for 23 minutes at school. This number made up almost half the time the average teenager spends on physical activity. This research sheds more light on the importance of recess. Most people (especially teenagers) take recess or any form of physical activity for granted. Even though physical activity is usually designed for students to have fun, teachers and parents need to constantly remind teenagers of the need for them to maintain a healthy lifestyle by engaging in the simple acts such as walking or playing their favorite sport.
