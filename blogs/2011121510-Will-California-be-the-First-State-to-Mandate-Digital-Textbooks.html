Will California be the First State to Mandate Digital Textbooks?

Kate Meersschaert

December 15, 2011

California may become the first state to mandate the creation and adoption of digital textbooks for college students. As explored in this article the new bill proposes that the state find content providers to create 50 new digital textbooks that would be free to students in the digital form and at a minimal cost in a printed form. The move to free digital textbooks was partly Inspired by a recent estimate that college texts cost CA students over $1,300 per year and "trigger cuts" to the higher ed sector. To accomplish this goal legislators will create a bidding process for faculty, publishers and other individuals to create "digital open source textbooks and related courseware." All materials developed, mostly lower-level intro course material, would be placed under Creative Commons licensure and would allow instructors to customize all material. Additionally, under the new legislature, publishers would be required to provide free hardcopies of widely-used textbooks to campus libraries.
