Soft Robot or Rigid? Now You Don’t Have to Pick

Melanie.Hering

June 19, 2018


Researchers at Harvard and the Wyss Institute have created a robotic structure that can rapidly change stiffness and structural dynamics on command, bridging the gap between soft and rigid robotics. The device consists of multiple layers of flexible material wrapped in plastic connected to a vacuum source. When the vacuum is applied, the material becomes stiff and shock absorbent.

This new design merges adaptability with precision, resulting in a technology that can have many uses and adapt to new environmental needs. The research team believes this technology can have many different uses in the future, from landing gear to surgical procedures.
