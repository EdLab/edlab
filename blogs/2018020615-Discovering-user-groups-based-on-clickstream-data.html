Discovering user groups based on clickstream data

SeungYeon.Lee

February 6, 2018

I'm sharing my slides used in today's D&R meeting in case you might want to see the results again.

https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1ZZM_26mzrsMc13bWB5xyBcjdR1bIO-D40wHxD8GlrUA/edit?usp=sharing


Also, followings are the ideas we discussed for the first activity.

Thanks for sharing all good ideas!


[Zhou's group]

- User behaviors after incorporating time sequence of actions

- Something is missing in the current data; for example, if users click the time code, it would directly play a certain part of a video (possible to have no data for watching 3 seconds or 50% etc)

- Whether people participate in discussion only once or come back later again for discussion

- Effect of pedagogical intervention; what would be the effect on the behaviors if instructors provided guidance/instructions on vialogues or discussion?


[Jo's group]

- What would be the culture differences across different countries

- Is there any communality with regard to whether all the comments were posted within an hour, same day or spread out?

- Difference with different devices, iPad, webs or phons?

- Are the discussion topics similar to the subjects of videos?


[Hui Soo's group]

- Qualitative analysis of discussion contents -to see if people really share different opinions (not like "good ideas" or "agree") and learn each other?


[Chris' group]

- Behaviors with different layers of users, for example, people at TC community, outside users, different countries
