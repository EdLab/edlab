Report on US Education

idrissa10

June 3, 2015

Every now and then, the federal government takes it upon itself to assess our public schools. A recent report from Congress shows that there is an increase in public school registration of students from low-income families. According to the report, 49.8 million students were enrolled in public schools during 2012-13. There's also a decrease in high school dropouts. This shows there is a shift happening within U.S education. Most families who are considered poor now value education more compared to previous years. In a previous era, some families opted for their children to work and support the family rather than enrolling in school. There could be several reasons for this. Our knowledge economy requires today’s and tomorrow’s workers to possess certain skills such as critical thinking. As such, families and students could be motivated by a need to make themselves ready and eligible for tomorrow’s job market.
