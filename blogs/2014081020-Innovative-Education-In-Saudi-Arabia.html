Innovative Education In Saudi Arabia

Nantwi

August 10, 2014


Saudi Arabia’s education system is going through a transformation. In an effort to move towards a knowledge-based economy, administrators and other stakeholders in the Kingdom have established the iThra Youth Initiative, which has a special focus on STEM, collaboration, and creativity. The initiative comes at a time when Saudi Arabia and the region are going through political, social, and economic changes. The initiative, targeted at 12-18 year olds, is hoping to reach over two million Saudi students, mainly in urban and rural public schools.

Through workshops, students are able to collaborate, engage and learn from their peers and educators. Student reflections about their experiences at the workshops show that the initiative is starting to have an impact on their learning, but there is concern about lack of knowledge retention once it ends. Ultimately, the iThra Initiative hopes to prepare students as citizens and professionals who will drive the Kingdom to unprecedented heights in the region and across the globe.
