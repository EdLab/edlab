How Fast are We Going?

Nara Kasbergen

May 10, 2013

We've talked before about how important it is to decrease page load times across our websites if we want to avoid our visitors browsing away too quickly. According to a blog post by Steve Souders, based on his keynote at the recent HTML5 Developers Conference in San Francisco, entitled "How fast are we going?":

- Bing found that searches that were 2 seconds slower resulted in a 4.3% drop in revenue per user. - When Mozilla shaved 2.2 seconds off their landing page, Firefox downloads increased 15.4%. - Shopzilla saw conversion rates increase 7-12% as a result of their web performance optimization efforts. - Making Barack Obama's website 60% faster increased donation conversions 14%.
Steve then goes on to show a lot of data related to various metrics evaluating how the web has gotten faster over time. What's really interesting is that the data shows that the web has indeed gotten faster, but this is mostly as a result of increased connection speeds and improvements in browser technology. What hasn't gotten faster are the web pages themselves; in fact, Steve notes that web developers seem to be increasingly deviating from "performance best-practices", in large part because of the pressure to deliver higher-quality multimedia content.
