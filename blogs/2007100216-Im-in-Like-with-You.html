I'm in Like with You!

Dan Mallinger

October 2, 2007

I was looking for a particular blog entry from a site I enjoy, when Google, in its infinite wisdom, suggested that I read this article on iminlikewithyou.com. The article provides a few interesting ways to incorporate "gaming commandments" into websites. Why? Because these commandments are really just the technological fodder for active social sites. So what are the five commandments?

  1. Collect all things
  2. Worship Points
  3. Honor Feedback
  4. Remember Exchange
  5. Customize!
Social sites rely on engaging large communities and the current wisdom suggests these elements (or something like them) work. From actual game sites, to EBay, to hipster dating sites, successful startups have been exploiting feedback and social action to engage an audience. We haven't explored these types of features much as of yet, but I'd like to see the EdLab try. One potential question, however, is "What do feedback and exchange look like in small niche communities?" Relying on mass response rates is something which many of these sites do, but may be ill advised for our small educational sites. So what's a lab to do? Innovate! And change the paradigm. As part of this, I would add a sixth commandment: Change. Death by determination seems too common and too easy. Craigslist started as an event finder, Flickr as a game for girls. Do we know enough about our audience to change?
