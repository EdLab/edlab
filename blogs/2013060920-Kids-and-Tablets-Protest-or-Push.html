Kids and Tablets: Protest or Push?

jvazquez

June 9, 2013


While parents and children haggle over screen time, perhaps the more fruitful argument should be over which apps to focus on. In the time it takes to finish reading the article referenced below, countless new games aimed at kids will have been downloaded and added to smartphones across the world. As technology becomes a more ubiquitous part of life, it comes as no surprise that parents would question the effect it will have on the development of their children.

In this video, Ray Suarez from PBS NewsHour interviews author Hanna Rosin of The Atlantic regarding her article, "Touch Screen Generation." As a mother herself, she understands too well kids’ fascination with all things digital. She has often had to negotiate usage times with her children and has balked at their suspect app choices. But like many parents who are in the same boat, Hanna has come to realize that maybe the issue of screen time isn’t paramount to the conversation; the more important consideration is whether kids are maximizing their time spent online.

What rules do you and your kids have in place when it comes to at-home tech time? What limits, if any, do you place upon yourself? Join the ongoing discussion on Vialogues to share your thoughts.
