Fantastic "Education" Special Report in The Atlantic

Kate Meersschaert

October 15, 2012

Stellar Special Report I grabbed The Atlantic magazine for a weekend analogue reading adventure and was excited to find a whole section entitled: "Special Report: New Ideas for Schools." This report includes engaging infographics and visualizations in-addition to articles on why students should grade their teachers (a growing trend), a writing revolution afoot at New Dorp High(learners need a blueprint for writing - they can innovate later)and ed "activists" aligned with both political parties (UFR promo?). Thoughts and Possible New Directions? I highly suggest exploring this "special report" and reporting-back in the comments below... what can we learn from the layout and content of this section for NLT? What article do you feel expresses a new direction that could be the most transformative to the learning landscape? I but my money on the "writing revolution..." and paying more attention to after school programs. Finland and South Korea spend less time instructing students in-class (see infographic from The Atlantic below) and invest more time out-of-class supporting learning... check-out their performance ratings as compared to ours. One of many potential factors to explore...
