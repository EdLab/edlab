How Young is "Too Young" for a Language Learning App?

Kate Meersschaert

September 18, 2013

Preschool? Rosetta Stone recently launched a digital app called, "Lingo Letter Sounds," aimed at helping young children begin the process of learning a new language (both English and Spanish). Using fun, brightly colored graphics and simplistic language exercises the app hopes to introduce the building blocks of new language acquisition. But, is this too soon? Here is an interesting article from Psychology Today that explores possible benefits and misconceptions. What are your thoughts? As young children continue to look to digital devices as playthings and virtual sitters, are educational apps for the very young a logical progression. I do have to say, on a personal note, I would have loved this as a child and perhaps may have actually become proficient in Spanish! For more on this new app and Rosetta Stone's foray into educational apps for young children, check-out this Forbes.com article.
