Video Experiment: An Animated, Contextualized EdLab

Molly Riordan

June 17, 2010

This experimental project is a collaboration between Gonzalo, Skye, and myself. I was wondering how we might illustrate the work of the Lab in the context of outside innovations in related fields in a fresh, engaging way. Inspired by the white-board animated lecture by Daniel Pink, we interviewed one of our outgoing colleagues about a conference she attended, hoping to draw connections back to the the Lab. Future iterations could focus on events, scholars, or developers whose work we think is truly exceptional and a profile of which might further outside understanding of what we do and hope to achieve. Check it out and tell us what you think! Does it help draw connections between the Lab and our areas of interest? Is the video itself interesting? What do you think of the animation, and the mixing of live-action and animation? Credits: Gonzalo [illustration and graphics], Skye [camera/cinematography, animation, editing/mixing], Molly [interviewer, video editing]; concept/story/etc. a total collaboration.
