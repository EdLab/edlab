Training Athletes in Virtual Reality

saraehardman

June 15, 2017


A startup has created a new type of immersive performance sports training that relies on virtual reality (VR) to prepare athletes for the field. Started as a master’s thesis at Stanford University, STRIVR is now used by 24 professional and college sports team in four different sports. This includes Clemson University, the Dallas Cowboys, and the New York Jets. VR training is particularly helpful for athletes with injuries who can’t physically train with their team, but is also used as a supplement to regular practice.

To become a good athlete, a person must have both the physical and mental talent to perform at the highest level during a game. While VR doesn’t test the physical, it does help athletes practice the mental side of the sport by placing them in real sports situations where they can run through rep after rep from various perspectives around the field.
