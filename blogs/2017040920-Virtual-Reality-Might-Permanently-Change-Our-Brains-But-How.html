Virtual Reality Might Permanently Change Our Brains, But How?

saraehardman

April 9, 2017


Despite the fact that virtual reality (VR) is a completely immersive environment, we experience it differently than we do the real world. A lab at UCLA is currently researching this phenomenon by studying rats in fully immersive VR environments. They expected a rat’s brain to fire the same neurons as it traveled through a maze in real life and an identical one in VR. Yet instead, they found that in VR, 60% of the neurons in the hippocampus, the part of the brain most responsible for memory, shut down compared to those used in reality.

Although researchers believe the consistent use of VR will have long-term consequences on the brain, they’re currently unsure whether those will be more beneficial or dangerous. As VR becomes more ubiquitous, however, continued research in this area is crucial. Some speculate that in the coming decades, VR will be as important in the workplace as computers. And if people eventually do spend 8-hour workdays strapped into a VR headset, it’s important to understand how our brains may permanently change in a virtual world.
