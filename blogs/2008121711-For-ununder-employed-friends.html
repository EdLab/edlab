For un/under employed friends

Anthony Cocciolo

December 17, 2008

Please forward to any friends who are looking for some part-time work and think they would be good at this job: The Center for Technology and School Change is collaborating with the EdLab to design and implement a social networking site for the The After School Corporation's (TASC) Building Healthy Communities program. TASC is looking for someone to moderate the site part-time during the roll-out. BHC Web Monitor — contracted position The Building Healthy Communities (BHC) Learn and Serve America program is currently looking for a website moderator to provide technical assistance and guidance for its new online community. The BHC website is designed for parents, after-school teachers and students grades 2-8 who are enrolled in the BHC after-school program and will have blogs for each group (estimated 15 groups — 5 in each partner state: NY, NJ, and PA). A moderator is sought to update the website, assist users with any technical difficulties they may come across, and check and approve all text comments by students to be posted within 24 hrs of student submission, but preferably within the hour during after-school program hours. Responsibilities:

  • Provide ongoing and timely technical assistance and support to users
  • Assign user IDs and passwords to enrolled youth participants and agencies
  • Screen youth participant submissions (comments)
  • Update and maintain website, making sure all information remains current
Qualifications:
  • Bachelor's degree preferred
  • Technical expertise in managing blogs and digital images
  • Web development experience a plus
  • Working knowledge of service-learning and obesity prevention (basic nutrition and fitness)
  • Strong interpersonal and excellent communication skills, both oral and written and the ability to effectively communicate with a variety of stakeholders
  • Flexibility, with the ability to adapt to changing circumstances
  • Must be available via phone/email 3pm-6pm, and provide phone number for technical assistance
  • Mush check email daily and respond within 24hrs
Compensation:
  • $25/hour
  • 3-6 Mon — Fri regular hours (some flexibility) + participation in training webinars (12/17 10am first webinar)
  • January 5 — Jan 29th with potential for additional project hours after that
To apply: Please send resume and cover letter to jferguson@tascorp.org
