Different Voices For Apple's Educational Announcement

Lu Wang

February 13, 2012

Laura and Kate both referred to the Apple's educational announcement in developing the future textbook market in their previous blog articles. Undoubtedly, Apple's move to march toward the education market again put the ambitious company under the spotlight. While some people vision Apple as a “friend of education” who helps the whole world undergo an extraordinary knowledge reform, there also aroused different voices, such as this one regarding whether it's inevitable or impossible for every American child to have an iPad in the classroom. People like Christopher Dawson argue that Apple's textbook plan is absurd since it's exclusive in terms of device and does not allow for textbook recycling. Thus this plan is only able to benefit the wealthiest schools that can afford the costs. People who stand on Christopher's side claim that technologies should be used in the classroom in a way that allows various kinds of devices, not just Apple products. It can be seen that technology is increasingly implemented in today's classroom. Yet we need to reconsider whether this rising reform is going to further worsen the education inequity based on family socio-economic status. It is crucial that technologies should gear towards making education more equal, more affordable, more easily accessed to, rather than the opposite way around.
