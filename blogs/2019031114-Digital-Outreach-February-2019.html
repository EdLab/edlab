Digital Outreach, February 2019

Cinthia.Fabian

March 11, 2019

INTRODUCTION


Can you spot the differences between this month’s report and last month’s (besides the new numbers)?


Answer: We’ve included two new sections to reflect updates made to our digital outreach in February 2019. 

  • We’ve begun using campaign links to track the impact of TCR Twitter outreach on website traffic.
  • As part of our content marketing strategy for PocketKnowledge, we (i.e. Paul) made our first PK blog post on the Learning at the Library pressible.


PART I: OVERVIEW OF EDLAB TWITTER ACCOUNTS


BASIC STATS


 

IN WHAT WAYS ARE FOLLOWERS ENGAGING WITH US?

@LibraryTC showed increases across the board, in both the total number of engagements received and in the average number of engagements received per post.

 

By contrast, @NLTTC and @TCRecord experienced a drop in follower engagement when compared to our January stats. This could be attributed to two things: February is a shorter month, and in general there is more engagement during January due to our end-of-year posts.

 


 

WHAT TWEETS ARE MOST POPULAR?

 

In February, the @NLTTC posts with the highest total engagements came from several different content streams (EdLab Review, Vialogues, Profiles, and Visualized) and had reaches ranging from the low hundreds to the thousands.

 

 

For @TCRecord, the three posts with the highest total engagement were for book reviews, followed by an excerpt from a commentary and a feature article. 

 

 

@LibraryTC showcased a mix of content, much of which circled back to other EdLab projects — PocketKnowledge, The Voice, the Learning Theater, etc.

 

 

Similarly, we used the @EdLabTC platform last month to cross-promote NLT and our recently commissioned art exhibit, “Knowing Together”.
