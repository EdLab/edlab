Women In STEM: 10 Historical Female Scientists You Should Know

Julia Martin

September 22, 2011

I was just reading this article about top female scientists! Emilie du Chatlet was a Mathematician actually, and though she is known mostly for her translation of Newton's Principia Mathematica, she also contributed greatly to math and science. She is featured in the book The Information. That said, I'm pretty shocked I didn't know about more about these ladies earlier on in my young life. Not for nothing, but a little extra credit should be given to some of these women for having achieved such advancements in light of the political climate in which they found themselves. Without mincing words, many of these women were actively prevented from participating in their fields and taught themselves or sought out tutors. Most of these women are women of means as well, which allowed them some of the flexibility in education. Alright, I'm going on a bit of a tear here, the point is: cool resource for women in science.
