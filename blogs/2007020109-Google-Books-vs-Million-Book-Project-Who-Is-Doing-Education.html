Google Books vs. Million Book Project: Who Is Doing Education?

Frank Webster

February 1, 2007

Should Teachers College Supply the Education Component of an International Digitial Library? The University of Texas at Autin has become the 11th research library to join Google Books. Meanwhile, the Million Book Project (MBP), led by Carnegie Mellon, closes in on its goal of digitizing 1,000,000 books by 2007. Both projects share the goal of free access to full-text on the internet. Both are developing collaborative partnerships among academic research libraries around the world. MBP seems to be specializing in China and India. Google is reaching out to the Spanish-speaking world through The National Library of Catalonia, the University Complutense of Madrid and selected Latin american collections. While Google will use it own search engine for discovery, MBP is developing a new interface. MBP partners must bear the cost of selecting the books and transporting them to the digitization centers in China and India. Google partners are responsible for selecting the books and transporting them to a central collection spot where Google pickes them up, digitizes them and returns them.
