If You Give a Kid a Lemon...

Dana Haugh

July 13, 2015


Entrepreneurship education is fast becoming a staple in K-12 curriculums across the country, and with good reason. As Lean Startup founder Steve Blank points out, traditional grade school entrepreneurship classes are still teaching kids 20th century small business practices. While there's something to be said for a foundational understanding of a small enterprise, these lessons are missing a significant opportunity to teach kids how to survive in today's entrepreneurial world. The Lean Launchpad methodology is designed to fill this gap, focusing on problem solving for real companies and merging science with business models for today's fast-paced workplace.

While this knowledge will undoubtedly help students in their future careers, you have to wonder if we are churning out young profit-minded entrepreneurs at the expense of a carefree childhood. With all this knowledge, can a lemonade stand still just be a fun afternoon activity?
