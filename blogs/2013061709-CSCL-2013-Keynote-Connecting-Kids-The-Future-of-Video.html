CSCL 2013 Keynote - Connecting Kids: The Future of Video

Sharon Hsiao

June 17, 2013

Kori Inkpen (Microsoft Research) Understanding how kids connect with video may hold the keys to delivering the long-awaited promise of video communication. Children's play is rich, creative and imaginative and research shows that children's play easily transcends distance through the use of video. And remarkably, children are extremely comfortable interacting over video. This talk highlights several recent projects that demonstrate children's rich, social interactions using video to connect with friends and family, regardless of whether they live in the same neighborhood, or on the other side of the world. As these technologies become embedded into the fabric of daily life, video will transform children's social interactions in both learning and play, enabling them to share rich experiences in ways not possible with current technologies.
