Peer Pressure

Bachir Diarrassouba

September 4, 2013

For most K-12 students in the New York City area, school is starting in a couple of days. The biggest thing that students need to be aware of at a early age is peer pressure. Peer Pressure is influence from members of someone's group. The pressure or push to do something that may or may not harm the individual. According to Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), alcohol use by people under the age of 21 is a major health problem in the US. Another big issue with teenagers is smoking. Health effects from cigarette smoking account for more than 440,000 deaths, according to the CDC. All these are the sometimes unintended consequences of peer pressure. These numbers are large and can be prevented if peer pressure is taken seriously. Public service announcements about the figures aforementioned can help to address these issues. I personally believe that addressing these issues early in students' lives will help them see the dangers of using such things at an early age.
