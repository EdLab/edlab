To Mars And Beyond!

Elysee Dougan

December 18, 2013

NASA and other space exploration programs have come a long way since Apollo's first man on the moon mission. NASA recently funded the University of Washington to build a fusion-powered rocket. The Fusion Powered Rocket, this new rocket will have the power to send humans to our neighboring red planet, Mars, in about thirty days. This new breakthrough in science technology, if it works as planned, will put NASA in a position that will be hard to reach. With this rocket, it will be easier and faster to examine Mars's surface to see if it can be compatible with human life. With such technology at hand, it makes me wonder why the government has started cutting funds for NASA.
