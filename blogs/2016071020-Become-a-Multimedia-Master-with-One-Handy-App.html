Become a Multimedia Master with One Handy App

dinosossi2

July 10, 2016


30hands Storyteller is a quick way for teachers and students to add multimedia slideshows to their classroom experience.

Pros:

30hands Storyteller has a very intuitive interface. To add a photo in your presentation, snap a new pic or pull one up from your camera roll. To add a title or text, just draw it on the screen. If you don’t have time to draw or shoot, just download a pic from Flickr, Dropbox, or Google Drive from within the app. Alternatively, 30hands provides ready-made video clips if you can’t find exactly what you need to create the slideshow of your dreams.

Cons:

As a kind of backhanded compliment, the worst thing about 30hands Storyteller is that it may be too easy to use. If you aren’t careful and don’t plan ahead, it's easy to make too many overly busy slides without much thought about a coherent message. Given this reality, you may want to use 30hands as a brainstorming tool rather than a finishing tool. The ease of use makes idea generation a pleasure, but the fun DIY nature of 30hands may incline you to make something fun and rough around the edges.

Like any free app, you have to pay to play. 30hands Storyteller allows you to purchase a video if you can’t find the content you want; however, with the breadth of YouTube content, you might find something more tailored to your needs than the closed system of the 30hands video library.

Our Takeaway:

Overall this is a really fun and intuitive way to make multimedia. If you are even slightly artistic and have any inclination to use media in your work (or to find a fun new way to pass the time), 30hands Storyteller is an easy way to create a variety of multimedia presentations.
