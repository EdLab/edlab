The Young Entrepreneurs Club

Kate Meersschaert

June 25, 2012

The Details: The Young Entrepreneurs Club (YEC) hopes to unite budding entrepreneurs with resources and networking opportunities that will help them to excel as leaders in their respective startups. Scott Gerber, a young entrepreneur himself, started the nonprofit, membership-based club to provide the resources he himself lacked when he attempted to start a company. Members of YEC include the founder of Mint.com and the director of Reid Hoffman's new initiative, based on the book of the same title, The Startup of You. The mission of the group is to,"spark an entrepreneurial revolution in Americaâ€“and help rebuild the economy in the process." Also, as Mashable reported in May, another aim of the group is to help make entrepreneurship a more acceptable career choice for young people and their parents. Why is this group unique? Taking the often-overlooked importance of encouraging young entrepreneurs is a fresh approach to a problem that everyone from the White House to NYC's Mayor is trying to help solve. Not only is entrepreneurship viewed as a source of new jobs, but also as a way to encourage American innovation. EdLab Relevance: As we move-forward with "edupreneurship" initiatives here at the lab, it is important to explore all manner of available resources for entrepreneurs... at every stage of their career! When we develop initiatives it is so important to take-into consideration what stage the resource would support. The needs of an early-stage startup are after-all, very different than that of a startup who is in beta testing mode. What do you think are the unique needs of young "edupreneurs"? Are they different than those of other industries?
