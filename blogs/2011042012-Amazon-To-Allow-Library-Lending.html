Amazon To Allow Library Lending

Julia Martin

April 20, 2011

Here is an article that was published in the library journal. "We're doing a little something extra here," Jay Marine, director, Amazon Kindle, said in a press release. "Normally, making margin notes in library books is a big no-no. But we're extending our Whispersync technology so that you can highlight and add margin notes to Kindle books you check out from your local library. Your notes will not show up when the next patron checks out the book. But if you check out the book again, or subsequently buy it, your notes will be there just as you left them, perfectly Whispersynced." This feature could raise privacy concerns within the library community as apparently Amazon would be maintaining a detailed record of reading habits. Commentary here and here.
