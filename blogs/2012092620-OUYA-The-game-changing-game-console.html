OUYA: The game-changing game console

jvazquez

September 26, 2012


Can I get an boo-yeah for OUYA? The open-source gaming platform (pronounced OO-yah) caused quite a stir during its recent kickstarter campaign that ran from July 10th to August 9th. The campaign’s original goal was to reach $950,000. It hit $8.5 million by the time it closed, making it the second highest earning campaign in Kickstarter’s history.

While it’s not slated to be released until March of 2013, it’s easy to see what has people so excited: OUYA is bringing gaming back to the TV and is granting creative freedom to aspiring and veteran game developers alike. And at $99, it’s more affordable than most mainstream consoles on the market.

The open nature of OUYA game creation is appealing to indie developers because it allows them to produce games cheaply and offers more room for experimentation. Anything goes! Their experimental games are likely to please those who crave a unique gaming experience.

Perhaps there is no market more ready for a little experimentation than educational games. OUYA presents an unparallelled opportunity for educational game developers to create fun learning experiences that are available for free to any learner who desires to play. But, the value of OUYA as a learning platform is certainly not limited to game play; for the uninitiated, the process of learning how to create a game is a rich and fulfilling experience in its own right.
