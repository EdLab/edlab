Mayor's New Living Small Project

Duncan Asiedu

July 11, 2012

New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg recently unveiled a plan to build a housing complex composed of tiny apartments; 300 square feet to be exact. These micro studios would accommodate the needs of singles looking for an afford-ably priced apartment amidst the inflated rents of NYC. In terms of pricing, each apartment is set to be rented out at about $2,000 a month, far lower than the average $3,418 monthly rent paid out by New Yorkers. The mayor is currently accepting proposals from developers interested in constructing the property. A simple requirement was laid out to developers: that the studios must have both a fully functional kitchen and bathroom. If the complex is indeed built, it would lower the minimum square footage of an NYC apartment by 100 square feet. The release of this plan marks another proposal by the mayor, which has received a lot of publicity. So far this proposal has garnered mixed reactions from New Yorkers alike. Some see this as a great way to avoid the insanely priced rents of city apartments while others liken it to living in a shoe box. What is your take on the mayor's proposal?
