EdLab Review: Projeqt

Kate Meersschaert

March 20, 2012

Projeqt was listed as a SXSWi 2012 awards finalist and is a self-declared "creative storytelling platform." Projeqt allows users to create multimedia-enhanced presentations using pre-set templates via drag & drop and selecting, media such as Tweets, videos (unfortunately not vialogues... yet!), sound clips and even interactive Google satellite map views. In addition, the platform allows you to create a profile to promote your work and share "projeqts" with friends. Pros: Easily allows users to select multimedia to include in their "projeqts." SoundCloud integration is a slick new feature that takes the usual audio file option of PowerPoint to the next level. The scroll-bar design and pre-set templates allow the novice to create professional, designer-quality presentations. The platform is device agnostic allowing users to easily view & share presentations across mobile devices. Cons: Despite easy integration, the media you can select to include is limited to material created on the platforms they support. The interface is almost too simple when you are first getting started, and a simple "how to" intro widget to take you through the process would be a welcome addition. EdLab Relevance: As we consider creating unique content for the New Learning Times... how we create and disseminate this content is an important consideration. What role could existent online digital publication platforms play? Educational opportunities on how to use the platform should be obvious and easily accessible. Our Takeaway: Create slick, beautiful presentations with rich multimedia options. Just don't give up trying to figure-out how to create a presentation on the first try... user educational opportunities can be as important as design.
