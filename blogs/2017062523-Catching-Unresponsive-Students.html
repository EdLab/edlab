Catching Unresponsive Students

Kevin.Camacho

June 25, 2017

Teachers don’t often know when students are not paying attention in class. A student could look interested during a lecture but has his mind and attention elsewhere. Don’t worry teachers, help is on the way. A business school in Paris will be experimenting with a software, Nestor, that uses artificial intelligence and facial analysis to determine whether students are paying attention in class. The software will be tested in two online classes starting this Fall.

 

This is how Nestor will work:

 

“The software uses students’ webcams to analyze eye movements and facial expressions and determine whether students are paying attention to a video lecture. It then formulates quizzes based on the content covered during moments of inattentiveness. Professors would also be able to identify moments when students’ attention waned.”

 

Marcel Saucet, the founder of LCA Learning, the company who created Nestor, is hopeful Nestor could vastly improve the performance of learners who take MOOCS or other online courses. Additionally, when pressed about concerns over data and privacy concerns, insisted the videos won’t be stored and the data will not be sold to advertisers. LCA Learning sells its products directly to schools.
