The Cloud Collective’s Subtle Green Revolution

Carmen James

January 23, 2015


This quiet, elegant, and effective green invention cleans the pollution above a highway. The Cloud Collective was selected to create one of 13 gardens for the summer of 2014 at the Festival Geneve. The festival is dedicated to the co-habitation of urban and natural within the context of the urban expansion of Geneva. The "garden" is a viaduct that goes over a small highway and is meant both to fit in with the generic space and to actively improve living conditions in the city. The tubes, which look simple, are actually impressively complex and range from solar panels to generate energy to algae to clean the air.

The Cloud Collective, based in Amsterdam and Paris, includes architects and designers who aim to create a sustainable today to carry us into tomorrow and to educate the public about the problems and solutions the firm seeks to address. They describe themselves as "an interdisciplinary design atelier for architecture, urban planning, research, installations, education and theory." The collective does place-based work and prides itself on the craftsmanship of the designers and architects that is tailored to the circumstances of each project.
