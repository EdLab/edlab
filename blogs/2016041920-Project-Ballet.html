Project Ballet

EdLab Studios

April 19, 2016


Founded in 1948 by George Balanchine and Lincoln Kirstein, the New York City Ballet is among the leading dance companies in the world and can be found in dozens of elementary schools across the five boroughs through Project Ballet. Taught over the course of ten in-class workshops, the program is not intended to train 3rd-5th graders to dance ballet but rather create it. Following a live performance at Lincoln Center and introduction to three pieces of repertoire, each choreographed by a famous figure in the company's past or present, students take what they've seen on stage and make it their own. Week by week, the NYC Ballet teaching artist presents different choreographic tools including levels, direction, pathways, and tableau for groups to slowly build their pieces; collaborating and problem solving along the way. Through Project Ballet the art form is made accessible and relevant while promoting student-generated work, culminating in a performance for family, friends, and teachers.

Music: Grande Tarantelle by Louis Moreau Gottschalk
