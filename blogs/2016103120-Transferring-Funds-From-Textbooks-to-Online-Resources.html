Transferring Funds From Textbooks to Online Resources

Jenny Abamu

October 31, 2016


This fall, for the first time, several schools across the country have chosen to take funds disbursed for textbooks and instead invest them in open online resources. Doing so presents new challenges for both teachers and students who are creating custom curriculums and mapping out individual learning pathways. The #GoOpen campaign, launched by the U.S. Department of Education, is helping to implement this exciting new policy.

The campaign works with school districts, edtech companies, and large corporations to make the transition from textbooks to open online resources a success. Grants from the government fund the creation, organization, and licensing of these resources. "I have seen the rest of our staff become less static teachers and definitely more dynamic," says Kathy White, a middle and high school teacher in the Williamsfield school district. Up until now, over 50 districts have taken up the mantel in implementing this change.
