Ramadan Explained

Kafoumba Doumbia

July 13, 2013

Ramadan is name of the ninth month of the Islamic moon calendar. It is the most significant month in the Islamic calendar. During the holy month of Ramadan, Muslims must fast (no food or water) from sunset to sundown. Depending on where you live, the fast can be as long as 17 hours, which is the case this year for those living in North America. During this month, Muslims ask for forgiveness and express gratitude for the things they normally take for granted. The purpose of the fast is to realize how important it's to have life and good health. This month is a way to thanks God for everything he has given us because some people around world just need good water and food to eat. Similar to the Hajj, Ramadan offers Muslims, regardless of race and socioeconomic status a chance to become equal before God.
