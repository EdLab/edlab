iPads: Ruining Snow Days, One Apple at a Time

Dana Haugh

March 9, 2015


Remember your first snow day? The inimitable joy of waking up to discover a boundless white wonderland just outside your door; a quiet, arctic paradise waiting to yield your every hope, wish, and architecturally brilliant design. The best part? The news ticker at the bottom of your local news channel just announced SCHOOL'S CLOSED. But just as you're putting on your snow pants and bundling up for what is sure to be an awesome day, your iPad sends you an alert: "Mrs. Green has just posted SNOW DAY ASSIGNMENTS, due by 3:00pm." Dreams shattered, you open a textbook and begin the day's at-home class assignments.

Unfortunately, this sad fate is a reality for many Minnesota students who've experienced an unprecedented number of school closures in the past few years due to relentless snowstorms. Minnesota schools had the opportunity to test their new fangled "flexible learning" program (in which every student is sent home with an iPad) last year, courtesy of the Polar Vortex that clobbered the Midwest. Much to the students' dismay, the program successfully kept schools on track with the academic calendar and the ever-important concept of "learning." With great privilege, comes great responsibility.
