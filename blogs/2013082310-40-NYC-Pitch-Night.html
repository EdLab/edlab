4.0 NYC Pitch Night

Brian.Sweeting

August 23, 2013

Last night 4.0 Schools hosted a pitch competition at the Dumbo Spot in Brooklyn among startups that have successfully moved through their essentials entrepreneurship program. Six brand new companies presented fresh approaches to overcoming barriers to learning, including real-life audio stories for low-income English language learners and an incentive program to keep creative, entrepreneurial teachers from leaving the teaching profession. Below is a short summary of a few of the pitches that I thought stood out: Nomad Schools Nomad Schools is a nonprofit organization creating innovative English Language Learning content for mobile devices. Customizable courses are designed to help adult immigrants and displaced populations who lack time and money to still move forward in gaining the communication skills needed for their education and career goals. Maker State MakerState is creating a system of publicly accessible STEAM learning labs that give kids real-world skills that enable their success in school, college and career. It offers a badge reward system for students pursuing who wants to be engineers, authors, programmers, designers, artists, and entreprenuers. Edmodified Edmodified wants to connect everyone who influences the education of students with diverse needs with user-friendly, technology-based tools that fit naturally within their daily workflow so that modifying and differentiating learning becomes efficient and effective. As always, these types of pitch competitions not only offer startups the opportunity to receive feedback and refine their products, but foster a community discussion about how to create the best products for learning and how to connect resources.
