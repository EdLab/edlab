Training students for the library of the future

Jeff Frank

January 12, 2006

The Montclair Kimberly Academy, a school noted for its Ethics Education program, also has a very interesting library program. From Kindergarten through third grade, MKA students learn how: to use the online library book catalogue; to use powerpoint to make electronic books; to use Inspiration software to map their ideas; to scan their own books; to search the internet effectively. MKA is not the only school with this type of program. The question, then, becomes: When these students reach college and graduate school, what will they want from a library?
