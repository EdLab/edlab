Vialogues tutorial designs from today's D&R

mason0

December 16, 2014

Today the team did a design exercise to solve the problem of teaching teachers the best way to craft an educational vialogue. Our requirements were:

  • Draws attention to conversation attributes, structural and pedagogic
  • Practical
  • Accessible for reference
  • Producible in two weeks by current EdLab staff
  • All future support possible by 1 admin and 1 dev
And here's what we came up with: Vertical site from the school of make-it-pretty-but-keep-it-simple. Break out to tasks designed to practice what was learned. On-the-fly tips from "Clippy for the new millennium" (Y3K). Light overlay, easy to take it or leave it. Curated attributes for the creator. Choose your requirements, then get directed help to build the right discussion. Pop-up guidance, from beginning to end in the "create" process. Empower the user to make decisions: pose questions and propose answers. Choose your own adventure! A game to pick up what you need to know to do exactly what you want. Could be a vialogue itself, or adapt to a more complex interaction. Micro-training library. Group 60s clips together to accomplish all kinds of tasks, from vialogues technics to conversational techniques. Learn to build a vialogue and scaffold a discussion, one piece of the puzzle at a time. **************** Keep me honest. If I described it wrong, set me straight in the comments.
