Visit Ancient Venice With a Virtual Time Machine

saraehardman

June 29, 2017


In a friary in a church in the city of Venice sits over 1,000 years of the cityâ€™s written history. Stacked across 80 kilometers of shelves, these records include maps, manuscripts, monographs, and documents detailing generations of Venetian bureaucracy. Yet this rich history has remained largely inaccessible to the public. Until, that is, researchers started the Venice Time Machine Project. This project aims to digitize the entire collection of archives by scanning each document page by page. Because all the documents are handwritten, machine learning is used to recognize words and create connections among the records.

By using maps and old records about shops and businesses, the Venice Time Machine Project will digitally recreate Venice as it evolved over the past 1,000 years. The virtual map would chart the creation and destruction of buildings, roads, and canals, as well as plot the locations of prominent historical figures. The project will create a social network of ancient Venice throughout generations of Venetian families. Not only that, it will provide datasets on any topic from financial markets to the spread of disease. Although this project is an immense undertaking that will take years to complete, a digital reconstruction of Venice might be the only way to truly preserve this sinking city.

Since the genesis of this project, a similar initiative has been taken up by many different countries in Europe. Why is it important to digitally archive historical documents?
