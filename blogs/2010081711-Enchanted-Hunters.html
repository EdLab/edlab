“Enchanted Hunters”

Julia Martin

August 17, 2010

Title: Enchanted hunters : the power of stories in childhood Author: Maria Tatar Publisher: Imprint New York : W.W. Norton, c2009 Check it Out: PN1009.A1 T37 2009 From the Publisher: Highly illuminating for parents, vital for students and book lovers alike, Enchanted Hunters transforms our understanding of why children should read. Ever wondered why little children love listening to stories, why older ones get lost in certain books? In this enthralling work, Maria Tatar challenges many of our assumptions about childhood reading. Much as our culture pays lip service to the importance of literature, we rarely examine the creative and cognitive benefits of reading from infancy through adolescence. About the Author: Maria Tatar, chair of Folklore and Mythology and the John L. Loeb Professor of Germanic Languages and Literatures at Harvard University, is editor of The Annotated Classic Fairy Tales Video of Author: Panel Featuring Author Washington Post Review Times Higher Education Review Los Angeles Times Review Guardian Review Harvard Gazette Review Harvard Magazine Review Google Book Preview
