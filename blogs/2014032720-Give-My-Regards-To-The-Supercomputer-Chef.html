Give My Regards To The Supercomputer Chef

jvazquez

March 27, 2014


What can computers teach us about creativity? Using food as a gateway, researchers at IBM are discovering how technology can help humans come up with completely new innovations.

The culinary arts provide a medium for creative thinkers to whet their proverbial appetites, which they often do by spicing up recipes that have been handed down for generations. IBM’s project (which is related to everyone’s favorite Jeopardy! guest) takes this a step further; it aims to concoct never-before-imagined recipes using its massive ingredient database. While its flavorful combinations sometimes miss with a tasting audience, the computer chef hits on all marks of creativity.

The more impressive part of the project goes beyond being a foodie’s dream come true. The intelligence that IBM is building-- an intelligence that mimic’s that of humans-- can extend into other areas of interest for an equally impressive display of creative thinking. What would happen, for example, if learning objectives could be entered as ingredients, and a recipe for a never-before-taught lesson followed?

Are you on board with this evolution of creativity, or does the rise of the machines stop before your kitchen? While we’re at it, would you eat a dish that a computer cooked up? Share your thoughts in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
