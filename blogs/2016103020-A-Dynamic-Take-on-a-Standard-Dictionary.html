A Dynamic Take on a Standard Dictionary

saraehardman

October 30, 2016


The creators of Wordflex Touch Dictionary aptly describe it as a "brain-friendly dictionary for your iPad." Wordflex gives users a dynamic take on the classic Oxford dictionary by plotting words in mind maps full of all the information that writers, students, and word-enthusiasts need to know to pick the perfect terms.

Pros:

Wordflex provides a great deal of information on each word, including origins, parts of speech, phrases, synonyms, syntax uses, and pronunciations. Some words are even accompanied by a small, black and white illustration. Each component shoots off the main word in an adjustable branch you can expand based on what information you seek. The mind maps forge connections among word origins, uses, and synonyms that are not always apparent in standard dictionaries to give users a deep and dynamic understanding of a word.

When searching for a word on Wordflex, the app pulls up all words with similar roots before you settle on just one. This places each word in a context that relates back to its origin and makes drawing connections between words easy. In addition, users can bookmark words and share word posters via email and social media.

Cons:

Wordflex touch dictionary is not a substitute for a regular dictionary. In fact, it does not provide standard definitions at all. Although this is not the appâ€™s intention, it is worth noting for anyone looking to trade their traditional dictionary for an app. It is a great tool for words you already know the meaning of, but less beneficial for unfamiliar words. For instance, the app taught me that an asarabacca is a plant, but did not tell me that it is in fact the scientific name for European wild ginger.

Our Takeaway:

Although Wordflex is not a dictionary substitute, it would be a great dictionary companion. It would be most useful for writers and students crafting texts, crossword puzzle enthusiasts, linguists, and word lovers. Unlimited access to the app costs $4.99, a worthwhile investment for anyone seeking a more dynamic way to learn words.
