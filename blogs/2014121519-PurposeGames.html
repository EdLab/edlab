PurposeGames

christophergu

December 15, 2014


PurposeGames is a website that allows users to play and create games in a variety of subject areas. Whether challenging friends or just learning something new, this tool uses games to enhance exam preparation and knowledge retention. From science, to geography, to Spanish vocabulary, PurposeGames has a database of over 100,000 free, public games. More games are added every day as new users register.

Pros:

Traditionally, students preparing for an upcoming history exam on U.S. state capitals could try memorizing them with notecards or from a list. With PurposeGames, they can easily search out and play a handful of existing games on the topic, all created by fellow users. To add an element of competition, users can challenge their friends or classmates. The massive library of games in the PurposeGames database is its greatest strength. The games span all the academic subject areas and even cover additional topics of interest, ranging from the Oscars to signs of the zodiac.

Cons:

The design options for creating games are limiting. Users can only choose from shapes and dots on a picture or a multiple-choice word format to design their games. While the number of games and topics seems virtually unlimited, there will need to be more visually appealing design elements in order to keep users engaged and motivated to continue playing.

Our Takeaway:

PurposeGames is a great tool for students to challenge each other, to test their knowledge and mastery of a certain topic, or for teachers to engage their students through a class tournament. After each game, users are able to see how they rank against the top performers, both in terms of score and timing. Users must be registered in order to create a game and their work can be set as private or shared publicly on the site. Gamification in education is nothing new, but the simple, quick games on the PurposeGames site are the perfect way to reinforce knowledge and have a little fun while studying.
