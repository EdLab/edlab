Hardcover Publishing + Slow Data

Melanie Hibbert

July 29, 2011

Google is launching Think Quarterly, a business-to-business publication featuring data analytics, data visualizations, opinion pieces, and other articles. Although the journal exists online, Google is also releasing limited hard-copy print editions which include tactile elements like ribbons, embossed seals, and heat-sensitive paper that changes color. This NYTimes article about the journal suggests it could be the beginning of a Slow Data movement (similar to the Slow Food movement), where the avalanche of data the Internet has compiled will be contemplated in slower, more longitudinal methods. I think the idea of having a hard copy small print-run of an online platform or digital publication is cool; maybe something EdLab could do in the future (?).
