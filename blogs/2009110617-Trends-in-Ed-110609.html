Ting Yuan

November 6, 2009

The Importance of Transliteracy Gena Hasket, a library professional, made an excellent presentation post on BlogHer to share with educators, learners, librarians and parents on the importance of transliteracy — the ability to read, write and interact across a range of platforms, tools and media. Gena's post pushes graduate students today to think more about transliteracy in the cyber space -- how to gather information needed in a most efficient way by using all the available online resources. For example, graduate students should know/be trained to know how to receive information updates via blog, twitter, different types of feeds on Google Reader, etc., and how to view statistics for tracking or research purpose via the WordPress analytics tool, Google Analytics and HootSuite, etc.

Book Release (e-copy available) Hanging Out, Messing Around, and Geeking Out: Kids Living and Learning with New Media The MIT Press just released the entitled book this month, which examines young people's everyday new media practices including video-game playing, text-messaging, digital media production, and social media use. The three-year extensive study was conducted through 659 semistructured interviews, informal interviews with 78 youths, as well as transcripts from 15 online discussion forums and over 5,000 observation hours.
This book was written as a collaborative effort by members of the Digital Youth Project, a three-year research effort funded by the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation and conducted at the University of California, Berkeley, and the University of Southern California. “Although youth online expressions may seem very foreign to those who have not grown up with them, youth values in this space are not so far off from those of adults.” (p. 342) “Rather than thinking of public education as a burden that schools must shoulder on their own, what would it mean to think of public education as a responsibility of a more distributed network of people and institutions?” (p. 352)
