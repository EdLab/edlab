Not-For-Profit

Elysee Dougan

November 22, 2013

Most companies sell products or services with the intentions of gaining something in return, mostly generating revenue. In fact, all the big name companies and organizations with billions in assets have this intention. But don't let this fool you, not all companies or organizations are out for profit. There are Non Profits or Not for Profit Organizations (NPO) scattered about in this world such as the Red Cross. A not for profit business, unlike most organizations, do not receive profit for redistribution between owners. These organizations believe in providing effective, equal and efficient services depending on their cause. This method of business is risky, as Non Profit Organizations, tend to be very financially weak. Many Non for Profits are financially struggling to stay afloat in this economy thus making it hard for managers to set priorities, make decisions, and evaluate performance.
