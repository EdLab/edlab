Trends in Ed: A $150 Million Gift from James H. Simons to Stony Brook University

Demetri Lales

December 14, 2011

I have just come across some great news for my alma mater, Stony Brook University. They are about to receive a $150 million dollar gift from James H. Simons, a hedge fund billionaire, philanthropist, and most importantly a former mathematics professor at Stony Brook University. This will be the largest gift in the history of the State University of New York (SUNY), and also the sixth largest ever given to an American open university, according to The Chronicle of Higher Education. Most of the money will go to research in medical sciences, including the construction of a life sciences building, the creation of a neuroscience institute, and a center for biological imaging. The gift will also fund the study of cancer and infectious diseases. This gift will make things interesting in terms of the university's growth. During my four year stay there, Stony Brook was always increasingly competitive in education and expanding its borders so much that the campus is essentially a small city. It has always been a very ambitious school striving to become a top-25 research university, which if true would make Stony Brook the first New York public university on the list. Dr. Samuel Stanely, the president of Stony Brook, plans to use the gift to strengthen the university's weakness: student-to-faculty ratio, the number of endowed professorships, and the life sciences. Dr. Stanley also calls this Stony Brook's rebirth day as the gift will certainly open doors for new possibilities if they spend it the right way, and I hope they really do take advantage of this. This could be the start to becoming a top 25 research university, and I will definitely be keeping an eye on their journey to the top.
