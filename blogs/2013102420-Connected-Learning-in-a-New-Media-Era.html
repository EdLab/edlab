Connected Learning in a New Media Era

chingfu

October 24, 2013


What is learning? Where, when, and how does learning take place? These are all big questions, and no consensus seems to have been reached among scholars, educators, parents, and learners. However, it is safe to say that most of us agree that learning activities should not be confined to school buildings and limited to lectures and textbooks. But can we be confident that kids who mess around or geek out in online gaming communities or fan fiction sites are conducting serious and meaningful learning activities? Scholars who advocate connected learning think it is well suited to the new media era because todayâ€™s technologies enable learners to more easily tap into distributed expertise across online and offline communities and make social connections that facilitate interest-driven learning. Not only will students' learning be connected to the real world in the connected learning model, but their interests will also be connected to academic learning that would provide them better social and economic opportunities.

How might these ideals translate into practice and what are some challenges facing this learning approach? Watch learning scholars explain what connected learning is and how it works, and share your thoughts on Vialogues.
