Notes UX Esssentials Event @ General Assembly

Megha Agarwala

August 8, 2011

Gin Lane Emmett Shine & Dan Kenger @ GinLaneMedia Comparison of user experience with restaurants - Restaurants really focus on giving a good experience You should know what your users are thinking and feeling. It has to be practical also. Good UX is Discoverable Efficient (No of clicks) Economical (Cognitive burden sense) Tactile (Action- Response) Recoverable (If user commits an error, can the user know about it - are you giving him errors) UX vs UI Good UI is the fundamental for a good UX North Star principles of UX -Simple - fast loading - Elegant - Efficient - Minimal design unless for a purpose - Try to let content speak for itself - Interactive - Keep the users attention - Clear direction and calls to action - Ability to navigate forward or back - Don't do more, less is more if done well - Sustainable - Future Test. Will it hold up over time - Always let the users know about what is going on. - Make users the medium and focus just on the experience . Apppe has done a good job with it. Don't focus on the actual materials, let the user just enjoy the experience. Examples: ginlanemedia.com (thought about who are audience will be) adidas.com - understand that we cant do e-commerce with it. tim-barber.com (Heat map is at the left upper corner of the page (40*40 pixel area) saturdaysnyc.com/store (Making the user safe in an e-commerce site) abovethefoldbook.com nikebetterworld.com (Parallexing effect, button navigation, while they scroll, they can interact interactively) nizoaap.com (on one end it has a great experience, but on the other hand, it doesn't tell me what this site does) marcusfuchs.dk (really immersive experience) nikeskateboarding.com thinkingforaliving.org (Content moves to the right) iwc.com/en-us/ apple.com/imac/ (Creating a depth, creating experience by how things are loading in) If hover is the action you use, how do you tell someone about an action, on an ipad/touchpad lunggrenlindqvist.se barntarnst.com francescobertelli.com store.apple.com/us - apple's check out process is very intuitive. one click checkout amazon invented and patented, now itunes uses it and has to pay amazon mrporter.com scotch-soda.com (Keeping fixed navigation on the top, puma.com Good reads: 37signals.com - What happens to user experience in a minimum viable product Book: Warhol economy andyrutledge.com/news-redux.php (critique on media sites) gigaom.com - Why instagram works psfk.com/2011/08/why-is-instagram-so-addictive littlebigdetails.com Book: Where good ideas come from? Bad example of UX: Ipad app of NYPL - movieapp.com app - There is a fine line between design & telling users what is going on. Future of UX: Web Mobile Experiential
