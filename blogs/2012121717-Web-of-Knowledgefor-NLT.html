Web of Knowledge...for NLT

Janice Joo

December 17, 2012

Does anyone know if there is a "Web of Knowledge" equivalent for news media? For example, I Googled something and got an article from Oct 2012. But I want to know if that story has been referenced in a more recent article, to indicate the complexity of an ongoing story, unfolding over time... It would also be interesting to know how sources are weaved together... So there's idea #1: To create a way for NLT readers who find themselves on past articles to "fast-forward" to relevant, yet more recent NLT articles. Or... is that how the "Related Links" section currently functions? If so, how can we do the "Related Links" section differently from other publications in order to infuse it with a more explicit "New Learning" theme? I.e. I really think we can encourage this concept of continued learning, deeper knowledge about a topic... Anyways, I'm inspired by my recent trip to the ITP winter show to create some new projects for the EdLab. New ways to present our work (at conferences, invited guest-talks), instead of just stating the numbers. And instead of just showing people how our tools work i.e. "get a CAS account here, enter your information here, and this is what you can do with the tool". I really believe we can push the boundaries for how we demonstrate the value of our tools... Here's another idea, for NLT: Let's map each article back 1 degree. Either by news source, institution/study referenced, key people referenced. Looking for data-driven trends in what new-learning "looks" like, and who/what are the key influencers. We might be surprised! I see a new study in the making... :O. Engineering team, is this possible? Is there a way to collect information about the information behind a link? Or would we need to tag it ourselves? Basically, is there an easy way to mine this data into a clean database? Comment below if you're interested in spending some "free time" working on this...
