What is the New Learning Movement?

bhughes

December 10, 2008

Two of today's events have inspired me to coin a neologism: the "New Learning Movement" in education. (Not a neologism? But Dewey and the rest who have used this turn of phrase don't have very good page rank... perhaps, then, it is a googlogism? (What!?! Two neologisms in one night?? Astonishing, but I digress.)) Oh yes, don't you think the time is ripe? At our seminar with Sandra Okita, we learned that avatars, robots, and other humanoid objects will soon be an integral part of schooling. And this evening, the After Ed TV team learned about pioneering new forms of reportage from master Tom Wolfe. So, you ask, what do these two events have in common? They remind us that starting a movement begins with the simple act of recognizing a shift in the "mental atmosphere" (Wolfe used this phrase three times during the evening!). Kids teaching robots? Yep. (And if not robots, then what are we waiting for exactly?) Of course, I cannot yet fully express the significance of the New Learning Movement. It will take a powerful "paradigm shift" to appreciate its revolutionary force (to quote one of Rick Moody's feeble attempts to appear not completely outwitted by Wolfe). Still, one cannot help but note that EdLab was here at the beginning, serving as a beacon on the often dim horizon of academia...
