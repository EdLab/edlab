Another Mobile App from Google

Stephen Asunka

February 4, 2009

According to this cnet story, Google is launching a mobile app - Google Latitude, which allows users to share their precise locations with friends and family members, and likewise be able to see friends and family members' locations. Latitude uses Google's technology to judge a user's location not just by GPS satellite, but also by proximity to mobile phone towers and wireless networks. When somebody is close, the software lets you contact the person in various ways - by calling or sending an e-mail or text message, for example. It can also let you hide from a specific person. This should be a very helpful application, but one interesting aspect is that a user can manually set his/her location, and this is a recipe for abuse and mischief. For example, Steve Lee (product manager for Google Latitude) gives a hypothetical case where a guy's girlfriend, though far away in Seattle, can virtually place herself next to him in New York!
