Seminar on Live: Mendeley

Arianna Choi

March 18, 2010

Mendeley is a London-based research collaboration start-up, funded by veterans of Skype, Last.fm, and Warner Music. Mendeley combines desktop software for managing and sharing research papers with a web platform for aggregating real-time research trend statistics. Since its public launch 15 months ago, Mendeley's article database has grown to over 16 million documents and is doubling every 10 weeks, putting it on track to become the world's largest academic database in 2010. Currently, its top five largest user bases are researchers from Cambridge, Stanford, MIT, Harvard, and Imperial College. Victor Henning, the founder and director of Mendeley, will share a demo and speak about the company's journey up to this point, development vision for the future, as well as advice for entrepreneurs. In addition to spearheading Mendeley, he is also a lecturer on consumer research and motion picture economics at the Bauhaus-University of Weimar. Prior to that, he worked in strategy consulting, film production, and the music industry.
