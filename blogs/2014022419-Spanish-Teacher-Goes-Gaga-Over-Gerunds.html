Spanish Teacher Goes Gaga Over Gerunds

jvazquez

February 24, 2014


If asked to name some popular language learning apps, the average New Leaning Times reader should be able to prattle off at least a few. But before technology, clever language teachers used other devices to help their students learn. Paige Clouse, a high school Spanish teacher in Papillion, Nebraska, is bringing one such device back with gusto.

In the video highlighted above, Paige pairs popular culture (Lady Gaga) with a less popular task (memorizing vocabulary) to form an entertaining musical Spanish lesson. Moreover, it appears that she enlisted many of her students to lend their talents in the production of the video. Preserving it in this medium, made public to the world, ensures that no only can her past, present, and future students benefit from the catchy lesson in present progressives, but anyone with internet access can profit as well.

Once you watch Paige’s video, you will never be able to listen to Gaga’s Alejandro the same way ever again-- and that is the mark of a brilliant lesson. Join the ongoing discussion on Vialogues to share what you thought of her video, and add what works for you when learning a new language.
