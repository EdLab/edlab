Believing You Can Get Smarter Makes You Smarter

Kevin M. Gong

May 24, 2011

Stumbled across this interesting article on the American Psychological Association website. A team of social psychologists showed that teaching college students about the malleability of intelligence caused them to improve their grades and value academics more than a control group that did not receive the message. These results were later replicated with 7th-grade students, who demonstrated "higher academic motivation, better academic behavior, and better grades in mathematics." Most notably, the studies found that Black-White and female-male academic achievement gaps were eliminated or significantly reduced after the intelligence malleability teachings.
