Africa's Emerging Market Boom

abagigah92

March 13, 2014

Africa currently has some of the fastest growing economies in the world. At the beginning of the century, emerging markets such as Brazil and India were receiving all the rave. However, the global recession have stunt the growth of these markets. However, economies in Africa have steered clear from most of the effects of the recession and continue to grow. The growth is credited to macroeconomic management and debt relief. While the growth has generated a sense of optimism among Africans, economists, and the private sector, it also presents some major concerns.

The overall economy of Africa is still not very competitive. Underdeveloped and lack of infrastructure, persistent inequality and lack of jobs in the midst of a population boom poses serious challenges to continued growth. The job of resolving these issues will ultimately fall on the government. Citizens, armed with access to endless information via access to the web, will make sure to hold government accountable. This new form of government accountability is a far cry from previous times when dissatisfaction often manifested into full-blown civil war. It is important that this sense of optimism carries over into real action and change for the continent's citizens.
