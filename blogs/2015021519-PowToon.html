PowToon

Ting Yuan

February 15, 2015


PowToon is a an online application that allows users to create animated presentations. In addition to animation and traditional slideshow functions, PowToon makes it easy to add narration, music, or embedded videos to presentations. The system seems like a natural fit for K-8 teachers making due with tools that don't encourage a spirit of fun, but PowToon also offers more subdued animations that could be applied in a variety of teaching and learning settings.

Pros:

With an intuitive interface, PowToon is a fun alternative to traditional presentation tools such as PowerPoint and Google Slides. Users can sign up with a current Google account. The PowToon library has a variety of graphic options for slide making including animated characters, markers, props, and backgrounds. Users can present using automatically-advancing movie mode or regular click-through presentation mode. PowToon's interface is simple enough that it has the potential to be used by teachers or students. One classroom application is digital storytelling; students could use it to act out a story scene from a book, re-enact a historical event, or make a short comic to explain a curricular concept.

Cons:

PowToon users can create quick, basic motion graphics, but may need to use other tools to create complex, customized animations. It has three pricing options: free, Professional, and Agency. The free version limits users to five-minute presentations but allows for unlimited productions. If users want to create longer slideshows or remove the PowToon logo from their files, they can select from payment options, such as a $14 one-time export fee or an unlimited professional plan that starts at $20 per month.

Our Takeaway:

In adopting a holistic idea of presentations, PowToon encourages users to make design decisions for animation-based storytelling. It could help to create a playful communication environment by letting users add humor to their work with a rich collection of animated characters and symbols. PowToon is well-suited to classroom and peer teaching, but it might also be a good option for flipped and blended classrooms to spice up out of class video work.
