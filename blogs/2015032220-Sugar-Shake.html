Sugar Shake

Alexandra Lederman

March 22, 2015


Sugar Shakeis a biochemistry game featuring protein characters based on real structures found in the human body. The user shakes and tilts sugar molecules using a device to set off chemical reactions and earn points. Each level of Sugar Shake represents a different protein. The app allows the user to explore the structure and function of each protein through the game and through a separate protein page where players can zoom in, out, and around. Sugar Shake is free and available on iTunes and Google Play.

Pros:

Sugar Shake is a colorful, interactive, and science driven game. Exploring chemical reactions through a mobile device is safe and fun for both children and non-biochemists. The three-dimensional iterations of the proteins and sugars make this game compelling and exploratory. It might even be better than a microscope and protein.

Cons:

The game is sometimes unresponsive when players try to release a sugar molecule. The protein page is also not interactive enough to inspire deep learning. A user cannot view 360 degrees of the protein, only 250. The scientific information about the proteins is not easy to view and takes away from the game. The user needs to leave the gaming part of the app and open the protein information page.

Our Takeaway:

Sugar Shake is a great way to introduce children to biochemistry without the laboratory and tools. Learning about proteins, sugar molecules, and chemical reactions can be accomplished on a superficial level while saving time, space, and money. The actual information about the proteins for the more curious is available on a page separate from the game. This game is a great way for parents or teachers to expose children to biochemistry in a fun, safe way.
