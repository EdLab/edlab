What Happens When You Make a Lamp Smart?

saraehardman

May 17, 2017


While Lampix looks like a regular desk lamp, it’s actually one of this year’s biggest tech innovations. As the world’s first smart lamp, Lampix turns any surface into a smart surface, which you can then interact with as you would a computer. This smart lamp lets you work with paper documents as if they were electronic: scanning, copying and pasting, finding text within the document, and tagging specific words. It also allows individuals to work on the same document from a distance, and to sync smartphone notifications directly to the surface you’re working on.

Lampix shows great promise among augmented reality technologies. In fact, it won SXSW’s 2017 accelerator event in the AR/VR category. "We believe we can take the human-computer interaction to the next level," says Lampix CEO George Popescu. "We think Lampix will impact humanity as much as smartphone and desktop computers." Although I don’t know if this is true, I do know that Lampix could have a significant impact on the workplace, school, or home. Also, it proves that even the most mundane household appliances, such as a lamp, can transform into amazing smart products.
