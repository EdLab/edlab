Butchery 101

EdLab Studios

April 26, 2015


Before becoming a butcher, Jason Yang was a criminal defense lawyer. On a quest to learn more about where his food comes from, he became an apprentice at Fleisher’s Pasture-Raised Meats in Red Hook, Brooklyn, where today, he’s the head butcher. Fleisher’s offers training sessions and demonstrations to teach the basics of butchery to curious New Yorkers: chefs, omnivores seeking alternatives to factory-farmed meat, and even the occasional vegetarian. Inspired by the old-fashioned butcher shops rarely seen in New York City anymore, Jason and the team at Fleisher's run one-afternoon workshops in which students can discuss sustainable farming and get hands-on experience with knife-work on large pieces of meat. The entire process encourages participants to take a holistic approach to meat. They learn which farm the animal has come from, know that it was raised with care, understand its anatomy, and practice the craft of butchery, cutting along each of a pigs' primals with respect and precision, and wasting as little as possible.
