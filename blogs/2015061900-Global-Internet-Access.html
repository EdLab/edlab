Global Internet Access

abagigah92

June 19, 2015

The internet continues to influence how people live as more and more people gain unlimited access. In developing countries, there has been positive feedback about the internet when it comes to education, the economy and personal affairs. However, many people in developing countries see the internet as having a negative influence on morality. About 42% of the people that were surveyed in 32 countries say the internet has a negative influence on morality. Whiles 64% of the people stated that the internet has a positive influence on education. Globally, the number of people accessing the internet varies widely. In many developing countries, cellphones are the cheapest way of accessing the internet. Countries such as Chile and China have the same usage rate of smartphones as the United States. According to information from the hyperlinked article, the rate of accessing the internet is higher in richer countries and in countries with high percentage of English speakers.
