Research Digest: Learning in the Digital Era

chingfu

Weigel, M., James, C., & Gardner, H. (2009). Learning: Peering backward and looking forward in the digital era. International Journal of Learning, 1(1), 1â€”18.

Article Review
Learning, though seems self-explanatory, is a contested concept in academic world. For some scholars, it happens everywhere in our daily lives; for others, it occurs only in selected conditions and environments. The digital era of social media and technologies adds new dimensions to the complexity of learning. In this paper, Weigel, James, and Gardner (2009) present and address a paradox of learning in the future: learning becomes more individual yet social at the same time in the digital era. They review the concept of learning from pre-literate era to modern era (see table below) and looking forward to the learning in digital age. The postmodern thoughts parallel digital era urge educators to think more about what is truth and what should they teach. The increasing complex and interconnected world demands metacognitive skills rather than merely "three R's". Furthermore, new digital media have affordances that could support informal, constructive, contextualized and social learning, though in some cases they also present as a distraction to student learning. While digital culture seems ubiquitous and young people are assumed as "digital natives", they caution that we should keep in mind the unequal access to digital technologies for all as an important piece in this picture. 
Edlab Connection
This paper provides a great overview of the changes of learning in human history. One of the most important themes is the paradoxical personal yet social learning environment that could be afforded in digital era. As we are developing/designing new learning experiences and platforms in D&R team, this theme could be central to the guidelines of all these efforts.
