Building A Strong Foundation For Education In Tanzania

Nantwi

July 23, 2014


In most developing nations, rural communities serve as de-facto governments. The community basically governs its own affairs. This is especially true in sub-Saharan Africa where governments often fail to provide citizens with basic needs. As such, the responsibility of providing basic services in developing nations usually falls to citizens and non-governmental organizations (NGOs).

In this video, Room to Read, a nonprofit organization that works with local communities across the developing world to build schools and libraries, visits Turiani in rural Tanzania to help locals build schools. Turiani is a predominantly farming town and locals are expected to provide materials for the new schools. The video reveals some of the challenges facing schools in rural Tanzania such as lack of books and school materials for students and teachers.

The residents of Turiani are responsible for the maintenance of the school upon completion. The onus is on them to ensure the new schools can provide quality educational opportunities for children in Turiani. Can this model be replicated in other rural areas across sub-Saharan Africa?
