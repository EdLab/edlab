English and the Summer Olympics

AbdulMalik

December 14, 2014

Sports can sometimes be used an educational tool while others might view it as two separate things. With the 2016 Summer Olympics look, advocates in Rio de Janeiro want to collaborate with the organizing committee to teach English to a million Rio residents. The hope is that this will enhance social interactions between Rio residents and those visiting for the games. The founder of this project was motivated to start the project after he discovered the importance of education and the English language after he studied abroad.

This partnership will create an online learning platform for about 550,000 high school students and 450,000 Olympics volunteers in Brazil to learn English for free. The organizers of this program, in conjunction with the Ministry of Education, explained that this program will also improve the country. I believe this is a great initiative and an opportunity for most students in Brazil to learn a second language. English is already the language of choice when it comes to commerce and other areas so it will bode well for Rio residents and Brazilians in the years following the games.
