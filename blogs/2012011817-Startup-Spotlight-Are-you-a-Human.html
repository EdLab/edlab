Startup Spotlight: Are you a Human?

Kate Meersschaert

January 18, 2012

The Details and Backstory Sporting quite possibly one of the most apropos names for a startup in the growing CAPTCHA technology space, Are you a Human? strives to pickup where other CAPTCHA generators like reCAPTCHA the current leader in the field, dropped us off: oftentimes feeling more confused and frustrated having attempted and often failed-at decoding the distorted letters meant to prevent bots and other internet n'er-do-weller's from accessing sites and hijacking content. This is where Are you a Human? steps in. Are you a Human? was born out of the aforementioned frustration, and more specifically after one of the founders, Tyler Paxton, attempted to buy concert tickets only to be thwarted by a scalper's bot as he struggled with a CAPTCHA's fuzzy print. The goal of Are you a Human is to take the CAPTCHA experience and "gamify" it through interactive drag & drop games (see image below) that replace the need for deciphering blurred text. Learn more here. Unique Point of Difference Are you a Human? provides entertainment value for users, safety for sites and sponsorship opportunities for companies and institutions, thereby solving a pressing problem (internet security) and addressing multiple user needs at once. This approach is novel in that it takes into consideration the needs of both an evolving internet security landscape (bots get smarter everyday), while entertaining and engaging users and providing a revenue stream for the startup. EdLab Takeaway Not only could we potentially add an educational component to these CAPTCHA-based games (as I proposed to the EdLab team internally), but we could simply explore this new CAPTCHA model for existent EdLab tools and interactions like Ednode and ResearchBroker. How are we currently using these bot-blockers and how can we potentially use them as a group in the future?
