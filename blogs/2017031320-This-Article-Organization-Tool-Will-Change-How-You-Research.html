This Article Organization Tool Will Change How You Research

saraehardman

March 13, 2017


If you’re a student embarking on a large research project, you’re going to want to know about Mendeley, one of the top organizational tools in academia. Available as a website, desktop plugin, and app, Mendeley gives scholars a place to manage reference articles and collaborate with others.

Pros:

Mendeley is excellent for managing PDFs and Word documents. It allows you to collect articles in a digital library, annotate them, and share them with others. You can also search the database for similar articles. Despite the fact that thousands of articles are available in its system, the interface makes searching and collecting simple. Mendeley also helps you keep track of your own published articles, making it useful for even established scholars. It also offers several social features to make the platform collaborative. Users have the ability to follow and connect with other scholars and share articles. Users can also create or join groups and share PDFs or reference citations among the members.

In addition, one of the most useful tools that Mendeley offers is a Microsoft Word plugin that can transfer in-text citations and bibliography entries directly from your Mendeley library to the document through an easy author or article search, formatted according to your preferences. And don’t worry, even if you prefer an obscure citation style, Mendeley’s massive citation database likely has the option available.

Cons:

While Mendeley is a fantastic tool for organizing articles in a traditional format (e.g., PDFs, Microsoft Word, etc.), it does not allow users to store images, audio, or other multimedia files in their collection. For this reason, the platform would have serious limitations for any student or scholar in the arts or whose studies incorporate various forms of media.

Our Takeaway:

Mendeley is a must-have tool for anyone invested in research. Although it would most benefit doctoral students writing dissertations, its collaborative capacities make it useful for anyone in a research community interested in sharing articles and connecting with others.
