Research Notes on Identifying Vialogues "BEST" comments

Sharon Hsiao

August 30, 2013

[[Keep PRIVATE please]] The research team has been working on identifying BEST comments from our collections. There are so many interesting things we are learning from our data and attempt to make our video discussion experience better. Why? and How? We hypothesize that BEST comments are associated with the videos and will increase users' engagement and learning. So, to begin with, we have to define what is qualified as BEST. We look at a range of parameters, mainly including 3 aspects: a) comment syntactics b) comment semantics c) user behaviors (interactions) For each aspect, we consider a bunch of variables, from comment quantities to the meanings of the comments, i.e. # of comments, # of words per sentences, informativeness (novel words compared to the entire conversation), sentiments, cognitive words, etc., So, assuming all these variables combined will denote the video views and comments counts. We are currently testing our featured Vialogues compared to the rest of our Vialogues corpus. We built a model which so far can approximately tell whether the Vialogues is influential or not.

video views = 45.5 +0.02comment_length -0.19word count -0.03word/sentence +5.96position +22.77informativeness +0.42social -0.78 affect* - 0.47cogmech + 0.22percept + 1.69bio - 0.65relativ +9.36sentiment_score** +3.27v.comment_count*** +0.01 v.duration.sec. Signif. codes: 0 ‘***' 0.001 ‘**' 0.01 ‘*'
If you know a bit of statistics, you would know what this model looks like....pretty intuitive, and makes sense. Let me explain it to you. If we do not consider whatsoever variables, eventually for a given time, the prediction for Vialogue views will be 45.5. In short, more sentiments and more comments predict good video views. Also noted, informativeness is highly appreciated. To visualize what are those good and not so good Vialogues' comments based on the model constructed above, I measured the Cook's distance value and plot the influential chart. positive influential vialogues: negative influential vialogues: Look at each Vialogue's comments distribution, these cases indicate that influential ones do not have to have a whole lot comments, but the diversity and intensity matter. On the other hand, none influential ones tend to have heavy conversation at the very beginning and flat out. *note1: Influence is a reference index of how the comments affect to video views with other Vialogues. *note2: java source code and R scripts to crawl and calculate each parameter values are available upon requests.
