The American Dream in Music

Kate Meersschaert

July 8, 2011

To continue our on-going exploration of the use of music as a lens with which to examine core subjects (in this case American history) in the classroom, here is a compilation of songs that express the American Dream. "American Ideals" expressed in these songs which range from James Brown's "Living in America" to U2's "The Refugee" include, manifest destiny, altruism, the American work ethic and the immigrant experience among many more. Songs not included in this compilation, but worth exploring in the context of American history and "Americanness" are: Dave Mathews Band - "American Baby" Don McClean - "American Pie" Tom Cochran - "Life is a Highway" Bruce Springsteen - "Born in the U.S.A." Rage Against the Machine - "Freedom" What other songs do you feel express both utopian & dystopian American dreams and ideals? What other meta-themes and subject-areas do you think can be explored through music in the classroom?
