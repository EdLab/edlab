The Key to Successful Online Learning? Well-Designed Pedagogy

Rebecca.Sullivan

August 28, 2018


What makes online learning effective? A recent study conducted by researchers in Mexico City reveals that simply reproducing existing pedagogies online does not improve student performance. However, the researchers found that thoughtful design of intentional pedagogy in virtual environments can extend learning opportunities.

The researchers first examined how existing online content affected learning in a masters-level course on Educational Program Evaluation. The course offered reading materials, PowerPoints, discussion forums, quizzes, and project instructions on Moodle, an online learning platform. Interviews with eight students indicated their dissatisfaction with Moodle’s inability to promote deep understanding because of its summarized and static content. The researchers administered a pre-test and post-test to measure the increase in learners’ knowledge about the course subject. Students’ scores showed only a 13% increase in content knowledge upon completion of the course.

In an effort to improve learning, the researchers worked with experts to construct a digital course according to the cognitive apprenticeship model. Like a trade apprenticeship, this model presents content situated within a community of practice. Through professional modeling and guided coaching, it seeks to support learners’ development of expert skills that they can apply to a variety of specific situations. The online course promotes apprenticeship learning through model case study videos, interactive exercises with immediate feedback, and collaborative discussion boards and blogs. These tools aim to prepare teams of students to apply their learned expertise to a specific case in the class’s culminating project.

The post-test revealed a 44% knowledge increase for students who engaged with the new digital course. While the cognitive apprentice model’s professional-oriented focus might not be appropriate for all courses, especially those in the humanities, the positive results of the study indicate the importance of thoughtfully designing online learning environments to adequately support student needs.

García-Cabrero, B., Hoover, M., Lajoie, S., Andrade-Santoyo, N., Quevodo-Rodríguez, L., & Wong, J. (2018). Design of a learning-centered online environment: a cognitive apprenticeship approach.Educational Technology Research and Development, 66(3), 831-835.
