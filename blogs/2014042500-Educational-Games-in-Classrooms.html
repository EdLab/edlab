Educational Games in Classrooms

Youssef Ballo

April 25, 2014

Playing games is one of the essential ways in which humans can learn. Some educators have yet to embrace gaming into their teaching as they have yet to recognize the value of it as a learning tool. According to several participants at Pax East 2014, there are plenty of ways to bring video games into the classroom and use them to expand traditional curriculum. A history teacher discussed how she used Oregon Trail, a classic education game, because it allows students to play a role in historical storytelling and also teaches them about what the travelers on the Oregon Trail faced. According to the teacher's husband, it's not enough for a game to be educational because every educator needs to be more involved in every step of the design and production process. There are a lot of ways to bring in video games into the classroom but administrators and educators just have to be creative.
