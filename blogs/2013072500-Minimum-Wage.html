Minimum Wage

abagigah92

July 25, 2013

Minimum wage is considered to be a price floor because the equilibrium price falls below the stated price. The minimum wage law was set by President Franklin D. Roosevelt (FDR) during the Great Depression to help the country. Now, the current minimum wage is set at $7.25 per hour rate; it is the lowest price a worker must be paid for labor under the laws of the United States. Minimum wage causes a large amount of unemployment workers because the labor supply is a lot compared to the demand. Economists who believe in efficiency not equity will state that minimum wage is normative economics because minimum wage is what is ought to be done. Most economists will agree that minimum wage should not be increased because, the money available for labor is small relative to the supply of workers. Increasing the minimum wage only means less people will be hired to work. Moreover, binding price floors is set above the equilibrium price and also creates a surplus of workers in the economy. This will allow workers to work of the books for low pay and it will encourage the employment of illegal immigrants to work for low pay.
