Trends in Ed, 4.6.09

jvazquez

April 6, 2009

Amaze, bewilder, and captivate: A new tool that can help your drab presentations shine Some of us have a good number of presentations coming up. One of the newest products in line to help put some pizzazz in those presentations is Prezi. Prezi is a web tool that helps you create stimulating visuals and images, all the while allowing you to zoom in and out of your brilliant mind map of a presentation. Be sure to watch the informational video on the homepage. See another blogger's example here. The basic version is free, as long as you don't mind keeping your file public. It looks like a fun new toy to play with. Maybe I can dazzle some AERA vets, eh? Need to collaborate? Say no more As long as we're mind-mapping, take a look at the best collaboration tools of 2009, created by Robin Good. In it you'll find examples of tools under such categories as: project management, event scheduling, co-browsing, and workgrouping. I saw some classics (google docs, Skype, youSendIt) but a lot of them were new to me ( Colaab, Meeting24, Skrbl).
