mSchool Initial Concept

Panisuan Chasinga

February 20, 2015

**Private** I started messing around with mSchool initial concept for the UX design today. I began by exploring the root definitions of metaphor and school which combine into mSchool, followed by translating them to spatial concept. Note how philosophers, educators and students in the School of Athens above mingle, discuss and learn in "temporal" places which transformed into impromptu classrooms, defining the etymology of school as we know. and lastly, I've come up with a fun terminology instead of module: With the initial concept, I played around with in-browser CSS and modified the "create module" page from this: to this: I simply followed the KISS rule. Here are changes I've made: Background color Changed the dark background to white. Text suffers on gray and so does the user. Menu Icons The current icons are small and misleading. ("setting gears" for permission, "download" for forking another module) I adopted the line icons for cleaner look and made them bigger. I used circles to symbolize a "cloning" action (sort of like amoebas I suppose) for better predictability. Border Radius Border radius have to go. It was the first quirk I noticed. It makes everything looks like an iPhone app icon but doesn't serve the same purpose and distract the purity of forms. I minimized the border to minimal dotted lines. The concept of a plane is just a boundary humans can make sense as a space for actions. I think "soft" boundary i.e. a change of texture or tone can ease the experience and keep the focus on content creation. Add button Buttons are less button-like now. Our elements should be more intangible, merging into the context rather than standing out as isolated elements that can overload users with information such as borders and shadings. Moreover, a button is a dot from afar, no matter what form it takes. This will also make mSchool more similar to a native app on mobile devices like the iPad, which is the device educators and students are using in classrooms. Tooltips They are like manual in disguise. With mSchool's complexity, I suggest we have tooltips everywhere within our application, holding user's hand along the way. Module With the initial concept of a meta-space of a school, I use textures to define boundaries instead of hard borders and solid colors. With this concept I think it's easier and simpler to develop the user interfaces and focus more on elements of interactions rather than how things should look like. This is just a very early concept but I really love to hear your feedbacks.
