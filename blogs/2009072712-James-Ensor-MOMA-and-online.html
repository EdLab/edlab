James Ensor @ MOMA and online

bhughes

July 27, 2009

I highly recommend swinging by MOMA this summer to see the James Ensor Exhibition. It's a fantastic show about a fantastic artist on the cutting edge of his profession (and "Modernity!"). Also of note, I hadn't been to the MOMA website in a while. The link above goes to their "exhibition" website... what's more or less the 'virtual' version of the show (and pretty much a rich online ad). I felt it gave me a ton of information, and also made me want to go to the real thing! -- That's not always an easy thing to achieve with web design. Art may be well-suited for this, since there is the aura of "the original" that can perhaps best be perceived in person... I wonder what the equivalent is in education? What is the in-person or social interaction that you would want to have in person EVEN IF you could get almost everything of (a practical?) value online?
