Fast Forward Five Years: The Classroom

Carmen James

June 1, 2014


Though it is now defunct, inBloom had big ideas for education, as evidenced by the video above. Tablets take center stage as vehicles to collect and view data on student performance, progress, and other assessment metrics. The teachers are portrayed as never without their mobile devices and are all too happy to add a last minute homework assignment to their students’ online learning portals.

The data capture component of this story has hit many a nerve. In the last several years, a host of articles have been published in the wake of rising concerns about student privacy in large data systems like inBloom. For example, as the New York Times reports, "Parents in Louisiana raised a ruckus after discovering that their children’s Social Security numbers had been uploaded to inBloom." In light of inBloom’s closure, policy makers and superintendents have been working to understand what data can and should be collected and how this data should be managed so that it can most effectively and safely advance the education of students.

InBloom controversy aside, how do you see this story playing out in 5 years? Does such a system raise concerns for you about student data? Share your thoughts in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
