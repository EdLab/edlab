A 9-Year-Old Boy Turns A Cardboard Arcade Into A Global Maker Movement

chingfu

May 14, 2014


Technological advances such as 3D printers, laser cutters, and programming software have propelled a new maker movement. Many young people enjoy making robots, electronic gadgets, video games, and apps with these new tools. While all these exciting developments might make people associate maker projects with investments in new technologies, the unbounded imagination of kids remains the core of any great maker project. In fact, it took only one kid's innovative idea to turn everyday recycled materials into a fun and influential maker project, and Caine demonstrates this to the world with the arcade he built in east L.A.

A then nine-year-old boy spending most of his summer at his dad's used auto parts store, Caine built an arcade out of cardboard and everyday objects. His creativity made the arcade a miniature of a real world arcade with fun passes, a variety of interesting game machines (e.g., basketball shootout, a soccer game, a claw machine) along with manually delivered score tickets from inside the game machines and prizes. His project went unnoticed until a film maker stopped into the auto shop and, amazed by Caineâ€™s arcade, made a short documentary film about it that went viral. This helped to turn a small maker project into a global movement of cardboard challenges that invites kids around the world to release their imagination and become cardboard arcade game makers.
