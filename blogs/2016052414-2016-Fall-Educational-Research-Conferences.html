2016 Fall Educational Research Conferences

Xiang.Liu

May 24, 2016

There are two educational research conferences in this fall I think might be good choices for presenting our current research. The first conference is the Northeastern Educational Research Association annual meeting. The conference is going to happen in the end of October at Trumbull, Connecticut. The second is the California Educational Research Association annual meeting. This one is going to happen in the end of November at Sacramento, CA. I'm planning on submitting one paper for each conference. They are both technical in nature. The first paper would be in the use of a hyperbolic cosine model in unfolding rater behavior. Specifically, the focus would be on the Bayesian inference of the model and comparison to the existing joint maximum likelihood approach. The second paper is going to be an extension of the previous one. It would primarily deal with using Bayesian posterior predictive checks on detecting aberrant rater behavior. Edit: Zhou, Hui Soo, and Gary are also going to be co-authors on the paper submissions.
