Is Coding the New Superpower?

Mable Yiu

July 18, 2013


Computers are everywhere, but how many people actually know how to read and write code? With only one in ten schools offering classes in computer science, who will fill the increasing number of programming jobs available for people around the world? In this video, a number of successful coders, including Mark Zuckerberg, Bill Gates, Drew Houston, Jack Dorsey, and even basketball star Chris Bosh, come together to send people a simple message about the importance of being able to code regardless of where you work. Many of them compare coding to sports, reading, or music; it may seem intimidating at first, but it’s an extremely useful skill once you learn it.

Moreover, these coders see computer programming as the future of technology and our lives. As Gabe Newell, the co-founder of Valve, said, "The programmers of tomorrow are the magicians of the future." Do you think coding is the new superpower that everyone should learn?
