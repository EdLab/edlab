Preparing Science Teachers At The American Museum Of Natural History

chingfu

April 14, 2014


Good science teachers have been in short supply but thankfully there are various government initiatives (such as President Obama's STEM Teacher Pathways) that aim to address this problem. In New York, aspiring educators have a great opportunity to obtain their science teacher certificates not from traditional schools of education or teachers colleges, but from one of the leading science museums in the country— the American Museum of Natural History (AMNH).

The Master of Arts in Teaching (MAT) program at AMNH is a fifteen-month-long program that specializes in earth and space science teacher preparation. In lieu of academic courses taught by doctoral level educators and student teaching in public schools, the MAT program incorporates museum residence experiences in which prospective science teachers assist and learn AMNH’s youth programs and work closely with the museum scientists to gain inquiry-based learning experiences in classes and fieldwork.
