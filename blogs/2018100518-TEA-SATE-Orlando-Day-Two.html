TEA SATE Orlando, Day Two

jvazquez

October 5, 2018

Today’s talks centered on the last two pillars of SATE: architecture and technology. 


There was no one thread that tied all of the day’s talks together; instead, it offered lessons from a wide range of industries. Topics included edutainment, leadership, horticulture, and retail. 

Here are some of my favorite lines and paraphrases from the day of talks: 

Every single kid has something absolutely vital to teach us. Kids can be our guide. 

-Scott Gass
Director of Zoological Communications and Interpretation, Seaworld Parks and Entertainment 

A memorial is a reflection of its community. Matthew Shepherd’s gravesite was erased. The Pulse Nightclub will forever be a place of remembrance, love, and acceptance. 

-Barbara Poma
CEO/Executive Director of onePULSE Foundation and Owner of Pulse Nightclub
(She was the only speaker to receive a standing ovation at the end of her talk) 

Sometimes you have to make an impossible decision between a job that promises security and a dream that makes no promises at all. 

-Brian Smith and Jackie Cuscuna
Founders of Ample Hills Creamery
(Keynote speakers)

(On creating a physical, walkable space:) Is there an opportunity to provide a level of comfort [within the parks]? It’s very important for people to be able to find places to sit of their choosing. 

-Francis Boettcher
Associate VP, Market Sector Leader for Themed Entertainment and Leisure, AECOM

(On the influence of smart technology in the household:) There is a level of intelligence being bred into our children that we don’t know how to manage yet. 

-Johnnie Rush
Chief Business Innovation Officer, The McBride Company
