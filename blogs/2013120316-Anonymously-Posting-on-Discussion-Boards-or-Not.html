Anonymously Posting on Discussion Boards or Not?

Sharon Hsiao

December 3, 2013

Most of the users do not understand why they need "accounts" to "sign-in". Creating accounts to sign in is a sustainable plan for content management and mainly for creating personalizations for users and filter out noises or information overload. In education, researchers study participation and contributions with anonymity. A recent journal report shows consistency with past literature: (I thought we could read this and keep it in mind for mSchool, Vialogues, NLT implementation)

H1: Wide variation in students' preferences, with only 1/4 of students indicating they preferred to post anonymously. H2: Consistent with previous research, overall students reported they are more likely to post when student discussion boards allow anonymous postings (medium size effect). H3: Students who prefer to post anonymously do NOT differ from those who prefer to make identified postings in their likelihood of posting on anonymous discussion boards, but they do post significantly less on discussion boards requiring identification.
Pedagogical implication is that enabling anonymous postings on student discussion boards may increase the engagement of those students who prefer anonymity. This means that there should be features that allows teachers to seamlessly configure that, but that would be assuming all teachers understand the differences. Article: "I'd be so much more comfortable posting anonymously": Identified versus anonymous participation in student discussion boards. Lynne D. Roberts and Camilla J. Rajah-Kanagasabai Curtin University, Australia Australasian Journal of Educational Technology, 2013, 29(5).
