When Will the Workplace Be Ready for Augmented Reality?

dinosossi2

July 7, 2016


An explosion of upcoming augmented reality devices will push them into all areas of life, including the workplace. A recent study examined the social applications of augmented reality (AR) at work by testing it with construction workers. Construction teams used an augmented video call software to improve work via smartphone. The AR program overlaid drawings to enhance video from the site. Teams could display live video, discuss challenges on site via instant messaging, and solve problems socially.

The study uncovered software problems like difficulties sharing complex processes and a need for direct communication channels over distance. These problems suggest a need to scaffold learning to address AR’s limits. Yet the program has a lot of potential: video calling could provide guidance at places of work because visual cues could be shared among team members and discussed collectively. It could also help remote workers or those in dangerous locations and reduce workplace injuries.

This study is a useful first step in using AR for professional and vocational training, and it hints at how AR apps could be used over long distances. There's no shortage of potential studies that could explore mobile AR for workplace learning.

Pejoska, J., Bauters, M., Purma, J., & Leinonen, T. (2016). Social augmented reality: Enhancing context-dependent communication and informal learning at work. British Journal of Educational Technology, 47(3), 474–483 . doi:10.1111/bjet.12442
