Using the World Cup to Teach Global History

Nantwi

May 10, 2010

In anticipation of EdLab's Development & Research weekly meeting tomorrow, where members are required to propose project ideas for the summer, I am proposing an online global history game based on the format of the World Cup specifically aimed at high school students. Given the amount of anticipation and enthusiasm that often accompanies the FIFA World Cup, especially among young people, the potential to use the showcase as a learning tool is enormous. The FIFA World Cup is a month-long soccer tournament held every four years consisting of the 32 best soccer nations from all corners of the world. It is the second most watched event besides the summer Olympics. There is a lot of video and trivia games already in place for the 2010 edition of the tournament but none of those games offer an educational component. With an increase in the use of video games and online tools as a learning resource by educators, the time is ripe to use sports games as another platform to teach. I've attached a brief outline of the functionality of the tool and how I envision it to work. Feedback on the idea, functionality, questions and suggestions are welcomed. I look forward to hearing other great project ideas during tomorrow's meeting.
