Little Competition to Have the Skills

rendy

January 9, 2014

Most people don't like sitting all day for an exam, especially teenagers. So it was really interesting to see two teenagers from Hartford High School in Connecticut, sit down for an all day math exam. Nicole Selmanie and Barbara Maidelis, teachers at a local school, wanted to start a math team to support talented students in that subject. Selmanie reported that students were struggling and testing below average in math. She also stated that nearly ten students from the school began weekly tutoring sessions to practice with no pressure and were helped by more advanced students. As the program grew, Maidelis tried to have other schools start a similar program so there could be competition at the next level. This is the best way to start an after school program. Having the students plan, spread the word, and pass out fliers about an after school tutoring program is one way to entice students to come.
