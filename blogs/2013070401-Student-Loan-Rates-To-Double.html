Student Loan Rates To Double

abagigah92

July 4, 2013

Interest rates on student loans soon if Congress does not take action. Student loans is one of the major reasons why many individuals end up in financial difficulties. In most cases, it takes a student over ten years after earning a degree to pay out their student loans. With the current recession, college graduates are finding it harder to find the right job after they graduate, which puts them in a situation of not being able to pay their loans. Missing student loan payment increases interest which means it will take many more years to be able to pay off the loans. In a recent article, I read about some college students taking loans for the fall semester and seeing interest rate double during the spring semester. This means subsidized stafford loans, which account for roughly a quarter of all direct federal borrowing, and will go from 3.4 percent interest to 6.8 percent interest. Congress is hoping to block that from happening after the July 4th holiday break.
