Math + Music = Magic in the Classroom?

Kate Meersschaert

July 12, 2011

As we continue to explore the possible intersections of music and core school subjects, here is a recent blog post from NY magazine proposing a collection of songs involving numbers. To take this thinking further, I found research into the math of music and early theories mapping of harmonics using Pythagorean tuning and ratios. Additionally, early astronomers cited the "music of the spheres" and explored early links between astronomical phenomena and musical structure. As we partner with the Rock & Roll Forever Foundation, how might these ideas and possible connections inform the teaching of math through Rock music? Is Mathematics better suited to one genre of music or another? Inspired by these questions, listed below are a few selections from NY mag's "mathematical mixtape" to which I added my own math-inspired favorites. Please feel free to add your thoughts and favorite numerically-inspired tracks! "4 + 20," Crosby, Still, Nash & Young "50/50," Frank Zappa / Mothers "2 + 2 = 5," Radiohead "5 to 1," The Doors "1 after 909,"The Beatles "Two Divided by Zero," Pet Shop Boys "2x2," Bob Dylan "1234," Feist "Numbers," Ryan Adams "Never Ending Math Equation," Modest Mouse "Supermassive Black Hole," Muse
