FarFaria

Ting Yuan

December 14, 2014


FarFaria, is an award-winning app for iPad and iPhone that provides a collection of more than 600 digital stories and songs, with five new stories released each week. The name FarFaria comes from the common fairy tale beginning: "Once upon a time, in a land far, far away…" The app is designed to evoke a sense of being transported to another world, much like the imaginary experience that an engaging story would present. FarFaria provides teachers and parents digital opportunities to encourage pleasurable reading for children starting in the early years.

Pros:

In the world of FarFaria, story genres are arranged on a fantasy map in eleven categories. Young readers can choose to have the stories read aloud or to read them on their own. In the read aloud feature, text is highlighted as each story is read aloud; the pages are flipped at the readers’ pace by swiping the page to ensure engaged listening. The app provides a clean and intuitive interface, without pop-up ads, prompts for purchases, or anything else that would distract or slow readers down.

Cons:

The app itself is a free download with one free book per day, while unlimited access to the content requires a subscription of $3.99 per month. The picture books made available on the mobile platform are well illustrated but don't contain any interactive elements. More social reading or animation elements might need to be considered for future development.

Our Takeaway:

FarFaria has great potential for use in both general and special education classrooms with a variety of reading levels and differentiated options. The subscription cost might be challenging for classroom application, as institutional options are not clear.

The Bottom Line:

FarFaria is a fun reading app that could be remixed with paper books for children in elementary grades or below.
