Singularity University

Kate Meersschaert

July 23, 2014


Our mission is to educate, inspire and empower leaders to apply exponential technologies to address humanity’s grand challenges. (Singularity University site)

School of Innovation
Led by a team of educators steeped in real world experience and focused on innovative "exponential technologies" and a futurist founder nicknamed "the restless genius," Singularity University is poised to help accelerate innovation across a wide variety of fields. Located in Silicon Valley at the NASA Research Park, the group counts a wide range of big-name thought leaders (including Google, Autodesk, and the Kauffman Foundation) as founding corporate partners and offers three programs: a graduate program for "future leaders, entrepreneurs, and technologists," an executive program for entrepreneurs and business leaders, and an "exponential medicine" program for physicians and those in health sciences fields. The graduate studies program focuses around 10 weeks of team work to develop possible tech solutions to global issues. The executive program is a tools-focused experience targeted at seasoned leaders who want to "ride the wave of accelerating change" while the week-long exponential medicine program is aimed at healthcare professionals interested in exploring and reconceptualizing the future of medicine.

Facilitating Exponential Innovation
In addition to workshops and programs focused on training future and current innovators, the group hosts conferences, curates and produces content focused on social innovation, and arranges speaking engagements for faculty, all while incubating innovative startups aligned with their focus on global impact and social innovation. In this last capability as an accelerator, Singularity Labs provides entrepreneurs with the space and support (mentors, corporate connections) to "collaborate to build the next great solutions by leveraging exponential technologies in an interdisciplinary way." The group’s Global Impact Competitions serve as the key to access, granting winners a scholarship to their choice of Singularity U programs.
