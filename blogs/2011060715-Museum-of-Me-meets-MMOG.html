Museum of Me meets MMOG?

Kate Meersschaert

June 7, 2011

Museum of Me is basically a glorified intel promotion that allows users to upload their Facebook images to create an automated virtual gallery of their connections, photos and location-based data. However, this potentially cool tool lacks any kind of interactivity, which seems to defeat the purpose of a virtual world. While pretty and fun to use (once), I feel that this tool is extremely limited in its potential to be a "game changer" in the virtual and social interactivity realms. Despite this... the idea of creating a virtual museum of your connections and online experience has real potential. Past explorations of personal social graph data has tended to lean more towards data visualization and quantification, whereas a virtual environment that focuses on qualitative info and experience could take the social networking experience to new levels of interactivity and relevance in an increasingly "gamified" world. Much like virtual learning environments, virtual social and learning spaces are continually evolving. Wikis, virtual MMOG worlds and more are continuing to be increasingly popular platforms for social and idea interchange. If anyone doubts this just check-out one of the World of Warcraft's wiki's... rated one of the largest wikis in the world and at one time #2 behind Wikipedia. What could Facebook become if there was more of an interactive, virtual world layered on top of the existent interface? Any ideas? World of Facebook Craft? Land of Likes? Third FacebookLife?
