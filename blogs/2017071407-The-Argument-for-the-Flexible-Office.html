The Argument for the Flexible Office

dinosossi2

July 14, 2017


Starchitect Norman Foster was given a most intimidating task. 

Work with Steve Jobs to create their now iconic new campus, Apple Park (a.k.a. The Circle).

Despite working on a highly designed office, Foster, in contrast, preaches from the gospel of flexibility. 

To fill in the detail on this, spoke about his one minor possible change.

The underground garage.


Given the constantly changing world we inhabit, Foster believes that we should incorporate so much flexibility in our design of spaces that we should even be able to change something as banal as a parking garage.

Given a deeply technologically infused space like the Learning Theater, striking a balance between legacy infrastructure like the venerable walls of Gottesman Library as well as constantly changing technology may be the biggest challenge in this space, the library, and TC at large.

In short, how do we respect our past, but also make way for the future?
