Four Emotional Technologies That Will Redefine What it Feels Like to Be Human

Renae Lesser

November 15, 2016


When we imagine the way that emerging technologies will impact our lives in the future, we rarely consider how these advancements might be used to augment our emotional and interpersonal experiences. But what if technology could make us more compassionate, offer us personal insight, or give us counsel in matters of the heart?

In this animated video School of Life predicts an explosion in the popularity of so-called emotional technology. The video forecasts four hypothetical tools that could help humans navigate their emotional lives in the not-so-distant future:

  1. The Mood Reader: This application will allow you to communicate better by monitoring your emotions and offering friends and loved ones summaries of what you're feeling.
  2. The Spouse Finder: This matchmaking tool will take the guesswork out of romance by understanding your inner needs and directing you towards the best available fit.
  3. Socrates: This wise wearable gadget will stop you from making bad choices by feeding you self knowledge in real time.
  4. The Career Locator: This high-tech career aptitude assessment will identify your latent talents and plug them into the needs of the economy.

These technologies could alter not just how people communicate, but how people interact in learning spaces: how they share and read moods, how they receive feedback, and how they make informed decisions about their learning paths.
