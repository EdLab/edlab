NASA Technology That Has Profoundly Impacted Life and Learning on Earth

Melanie.Hering

June 24, 2019


There are dozens of technologies originally invented by NASA for the purpose of better, safer space travel that now affect our everyday lives here on Earth. From memory foam to LEDs to wireless headsets, inventions spearheaded by NASA researchers and partnerships have revolutionized so many aspects of our life and learning. Whether adapted to support safety, learning, wellness, or connection, these inventions have undoubtedly changed how we live our lives today.

From GPS signal correction software to high-pressure oxygen tanks, NASA technology proves the adaptability of new technology for a variety of contexts. However, many people are uncomfortable with the amount of money dedicated to space exploration. Do you feel this way? If not, what arguments would you make to convince people of the importance of NASA research to everyone here on Earth? Join the discussion on Vialogues.

Excerpts from the discussion

@01:15 Hobie Terpeluk: We are made of stardust. Exploring space is just another way to understand life on earth.

@01:15 Anna Curry: I think the effort to expand human potential is a worthwhile effort and it opens us up to new solutions and possibilities in other areas.

@01:15 Betsy Harrington: Pointing out how NASA discoveries have contributed to technologies we use on earth isn't a convincing argument for why NASA is worthwhile. Instead of spending time and money figuring out how to make it possible to breathe in space and then spending more time and money transferring that learning to mak firefighter gear, why not just start by tackling our problems on earth?
