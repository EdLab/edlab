Penn Tags: Folksonomy at the U. Penn Library

Frank Webster

July 14, 2006

Anita Lauer forwards this part of a discussion on folksonomy that is underway on AUTOCAT, the cataloger's listserv: Date: Thu, 13 Jul 2006 13:25:48 -0500 From: Beth Picknally Camden Subject: Re: The place of a folksonomy in libraries Erica: You may want to look at the PennTags project from the University of Pennsylvania. http://tags.library.upenn.edu/ Although still in a beta release, PennTags has allowed our users to create annotated bibliographies with their own tags/social bookmarks/folksonomies. These can include tagged records from our catalog (Franklin http://www.franklin.library.upenn.edu/ ), our video catalog (VCAT http://www.library.upenn.edu/catalogs/vcat/ ), e-journal articles and the web. Example: http://tags.library.upenn.edu/search/journalism (many of the entries are tagged records from Franklin) OR search Franklin for the book: Journalism in the movies by Matthew C. Ehrlich Scrolling to the bottom of the record, you will see the tags and annotation added by a user (in the yellow box). (note: you may experience a slight delay before the box appears) We hope, that as PennTags develop, to do an evaluation of user tags as compared to standard LCSH terms. Asking questions such as: How well did the cataloger capture the essence of the book, as compared to someone who read it? Are there new subject aspects in the user's tagging that could be converted to standardized language? Do new natural language terms need to be added as cross references, should the subject heading be updated? What can tags teach us about the way users approach the organization of materials, and how might that change the way catalogers approach subject analysis? To read more about PennTags see: http://labs.library.upenn.edu/ and follow the links. Beth Picknally Camden Director, Goldstein Information Processing Center Van Pelt-Dietrich Library Center University of Pennsylvania 215-746-4149 bethpc@pobox.upenn.edu http://pobox.upenn.edu/~bethpc/
