StartupCity: Ebay Buys Hunch

Skanda Amarnath

November 22, 2011

Welcome to StartupCity, a content stream that covers entrepreneurship in New York City with a mild slant towards education. This will be a new content stream that I will be piloting for potential inclusion in New Learning Times. For those who were here during the summer for Walkabout NYC when we visited a handful of startups in NYC, some big news regarding Hunch! Hunch has been acquired by Ebay for $80M. Hunch is a recommendation service that takes a users' preferences on a variety of goods and services to find other things users would be interested in but might not otherwise hear about. Acquiring Hunch certainly makes sense for a company like Ebay but it is yet to be seen whether 80M is the efficient price. On the one hand, I can seeing it be the case that Ebay actually made a reasonable and appropriate acquisition this time around. On the other hand, I'm skeptical of the value-added Hunch really has to offer. In all, I have to think Facebook is kicking themselves for not being able to market Facebook users' information in this way. Facebook was supposed to this (sell information and market more efficiently); instead it's Hunch...I mean Ebay.
