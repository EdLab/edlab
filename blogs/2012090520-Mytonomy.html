Mytonomy

LauraCostello

September 5, 2012


Mytonomy is a video-based network focused on college choice and career planning for students in high school, college or recent graduates. The site aims to crowdsource and flip college counseling, operating on the assumption that students will respond better to "near-peer" advice and to the diversity of perspectives in the massive video network. At present, the site offers more than 2,200 videos featuring general college application advice, reviews of specific schools, and program advice for particular careers.

Pros:

The site clearly focuses a lot of energy on content creation. Each submission is evaluated for content by Mytonomy staff and the resulting pool of 2-10 minute, testimonial-style creations are engaging and earnest. The site is in the midst of a redesign that will add a bit of color and better presentation of featured videos, though the planned changes retain the expansive alphabetical video-browsing list on the main page.

Cons:

Growth on the site is slow and deliberate, but the quality of the content ensures that the mission of the site is clear in each video. This impressive commitment to content makes one wonder whether the incentive and moderating systems are sustainable should the site rapidly expand. At this point, Mytonomy offers a $20 Amazon gift card to any student, who uploads a set of four approved videos. The caliber of video content sets Mytonomy apart from other similar sites, and courting institutional partners might help the site share some responsibility for quality control and inspire more rapid growth.

Possibilities:

Mytonomy is one of a set of recently launched sites offering tools aimed at forward-looking students in high school, college, and recent graduates. It has so far avoided the fate of similar sites that become places where companies and schools advertise aggressively to prospective students. If Mytonomy can grow and still maintain its refreshing approach of being a site for quality peer-created content, it can play a unique role in providing important information to students.
