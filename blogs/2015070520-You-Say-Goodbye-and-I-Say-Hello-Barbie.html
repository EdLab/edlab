You Say Goodbye, and I Say Hello Barbie!

Christina Terracino

July 5, 2015


Attending school is not only an academic experience for young kids, it is also the perfect environment for socialization. Children learn how to talk to new people, make friends, and share their interests with others. But what if they don’t need their peers to be able to do all of these things? What if they can do it all with the latest toy?

Hello Barbie is such a toy. The doll, a longtime friend of kids, will now be able to communicate with individual children via voice recognition technology. The new Hello Barbie is WiFi compatible, and can be wirelessly updated by Mattel, the doll’s creator, to stay current with the latest trends. Barbie will store information she learns about kids in the cloud, where she can call upon it during playtime to customize conversations. She might use it to mention the name of a pet or to ask about the child’s favorite school subject.

As magical as it may be for kids to chat with a doll that can actually remember their previous talks, many parents are vehemently opposed to this toy. Fears for their children’s and their own privacy have been widely recognized. There is even a petition to stop Mattel from releasing this "eavesdropping" toy.

There are certainly concerns over how this doll will affect the socialization skills of children, their reliance on technology, and their ability to determine reality from make-believe. Will it erase the need for imagination during playtime, and isn't that the whole benefit of playing with toys?
