Learning Theater Spotlight: The Art of Body-based Interpretation

EdLabStudios

November 12, 2017


"The edge of a colossal jungle, so dark green as to be almost black, fringed with white surf…" While these words could be parsed and diagrammed and explained in a 5-paragraph essay, Donna Costello encourages students to use movement to delve into literature for an entirely new artistic experience. How could you use your body to express the word jungle, or surf, or almost? More than that, how does movement convey something that words cannot? These are the questions Costello brought to the Learning Theater for the Literacy Unbound summer institute this past July, where high school students and teachers came together to read and create an original performance based on Joseph Conrad’s Heart of Darkness.

For over 15 years, Costello has been teaching people how to use movement to reconnect with their bodies and learn new things about themselves. As a dancing artist, Costello was a founding member of Shannon Hummel’s Cora Dance and has since performed throughout the U.S. and Germany. Her own work has been performed in several states as well as in Mexico. As a teacher, Costello has taught at Brooklyn Arts Exchange since 1999, where she is the Youthworks Director, and facilitates workshops throughout NYC in public schools and other educational spaces.
