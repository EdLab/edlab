Startup Spotlight: Kaggle

Kate Meersschaert

January 11, 2012

The Details and Backstory Kaggle hopes to help to make data science more "sportlike." Started in 2010 and described on their site as, "a platform for data prediction competitions that allows organizations to post their data and have it scrutinized by the world's best data scientists." Contests hosted by Kaggle create a data analysis outsourcing opportunity for companies and organizations, while offering cash and prestige to scientists. Contests have run the gamut from from mapping dark matter for Nasa to predicting liability in car accidents for Allstate. The team behind Kaggle includes Anthony Goldbloom, who founded the site after working in macroeconomic modeling and attaining degrees in both economics and econometrics - learn more in this Forbes 30 under 30 profile. Unique Point of Difference? Kaggle stands-out in that the site, as this FastCompany article points-out, is not really selling anything and instead brokers data analytic opportunities, "After all, it's not selling anything, creating another social network, or peddling yet another useless app." However the service the site provides could be game changing both to organizations, scientists and potentially the world. EdLab Take & Takeaway? Perhaps the biggest takeaway for the lab could be for us to form a team and enter a Kaggle contest!? I wonder which of the many offered would be most applicable? Perhaps this one from the Hewlett Foundation? Or like Grockit, we could offer a contest of our own...
