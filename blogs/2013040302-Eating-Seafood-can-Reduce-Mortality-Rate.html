Eating Seafood can Reduce Mortality Rate

Khalil Abubakar

April 3, 2013

Scientists have proved that eating seafood high in omega 3 fatty acids may help protect against cardiovascular disease and reduce death in people over age 65. Blood tests were used to track different types of omega 3 in 2,692 randomly selected people, average age 74 at the start of the study, for 14 years. All were healthy without heart disease and none used fish oil supplements. Over the 14 year period 1,625 deaths were recorded. There were fewer deaths from stroke in those with high levels of omega 3. Overall, the researchers calculated those with highest omega 3 blood levels lived 2.2 years longer than those with lowest levels. The most beneficial levels of omega 3 can be achieved by consuming 3.5 ounces of farmed salmon, 5 ounces of anchovies or herring, or 15 to 18 ounces of cod or catfish. Also consuming foods with omega 3 fatty acids can help reduced heart rate, lower blood pressure and lower triglyceride production which contributes to reduce mortality.
