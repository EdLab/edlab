Paramedic Training

EdLab Studios

February 16, 2016


What's a typical day on the job for a paramedic? The answer is, there isn't one. Over the course of 12 months and 20 instructional hours per week, the Paramedic Program at LaGuardia Community College instills in its students the skills and understanding needed to do the best they can when faced with any number of possible emergency situations. From heart attacks to respiratory calls, seizures and car accidents, paramedics are first on the scene; responsible for the patientâ€™s individual physical and psychological needs from start to finish. In-class emergency scenarios featuring life-sized training mannequins provide the opportunity to practice and improve skillsets like intubation and IV administration while preparing students to be great patient advocates overall. Under the guidance of preceptors, students then board ambulances across New York City for 20 additional hours of clinical rotations where they'll respond to real calls from real patients and anything can happen.
