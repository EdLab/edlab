And the nominees are...

Adrienne Garber

April 23, 2010

Often referred to as "the Academy Awards of the Web," the Webby Awards are touted by many as the leading international award honoring excellence on the Internet. The awards are presented by The International Academy of Digital Arts and Sciences, which includes an executive 750-member body of leading Web experts, business professionals, visionaries and celebrities (as well as Associate Members who are former Webby Award Winners and Nominees). Established in 1996, the Webby Awards presents two honors in every category, The Webby Award and The People's Voice Award. Last year, an astounding half a million votes in aggregate were cast for the People's Voice Award. See video below from the Muppet's Animal encouraging the public to "make his mama proud!" Of particular interest to those of us in the education community, the five nominees in the Rich Media- Non-Profit/Educational Category are:

  • Ad Council- That's Not Cool
  • Cyber Bullying Affects Real Lives
  • How Hetero
  • Stanislav
  • Synchronous Objects

Click on the "read more" link below for additional details about IADA's Webbys and the upcoming Internet Week New York. By way of background, for each category, the Academy selected five (5) nominees which represent the five best in the category. Out of the nominees, one (1) winner will be selected by the Academy. In addition, the online community, through a public vote, will select a winner from the nominees in each category. Voting is currently open for the website, interactive advertising, online film & video and mobile categories. FYI- The Webbys are a forerunner to NYC's Internet Week New York, to be hosted by The International Academy of Digital Arts and Sciences and The Mayor's Office of Film, Theater, and Broadcasting from June 7-14, 2010. Internet Week New York is a week-long festival of events celebrating New York's thriving Internet industry and the many talented companies, organizations, and innovators shaping the future of online media. Stay tuned for more information about this event as details develop!
