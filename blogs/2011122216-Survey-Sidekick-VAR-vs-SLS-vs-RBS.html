Survey Sidekick - VAR vs SLS vs RBS

Manav Malhotra

December 22, 2011

In our redesign of Survey Sidekick we've been looking at different ways to present survey questions. When considering the 5 point Likert scale we thought about the merits of supplementing the discrete answer set (radio button scale or RBS) with a more continuous sliding scale (visual analog scale or VAS) or an amalgam of the two (slider scale or SLS). An example of all three are in the image below: Big marketing survey tools like Qualtrics do have sliding scales available, but it was unclear whether this was useful from an academic perspective. There's been some research done on the merits of the VAS in survey research but with mixed results. One study from 2006 reported that using VAS for questions resulted in higher rates of non-completion, higher rates of missing data, and longer completion times than other methods of input with no apparent advantage in terms of the distribution of responses. A German study from 2008 found that the data was measurably different, implying that there may be situations and statistical techniques for which VAS is better suited to the job. In terms of data interpretation, from an abstract perspective, having a more continuous data set when mapping qualitative responses to numbers would offer greater flexibility in terms of the analyses that could be performed. The biggest downside, as reported in the work of Frederik Funke, seems to be the extra cognitive effort required by the VAS. Overall, the jury still seems to be out so it might be interesting for us to try and do studies in this area using Survey Sidekick. Does anyone have experience with this or have strong feelings either way?
