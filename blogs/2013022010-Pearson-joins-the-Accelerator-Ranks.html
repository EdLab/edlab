Pearson joins the Accelerator Ranks

Kate Meersschaert

February 20, 2013

Following the news that Kaplan has entered the edtech accelerator race... Pearson, under the guidance of Diana Stepner (Read her NewLearningTimes.com profile here!) and her Future Technologies team after they announced their entry, Pearson Catalyst. Listed below is the EdSurge.com take from their recent newsletter:

The program offers up to $10K for startups--with no equity taken. Here's the catch. They need to be working on things "that complement or enhance a Pearson brand," which presumably includes its extensive list of products and services. Ten teams will be chosen for the three-month program that will start in mid-April. Here's what it sounds like: work with us to deliver and promote our content, and in return we'll get you access to the markets where we've already established relationships.
What are your initial thoughts? Is the accelerator trend just that... a trend? Or, will these new funding models help the edtech ecosystem to thrive and grow in exciting new directions?
