Podcasting Doesn’t Have to Only Be a Supplemental Tool in Education

Ryan.Allen

May 10, 2018


Podcasts have rapidly grown in popularity in recent years, yet this medium’s connection to education is still not fully understood, and podcasts are often seen as merely a supplemental tool for educators. In an attempt to challenge this notion, Dr. Christopher Drew of Teesside University analyzed characteristics, practices, and trends in a sample of the most popular education-related podcasts.

The researcher first sampled the top podcasts listed in the "Education" category from the Podcast Awards, along with other notable series. His final sample accounted for 20 of the most popular educational-focused podcasts. This selection ranged from science podcasts like Neil deGrasse Tyson’s StarTalk, hit history podcasts such as Hardcore History, and even some various language-learning podcasts.

For these 20 podcasts, Dr. Drew analyzed the first three episodes, five random episodes, and other special episodes. He identified key trends and themes: purpose, series structure, type, participants, materials, length, and subject. Some of his findings challenge commonly held notions of podcast usage in education.

Despite the contention that long podcasts should be avoided for educational use, almost all of these chart-topping podcasts ran over 30 minutes per episode. History podcasts tended to have especially long run-times, as they often offered deep, nuanced approaches to the subject. Meanwhile, shorter podcasts seemed to only provide basic, introductory information.

Another common belief challenged by the findings is that podcasts are just a supplemental tool in education. On the contrary, in the study, podcasts were the central educational feature, while other worksheets, transcripts, and blogs acted as the supplemental material. The language-learning podcasts especially incorporated outside materials.

While Dr. Drew admits that his sample is small and non-generalizable, he does contend that his research should begin a dialogue about the use of podcasts in educational settings. The medium is becoming far too popular and important to be dismissed as a supplemental tool. He hopes that educators will begin to think of innovative practices to incorporate podcasting into curriculum and pedagogy.

Drew, C. (2017). Edutaining audio: An exploration of education podcast design possibilities. Educational Media International, 54(1), 48–62.
