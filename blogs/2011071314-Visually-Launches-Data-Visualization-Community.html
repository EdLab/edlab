Visual.ly Launches Data Visualization Community

Kate Meersschaert

July 13, 2011

Visual.ly, is a new, online data visualization and infographic clearinghouse described in their launch email as, "the world's largest community for exploring, sharing, creating, and promoting data visualizations." After a quick exploration I found the infographic gem on the "Class of 2011 Social Media Yearbook" attached below and am excited to explore both Visual.ly Labs, where you can create your own visualizations by uploading data from Excel, Tableau, Powerpoint etc. and their Twitter visualizer. My only criticism so far, would be that you can't seem to search by date. As a researcher or educator I would imagine timeliness to be one of the most important aspects of integrating these visualizations effectively into one's work. A data viz from 2009 could easily become irrelevant in less than a year, and it seems you would waste a lot of time on the site unless they added this filter. How do you feel we can best integrate data viz into our projects here at EdLab? What projects would be a best fit? What added value might they contribute to user experience?
