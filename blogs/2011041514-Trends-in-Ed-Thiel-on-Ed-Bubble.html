Trends in Ed: Thiel on Ed Bubble

Fred Rossoff

April 15, 2011

This week TechCrunch reported on an interview with Peter Thiel, paypal co-founder and venture capitalist, in which he made a few controversial claims. They open by claiming “this article will piss off a lot of people,” but for me it feels more like a rare whiff of common sense. Thiel contends that higher education is the American economy's new bubble, replacing the previous housing and technology bubbles. He says: “A true bubble is when something is overvalued and intensely believed. Education may be the only thing people still believe in in the United States. To question education is really dangerous. It is the absolute taboo. It's like telling the world there's no Santa Claus.” Thiel's case, it turns out, is moral as well as economic. He finds it unacceptable to be prescribing for everyone such an exclusive college education. As a solution, he wishes to develop alternative paths for ambitious young people to pursue, like his “20 under 20” program, which provides cash incentives for students to drop out and start their own companies.
