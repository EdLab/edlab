Museums, Mobility and Social Tagging

Nabeel Ahmad

March 28, 2007

Two articles in the NY Times Art section described a progressive movement by some museums to provide value to and learn from their audience through audio tours and social tagging. The audio tours article talks about ways in which museums have used their on-the-go visitors to enhance their access to information. Translating this to the library, imagine browsing the stacks and being able to pick up an audio device with preloaded resources describing the contents of each floor. That would definitely add some excitement to the stacks, but would it add value to the TC community? Something to ponder. The social tagging article talks about ways in which museums have been making efforts to digitize their collections. The interesting aspect is that some have allowed the public to "tag" the art. The results have given museums a better understanding of public perception of their art. PocketKnowledge, of course, employs this feature. Can we use some benchmark techniques that museums have been using to discern semantic differences between the pre-tagged content and user-tagged content? Do we even think this is important? I also think it's interesting that some museums pre-screen user tags before adding them to their database.
