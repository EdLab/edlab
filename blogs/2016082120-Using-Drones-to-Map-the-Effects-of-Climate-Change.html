Using Drones to Map the Effects of Climate Change

saraehardman

August 21, 2016


In California, some people are employing their personal drones to help document the effects of climate change, particularly after El Niño ravaged the coastline this past year. Although typically scientists rely on satellites and aerial photography to map the coast, they have recently asked people to document high levels of water and changes in terrain by taking pictures on their phones or personal drones.

The scientists collect these images and use them to map the area. Drones and phones work better than satellites and aerial photography, says one scientist in the video, because they are more immediate and collected by a greater number of people. One of those people is a man who flies his personal drone up and down the California coast; he calls himself a volunteer citizen scientist. "The thing I like about being a citizen scientist," he says, "is that I know I’m being a part of something bigger than me."
