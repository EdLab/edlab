Ed in '08

HSC

May 6, 2007

Unhappy with their $2 billion return on investment (ROI) in American public education, Eli Broad and Bill Gates have joined forces with the Rockefeller Philanthropy Advisors to form Strong American Schools, a nonpartisan public awareness and action campaign. According to their website, they want every presidential candidate to address three priorities to improve education: 1) American education standards 2) Effective teachers in every classroom 3) More time and support for learning Download their "Policy Primer" to read more. While I applaud the group's efforts to make education a top priority in the 2008 presidential election, I wonder if any of the candidates will offer anything in the way of a long-term/feasible solution to the current problems in the U.S. educational system. To read what the candidates are saying about education, visit their official Web sites.
