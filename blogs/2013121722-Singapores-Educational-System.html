Singapore's Educational System

Bismark Appiah

December 17, 2013

While I was searching videos relating to my theme on global education, I stumbled the one below about why Singapore's education system is so successful and serves as a model for other nations. In most of my Vialogues on global education, I've noticed that a lack of resources, quality of teaching and unqualified teachers, and a country's failure to invest in its education system are some of the reasons why a lot of undeveloped countries have terrible education systems. In Singapore, policy makers focus on hiring qualified teachers who are passionate about their job and are willing to help students learn. The video shows and explains the training teachers in Singapore go through before they are hired. Singaporeans believe that "no educational system can be better than its educators." In essence, having the right teachers can help improve the performance of a school, while the opposite can cause a school to perform badly.
