Lifestyles of the Rich in Data

Brian.Sweeting

July 1, 2014

During yesterday's Visualized meeting we were discussing comments made by Werner Vogels, CTO of Amazon, on how big data has the potential to suck the joy out of life (e.g., a leisurely walk through the park becomes an analysis of steps, pace, & heart rate). In the same vein and also worth reading is the story of how David Sedaris' Fitbit obsession improved and ruined his life: Stepping Out: Living the Fitbit Life

I Look back on the days I averaged only thirty thousand steps, and think, Honestly, how lazy can you get? When I hit thirty-five thousand steps a day, Fitbit sent me an e-badge, and then one for forty thousand, and forty-five thousand. Now I'm up to sixty thousand, which is twenty-five and a half miles.
