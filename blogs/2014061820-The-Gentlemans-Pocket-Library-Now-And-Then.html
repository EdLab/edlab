The Gentleman's Pocket Library: Now And Then

Carmen James

June 18, 2014


James Loeb founded The Loeb Classical Library in 1911 with the original purpose of making classical works of Greek and Latin literature available to all. More than a hundred years later, the reach of the original mission has increased exponentially with the aid of technology. This video takes you on a tour of the new virtual library, set to debut in the fall of 2014.

Loeb designed books of classical literature into little volumes that could fit "in a gentlemanâ€™s coat pocket." As Jeffrey Henderson, General Editor of the Loeb Classical Library, points out, now you can "fit the whole library in a coat pocket." The easy-to-use interface allows users to access books on various kinds of devices, search for key words, annotate and send favorite passages to other users. With a storied past and renewed future, the entire library is now at the fingertips of users. What do you think of this digital library? How would you use it? What would you want to improve?
