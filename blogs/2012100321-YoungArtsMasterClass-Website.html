YoungArts/MasterClass Website

Nantwi

October 3, 2012

We are hoping to launch the YoungArts/MasterClass website very soon. YoungArts is a national program based out of Florida that chooses the best high school students in nine cinematic and performing arts areas such as dance, jazz, and theater, among others. Pursuing Your Passions: Lessons from YoungArts/MasterClass is a study guide inspired by HBO's MasterClass, a series of episodes that captured workshops and moments between high schoolers chosen by YoungArts and “masters” in their respective fields. Some of the masters in the series include architect Frank Gehry, choreographer Bill T. Jones and opera singer Placido Domingo. The study guide is divided into five chapters and each chapter contains activities in three subject areas: Humanities, Math & Science and Visual & Performing Arts. Below is a sample clip from the MasterClass episode of Danish artist Olafur Eliasson in which he suggests that it might be time to reevaluate the master/student concept. In addition to the website, we are working on gaining access to classrooms piloting the study guide so we can create professional development videos similar to those we did for UFR. A special thanks to Megha, Zhou Zhou, Hui Soo, Luke, Daniel and Pranav for all their work in moving the website and the project forward.
