A More Dynamic Web Video Experience

Doug Beacom

January 28, 2009

I'm getting ready to publish a video on After Ed this Friday, and in preparation for putting it out there, I'd like to try and foster a more interactive experience. I thought I'd take it to the blog--after seeing the innovative tsunamis at the seminar this afternoon, I'm sure someone must have something up their sleeve... so, the video is a short 3 minute piece asking people around columbia, on the street, and in a middle school: ""what would you text obama in response to his goal to 'transform education to meet the needs of new age' ??"" You'll have to wait until Friday to see the results, but I'd also like to encourage viewers to quickly contribute comments, a text-message, something to go with the novelty of the whiteboard and format we narrowed in on for this video. (in the video, participants use a whiteboard mocked up like a cellphone text message screen, and write their messages with a dry erase marker) Josh recently led me to: http://firesidelearning.ning.com/ where educators have created a social network talking about...education! It looks like they have regular viewers of After Ed, and I'm going to seek their feedback--but I wonder what else might be out there-- any fun apps or codes I could paste into the blog entry to take it a step further than the comment feature? I've considered creating a 'ning' network to go with Globalization-themed videos, but don't think that is my best first step.
