A New Graduation Requirement In Chicago

ABARRY

July 4, 2017

To graduate in many states you are required to fulfill several requirements including obtaining a high school diploma. However, in Chicago students will soon have a strange but promising requirement. The requirement is students will have to outline a plan of future in order to graduate despite obtaining their high school diploma. The plan includes: a college acceptance letter, a job, a trade apprenticeship, a gap year program or the military. Mayor Rahm mentioned " We are going to help kids have a plan, because they’re going to need it to succeed,” he said. “You cannot have kids think that 12th grade is done" This plan is set to assist students who are uncertain about life after college. In some schools students have no advisors or mentors to help them construct a solid plan for life after college.

As one-on-one counseling  will be crucial to this plan's success. It has raised many questions since it's been approved this may. Many are wondering if the program can provide enough mentoring and counseling to fulfill this goal. 
Jermerica, a rising senior at Morgan Park High said she had few interactions with her counsellor and she mentioned they never had a conversation about life after school or what she wanted to do after high school. In this particular schools counseling budget are very thin, which means not every student has a counselor. However, this plan intents to overcome those barriers and help students flourish  affer high school.
