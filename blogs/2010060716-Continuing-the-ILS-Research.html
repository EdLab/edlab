Continuing the ILS (Re)search

jvazquez

June 7, 2010

I've been researching various open source ILSs and found two giant heavyweight contenders: Koha and Evergreen. So, I placed these two in a head-to-head cage match and watched them fight to the death because I'm hardcore like that. The winner? See for yourself: To be fair, the question marks denote a lack of clear evidence, and not necessarily a lack of that feature. Still, I chose Koha because of the abundance of information on it, a lot of developers are currently hacking away at it, and it's supported in a number of academic and public libraries. In a later post I can specifically compare Koha with our current ILS (i.e. reigning champ vs. challenger) and we can see whether one is a clear winner or not. The main question regarding an ILS switch remains: Will it be worth it? The answer, for now, is “Let's try it and see.” For more info on Koha, check out the demo below.
