Push to Relax High School Standards

abagigah92

March 25, 2013

In this recent article, the idea of relaxing high school requirements has been proposed by Texas in order to allow for more career training. This idea has triggered a lot of concern over training to push minorities into career paths instead of pushing them towards college. The approach was done in respond to business leaders complaining about the issue of not finding enough skilled workers after high school. In 2007, a bill was introduced to cut on the number of standardized tests and high school requirements. Minority leaders and those against the bill see this approach as being negative towards minority students in Texas. A change in the academic system will disproportionately place low income and minorities students into careers in tech over going to college.
