Students Invent Natural Disaster Relief Capsule

Rebecca.Sullivan

August 6, 2018


In the wake of natural disasters, fast medical relief can save lives. But flooded neighborhoods, collapsed buildings, and blocked roadways often impede transport of supplies to those who need them most. In response to the unprecedented number of natural disasters the United States faced in 2017, 3M hosted a Disruptive Design Challenge, asking teams of college students to create a relief capsule that is resilient, easy to transport, reusable, and waterproof. North Dakota State College students won the contest with their Dazzle Box.

Dazzle Box’s unique truncated octahedron shape makes it easy to stack and able to survive a 150ft drop. The team lined the box with foam, which increases the device’s resilience and can be reused to make bedding, sponges, or other supplies. Dazzle Box would be hard to miss, even amidst rubble, with its LED lights and dazzle camouflage design, and every piece of the capsule is modular, making it simple to repair.

Dazzle Box passed feasibility testing, but will it be able to respond to unpredictable, real-life disasters? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
