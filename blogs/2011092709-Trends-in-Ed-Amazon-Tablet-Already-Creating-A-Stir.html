Trends in Ed: Amazon Tablet Already Creating A Stir

Skanda Amarnath

September 27, 2011

Looks like Amazon is getting ready to make its big splash into the tablet market. Word on the street is that Wednesday, when Amazon has scheduled a big press conference, will be the day of the big announcement. Beyond the usual "Tablet Wars" commentary, I'm curious to see what Amazon's vision for the Kindle Tablet will be. From the latest accounts, the initial product will resemble RIM's playbook, yet I have to think Amazon has more in store if they're investing so heavily. PC World reports that the tablet will likely be significantly cheaper than competing tablets, a similar pricing strategy Amazon used for selling the Kindle. As I said, I'm more interested to see what Amazon wants the Kindle to become, how it will be integrated with other Amazon services as well as its capacity to tap into new use-cases for the tablet. This will probably be one of the cheapest tablet PCs on the market upon release, thus offering more people the opportunity to access tablets. Does this mean greater adoption of tablets in the education sector? I remember not too long ago, ProctorU stated they couldn't afford to give every one of their test-takers a tablet to show math scratchwork. Does the cheaper cost of Kindle change that? This also funny to reflect on how this tablet revolution started...with Nick Negroponte's wish to build a super-cheap tablet. While his technology got the ball-rolling, it looks like private enterprise is catching up quickly, making tablets more affordable without any of the subsidization Negroponte's One Laptop Per Child project so desperately needs. The question now is whether greater availability and affordability of tablets will lead to increased educational opportunities as Negroponte claimed?
