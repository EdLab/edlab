ESL Students Read to Dogs to Improve English Skills

Dana Haugh

April 14, 2015


Did you know that man's best friend is also his best reading partner? Manhattan's P.S. 163 does, and has been using these lovable canines in their after-school reading programs for the past 7 years. Students who struggle with reading aloud are given the opportunity to practice their storytelling in front of their tail-wagging companions, helping bolster both confidence and comprehension. This weekly session is part of a larger after-school program providing students with after-school meals, one-on-one tutoring, and extra help. But after seeing the program in action, it's clear reading time is the most widely anticipated.
