Project DragonflEye Turns Insects Into Hybrid Drones

DebraLee

April 29, 2018


Dragonflies are some of the best fliers in the insect world. Their highly evolved wing morphology enables fast flight, gliding, hovering and backwards flight, and they can travel thousands of miles over land and water, reaching altitudes of up to 6,000 meters. This gives them a major advantage over micro air vehicles, which can usually only fly for a few minutes at a time.

That’s why scientists are equipping the bug’s body with a solar-powered backpack and turning it into a drone. The backpack taps directly into its nervous systems so it can be "steered" to fly in certain directions. Tiny structures called optrodes in the backpack emit pulses of light that activate the steering neurons, which, in turn, activate muscles that operate the dragonfly’s wings.

Researchers say dragonfly drones could be used in remote environment monitoring, search and rescue in dangerous buildings, and for biomedical purposes. The platform could also be transferred to honeybees to help save crops that might otherwise be lost. What other uses do you imagine for this part insect part robot technology?
