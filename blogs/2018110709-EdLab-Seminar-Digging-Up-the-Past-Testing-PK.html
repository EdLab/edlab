EdLab Seminar: Digging Up the Past (Testing PK!)

jvazquez

November 7, 2018

Hello! Let’s get down to business.


The new PK site can be found at https://pk.tc.columbia.edu


Instructions

As a group, work through the list below, providing a PK link as an answer for each. Nominate someone from your group to record and post your answers to the blog. One winner will be announced based on item completion, answer accuracy, adherence to rules, and one mystery criterion.


So, do your best and aim to win the undying respect and admiration of your fellow excavators (and me, your judge).


Your digging orders

  1. One of the most-filled pockets on the old PK was Gottesman Libraries Archive. Find it as it exists now, and post the most recently added item to it.

  2. Find a file published before 1800.

  3. Find a portrait of ONE of the namesakes behind any of TC’s buildings (or halls).

  4. Find a doctoral dissertation from 2016.

  5. Find a paper authored by famed anthropologist, Margaret Mead.

  6. Find a document on high school health education from the 1960s.

  7. How many total files are in PocketKnowledge?

  8. This one has two parts:

    1. Each member of your group must

      1. Add a pdf, image, OR video to the repository

    2. Give one word to describe the process (one word from each person).

  9. Where can contributors find information on our copyright policy?

  10. As a group, find an archive item worth highlighting on the front page of PK. Bonus: Try to download it! (Everyone should try to download)

  11. Name one thing that differentiates the new site from the old. (hint: here’s a link to the original PK)


If you find yourself with PK feedback for the team after the seminar, email Hui Soo and me and we shall diligently pass it along.
