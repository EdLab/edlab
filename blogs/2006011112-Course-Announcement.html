Course Announcement

Anthony Cocciolo

January 11, 2006

For those of you who haven't received this course announcement: Course Title: Possibilities of Virtual Worlds MSTU 5510, Section 13, Spring 2006, CRN 52005 3 points; special dates as noted below; meeting place 345I Macy Course Instructors: Christopher Carella and Chuck Kinzer Chris Carella is a founder and managing partner of Future Prototype (Metaverse Consulting Group; www.futureprototype.com), which is an interactive serious games consulting group. Future Prototype is working with clients in fields as varied as healthcare, education, marketing, and emergency response, operating from a philosophy grounded in a belief that massively multiplayer online virtual worlds provide a unique opportunity for collaborative learning, training, and social interaction. Before founding Future Prototype, Chris was a Serious Games researcher at the Institute for Security Technology Studies at Dartmouth College. Chuck Kinzer is a professor in, and coordinator of, the program in Communication, Computing and Technology in Education at Teachers College, Columbia University. The following is a very brief description of the class and includes meeting dates. A full syllabus will be available at the first class meeting. The class meets for extended time periods to allow both discussion of readings as well as significant hands-on components during the class sessions. In additional to class sessions, students are expected to participate in Second Life's virtual environment and complete assigned readings. Jan. 20 Introduction; general discussion and demonstration of virtual worlds. Set up accounts in Second Life, use the tutorial, basic exploration of Second Life. Feb. 10 What are issues in virtual worlds for education? Design in gaming? Socialization? Readings about socialization, issues of representation. Theoretical discussion with hands on in a group situation. Mar. 3 Building and using environments: business, education, games. Identity issues; doing research in virtual worlds. Demo, hands on, readings discussion. Mar. 24 Video and course design in virtual worlds. Using browsers and video in Second Life. Readings discussion and hands on time for projects and mini-designs. Apr. 14 Project presentations, discussion of next steps in design, wrap up.
