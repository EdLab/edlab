Cheating the New York Times

Manav Malhotra

February 13, 2012

private please A quick note about something I noticed that may be relevant to how we position NLT. The New York Times now has a 20 article per month limit for those who don't pay for either a digital or print subscription. I hit that limit early this month and was prompted to pay. I assumed they used some combination of IP address and a cookie to track how many articles you've viewed, but I wondered how they dealt with people on the move. After a little fiddling, it turns out they don't use an IP address at all. It's a simple cookie! Clearing cookies from NYTimes or even switching browsers resets your article limit. That's a surprisingly low barrier to free access and I wonder what the rationale was behind it. As we think about the NLT model and whether we would want a free article limit, this might not be the best way to do it. Also, I'm not encouraging anyone to do this; we should all support the Times. But it is pretty easy. So if you do, at least click on some ads.
