Special Report from the Road: Wellesley College Library 1st Floor Space

Kate Meersschaert

June 21, 2012

We are currently filming in the WCTV studio on the first-floor of the library and it was interesting to take a quick foray out into the rest of this ground-floor space. The area is extremely mixed-use, with a range of offices, the Knapp Multimedia and Technology Center (an area-full of computers for various archiving and media storage/retrieval uses), conference rooms, a recording studio, various soft-seating arrangements and shelves of collections featured throughout (see the floor layout guide below). Seating... What strikes me as most unique is the wide variety of seating, which seems to exemplify the many uses of the space. These arrangements run the gamut from amorphous space-age green chairs to modern bistro seating and classic "captains" chairs. Vending is integrated well on this floor and massive windows with views of the rolling campus greens and surrounding buildings frame the space. Take-aways... Despite a best effort, from a design perspective, the first floor seems a bit disjointed and unclear in its focus, but perhaps that is the whole point. I definitely feel this "randomness" encourages "free-wheeling" thoughts and collaboration. I witnessed this first-hand as a conference, private Skype-based conversation and loud celebration (not to mention our filming) all were happening successfully, concurrently on the same floor. Note: Wellesley has a "Book Arts" program housed in a lab on the 4th Floor. Space-age green chairs... (with shelving?!) Long-shot of the green chairs and stacks... Vending tucked-away...
