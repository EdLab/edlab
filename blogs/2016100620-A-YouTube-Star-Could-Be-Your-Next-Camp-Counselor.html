A YouTube Star Could Be Your Next Camp Counselor

saraehardman

October 6, 2016


"At Camp17, our vision is simple: to provide one-of-a-kind camp experiences where fans interact with their favorite digital influencers in a fun and safe environment that nurtures authenticity, personal growth, and the creation of friendships and memories that last a lifetime." (About Camp17)

Where Creative Souls Mingle

A few YouTube stars are going to make their fans very happy this summer. Camp17 is the only camp in the country that brings teens together with their favorite internet celebrities. In some sessions, the YouTube stars are the head counselors who lead the campers through swimming, climbing, and talent show activities. Other sessions involve workshops in editing, photography, and building a fan base—valuable skills for any budding YouTubers.

This summer, the camp’s inaugural season, Camp17 will hold three camps with YouTube stars Tyler Oakley and Bethany Mota, Connor Franta, and Meghan Rienks. And if you don’t recognize these names, your tween certainly will. Camp17 aims to teach kids ages 10-16 the basics of YouTube video creation while also giving them a genuine summer camp experience with their digital role models. Part of the camp’s allure is that it often requires teens to turn off electronics and connect with those around them, including the YouTube stars they would be watching on their phones back home.

The YouTube Effect

With YouTube stars more popular among teens than mainstream celebrities, the 4-6 day camps demand a hefty registration fee of $1,995. Granted, the camp also offers need-based scholarships for YouTube lovers of all economic backgrounds. Other programs similar to Camp17 are popping up around the world (such as 2bcamp in Spain). But for now, this is the only one in America guaranteeing that kids who love spending all day on YouTube will actually go outside and enjoy summer camp activities.
