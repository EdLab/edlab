Math video game: Taking a formal walk into VA classrooms

Ting Yuan

May 5, 2009

As reported by eSchool News, Virginia has become the first state to implement a state-wide pilot program using Tabula Digita's 3-D DimensionM video games to help boost student test scores in mathematics and motivate students to learn.

  • The state's decision on the program is made after reviewing previous studies on video games. One study conducted in 2008 by scholars at the University of Central Florida shows DimensionM's immersive educational video games significantly improved students' understanding and raised scores significantly on district-wide math benchmark exams.
  • The study was based on a sample size of 193 algebra and pre-algebra students and 10 math teachers from Orange County, Fla. Students in the experimental groups who played Tabula Digita video games over an 18-week period scored significantly higher (in some cases, twice as high) on district benchmark tests than students in the control group who did not play video games.
    • Comments -- Though the study, excitingly, shows dramatic increase of math scores, we are not sure if the effect brought by video games will be long-term or short-term. My lingering questions are: does students' motivation of practice come from the "novel" experience/feeling brought by video games? Or does it come from a mixed experience of spacial reasoning, mathematical decision making, and simulation? Will math video game replace traditional pencil-and-paper practices? How can video game bring a real cognitive understanding of math problems instead of attracting students to participate in "drilling" in a playful way?
