A Giant Leap For Brick Kind: LEGO Breaks Its Own Gender Norms

jvazquez

October 24, 2013


In the world of LEGO minifigures, none have grown so large in stature as the newest addition to the family: Professor C. Bodin. What makes this newbie so special? Professor Bodin is LEGO's first ever female scientist! While her exact profession is vague (she carries Erlenmeyer flasks but her bio describes her as having talents reminiscent of Dr. Moreau), her connection to the sciences is undeniable.

The ratio of female to male LEGO characters is 1:4, and many of those females have historically been based upon gender stereotypes (see the party-crazed Lady Robot, pretty-in-too-much-pink Intergalactic Girl, and the entire LEGO Friends collection). But, thatâ€™s not to discredit the few female STEM-related characters that LEGO has introduced us to over the years, beginning with a doctor in 1978. Still, a quick glance over the available sets on the LEGO site reveals none but the Friends collection feature female minifigs on their covers.

At a time when all eyes are on the STEM gender gap, hopes are high that the young girls who pick up the new Bodin-laden sets see in her what they too can become. It is also hoped that Professor Bodin will be just one of many more admirable characters to come, one that girls (and boys) can look up to.
