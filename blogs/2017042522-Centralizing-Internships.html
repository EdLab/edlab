Centralizing Internships

Kevin.Camacho

April 25, 2017

Most college students are very familiar with the Common App. It is one of the easiest ways for them to apply to a host of schools at the same time. Two students from Georgia Tech have taken that approach and applied it to internships. The students, Nathan Dass and Murtaza Bambot, founded InternBlitz as their response to what they saw as a broken internship process. Last year, Murtaza applied for 125 internships and received only three responses. Additionally, they found that students waste too much time filling out the same information (e.g. GPA, major, email) on all their internship applications.
 
The app is still in its infancy and the founders have yet to develop a business model. InternBlitz is currently open to only Georgia Tech students. Read more about their story here.
