Oldies but Goodies: Michael Moore's TV Nation

Gus Andrews

November 20, 2008

Someone told me today that they'd never seen documentarian Michael Moore's show TV Nation back in the early '90s. Small wonder, really; NBC and Fox did everything they could to bury the show late in their lineups and kept moving it around, so it was hard to find and got little attention. I think they were worried about audiences (or advertisers) getting pissed off. But Moore, whether you like him or not now, really had something a lot more fun going on with this show. Less of the hamfisted moralizing; more stunts which almost resemble a political Punk'd. He did a great job of dramatizing what could otherwise be boring, dry political and academic issues. I've linked four of my favorite bits. They seem slower and older to me than they did at the time, but part of that really works. Watch the looooong closeup on the direct mail guy in the fourth piece; Moore is a master at making you squirm by not shifting attention off of awkward moments. Check it out!
