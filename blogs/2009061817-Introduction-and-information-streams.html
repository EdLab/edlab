Introduction and information streams

Dan Paluska

June 18, 2009

Hello all. I'm one of the new artists in residence. If you see me around the lab, please say hello. Like most of us, I'm a copious internet user. I've recently come to think of the internet as a gigantic lab notebook for everything I'm thinking about and doing. It's always amazing how much I forget. Given this, how can I leave myself a trail of crumbs to help me remember how I got here? And why not leave this trail open so others can take a peek and give me relevant advice they might have? ( My EDAR project is also focused on analyzing the benefits of open access but more on that later. ) For now, I'll just let you know the things that I am doing. I keep a friendfeed account: http://friendfeed.com/danpaluska Friendfeed is an RSS feed aggregator, sort of like a facebook homepage but outside of facebook. It keeps tracks of my activity on a variety of internet sites like Twitter, Facebook, Flickr, YouTube, Vimeo, Scribd, etc. You can set it to be world readable or private. You get to my blog, Facebook, Flickr, YouTube, etc from there as well. I occasionally make tutorial videos about how I link these services together. Here are a couple if you'd like to try this out -> http://www.flickr.com/photos/sixmilliondollardan/3384118138/ http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X-oaw_c6Rjo Feel free to ask me any questions. I'm looking forward to working with you all.
