Learning Rare Languages Through the Shared Course Initiative

Nantwi

January 12, 2017


Globalization has made language learning more attractive to college students; however, most universities can only offer courses in a few languages regardless of student demand. A new collaborative program by Columbia, Cornell and Yale offers opportunities for students to learn less common languages, even if they're not offered at their school.

The Shared Course Initiative allows students of these universities to enroll in classes such as Hungarian, Bengali, and Yoruba. Instructors supplement their usual face-to-face instruction with video conferencing, and the end result is a highly collaborative endeavor, on and off line, between students and instructors.

What are some implications for such an approach to language learning? What other subjects can potentially take advantage of this type of collaboration? Join the conversation on Vialogues.
