It's Getting Easier for Pre-K Kids to Learn Robotics

chingfu

September 20, 2016


Robots are engaging tools for helping young learners grasp the basics of computer programming. Research has shown that children who explore science and math topics at a young age are less likely to face obstacles entering technology fields; however, there still aren't that many robotics kits available to kids.

Researchers from Tufts University addressed this problem by designing a robotics kit called KIWI, geared toward 4 to 7 year-olds. KIWI is special because it uses wooden blocks with unique barcodes that can be scanned to program its actions. It's also equipped with light, sound, and distance sensors for rich interactions and performances. Researchers used these blocks to evaluate whether the kid-friendly features would facilitate young childrenâ€™s learning in robotics.

Findings from this study showed that all kids, including the youngest Pre-K children, can use sequencing concepts to control their robots. K-2 kids can perform advanced programming activities that include repeat loops and conditional statements, and can also understand various parts and their respective functions in the KIWI robot.

Technology and engineering applications are everywhere in young learners' lives nowadays, and robotics kits like KIWI provide exciting opportunities to prepare for futures in these fields.

Sullivan, A., & Bers, M. U. (2016). Robotics in the early childhood classroom: learning outcomes from an 8-week robotics curriculum in pre-kindergarten through second grade. International Journal of Technology and Design Education, 26(1), 3-20.
