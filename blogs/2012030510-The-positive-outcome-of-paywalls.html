The positive outcome of paywalls

Josh Lopez

March 5, 2012

Techdirt had an interesting article about paywalls this weekend that got me thinking about long unavailable opportunities that might become accessible again.

It seems Gannett and the LA Times are going to jump into the paywall club sometime later this year. Paywalls themselves are hardly news anymore, but the Techdirt article raises an interesting point. Since readers commonly post links to the articles they read, paywalls eventually lead to blocked (dead) links, making it more difficult to verify the validity of the content in an article.

I don't think this will necessarily lead to users shelling out the money to access the source sites. History has shown users will not stand by companies who cannot evolve with technology.

This situation reminded me of the seminar from Dulcinea Media EdLab hosted a few weeks ago and the discussion about checking the reliability of sources on the internet. Since users will eventually find themselves blocked from accessing "credible" news sites after they activate their paywalls, newer and more user-friendly publishers will likely have the opportunity to establish themselves as a more authoritative source of information.

The "authoritative" sources may not be the go-to resources for establishing the credibility of articles, bloggers, and sites and this leads me to wonder how EdLab can get involved. With the heavy activity on the EdLab Blog and Vialogues, and New Learning Times and the mSchool on the way, there are a lot of opportunities to grab a piece of the "credible source" pie that for the first time is growing instead of disappearing (as pies usually tend to).
