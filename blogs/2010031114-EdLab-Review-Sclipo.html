EdLab Review: Sclipo

Arianna Choi

March 11, 2010

Sclipo Tags: social learning, learning management system www.sclipo.com Price: Free for social, K-12, higher-ed instructors; $9.95/month for Professional Membership Overall 3.25 out of 5 Design 4 out of 5 Pedagogy 2.5 out of 5 [The Good] Learners are given a wide range of choices in each of the following areas: 1) signing up for individual courses, 2) becoming a member of "Academy" which offers a selection of related courses or a "Group" within a certain "Academy," and 3) becoming a member of independently formed "Community." Course contents are also organized by media type - and not by topic - so individuals can choose according to his/her preferred medium or learning style. [The Bad] On the flip side, the sheer variety of choices - without clear distinctions between them - can be crippling. These distinctions are not intuitive and there are no visible guidelines explaining the differences between the three major features: "Learn," "Socialize," and "My Academy." Also, dissemination of knowledge (i.e. 'lateral movement') for instructors is much better supported compared to students' knowledge development/growth (i.e. 'vertical movement'). For example, navigating through course contents in a certain sequence (e.g. order of importance or difficulty level) is not addressed at all. [The Bottom Line] Dear Sclipo: For you, less is more. Clarify the names of your major features. Learn vs. My Academy... what's the difference? Recommended uses K-12 Teachers or professional freelance instructors of any subject. All popular media types and formats are supported on Sclipo and managing as well as assessing student feedback is well-supported. Learners, however, should be warned that there is a high learning curve for navigating through the variety of complex functions - even though the user interface looks intuitive at a first glance.
