Economics & Education: Diane Ravitch and Class Size

Skanda Amarnath

June 28, 2011

New York Times has an article out on San Diego schools fearing the effects of larger class size. From the very beginning, I was skeptical of the article's slant, given Diane Ravitch's endorsement. The article was surprisingly sharp in its insight on class size but nevertheless came short when it came to concrete evidence. Comparing Bill Gates and Arne Duncan's views to that of Ms. Taylor and Ms. Penamoya is not helpful. Gates and Duncan are taking views based on empirical evidence, Taylor and Penamoya's views are almost purely anecdotal. The low-down on class-size: No informed commentator can say reducing class-size does not improve student achievement. On the other hand, reducing class-size is costly. The question is: Is it worth it? Commentators like Diane Ravitch fail to consider this question honestly. If we consider opportunity cost and take into account econometric analyses, the answer to the aforesaid question is no. The data tells us that there are better ways to spend resources to raise student achievement than to simply hire more teachers (regardless of quality) to reduce class size. Instead, focusing on raising the quality of teachers within a school is a more cost-effective way to raise student achievement.
