Cowbird: Beautiful, slow storytelling

Janet Jang

December 16, 2011

"Cowbird is a simple tool for telling stories and a public library of human experience." In a world dominated by Facebook, Twitter, and Tumblr, it's all about fast updates, hyperspeed blogging, and short attention spans. Cowbird is a refreshing antithesis to it all, promoting participatory journalism to capture simple human stories behind major news events. Their long-term goal is to build a public library of human experience to pass on to future generations of people. The site and its contents remind me of the hipster-poet cousin of FlipBoard. Also, probably because this is the brainchild of Jonathan Harris, the the website is easy to navigate and stunning. The only thing I don't like: its logo. The vision of the platform is quite political, focusing on tracking the saga of the Occupy Movement and the experiences of the "common people" as they keep mentioning. So if you're the 1%... you probably wouldn't fit in this community of storytellers. You also have to request an invitation in order to create your own account of stories, which apparently can take up to several weeks due to the massive influx of requests they've been receiving, despite the fact they've only released this platform just over 2 weeks ago.
