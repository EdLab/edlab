Meety testing: good, OK, and weird

Anthony Cocciolo

August 13, 2007

I have been doing some testing on the Meety speech recognition and research results feature. The initial findings seem to indicate that there are some interesting research articles that do come up, and some seemingly unrelated content. This emphasizes the importance of the ratings engine, yet to be implemented into the site, that allows students in a collaboration session to rate up or down certain materials (in effect, removing from view those resources that are not relevant). For example, I tested Meety with the first 390 seconds of the Hank Levin podcast made during the EdLab seminar. There were several upsides, including some fairly good translations, such as this: “Professor Hank Lemmon is the William Herger Patrick professor of economics and education heritage college”, which should have read “Professor Hank Levin is the William H Kilpatrick Professor of Economics & Education at Teachers College”. There were also some pretty compelling articles that did come up as “Meety-made notes”, including the following: Good More schools go year-round to boost achievement Neglected Evidence: Educational Research Wikipedia: Standardized Coefficient Small Schools Project OK Newport News - Withdrawing Your Kids From Newport Public Schools Weird, but kind of interesting Bald eagle soaring 'success,' but at what cost?
