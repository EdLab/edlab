DIY U - EduPunks Unite!

Kate Meersschaert

June 14, 2011

Anya Kamenetz has been called an education "game changer" by the Huffington Post and her newest book will be released as a free ebook funded by The Gates Foundation this summer. After visiting her site, however, I feel the description "punk-rock education rebel" would be more apt. Anya focuses on the DIY model of education and truly seems to believe that the power lies with people to learn and not with institutions. For example, she states in her Fast Company column, Life in Beta, "The future of learning is open--and it's in your hands." This idea is more explored more fully in both her 2010 release, DIY U: Edupunks, Edupreneurs, and the Coming Transformation of Higher Education and her forthcoming free ebook,The Edupunk's Guide to a DIY Credential, available on the web this summer. Anya states her primary and secondary goals for the upcoming web-based ebook project on her site: ...to reach low-income students and potential students to help them find alternative paths to a credential using online and open resources. The secondary goal is to reach educators and administrators interested in incorporating the latest technology, social media, and collaborative learning into their approaches in order to cut costs while improving learning, socialization, and accreditation both inside and outside the classroom. What do you think about this open-learning model of getting your credentials from online resources, instead of relying solely on more traditional institutions?
