Skip College, Work for Startups

Janet Jang

February 8, 2012

Enstitute proposes a solution to monstrous student debt, wasted time in libraries, and unemployment for young students. The founders have rounded up 30 entrepreneurs from the NYC tech scene and asked them to take apprenticeships for two years. The minimum requirement to apply is a high school diploma and must be between the ages of 18-24. They just started accepting applications yesterday. (Mashable) They still encourage high school students to only defer their acceptances to college and not completely throw the option out - "just in case". I'm curious to see how many of these Enstitute graduates will be able to sustain a career afterwards without a college degree. Most likely, the first year of applicants will probably be people who already have college degrees. One can argue all they want about being just as qualified as a college grad (and they probably are), but we can't ignore that society still cares about how someone looks on paper.
