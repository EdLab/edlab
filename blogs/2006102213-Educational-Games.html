Educational Games

Dan Mallinger

October 22, 2006

With the holidays approaching, VTech has been running a new promotion to get kids to design the next big educational game. It'll be interesting to see (1) how VTech does in the market (they're an old company with some new tricks); (2) how this new game works out. Companies have been turning toward techniques to make the customer feel more involved for some time, but I think the new zeitgeist shifts the paradigm from a feeling of customer centeredness, to true customer influence. Even the Bob-Vila-ites have spun off with "Ask This Old House" to replace "This Old House!" Video game learning isn't really my cup of tea (at least not yet), but there seems to be some interest in it at the EdLab. And since we don't have the resources to build a full game from scratch, it seems to me that we have two options: 1. Utilize the Open Source movement so that we're not building from scratch (this is pretty typical). 2. Capture on the growing uncertainty of how this new "democratic" (not really...) market works by coaxing companies like VTech to work with us. Could be fun!
