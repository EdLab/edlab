Trends in Ed, 3.8.10

Arianna Choi

March 8, 2010

More Classroom Hours? Definitely Not for Some. "A small but growing number of school districts across the country are moving to a four-day week..." According to today's article on Wall Street Journal, more than 100 school districts out of the 15,000-plus districts nationwide are currently using the four-day system to hedge budget holes and teacher layoffs. This movement goes against the current plan Secretary of Education Arne Duncan has been promoting: America needs longer school days and school years. The article adds that there is no significant research done to prove or disprove any negative effects of shorter school days, but teachers and parents are feeling the extra cost of shorter school hours on their part. As for solutions, there is no mention of what could be done for those students who may later have to pay the cost for those void hours on Fridays. Just a food for thought to fuel more ideas and passion for everyone (including myself).
