A 50 Cent Origami Microscope

saraehardman

November 20, 2016


Manu Prakash is a physical biologist who won a MacArthur Genius Grant for his work on frugal science inventions. These inventions are remarkable because of their simplicity. His most notable creation to date is the foldscope, an origami microscope made of paper, copper tape, and batteries. These microscopes are incredibly inexpensive to create, making them easily accessible to people around the world.

Prakash hopes that foldscopes will be as ubiquitous as pencils in classrooms and available to all children. In the next year, he plans to make 1 million foldscopes, each of which will cost only 50 cents. The foldscope is printed on thick paper, which children can then fold origami-style to create their microscopes.
