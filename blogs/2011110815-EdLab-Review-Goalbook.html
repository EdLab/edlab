EdLab Review: Goalbook

Fred Rossoff

November 8, 2011

Goalbook is a social platform developed for the teachers and students. It has all of the features of general social media (profiles, messaging) plus some applications specifically developed for managing a classroom. Pros: The program incorporates some things we've talked a lot about on Edlab Review, like gamification and progress tracking. It lets the teacher develop student goals, compare progress across the class, and provide awards or demerits based on their progress. This seems like a real step up from ClassDojo and other classroom management systems I've seen. Cons: I worry that the developers may have borrowed too much from regular social media applications like Facebook. There are profile walls, update streams, and other communication tools that may end up distracting more than enabling cooperation or encouragement. EdLab Relevance: Many of the features of Goalbook might be too much for higher education oriented programs like pundit, but I'm wondering if we should start thinking about how Vialogues might be incorporated into a broader suite of applications. Bottom Line: The program is still in beta, and isn't particularly widespread as of yet, but I like it the most of all the programs I've seen so far in its niche. It's simple, cheerful, and comprehensive.
