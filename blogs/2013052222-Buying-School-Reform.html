Buying School Reform

abagigah92

May 22, 2013

School reforms are basically measures that are put in place to ensure very conducive environments for students to learn in schools. It is obvious that money plays an essential role in the outcome of these reforms. The amount of money being induced in these school reforms are as high as what the major political parties fuse into their electoral campaigns. However, during elections, in terms of educational reforms, teachers unions have no particular say in decision making. In a nutshell, those who are wealthy make the decisions. It contradicts with our notion of a full fledge democracy but it is evident that changes will not be made to this favoritism based on wealth in a very long time.
