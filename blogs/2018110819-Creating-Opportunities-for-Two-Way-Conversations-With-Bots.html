Creating Opportunities for Two-Way Conversations With Bots

Melanie.Hering

November 8, 2018


What if you were able to have a real conversation with a bot like Alexa, one that allowed you to engage with AI in a new, more meaningful way? Is such a thing even possible, or is it just another Hollywood movie plot? A team of students at Brigham Young University thinks it is possible, and hopes that its new socialbot could make two-way conversations between humans and robots more feasible.

As their entrance in Amazon's Alexa Prize Challenge this year, the students have created Eve, a bot they hope will be able to engage in more meaningful conversation with humans. Using machine learning combined with models of human cognition and conversation, the team has designed Eve to be utilized by people who can't read or write, engage autistic children in new ways, provide therapeutic support or companionship, help students brainstorm, or provide educational opportunities to those who don't have access to them.

What kinds of learning opportunities do you think Eve could provide, once fully developed? What advantages and disadvantages can you imagine come with its use? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
