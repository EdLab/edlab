Teenagers Predict 4 Ways VR Will Transform Education

Renae Lesser

November 20, 2016


A group of high schoolers is introduced to immersive Virtual Reality technology (VR) for the first time. Inspired and energized by this new experience, these teens share their thoughts about how VR might transform education. Here are the four insights we came away with:

1. VR could help educators cater to diverse learning styles.
Lecture-style classes may work well for auditory learners, but bringing VR into the classroom could benefit those with a more visual or kinesthetic learning style.

2. VR could be the ultimate playground for the imagination.
VR learning doesn’t have to be reserved for just high school or college aged students, it could also give young kids new tools for expanding creativity and developing abstract thinking.

3. VR could be a "safe space" for those with emotional difficulties.
A teen who struggles with anxiety imagines that VR could be a "safe space" in the classroom for those with mental or emotional health challenges who need to disengage or decompress.

4. VR could be . . . anything!
There is a sense amongst those interviewed that VR has already had a profound effect on them. It’s not often that technology leaves us in a state of wonder and awe.
