New York Times Launches first Accelerator

Kate Meersschaert

July 29, 2013

The New York Times has launched their first incubator class as a part of timeSpace. The three companies who will take part in this first cohort include Delve (EdLab seminar alum... check-out their seminar here!), Mahaya and OPBandit. What do you think of this cohort? The Times getting-into the incubation game? Here is a description of the new initiative from the timeSpace site listed above:

timeSpace is a new initiative from The New York Times that brings entrepreneurs to our headquarters to refine and grow their businesses. Over four months, you and your team will work out of 620 8th Avenue, meet with relevant Times staff, demo your product and teach/learn alongside entrepreneurs and employees who make their livings in digital media, technology and journalism.
