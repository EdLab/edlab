Social Media Saving The World

abagigah92

January 10, 2013

Development in the world has become digital, as social media has rapidly become an agent of change. The worldwide web is currently the fastest and most convenient way of communication, sharing or gaining access to information and breaking news. Social networking sites, a not so new phenomenon in this era, has allowed people and organizations to create their own world online. These sites have created an environment for all users, especially young adults, with the opportunities to socialize and express their views on practically on any issue. Since social networks offers a direct link in which governments and corporations can target their respective populations, it should come as no surprise then that companies and political figures are using it to promote their works and agendas. This article highlights five instances, as examples, in which governments and organizations are using social networks:

  1. The World Bank has created a mobile app which makes it available for users to monitor projects they are funding.
  2. Ministermondays showcases situations in which Rwanda's Minister of Health (@agnesbinagwaho) uses Twitter to communicate with citizens.
  3. Digital Green works in connecting farmers in a remote locations to outsiders
  4. Catapult uses a crowdfunctioning platform to fund projects aimed at improving the lives of women and girls in the developing world.
  5. I Paid a Bride allows anonymous users to report instances of bribery and corruption.
What other ways do you know of in which organizations and governments are using social network to promote or bring about social change?
