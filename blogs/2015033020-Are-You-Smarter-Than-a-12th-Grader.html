Are You Smarter Than a 12th Grader?

Dana Haugh

March 30, 2015


Quick: List three of the original thirteen states! Name one of the writers of the Federalist Papers! Soon both U.S. citizen candidates and high school students in Arizona will be required to take a U.S. civics test, but passing grades will grant very different privileges. Arizona Governor Doug Ducey recently passed a bill that will require all Arizona students, graduating in 2017 and after, to correctly answer at least 60% of questions on the U.S. Immigration and Naturalization civics test in order to receive their high school diplomas. Students can take the test starting in 8th grade (while U.S. history is fresh in their minds) and can re-take the test until they pass. The bill is expected to go nationwide in the coming years, reinforcing the need to instill basic civics knowledge in the minds of all students. The intention is honorable, to say the least, but why stop at high school students? Are elected officials required to pass the same test before running for office?
