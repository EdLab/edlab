California Governor Signs Bill Ending Current Standardized Tests

Bismark Appiah

October 2, 2013

As a way of adopting to the new national curriculum standards known as the Common Core, California Governor Jerry Brown has introduced a learning assessment, which in turn has terminated the traditional standardized tests that has been in the state's public schools since 1999. In other words, the bill signed by the Governor Brown mean students will be taking their multiple-choice tests on the computer instead of the usual pen and paper. I really do not know what the difference in this new way of taking the state test makes, but one thing I am sure it shows how the use of technology is gradually taking over everything. Although the Governor of California has signed this bill into law, U.S. Secretary of Education Arne Duncan has threatened to end federal funds to the state if he continues with the plan. The Common Core State Standards as we all know is a set of standards that present clear educational standards in English Language Arts and Mathematics to adequately prepare students for entry courses in two or four year college programs and future careers.
