Oregon's Way to Attend College

Christopher Zamora

July 3, 2013

Attending college is a choice, but it has almost become a requirement for success. Recent news about college is about its rising costs and huge student loads for many upon completion. The state of Oregon is trying to help its college students avoid high student loans. The state's legislature recently approved a plan that allowed students to attend public universities without paying tuition or taking out loans. Instead students have to commit to a small percentage of their future incomes to repay the state and those who earn little would pay little. This method has been used in Australia, and Oregon will be the first in the United States to try to this method. I think the idea of eliminating tuition and loans may be a good idea as it takes off the stress of owing so much money though you have to repay the state once you start working. Check out this New York Times article on Oregon's model. What are your thoughts on Oregon's plans to eliminate tuition and loans?
