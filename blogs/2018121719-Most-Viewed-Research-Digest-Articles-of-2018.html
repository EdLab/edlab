Most Viewed Research Digest Articles of 2018

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2018


The most viewed Research Digests of 2018 reflect our anxieties about technology’s impact on society, from the spread of fake news to internet addiction to the effects of screen time on children. In these articles, researchers seek to better understand these phenomena in the hopes of equipping us to maximize technology’s potential and mitigate its negative effects.

1. What Causes Fake News to Spread Online?
A group of researchers set out to understand how truths or falsehoods diffuse across social media, and to what extent bots or humans factor into their proliferation.

2. Internet Addiction Is a Growing Problem, But How Widespread is the Issue?
The internet is an invaluable tool, but some of us have become addicted to this connectivity. Just like any addiction, internet addiction can have detrimental effects. Researchers in this study examined the reach of internet addiction among Chinese adolescents.

3. Does Limiting Screen Time Lead to Better Future Academic Performance in Children?
Researchers at the University of Zurich set out to determine whether such restrictions on technology use during childhood had an effect on individuals' future academic achievement.

4. How to Keep Students Engaged in Course Readings
How can student reading habits be improved, and how can this change improve their performance? A group of researchers decided to use online forums for pre-class reading discussions in physics classes to find out.

5. These Six Features Make Video Lectures More Interactive
There’s no denying that one’s focus wanes more easily with a video than with a traditional, in-person lecture, yet more and more online courses are using video lectures to convey information. To address this issue, researchers experimented with an embodied interactive video lecture (EIVL) to keep viewers focused and boost their learning.
