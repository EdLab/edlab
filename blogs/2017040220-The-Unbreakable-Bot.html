The Unbreakable Bot

csd2126

April 2, 2017


Researchers at MITâ€™s Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory have created a soft rubber cube that can autonomously jump more than twice its height. These unbreakable bots are made of soft 3D-printed materials that make them more durable and more precise in their movements. The cube jumps by using a set of steel flaps that are programmed to propel it into the air.

The spring-loaded cube is able to jump, bounce, and roll along different terrains. Future versions of the cube could carry cameras to assist in exploration or surveillance. The team is working to create new versions that can jump higher and go further.
