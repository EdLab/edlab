Univ of Texas ~Fast Tex Program

Erin Murphy

August 4, 2008

I meant to post this blog a week ago, sorry for the delay! I thought of this during the seminar with Gary Natriello. He mentioned that many faculty members are approaching the EdLab with ideas for projects they want us to do. I was reminded of my first job while I was a graduate student at the University of Texas at Austin. I worked for a program called ~Fast Tex. (People in Texas love to use Tex for everything). The program was developed by the Division of Instructional Innovation and Assessment. Each semester (or year) professors would apply for ~Fast Tex grants and the best projects would be awarded funding and the services of a student developer. For example, I was hired on as a video producer to develop online professional development for elementary school teachers. I was somewhat of an exception. Most students were hired to do programming and computer software development. You can find a better description here. I was thinking, if we are really getting that many requests for projects, this might be something interesting to consider.
