Trends in Ed: Where Have All the Magnets Gone?

Fred Rossoff

December 23, 2011

Last August City Journal published an article by Sol Stern that extends and deepens the critique of our current system I referenced in my last Trends in Ed post. Stern's focus is on how our education serves (or doesn't serve) America's best students. In the article he attacks NCLB, but from an entirely different direction than expected: “As noble as combating “the soft bigotry of low expectations” is, America's global standing and economic well-being are more likely to be improved by nurturing a culture of academic excellence and creating programs that support elite education in math and the sciences.” By his account, NCLB only cared about students demonstrating “proficiency,” and thereby incentivized school districts to disinvest in nurturing high-end talent so that they could shift resources to increasing low-end achievement. This official neglect, Sten argues, has led to a particular decline in STEM education, seen both in our decreasing international competitiveness and the lack of math and science specialized high schools.
