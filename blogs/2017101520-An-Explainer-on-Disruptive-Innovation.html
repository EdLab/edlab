An Explainer on Disruptive Innovation

Ryan.Allen

October 15, 2017


AOL used to be the global leader in Internet communication, as every high school kid in the late 1990s and early 2000s who spent hours chatting the night away on AIM knows. Then along came Google and other tech giants, dooming all those free Internet CDs that came in the mail. So, how did this phenomenon happen? It’s called the cycle of disruptive innovation, as coined by Harvard professor Clayton Christensen.

Industry leaders lose sight of innovation, and instead focus on amassing more high-paying customers or adding needless cosmetic upgrades. Meanwhile, new players enter the market with better alternatives for regular customers. By the time the aging giants notice, it’s too late and they are overtaken.

While Dr. Christensen offers strategies for fighting this cycle, it will always persist through innovation. What are some recent disruptive innovations that have impacted your life? Join the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
