Story Pirates Find Gold In Students’ Writings

Karen Bryner

October 11, 2013


What would it be like to have your recent writing project performed for all of your classmates? It’s a far cry from getting that story tacked up on the classroom bulletin board—and that’s the whole point. Story Pirates, an education and media organization, is determined to inspire students to be better writers. The group holds a series of writing workshops with elementary school students in their classrooms. After the workshops, members of Story Pirates select a few stories to perform. They then return to the school, bringing students’ stories to life through musical and comedy shows, cartoons, and films performed by actors, comedians and puppets for all to enjoy.

How can transforming students’ written works into stage performances by professionals encourage better writing and writers? Are there other ways to shift the writing of stories from a task into an enjoyable creative process without involving professionals? Join the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
