A New Take on Teacher PD with Video-Based Visual Learning

Melanie.Hering

February 21, 2019


Working at institutions already tight on money and relying on professionals often spread way too thin, teachers often donâ€™t receive the professional development they deserve. Technology has been able to provide some services to alleviate this problem, such as Earshot, a program that automates audio feedback for teachers. But what about visual feedback?

Kettle Moraine High School has been employing a new method of teacher professional development to fill this specific gap. Their new system involves using a video tracking system alongside coaching to help teachers pinpoint problems in their classroom and find ways to monitor and fix those problems in their practice. Through the use of this new type of visual learning, teachers can better track student engagement and address their own blind spots in a way they can't when at the front of the class.
