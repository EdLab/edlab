Using Google Maps to Find People You Don't Like

Anthony Cocciolo

February 9, 2009

Eightmaps.com is an interesting use of Google Maps. It takes the donors of Proposition 8 (the one outlawing gay marriage in California) and plots it on a map. What is interesting about this is that it allows people to a little more objectively and geo-spatially channel to their annoyance. I watched the Focus Features movie Milk this weekend, and in the movie almost all of the animosity was directed towards two people, or the leaders (John Briggs and Anita Bryant) pushing Proposition 6 in California (the Briggs Initative), which would have eliminated any gay people from working in education (which fortunately failed, but not by a lot). This acts to vilify a few people and leave a lot of other people untarnished. With that said, I do feel a little sorry for the few folks who live a couple of minutes from the corner of Castro and Market Streets. Luckily there aren't any people near the corner of Robertson and Santa Monica Blvd in Los Angeles. What is interesting is there are a number of people from Salt Lake City who felt it was their duty to get involved in California politics (surprise, surprise!).
