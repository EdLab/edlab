Google Suggest and What it Tells Us About Ourselves

Nara Kasbergen

November 27, 2012

Hui Soo just linked me to this article in the NYTimes from a few days ago, "In Search Engine Results, A Peek at What We Wonder", which talks about how Google and Bing's data about frequent searches reveals some interesting things about ourselves and our culture and what we spend a lot of time thinking about.

Frequently asked questions include: When will the world end? Is Neil Armstrong Muslim? Was George Washington gay?
Anyway, when I saw the article it provoked a moment of nostalgia because I actually did a small project on this in college! It's not very well-designed or pretty, but it was one of my first projects with Processing and I had less than 2 weeks to do it. I decided to use Google Suggest to figure out what the common cultural stereotypes are. The application is still online, so you can access it at here. As a note, you'll need a 64-bit browser (Safari works best) and the Java plug-in which you can download here. If you don't want to install all that and just want to see the output, attached is a screenshot (but there's some interactive features you'll miss out on).
