Kids’ Show Proves it’s Never Too Early to Start Learning Electronics

jvazquez

June 2, 2013


You say there's nothing good on TV? It might be time to switch over to the internet to find some quality educational programming. From Adafruit, the go-to source for all things maker, comes a new online series geared towards the toddler set: Circuit Playground. Using puppets and flashy animations to teach the basics of electronics, Circuit Playground brings to life a topic many kids find simultaneously fascinating-yet-boring. But, where others have failed to hold the dwindling attention spans of kids these days, Adafruit's short, engaging lessons strike a chord in an otherwise plugged-in generation.

During an age when puppets reign supreme in the teaching department, Adafruit’s approach seems to fit right in. Could internet-based, modular kids’ programming be the next big thing in edutainment? Weigh in on the topic (and learn what an "ampere" is) over at Vialogues.
