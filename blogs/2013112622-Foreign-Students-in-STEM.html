Foreign Students in STEM

rendy

November 26, 2013

International students have a big role in sustaining the quality of STEM graduate programs across the country. The University of Texas-Arlington, University of Houston, SUNY Buffalo and others have at least 90% of international student enrollment in their STEM programs. According to Jonathan Bredow, professor and chair of the electrical engineering department at Texas-Arlington, domestic students usually try to get a job right after completing their bachelor's degree. Well a lot of people do that and mostly it's because they want to have enough money before they aim for more degrees. I believe the reason the high percentage of international enrollment is because they tend to be more motivated for several reasons. Most consider the opportunity to study at American universities to be a once in a lifetime opportunity and therefore maximize it. Second, they have individual dreams and aspirations and thus they have the motivation to go through the strenuous and challenging work associated with STEM.
