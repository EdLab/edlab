Can Creativity Be Taught?

Evans.Asamoah

May 19, 2017

Can creativity be taught? In this op-ed  Forbes, the author argues that creativity should be a “necessary part” of undergraduate education. She thinks that the current undergraduate education teaches students how to solve problems that have one answer. As a result, she believes students should be to think critically to draw multiple conclusions or solutions. She argues for undergraduate institutions to divergent thinking. From her perspective, this form of creativity is needed for today’s students, who represent our future leaders. 


During my now four years in high school, I was not introduced to divergent thinking approach to problem-solving. We were basically taught to just find a solution to the problem. I am skeptical this will change in college but I am hopeful. In our ever evolving world where creativity and critical thinking ability is heavily sought after by industry, a divergent thinking approach at all levels of the schooling process could be the solution to that old age problem of whether we can teach creativity.
