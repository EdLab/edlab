Minecraft in Classrooms

Youssef Ballo

May 2, 2014

Seventh graders at Maple School in Illinois recently started learning about the Cold War. Educators at the school are using a simulator in an effort to bring the events, people and moments during the Cold War directly to students. Minecraft is one of these simulators and games educators used to build a model of a divided Germany to help students understand the topic. Minecraft is a video game that allows users to build almost anything they can imagine out of multicolored cubes. According to one of the students, Minecraft is a great visual tool for presenting people's knowledge and channel thoughts into understanding how something might have actually looked or happened. Many teachers use Minecraft in their classrooms because of its creative power to educate students in several academic disciplines such as from history to biology. This video game offers teachers a new type of learning experience and it seems students are enjoying and learning from it. TeacherGaming has sold MinecraftEdu version of the game to 2,500 schools in the world.
