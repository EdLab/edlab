Add a Design Method to your Madness: IDEO Method Cards

Kate Meersschaert

July 10, 2014

(Image via IDEO Site) In the spirit of Carmen & Chingfu's wonderful EdLab seminar and Yang's recent EdLab blog post, I thought I would introduce IDEO's Method Cards to the mix. Used by engineers, researchers and designers, this analogue deck of cards (and optional, corresponding app) are a great way to organize and push the creative, human-centered design process. Organized by four "suits" (Ask, Watch, Learn & Try), the cards are meant to help facilitate creative problem-solving and include real-world use cases of each "suit." Description from IDEO's site:

IDEO Method Cards is a collection of 51 cards representing diverse ways that design teams can understand the people they are designing for. They are used to make a number of different methods accessible to all members of a design team, to explain how and when the methods are best used, and to demonstrate how they have been applied to real design projects.
I have a set at home that I used when I was studying Urban Permaculture Design here in NYC, would anyone like me to bring the cards into the lab? If so, how might we experiment with them?
