I, Robot: Cafe Collection

kk2012

August 19, 2016

Hello EdLabbers & Librarians! Next month our cafe collection will include books about robots!  Inspired by the work of Dr. Sandra Okita, Jennifer has gotten us started with the 10 books below, but we would love your suggestions to round out the collection.  What books have you read or do you want to read that we are missing? (Leave 'em in the comments)

  1. I, Robot, by Isaac Asimov (1950)
  2. The Age of Spiritual Machines: When Computers Exceed Human Intelligence, by Ray Kurzweil (1998)
  3. Almost Human: Making Robots Think, by Lee Gutkind (2007)
  4. The New Cool: A Visionary Teacher, His First Robotics Ream, and the Ultimate Battle of Smart, by Neal Bascomb (2010)
  5. Boy + Bot, by Ame Dychman (2012)
  6. Robotics: A Very Short Introduction, by Alan Winfield (2012)
  7. Spare Parts: Four Undocumented Teenagers, One Ugly Robot, and the Battle for the American Dream, by Joshua Davis (2014)
  8. Inside Robotix: An Easy Way to Enrich Your Child's Education Using STEM and Robotics, by Monica Burch (2014)
  9. The Second Machine Age: Work, Progress, and Prosperity in a Time of Brilliant Technologies, by Erik Brynjolfsson (2014)
  10. Machines of Loving Grace: The Quest for Common Ground between Humans and Robots, by John Markoff (2015)
