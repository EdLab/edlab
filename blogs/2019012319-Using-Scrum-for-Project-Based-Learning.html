Using Scrum for Project-Based Learning

Rebecca.Sullivan

January 23, 2019


AP physics teacher Johnny Devine uses daily scrum meetings to keep his students accountable and on-task during group projects. Every morning, students meet and report to their group members what they have accomplished since their last meeting, what they will work on before their next meeting, and current worries they have about the project. Every group uses a poster to visualize their progress. Pending tasks are tracked in one column, and when students begin working on a task, they transfer a sticky note with the task and their name on it into the in-progress column. Devine considers scrum a process that both provides structure and encourages agency.
