Google Creates Sensors that Allow Hand-Controlled "Hands-Free" Interaction

Christina Terracino

June 9, 2015

As I was perusing technology and education related videos for possible Vialogue pitches, I came across this video of a motion sensor Google is developing. I wanted to share it here since I don't think it is quite under the category of Vialogues (or maybe it is?). Anyway, I think that this continues to add to public fears of privacy invasion. Radio waves can trap the motions of your hand, but where is this information going? Who has access to it? Also, I wonder why if you are using your fingers to push an imaginary button or raise volume on something, why not just press an old fashioned button or turn a volume knob? The conversation today regarding edtech and potential problems led me to consider whether or not this particular example of technology is a similarly problematic "breakthrough" that is really just designed to "Wow" and impress people.
