The SUV of the Microbot World

Melanie.Hering

May 10, 2018


Researchers at the University of Purdue have developed a new kind of all-terrain microbot that can tumble and climb uneven surfaces in both wet and dry environments. At 400 by 800 microns, mere millionths of a meter, these bots have promising potential for biomedical applications, such as targeted drug delivery.

These microbots are animated through magnetic fields and can be controlled by commercial MRI . They can be steered and rotated along a set trajectory after being injected into patients, tumbling to a specific goal location to help fight cancer and other serious diseases and conditions.
