How to Teach Adult Learners

chingfu

January 26, 2014


The advent of an information society and knowledge economy has transformed how we live, learn, and work in modern culture. Increasingly, fewer people envision a life where learning stops once they graduate; rather, most plan to be lifelong learners -- catching up with the latest technologies and knowledge in order to work effectively in a dynamic new world. As "learner" no longer describes solely young people but also refers to many adults, more attention needs to be paid to how to teach adults effectively.

The current industrial model of education remains deeply rooted in traditional ideas about teaching and learning, and instructor-centered professional development reminds many adults of their boring-yet-required school days. What are some pedagogies that would tap into more adult brain power and engage them in meaningful deep learning? How might problem-based and constructivist approaches be used for adult learning? This short, 3-minute clip shares a few simple principles and tips for teaching adults.
