Turning Parental Fears of Social Media into Learning Opportunities

Melanie.Hering

January 10, 2019


The fear that their children's lives could be "ruined forever" by poor decisions made on social media is one that plagues many parents today. Since digital literacy is a topic that is new both to adolescents and many parents, knowing how to navigate privacy, reliable sources, safety, and relationships online can be overwhelming, and can result in many parents depriving their kids of the valuable learning opportunities social media can provide.

Author and counselor Ana Homayoun aims to help parents understand what moves they might make to protect their children and open up opportunities for them to benefit from these platforms. Featured in The Atlantic’s series of videos, Home School, she explains how parents can teach their kids to be more intentional about how they spend time online to ensure their decisions are in line with their values.

What role do you think social media should play in students’ lives and education? How, practically speaking, can parents teach their kids to be empowered by "opting in and out of experiences" like this video suggests? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
