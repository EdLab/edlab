Teenage Brains Are Wired to Learn from Experience

Rebecca.Sullivan

April 22, 2019


Daphna Shohamy, a neuroscientist at Columbia University, has made an interesting discovery about the teenage brain. She designed an experiment in which she scanned the brains of adults and teenagers as they played a competitive digital game. One result was that the teenagers outperformed the adults in the game. This finding came as no surprise, since the part of the brain that seeks rewards is very active in teenagers. However, the teenagers were also better at remembering random images that were flashed on the screen and had no relation to the game. The brain scans suggest that this is because the part of the brain that seeks rewards is in direct communication with the part of the brain that stores memories. So, as the teens were acting on their desire for rewards, they were simultaneously learning content.
