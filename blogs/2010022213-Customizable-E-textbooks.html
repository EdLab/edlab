Customizable E-(text)books

Molly Riordan

February 22, 2010

An article in yesterday's New York Times introduced Macmillan's DynamicBooks software which allows professors to edit textbooks on a chapter, paragraph, and even sentence level. The article describes the publisher's impetus for the new software, citing the professorial tendency to skip textbook chapters, add supplemental material, or otherwise modify textbook usage as they structure their classes. This modifiable e-book, however, would allow professors to make all these changes in advance, giving their students the opportunity to buy a (cheaper!) electronic version already customized to the class. ZD Blogs has some screen captures of the program from the professor's and student's POV. This reminded me of the "Always New Teacher Handbook" we proposed in the Print Shop at last week's SECTtOR event. Turns out an easy way to modify textbooks--without the publisher's consent!--is already upon us. Though Macmillan is a massive publishing house, I wonder whether this model will eventually be feasible for the presses of TC or other smaller educational publishers?
