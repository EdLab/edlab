Planned Parenthood Hopes to Build Empathy With a Virtual Reality Film

saraehardman

June 3, 2018


Planned Parenthood is increasingly using emerging technology to reach out to younger generations. Among their tech projects, they created a virtual reality film documenting what it’s like to be a patient walking through the line of protesters roosted outside many Planned Parenthood clinics. The film, Across the Lines, was debuted at the 2016 Sundance Film Festival and has since been used in many different educational contexts, including with government officials.

Dawn Laguens, Executive Vice President and Chief Brand Officer of Planned Parenthood, says the goal of the VR film is to increase empathy for staff and patients regarding the struggle they face each day walking into the clinics. The film is set in three parts and uses a combination of real footage, CGI, and audio collected from protests. The first part takes place in a real examination room and depicts a conversation between a visibly shaken patient and her doctor. The second depicts a flashback to the patient driving up to the Planned Parenthood clinic and being approached by protestors. The third section transitions to CGI and places the viewer in the shoes of the patient, where they encounter attacks from protestors.

Across the Lines is meant to educate and build empathy at a precarious time for women’s health in America. Regardless of political affiliation, it hopes to build a more supportive environment around Planned Parenthood clinics, one in which women don’t have to feel traumatized just for walking through the door.
