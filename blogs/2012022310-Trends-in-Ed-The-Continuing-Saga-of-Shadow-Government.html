Trends in Ed: The Continuing Saga of Shadow Government

LauraCostello

February 23, 2012

What does it take to create the educational video game of the future? Props if you guessed a successful gaming startup, data and software from the Millennium Institute, Facebook, Swiss angel investors, and a million dollars. Shadow Government has been in the pipeline for over a year but with Playmatic's first political title set to drop later this month, the hype may be on the up. The startup was behind the much talked about Find the Future gaming experience put on by the New York Public Library last year and seems to have an ongoing commitment to creating games with tie-in or real life applications. Shadow Government will harness the modeling power of the Millennium Institute and real life economic data to allow users to create and govern their own countries. Much like American Public Media's Budget Hero, users will be able to see the direct consequences of their actions as their virtual countries thrive or fail. The focus will be on data interpretation, representation, and the creation of policy. Millennium's intention is to push Shadow Government as a curriculum enhancement tool for K-12 civics and social studies courses, but it doesn't appear that the game will be monetized for personal mobile or Facebook users. It'll be exciting to see what Shadow Government can do, especially poised as it is in a moment of increased acceptance for gaming in education.
