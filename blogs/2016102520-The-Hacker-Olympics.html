The Hacker Olympics

Jenny Abamu

October 25, 2016


Turning hacking into a spectator sport has finally happened, and with a multi million dollar prize pool, the stakes are high. DARPA, a U.S. government defense agency, led the charge by collaborating with gaming companies to display artificial intelligence battles on the big screen during their Cyber Grand Challenge. They even had broadcasters to comment on plays, keeping the audience engaged.

The event took place in August and pitted seven autonomous hacking bots against seven super computers. The graphics created by the gaming company, Void Alpha, displayed the battle in real time, and allowed the audience to visualize what was happening inside the computers the moment programs were hacked. Taking hacking out of the abstract promises to produce a heightened awareness of the digital realm. It also serves as a model for using computer programming design challenges as learning opportunities, and it creates some serious questions surrounding the ethics of hacking.
