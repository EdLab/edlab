Pioneer Works

EdLab Studios

March 5, 2015


David Sheinkopf and his brother spent the better part of their youth destroying their parents' record collectionâ€¦ for the sake of science. Fascinated by how sound works, they experimented with building speakers out of office supplies and testing them out on their parents' records. A few decades later, Sheinkopf, now the director of education at Pioneer Works Center for Arts and Innovation, teaches the history and physics of sound to curious New Yorkers using similar experiments. The class (its next session is coming up April 30) is a part of the Center's program of short and long-form classes that seek to expose people to process: the process behind visual thinking, for instance, or behind writing, physical storytelling, computation and wet-plate photography. The goal is to bridge the gap between artists and scientists and create a safe, inspiring place where they can ask questions, get their hands dirty and exchange knowledge across age and discipline.
