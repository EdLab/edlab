TapFame

Daniel Vergara

November 12, 2012

I've often found it interesting what new endeavors former EdLabbers are dabbling in once their time here has come to an end. Some drift off to new professions and careers while others continue on the path they started here at the Lab. Ankit Ranka, one of our alumni, has a startup company, TapFame, which allows for businesses to find freelance developers to start a project. It essentially allows for aspiring or existing businesses to find mobile app developers to create something new or enhance an existing product. It was recently named one of the top 30 startups in New York City. TapFame has an easy three-step process for all interested parties. The first step asks companies or entrepreneurs to talk about their project regardless of the stage in development. The second step uses the information provided in the first step to match them with a developer who has done similar work or is interested in doing that kind of work. Once a connection has been made, TapFame steps out of the way to allow the two parties to work on the project. However, TapFame helps manage the funds for the project per the agreement between developer and company. I think this app is extremely useful for aspiring entrepreneurs as it gives them access to a pool of developers who can help build or remake their brand. Often times, finding someone with the necessary experience when creating a business or app can be difficult for entrepreneurs and TapFame aims to eliminate this problem.
