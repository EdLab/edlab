Journey Through Space and Time with VR Hangar

Melanie.Hering

March 11, 2018


VR Hangar is a new, free app released by the Smithsonian's National Air and Space Museum. Using real 3D scan data, this app brings to life three of the museum's most important artifacts: the Apollo 11 command module Columbia, the Bell X-1 Glamorous Glennis, and the 1903 Wright Flyer.

Pros:

VR Hanger is more than just a 3D tour of different important artifacts; it combines video elements, exploratory learning, and simulation to expose users to the science and historical context behind the creation of each of these artifacts. Therefore, while the app provides great experiential learning to those who are unable to visit the Air and Space Museum, it can also enhance the learning experience of those who are actually able to go and visit in person.

The variety of activities available through this app are excellent. From touring the Wright brothers’ workshop to flying inside a Bell X-1, VR Hanger provides three different sets of experiences for each artifact. It’s also nice that the three artifacts are from different decades, offering a look into the evolution of air and space travel across the century.

Cons:

While some aspects of this app are very well developed, other aspects don’t feel like they’ve been considered at a high production level. The VR utilizing real images, such as the inside of the Bell X-1, was fantastic, but the surrounding animation wasn’t as high quality. Having 3D scan data supply more of these images would enhance the experience.

A great addition to this app could be an interactive element. As it is, although this app allows you to move around and explore different artifacts and the spaces they are housed in, it is rather passive. The potential of a gamified version of this app, with ability to control an airplane for example, could make it that much better.

Our Takeaway:

VR Hangar is an excellent way to guide yourself through the history of American air and space exploration. It is fun for both kids and adults, and can add to or recreate the museum experience simply through the magic of your phone and some cardboard VR glasses.
