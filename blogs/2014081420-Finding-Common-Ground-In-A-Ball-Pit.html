Finding Common Ground In A Ball Pit

Carmen James

August 14, 2014


We have been hearing a lot about how important empathy and human-centered approaches are. For example, empathy is central to Design Thinking (see the NLT piece on Susie Wise), but how do we develop empathy? How do we practice or engage in it?

If you are looking to learn more, check out SoulPancake's latest. Founded by Rainn Wilson in 2008, SoulPancake creates content and opportunities "that encourage people to explore what it means to be human." In this video, the SoulPancake team invites strangers to sit together in a ball pit. Each ball has an icebreaker question written on it to get the conversation started between the strangers. This project seeks to humanize urban spaces and teach strangers to engage rather than ignore each other.

Do you like this idea? Do you think everyone would be so nice to each other if they found themselves in a ball pit with a stranger? Share your thoughts in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
