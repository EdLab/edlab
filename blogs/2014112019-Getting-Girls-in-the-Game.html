Getting Girls in the Game

Nantwi

November 20, 2014


The need for our schools to provide students with a strong background in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) is one of the hottest current issues in education. The argument is that our students need a robust education in these areas to be able to compete in a global economy as adults. However, there is a large disparity in the percentages of boys and girls who pursue STEM fields in college and subsequently in professional life.

How can we close this gender gap? Girls Who Code is a national organization that hopes to play its part in increasing educational and professional opportunities in STEM for girls. Although 74% of girls express an interest in STEM subjects, only 0.3% choose computer science as a college major. Additionally, fewer than 12% of computer science graduates are women. Girls Who Code offers a host of programs for young girls in subjects such as web design and mobile development. With a major increase in computer science jobs anticipated in the coming years, Girls Who Code is working to ensure that girls have the interest and necessary skills to fill at least half of those positions.
