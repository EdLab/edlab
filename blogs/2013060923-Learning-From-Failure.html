Learning From Failure

abagigah92

June 9, 2013

It is coming to the end of another academic year, teachers, counselors, and principals have to deal with students who have not met requirements to move onto the next grade. This means decisions have to be made to help struggling students and often times, it involves dealing with angry parents and disappointed students. School leaders agree that summer school is an option to help students but they also agree that an opportunity is lost if data are not collected to determine the causes leading to student failures. School leaders must determine reasons why a student failed and the ways and means to avoid that from repeating. Problems such as issues in the home, bad teaching, student attendance and preparedness for class should all be taken into account. Student data is gathered on a case study basis in order to determine where, why and how students are failing. State officials have also made policies to help struggling students. In a study, it was discovered that grade retention in most cases do not help the students because the incident is likely to happen again. Therefore the best way forward is to plan programs, policies and counseling for struggling students. This way the incident will be highly unlikely to happen again.
