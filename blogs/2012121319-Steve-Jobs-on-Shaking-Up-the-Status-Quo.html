Steve Jobs on Shaking Up the Status Quo

Laura Scheiber

December 13, 2012


In a video interview from 1995, Steve Jobs explained that we are often told while growing up that life is the way it is and that we should do our best to live within its parameters. Contrary to this limited perspective, he believed that life could be far more fulfilling and exciting once realizing that everything in life was created by people. What this means is that we have the capacity to change things and make them better. In fact, Jobs advocated that people not just accept the status quo, but aim to shake things up, and improve upon them. His words seem apropos in relation to the education sector and learning landscape, which has much room for improvement and endless possibilities in light of advancements in technology.
