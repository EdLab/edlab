Should you "Pin it?"

Kate Meersschaert

March 15, 2012

I am sure by now, everyone has heard of Pinterest?! If not, it is a massively popular online, social pin-board. Users can post content (primarily images and videos focused on lifestyle-related subjects) to their board to share with the world. Simple, and apparently, simply addictive. I have yet to set-up an account... but according to this study this past January Pinterest sent more referral traffic to content publishers than the combined totals of YouTube, Google+ and LinkedIn. Twitter followed closely behind. As Pinterest is mainly used to collect images and video, perhaps we could consider adding a "Pin it" button Pin It along with our "Tweet" option on Vialogues? Could we add this to PocketKnowledge for "free use" images from the archive? Perhaps, if we eventually create proprietary infographics to support UFR and our other projects we could make these "pinnable?" What about Analytics? Apparently you can use Google Analytics to easily track stats and this article provides more easy tips for tracking your content on Pinterest. Including this handy url to easily discover if anyone is currently "pinning" anything from your site.
