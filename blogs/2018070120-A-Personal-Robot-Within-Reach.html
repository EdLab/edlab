A Personal Robot Within Reach

Melanie.Hering

July 1, 2018


"One amazing robot. Many amazing minds." (Misty Robotics)

It Takes a Village to Raise a Robot

Born out of the robotic toy and digital tech companySphero, Misty Robotics is a company that believes robot use shouldn’t be restricted to a small population of makers and programmers with bountiful time, resources, and experience. In order to make robots more accessible, Misty Robotics is creating the first advanced, multi-purpose, programmable robot that requires only basic programming knowledge to create thousands of skills. By working with a community of programmers to create the most advanced bots they can, Misty hopes that it will prevent users from spending months assembling and programming their own robot.

A Robot In Every Home (and Office)

Misty Robotics’ head of company, Tim Enwall, has a vision of the future in which personal robots are as prevalent in daily life as cars, computers, and smartphones. Through making this technology more affordable and accessible, the company has a 10-year plan to put "a robot in every home and office." With its versatility of functions, Misty robots can also be integrated into schools and other educational environments.

One way in which Misty Robotics hopes to achieve this goal is through selling their creations at a reasonable rate. While a fully-featured robot like Misty II would have cost tens of thousands of dollars just a couple of years ago, Misty Robotics has promised a 50% cut to the current price when the bot is released later this year. The ability to increase quality while decreasing price is key to solving the problem of accessibility, and doing so (along with increasing the ease of programming) might just allow Misty Robotics to make the future it envisions a reality.
