On the Front Lines With Social Anxiety

saraehardman

August 21, 2016


"We believe everyone deserves great mental health. This means access to timely, effective, and affordable solutions. Our mission is to make this dream a reality." (About Joyable)

The Problem

It’s a fact: anxiety disorders are the most common mental illness in the U.S. Although many of us have occasionally experienced anxiety before a social event, about 15 million American adults are living with social anxiety disorder, which may severely inhibit their social lives. Another fact: 36% of people who suffer from social anxiety disorder live with their symptoms for 10 years before seeking help.

The Solution

A new company called Joyable is changing this sad reality with its easily accessible online platform designed to help people overcome social anxiety. After new participants are screened via an online quiz, they are assigned a personal coach who will remain with them throughout the 12-week program. Joyable uses a tried-and-true anxiety therapy method called Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT); participants complete 10-minute online CBT activities throughout the program aimed at helping them understand, identify, and challenge their anxiety.

The Results

Joyable’s program seems to be as effective as it is easy to use. Almost all participants see a decrease in their social anxiety (the average decline is 39%) and the more online activities they complete, the better the results they see. After participants complete the program, they learn how to incorporate CBT into their daily lives and permanently lock in the benefits.
