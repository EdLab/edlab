"If You Like Your Tablet, You Can Keep Your Tablet"

Brian.Sweeting

February 13, 2014


At first glance, President Obama’s coordination of $750 million in corporately donated education technology resources to public schools seems like an impressive investment. However, considering the cost of additional resources necessary for successful tech integration (training programs, IT support, software applications, curriculum development, data collection policies), the predictable failures of massive iPad distribution programs (see: Los Angeles), and more pressing fundamental problems (pedagogy, infrastructure), one might question the value of dumping new tablets on schools.
