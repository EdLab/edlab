E-Voting Source Code Leaked

Jeannie Crowley

October 22, 2009

Sequoia, a company that produces electronic voting machines, accidentally leaked the source code that powers their voting machines. Sequoia has used legal threats in the past to keep anyone from auditing their machines. Since they leaked the information, though, there is not too much that can be done at this point. The initial investigation by the Electronic Defense Alliance, an election integrity watchdog group, indicates that the code violates the rules established by the Federal Election Committee. A public wiki has been established to encourage anyone with an interest in voting reform and/or technology to help the EDA review the information quickly. (Great use of crowdsourcing!) The spirit of the project is summarized by this quote on the homepage:
