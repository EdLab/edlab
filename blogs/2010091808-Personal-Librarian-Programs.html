Personal Librarian Programs

gnatriello

September 18, 2010

As we consider ways to develop the services program of the library, we might want to consider the personal librarian programs that assign a librarian to each student or each entering student at an institution. Among those institutions with such program are Drexel University and Yale University. These two programs focus on undergraduate students, but similar approaches have been initiated in programs for medical school students such as the program at Yale Med School, the program that inspired the Yale program for undergraduates, as well as programs for faculty such as the program at Emory University. So, what are we to make of the personal librarian strategy? An evaluation of the Yale Med School program in the journal Medical Reference Services Quarterly finds students reasonably satisfied and librarians seemingly very pleased with the program. An entry in the blog Closed Stacks indicates excitement among librarians at other institutions upon hearing about the Yale program. A piece in Fast Company sees the program as a response to the challenge posed by the internet and Google. Perhaps most revealing, an article in the Yale Daily News indicates relatively light use of the service by undergraduates.
