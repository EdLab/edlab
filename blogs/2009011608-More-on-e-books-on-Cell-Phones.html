More on e-books on Cell Phones

gnatriello

January 16, 2009

There seems to be growing activity to put e-books on cellphones. I have been thinking about the resistance to such ebooks when they are compared to traditional print books or even to dedicated devices such as the kindle or the Sony book reader. Such comparisons might not be appropriate if the real value of ebooks on cell phones is that they open up possibilities for reading that did not exist before. In this regard the Shortcovers approach is interesting because it suggests a new purpose for ebook material on cell phones -- a discovery service to introduce people to new things to read. To think about ebooks on cell phones from an innovation perspective I would suggest two questions: 1) What individuals who are not currently "readers" might become readers if more reading material appeared on cell phones? and 2) What new kinds of reading materials -- new in substance and/or format -- might be developed specifically for the cell phone distribution channel?
