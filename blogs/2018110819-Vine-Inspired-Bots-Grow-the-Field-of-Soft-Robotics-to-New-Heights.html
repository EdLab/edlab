Vine-Inspired Bots Grow the Field of Soft Robotics to New Heights

Melanie.Hering

November 8, 2018


Robots made in the image of animals is no stretch of the imagination, but plant-inspired robots, though less intuitive, might have just as much to offer. Vines, for instance, can weave through tight spaces and around structures. Interested in how these properties might be put to use in the field of robotics, mechanical engineers at Stanford University have been developing soft robots that look and function like vines. The difference is that these bots can grow much faster, thanks to an inflation machine positioned at their bases.

Made out of plastic and fabric, these flexible and shape-shifting vine robots extend outward as they are filled with air, and can explore hard-to-reach locations, configure themselves into 3D structures, and adapt to the space around them. Since these robots can create their own shapes and structures, the team hopes that one day they will be able to weave themselves into novel shapes based on the functions required.
