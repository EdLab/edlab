Genspace

EdLab Studios

January 11, 2014


At Genspace, a non-profit community laboratory in Brooklyn, the public is seen as an important stakeholder in the dialogue around 21st century science, particularly biotechnology. This requires education about and, more importantly, the ability to participate in contemporary scientific work. Genspace offers biotech classes for trained biology researchers as well as those with no prior lab experience - often all together. It also functions as a community lab, providing space, equipment, and resources for would-be scientists and entrepreneurs to work on their own projects in molecular and synthetic biology and other biotech fields. It is founded and run by "science enthusiasts" - artists, engineers, writers, and biologists - who believe their diversity begets innovation. Genspace models how lab-based science can be valuable to non-science professionals in many fields. It seeks to encourage scientific entrepreneurship and public education outside the often-limiting confines of large research and education institutions, and it aims to erode the traditional delimitations of scientific/non-scientific fields, projects, and people.
