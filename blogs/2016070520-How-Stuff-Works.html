How Stuff Works

saraehardman

July 5, 2016


If you’ve ever wondered why snowflakes are symmetrical or what an itch actually is, How Stuff Works might be the app for you. This creators of this app, as well as the website with the same name, acknowledge that there’s too much stuff to learn and too little time, so they’ve graciously condensed all the interesting bits into one mobile platform.

Pros:

For a curious, trivia-loving person like myself, How Stuff Works provides both incredible learning and entertainment opportunities. It incorporates several multimedia outlets, such as videos, articles, podcasts, and quizzes, to give variety to the mini-lessons. Users can explore the app via category, podcast show, or specific topic; it’s likely they’ll never run out of new information to discover. Even in my quest to come up with the most oddball topics to search (e.g., spontaneous combustion and sweetbreads), this app provided results to almost all of them (the only one that stumped it was "blobfish").

The article and quiz topics are quirky and fun, such as "What makes us crave comfort food?" and "How well do you know the countries of the world?" Additionally, if you’ve ever listened to one of the podcasts How Stuff Works produces (such as Stuff You Should Know and Stuff Your Mom Never Told You), you’ll be happy to know that every episode is accessible on the app.

Cons:

Users interested in a social media component may be disappointed, because this app has none. Although it’s certainly not the main point of the app, being able to comment on articles or compare quiz scores with other users would make the app more engaging and interactive. After all, learning information tends to be more fun when you can share the experience with others.

Our Takeaway:

How Stuff Works is no substitute for Google, but it is a great platform for anyone curious about how the world works. It asks the types of questions you’ve never thought about before, but once you hear them, you have to know the answer. For example, "Are there secret space programs?" Excuse me while I go check this one out.
