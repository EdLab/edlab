#foodFriday "Fifteen New Ways for Oysters"

Meredith Powers

February 13, 2015

Happy Friday! Today we're bringing you recipes from the great Mrs. S T Rorer, author of Colonial Recipes, Twenty Quick Soups, and many more! This week's selection comes from Fifteen New Ways for Oysters, published in Philadelphia, 1894. [gallery link="file" ids="2138,2137,2136"] Sure, you could go out for oysters—but in this weather, why not stay in and cook up twenty-five or fifty "good, fat oysters" any way you want them? Start with a sauté of oysters, or boil them. [caption id="attachment_2139" align="aligncenter" width="300"] "Sauted Oysters" / "Boiled Oysters"[/caption]   If you're feeling more ambitious, try a "Scallop of Oyster and Macaroni" or the "Bisque of Oyster." [gallery link="file" size="medium" ids="2140,2141,2145"] There's still eleven new ways to prepare oysters—including curried, creamed, and baked en coquille—so stay tuned for more of Mrs. Rorer's recipes! (Just make sure you sharpen your silver chopping knife!)
