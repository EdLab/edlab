Mobile Learning Thro' Blackboard - A Project Proposal

Stephen Asunka

May 11, 2010

Introduction and Context Blackboard is one Teachers College's LMSs, and has been in use since the summer term of 2002 as a platform for the delivery of fully online courses, and for supporting hybrid and face-to-face courses. Over the years however, Blackboard adoption and usage be faculty members and instructors has stagnated (after a steady increase during the first 3 years), and is therefore now only being used by the same group of persons semester after semester. In the meantime the college is making a lot of investment to keep this technology running and supported, calling into question the effectiveness of the current installment of Blackboard as a resource for promoting and enhancing teaching and learning at the College. As the EdLab has the responsibility of administering Blackboard, it behooves on us to ensure a much higher ROI by exploring innovative ways of not only increasing Bb adoption and usage among faculty, but also how the resource can be used optimally to promote learning in both the cognitive and affective dimensions. In this regard, and given the fact that Blackboard Inc. is introducing a Bb mobile platform, we are proposing a project that will work towards deploying Blackboard Mobile and also making TC's blackboard courses mobile compliant. Mobile learning (or learning through mobile electronic devices) promises ubiquitous, pervasive, personal, and connected learning as it can respond to on-demand learning interests of learners, connect formal with informal educational experiences and facilitate situated learning (e.g. receiving performance support while on the job). Mobile learning thus represents the next step in technology-mediated learning, and its success will require new strategies, practices, tools, and resources.
