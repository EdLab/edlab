Five Videos to Raise Your Anxiety Over Robots

Brian.Sweeting

December 17, 2017


For decades movies have featured fantastical stories of robot uprisings or other AI moral quandaries. Much of this science fiction is now real science. Check out these vialogues that might make you a little concerned about the future of our relationships with robots.

1. How Human is Too Human for Robots?
Robotic engineer Hiroshi Ishiguro believes the only way to bridge apprehensions in human-robot interactions is to create androids that are more human than human. He built his robot Erica to blur the lines between humans and machines, perhaps forgetting about the a Uncanny Valley qualm.

2. Soft Robots That Heal Themselves
The scary liquid metal robot of Terminator 2 will soon be more than just 90s CGI. Scientists have synthesized a material that can heal itself when it becomes damaged, and have already been testing it on artificial hands, claws, and muscles.

3. Pregnant Robots Give Birth to New Forms of Experiential Learning
Don’t worry, robots aren’t yet giving birth to their own robo-babies. Instead, hospitals are utilizing these replicants to simulate human pregnancy for nursing students.

4. The Unbreakable Bot
Researchers from MIT have created a robot that is indestructible. The little bot can already jump, bounce, and roll on a variety of terrains. Hopefully, a larger version doesn’t come chasing after us one day.

5. The People Who Tame Robots
In the future, when robots are bigger, faster, stronger, and smarter than us, what happens when they get out of line? Right now, we have robot tamers. These robotic engineers are making androids more responsive to human gestures and movements.
