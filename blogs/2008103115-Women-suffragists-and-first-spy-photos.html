Women suffragists and first spy photos?!

Jessica Pang

October 31, 2008

As I am doing online research for the "Iron Jawed Angels", a HBO movie about women suffragists in the early 20th century, I came across a very interesting article from BBC that I must share with everyone. (Maybe because election day is coming?) Do you know, women suffragists are once considered terrorists, biggest threat to the Brit Empire, and possibly the first subjects of government SPY photographs/ secret surveillance?! Instead of spying heads of other nations (the photo below was taken in 1913, WWI started in 1914, not to mention all the colonial countries that wanted Independence from the Empire), the UK govt chose suffragists. (Maybe because they were "easier" targets?) The government also mastered the art of "photoshop". Back in those days its called "doctored image". (First photo: the real thing. Second photo: how the photo appeared on the documents, scarf instead of the guard's arm) All of these photos were uncovered in 2003 by the UK National Archive, which brings me to the second point of this blog post -- thank god for technology. With all the new archiving technology (yea~ Pocket Knowledge) we have today, our pictures probably don't have to wait a century before they are "uncovered" again.
