Andrew Gelman's Excellent Points on Online Education

Xiang.Liu

April 9, 2015

I ran across Andrew Gelman's blog and saw this excellent article on online education. He pointed out watching video or powerpoint slides should not be substitute for reading books. In his article, he identified a critical difference between 1. Attending a lecture, reading a textbook, or watching a lecture video. 2. Attending a concert or listening to broadcast or recorded music. He said "people are doing 1 to learn, and they're doing 2 for enjoyment" and "lots and lots of people listen to music just to hear it, not to study it, while not so many people watch college classes just for fun". In addition to above points, he also talked about practice of lecturing in classroom. This is a great article. Andrew Gelman is a world-renowned statistician. He holds joint appointment from department of statistics and department of political science at Columbia University. I also noticed that he recently received a big multi-year research grant from Institute of Education Sciences. His team are researching problems in education that requires sophisticated Bayesian data analysis. Having him for a seminar would be very interesting!
