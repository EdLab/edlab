The Online Learning/Teaching Manifesto

Kate Meersschaert

February 24, 2012

What would a manifesto for online learning contain if it was written strictly from the learner's perspective? How would it differ from one written from an instructor-driven perspective? This manifesto was created by teachers and researchers in online education at the University of Edinburgh, MSc in E-learning 2011 and aims to do just that... Favorite lines: Visual and hypertextual representations allow arguments to emerge, rather than be stated. Distance is not a deficit, but a privileged perspective. Assessment is a creative crisis as much as it is a statement of knowledge. Listed below is my attempt at a learner's manifesto - feel free to add your own in the comments below! Lets generate ideas to use as inspiration for our mSchool course delivery platform! Learning 3.0 Manifesto... -Students are consumers of media/learning/knowledge and should be treated with the same respect and individual attention you would give a client or customer. -As a consumer of learning I demand to be offered 24hr access to my learning materials, platform and discussions related to the course. -The best "tools" and techniques for the job should be sourced and integrated into all materials, whether this is video-based lectures, adaptive learning technologies or advanced discussion boards. -Let the best content creators win... whether big-name, small boutique or an individual. The proof is in the quality of the material. -BYOD... I demand to be able to access my course materials on the device of my choosing, or one rented from an institution. -Grading and assessment should be both qualitative and quantitative. Given throughout course delivery with frequent opportunities for all parties to pivot and provide remedial help based on micro-tests, not simply 2-3 x's a semester after a major exam. -IQ and EQ will be united... through the quality of my engagement with others in online discussion boards and peer feedback. Self assessment will play a larger role in my experience as well... Is this too much to ask of online learning? Or learning in the classroom? Perhaps. But it never hurts to ask!
