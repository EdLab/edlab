TC R&W Project 8.13.2009: Teaching Points in Picture Books

Ting Yuan

August 14, 2009

I have been immersed in a big number of children's picture books these days, which I feel supper happy about. To become a children's book editor and writer is one of my career goals when I get back to China in the future in addition to being an academic. All the recommended picture books by different speakers are interesting in a special way. They offer various teaching points for classroom writing workshops. I especially want to share the following books: It is about a child who hates to eat peas and other vegetables.

I loved this book upon a first look. It is the first wordless English book I have ever known. It "tells" a story about a little girl walking, thinking, and feeling the waves at a beach. The book shows great closeness to nature, and presents a limpid and bright world, combined with reality and imagination, in the eyes of the little girl.
This book shows how babies learn to walk. Possible teaching points in picture books:
1. Punctuation is the craft (e.g., …) 2. A sentence can be written across pages 3. Use speech bubble to add conversation 4. Use text in boldface and exclamation mark to express strong feelings 5. Write words into different shapes (to match pictures) (e.g., a curvy shape) 6. Use repetitive format 7. Dialogue can be added by people not shown in the picture 8. Use pictures to show emotions 9. …
