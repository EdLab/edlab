The Right for Your DNA?

Francisco.Mendoza

June 9, 2013

Last week the Supreme Court ruled in a 5-to-4 decision that DNA samples can be taken from people arrested in connection with serious crimes. This may be a very valuable decision for detectives still investigating unsolved crimes. This is accepted by the federal government and 28 states. I'm not completely sure if I am in favor of this. Justice Scalia said "make no mistake about it: because of today's decision, your DNA can be taken and entered into a national database if you are ever arrested, rightly or wrongly, and for whatever reason", which doesn't seem right to me. If you are ever arrested wrongly and DNA is collected from you, then your DNA should be removed permanently. What are your thoughts about DNA samples being collected from you, on an arrest that can be right or wrong? This article sheds more light on this case.
