Trends in Ed 03.31.10: Seton Hill Gives iPads to Full-Timers

Jeannie Crowley

March 31, 2010

Trend: Educational Technology Use
Related Posts:: Laptops in Schools and Kindles for Students
Seton Hill recently announced it will give an iPad to all 2,100 full-time students. New students will also receive a new MacBook. The free tech products are part of the universities Griffin Technology Advantage to:

  • Prepare students for the 21st century workforce.
  • Help students move beyond information literacy to "creative literacy"-solving problems with the information online.
  • Encourage faculty to create engaging classroom environments through the use of technology.
  • Use technology to accommodate different learning styles.
    • The research isn't clear whether laptops for every student (at least at the high school level) lead to academic achievement (see related post). Although laptop programs started in universities 14 years ago, there is very little data available to support campus-wide laptop initiatives. Valley City State did conduct research as part of its National Council Accreditation for Teacher Education evaluation and found that laptops led to a marked increase in technological competence. . .and not much else.
