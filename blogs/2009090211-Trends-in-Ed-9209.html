Trends in Ed 9.2.09

Jeannie Crowley

September 2, 2009

Today it is common practice to save our ideas online (GoogleDocs, PBWorks, etc.). While most of us check the "do you agree to the Terms of Service" box without a second thought, it's a good idea to start investigating the ToS before we click. Some companies, such as PBWorks, have summarized their ToS very clearly so we don't have to read the whole painful document. According to the PBWorks site:

  • Your stuff is yours; we don't own it when you post it.
  • Your secrets are safe with us; we'll defend them as best as we can.
  • We don't police you, but if the police tell us to take your stuff down, we will.


    • Very few of us have the time to read every ToS. Luckily, there are people out there who have read every detail and posted blogs about the potential pitfalls of the ToS for different companies. We can benefit from their labor just by browsing their blog posts. This may seem paranoid now, but that idea you're working on now could be the next Facebook, Dell, or FedEx. Those companies all started as ideas in college and the creators would be kicking themselves if they gave away their multimillion dollar idea with the click of a button.
