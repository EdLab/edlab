Khan + Kno = Better eTextbooks

Kate Meersschaert

August 22, 2011

Digital textbook maker Kno announced today that they will integrate video content from Khan academy into their etextbooks. Kno, who has attempted & abandoned a previous inroad into revolutionizing the textbook, with initial hardware-based physical "notebooks," are now solely focused on digital content. Khan academy on the other-hand has remained at the forefront of the online learning revolution and will lend much credibility and attention to Kno's digital content. From today's Mashable article announcing the partnership... Kno worked with Kahn Academy to implement its tutorials for the feature's launch. Eventually, Kno Vice President of Marketing Ousama Haffar says, the feature will expand to include other educational images and videos. How will users experience this integration? A new "smart links" option will join Khan academy videos with corresponding Kno content and will be available through an additional tab navigation on a topic's page. For example, a student who wants to know more about photosynthesis can click on a tab that will navigate to a video of Khan discussing that topic. Additionally, Kno plans to add 3D objects & renderings that can be rotated in real-space and manipulated by the user as additional interactive content. EdLab relevance... This move is especially apropos to EdLab's New Learning Times initiative and overall interest in the future of both education and publishing. If South Korea's recent mandate to transition all students from paper-based to digital texts by 2015 is any indication, Kno & Khan are on the fringe of an impending movement & as we have seen with companies ranging from Apple to Zip Car the "fringe" as we know, often predicts the future.
