Trends in Ed, 6.2.09

jvazquez

June 2, 2009

History students a-Twitter in the classroom At the University of Texas at Dallas, history professor Monica Rankin has decided to bypass standard look-and-listen teaching practices and has instead embraced the technological classroom by using Twitter as a teaching tool. During class, students will tweet discussions about a given historical topic. They can choose to follow the discussion on their mobile devices, personal computers, or on a projected image of the live tweets, located in front of the class. While the results of this method have been mixed, Rankin is finding that it is bringing out discussions from even the shyest of students. She feels that this method is ideal for large, auditorium-style classrooms that sit too many students to shake a stick at. I like this idea, but I wonder if there's a better medium than Twitter? It's notorious for having a poor search service (hashtags or not) so it brings up questions as to how and whether students could retrieve their comments for later reference. Or is it implied that all tweets are fleeting thoughts?
