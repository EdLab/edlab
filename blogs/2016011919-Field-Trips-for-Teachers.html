Field Trips for Teachers

Jenny Shen

January 19, 2016


Teachers often spend days planning out the details for field trips their students will take, making sure that the experience is closely tied to the class curriculum. Isn’t it time for teachers to get the chance to go on field trips too?

That’s what National Geographic is doing—bringing teachers to faraway places like the Arctic Circle in order to give them first-hand experiences directly related to their course content. The teachers go whale watching, observe polar bears in their natural habitat, and take photos of the area’s flora and fauna. Giving teachers the opportunity to use all their senses to better understand the biome will allow them to teach their students something classroom textbooks simply can’t.

"It gives them a whole new understanding, and when they come back to their classrooms, they can translate that to their students," one National Geographic representative says.

In what ways can programs like National Geographic’s give teachers the tools to teach science and geography to their students? In what ways is this approach still lacking? Add your thoughts on Vialogues.
