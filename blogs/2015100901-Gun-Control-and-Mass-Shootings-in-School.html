Gun Control and Mass Shootings in School

abagigah92

October 9, 2015

Gun control is one of the current hit button issues in America. The recent mass shooting at Umpqua Community College in Roseburg, Oregon has brought gun control to the forefront of the national conscious again. The issue, similar to most other hot button ones, is split along partisan lines. Democrats argue that owning a gun is protected by the second amendment. However, there is a need to have regulations to check who can own guns by eliminating loopholes in the system. On the other hand, Republicans argue that regulations are good and the country needs to focus more on mental-impaired individuals and criminals. Some have suggested amending the constitution by revising the second amendment and banning guns in the Unites States. Countries such as Australia, Canada and England have stricter gun control laws. The approach has led to a tremendous decrease in gun related crimes in those nations. However, the United States recorded about 10,000 gun related murders in 2014 and a total number of 40,296 gun violence incidents in 2015 so far.
