Teaching Social Skills Through Robots

Melanie.Hering

August 30, 2018


"Working with experts in the autism community and school systems around the world, RoboKind has developed two products [...] to give children opportunities to realize their potential and pursue their passions for all things tech related" (RoboKind)

Inclusive Education Through the Use of Robots

RoboKind is a Dallas-based company that aims to create more diverse, inclusive education for students who have traditionally been disadvantaged in education systems. In order to do this, they have created two different programs that utilize humanoid robots in two unique ways. RoboKind’s programs are currently in use in multiple Texas partnership schools.

Robots4Autism

Non-verbal communication is important for creating solid, functional human relationships, and yet those with an Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) struggle to pick up and identify these cues. In order to help students with ASD gain the confidence and skills they need to succeed academically and socially, RoboKind created the Robots4Autism program. Robots4Autism utilizes Milo, a socially advanced robot who you can see in action in this Vialogue, and an accompanying curriculum. Milo delivers verbal lessons and conveys human-like expressions, providing ASD students ages 5-17 with a reliable, controlled, and non-threatening partner with whom to learn, practice, and repeat social behaviors and emotional identification. After becoming more confident, students can interact more comfortably with people in their environment.

Robots4STEM

Building upon the mission of creating inclusive education for all, RoboKind’s second project, Robots4STEM, aims to create access and opportunities for students to learn programming and computer science. Robots4STEM teaches visual programming through the use of a personalized robot, Jett, who, like Milo, has advanced facial expressions and movement abilities. With Jett, students can learn coding, computational and critical thinking, and collaboration in class, after-school programs, STEM camps, and labs.

In reaching those who might not otherwise have access to the learning opportunities their robots provide, Robots4STEM and Robots4Autism fill an important niche, one RoboKind hopes to continue growing.
