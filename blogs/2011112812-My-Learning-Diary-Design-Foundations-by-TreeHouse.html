My Learning Diary: Design Foundations by TreeHouse

Janet Jang

November 28, 2011

Treehouse is a fairly new website that concentrates on providing instructional videos on Web Design, Web Development and iOS Development. It charges for its full service, starting $25-$50/month, but I found that most of the HTML videos themselves can be viewed for free. The project is backed by well known investors, namely; Reid Hoffman, Kevin Rose, David Sze, Chamath Palihapitiya and Mark Suster. Part EdLab Review, Part Learning Diary, my new project will be to try out TreeHouse's web instruction for "Design Foundations", and watch all 117 videos on HTML, CSS, CSS3, Aesthetic & Technology Foundations, and Responsive Web Design. I've been really impressed with the videos that I have watched so far (about 20 videos). Very well-made and I feel like I already know more about design and coding. Ever since starting at EdLab, I've been learning fast about web design and development, but it made me feel like I needed more structured lessons in order to better contribute to EdLab. Honestly, I feel like $25 is not that much and still a worthy personal investment on my part to increase my own knowledge and use it to further the D&R work at EdLab. Also, it'll be a good chance for me to test how well video instruction works as the main method of learning/teaching as a whole! Wish me luck :)
