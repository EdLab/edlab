Data-driven Classrooms for Personalized Learning

csd2126

March 26, 2017


Educators at Summit Prep in Redwood, California use data to shape classroom instruction. From regular surveys, teachers are able to find out the best way to adapt lessons to meet students’ needs. The Summit approach views collecting data as important no matter how that information is gathered. Surveys can be as simple as a thumbs up/thumbs down or as tech-dependent as a Google form. While assessments are the differentiating factor at this school, the focus is on the results of these surveys to shape instruction.

In addition to real-time surveys, Summit uses an online platform to provide students with instructional content. Every student at Summit has a personalized learning plan they can access online to track their ongoing progress. The platform allows students to take quizzes, find readings, and receive feedback.

While surveys can help teachers understand students’ levels of understanding, how does this approach differ from traditional classroom structures? Share your ideas on how students can have a greater voice in shaping their instruction on Vialogues.
