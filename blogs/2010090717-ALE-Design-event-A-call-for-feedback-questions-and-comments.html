ALE Design event: A call for feedback, questions, and comments!

jvazquez

September 7, 2010

With the [first] big ALE design event on the 30th looming ever closer, we are nearing the completion of our carefully crafted day's specifics: The overall structure is set, the guest list is good to go, and the key EdLab players have been identified. What isn't as certain is the makeup of a few of the activities, notably the research stations and prototyping activities. __________________________________________ Quick background: Research stations- there are 4 stations that correspond to the 4 infrastructures. Two "Researchers" will lead a discussion based on their assigned infrastructure with groups of 4-5 participants for 20 minutes. After 20 minutes, the group will move clockwise to the next station. (Most of you should be familiar with this activity, as we did a run-through at our last seminar) Prototyping activity- here the participants will work within their groups to design the infrastructure to support adaptive learning. __________________________________________ So... do you have any ideas to enhance either of these activities? Whether you have been on the ALE design event team from the beginning or if this is as unfamiliar to you as a nudibranch, (go ahead, click it. I bet you anything it's not what you think it is) your input is welcome!
