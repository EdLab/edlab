New Personal Finance Course

AbdulMalik

April 8, 2015

Personal finance remains a problem for most college students across the country. This issue mainly ranges from how students should save their money to how students can pay back student loans. In a recent survey of 42,000 American college students, an educational technology company EverFi and other financial aid disbursement companies indicated that, teens are not likely to make very smart decisions with regards to their personal finances. This article sheds more light on an initiative by the University of Mississippi to address this issue. The school is offering a six-week personal finance course for student athletes. The course is geared towards helping students save and use their money judiciously. The curriculum will include banking basics and will require students to analyze real life financial situations. This is a step in the right direction because students will be able to acquire basic knowledge in finance and hopefully apply it to real life situations. Regardless of a student's major, this course will create an avenue for all students to be able to manage their finances once they start professional life.
