Trends in Ed: Blackboard’s Open Source Pivot

LauraCostello

April 5, 2012

In a series of announcements last week, Blackboard seemed to finally nod at their open source competition. Blackboard acquired two Moodle support companies and appointed Charles “Dr. Chuck” Severance of Sakai to head up their new open source services. The company also halted the cancellation of Blackboard Learn ANGEL Edition. The effect of these changes seems to be a move away from a sell/upsell model focused around the LMS itself and towards broad support and and supplementary services. Many in the edtech world have been critical of Blackboard's shifts, accusing the stately LMS of acting too late and appearing “unfocused (and a bit scared).” It's unclear whether the growing influence of open systems like Open Class and the MOOC movement have had anything to do with Blackboard's change of face, though it seems likely. Certainly there will be a place for the subscription LMS in the future of online education, but it seems wise to diversify.
