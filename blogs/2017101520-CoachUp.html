CoachUp

Ryan.Allen

October 15, 2017


"Our mission is to help athletes reach the next level in sports + life." ( CoachUp)

Coaching Community

CoachUp is a Boston-based private coaching community founded by Jordan Fliegel in 2011. The network has amassed over 12,000 coaches from around the United States who offer one-on-one, small group training, or virtual training in over 30 sports or activities. The communityâ€™s most popular sports are soccer, baseball, lacrosse, and basketball, but it also includes activities like yoga and dance. Users just need to enter their basic requirements and location, choose a coach from the match list, and then schedule a training session. Prices vary with each private coach and the training style of the session. All coaches can be rated and reviewed by the users.

Maximizing Success

CoachUp does not let just anyone become a coach on the network. In order to join the service, prospective coaches must send in an application on coaching and athletic experience, complete a general coaching and specific sport quiz, and pass a background check to ensure user safety. Some of the athletes that I found in the New York City area had collegiate or even professional experience. You can get shooting lessons from a former NBA player at around $100 per session.

The network flexes the prowess of its community through CoachUp Nation, a website connected to the network. It offers articles with tips and advice written by various coaches of different sports from around the country, such as 3 Benefits of Playing Multiple Youth Sports and Becoming a Parent-Coach Team. This resource is an added benefit to an engaging community of private coaches, and helps to make their expertise more widely available to average users.
