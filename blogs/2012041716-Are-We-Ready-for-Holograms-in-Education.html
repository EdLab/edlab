Are We Ready for Holograms in Education?

Kate Meersschaert

April 17, 2012

Smoke and mirrors... Regardless of whether you consider Tupac's recent "live" performance at the Coachella Festival to be a hologram or a "Pepper's Ghost"... this development in virtual performance is bound to trickle-down into other industries in the near future. Although the actual Tupac video is extremely NSFW, the Vialogue embedded below explains the simple science behind what appears to be a complex technology. Could this technology have a future in the education space? The University of Phoenix thought so when they partnered with MIT to create the hologram-based lecture also embedded below. Could the "Pepper's Ghost" technique which apparently delighted jaded festival-goers and is slated to "bring-to-life" even more performers in the near future, beam university professors directly into student's homes? Historical figures into classrooms? Instead of Tupac, imagine watching Abraham Lincoln deliver the Gettysburg Address "live." Could this also be a possible evolution of online learning? Could personal, cost-effective projection systems be made? Perhaps using Pico projectors?
