sySTEMic Change Mathematics Edition: More Analytics Please!

Demetri Lales

December 22, 2011

Starting fall of 2012 Northwest University will have 30 students walk down a new path through the newly built road of the Master of Science in Analytics program. This is an interesting move made by Northwestern University and IBM that tries to solve the problem of the lack of analytics. Analytics, or the science of analysis, is the application of computer technology, operational research, and statistics to solve problems. The program will have a classroom size of 30 students studying full-time, and on-campus. This is the second on-campus analytics program, the first being the Master of Science in Analytics program in North Carolina State which takes 40 students. Northwestern University will introduce an online Master of Science in Predictive Analytics, a program completely online. There will most likely be more programs introduced in other universities, that will teach students the discipline of analyzing data. Potential for sySTEMic Change: I believe that this growing trend of Master of Science programs on analytics will have a big impact not only in the fields of STEM but for other fields as well, like business for example. We will have much more data we will need to interpret and find the value of in all fields. So we need more people who can take data from a business, school, library, etc. and find a way to use the data to improve different aspects of the business, school, library, etc. According to NC State there wasl be 1.2 zettabytes (ZB) of digital data produced in 2010 and could grow up to 35 ZB of data in 2020! An increase in data that big would require more people who can decipher and create value of it, so hopefully more universities can start adding an analytics degree to their rosters so more people can make sense out of all this data. Let's analyze more data everyone!
