Apple and Its Competitors

Daniel Vergara

October 14, 2012

There seems to be a never-ending legal saga between Apple and its competitors, mainly Samsung, over patent and design rights. Most of the argument stems from Apple's argument that Samsung copied some its design for products such as the iPad. A judge awarded over a billion dollars in damages to Apple after they successfully sued Samsung for stealing their designs. In an effort to stop such future instances, Apple recently secured patents for some of their newer innovations such as the Siri, which made the iPhone 4S a must have item in the market. I found the information in this article very interesting as it talks about a few of the things already aforementioned about the lawsuits and fights between Apple and its competitors, this time with Google regarding the iPhone and Android phones. The thing I found most fascinating is that both Apple and Google spent more money securing inventions patent than they would invested researching for new products. I'm sure this argument is not slowing down anytime soon and choosing between an iPhone and Android will become harder for consumers.
