Cory Booker to Help Launch Video/News Site for Teens

Kate Meersschaert

March 13, 2013

Overview... News has been bubbling-up recently re: Cory Booker's newly launched Video/News aggregation site, #waywire. #waywire is billed as a content aggregator... so it will be interesting to see how they compete with existing players like the Huffington Post and the wealth of "content farms" that already flood the market. What seems to be most intriguing to reporters is both the possibility that an already busy, urban mayor might have time to help launch a company and that the "Doogie Howser" of startups has been picked to sit on the board. What is most compelling to me, however, is the fact that this social site was developed by one of the most prolific "Tweeters" on the American political scene and that the site is targeted at teens. Both seem to go hand-in-hand and will hopefully help connect the youth of the world with news and content that is relevant, timely and inspiring. Time will tell. And a few (thousand) tweets whether or not this model gains traction. #MTV3.0? Relevance to our work at EdLab It will be interesting to explore the "share" and interaction points that the platform has built-into their site.
