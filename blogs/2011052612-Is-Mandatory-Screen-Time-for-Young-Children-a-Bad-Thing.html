Is Mandatory Screen Time for Young Children a Bad Thing?

Kate Meersschaert

May 26, 2011

The National Association for the Education of Young Children (NAEYC) and The Fred Rogers Center for Early Learning and Children's Media (Fred Rogers Center) plan to revise their position from a 1996 NAEYC statement, Technology and Young Childrenâ€“Ages 3 to 8. From the NAEYC site , "The purpose of this position statement is to provide a framework to guide practice in the selection, use, integration, and evaluation of technology tools and screen media in early childhood settings serving children birth through age eight." The new position will be presented in Fall of 2011. The group Campaign for a Commercial-Free Childhood raises a number of concerns about this proposal including that it will undercut existent public health efforts to stem the tide of childhood obesity in our country. This is especially interesting in-light of recent e-book stats cited in today's New York Times article that point-out a lag in children's e-book sales despite gains in other genres such as fiction and mysteries. I wonder if this lag has anything to do with parental concerns over the effect of screen-based technology on children?
