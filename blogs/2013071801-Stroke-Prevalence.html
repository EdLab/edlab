Stroke Prevalence

abagigah92

July 18, 2013

In a recent news article I read, socioeconomic status affects the prevalence and incidence of stroke among African Americans. A study showed that eight or more years of education among African Americans had an influence on their stroke prevalence and incidence. Those with eight or more years of education had a lower incidence and prevalence of stroke compared to those with less. From a CDC data collected between 2006-2010, it was evident that individuals with less than a high school diploma had the highest prevalence of stroke followed by those with high school diploma and then some form of college experience. Those with a college degree or higher had the least stroke prevalence from 2006-2010. The CDC data collected from 2006 to 2010 also showed a decrease in stroke prevalence in the United States. Overall, there was a decline in prevalence of stroke from 2.7% to 2.6% from 2006 to 2010. Based on data collected from 1990 to 2000, mortality rate for stroke increased by 10% and according to the National Institute of Health, stroke mortality in the United States will increase by 98% by 2032.
