Shaq’s Unbeatable Opponent?

Jeff Frank

July 18, 2007

Though determined not to pay a cable bill, we pick up ABC relatively clearly thanks to the old-style antenna that ascends fifteen feet above the peak of our roof. Thanks to this antiquated technology, I watched my first episode of Shaq's Big Challenge last night. A short synopsis: Shaq attempts to help a group of obese children lose weight. In the process, Shaq realizes that public schools have done a lot to exacerbate childhood obesity. This realization leads Shaq to the realization: “I must reform schools.” So, an interesting subplot emerges. Shaq the reformer finds that it is harder than he could have imagined getting schools to change. Though this struggle of Shaq vs. the system is certainly setup by the editors of this show to hook viewers, it may prove telling. If someone with the stature of Shaq (not to mention his magical powers) can't nudge this system in a new direction, what can?
