No Cellphones, More Learning

rendy

May 22, 2015

The technology versus school debate has often come to a head when it involves student use of cellphones in the classroom. Proponents argue that students are already comfortable using cellphones and represent an opportunity to adapt the curriculum to meet this. Opponents argue that it is very distracting and students will use for non-educational purposes (e.g. Facebook, Snapchat, etc.,). Researchers in London recently found that was a 6.4% improvement in schools that banned cellphones. The researchers, when comparing grades before and after phones were allowed back in schools, found that students had better test scores when phones were banned. NYC recently pulled back its cellphone ban in schools after complaints from parents who worried they wont be able to reach their children during an emergency.
