Get Your Body Movin’

csd2126

March 6, 2017


Neuroscientist Wendy Suzuki summarizes what we know about the impact of exercise on the brain. Evidence has shown that exercise has many positive benefits on its functioning, but what type of exercise is the best? Dr. Suzuki explains that researchers know most about aerobic exercise and explains that regular exercise (3-4 times a week) is an average amount of exercise to strive for to help your brain. However, it really depends on where an individual is starting from.

Early morning exercise can give our brains a boost first thing in the morning. Morning exercise increases neurotransmitters and growth factors at a time right before you need to use your brain for learning, remembering, and creative thinking. Dr. Suzuki recommends planning your exercise around your daily cognitive functions.
