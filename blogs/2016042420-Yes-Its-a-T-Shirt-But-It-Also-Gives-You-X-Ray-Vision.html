Yes It's a T-Shirt, But It Also Gives You X-Ray Vision

saraehardman

April 24, 2016


As a child, you might have wondered what it would be like to have x-ray vision. With advances in virtual reality technology, what was once an unattainable superpower looks more like a reality. This vialogue introduces the Virtuali-tee, a device that incorporates augmented reality to grant you vision past the skin and into the internal organs and skeletal system of anyone you know.

By connecting the Virtuali-tee shirt to your smartphone, the app brings human anatomy to life. Point your device at a person wearing the shirt and you’ll see all the bones and organs that comprise their chest and abdomen. Watch as the heart beats and the lungs respire or, for those seeking more adventure, dive even deeper into the body to float through the veins alongside red blood cells.

The creators of this technology believe that it could transform learning for children. It’s certainly easy to imagine how the human body could come to life as kids spy into the internal systems of their friends and classmates. If a glimpse of x-ray vision doesn’t make someone excited to learn anatomy, it’s hard to imagine what would.

Do you think this type of technology could make kids excited to learn about anatomy? What types of applications do you think it has in the science classroom? Join our discussion on Vialogues.
