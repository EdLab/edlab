Design Thinking in schools

Frederik G. Pferdt

May 22, 2009

At the Stanford School of Education, Shelley Goldman presented an overview of the "Bringing Design Thinking to Schools" project, a collaboration with the Hasso Plattner Institute of Design (d.school). Two problematic aspects concerning the introduction of Design Thinking into classroom are very interesting: First, the fact that teachers saw the value of the design thinking approach but questioned the place in the classroom culture and secondly, to combine design thinking projects with content standards. I am looking forward to their findings and solutions in the future concerning these problems. In my opinion, at least one problem is strongly linked to the school testing standards (see: EdLab Blog). Anyone interested in Design Thinking for K-12 education should also check out the new website of the d.school K-12 Lab. Also be prepared for next week's EdLab seminar about Designing Educational Innovations (DesignEd Lab) Seminar EdLab).
