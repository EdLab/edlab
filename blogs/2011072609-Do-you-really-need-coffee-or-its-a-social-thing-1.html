"Do you really need coffee or it's a social thing" -1

Angela Lee

July 26, 2011

Quoting Hui Soo's question to Megha, George and Ankit during the Sloan Conference: "Do you guys really need coffee in the morning or is it a social thing?" I'd like to start a mini blog series. The series could be about any casual constructive conversations we have on our way for coffee and pastries. I'd like to present the grand opening series ---------------------------------------------------------------- Megha and Angela's conversation on "Pursuing a master's degree." Angela: " I am thinking about getting my Master's in the near future, but I'm not sure what I'd like to major in" Megha: " I suggest you figure out what your passion is...don't just get a master degree for the sake of getting one - or it's useless." ----------------------------------------------------------------- Of course the above conversation is shortened, but it sparked an interesting thought- How many people are simply getting their Masters because they believe a Master's degree will ensure employment more so than those with a four year degree. I will use Louis Vuitton as an example as it is one of the most high end brands in the industry. It is famous for it's sturdy leather, beautiful pattern and of course - a skillful PR campaign. It seems that we all crave for a one of a kind and unique product such as Louis Vuitton, which is slowly becoming an "entry level" bag for the rich and famous. As the Louis Vuitton bag is seen almost everywhere on the street- Hermes Berkin's series started to sneak on to some of the most fashionable figures at a price ranging from $6000 to $50,000. I wonder if or when the exclusive Hermes Berkins bag will become the next Louis Vuitton. And finally quoting the article: "Will the Ph.D. become the new master's?"
