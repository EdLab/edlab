Luddites and Learners... Will Students ever really miss the Printed Book?

Kate Meersschaert

May 9, 2012

Image source: New York Public Library Visual Materials Nostalgia vs nerve damage... Inspired by this recent Slate.com blog post, I ask the world... will students ever really miss printed books? Unlike life-long-learners and luddites of every persuasion, text-based learning has typically involved lugging-around large tomes of knowledge. There are even cautionary reports from relatively reputable sources on the dangers to kid's spinal health from carrying-around these book-based loads. Despite economic and technological barriers can anyone make a claim against replacing paper with pixels (or E ink)? Collectors, badging and students... I know people who collect and display books in their homes or who want to show-off what they are reading on the subway through book-cover-based "badging," but, in addition to asking if we should replace text with tablets, the real question I want answered is, will any student really mourn the loss of the "luggite" (new word to describe the potential positive experience of lugging-around paper-based texts) experience?
