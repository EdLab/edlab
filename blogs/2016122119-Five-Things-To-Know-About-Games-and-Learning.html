Five Things To Know About Games and Learning

Brian.Sweeting

December 21, 2016


Everyone likes to talk about how games are useful learning tools, but when exactly are they beneficial and how so? This year we reviewed a lot of research on why games are more than just fun. Here are a few of our favorite findings:

1. It's Time to Put Educational Games on Pause
This study examined the relationship between a popular education gaming site and academic success; unfortunately personal interest in gaming isn't a great predictor of good grades.

2. Game On In Special Education Classrooms
Researchers take a closer look at how math mini-games can improve special education studentsâ€™ multiplicative reasoning ability, which is closely tied to their ability to multiply and divide..

3. Using Video Games to Express Emotions
These authors completed a study to understand why some adolescents are more prone to video game addiction, exploring the connection between video gaming and emotional regulation, intensity, and expression.

4. When Kids Create Computer Games, Their Minds Start to Spin
This study examines how the process of designing computer games helps kids develop more sequential thinking processes.

5. Disadvantages in the Virtual World Classroom Might Not Be So Simple
A new study looks at whether non-gamers and women are at a disadvantage in the virtual world classroom.
