A Tangible Platform For Learning

Carmen James

July 23, 2014


Students in many parts of the world do not have the basic necessities they need to learn. They sit on the floor, hunched over their work, developing bad posture. Tools like desks, and resources like books and backpacks, are essential for student success. New Mumbai-based not-for-profit organization Aarambh has designed the Help Desk, a desk that folds up into a backpack and is made from 100% recycled cardboard.

While Help Desk was designed to solve a problem in India—the desk/backpacks were distributed throughout rural areas of Maharashtra, a state in the western region of India—the problem is not specific to India. The desks cost less than 20 cents each and fold up to become a backpack the students can use to carry books between home and school. The low cost, easily replicable design, practicality, and usefulness of Help Desk make it prime for expansion to other countries in similar need.
