EMINENT domain and the future of edlab

Hugo Ortega Lopez

February 19, 2006

The recent "growth" of the seminar space The consideration of flexible spaces to adapt to current and future needs is not only solve by putting everything on wheels. Although there is always a momentary need for expansion to cater to events the physical motions have to be done in a ripple effect to maintain a flowing surface. Simply expanding one point makes it seem more like a geological shift that permanently changes the topography of a landscape. By this I mean assesing how enlarging a space affects other spaces, entrances and specially traffic paths. By the circular wall taking over the main hallway, instead of a continent we are creating two islands, whose thin connection makes it hard for the water guy, the package guy, and more than one person to use the bridge between the lands. Let me be clear that there is no need for a meeting or official communication about the expansion. I see it as the responsability of the "others", immediate neighbors, or affected ones to relocate and keep the flow of the space. I also understand that most are not aware or consicous of this things which brings me to the future of 4th and 5th floors. Will we be able to handles and use to its full potiential a flexible space? Expanding from the inside without any consideration to the outside, just remind me of the controversial issues surrounding eminent domain for the development of communities.
