Inspiring Students Through Design Thinking

Nantwi

June 8, 2014


At a time when there is a great emphasis on Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM), the opportunities for students to express their creative sides are limited. At People Serving People (PSP), a homeless shelter for teens and their families in Minneapolis, everyone is a designer.

Several years ago, PSP established a D3 Lab to give the teens a chance to bring out their creative talents. The mantra for D3, "dream it, design it, do it", is built on the premise that design is not a complicated process. This video highlights the efforts by the students at PSP to redesign the lobby at the shelter.

According to Daniel Gumnit, CEO and Director of PSP, the students have applied what they learned at the D3 Lab to help their families make informed decisions, such as choosing an apartment.

Are you a designer? Can design thinking be taught? What if every K-12 school adopted design as part of their curriculum? Just imagine the possibilities. Join the creative discussion on Vialogues.
