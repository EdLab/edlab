Photos: Of Pictionary and Hackathons

Kevin M. Gong

June 20, 2011





Some were NOT happy with the Pictionary results... But we did get free pizza! Nothing? Aww...
In more serious matters, Ankit, Ariel and I attended the Donors Choose hackathon on Saturday, hosted by the General Assembly. (Ariel and I pretending to be programmers. Ankit is actually programming.) Aviary, Foursquare, and several other companies presented their APIs to the crowd, and Donors Choose then challenged attendees to create projects that would enhance the organization through data analysis, javascript projects, or a variety of other categories. (more photos after the jump) Since we didn't know how to code, Ariel and I explored General Assembly's rather nice space: The groups that ended up presenting showcased Foursquare/Facebook mashups, donor visualizations, and other social-oriented projects. The hackathon is still ongoing if you are interested in participating: http://www.donorschoose.org/hacking-education More photos here.
