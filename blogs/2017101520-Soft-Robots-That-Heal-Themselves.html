Soft Robots That Heal Themselves

Ryan.Allen

October 15, 2017


Soft material design is a growing innovation trend in robotics. These types of robots offer unprecedented adaptability, but they are often quite delicate. A simple puncture to the soft casing can easily render these machines unusable. The solution to this vulnerability comes straight from Terminator 2: self-healing robots.

Researchers have created a synthetic material called Elastomer. When Elastomer is damaged, heat can be applied and the material can repair itself. Within a day of healing, only a tiny scar remains.

Elastomer has already been tested on robotic hands, claws, and artificial muscles. When built from this material, the devices were all able to fully function once healed.

This innovation could make soft robots much more ubiquitous. What are some other uses for these machines (aside from time traveling back to 1995 to kill John Connor)? Join the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
