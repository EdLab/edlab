Video Games Can Impact Students

Youssef Ballo

April 11, 2014

Some parents think video games are not helpful because of the effect it has on their children. However, the use of video games for education can have unique and positive effects. It's utilized as teaching tools that can change lives and teach excellent skills. Video games teach critical thinking, problem solving skills, and perseverance. The National STEM Video Game Challenge is an annual event for middle and high school students to submit their own video game designs and compete with other children their age in a national contest. Designing video games help children learn coding, strategy, and digital creation. The The Mind Research Institutes ST video game is an educational math game that teaches students to conceptualize math in brand new ways. This allows children to have visual representation of math problems in motion and helps them figure out solutions. Children may be born into the care of screens, but if they use it the right way, those screens can create major improvement in areas such as reading, science, math, and other subjects.
