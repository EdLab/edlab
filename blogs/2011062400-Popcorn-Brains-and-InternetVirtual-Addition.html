Popcorn Brains and Internet/Virtual Addition

HSC

June 24, 2011

Check out this piece from CNN titled, "Does life online give you 'popcorn brain'?" Some highlights: The worry is that life online is giving us what researcher, David Levy, calls "popcorn brain" -- a brain so accustomed to the constant stimulation of electronic multitasking that we're unfit for life offline, where things pop at a much slower pace... Clifford Nass, a social psychologist at Stanford, says studies show multitasking on the Internet can make you forget how to read human emotions. When he showed online multitaskers pictures of faces, they had a hard time identifying the emotions they were showing. "Human interaction is a learned skill, and they don't get to practice it enough," he says. Other studies are showing there are serious side effects of internet/virtual addition. For example: Microstructure Abnormalities in Adolescents with Internet Addiction Disorder. If you are worried about internet abuse, download the Virtual Addiction test (and turn off your computer).
