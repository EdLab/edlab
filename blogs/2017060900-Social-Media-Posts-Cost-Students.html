Social Media Posts Cost Students

Derrick.Cordova

June 9, 2017

I have been told by several teachers and mentors about the importance of maintaining a “clean” social media presence. Like most people, social media is a place for me to share my life with friends and family (especially those in my native Honduras). However, like most things in life, some take it to an extreme. Some individuals post certain statuses, pictures, and videos that are downright offensive and insensitive to others. This lack of awareness has cost 10 students their Harvard admissions.

 

The prestigious university has rescinded acceptance offers to at least 10 incoming freshmen after administrators found the students were sharing explicit memes via a Facebook group. The group was an offshoot of a wider Class of 2021 Facebook group. However, the broader 2021 Facebook group inform members that it is “not responsible for any unofficial groups, chats, or content within” and "Harvard College reserves the right to withdraw an offer of admission under various conditions."

 

Is Harvard right in its decision to rescind the offers? How could this serve as a teachable moment for students?
