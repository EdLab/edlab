Education Advocates

abagigah92

July 22, 2013

A nonprofit group called Mocha Moms is in support of empowering black parents to take an active role in their children's education and development. The program has over 100 chapters across the country which primarily focuses on African American mothers. The group was spurred to act after a U.S. Department of Education report released last year found that black students had less access to more challenging courses in schools and that black children were much more likely to be suspended and expelled. This situation needed support not only from teachers, guidance counselors but parents who have the most influence on their children. The efforts made by these Mocha Moms, called Occupy Schools, begins with a pledge to spend more time at their children's schools, communicate regularly with their teachers and stay on top of their children's education and performances. Moms can also be involved in school board meetings and are allowed to volunteer and help out local schools.
