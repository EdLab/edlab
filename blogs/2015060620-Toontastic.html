Toontastic

jacob.albert4

June 6, 2015


Launchpad Toys’ Toontastic iPad app is an animated storytelling tool designed for children between six and eight. This free and simple create-your-own-cartoon platform combines ready-made characters, settings, and special effects with photography, paint, and voice recording features all within a basic lesson on how to craft animated stories with narrative arc. Toontastic’s optional audio guide directs young storytellers from conflict to climax to resolution along a customizable storyboard. The guide explains each story element’s function, provides encouragement, and pauses between completed scenes to allow little directors to identify and capture a tale’s tone with music.

Each Toontastic session culminates in a feature presentation of a semi-polished, multi-scene production complete with a simulated theater and rolling credits. This product includes a Parent and Teacher Guide for Facilitating Creative Learning with the features it offers. Toontastic is on the iTunes Hall of Fame, a New York Times list of Top Ten Apps, and sits among Khan Academy and SesameStreet.org as a 2013 Common Sense Media ON for Learning Award Winner.

Pros:

Toontastic’s simple interface makes it very easy to use. Large, obvious buttons accompany step-by-step verbal instructions that guide children past the practical steps of using the app and into making more complex decisions on a story’s setting, character, and plot. Voice recording, drawing, and photography features allow children to personalize stories without losing the professional quality of standard animations or the feeling of participating in a large production.

Cons:

The act of building cartoons with characters that move simultaneously depends on users manipulating several fingers at once, which for some children could be a challenge overcome only with unintended consequences to the quality of their animations. Additionally, several iTunes customer reviews for Toontastic lament the disappearance of a video sharing component present in an earlier version of the product.

Our Takeaway:

The unique way Toontastic offers goofy characters and silly scenes to accentuate a simple, vibrant, and easily navigable environment suggest to us that young people who use this interactive media tool could learn the art of storytelling perhaps without realizing it. The product’s Parent and Teacher Guide highlights the potential for cross-generational collaboration that lives within this app.
