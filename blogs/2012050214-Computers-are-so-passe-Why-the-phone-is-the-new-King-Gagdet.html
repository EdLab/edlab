Computers are so passe'-- Why the phone is the new King Gagdet

jvazquez

May 2, 2012

Is the computer of the future... a phone? Mobile phones are increasingly replacing PCs and laptops as the most used device in our lives, according to a recent Google study. In the same study, we learn that smartphone adoption rates continue to grow as people abandon their feature-poor feature phones for the ease of accessing the Internet on heartier mobile phone devices. Also growing are the percentages of smartphone users who are women and people who are above the age of 45. But the impact of the mobile phone goes beyond prevalence. It appears as though the phone itself is becoming a computing hub, turning traditionally accessed devices, like computers and TVs, into nothing more than output displays-- “Nomadic Computing 2.0”, as explained by John SanGiovanni, is changing the way users experience technology. Consider games, like Real Racing 2 and Chopper 2, that allow you to use your iPhone as a controller while the game is displayed on the larger screen of your PC, laptop, or Apple TV. The phone is controlling the game and can render two different UIs: one for the controller and one for the output display. Then there are apps like Clik (covered by Laura in a previous EdLab Review) and HippoRemote Pro 2.0 that let you control your PC or laptop with your mobile device. As more people adopt mobile technology, they are discovering new ways to harness the power of the small, portable, and feature-rich smartphone. Looking into the future, Nomadic Computers just might overtake PCs and laptops as the preferred computational device; but, for now, the growing prevalence and adoption rates of mobile technology points to the willingness of users to embrace a changing paradigm of information delivery. Once upon a time, the phone was a uni-tasker. Now, it is fast becoming the most compelling piece of technology available.
