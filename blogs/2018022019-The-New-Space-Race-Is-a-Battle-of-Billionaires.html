The New Space Race Is a Battle of Billionaires

Ryan.Allen

February 20, 2018


The old Space Race pitted the United States against the Soviet Union in a battle rooted in military supremacy. The new Space Race is guided by profit, as billionaires like Elon Musk and Jeff Bezos have taken the lead from NASA and are competing for the lucrative captain’s chair in the exploration of the final frontier.

Bezos, the world’s richest person, plans to begin space tourism operations with his Blue Origin company in the next two years. This trip won’t come cheap, costing around $150,000 to $250,000. With more permanent aspirations, Musk’s SpaceX is focusing on efficiency and affordability, with ambitions of celestial colonization in less than a decade.

By 2024, Musk has plans for manned Mars missions to begin construction on a permanent settlement. He envisions a Martian city with a population of over a million people in the next 50 to 100 years!
