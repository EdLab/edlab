EdLab Review: GlogsterEDU

Janet Jang

November 1, 2011

Glogster and GlogsterEDU have been around for awhile, but I heard that they've recently made some changes to their tools that I thought were worthy of a fresh review. GlogsterEDU is a tool to create online multimedia "posters" that can incorporate all types of elements into a visual space: links, images, text, videos, music, and more. Here's one that I made in 25 minutes, with no prior knowledge of the tools. I like how the video plays right on the poster. There are social networking aspects of it too, where students can express themselves creatively online and interact with their peers as well as their teachers. Pros: I remember making a "glog" for the first time a couple years back, and there has been a lot of improvements since then. There was a much bigger graphics library and just the technical aspects of making the poster was a lot more user-friendly than before. Even a teacher/student who is just learning to use edtech tools would be able to figure this tool out easily. Cons: Not all school districts or homes have servers which can handle the load from Glogster; server crashes or a slow network could make students and teachers very frustrated. EdLab Relevance: Multimedia tools have always been used by educators and are increasingly growing in popularity. Vialogues is our beat in that rhythm and I think there's a lot of room for growth there as well. Our Takeaway: As a future teacher, I'd definitely use this tool for lessons and lectures. I am a little anxious about how students (let's say, middle schoolers) could use or... abuse the social-networking aspect of this tool and how this can affect classroom management.
