Obama Staff Finds White House in Technological Dark Ages

Stephen Asunka

January 23, 2009

"If the Obama campaign represented a sleek, new iPhone kind of future, the first day of the Obama administration looked more like the rotary-dial past" begins this Article in the Washington Post to emphasize the fact that the White House was still stuck in the static Web 1.0 era. And I used to think educators are the only ones who are slow in embracing Web 2.0 technologies I guess now that even the Pope has launched a YouTube Channel and Encyclopaedia Britannica, the authoritative reference book first published in 1768, is to allow readers edit its entries (though denying that they are trying to be a wiki), no one has an excuse to remain in the "dark ages."
