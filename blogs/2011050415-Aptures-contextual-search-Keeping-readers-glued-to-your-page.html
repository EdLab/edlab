Apture's contextual search- Keeping readers glued to your page

Megha Agarwala

May 4, 2011

I came across Apture's Contextual Search while browsing through The Economist. Apture gives readers the power to search and explore rich content and media from the web without even leaving the page. According to the makers of Apture, "publishers who use Apture benefit from increased traffic and reader engagement. When visitors use Apture, they spend 2-4x more time on the page and view 1.5-2x more pages per visit." Just highlight text within the page and Apture searches the text for you on Wikipedia, Google, Youtube, Twitter feeds and others. Wait. The awesomeness does not end here. Apture is free! I feel that this can be a great add-on to Pressible and our other publishing initiatives. Also, it is a sweet example of putting to work the understanding of user behavior on the web, which in this case could be that a lot of people highlight text while reading and also refer to external sites like Google search during their reading. I realized, I do this most of the time.
