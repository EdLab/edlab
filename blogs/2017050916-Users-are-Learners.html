Users are Learners

jvazquez

May 9, 2017

I know. It’s not the most groundbreaking revelation and it almost felt insulting to use as my headline, but the fact remains that it’s easy to remember and makes a damn fine ethos: Users. Are. Learners.


The onboarding process is perhaps the most crucial experience a user will have with an app. First impressions are everything and if an app leaves them feeling anything but satisfied, chances are they won’t be returning (unless they have to, but they’re not gonna have a good time). That’s why the process of educating through design is paramount to a successful experience. One thing that Sabarish and I strive for in creating experiences for our users is to not only make something intuitive, but easily learnable. We’re sensitive to the fact not everyone will understand everything from the get-go, but we aim to provide helpful and subtle prompts that will allow them to learn in their own time. For example, Sab is aces when it comes to picking the perfect, readable icon and we like to provide hint text whenever possible. 


To better understand how to design for learners, I started digging around in the research and unearthed a gem of an article on exactly that; that is, designing digital learning spaces for adults. 


The article recommends six learning principles that can be used as best practices as we design learning spaces:


  1. Learners need to be involved in the planning and evaluation of their instruction.
  2. Learners prefer self-direction when learning new things.
  3. Learners build on prior experiences.
  4. Learners need to learn quickly—in real-life situations.
  5. Learners’ interest in learning is centered on problem-solving.
  6. Learners’ motivations to learn are internal as well as external.


Victor Yocco goes on to explain how UX designers can take advantage of each principle in his article, Incorporating Six Principles of Andragogy into UX Design
