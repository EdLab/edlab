More Sleep, Better Academic Performance?

Evans.Asamoah

April 24, 2017



Are students really better when school starts later? The answer is an affirmative yes from the American Academy of Sleep Medicine. In a new position statement, the researchers argue that students will be able perform at a higher and faster rate in school when they get 8-10 hours of sleep per night. This recommendation comes as research shows almost 70% of high school students report seven hours or less of sleep every night. Lack of sleeping at least 8-10 hours a night can have long term health effects such as obesity, athletic injuries and depression, among others. Since most schools start around 8am and teens typically feel tired around 11:30pm every night, a later school time was recommended as one of the best ways to ensure students are receiving adequate sleep, which in turn leads to better academic performance.
