Trends in Ed, 12.17.09

jvazquez

December 17, 2009

Rundown of the countdowns The end of the year always compels people to make predictions about the coming year. Big thinkers, the practically-minded… they all have their ideas of what will happen. With that, I give you 3 of the 2010 trend lists that I found particularly intriguing... 5 K-12 Technology Trends for 2010 from THE Journal 1. eBooks Will Continue to Proliferate 2. Netbook Functionality Will Grow 3. More Teachers Will Use Interactive Whiteboards 4. Personal Devices Will Infiltrate the Classroom 5. Technology Will Enable Tailored Curricula Wow, this is heavy on the tech side! What happens, though, to the schools who can't keep up with any/all of these trends? And the divide widens… though, I do agree with the increased use of Netbooks. 5 Higher Ed Tech Trends To Watch in 2010 from Campus Technology 1. More Interactive Classrooms 2. More Information at Your Fingertips 3. Mashed-Up Technologies 4. Breaking Out of Technology Isolation 5. Capabilities That Go Beyond 1:1 Again with the techiness but I guess when I get my list from Campus Technology, it can only be expected. Like the list above, interactivity and manual manipulation of information seem to be popular ideas. These ideas all seem relegated to the classroom, though. Where's the mention of the future of distance and eLearning? 10 Ways Social Media Will Change In 2010 from Read Write Web 1. Social Media Will Become a Single, Cohesive Experience Embedded In Our Activities and Technologies 2. Social Media Innovation Will No Longer Be Limited By Technology 3. Mobile Will Take Center Stage 4. Expect an Intense Battle As People and Companies Look To Own Their Own Content 5. Enterprises Will Shape the Next Generation of What We've Called "Social Media" 6. ROI Will Be Measured -- and It Will Matter 7. Real, Cool and Very Bizarre Online-Offline Integration 8. Many "Old" Skills Will Be Needed Again 9. Women Will Rule Social Media The biggest trend I saw mentioned was the integration of social media into our online practices; that is, it will cease to be a mere sidenote to what we do and instead become [even more] enmeshed within our activity. People are connecting and sharing information without a second thought (speaking of sidenotes, check out memcatch.com for an interesting way to share information!). And here's a bonus: The 10 Commandments of Social Media Here's to the trends of 2010!
