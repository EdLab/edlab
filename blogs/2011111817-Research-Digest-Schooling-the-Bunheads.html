Research Digest: Schooling the Bunheads

LauraCostello

November 18, 2011

Pegrum, M. & Kiel, R. (2011). “Changing the way we talk”: Developing librarians' competence in emerging technologies through a structured program. College & Research Libraries, 72(6), 583-589. Description: “Changing the Way We Talk” describes an online continuing education program for librarians piloted by the University of Western Australia. The course was based on passive teaching module aimed at educating librarians about current educational technologies. The module was doing a meta-poor job, itself an example of what not to do with e-learning. It was reborn as Emergent Technologies in Education, a blended course consisting of thematic face-to-face sessions, guided exploration of tools, and online augmentation. The 10-week intensive program culminated in the development of a course wiki with class-developed resources and links. The course evaluation was developed through Kirkpatrick and Kirkpatrick's Evaluating Training Programs model, a four-pronged approach measuring participants responses and actual impact on work behaviors and organization structure. The results showed that participants felt better enabled to communicate with colleagues about emerging technologies and approach current research. The group saw increased discourse around e-learning and increased use of these technologies amongst office groups. EdLab Relevance: Though we might not need a technology primer, I think the way the authors approached this study is extremely interesting. Bridging the generational gap in librarianship is a big issue and not just a personal one. Techphobia in library leadership is a significant barrier to change and innovation in institutions. I think an active, intensive course and conversation is pretty revolutionary for many libraries.
