Teachers Learn in a Virtual School

Mable Yiu

July 26, 2013


Virtual schools have become increasingly popular in recent years as educators aspire to transform education on a global level. In an attempt to achieve this ambitious goal, a number of educators have moved their instructional prowess online. One popular online outlet is the Florida Virtual School (FVS). In addition to its vast catalog of courses for students, FVS also offers extended professional development programs that combine both digital and face-to-face approaches. In attending weekly meetings with other principals, instructors, and administrators, participants learn how to improve their teaching techniques by sharing best practices and working together to solve shared issues. At these meetings, they make it a point to not follow a specific agenda laid out by the administration, but instead hold open discussions on what they find most important.

Due to these interactive in-person meetings, in combination with frequent digital messaging, teachers end up more engaged with their jobs, working even on days they are not teaching. As a result, they build strong relationships with each other as well as their students.
