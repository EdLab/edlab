Bill Reducing Students Loans

Oumar Soumahoro

May 13, 2013

College students now average about $27,000 in student loans after four years in college with a interest rate set at 3.4%. Six months after graduation, students with no job therefore encounter extreme difficulties in paying their debt. Through this hardship, the interest on college loans is projected to double this summer if no action is taken by the US Senate. This article sheds light on a bill introduced by Senator Elizabeth Warren (D-MA), which will temporarily reduce loans to under 1%. I personally think this bill will prove very beneficial to college students in the long term as it provides some kind of relief, especially for those graduates who have yet to find a job. What do you think of this bill? Does it go someway in alleviating some of the debt problems facing many college students?
