Four Ways Your Cellphone Stresses You Out

Duncan Asiedu

August 3, 2012

Ever thought your cellphone could be a source of unnecessary stress? Turns out it is. Findings from research conducted by the Pew Research Center's Internet & American Life Project states four reasons why your cell phone causes you stress. Dropped calls unsurprisingly comes in first on the list. Cell phone carrier AT&T is statistically noted to be worst offender of this with almost 3% of all calls being dropped every month. A dropped call can lead to even greater stress if it occurs during an important conversation such as an interview. Another way your cell phone becomes a source of stress comes from having slow Internet browsing speed. Browsing the Internet with a slow connection indeed can be a frustrating experience especially given the fact that we're so used to browsing Internet at fast speeds on a normal basis. Rural areas traditional tend to be hotbeds for slow Internet speeds but nevertheless can still occur even in areas with normally excellent coverage. The final two ways your cell phone can stress you is from spam calls and spam text messages. Overall, these stress factors go to show that even with the convenient benefits that a cell phone (especially smartphones) provides, it still has the potential of becoming a stressor in your life.
