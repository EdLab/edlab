sySTEMic Change Science Edition: Interactive Texbooks

Demetri Lales

December 19, 2011

Welcome to the first edition of sySTEMic Change! This column will feature news on advances in the ways of teaching STEM subjects. Each day will focus on a specific subject in STEM: Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. Today we heard an announcement about a new online science textbook called the Principles of Biology from Nature Publishing Group. Principles of biology will be an interactive online textbook book tailor made for college level students. The book will consist of 200 customizable modules that will cover chemistry, genetics, cell biology, animal physiology, and ecology, and each module will have textual instruction, figures, simulations, interactive exercises, self-tests and formal tests. The ability to customized the textbook is a great feature for professors. The interactive book will be priced for $49 per student which, if you ask me, is a nice deal. This book is a third of the price I used to pay for science textbooks! Potential for sySTEMic Change: This Interactive science textbook sounds like it will be a great way to keep students engaged while learning about science. With your average printed textbook, most students would need to read the material several times to memorize the material, it is tough and time consuming to read hundreds of pages of text and store all the information in your head. I believe that if students are not only reading text but viewing video simulations of scientific principles and doing interactive activities they absorb the information easier and have the skills to actually apply them. I think this should be the next step in the evolution for all textbooks and great way to change science education and making it even much interesting to all students!
