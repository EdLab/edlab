New High School Course: How to Deal with Cops

AbdulMalik

November 24, 2014

Majority of students (myself included) have come in contact with law enforcement officers one way or another such as the infamous stop and frisk policy of the NYPD. I am sure most students understand the duties of law enforcement agents and how to properly engage with them. This article sheds more light on an initiative by a high school in New York that teaches its students on how to deal with cops. The program is geared towards educating students about their rights and responsibilities when a cop confronts them. Criminal justice professors and cops teach the course. Students are given booklets that contain summaries of what is discussed. Undoubtedly, this is a great program as students will benefit from the program and will definitely educate their peers from different schools on the subject matter. The program is not necessarily teaching students to like cops. It is giving information to students about their rights and responsibilities when they are interacting with the police.
