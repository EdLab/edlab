Internet Access and Democracy

ABARRY

October 23, 2015

The worldwide web is really an extraordinarily and powerful tool. It has altered the way we do business, how we participate in the democratic and political process, and even the way we change our leaders. There is a lot of controversy about whether internet access creates democracy. Kosta Grammatis, the founder of the internet advocacy group A Human Right, believes it does. He believes that the internet is a place where people can express themselves as individuals, without being judged. This free exchange of ideas creates a democracy. Some of our leaders make it seem like they're implementing democracy. However, they are sometimes the primary reason why their countries lack equality.
