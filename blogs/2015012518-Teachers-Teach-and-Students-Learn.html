Teachers Teach and Students Learn

Bismark Appiah

January 25, 2015

A student's ability to perform well, for the most part, depends on the teacher. In any institution, it's the responsibility of teachers to help students learn. A teacher's performance is reflected in students' learning. However, teachers are not always to be blame when students are performing poorly. For instance in Pakistan, a normal classroom consists of 70 students and two teachers. Every teacher will struggle to get the best performance out of his or her students in this kind of condition. Thus, quality is a major contributing factor for poor teacher performance. Ironically, 90% of Pakistan education budget goes toward paying teacher salaries, yet student-learning outcomes are mixed. I believe the education system in Pakistan can improve if teachers are provided with pre-service and in-service training that has up to date teaching methods. Additionally, classroom sizes should also be reduced to a reasonable number.
