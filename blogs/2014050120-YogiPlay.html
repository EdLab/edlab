YogiPlay

Fred Rossoff

May 1, 2014


YogiPlay is an educational app recommendation and management tool that allows parents to find and monitor appropriate and expert-vetted educational apps for their children.

Parents can choose from customized, age-appropriate lists of educational apps, screened by educational experts and modified to follow their child’s progress. Regular information on the child’s use (or non-use) of the app is provided, including playing time and customized reports. The app includes a motivational tool in the form of YogiPlay points; by using YogiPlay approved apps, users can earn points redeemable for virtual pets.

Pros:

Although the educational opportunities provided by always-available smartphones seem limitless, we often use them to play games like Temple Run and Candy Crush. There is a role to be played by software that helps us manage and motivate our learning. By helping parents find the proper tools, and ensuring that they are being used, YogiPlay could theoretically be useful in this regard.

Cons:

In practice, however, the tool is more questionable. The recommendations provide little more information than could be found in a few minutes research, and the progress reports provided to parents seem more likely to produce intergenerational tension than to encourage real learning.

Our Takeaway:

YogiPlay’s usefulness depends on the parent and the child. If they have already agreed to cooperate in making the best use of smartphone time, this app may help them more easily overcome administrative hurdles. If, on the other hand, it is imposed unwillingly upon a Temple Run addict, it is unlikely to have a positive impact.

The Bottom Line:

In the right hands, this service may help us protect our children from the idleness that is the root of all mischief.
