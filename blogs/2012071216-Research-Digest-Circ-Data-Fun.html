Research Digest: Circ Data Fun

LauraCostello

July 12, 2012

Zweibel, S. & Lane, Z. B. Probing the effects of policy changes by evaluating circulation activity data at Columbia University Libraries. The Serials Librarian, 63(1), 17-27. This one comes from our friends across the street! As part of a mass digitization effort, librarians at CU explored 7 years of circulation data to determine methods for assessing policy and procedure changes. The paper began with an acknowledgement of the survey-based history of library assessment and the growing need for data-driven decision making in increasingly interdisciplinary and transparent organizations. The findings were none too shocking: physical item circulation has been trending down, renewals decreased when the lending period was changed to a full semester in 2008, and changing borrower classifications had no effect on library usage, but the use of this kind of assessment in libraries is going to change the way we do business. EdLab Relevance In the conclusion, the authors mention that they also investigated data about patron movement between libraries and suggest that this could be used to provide personalized service and inform collection development at specialized branches. As users come to expect a personal, cumulative experience, analyzing this kind of data might become essential to library service. On a relevant note unrelated to the publication, author Stephen Zweibel created an awesome-looking homebrew RFID system using parts from our friends at XBee.
