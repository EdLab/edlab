Summer Bust

Kevin Camacho

November 18, 2016

Summer, a time where the endless outdoor hours propel students to lose weight right? A recent national study of kindergarteners found that they are more likely to gain weight during the summer than during the school year. This must come as bad news to an education system with its recent regulations such as soda bans and increased emphasis on physical and outdoor activities. This summer weight gain mirrors the so called summer slide when students regress in math and reading. Though the major reason for this increase in weight is still being examined, experts have noted several contributing factors. The school year provides students with a structure that is missing during the summer months. As a result, students spend idler time (where they snack) and receive less sleep. Already some pediatrics are talking to parents about eating healthy habits during the summer months. Schools and community organizations also have a part to play in ending the summer weight gain.
