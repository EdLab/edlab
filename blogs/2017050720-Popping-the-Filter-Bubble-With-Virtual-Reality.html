Popping the Filter Bubble With Virtual Reality

saraehardman

May 7, 2017


Imagine reading a news article about Syria while completely surrounded by images and videos of the destruction taking place in the country. This is possible with a virtual reality headset and a new app called Little Maxima. Although the news would remain the same whether you’re reading it on your desktop computer or in VR, readers’ emotional reactions might differ between the two. In fact, many people believe that VR has significant potential to build empathy in its users.

A graduate student at Syracuse University created the app with the hope that it would combat fake news and help pop the filter bubble. Little Maxima helps achieve this by presenting readers with many different articles concerning the same stories and by letting readers fly around the globe and choose stories published across different countries.

Why is it important to combat fake news and the filter bubble? Do you think this can be better achieved in VR than on other platforms? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
