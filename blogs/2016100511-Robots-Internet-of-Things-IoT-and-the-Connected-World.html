Robots, Internet of Things (IoT), and the Connected World

chingfu

October 5, 2016

Earlier this spring we had an EdLab Seminar on robots and their potential to be the portal for the Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem. Zenbo, one of the latest family robots that will be soon available on the market, presents several interesting use cases. Zenbo reminds me of Jibo, a social robot designed by MIT professor Cynthia Breazeal. From appearance, the major differences between Zenbo and Jibo are that the former can move and has a more competitive price tag. One key capacity to making both robots work successfully is speech recognition. For this, Amazon's IoT product Echo probably has a better advantage as it has been in development for years. The recent price drop on the 2nd generation Echo Dot is an interesting development in IoT. It seems that Amazon’s strategy is to dominate the portal of home IoT ecosystem. If augmented with Amazon’s Kindle tablets, one can find alternative solutions to many tasks that are performed by Zenbo or Jibo.
