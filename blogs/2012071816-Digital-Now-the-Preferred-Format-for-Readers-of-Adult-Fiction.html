Digital Now the Preferred Format for Readers of Adult Fiction

Greg Schrank

July 18, 2012

The internet has been abuzz today over a report by the Association of American Publishers and Book Industry Study Group that, among other things, states adult fiction is being primarily consumed in digital formats, signalling a breakthrough for E-Books. Still, E-Book sales only make up about 15% of total publishing revenues, meaning we won't see books go the way of the dinosaurs anytime soon. But sales data shows trade book sales have stagnated and brick and mortar bookstore sales are down 12.6% from last year (though they still contribute 32% to publisher's pockets). Combine this data with recent news about eBook sales doubling in the last year and the use of tablets exploding, and we can expect to see continued growth for the E-Book sector in 2013. Pundits will (and have) offered their take on this report, but I don't think there's much more to be said than what's in the numbers -- we've seen how technology has changed other industries -- now publishing is the latest sector up for grabs. Consumers will dictate the market, and corporations will either adapt or fail. Anyway, it's another milestone for E-Books, and it will be interesting to see what the market looks like a year (or even 6 months) from now.
