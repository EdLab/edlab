Texas Students Stand in Solidarity with Occupy Wall St.

Kate Meersschaert

October 14, 2011

There has been a lot of recent discourse regarding the nature of the Occupy Wall Street movement and protestors & as the photo above points-out some of these assumptions might not always be true. The protestors have been called, "Lazy, trust-fund living, un-american, unexperienced, white kids" (the list goes on and can be found exhaustively here). Regardless of what you think of their intentions or back-grounds, Occupy Wall Streeters intend to stay, and do just that, occupy spaces traditionally dominated by the country's wealthiest "1%". How do Students in Texas Fit-into the Movement? The students at Texas State University, San Marcos are making their own stand in solidarity with the Occupy movement, but focused on Educational reform. Specifically, as laid-out in today's Inside Higher Ed piece: When the time comes to carry out their plans — which address such pervasive student issues as loan repayment and the poor job market — they plan to actually do most of the legwork themselves. They want to attend city council meetings and lobby for bills in the Legislature. They're going to work with local businesses to establish more scholarships for students. And they plan to get more students registered to vote and active in local and state politics, so they can have a say in where the money goes. Do you feel that student issues with higher ed institutions and the government's role in educational reform & regulation is a good fit for the Occupy Wall St. movement? Why or why not? What similarities are there between corruption on Wall St. and universities?
