Wicked, Wicked Wiki

Frank Webster

March 15, 2007

Ouch! Wikipedia takes a heavy hit in this article: "Read Me First: Oh, what a tangled web we weave when first we practise to deceive" "Read Me First: Oh, what a tangled web we weave when first we practise to deceive" Seth Finkelstein Thursday March 8, 2007 The Guardian "Wikipedia's latest scandal is the revelation that a very high-ranking administrator, and employee (until this month) of an associated commercial venture, Wikia, had falsified his academic credentials...." But the author does not blame the 24 year old perpetrator/victim who passed himself off as a tenured professor of religion at a private university. He was obviously sucked into the cult-like Wiki culture: "Frequently, what is naively viewed as spontaneous generation is in fact the product of a relatively small number of people who have been induced to provide a huge amount of unpaid labour. The lifeblood of Wikipedia is selling heavy contributors a dream that their donated effort will give them the prestige of an academic. This is very clear in the Wikipedian's credo of "writing an encyclopedia". But all that'll happen is they will work for free, while elsewhere the Wikia investors will reap the rewards. But it's a powerful dream. And 'Essjay' - Ryan Jordan - was that dream's poster child."
