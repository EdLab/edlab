Re-cap of Last-night's Columbia Engineering Entrepreneurship Event

Kate Meersschaert

December 7, 2012

Tweets about "#ceeNYC" Last-night's Columbia University Engineering Entrepreneurship night was a packed event featuring both startup "titans" and budding student entrepreneurs. Here is a run-down of my event highlights: -@bhorowitz: "If you dont know Bill Campbell you are screwed" -The company landscape is changing so quickly... move away from physically located co's like Borders etc. -Software playing an even bigger role and allows the est. of co's like Air BnB - Rap Genius!!! -Surprising takeaways from @bhorowitz for young entrepreneurs: Managing your own psych is the most important thing. You will potentially have to both ask the person you most admire in your life to work for you & on the "dark-side" Everyone who believed in the Co might possibly loose their job... if you can stop yourself from freaking out you can make it. -Bill Campbell share fun, funny and inspiring quips & stories from a wide range of Silicon Valley luminaries... mostly known - but surprisingly negative re: working with Vinod Khosla, who he described as "very difficult." -It was funny to hear @bhorowitz quote rap lyrics throughout the night... but cool and "admirable" as he really is walking the walk of his new venture, Rap Genius. (see this BusinessWeek Article) -Speaking of Rap Genius, see the tweet below linking the annotation platform to Vialogues. (See my EdLab blog post) -Finally, the student entrepreneurs were behind "booths" in the space and felt like "science fair" participants in the set-up. However, the substance of their ventures was impressive. From a device to help stop hemmoraging during childbirth and "light engines" to "data-driven character development" for K-12, the budding entrepreneurs were certainly impressive. A note to the organizers... not allowing photography or filming is one thing, but no open WiFi is completely ridiculous in the age of networked communication and esp. sad at a "tech-focused" event. On a positive note: the sign-in process was seamless and it was great to have a beautiful pre-printed name badge to keep after the event. Here are a few "contraband" pics: We almost attended about three different holiday parties by mistake! Social media is encouraged, but not supported with WiFi... I weakly attempt "peace"! Bill Campbell & hair... it was PACKED! a bit of entrepreneurial humor... Edtech represented! (they are very alpha with no site yet... but describe their platform as "data-driven character development") Me, Manav & Radiator Labs...
