Eli Lilly Chief Invests in Children

rendy

January 23, 2014

Eli Lilly and other companies are trying to take steps to help the U.S. catch up in STEM. During a recent meeting, John Lechleiter, CEO of Lilly, noted ”many people would be surprised if they saw the high-tech nature of manufacturing in our industry and in other industries today”. According to this piece, Lilly used to hire people straight out of high school to work on their production team, but now, in some places like Indianapolis, they require at least two years of college education. The Lilly Foundation donated about $1.5 million to create an “inquiry-based, hands-on science curriculum” in kindergarten through eighth grade and believes the key to success is inspiring children to become interested at early age.
