Typo-maniacs

Joachim Castellano

May 17, 2007

Recently, Gonzalo has been breaking down fonts and typefaces to the MDC group in weekly mini-seminars. After his workshops I must admit that I find myself more thoroughly analyzing the fonts I come across in daily life: advertisements, street signs, gum wrappers, etc. Anyway, Gonzalo is definitely not alone in his typo-mania. In last month's XLR8R magazine, I came across an article covering a new documentary dedicated to one of Gonazlo's most loved fonts, Helvetica. Originally intended to be a neutral font, we have embedded much meaning into this ubiquitous typeface, as it is found in cultural icons large and small, such as the space shuttle, music albums (such as the Beatles White Album and Massive Attack's Blue Lines), and the New York City Subway. Apparently, Helvetica turns 50 this year! Be sure to check out clips from the documentary too.
