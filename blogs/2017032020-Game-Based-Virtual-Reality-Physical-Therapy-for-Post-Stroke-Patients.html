Game-Based Virtual Reality Physical Therapy for Post-Stroke Patients

saraehardman

March 20, 2017


Virtual reality (VR) has been shown to be an effective therapeutic tool for overcoming fears and helping children with autism work through social anxiety. Now, it is being tested as a supplement in physical therapy. A new study examined whether VR could have positive therapeutic effects on post-stroke patients as they relearned how to walk.

The study included 30 patients who had recently suffered strokes. Half participated in regular physical therapy, while the other half participated in a VR-supplemented therapy. While no VR headsets were used in this experiment, the latter therapy involved creating a virtual environment by projecting Nintendo Wii games onto a wall in an isolated room. In each case, the researchers measured differences in gait balance and occurrence of falls before and after therapy.

Regarding gait balance, both the control group and the VR group improved on a comparable basis. Although both groups also saw a reduction in the occurrence of falls, the decrease was more significant in the group emersed in the virtual environment. In addition, the researchers noted that the patients who used VR-supplemented therapy had greater levels of motivation to complete their sessions.

This suggests that VR-supplemented therapy, through the use of a Wii or similar technology, could have significant impacts on physical therapy for post-stroke victims. The differences between the two groups were not great enough to suggest throwing traditional physical therapy to the curb, but the study does support game-based VR as a motivational tool to help patients regain balance and walk steadily once more.
