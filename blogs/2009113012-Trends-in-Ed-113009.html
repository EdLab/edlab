Trends in Ed, 11.30.09

Arianna Choi

November 30, 2009

The New York Times' article on teachers selling lesson plans prompted interesting debates both online and offline. See Gary's earlier post. Of course, there are many issues entangled such as the ownership of lesson plans, teacher salary in general, and teacher professional development. But for this blog post, put yourself in the buyers' shoes and think about where that demand might have come from. One reason could be that some teachers purchased lesson plans because they wanted to teach better. Another could be that they simply wanted a shortcut so that they don't have to spend hours thinking, researching, and designing. Is there a similar demand for educational VIDEOS as well? There are tons of videos available on the internet that can potentially make teaching a particular unit more effective than without it. The challenge, however, is to first find the video(s) that works. WatchKnow is a non-profit online community that works like a 'video wiki' as it provides filtered and vetted educational videos.
