The Startup Experience is the Real Deal at Stanford

jvazquez

October 11, 2013


You may have heard of project-based learning before, but how about product-based? At Stanford University, entrepreneurial students are finding a haven in the Startup Garage, a course that lets them to go from concept to company in just two quarters. The Startup Garage has a "B. School" meets "D. School" feel to it, as students actively engage in the design process throughout their tenure in the class.

Working in interdisciplinary teams across the university, students develop and prototype ideas, work with users to understand their needs, test and reiterate, and then pitch their products to investors. By the end of the entire experience, students will either have a viable product to launch or will at least have the know-how to get the ball rolling in their own time.

While acting as an incubator, this class gives students the opportunity to practice and fail within a safe learning environment. As with other experiential learning approaches, it also allows them to try on the entrepreneur hat and get a taste of what it takes to run a startup.
