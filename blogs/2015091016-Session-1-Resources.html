Beekeeping :    An EdLab Studios original The Three P's: Pre-production, Production, Post-production https://vimeo.com/59721561 Example of Framing an Interview: https://vimeo.com/132688253 Glossary of Common Video Terms on Vimeo Aperture: the size of the opening within your lens that allows light onto the image sensor. Aperture is measured by f-number or f-stops. F-stop is a term used to describe the size of the aperture opening. The lower the F-stop number, the bigger the aperture. If the aperture is low, more light is able reach the image sensor. F-stop settings are normally displayed with a forward slash. Common f-stops are: f/1.4, f/2, f/2.8, f/4, f/5.6, f/8, f/11, f/16, and f/22. A low f-stop number (large aperture) results in a shallow depth of field and a high f-stop (smaller aperture) gives a deep depth of field. Shutter speed is the amount of time that each individual frame is exposed for when shooting video on a DSLR. For example, if you set your camera’s shutter speed to 60, each frame is being exposed for 1/60th of a second.
