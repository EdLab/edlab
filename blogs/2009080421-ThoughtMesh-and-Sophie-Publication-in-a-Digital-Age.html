ThoughtMesh and Sophie: Publication in a Digital Age

Ting Yuan

August 4, 2009

Carnegie-Mellon University's ETC Press, a publishing imprint dedicated to printing books across multiple media formats, has started a collaboration with ThoughtMesh and Sophie. Sophie is an open tool for writing, creating, and sharing web-based multimedia book, which can also enable writer-reader interaction through incorporating readers' comments. A 2.0 version will be issued in October, 2009. I can imagine that this type of digital publication tool will bring a revolution to the future of books/journals, teacher education in literacy, as well as students' learning in literacy (as students at most public classrooms do frequent book publishing activities in traditional format). I want to test it out in the fall when I start a digital story telling project with a 5th-grade classroom at PS. 199. ThoughtMesh was developed with support from USC's innovative online journal named Vectors Journal of Culture and Technology in a Dynamic Vernacular. It generates tags to connect scholarly essays published on different Web sites.

ThoughtMesh is an unusual model for publishing and discovering scholarly papers online. It gives readers a tag-based navigation system that uses keywords to connect excerpts of essays published on different websites. So let's say you are reading an essay on Modern art. You can pick a single word out of that essay's tag cloud- -say Picasso- -and view a list of all the sections from that essay that relate to Picasso. Or you can view a list of sections of other articles tagged with Picasso, and jump right to one of those sections. You can also combine tags to narrow your search, such as Picasso + Cubism + 1900 Readers claiming expertise in an article's subject matter may rate and comment on each article, affecting its ranking in search returns.
