High School Brings Classroom Learning to the Workplace

jvazquez

July 14, 2013


They say experience is the best teacher, and MC2 STEM High School is living proof.

MC2 (pronounced "MC squared") STEM is a project-based public high school in Cleveland, Ohio that boasts a 95% graduation rate. As part of the pedagogy, students are paired with STEM professionals who provide mentorship, academic support, and career planning help. Each grade of students is headquartered at different workplaces throughout the city, including the Great Lakes Science Center and General Electric. This workplace-as-classroom model allows students to put into practice the real-world applications of their educational experiences.

At MC2 STEM, "real world work" relates to 21st century skills; namely, addressing how students think (e.g., creativity, problem-solving), how they work (e.g., collaboration), their resourcefulness with tools (e.g., information literacy), and life and career skills (e.g., social responsibility). There is research to suggest that teaching real world work works. A recent Gallup poll found a link between people who learned these skills in school and perceived higher work quality later in life.
