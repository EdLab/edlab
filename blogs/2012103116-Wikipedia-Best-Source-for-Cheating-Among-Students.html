Wikipedia: Best Source for Cheating Among Students

Francisco.Mendoza

October 31, 2012

There've been a lot of stories in the news about cheating in schools. The problem has affected prestigious high schools such as Stuyvesant and colleges such as Harvard. The increase in smartphones and other technology gadgets can be attributed to changes in cheating; cheating is an old age problem that advances in technology has only increased. This article notes Wikipedia as the source of choice for students who plagiarize. It states that over nine million high school student newspapers and more than 24 million college student papers plagiarize directly off Wikipedia. Other websites such as Paper Mills are used for cheating but Wikipedia remains at the top, especially among college students. It is very easy to cheat on Wikipedia as the site has over 18 million articles on countless topics, but what most students don't realize is that a lot of the information is not accurate. As a college student, my professors constantly remind us not to plagiarize, and some colleges have stiff penalties for students who plagiarize. What are some ways to get students to stop cheating? What would be a fair penalty for students who are copying from Wikipedia or other websites?
