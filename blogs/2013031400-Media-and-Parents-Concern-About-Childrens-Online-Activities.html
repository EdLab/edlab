Media and Parents Concern About Children's Online Activities

Khalil Abubakar

March 14, 2013

The internet has become a powerful tool for all of us in most things we do these days, whether social, employment or education. Social media like Facebook and YouTube are trying to find ways to negotiate with parents on different methods to restrict under-aged children's access to censored material. According to this article, Google, the parent owner of YouTube, has launched a program with instructional videos related to topics on cyber bullying and privacy for much safer YouTube use. Additionally, Facebook has teamed up with Edutopia to help school create social media guidelines. Facebook allows children under the age of 13 to use the site and are working with stakeholders, regulators and other policy makers to enforce this restriction. There is also Footprints, an app that will help parents track their child's movements. The New York Times reported there are slang translation apps which track the child's social media pages while another site alerts parents when their child tries to access an inappropriate site and one that monitors hours spent on Facebook. New York City is trying to incorporate social media guidelines to check the appropriateness of teacher-student interactions.
