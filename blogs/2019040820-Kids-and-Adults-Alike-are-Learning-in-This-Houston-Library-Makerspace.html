Kids and Adults Alike are Learning in This Houston Library Makerspace

Melanie.Hering

April 8, 2019


Just down the street from NASA Johnson Space Center in Houston is a library which has found a fun new way to cater to its Engineering-loving community. After receiving a grant, the Clear Lake City-County Freeman Branch library built a makerspace to allow patrons of all ages to create, work on STEM-related projects, and take classes to learn new skills.

This makerspace, known as the Jocelyn H Lee Innovation Lab, tries to make as many of the resources as possible free, from circuit-building to soldering to 3D printing. Whether the lab is hosting small-business owners or science fair winners, the library prides itself on the fact that the community drives the projects and uses of the makerspace.
