Trends in Ed: Are 99c Apps Going to Cover the Costs?

Skanda Amarnath

November 22, 2011

Children's App Manifesto (via EdSurge) wants app developers to seriously think about how apps should be priced. The way they see it, high-quality app development is simply not sustainable even in the 99 cent model. Yet as my father has constantly engrained in my head, it's all about pricing power. As a technophile, I have to admit that the returns from apps are actually somewhat underwhelming. Apps are simply not so revolutionary that they make our lives far more enjoyable or efficient than they otherwise would be. Yes, they help at the margins, but there's a rational basis for why consumers don't spend a whole lot of money on apps. I think the tech community is going to have to come to terms with this reality, rather than develop collusive systems to keep app prices artificially high. Ultimately, really good apps that harness the full potential of AI will help create sustainable business models for app development. Until then, I suggest we all take the hype surrounding selling apps, particularly educational apps, with a grain of salt.
