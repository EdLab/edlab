Monetary Policy: Money Creation

abagigah92

October 21, 2013

At any given time a bank must only keep a certain percentage of its total deposits on reserve. An initial deposit of a certain amount of money will be multiplied as the bank loans out any excess reserves, whose spending leads to new deposits and even further loans in the economy. In the United States, the Federal Reserve is in charge of monetary policy. Monetary policy is one of the ways that the U.S. government attempts to control the economy. If the money supply grows too fast, the rate of inflation will increase; if the growth of the money supply is slowed too much, then economic growth may also slow. In general, the U.S. sets inflation targets that are meant to maintain a steady inflation.
