Trends in Ed, 2.12.09

jvazquez

February 12, 2009

Edtechies upbeat after passage of stimulus bill While President Obama has not yet signed the final bill, people in ed tech have reason to believe that funding will be provided to facilitate the use of technology in the classroom. Title II D of No Child Left Behind Act, Enhancing Education Through Technology (EETT), is slated to receive one billion new dollars (in addition to the $267 million still in the program). This aspect of the bill isn't the only positive for those supporting more tech use: both the House and Senate versions call for billions of dollars ($6 and $9 billion, respectively) in broadband and wireless services to help reach underserved areas. Both bills also have money for school modernization, which includes using funds to update and upgrade existing technologies. If Shakespeare had a Twitter account A few twitter users have decided to take it upon themselves to carry out an entire Shakespeare play, aptly titled, Twitter of the Shrew, over 12 days beginning February 14th. If you're familiar with Twitter, that means they are limited to 140 characters per entry (!). Is it a good idea? Sounds kind of hard to keep up with, seeing as it will span 19 accounts (one for each of the main characters and a few supporting).
