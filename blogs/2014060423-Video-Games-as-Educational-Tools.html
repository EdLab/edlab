Video Games as Educational Tools

Youssef Ballo

June 4, 2014

Video games easily grab the attention of children and adolescents. It is important to assess the extent to which video games impact childhood education. It also represents one technique that may be available to classroom teachers. Since video games have the capacity to engage children into learning, children build different types of skills such as visualization ability. Video games are also more effective for children who started out with relatively poor skills. There are many reasons video games are useful in for students.

  1. Video games can be used as research tools.
  2. Video games can assist children in setting goals by providing feedback and reinforcement.
  3. Video games allow participants to experience curiosity and challenge.
  4. Video games may help in the development of IT skills.
