Delivering Secret Codes Through Print Media

HSC

February 17, 2007

Fujitsu has developed a technology that can encode data onto an image that is invisible to the human eye but can be decoded by a cell phone's camera (This technology is actually based on a 2,500-year-old Greek practice known as steganography). Mike Nelson, at Fujitsu Europe, says, "The concept is to be able to link the printed page into the digital domain." Music Club, a company in Japan, already is using the technology to embed codes into flyers it sends to subscribers. Through a flyer customers can link to music clips via their cell phone. This technology has lots of implications for publishers and other producers of content. For instance, imagine if every item in the Teachers College Record, the TC Historical Art Collection, or the Teachers College archives contained encoded data linking researchers to additional resources on the web? This could fundamentally alter the way people do research. Read more about the process and potential applications.
