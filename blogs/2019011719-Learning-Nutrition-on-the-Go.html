Learning Nutrition on the Go

Rebecca.Sullivan

January 17, 2019


FoodSwitch aims to promote healthy eating by offering real-time, consumer-driven coaching to shoppers. Instead of doctors providing patients with a list of healthy foods, the free app invites shoppers to scan their favorite food. Then, FoodSwitch gives a breakdown of its nutritional value and suggests healthier options. Users can also compare similar items they see on the shelf to help them make the best choice. The app strives to put nutrition education in the hands of consumers, empowering them with the knowledge they need to shift their food choices.

FoodSwitch relies on crowdsourcing to update its database of food items. If a user looks up an item that is not in the database, the app requests that the user scan the name and nutritional facts so it can be added. In this way, students learning from the app also become teachers for others.

Would you use FoodSwitch to help you learn about healthier choices? Add your voice to the ongoing conversation on Vialogues.
