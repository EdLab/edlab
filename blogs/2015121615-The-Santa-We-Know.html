The Santa We Know

Panisuan Chasinga

December 16, 2015

As we're approaching the most celebrated day on earth, December 25th, or Christmas Day, I'd like to share with you images of the iconic Santa Claus we may not have seen. Although the red-and-white garbed version of St. Nick wasn't actually invented by the Coca-Cola Company, it actually pinned the image of a chubby, friendly man with white beard in a red outfit on the global imagination. This is one of the first Santa Claus ads from Coca-Cola presenting the Santa as we know today: Before Coca-Cola, Santa Claus was portrayed in a number of ways, including being a hideous-looking elf. He wore several styles and colors of outfits, including red. If you have the stomach to learn about the weird, awry and downright scary Scandinavian Santa Clauses and don't mind ruining your dream image of Santa and Christmas forever, check out this magnificent post on the "real" Santa. Warning: Some is graphic and disturbing. Better be nice than naughty... Have a Merry X' mas!
