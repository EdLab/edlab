The Science of the Summer Olympics

Carmel Addae

August 1, 2012

I am sure most of us have been enjoying the summer Olympics as it offers us a chance to watch many sports we seldom get a chance to watch. NBC and its other networks have broadcast the games via tape delay during primetime, though users of social networks, especially Twitter, usually announce results prior. In light of Scott's D&R presentation regarding video resources for STEM students and teachers, I came across a resource that NBCLearn have created that focuses on the science and technology propelling the athletes in London. The set of videos, The Science of the Summer Olympics, is the latest in series of videos that looks at the science of certain sports. Previous editions have included the sciences of the NHL, NFL and Winter Olympics. The videos were created by NBC in collaboration with the National Science Foundation. Participating athletes in the current series include sprinter Usain Bolt and highly rated swimmer Missy Franklin. What are some ways in which we can use these sets of video resources for the STEMedia project and how can educators incorporate the science of sports in their lessons?
