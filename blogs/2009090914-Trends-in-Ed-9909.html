Trends in Ed 9.9.09

Jeannie Crowley

September 9, 2009

When the Internet gained widespread popularity there were warnings by psychologist that excessive Internet use would lead to social isolation and depression. It seems the pendulum has swung in the other direction: the Internet is now deemed to be too social. President Obama warned students yesterday to be leery of Facebook because their information will be out there and retrievable by anyone in years to come. The Meet the Press discussion embedded below revolves around the socially intrusive nature of the Internet. With people "armed" with video phones and Tweets our real-world actions are quickly digitized and shared around the world. The result, according to the guests of Meet the Press, is we can no longer assume anything we do (real or virtual) is private. Is this call for voluntary isolation from the digital community reactionary rhetoric? Or, is the concept of privacy soon to become a thing of the past?
