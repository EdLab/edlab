W3 Innovations fined for Violating COPPA

Kate Meersschaert

August 16, 2011

To continue EdLab's ongoing examination of privacy & social media, I found one of today's Mashable posts especially interesting. Apparently, W3 Innovations the parent co. of BrokenThumbsApps was fined $50,000 by the FTC on Friday for violating the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA): “In addition to the collection and maintenance of over 30,000 emails, containg email addresses, Defendants have collected, maintained, and/or disclosed personal information from over 300 Emily's Girl World app users and approximately 290 Emily's Dress Up app users who have registered to submit comments.” To help educate young people re: the dangers of an online presence the FTC recently launched “Living Life Online”. As Mashable points-out the site's UI is truly terrible, but it is a step in the right direction and will hopefully help start a trend towards educating young people about the potential dangers of engaging in anything online from gaming and shopping to potentially damaging blog posts. As I commented in today's D&R session in response to Sharon's vialogue on Google's privacy settings: "This is yet another call to arms for better media literacy education in our schools we need to share this information along with the real-world ramifications of the social web's permanent record." Perhaps EdLab can add this idea of media literacy? Ethics? Law? to our focus and plans for future initiatives? We could create a special heading under trends in Ed similar to the FTC's site... Media Safety for a new Millennium?
