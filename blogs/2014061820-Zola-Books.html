Zola Books

Fred Rossoff

June 18, 2014


Zola Books is a social reading app for the iPhone that also doubles as a one stop-shop for all your reading needs, from book recommendations to e-publishing. The core of Zola Books is the app, which lets users read in a more social manner than, say, Amazon’s Kindle. However, the creators have added on a smorgasbord of features, including book review aggregation and daily deals.

Pros:

The simple and attractive app is easy to use. Going the extra mile, Zola also has an in-house ebook publishing arm to help new authors get their work out there.

Cons:

The app is still in beta, so there’s as yet almost no community to take advantage of its social tools, either by connecting with authors or reading along with friends. In addition, the catalog is at this point far from comprehensive.

Our Takeaway:

It’s good to see a company competing with Amazon in the e-book space, and a few ideas implemented by Zola look useful. As it stands, however, it doesn’t yet have the community or the muscle to deliver on its promise.

The Bottom Line:

Still in it’s early stages, anybody who cares about reading will be rooting for Zola Books to provide a feasible e-reading alternative to Amazon.
