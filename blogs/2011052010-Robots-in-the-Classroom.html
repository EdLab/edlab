Robots in the Classroom...

Kate Meersschaert

May 20, 2011

Isaac Asimov would be proud... more than 50 years after the publication of his classic, I,Robot, we are just now beginning to realize the potential for utilizing robots in the classroom. The Finch, a new programmable robot intended to help teach computer science at the high school and college level was recently released by Carnegie Mellon's CREATE lab. Finch resembles a cute combination of Pixar's Wall-E, and a turtle or manatee and is designed to allow users to easily create and test rich, interactive programs. Finch also has cool LED lights, on-board sensors, an accelerometer, motors and even a pen-mount to use for drawing (I imagine finch becoming an amazing interactive spirograph). Python and Java script are currently supported with more languages on the way. Software, lesson plans and an incentivized reward program for developers can all be found on the site. CMU's research shows increased retention and interest in CS courses. This is the exciting take-away for me... we are constantly looking for new ways to create excitement and "coolness" around STEM subjects and this is a great step in that direction! Obama is trying to further this idea through his "Educate to Innovate". What do you think could be a cool way to integrate robots into the classroom? What other subjects could be served by bots?
