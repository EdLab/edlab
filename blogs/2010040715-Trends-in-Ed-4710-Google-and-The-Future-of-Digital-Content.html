Trends in Ed 4.7.10, Google and The Future of Digital Content

Jeannie Crowley

April 7, 2010

Trend: Digital Publishing and Libraries
Related Posts:: The Numbers Behind Digital Content, Pocket Knowledge and DuraCloud

As universities race to put their content online, there are two big questions to the institution has to answer:

  • Where will all of this content be hosted?
  • What will we do with the content once it is put online?
    • The answer to the first question is fairly easy to come by and is usually reached after looking at the budget, staffing resources and technological capabilities of a university. There are those who afford to create an in-house system (like the University of Washington) and those who cannot. Those who cannot afford on campus hosting can go with a cloud service like DuraCloud
    The second question is not as easy to answer. What exactly are we supposed to do with all of this digital content? Too often it goes online and sits there, like a dusty digital representation of the unused traditional library stacks. This is where Google comes in. Google is reaching out to universities and a select group of scholars to "build tools to comb and improve Google's digital library". Specifically, the project will focus on:
  • Building software for tracking changes in language over time.
  • Creating utilities to discover books and passages of interest to a particular discipline.
  • Developing systems for crowd-sourced corrections to book data and metadata.
  • The testing of a literary or historical hypothesis through innovative analysis of a book.
    This project is a step in the right direction to make sure that digital content has an edge over printed content. . .not something that is currently guaranteed.
