Alexa, Open the Gottesman Libraries

chingfu

January 9, 2019

We may not need and want an IoT device to open the door for us at the library, but Alexa and Alexa-enabled devices have great potential to open a new broadcast and outreach space for our library and publications. Check out this quick demonstration of Alexa’s skill (i.e. app) for New Learning Times.



As reviewed in these earlier pieces (see Gary’s post and Nikita’s post), we need to explore further about when, where, and how users would like to engage with these Alexa skills, and what types of contents are effective in this format. It all depends on how hands-free people are, need, and/or want. In that respect, I think a TCR Digest skill broadcasted through Echo Auto could work well for educators and researchers who drive to work -- well, before driverless cars dominate the roads.

Here are a few more Alexa testing skills for you to try, and you can add them to your Alexa-enabled devices by following these steps:


  1. Go to Amazon or Alexa Skill Blueprints, and log in with your amazon account.
  2. Click these skill links: 1). New Learning Times (adapted from Research Digest); 2). TCR Digest; 3). TC Library Events.
  3. Enable the skills.
  4. Use the phrase "Alexa, Open (skill name)".
  5. Have fun!


Oh Alexa, thanks for coming in for the interview. We'll be in touch.
