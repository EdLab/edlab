Stanford to Offer 'Lean Launchpad' Course Under New 'Coursera' Umbrella

Kate Meersschaert

February 2, 2012

Coursera is the new umbrella for Stanford Engineering Everywhere's collection of sixteen, new "enhanced" online courses. Coursera will feature a start-up focused, "Lean Launchpad" (could they have been inspired by our LaunchPad39B?) course in entrepreneurship along with the other courses later this month. This collection of courses grew-out of an effort to offer more interactive, free online courses in a similar format to that of Sebastian Thrun and Peter Norvig's blockbuster AI course. Despite Thrun's recent departure to start his own online learning venture, Udacity, Stanford appears keen to continue to publicize and grow what started-out as an experiment in online learning. After my overwhelmingly positive experience with Stanford's AI course, I plan to take as many of the following courses as possible as they look especially apropos to the mission of the EdLab: The Lean Launchpad, Technology Entrepreneurship, Human Computer Interaction and Game Theory.
