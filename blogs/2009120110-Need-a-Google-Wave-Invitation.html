Need a Google Wave Invitation?

Adrienne Garber

December 1, 2009

Many of us in the EdLab are currently testing out Google's newest application, the Google Wave. It is an online tool for real-time communication and collaboration. A wave can be both a conversation and a document where people can discuss and work together using richly formatted text, photos, videos, maps, and more. I have eight invitations up for grabs. If you would like to receive one, comment to this blog post with your email address. The more of us that participate, the richer the experience! For those of you already participating in the Google preview, there is an unofficial guide to Google Wave written by a community of contributors and edited by Gina Trapani with Adam Pash (editors of Lifehacker). This guide is not published by or affiliated with Google, Inc., but it is very useful in getting acclimated to all things Wave. In addition, Google has published its own video tutorials on Youtube on how to use the Wave. An example video is available here.
