Mueve la Cabeza, Danza Kuduro

Nantwi

June 1, 2015


Too often the casualties of the STEM movement and budget cuts currently sweeping our nation’s schools are arts education and culturally relevant pedagogy. This is especially true in inner city communities where arts programs offer at-risk students a chance to express their creativity and stay in school. An arts-focused education opens doors and opportunities for students to explore the world outside their immediate surroundings. It is in this spirit that the OS Kuduristas Youth Program operates. Kuduro is a special form of dancing and lifestyle in former Portuguese colonies in Africa, especially Angola.

The program uses kuduro as the platform to connect students in Brooklyn with their peers in Luanda, Angola’s capital city. Built on the idea that arts and music transcend borders, the program focuses on introducing cultures through music and dance; it is in its essence a unique cultural exchange program. In addition to learning about different cultures, the students also create their own kuduro music using digital software. This offers educational opportunities for the students, as creating music requires some use of math and other subjects.

Do we need to revive arts education in our schools? How important is cultural exchange to students’ educational development? Join this lively discussion on Vialogues.
