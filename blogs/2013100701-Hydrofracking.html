Hydrofracking

Elysee Dougan

October 7, 2013

This topic, unrelated to my series on marketing, has caught my attention. Hydraulic Fracturing is the mining process of withdrawing natural gasses from beneath the ground. The reason this has my attention is because of the way this is actually done. People are pumping what is called "slick water" into shale to fracture the rock to release the natural gasses. Slick water is a mixture of water, sediments like sand, and many toxic chemicals; this process requires much more water than regular drilling, but is used because of how much cheaper it is. Hydrofrackng has an extremely negative impact on the environment, as well as the many species sheltered in the forests that are now being destroyed to make these mining plants. This form of mining contaminates the water supply on the surface of the earth, as well as ground water. Hydrofracking has already resulted in many casualties and diseases like leukemia. Major companies funding and creating this mining process plan to create a plant here on the coasts of New York.
