Flash on TV

zhou

April 23, 2009

Three days ago Adobe announced that they would make their renowned Flash platform available on TV by the end of this year. There have been a lot of discussions on whether and when TV and PC will eventually merge into one mixed media. The digitization of television this year and Adobe's recent move are among the first signs of the merge. But separating TV-based Flash from the big trend, this product alone could immensely affect people's TV-watching as well as computer-use experiences. For one, people can watch videos, movies, and television programs from web-based video sharing websites such as YouTube instead of subscribing to all those pay-per-month television channels. And people would enjoy playing Flash-based video games on their 32' HDTV instead of the laptop simply because of the huge screen. And one added value of Flash is that there are so many possibilities to build applications that employ the latest technologies upon this platform. For example, the augmented reality I blogged about a few days ago. And also the possibility of using Wii remote with Flash. Now it's the time for tech-wise educators to think what this might mean for education. By the way, I think MOMA should exhibit the wiimote doodle creations.
