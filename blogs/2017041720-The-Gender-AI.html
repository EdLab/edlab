The Gender AI

csd2126

April 17, 2017


Women in film are often relegated to the roles of damsel in distress, victim in a horror film, or manic pixie dream girl. The lesser presence of women in films can now be analyzed on a larger scale using artificial intelligence software. The software is able to identify a characterâ€™s gender, how long they speak, and how long they appear on screen.

Gender differences in films can be measured using a new tool called GDIQ produced by engineers from Google and the University of Southern California. The software is being used by the Geena Davis Institute on Gender in Media which works to change gender bias in the media and film industry. The gender ratio in films has consistently favored men and has not shown significant signs of change. While these tools may not change the film industry, they are helpful for calling attention to gender differences in film.

Was there a moment when the gender difference in film seemed especially salient to you? Tell us when you first noticed the different roles women and men typically play in films in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
