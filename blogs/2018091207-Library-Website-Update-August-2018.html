Library Website Update, August 2018

jvazquez

September 12, 2018

Well, we did it, everyone. We got through those sweltering August days and are all the stronger for it. While we were melting in the AC, our patrons were busy clicking away on the library website. Let’s see what they were up to….


Overview

Total visits: 8862 (73% off campus | 27% on campus) +0.7% from last month

Returning visitors: 5486 (73% off campus | 27% on campus) -0.4% from last month


Not too much of a difference from July.


Overall, users spent 5min 34sec on the site, completing an average of 5 actions per visit.


Most popular actions

  1. Search

  2. View resources (from nav bar)

Resources in order of popularity:

  1. Journals and Databases* most accessed off campus

  2. EDUCAT** most accessed on campus

  3. Docdel

  4. Lib Support

  5. CU CAS

  1. View services (from nav bar)

Content pages in order of popularity:

  1. Request materials *

  2. Room reservation guidelines **

  3. Course reserves

  4. Request materials (faculty)

  5. Printing and scanning

  1. Reserve a room (quicklink)

Search behavior


Total searches: 8249 (68% off campus | 32% on campus) +2% from last month


Of these, only 20% were filtered by type. Here’s how they stacked up:

  • Articles: 30%

  • Books: 29%

  • Everything: 19%

  • Journals: 13%

  • Website: 5%

  • Knowledge Base: 3%


13% of total searches went on to look for “More Results.”


Unsurprisingly, the most expanded categories were Articles (643 clicks) and Books (365 clicks). People were not as eager to expand Journal (53 clicks), Website (15 clicks), or Knowledge Base (1 click!) results.  


Room reservations

Total number of reservations: 509 -32% from last month

Total cancellations: 116 -28% from last month


Here’s a direct comparison of website vs app usage:



The vast majority of reservations are made on the web, despite efforts to revitalize the process via the app. Something to work on!



Look at those team meetings and study groups! It’s the first time “conference call” has been surpassed.


Singles still reign supreme on reservations made on the site, but in an interesting turn of events, 4- and 5-person rooms were the most popular reservations on the app.


Very excited to see what September numbers will look like. See you then!
