Follow up to social networking for academia (keep private)

Jamison Judd

October 19, 2006

Just wanted to share the interesting experience I've had with the last posting. I received an e-mail from Chung Lee at the Taktse International School who is working on a proposal to build a social networking site around academic topics. From the business proposal he sent: "Essentially we are trying to do what the Internet has promised all along: a way to bring user knowledge together and allow knowledge, ideas, and opinions to grow and be tested by a larger community. The goal is to create kneighborhood.com into a simple portal in which any casual visitor wishing to fully understand a topic such as global warming can see what users have said about the subject. Kneighborhood.com is similar in concept to the very successful wikipedia project, but it is also very different. Kneighborhood.com will allow more users to contribute and create, as well as allow more users to comment on such postings. Visually, the layout will not appear like an encyclopedia entry, but will look like a simple text posting and ideally will be much shorter." An interesting concept, but how will they attract and retain users? If anyone is interested I've attached the e-mail and proposal. I'll try and update this posting with any further information.
