Trends in Ed, 8.20.09

Arianna Choi

August 20, 2009

Colleges Show Interest in Applicant's Personality for Admissions The Wall Street Journal reported an interesting article: Adding Personality to the College Admissions Mix. According to the piece, Boston College, DePaul University and Tufts University are planning to measure prospective students' personalities using recently developed evaluation systems that quantify noncognitive traits such as leadership, resilience and creativity. College Board, ETS and ACT are also following the trend. ETS, for example, recently launched its own Personal Potential Index to provide information about “the personal attributes that are critical to success in graduate and professional school.” Critics, including a Harvard education professor, argue that the methods to collect data (esp. if it's self-reported) or to quantify noncognitive traits may result in unreliable information.
