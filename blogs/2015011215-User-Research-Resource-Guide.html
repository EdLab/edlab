User Research Resource Guide

Abril Ibarra siqueiros

January 12, 2015

Icons from The Noun Project If you’re working on a project, study, or prototype, among other things, at some point you may need user feedback.  Disciplines tend to have their own standards when it comes to how a study is conducted, so that is something to be mindful of.  If you would like to incorporate user input or use it to inform decisions for your project this is a guide to conduct user research with resources from the Gottesman Libraries. Research question:

How can I go about including user input in my project or research?
The following resources for online catalogs and federated search engines are credited to Allen Foresta.

Online Catalogs

EDUCAT – The most definitive record of the holdings of the Gottesman Libraries at Teachers College, including books, e-books, journals, e-journals, dissertations, media, and other specialized collections of all kinds.
  • See our library’s video tutorial on searching EDUCAT.
CLIO – The union catalog of Columbia University Libraries (exclusive of the libraries of Teachers College, the Columbia Law School, and Jewish Theological Seminary), providing the most definitive record of individual libraries’ holdings of books, e-books, journals, e-journals, dissertations, media, and other specialized collections of all kinds. WorldCat – The union catalog of the contents of over 10,000 libraries worldwide, providing the means to do exhaustive searching by author, title, subject, and numerous other criteria and to find locations for specific items in libraries nearby and elsewhere.  Also freely accessible at worldcat.org.
  • See our library’s video tutorial on searching WorldCat.

Federated Search Engines

Quicksearch (via Columbia University Libraries) – To search the catalog, multiple periodical indexes, and other web resources simultaneously within a variety of subjects and disciplinary subcategories. Super Search (via Gottesman Libraries) – To search some or all of our periodical indexes, plus EDUCAT and CLIO, simultaneously for books, journal articles, reports, dissertations, etc. Google Scholar – Provides the means to search for scholarly research across many disciplines and sources: articles, theses, books, abstracts and court opinions, from academic publishers, professional societies, online repositories, universities, and other web sites.
  • See our library’s Google Scholar video tutorial.

Books

Print Library of Congress Classification Many user research resources fall under two classes: Q (Science) and T (Technology).  The subclasses below are the more specific areas where these resources would be found.  Aside from searching the online catalog, browsing through these areas on the shelves is another way to find resources. Q - Science Subclass: QA71-90              Instruments and machines QA75-76.95              Calculating machines QA75.5-76.95                Electronic computers. Computer science T - Technology Subclass: TS155-194           Production management. Operations management Electronic Ebooks There are various ebooks about user research and user experience methods available through the library.  The can be found by searching EDUCAT and using SuperSearch or by going directly to Ebrary, which is where the collection of ebooks is hosted. Ebrary Academic Complete Subscription Collection -  Ebrary Academic Complete is a collection of more than 44,170 full-text books and other authoritative content from leading publishers. Academic Complete spans multiple subject areas such as Business & Economics, Computer, Engineering & Applied Sciences, Humanities, Science, Medicine & Allied Health, and Social Sciences. Ebrary Perpetual Access, PDA and STL - Ebrary Academic Complete is a collection of more than 44,170 full-text books and other authoritative content from leading publishers. Academic Complete spans multiple subject areas such as Business & Economics, Computer, Engineering & Applied Sciences, Humanities, Science, Medicine & Allied Health, and Social Sciences.

Databases

The following databases cover multiple disciplines, which make them more suitable to search for a variety of resources on user research.  Within these databases are journals, articles, peer-reviewed sources, book reviews, and more. Academic Search Premier ERIC ProQuest

Keywords

When searching for resources in planning user research the following keywords in EDUCAT, CLIO, Super Search, and databases will yield basic results.  To tailor these methods for a specific area of study, combine them with a discipline, which may have a more relevant applications. user research user study user experience user engagement user centered design focus groups survey design survey research guerrilla research methods questionnaires

EdLab Tools

Survey Sidekick An online survey creation tool that provides just-in-time instruction in good survey research practices. The tool is designed to prevent the mis-application of survey research techniques.

Hand Selected Resources

Just Enough Research by Erika Hall This book helps to decide what method of user research is the most suitable and efficient depending on the project and goal. Handbook of Global User Research edited by Robert M. Schumacher (CLIO) This in-depth handbook gets into the topic of global and local user research, what it’s like among other disciplines, project management aspects, and examples of user research around the world. Reflections on Lexicographical User Research by Sven Tarp A citation for an article that applied user research within the discipline of Language and Literature, as well as reviews the pros and cons of various methods, such as questionnaires, interviews, and logs.

Additional Library Resources

Ask a Librarian Librarians and services associates are available to answer questions and provide guidance during the library’s open hours.  Help can be provided by submitting a support request and choosing the category that best fits your question, stopping by the services desk, or calling the library by phone. Research Consultation If more in-depth research help is needed, one-on-one appointments are available with librarians a services associates.  To book an appointment, submit a support request and in the drop down menu choose: “I would like to consult with a librarian about a research question.” Request Materials Another option to gain a specific resource is to submit a request for an item to be purchased and added to the collection.  This can be done for print and electronic materials. Columbia University Libraries If the Teachers College Library does not have a resource you’re interested in, an alternative is to check Columbia University’s library catalog called CLIO.
