Versal

LauraCostello

September 26, 2013


Versal is a highly customizable course creation platform set up for learning in a variety of disciplines. The system goes beyond traditional static learning tools like video and recorded lectures to offer interactive "gadgets" that let instructors create and customize learning experiences without programming knowledge. Each gadget is a standalone, app-like module that can be combined in any configuration within the course space. The minimalist course structure gives educators total freedom to arrange their content. The interface is easy to use and augmented with folksy art that embodies its collaborative mentality.

Pros:

Versal is still in beta and is on the verge of releasing a new feature which will allow users to develop their own gadgets and share them with the teaching community on the site. The team has also set up a foundation to incentivize the creation of gadgets and innovative educational tools through grants. The site is currently free for both teachers and students and CEO Gregor Freund has stated that he has no plans to ever charge for access.

Cons:

Because of the way gadgets are set up, it is unclear whether instructors will be able to reap data from student interactions with these items. In some of the sample courses Versal has set up, specific actions are required within the gadget, but there seems to be no way for an instructor to know whether students have completed this action or interacted with these items at all.

Our Takeaway:

Versal is doing something really different with online learning. Many course platforms have debuted in the past few years with the goal of creating a more beautiful, customizable course experience, but most still rely on the same video and lecture-based tools. It combines the interactivity seen in specialty instructional apps with the freedom of a modern learning management system.

The Bottom Line:

Versal is on the verge of something new in online education.
