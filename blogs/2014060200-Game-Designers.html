Game Designers

Youssef Ballo

June 2, 2014

Across the country, there is a growing academic interest in video games. Design experts, educators, students and all interested stakeholders have their thoughts and ideas on how to effectively design, create and implement video games in the classroom. For instance, New York University's Game Center has a collection of 5,000 video games, which are mostly used in classrooms.

A designer of Doom helps run the master's program at the University of California. The creator of Deus Ex is starting a postgraduate program at the University of Texas. A lead designer of the Uncharted series is a tenured professor at the university of Southern California. Most of these game designers are trying to find more knowledge on games and also spread the knowledge to other designers.
