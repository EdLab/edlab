Deep Beats, Shallow Storytelling

Meredith Powers

July 9, 2015


A research paper released last month describes an artificial intelligence system that mines a database of rap lyrics to create new songs—the researchers call it DeepBeat, and it harnesses two machine-learning techniques to come up with novel lyrics created from existing ones. The paper describes DeepBeat as a success, insofar as the new song lyrics demonstrate much greater rhyme density than even the best human rappers. However, while DeepBeat certainly seems to have mastered rhyming, the new songs are pretty incoherent. Since storytelling is kind of the point of rap, human rappers needn’t fear for their jobs quite yet—but DeepBeat is an interesting example of computers approximating human creativity.
