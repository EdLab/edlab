Takeaway from NYCPython Meetup

Panisuan Chasinga

February 20, 2015

**Private** Tonight's NYCPython Meetup group's topic was How to Submit a Conference Proposal. Veteran Pythonista speakers spoke about how to submit persuasive proposals to local and global conferences (such as PyGotham, OSCon, PyCon) and other helpful tips like overcoming the stage fright. There were different people attending from elite Pythonistas working on web APIs, voice recognition, data scientists working with Pandas as well as those with little Python experience and public speaking trying to learn. It's always nice to hear that even the most experienced speakers are nervous--accepting that fact is the key to being a great speaker--and you don't have to be an expert or have anything original to submit to speak. People who dare to share their knowledge are really, really rare. The meetup was informal, and with this kind of weather, only a handful showed up. Nevertheless, what I expected to be an auditorium full of a hundred attending a lecture turned out to be a cozy, friendly environment in which everyone had his chance to speak up and share his/her thoughts. NYCPython holds an "official" meetup every Thursday (often with free pizzas and drinks), and office Hours/study groups every Saturday and Sunday. Anyone wants to come with please let me know so we can come together. :)
