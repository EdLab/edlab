TCR Marketing - International Schools of Education

Christine.Odenath

January 16, 2019

Teachers College Record - Marketing Outreach

International Schools of Education - Institutional Subscription


Outreach Overview:

  • Emails were sent to 188 schools of education located outside the United States from tcr@tc.columbia.edu inviting them to consider an institutional subscription to Teachers College Record

  • 83 of these schools received a targeted message highlighting a member of their community who had been published in TCR, followed by an institutional subscription ask

    • Sent: 11/15/18 & 11/19/18

    • Email copy: Appendix A

  • 105 of these schools received a standard message with an institutional subscription ask

    • Sent: 11/20/18 & 11/26/18

    • Email copy: Appendix B



Countries w/most schools of ed contacted:

  1. UK (30)
  2. Australia (17)
  3. Germany (14)
  4. Canada (12)
  5. Spain (11)



Results:

  • 9 email responses were received, 100% of which were from library circulation desks or automated reply systems confirming receipt of the message.

  • 6 recipients clicked the campaign link driving to the institutional subscription page.

    • If you click the link, you can see that international schools need to pay more than schools in the US & Canada -- this probably isn't the best way to pose the institutional subscription offer





    Interpretations:

    • Email reads - We don’t have data on who opened the email, just on that one campaign link.

    • Language barriers - we sent messages in English. The message’s undeniable appeal could have been lost in translation.

    • Spam - potentially a function of language discrepancies/translation factors, or the salesy tone of the email - we may have been sent to spam.


    Next Steps:

    • I’d like to get data on whether recipients are opening our emails or clicking any of the links included - any suggestions?

      • Metabase - Tracking the metrics of one campaign URL through metabase works well for stock emails sent to large groups (like we did with the 105 standard email recipients above), but in the cases where we’re customizing messaging (like the 83 schools who had TCR authors in their community), we should find a more efficient solution to see whether we get traction on any of the links included.

      • Boomerang - I was able to set up read receipts on my TC gmail via Boomerang. It claims I can see when links were clicked as well? Has anyone used this feature with any success?
