Absolute and Comparative Advantage

abagigah92

January 14, 2014

Absolute and Comparative advantage is one of the key theories in economics when dealing with opportunity cost, trade and production. Opportunity cost is value of an alternative that must be forgone in order to pursue a certain action. In essence, it is the benefits you can receive by taking an alternative action. This theory is explained using a simple model in which a country produces only two goods. For instance, the United States and Canada both produce NLT and Vialogues. A country has an absolute advantage in the production of a good relative to another country if it can produce more quantities of the good. On the other hand, a country has a comparative advantage in the production of a good or service if it produces at a lower opportunity cost than its trading partners. These economics theories are very important in trading. If Canada produces more NLT articles than the United States then Canada has an absolute advantage in producing NLT articles. Although Canada might have an absolute advantage in producing NLT articles, it does not mean they also have a comparative advantage unless they have a lower opportunity cost in production than the United States. Having a lower opportunity cost means a country is much more effective in producing a certain product.
