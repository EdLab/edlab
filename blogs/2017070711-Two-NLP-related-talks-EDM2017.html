Two NLP-related talks @EDM2017

xk2120

July 7, 2017

I went to China last month for 2017 International Educational Data Mining conference, where I met some interesting researchers and learned a lot of wonderful techniques and ideas from these new friends. 


According to my notes, I went to 25 talks in total, and 21 of them had been published in either full, short or industry papers in the EDM2017 proceedings. I took the introduction, method and discussion parts of the articles for topic model analysis, using my notes for topic verification/ manual coding. 


After perplexity and entropy measurement of various topic number models, three topics might be the optimal choice for the topic number selection. Please see the Figure 1 for the words with the highest frequency in each topic, which suggested the topic of each cluster of papers: "adaptive learning system, learning behavior and natural language processing (NLP)". I would focus on two NLP related talks in this blog.

Epistemic Network Analysis and Topic Modeling for Chat Data from Collaborative Learning Environment 

The first author, Dr. Cai, is a software engineer and a poet in fact. He developed an NLP tool for dialogue analysis which involved topic modeling and epistemic network analysis (ENA). I explained topic modeling in my previous blog post and would like to talk about ENA in this one. 


"Epistemic Network Analysis (ENA) is a method of identifying and quantifying connections among elements in coded data and representing them in dynamic network models. A key feature of the ENA tool is that it enables researchers compare different networks, both visually and through summary statistics that reflect the weighted structure of connections. The interface allows users to see the original data that contributed to each of the connections in the network representation. ENA can thus be used to address a wide range of qualitative and quantitative research questions. Researchers have used ENA to analyze and visualize a wide range of phenomena."

                                                                                                                   ---www.epistemicnetwork.org

In this paper, Dr. Cai and his colleagues applied ENA and topic modeling as the combination to analyze chat data to detect the differences of content connections between two student groups (high/low learning gain). ENA allows researchers to compare the mean network graph in ENA space between two different groups of students. The result indicated that the low learning gain students had the stronger connection with the "people/social" topic, which was the off-task topic. While the high learning gain students had the stronger connections with the topics of "psychology, health care, biology, illness, and education", which were the on-task topics (Figure 2). This result suggested that the high learning gain students were more likely to focus on the targeting learning materials in chats than that of the low learning gain students.

Linking Language to Math Success in an On-Line Course

We were so lucky to have the PI of this project, Dr. Danielle S. McNamara, to present this study. She was so charming and funny in all her talks, and she was also one of the panelists in the Ethical and Policy Issues in EDM where she gave very impressive and strong opinions about the student data usages. 


Dr. McNamara's lab, Science of Learning and Educational Technology (SoLET), is focused on the cognitive processes in comprehension, writing and learning by the development of educational technologies for cognitive measurement and NLP. There were 3 papers from her lab got accepted in EDM2017, and they were all related to NLP. 


This blog would focus on their techniques used to measure the linguistic features of math discussion. They used several NLP tools to measure the linguistic features:

1. Automatic Analysis of Lexical Sophistication (word and multiple-word-unit level)

  • This tool analyzes the lexical frequency, range, psycholinguistic word information, and academic language. 

2. Automatic Analysis of Cohesion (sentence and paragraph level)

  • This tool utilizes a part of speech tagger and synonym sets from the WordNet lexical database
  • It counts the sentence and paragraph markers of cohesion and incorporations.  
  • It calculates the global cohesion, the logical connectives, and the sentence linking connectives. 

3. Automatic Analysis of Syntactic Sophistication and Complexity (construction level)

  • This tool measures grained clausal (31), phrasal (132), and usage-based (190) syntactic complexity and sophistication. 
  • It measures the association strengths for verb-argument construction in the text. 

4. Sentiment Analysis and Cognitive Engine (emotion level)

  • This tool analyzes the sentiment of the text through the pre-developed word vectors of social positioning, cognition dictionaries.
