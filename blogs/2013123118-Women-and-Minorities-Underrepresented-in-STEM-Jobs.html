Women and Minorities Underrepresented in STEM Jobs

rendy

December 31, 2013

Two recent studies by the U.S. Census Bureau found that women's employment in STEM has decreased from a high 34 percent in 1990 to 27 percent as of 2011. Despite women making up half of the overall workforce, only a quarter are involved in SETM jobs. According to Liana Christin Landivar, a sociologist in the Census Bureau's Industry, women employment has been increasing in STEM but they are still not present in engineering and computer occupations. Landivar also stated “women are less likely to major in engineering or computer sciences unless they go to a graduate school”. The main reason is that students don't go into STEM related jobs even if they have a college degree in a STEM major. Additionally, Blacks and Hispanics are also discounted in STEM jobs. During the research, they found that as of 2011, only 6 percent of Blacks worked in STEM occupations and the numbers were similar for Hispanics.
