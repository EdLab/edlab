Steve Van Zandt Goes Underground to Celebrate the Roots of Rock

Kate Meersschaert

November 23, 2011

To accomplish this Steve Van Zandt will interview rock legend Robbie Robertson of The Band on his, "Little Steven" Van Zandt's Underground Garage and Outlaw Country channel on Sirius. Learn more here. "Robbie Robertson Week" will begin Thursday, November 24 at 4:00 pm ET on Little Steven's Underground Garage, channel 21, with additional airing on Underground Garage and Outlaw Country, channel 60. These broadcasts will also feature the complete Last Waltz farewell concert held over 35 years ago on Thanksgiving by The Band. This farewell included such musical legends as Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton and Joni Mitchell and was later made into a documentary by Martin Scorcese. Why was The Band significant to rock history? The group has been revered by music critics and aficionados (less so by the general public) for their impact on "roots rock" and the development of a sound that was a fusion of country music, early rock and even Motown. The Band would go on to influence musicians as disparate as The Grateful Dead and The Counting Crows to Eric Clapton and many in-between. The Band's song "The Weight" was ranked the 41st best song of all time by Rolling Stone magazine, they were inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in 1994 and in 2008 the group received the Grammy's Lifetime Achievement Award.
