Preparing for Book Reviews

Dan Mallinger

October 22, 2006

To rekindle Jeff's blog as we move forward with the book review project, I'd like to learn more about how others look for and read reviews. 1. What do you look for in a book review? What makes you trust the opinion offered? Does this vary with book type? 2. How do you decide where to look for a book review in the first place? For instance, when that book review really pursuaded you to read "Conversations on Consciousness," where did you find the book review and why? 3. If you're a librarian, what makes such a site look legitimate? If you're a real person, what makes such a site enjoyable and useful? Where's the overlap?
