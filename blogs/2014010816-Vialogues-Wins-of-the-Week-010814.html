Vialogues Wins of the Week, 01.08.14

Kate Meersschaert

January 8, 2014

Private! Hi Everyone! Happy New Vialogues Year! Once again, Jo and I would like to share the week(s) "V-Wins", updates and ideas with you! Usage Stats For the extended period of 12/18 - 1/7 New Users - 39 Vialogues - 78 Visits - 7,170 Giving them something to talk about Margaret Sass from the Center of Instructional Excellence at Purdue University presented this past Saturday at a Lilly Conference on College and University Teaching and Learning in Austin, TX. Her presentation was entitled, "Service-Learning & Reflection through Social Media." She highlights Vialogues as a social media tool for reflection, as evidenced by her session handout. Our AASL flame burns on Deborah Rinio of the Alaska Association of School Librarians (AKASL) gives a run down of new apps she learned about from a recent trip to an AASL conference. I got your resource right here Carl Sandburg College lists Vialogues on its Faculty Teaching & Learning Center page. Glendale Unified School District in Glendale, CA also lists Vialogues for its teachers (under collaboration tools) It may have been subtle but we saw that nod, EdSurge In describing San Diego's High Tech High MOOC experiment, author Christina Quattrochi explains that it will rely on Vialogues "for timestamped asynchronous chatting over videos." (It's not considered snooping if it's a public CV) Dr. Curtis Bonk of Indiana University lists Vialogues on his CV as a "technology [he] used in teaching and research." Twitter Convos!
