NYC Parks Receives “Good Report Cards” Despite their Problems

Bismark Appiah

March 10, 2013

New York City parks are public recreational areas that brings people together within specific areas of the community. Creating parks within communities has to be in the government's interest because it provides a lot of advantages for citizens. That is, public parks are sometimes used for events such as Aids and Breast Cancer Walks and other social events. As such, it is important for the leaders in the community to tend to the problems these parks are facing. For the most part, one area in a park with an issue may be solved while other areas have setbacks. This is one of the points made in this article. For instance, a water fountain in a park will be working efficiently; meanwhile there might be broken benches in that same park. As a person who enjoys playing soccer under the hot sun on summer days at Macombs Dam Park, I would want the park to be in a better condition (which is actually in a good condition) before it gets good scoring grades.
