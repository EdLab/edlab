Universities Raise Concerns About Adopting Google Apps for Higher Ed

Adrienne Garber

May 6, 2010

In Ankit's recent blog post, he highlighted a Mashable article about UC Davis's decision to decline campus-wide adoption of Google Apps. In keeping with this trend, Yale University recently reported delaying the decision to formally incorporate Google Apps into their institution, citing similar reservations as UC Davis, such as security issues and Google's lack of "green" servers. For those of you with opinions about this topic, or looking to learn more about what it means for Teachers College, Academic Computing has created a site which includes a forum for discussion and is currently soliciting feedback from the TC community. To access the site (which includes a blog, slideshow, and an FAQ), login to the myTC portal. On the right side of the “Welcome” tab page, locate the link with the title “Google Apps for Education”. Click on the “Learn more and Comment here” link. If you do not have a TC account, you can also provide feedback via email to acs [at] tc [dot] edu. In addition, Academic Computing will hosting open meetings to promote discussion about Google Apps at TC. Details:

  • Who: Academic Computing
  • What: Open discussions about adoption of Google Apps for Education
  • When: Thursday, 5/6 at 12:15pm (after the faculty meeting) and Monday, 5/10 at 1:30pm
  • Where: On 5/6, in 179GD; On 5/10, in 140 HM
  • Registration: No cost to attend, no registration required.
