No Hope for Dinosaurs

gnatriello

September 3, 2005

As a follow-up to a recent conversation with Anthony and Hui Soo, I want to call attention to this article from e-Week detailing why U.S.-based firms like Accenture will ultimately lose out to Indian firms like Wipro because they are not investing enough to improve the efficiency of their operations. If we borrow this analysis and apply it to higher education, we can ask whether traditional universities are investing enough in improving the efficiency of their operations to remain viable over the long run. In this regard, the University of Phoenix provides an interesting contrast to most of the traditional players in the higher ed sector. According to a June 17, 2005 report in the Chronicle of Higher Education (U. of Phoenix Owes Rapid Growth to Use of Technology, Its President Says by Jeffrey Selingo - link omitted because a subscription is required), Phoenix is investing substantial resources to improve operations. For example, 40% of all admissions to the university are now handled without human intervention, and materials for all Phoenix courses can be delivered entirely online, including 600 e-books that have been written completely for the university. As they say, the cluetrain stops four times a day, but no one pays attention.
