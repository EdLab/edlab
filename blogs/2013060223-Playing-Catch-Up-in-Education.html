Playing "Catch Up" in Education

Francisco.Mendoza

June 2, 2013

I recently blogged about Latino students going to college at a higher rate than usual. In a speech last week, Secretary of Education Arne Duncan stated that despite their educational gains, Latinos lack behind in a key area: pre-k schooling. Last year, the Future of Children wrote an article on how access to pre-school could close the Hispanic-White school readiness gap by 26 percent. So for majority of the time, Hispanics are playing "catch up" in education due to whites already having more experience in school by the time they start their K-12 journey. In order for stop this trend, Hispanics need to increase their enrollment to pre-schools rate. As a Latino I believe that it can be challenging to like school at a young age. Starting kindergarten without knowing what to expect can be scary, and already fear can play a big role in a child's mind about school. Since they enroll in pre-K programs, white children already know what to expect and use to the idea of schooling when they start kindergarten. This article sheds more light on how Latino children lacking pre-schooling often have long term consequences. What are your thoughts?
