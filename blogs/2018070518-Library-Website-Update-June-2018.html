Library Website Update, June 2018

jvazquez

July 5, 2018

Can you believe it’s been one month since we launched our new library website and app? It’s time to check in to see how things are going. 


Today, let’s start with some interesting developments on the website. And you’re in luck -- this all-release bonus package includes commentary!  (To zero in on the main stuff, just skip over the italics.)


Overview

Total Visits: 5103 (68% off campus | 32% on campus)

Returning visitors: 2451 (48%)


On average, users spent 5 mins 27s on the site, completing 6 actions per visit. 


Overall, there was more activity on the site coming from off campus, something we’ll see repeated throughout this report in most cases. 


Most visited page (aside from homepage): Resources (58% off campus | 42% on campus)

Second most visited page: Services (56% off campus | 44% on campus)


No surprise there! But what happens when we go a level deeper and look at the top subpages?

Search behavior 

Total Searches: 4,791 searches (70% off campus | 30% on campus)

In this latest release, we introduced the ability to sort searches by category - Everything, Articles, Books, Journals, Website, and Knowledge Base - hoping it would connect people to their desired resources faster. 

When people chose to filter by category (which accounted for only 30% of search behavior), here’s what they preferred:
 
Articles (30% of filtered searches used Articles)
Books (27% of filtered searches used Books)
Everything (20%) (meaning they toggled back to this tab)
Journals (17% of filtered searches used Journals)
Website (4% of filtered searches used Website) 
Knowledge Base (2% of filtered searches used KB)

So, does our new search help people find what they’re looking for better than before? 

Jury’s still out but let’s just say, I admire the persistence of our patrons. 

(These are my observations based on a few search logs; not enough to cause a stir but just enough to float onto a radar.)

First, people like to make very specific searches, searching either the title of a book or full reference (including author names and year). This means these people know exactly what they’re looking for.

One patron made several attempts to find a book by its title, searching both in Everything and in Books. I tried it myself and also couldn’t find it, but it’s the first result when searched on EDUCAT directly. The title is “Handbook of Critical Race Theory in Education”, if you want to play along at home. 

Similarly, another patron tried to search by book title and couldn’t find it. Then they added the author’s name…. and still nothing. The same search on EDUCAT produced a top result. The book title is, “The Death and Life of the Great American School System” by Diane Ravitch. 

One last example of this phenomenon is with the ebook, “The Black Child-savers: Racial Democracy and Juvenile Justice.” This one also wasn’t found on our main search, but when you “view and filter results”, Summon has it as its top hit. 

And then there were people who almost got it right, but weren't close enough for our search to be able to help them. For example, in using a title to search, one patron typed “masterpiece” instead of the actual word, “masterwork” and their desired result wasn’t found. However, when they used the right word, their result was the top hit. This one was “Chris Peterson's Unfinished Masterpiece” (wrong) vs “Masterwork" (right). 

But the good news is that with all of these real searches, our “Matching Terms” are only getting better!

This is my favorite finding of the month. In our previous room booking system, patrons identified how many participants would be at their event by selecting from a range (i.e., 1-4, 5-8, 9-35). Now patrons pick a number from 1-8. This means there’s no more guessing-- we know exactly how many people are using a room at a given time. This month, most rooms were made up of singles. Now, once the school year starts up again this may change things, but for now our summer patrons are enjoying their solitude.
