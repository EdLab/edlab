Newseum: A Media-Rich Learning Space

EdLab Studios

July 1, 2015


The Newseum is an interactive museum of news and journalism in Washington D.C. Through partnerships with corporate sponsors in the media industry, it has developed a robust set of interactive, media-rich galleries dedicated to teaching visitors of all ages and backgrounds about the history of news in America and the history of America through the news. All media is controlled through a central nerve center, which looks like a broadcast control room and is an exhibit in and of itself. Through iterative design and extensive learning research, each space is designed to engage the visitors on multiple levels, no matter what their comfort level with technology. The physical space itself is augmented by online learning tools and media literacy workshops for teachers and students that adhere to common core standards.
