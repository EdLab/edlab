Spoken Lecture

Anthony Cocciolo

October 31, 2008

An interesting project Hui Soo and I ran across as EDUCAUSE is the Spoken Lecture project, which uses speech recognition as a way of searching across lecture materials. This project is very similar to Meety in a number of ways, most notably that you can search across video footage for keywords. However, it is a bit different because it really makes the dictation salient in the user interface, where the dictation is somewhat buried in Meety (which was a design decision based on the fact that automated speech dictation is nowhere near 100% reliable). It also uses the Real Player, which is much more annoying than Flash, as well as it doesn't allow you to capture video on the fly, or generate external research resources. However, it does a nice job with tying in the dictation with the video footage, which Meety doesn't do well.
