Facebook's Social Movie Experiment

Kate Meersschaert

August 19, 2011

Now you can "talk" during a movie... and not get kicked-out of the theater or off the couch. In March Warner Bros. started a movie rental service on Facebook offering the Dark Knight through the movie's Facebook page. This "Social Theater" experiment continues to gain traction with NBC Universal's recent FaceBook release of cult favorite, The Big Lebowski. Movies can be rented for Facebook Credits, the virtual currency gaining popularity on the social network for the purchase of online goods & games. Rentals also have a standard 48-hour rental window and are viewable only through a player on a Facebook page. Facebook describes the features of this new interactive movie experience, "Users that rent the film can “like” and make comments on memorable scenes and quotes throughout the film. They can also see what other Facebook fans and friends have said while watching the movie." See the infographic above for more on the interactive social features of this film-to-Facebook pairing! Many of the social features of this new service remind me of Vialogues, primarily the ability to share comments around video. However, comments appear to be streaming in real-time and not time-stamped for annotation. I will have to actually try the service before making an official comparison, but from the Mashable article it seems that Vialogues still remains uniquely embeddable, private, time stamped, and as an added bonus, FREE! Perhaps in the future we can consider licensed content and a payment schedule to "rent" content? What are your thoughts on Facebook's attempts to make movie watching a more social experience?
