Google Profiles to be made either public or deleted

Megha Agarwala

July 6, 2011

Google has announced that all private Google profiles will be deleted after July 31, 2011. They suggest that the emphasis of Google profiles is to help people find and connect with one another, which requires all profiles to be made public. By default, only your name and gender are the required public information. If you don't want other information to be public, you dont put it. If you have a private profile, and you don't chose to make it public, it will simply be deleted after July 31. Here is the information from the Google help page and here is the related article on Mashable. This highlights some questions that we can also place in the context of Ednode. What kind of connection building do you foster with public profiles? How can we strike a balance between having enough information to build connections, but at the same time giving a lot of room for privacy? And finally, how much control should be given to the user about his own information?
