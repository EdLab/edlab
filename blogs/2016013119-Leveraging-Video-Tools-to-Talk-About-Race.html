Leveraging Video Tools to Talk About Race

dinosossi2

January 31, 2016


Racial issues are always near the surface and can be difficult to speak about in a respectful manner given our politically polarized environment, especially in educational contexts. Regardless of the goodwill of participants, it is challenging to explore the nuances of racial conflict in a way that puts all stakeholders at ease.

The #Blacklivesmatter movement is encouraging expanded conversations about race and social justice, both online and in the classroom. This Vialogue examines the discomfort that White students have when engaging in the topic by measuring the degree to which they feel tense, calm, or focused throughout conversations about race; it contrasts their reactions when confronted with race to those of people of color.

Do you think using these types of measurements are a useful starting point for engaging in this conversation? How else can you incorporate technology to discuss issues related to race? Tell us about your thoughts on racial discourse in educational settings in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
