InstaGrok

chingfu

October 21, 2013


instaGrok is a search engine tailored for learners that features concept visualization and research capacities. Learners put keywords of their interests into the search box, and a graph of connected concept circles along with supporting materials (such as websites, videos, images, and glossary) on the side panel will be provided in a "grok". Learners can keep journals as they learn various concepts, relationships, and facts from each concept graph. Teachers can also subscribe to advanced services and use the dashboard to scaffold and monitor students’ learning process with instaGrok.

Pros:

One of the biggest strengths of instaGrok is its visualization of search results. Each interactive grok enables learners to grasp a big picture of the search topic and zoom in to gain deeper understanding of different components of the topic. The difficulty slider allows learners to adjust contents to fit their comprehension capacities. A set of evaluative questions for each source can help learners to be aware of the credibility and reliability of these online sources.

Cons:

Not all of the resources found and recommended in the groks are reliable and credible. In addition, some concept circles seem less relevant or are of little importance to understanding the topic. Furthermore, there is no easy or intuitive way for users to add useful online resources in their groks (except in their journals).

Our Takeaway:

Learning scientists have found that organized-knowledge and fundamental frameworks within each field are important assets for expert learners in their thinking process. Helping novice learners to establish conceptual frameworks beyond simply searching and consuming information online is critical to enabling them to build expertise and learn advanced research skills. InstaGrok provides a visual and interactive approach to do this. With careful guidance and filtering from instructors, it would have great potential to help learners gain the fundamentals for expert thinking.

The Bottom Line:

InstaGrok is a good starting point for developing concept maps for students’ research projects.
