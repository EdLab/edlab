Are Gap Years Worth It?

ABARRY

May 12, 2016

A year without tests, classrooms and textbooks sound like paradise, but is it worth it? Many recent high school graduates are taking a gap year, a yearlong break between high school and college. The idea is for students to go on adventures, develop new life skills and discover a hidden passion. Malia Obama, the president's daughter, is one of the latest (and most high profile) students to take a gap year. The specific reason behind her deferral is yet to be determined. Despite its increasingly popularity, gap year is still a controversial issue. Opponents believe it's a waste of time. However, higher education experts have indicated students who take a year off tend to do better than their non-gap-year classmates and manage to choose better satisfying careers. I graduated high school last June, and I'm currently on my gap year. The reasons behind my decision on deferring a year are somehow similar to others. The main reason I took a year off was my age, I graduated at 16 years old. My parents and I felt I was not ready to manage the maturity required in college.
