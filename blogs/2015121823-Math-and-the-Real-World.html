Math and the Real World

idrissa10

December 18, 2015

In high school, I remember constantly asking what’s the purpose of learning trigonometry? Like most high school students, I didn’t understand what I was learning trigonometry and how that would be useful to me in professional life. Well I received the answer this semester when I took a class aptly titled Ideas in Mathematics. The class focused on the application and history behind mathematics. It covered how the use of simple mathematics could lead to million dollar ideas and innovative technology. For our final project, we had to research the role of mathematics in everyday life. I focused on how probability affects us everyday. I based my research and focus on the probability of gambling. I found the gambling industry makes more money than the music and movie industries combined. Casinos bring in revenue of about 37 billion while the movie industry and music industry bring in combined revenue of about 17 billion. That's 10 billion for movies and seven for the music industry. This is all thanks to simple probability. Casinos use peoples' judgment and confidence and combine with probability to bring in millions. I learned how geometry is necessary in pool and how math could be used in poker. Overall the class changed my perception of math forever. Although it did not persuade me enough to major in math, the class made me more appreciative of the subject.
