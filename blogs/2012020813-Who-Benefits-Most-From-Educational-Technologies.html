Who Benefits Most From Educational Technologies?

Lu Wang

February 8, 2012

A recent article on education technology poses a reflective question on who benefited most from education technologies in the classroom. While some people proclaimed that technology in the classroom failed to improve student performance but only cost a huge amount of money, this article exemplified how both educators and students benefitted from high-tech gadgets. However, good teachers in the classroom who would be able to do excellent job in coordinating, organizing, and facilitating students' learning activities are still essential, especially in improving the students' test scores. EdLab Relevance: While we are developing new technology applications and trying to promote them into classroom settings, it's also important to provide training to teachers on how to use these technologies. The training session would not only focus on the A-B-C's of using a newly developed application, but also how to integrate the using of this application to the teacher's education ideology. The ideal goal is to make teachers really fall in love with the new technology gadgets, which help to accomplish, rather than takes the place of, their teaching methods and education goals.
