Live Blog: IBM Research/Teachers College Collaboration Exploration Meeting

HSC

April 9, 2014

* PRIVATE Bios of the Watson team members for Education Transformation CHALAPATHY NETI: Chalapathy Neti is currently the Director of Education Transformation, leading IBM's global research to define and develop information technology innovations to transform education towards a more personalized, outcome-based and low-cost system. Prior to this role he was the Director and Global Leader, Healthcare Transformation, at IBM Research (2010-2012). In this role he co-led an IBM wide strategy to define IBM's approach to healthcare transformation and led an orchestrated innovation effort across the worldwide team, culminating in the launch of foundational solutions like IBM Patient Care and Insights in 2012. Prior to this role, he held a variety of senior management and technical positions in IBM Research and IBM business divisions: Executive Architect/Consultant (2009) in IBM Software Group to drive consultative sales of Information Management solutions to healthcare institutions; Senior Manager of Information Analysis and Interaction (2005-2008) technologies, managing several research groups to develop text, image and video analysis technologies and its application to Bio-medical Informatics, Medical Imaging and Decision intelligence (e.g. Customer Intelligence, Fraud detection and Video Surveillance); CTO of IBM's Digital Media business (2004) leading the development of Digital media management offerings; Manager of audio-visual speech technologies (2001-2003) to lead the development of rich media analysis (speech, audio and video) and mining. He has been with IBM since 1990. Chalapathy Neti received his Ph.D. degree in Biomedical Engineering from The Johns Hopkins University (1990), and B.S degree from Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur (1980). He has more than 25 years of advanced R&D, sales and consulting experience and has authored over 50 scientific publications and 16 patents in various fields related to bio-medical informatics, medical imaging, speech and video analysis, Computational Neuroscience and VLSI Design. He was an active contributor to Institute of Medicine on Evidence-based Medicine and Learning Healthcare Systems. He was a co-organizer of the National Cancer Policy Forum/Institute of Medicine Workshop on “A Foundation for Evidence-Driven Practice: A Rapid Learning System for Cancer Care”(2009), and IOM workshop on “Improving the clinical data utility” (2009). He was a former member (2008-2010) of the Executive Review Board of MI3C (Medical Imaging Informatics Innovation Center — a collaboration between IBM and Mayo Clinic). He is an active contributor to the scientific discourse on the role of I/T in healthcare as a panelist/speaker including Health 2.0 (2011), NIH conference on “Advancing Rare Diseases Research” (2010) and the Congressional Panel on “How I/T is changing Medical Research” (2009). He represented IBM strategy in healthcare transformation through media interviews and social media presence (http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I7aPPP9nosY&feature=related). He serves on the Editorial Board of IEEE Lifesciences Portal. He is a former member of IEEE Multimedia Signal Processing technical committee (2001-2004), an associate editor of IEEE transactions on Multimedia (2002-2005) and a guest editor for the IBM Systems Journal. He is currently a member of the IBM Academy of Technology (an association of the top IBM technical leaders). SARA BASSON Sara Basson works at IBM Research on the Education Transformation initiative, which strives to achieve better academic outcomes using personalized learning and analytics. Her particular focus is developing technologies and solutions to improve career pathways. In 2011, she completed an international assignment at IBM Research-India, where she was working on strategy, business, and user interface design issues for Spoken Web, a tool that created internet-like access for developing regions, using mobile phones and speech recognition. In previous roles, she has driven strategy and business opportunities for speech transcription technology and for accessibility solutions. Sara has more than 50 articles published on topics in speech technology and accessibility in technical journals, as well as a number of pending and issued patents. She was granted the title of Master Inventor in IBM Research in 2011. Throughout her professional career, Sara has had a passion for advancing education and increasing access to educational opportunities. She launched a tutoring program for underserved children during her tenure in India. She has also served as principal investigator from IBM Research to drive improved educational access for university students with disabilities through the Liberated Learning consortium, working with 20 universities worldwide. She has also taught English as a Second Language to university students in the US and abroad. Sara holds an MBA in Management/Marketing from Stern School of Business, New York University, and a Ph.D. in Speech, Hearing, and Language Sciences from The Graduate Center of the City University of New York. She also holds an Honorary Doctorate degree from Saint Mary's University in Halifax, Nova Scotia, in recognition of her extensive activities using speech technologies to enhance accessibility in the educational arena. Sara is on the Executive Board of Directors of AVIOS, a speech technology applications professional organization; she served as President of the Board of Directors in 2000 -2001. Sara is also on the editorial board of the International Journal of Speech Technology. She serves on the Board of Directors of Meals on Wheels of White Plains, and on the Watson Diversity Council at IBM Research. RICK HULL: Rick Hull is a Senior Research Scientist at the IBM T.J. Watson Research Center, a position he took in May, 2008. Hull has broad research and innovation interests in the areas of data and information management, workflow and business processes, web and converged services, and more recently, education transformation and applications of text analytics. Between 2008 and 2013 Hull led team at IBM Research working on data-centric business process management, which has spawned research by the international communities focused on BPM, on data management, and on AI; provided the foundation for the recent OMG Case Management Model and Notation (CMMN) standard; and formed the basis for the IBM Case Management product. Prior to joining IBM Research, Hull spent 12 years at Bell Labs Research, a division of (now) Alcatel-Lucent; before that he was a professor of Computer Science at the University of Southern California. While at Bell Labs, in addition to pursuing research on semantic web services and personalization, Hull was instrumental in developing and transferring new technologies into Alcatel-Lucent's product line, including the Vortex policy engine and the Datagrid data integration tool. Over the years, Hull's research has been supported in part by grants from NSF, DARPA, and AT&T. Hull is co-author of the book "Foundations of Databases" (Addison-Wesley, 1996); has published over 140 refereed articles in journals, conferences and books; and holds seven U.S. patents. Hull was named Bell Labs Fellow in 2005 and ACM Fellow in 2007. SATYA NITTA Dr. Satya V. Nitta is currently the manager of the Emerging Technologies research group at IBM's T J Watson Research Center where he is focusing on applications of cognitive computing. Previously, he spent a year as Technical Advisor to the office of the Vice President of Science and Technology for IBM Research Worldwide where he helped set the strategy for IBM's technology and systems roadmaps. As manager and technical leader of the Advanced Interconnect Technology area at the IBM T.J. Watson Research Center in Yorktown Heights, NY he led large teams of scientists and engineers which invented and defined several generations of IBM's chip technologies ranging from the 65nm node to the 7nm node. He has also participated as an author and lead scientist on IBM's Global Technology Outlook, which forecasts the major emerging trends in the computing industry. Dr. Nitta received a PhD from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Chemical Engineering in 1998 and has been at IBM since 1999 during which time he has served as a technologist and a technical leader of teams which developed several new aspects of on-chip interconnect technology. He was named as the IEEE ACE “Innovator of the Year” in 2008 and also won an IEEE Ace Award for “Technology of the Year” in 2008 as well as an IEEE Spectrum award for Technology Winner of the Year in 2008. Dr. Nitta serves as one of IBM's technical liaisons to Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute where he is a member of the Industrial Advisory boards for the Rensselaer Nanotechnology Center as well as the Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering. He has authored or co-authored over thirty publications, one book chapter, and currently holds over 70 US patents with over 40 patent applications pending at the USPTO. His current interests are in cognitive computing, digitization of education, silicon technology, and alternative energy technologies. HANY SALEEB Dr. Saleeb is a Program Director in Business Development at IBM Research. His responsibilities include commercializing global research assets through client engagements, researching market dynamics for internal product strategies and identifying areas of growth. His recent work has focused on opportunities in analytics and risk management in the Education and Finance industries. He led highly successful initiatives at GE Asset Management, GE Treasury and SwissRe. He has taught various courses at New York University, MIT and Harvard in Data Mining, Project Management and Information Systems. He holds a PhD in Computer Science from Harvard, a M.Eng in Computer Science from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and a MBA in Finance from New York University., ROBERT SCHLOSS Robert J (Bob) Schloss, Senior Technical Staff Member, Cognitive Knowledge & Information Systems department Bob joined IBM T J Watson Research Center in 1982, after product development experience with IBM in the Hudson Valley which followed his degree in Mathematics and Computer Science from Yale. Bob's life was changed through an amazing NSF-funded summer experience he had during high school. Bob is an expert in metadata and semantic data models, having made major contributions to, or lead the standardization and open-source implementation and IBM proprietary implementation of content labelling standards (W3C Platform for Internet Content Selection, Dublin Core, Resource Description Framework (RDF), Extensible Markup Language (XML), and prefers to approach new domains (in the last 5 years, the domains he worked intensively with include Smart Power Grid, Smarter Cities Operations Centers, and now Education) by learning about what behaviors by people and by applications product and consumer what kinds of information, how that information is distributed, how the credibility of that information improves or decays over time, and then adjusting web technologies (whether URIs, CSV, RDF, XML, JSON, XMI, OSLC, OWL, REST APIs, Query Languages, Rules Languages or whatever) to be as simple as possible for those people, organizations and applications, while meeting requirements and allowing for "as the system continues to run... new attributes and new entity types and new cross-entity relationships can be introduced". He is currently evaluating the potential of Linked Data to be used by IMS Global standards and by IBM's IDEAL software for Education. Bob is a senior member of the ACM and of the IEEE. Bob's technology is used in the infrastructure for IBM offering's as diverse as Watson, Websphere, DB2, InfoSphere Information Server, Intelligent Operations Center... and he is currently working on enhancements to the IBM Filenet and IBM Content Analytics offerings. Bob was a major contributor to the 2013 and 2014 IBM "Global Technology Outlook" positions on Contextual Computing, Cognitive Computing, and Data Services in the Cloud and believes these ideas apply strongly to the Education and Learning domain He is a member of IBM's Academy of Technology, the cross-company body of IBM's most active technical innovators. He was a member of the National Research Council's 30-month study group which produced the book "Youth, Pornography and the Internet" (National Academy of Sciences Press), and continues to be interested in how people's use of information, applications and communications in "cyberspace" changes them. LIN ZHOU: Dr. Lin Zhou has over 10 years of experience managing large complex global initiatives. He earned his PhD from the University of Glasgow UK and a Master Certificate from the Business School of George Washington University. Lin has worked at a number of leading U.S. hi-tech companies, holding senior positions in both program and people management. He is currently the Program Director of Education Transformation, leveraging Watson Power for Smarter Education. He has authored several patents and papers, and lectured at international conferences. Lin developed and taught courses on Program Management at a number of universities. He has been a frequent reviewer of publications for academic journals, and a recipient of many technical awards.
