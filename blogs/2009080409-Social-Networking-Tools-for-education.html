Social Networking Tools for education

fanwar

August 4, 2009

Recently, I came across a new Social Networking platform called Elgg. It is an open source social networking suite that you can download, install and modify as you like on your local network. In that way, you can think of it as analogous to what Moodle does, only in the social networking domain. We've installed the platform on one of the Edlab servers and tried it out. We've found that the Elgg system itself is a little rough right now, but the open-source element does allow for making improvements. Related to this tool is a conference/workshop being held in Cambridge, MA on social networking technologies for education. The Elgg platform, the conference, and other related social networking developments recently prompt many questions to consider: - Where are educational platforms with respect to their support for social networking today? For those that do incorporate this type of web presence, how is it done? - Have educators and developers thought of learning platforms around many of the ideas related to the social benefits of schooling (ie. that beyond just taking courses, there is a value in the peers you meet, the alumni network you are linked to, etc.). - What would an ideal online learning platform look like that strikes the right balance with respect to helping students learn and gain skills through courses and other means while also allowing them to take full advantage of the networks available to them at a particular educational institution.
