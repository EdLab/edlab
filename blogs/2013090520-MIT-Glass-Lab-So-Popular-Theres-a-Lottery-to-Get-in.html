MIT Glass Lab: So Popular, There’s a Lottery to Get in

Mable Yiu

September 5, 2013


At MIT, students have the opportunity to take classes at the MIT Glass Lab, a place where people from various backgrounds and skills come together to learn the art of glass blowing. Glass blowing is one of the few craft arts that actually requires team collaboration in the lab; students work together taking turns to blow into the pipe and shape the molten glass, while also improvising to create different shapes with different colors.

MIT undergraduate and graduate students alike make up the glass community, along with alumni and glass art professionals, who take on the role as instructors. In this video, students discuss their experiences at the Glass Lab and an MIT Materials Science professor and creator of the Glass Lab, Michael Cima, discusses how the "Glass Band" was created. This student-driven band uses different glass shapes and instruments to create music (watch how the band experiments with their music here). Not only do they experiment with what sounds they can produce using a range of techniques, they also perform their music to spread creativity and inspiration around campus.
