NASA Challenge Has Students Launching Mini-satellites into Deep Space

saraehardman

October 15, 2017


Citizen scientists and engineers raced to complete a NASA challenge over the course of the last two years. The competition was open to high schools, universities, and nonprofit organizations and required teams to design, build, and deliver a small satellite that can communicate with earth from deep space. These satellites, called cubesats, are barely larger than a loaf of bread, yet must be designed to travel around the moon and beyond, all while collecting data and transmitting it back to scientists.

The Cube Quest Challenge features four stages and will distribute $5 million among its winners. This past summer, three finalists were named, two of which were university teams. These finalists will compete in one final round: sending their cubesats into deep space. Final awards will be given for orbiting the moon, communicating the fastest and farthest, and surviving the longest. This competition provides an opportunity for citizen inventors and students to contribute to NASA, as well as the ongoing quest for space exploration.
