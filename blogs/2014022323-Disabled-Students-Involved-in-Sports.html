Disabled Students Involved in Sports

AbdulMalik

February 23, 2014

There is always misconceptions by most people that individuals cannot be keen participants in sports if they a disability. It is not really the case that disability hinders participation in sports. Most people with disabilities still have the mental ability to learn strategies and actually compete in sporting activities as well. However it is quite evident that most schools do not acknowledge that some disabled students want to participate in sports and compete with others. This article gives a brief overview about how disabled students could engage in sports. For example, the civil rights division of Washington's Education Department explicitly tells schools and colleges that access to interscholastic, intramural and intercollegiate athletics is a right for all students. This emphasizes how important it is to consider that a disability should not hinder the ability of an individual to participate in sports.
