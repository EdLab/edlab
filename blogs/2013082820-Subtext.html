Subtext

Demetri Lales

August 28, 2013


Subtext is an iPad app that enables discussion between teachers and students within the actual pages of an ebook. Teachers can highlight specific passages in the book to start new discussions, meaningfully tied to particular words or phrases. Subtext also encourages teachers to post reading-relevant content from the web and create assignments and quizzes. Students can share ideas and collaborate to understand what they are reading and develop their own ideas about the reading. Teachers can create their own "closed groups" to invite their students to read and interact with a digital book in complete privacy.

Pros:

The option of private discussion is an essential feature for teachers. Data on studentsâ€™ activities is kept private and provided to teachers for analysis. Instructors can digitally distribute quizzes through the app. Sign-in through Facebook or Google accounts makes getting started easy for students. Teachers have similar facilitation through Edmodo.

Cons:

Subtext is only available on the iPad. Making it available for Android devices and accessible on the web is an obvious next step. While student access to mobile devices has increased, most classrooms are not equipped with iPads.

Our Takeaway:

Subtext is an app that literature educators can use to promote efficient dialogue-based learning. The most interesting aspect of the app is that it has a point system to reward social actions on the site. Users can trade these points for prizes in the app to encourage them to be more active in the discussions. Subtext also lets users pull articles from the web and facilitates discussions around particular sections of articles. This functionality makes learning more relevant to students who can select articles that matter to them and discuss or take quizzes on self-selected content. The app should emphasize this feature as it is a way for teachers and students to have a private conversation about real world articles.

The Bottom Line:

Subtext encourages discussions around sections of literature.
