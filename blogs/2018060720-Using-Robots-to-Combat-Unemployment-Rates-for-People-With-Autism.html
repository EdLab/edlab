Using Robots to Combat Unemployment Rates for People With Autism

saraehardman

June 7, 2018


In both the US and UK, over 80% of adults with autism are unemployed. This statistic isn’t necessarily based on lack of skill, but rather on the difficulty many adults with autism have reading emotions in others. People on the autism spectrum often struggle to read facial expressions and are more likely to misread even the most basic emotions, such as happiness or anger. This can cause difficulties in interactions with coworkers and understanding the requests of supervisors.

To combat this problem, researchers at Heriot-Watt University in Edinburgh, Scotland created the socially competent robot, Alyx. Alyx teaches simplified social cues to adults with autism to help them learn which expressive signals matter and which don’t. During a training session with Alyx, an adult learner completes a basic clerical task, then watches as Alyx responds with either approval or disapproval. The participant will learn how to recognize basic facial expressions associated with each feeling, and the research team has confidence that this will translate to real-life circumstances.
