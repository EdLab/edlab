Trends in Ed, 3.20.09

Arianna Choi

March 20, 2009

Another Online Student Writing Aid A start-up backed by Microsoft launched the public beta test for Eduify, a writing aid application targeted at high school and college students. It is one of the first to use Microsoft's Azure Services Platform, a cloud-based application framework. One of the features also lined up for the near future is the ability to scan a book's ISBN and automatically insert the citation into a word document. Will Eduify be the next Google Docs meets End Notes — also meets Proof Writer? Read more on what tech media based in Seattle have to say about it. WikiLeaks & Australian Censorship Just yesterday, WikiLeaks exposed a blacklist of web pages that the Australian government was secretly considering to block access. A statement by WikiLeaks said, "This week saw Australia joining China and the United Arab Emirates as the only countries censoring WikiLeaks." Read more. Today marks the official first day of spring this year, happy sprouting (to come)! Better education is indeed in everyone's interest and the link is obvious: it may be hard to get experience out of ideas, but it helps when you get ideas out of experience. - Quoted from Global Education 20/20: What role for the private sector? — an Economist Intelligence Unit report (March 2009)
