Library Services, Spring 2018: A Semester Report in Search of a Theme

Allen.Foresta

May 13, 2019

During Spring 2018, Services librarians consulted with students on some of the following research topics and fields of inquiry:

  • Adaptations of interpersonal psychotherapy to Arab countries.
  • Educational opportunities at Columbia University that were available to women during the 19th century.
  • Racial diversity in higher education music education faculties.
  • Cognitive studies and education: managing group dynamics in an interdisciplinary research environment.
  • Impact evaluations of university-school partnerships.
  • Searching for examples of teaching a core concept in any discipline (e.g., the concept of nation in history, teaching of the scientific method).
  • Cultural policy:  Costa Rica and nonprofit organizations and relationship to sustainability; social change and culture in Costa Rica.
  • The effect that education has on political inequality and participatory democracy in America, drawing from economics, political science, and education to track specific effects within education: civics classes, focus on standardized testing (so curriculums more focused on math and reading), a change in the teaching process and classroom environment, peer effects.
  • The educational barriers that Latino migrant students face in the United States, specifically migrant students in Oregon and the Pacific Northwest.
  • The connection between the cognitive/psychological and managerial styles in educational (pedagogical/learning/research/academic) groups (communities/environments).
  • Leadership development post-graduate programs from Brazilian elementary public school principals' perspective.
  • Adult literacy statistics for the U.S. since 2003, including poverty, demographics, incarceration.
During Spring 2018, Library Services provided support and assistance to College students as follows:
  • Senior librarians and services associates responded to a total of ____ in-person and phone queries.
  • We responded to 662 research- and information-related support requests, including reference questions, requests for one-on-one research consultations, inquiries concerning library hours and operations, questions and problems pertaining to room reservations, and circulation issues.
  • Librarians provided 46 research consultations to individuals or small groups.
  • Senior librarians and services associates presented seven course-specific library information sessions, either in the classroom or in library spaces, for a total of 87 attendees.
