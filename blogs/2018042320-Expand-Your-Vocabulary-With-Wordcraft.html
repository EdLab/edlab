Expand Your Vocabulary With Wordcraft

Ryan.Allen

April 23, 2018


Have you seen those genius kids at the Scripps National Spelling Bee spell some impossibly obscure word because they knew the linguistic root? Wordcraft is an app that helps people learn this skill and more. It’s a vocabulary-building platform that utilizes Greek and Latin roots to gamify learning.

Pros:

The creators of Wordcraft claim that the platform will allow students to "learn the entire dictionary" for just $3.99. The app contains six different subject areas: ESL, Math, GRE, SAT/ACT, Biology, and Everything. Each section includes quiz-like games that help users learn words by identifying their Latin or Greek roots. Below is a sample question:

TWISTing, either of one’s body or the manipulation of truth

CON ____ ION

  1. PLAT (flat)
  2. ORTHO (straight)
  3. PO (drink)
  4. DENDR (tree)
  5. TORT (twist)
  6. ARIST (noble)

The app even has interactive multiplayer features that are helpful for educators. Students and teachers can track progress, accuracy, and ranking of usage indicators. Registered classes or schools can compete with one another or even with others around the globe. If a student needs help, they can send a message with the app’s questions embedded in a text.

Finally, the design of the app is simple and elegant. The beautiful drawings that accompany quizzes will help students memorize the vocabulary quicker.

Cons:

Wordcraft is an impressive vocabulary-building tool, but it is somewhat limited. Knowing Greek and Latin roots will not account for the full range of English vocabulary, which includes words derived from many other languages.

The app might also be too slow for students who already have a fairly advanced vocabulary and understanding of the English language. Users must start from the basic levels and cannot diverge from the established paths in each subject area. A more versatile vocab-building tool might be a better option for advanced studies.

Our Takeaway:

While the app is currently in public beta, it’s still a solid and innovative option for vocabulary building. Students gearing up for various college entrance exams will certainly want to check it out. Likewise, the app will help non-native English speakers demystify some of the building blocks to the language. However, because English borrows from more languages than just Greek and Latin, there are some limits to Wordcraft’s utility.
