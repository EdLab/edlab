Financial Disincentive. The U.S. Army and White Hat Hacking

dinosossi2

July 30, 2017


Taking the moral high ground is all fine and good, but money pays the bills.

And if you are a programmer, hacking is an awfully lucrative, though morally gig.

But in an attempt to stop today’s youth from going bad, the U.S. army is attempting to get kids to act ethically at the start.

Their DEFCON camp is training the next generation of ethical hackers, those who are colloquially known as white hat hackers.

Although the army's effort is greatly appreciated and will almost undoubtedly lead to some youngsters who choose to do the right thing, black hat hacking is almost too financially attractive not to explore those who embrace the hacker ethos.

Also, in today’s nihilistic times, mucking about in a third-party system seems like the cool thing to do for today’s detached teens.

Only the future knows what will result…
