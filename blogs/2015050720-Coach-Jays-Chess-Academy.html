Coach Jay’s Chess Academy

Ting Yuan

May 7, 2015


Coach Jay’s Chess Academy is an iOS and Android app that teaches chess through a series of puzzles. Because the app breaks the complex game into different levels, players are able to practice each aspect of the game separately with targeted lessons for checkmate, defense, endgame, strategy, and tactics. Overall, the app provides over 150 lessons and over 1,250 different chess puzzles. The basic app is free, but users can access all the locked chess puzzles for $4.99.

Pros:

The app's interface is full of fun graphics and succinct in-game adaptive tutorials that show up as sidebar notes. The app stands out from other chess games because, instead of just staging in-game competitions, it teaches the basics of chess playing from rules to strategies as part of a progressive learning system. The app's method provides interactive short lessons that focus on a single idea, followed by a series of increasingly difficult puzzles that reinforce the concept before a player gets to the next lesson.

Cons:

Though Coach Jay’s Chess Academy is a good space to learn and master the fundamentals of chess playing, the focus on theory over practice might bore players motivated by actually playing the game. The app is not designed to support matches and there are no options for players to communicate virtually with each other.

Our Takeaway:

Coach Jay’s Chess Academy is a low-cost educational app for beginners. It can be used in both home and school settings. Its adaptive features support differentiated learning and could help promote children’s abstract reasoning, critical thinking, and problem solving.
