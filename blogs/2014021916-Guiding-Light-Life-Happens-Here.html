Guiding Light: Life Happens Here

Brian.Sweeting

February 19, 2014

After hearing some confused conference attendees ask for directions to Thorndike Hall this morning, I thought that a platform like MIT Media Lab's augmented reality app, Guiding Light, could be helpful for simultaneously assisting first time TC visitors and keeping the buildings sign-free.

The system consists of a badge with magnetic sensors and a software app that can project arrows onto the floor to guide people to their destination, making use of a projector built into their smartphone.
In addition to pairing this technology with existing apps like TC Route Finder to navigate the college, it could have other uses, like helping patrons locate books or connecting students with study groups and subject experts. Or potentially be incorporated into a 4th floor learning theater? Would you use an app like this or is it too much? More on Guiding Light
