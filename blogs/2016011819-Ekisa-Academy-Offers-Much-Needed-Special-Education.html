Ekisa Academy Offers Much-Needed Special Education

Jenny Shen

January 18, 2016


In 2011 USAID reported that there were more than 184,000 children in Uganda with learning disabilities. Although there have been initiatives and programs that seek to bring more inclusive schooling to the country, Uganda and many other countries across the world still lack the necessary resources to educate students with disabilities.

Ekisa Academy is one school that specifically caters to students with disabilities, currently enrolling a total of 45 children. The school focuses on on motor, daily living, and language skills throughout the day. And they always leave time for sports, too!

"Special education is not very common in Uganda," says Emily Henderson, Head Teacher at Ekisa Academy. "The majority of children with special needs are getting no education right now."
