Apple Not Only Takes-on Textbooks, But Redesigns iTunes U

Kate Meersschaert

January 23, 2012

Apple's iTunes U has gotten a powerful new makeover. No longer just a repository for user and institution-generated course content, Apple has introduced--as a part of their much anticipated iBooks Author & iBooks for iPad announcements last week--a new suite of tools for iTunes U that allow instructors and users to upload all the resources necessary to support online learning. For example, an instructor can design and/or upload video, audio and iBooks in addition, to creating a newly designed app to allow easier distribution and download of this content. Do you feel that this new development is a natural extension of Apple's iTextbook announcement?
