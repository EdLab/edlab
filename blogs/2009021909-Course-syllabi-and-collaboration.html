Course syllabi and collaboration

Adam Mitchinson

February 19, 2009

Recently, as part of the development work on LiveSyllabus I have been conducting research into the various tools and materials that are available to help educators produce and implement course syllabi. Surprisingly there is not much out there! The majority of materials and tools that I have found predominantly serve two main functions: 1) to increase the knowledge of the syllabus creation process, and 2) to facilitate in the syllabus creation process. My search is by no means exhaustive and so if anyone knows of some cool tools out there please post them.

One interesting article I found was this discussion on creating a ‘collaborative syllabus'. The author uses Moodle to survey course students and uses their feedback to refine and edit the elements of the syllabus that he sees as “negotiable”. I like the idea that this collaborative process could improve the sense of ownership for students and increase perceived procedural fairness. However, the fact that this is not practiced more suggests that there are some negatives overlooked in the article — for example, what do involving students in the syllabus creation process do to the teacher's authority in the classroom? Has anyone in the lab used an approach like this? What are the costs and benefits?
