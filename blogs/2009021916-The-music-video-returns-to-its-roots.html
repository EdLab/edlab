The music video returns to its roots

jvazquez

February 19, 2009

$99 Music Videos is an independent music channel that features videos all made for... $99. The site was created with the idea of "getting back to basics" and its creators wanted to show that you don't need a budget of thousands of dollars to produce quality videos. In fact, say the creators, all you need is a collaborative spirit and creativity; you shouldn't have to worry about cost. The site releases a new video every Thursday, along with a behind-the-scenes video to show would-be producers how easy it can be (today was the first release date!). The site accepts user-submitted content, but it does have rules:

It must be made for $99 (or less). It must be shot in one day (24 hours). It must be edited in one day (this doesn't include rendering, digitizing, or exporting â€“ just the creative part of editing). It must be a collaboration between the band and the filmmaker.
I like the challenges that this site poses to its potential users: be as creative as possible, but shoot to see immediate results. Sounds like the EdLab mantra...
