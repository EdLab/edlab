Brown Bag Session--Extracurricular Activities

Samuel.Fordjour

August 15, 2018

A few weeks ago, I led a Brown Bag session where we discussed the impact of extracurricular activities on students. During the discussion, participants shared their own extracurricular experiences and looked at some other aspects of extracurricular activities: costs, access, importance, and skills it supposedly builds. Overall, I think we all concluded that extracurricular activities in schools are highly beneficial to students. This TCR article by Jennifer Fredricks was the required reading for the discussion as it provides a solid overview of extracurricular activities in schools. Fredricks argues for broadening extracurricular activities to make it an essential part of the curriculum and educational experience.

She further notes that physical extracurricular activities, for example, help reduce obesity. Youth report that they learned emotional, cognitive, physical, interpersonal, and social skills by participating in organized out-of-school contexts. However low-income school communities find it difficult to have extra-curricular activities due to lack of money in the budget. Overall, having extracurricular activities in schools are beneficial to students and the community as well as it promotes healthy lifestyles and the development of life skills such as problem-solving and time management.
