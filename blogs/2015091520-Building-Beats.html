Building Beats

EdLab Studios

September 15, 2015


For two long days, the lights went out in New York City and an art form was ignited. Releasing equipment once unattainable for many behind glass display cases and lofty price tags, the mass looting enabled by the blackout of 1977 proved the catalyst for the DJ boom and rapid-fire spread of hip hop beyond the Bronx. Today, Building Beats does its part to keep the artistic legacy alive in the very birthplace of hip hop, breeding a new generation of students through music education programs. Without the provocation of a power outage, the non-profit, Board of Education Standards-aligned program exposes students of all ages to the skills and techniques needed for scratching, building rhythms, and even mixing beats. In addition to the essential equipment used in the classroom for modern beat making, field trips to Jam Master Jay's music production learning center, Scratch Academy, enables students to get their hands on authentic turntables and vinyl, likely for the very first time.
