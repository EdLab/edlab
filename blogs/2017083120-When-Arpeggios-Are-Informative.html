When Arpeggios Are Informative

saraehardman

August 31, 2017


We mainly think of data and charts as visualizations, but with a process called sonification, this information can be turned into sound. While the idea has been around long enough for NASA and some others to adopt it, analytics company SAS recently transferred this tech into a free Chrome plug-in called Graphics Accelerator. The app can be applied to all data representations and charts and turns individual data points into piano notes. Users can therefore ascertain how data changes over time. When it increases, the notes flutter up; when it decreases, they descend.

This technology is geared toward the visually impaired. Researchers believe that sonification will transform science, math, and technology fields for those who cannot see. Already, many visually impaired scientists and researchers have lauded sonification as an effective and inclusive tool. Some even believe that sonification provides a more nuanced presentation of information than a visualization can.
