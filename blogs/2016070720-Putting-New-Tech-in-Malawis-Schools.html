Putting New Tech in Malawi’s Schools

saraehardman

July 7, 2016


A few years ago, Malawi’s educational system accomplished a great feat when it opened its schools to all children in the country. There was only one problem: many schools didn’t yet have adequate resources to teach so many students. A typical classroom in Malawi has around 80 children, making it nearly impossible for the teacher to track the progress of each student.

To fix this, schools across Malawi have partnered with a U.K. charity to bring tablets and solar-powered projectors to their classrooms. Now students receive two sessions each week on their tablets, where they are taught lessons in math, English, and the local language. Not only do the students seem to learn more efficiently during their individual tablet sessions, their teachers are able to access students’ progress quickly and easily and tweak each child’s lessons accordingly.

This Vialogue demonstrates how technology can revolutionize schools that have few resources and make education more interactive, individualized, and accessible to students and teachers alike.
