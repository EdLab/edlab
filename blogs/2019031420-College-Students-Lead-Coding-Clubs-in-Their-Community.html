College Students Lead Coding Clubs in Their Community

Toland.Lawrence

March 14, 2019


The University of Wisconsin offers a service learning class that prepares students to lead their own coding club in the local community. The class teaches them how to explain concepts clearly and succinctly as well as develop the interpersonal skills needed for classroom management. In the after school club, they lead a group of elementary school students as they create animations and games using the Scratch programming language. Some students have been so inspired by the experience that they started coding clubs in their own home towns.

The hope is that when young kids meet and interact with college students in STEM fields, they will be inspired to pursue a similar path. Itâ€™s clear that many of the students enjoyed the experience and appreciate the effort put in by their club leaders.
