ALA Alex Awards

Jessica Blum

January 12, 2010

Since we've all been asking ourselves what teachers need to be successful in their practice, I wanted to share one of my favorite resources I used as an English teacher: "What books would reluctant readers like to read and study?" In my on-going search for answers and ideas, I came across the American Library Association's Alex Awards, which are given each year to ten books written for adults that would likely have a special appeal to adolescent readers, aged 12 through 18. I found a lot of gems scouring through the winners lists, and I am anxiously awaiting the announcement of the 2010 recipients. My only wish is that I had found this resource earlier . . .
