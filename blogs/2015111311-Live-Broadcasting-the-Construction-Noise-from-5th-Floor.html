"Live Broadcasting" the Construction Noise from 5th Floor

chingfu

November 13, 2015

(Private) As the construction work continues, I think this real time* construction noise simulation for the 5th floor can give those who are not on the floor a sense of current noise level, especially those who are migrating across floors, missing to come back to the fifth floor yet not sure if the noise level is bearable (though it seems that we have all adapted to the noises and no longer migrating). Essentially, this is a sound-activated simulation program. The movements of these animated characters in the simulation reflect the noise level at the 5th floor. Currently this Scratch program is running on a Mac, and the screen is broadcasting through Google Hangout Air (can live broadcast for a event for up to 8 hours). We can use Rasperry Pi to run similar programs and set up interactive simulations easily at different library and learning spaces. For instance, we can have a live "collaboration simulation" that indicates the liveliness of conversations at the 2nd floor collaborative space or Everett Cafe.
