Teachers Have Mixed Opinions About Technology

Janice Joo

January 6, 2013


In a recent study conducted by the Pew Internet Project, 2,462 teachers shared their thoughts on technology and learning. Of the teachers surveyed, 77% felt that the Internet had a "mostly positive" impact on student research skills, making them more self-sufficient researchers. At the same time, 87% of teachers felt that digital technologies were also encouraging an unfocused generation of students with poor attention spans. While researchers point out that the findings are subjective since they are based on teachers’ perspectives, the study has encouraged researchers and practitioners to examine the impact of technology on learning further. In what ways can we maximize its benefits while minimizing potential downfalls?
