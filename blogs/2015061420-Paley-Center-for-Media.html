Paley Center for Media

EdLab Studios

June 14, 2015


The Paley Center for Media, formerly known as the Museum of Television & Radio, is home to an enormous collection of radio and television broadcast content, which visitors are freely able to view and listen to. It’s also home to a unique collection of technological devices that have become obsolete in radio and television studios over the years, say for example, a sound machine once used in NBC’s studios to mimic the sound of a creaking door, or a wind machine used by NYU radio to mimic the sound of stormy gusts. These items, once used to tell live radio stories, are no longer necessary thanks to the advent of low cost recording infrastructure and online archiving and downloading capabilities. They are, however, extremely powerful agents for teaching history. As part of its education program, Paley hosts students from within and outside of the United States to participate in workshops that teach the history of radio and television. The ultimate goal is to make students more media literate in a world saturated with digital content and production tools. In this spirit, a group of young actors visiting New York City for an immersive theatre education experience, put on a live radio drama show using the very tools once used in New York City’s oldest broadcast stations.
