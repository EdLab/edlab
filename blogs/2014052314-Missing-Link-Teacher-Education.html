Missing Link: Teacher Education

Carmen James

May 23, 2014

Getting Smarter makes a great point about the maker movement in schools: "There's a missing link between real-world maker movement popularity and classroom making. But the mystery is solved if you consider that most educators have never learned how to teach making. The missing link is teacher understanding." Full article. I think this is true for a lot of things in the education world that involve technology, design and engineering. Students are digital natives (as Prensky calls them, see here for more information and teachers are new arrivals. How can we develop tools, programs and platforms to support teacher learning? How can teachers have the experience and understanding of all the platforms, tools ideas available to them so they can hand select the best ones to support learning in their classroom?
