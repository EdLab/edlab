eRwanda Project

abagigah92

November 30, 2013

The eRwanda Project helps to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of some internal processes of the government of Rwanda. The project works with key sectors to improve better access to information through the use of technology. The project supports the use of technology as an enabler to growth and development. This shows that communication is very important when it comes to the growth and development of an economy. While there are many activities which relies on communication and information technology (ICT), in all cases, the anticipated results relate to selected improvements of government's internal functions, its ability to deliver services and information to its citizens, and strengthening of the private sector for economic growth and poverty reduction. Ratings for the eRwanda Project proved to be satisfactory. The risk to development outcome was moderate and the performance from banks was satisfactory. Some lessons learned from project were with the (ICT) buses that are reaching out to people in rural areas. The ICT buses have 20 laptop computer stations with an Internet connection. In addition, the bus has a large monitor and DVD player, a projector and screen, and its own power generator. The ICT buses allowed youth and rural people, who do not have household electricity, an opportunity to become familiar with ICT by using computers.
