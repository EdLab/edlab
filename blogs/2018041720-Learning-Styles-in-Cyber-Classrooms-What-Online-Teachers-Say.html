Learning Styles in Cyber Classrooms: What Online Teachers Say

DebraLee

April 17, 2018


Differentiation refers to how teachers tailor instruction to individual needs. Research has shown that it positively impacts achievement and keeps high-ability students challenged in heterogeneous face-to-face classrooms. In this study, online teachers were asked to define what differentiation looked like in full-time online schools. It turns out that online teachers see it from two distinct perspectives: why a student needs differentiation, and what a student needs differentiated. And this is surprising because the finding is inconsistent with studies in face-to-face classrooms, and it doesn't support research about what is known to impact student achievement.

The study was conducted with 118 state certified teachers from two cyber schools, who answered a 32-question survey that included both Likert Scale and open-ended questions. Both schools serve diverse populations in the northeast, where around 28,000 students attend virtual charter schools. Classes are offered through a Moodle-based learning management system where students can log on Monday through Friday to complete assignments, and materials are accessible 24/7.

Three primary findings identified differences and similarities that could benefit from further research into how online teachers (OTs) uncover, then meet, the needs of students in an online classroom:

Finding 1: Falling for the myth of learning styles as a key to differentiation. Despite contrary evidence, OTs focused efforts on ineffective learning styles rather than best practices.

Finding 2: Needing to differentiate content, product, and process. Future research is needed to explore what OTs mean by content, product, and process to see how they align with face-to-face definitions and practices.

Finding 3: Using assessment to inform differentiation. Only one participant stated that differentiation was "meeting the needs of the individual learners based on formative assessment," a surprise given the wide use of data for assessment by online school leaders.
