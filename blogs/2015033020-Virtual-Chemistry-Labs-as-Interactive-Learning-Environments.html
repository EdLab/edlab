Virtual Chemistry Labs as Interactive Learning Environments

Rachel Smiley

March 30, 2015


An important factor in effective chemistry learning is experimentation in a laboratory setting, but when schools cannot build, fund, or sustain chemistry labs, how can they provide students with the necessary stimulation and reinforcement inherent in lab experiments?

The authors of this study used Kinect for Windows to develop a virtual chemistry lab that would respond to gesture recognition and movement tracking in order to create the interactive experience of a Chemistry lab. The study included 40 experiments appropriate for junior high and senior high chemistry students in Poland along with assignments such as the comparison of the chemical reactivity of metals and the study of the effect of temperature on the solubility of gases in water.

In order to measure the effectiveness of the virtual lab, the researchers conducted pre- and post-tests of student knowledge. In addition to experimental and control groups, comparisons were made among students receiving virtual lab instructions from a teacher, a video tutorial, and a text document.

The findings showed a marked increase in student learning and retention as compared to the control students who did not interact with the virtual lab. Interestingly from the study, students receiving lab instructions directly from a teacher performed the best of any group, indicating a preference and need for instructor input. Upon completion of the study, the students reflected on their own growth and indicated improved efficiency and increased interest in participating in lab work in the future. Based on this study, virtual chemistry labs have the potential to augment the learning process, provide practice and preparation outside the physical lab, and increase overall interest in the physical sciences.
