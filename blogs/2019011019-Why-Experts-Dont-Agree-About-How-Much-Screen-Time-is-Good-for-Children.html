Why Experts Don’t Agree About How Much Screen Time is Good for Children

Rebecca.Sullivan

January 10, 2019


Is digital technology good for children? Parents in search of an answer to this question receive conflicting messages from education and health professionals. Australian researchers recently explored the reason for the disparate advice parents encounter, and offer recommendations to guide interdisciplinary research about the effects of technology on health and development.

While educational experts point out the benefits of digital technology in promoting learning and preparing children to succeed in a digitally enhanced world, health professionals warn of the adverse effects of screen time on young children. This conflicting advice makes it difficult for parents to guide children’s technology use. While educators are mainly concerned with cognitive outcomes, developing skill sets, and promoting social connectedness through digital technology, the guidelines from Australian health professionals reflect a dominant concern with the negative consequences of sedentary behavior. Furthermore, these health guidelines are based on research about the effects of television screen time on children, but do not include studies about the use of mobile digital technology.

The researchers call for interdisciplinary research studying the long- and short-term effects of early childhood digital technology use. They recommend that guidelines contain neutral language that lists healthy ways to use digital technology. In order for parents to prepare their children for life in a digital world, they need a clear understanding of how to use digital technology appropriately. Thus, guidelines must stress the importance of the whole family using digital technology in a healthy way. Finally, the researchers emphasize the need to separate sedentary behavior guidelines from digital technology guidelines, since digital technologies do not necessarily involve sedentary behavior.

Straker, L., Zabatiero, J., Danby, S., Thorpe, K., & Edwards, S. (2018). Conflicting guidelines on young children's screen time and use of digital technology create policy and practice dilemmas. The Journal of Pediatrics, 202, 300-303.
