On the Ethics of Regulating Professors' Tweets

Jihii Jolly

May 29, 2014

Pretty interesting piece from Oliver Bateman on Al Jazeera America: Don't Regular Professors' Tweets. It's essentially a reaction to the comprehensive social media policy approved in December by the Kansas Board of Regents (after journalism professor David Guth's kind of careless tweet in reaction to the Navy Yard shooting). Worth a read. Aside from the obvious first amendment issues that any kind of censorship brings up, Bateman argues that the policy could lead to (America's growing force of) untenured faculty being fired perfunctorily and a chilling effect on liberal arts/social science research (since scholars are increasingly discussing their research on public social channels).
