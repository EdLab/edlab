Taxidermy

EdLab Studios

October 25, 2016


What began as the ancient Egyptian practice of preserving deceased household pets through the process of mummification has evolved into a group of tattooed metropolitans gathered in a converted Greenpoint barber shop around a table of frozen mice. The Mouse Taxidermy Class taught by Brooklyn-based taxidermist and entomology artist, Amber Maykut at HoardaCulture invites curious minds and lifelong collectors to finally understand the mystery behind the art of taxidermy that has flourished across cultures and time. The course pays homage to Victorian-era heavyweights like Hermann Ploucquet of Germany and Walter Potter of England who popularized the tradition of anthropomorphic taxidermy that fascinated audiences during the so-called golden age of taxidermy. Using the less conventional choice of mice which prove better suited to New York City's spatial restrictions, students learn how to skin their ethically-sourced specimens, mold mannequins with various materials, sew fur, set wire armature to create poses and gestures, fit glass beads for eyes, and apply props and costumes to establish setting and character. Thanks to dedicated innovators who paved the way, students also treat their taxidermy with a non-toxic dry preservative and an injection of embalming fluid to maintain their pieces over the years. At the end of three hours not only do students leave with an artistic creation to call their own but also a deeper understanding of anatomy and the natural world not readily found by other means. This craft-style course acts as a gateway for students with a strong passion and constitution to match, testing their dedication to, and perhaps stomach for, the art and science of taxidermy.
