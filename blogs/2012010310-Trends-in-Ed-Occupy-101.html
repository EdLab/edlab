Trends in Ed: Occupy 101

Janet Jang

January 3, 2012

The Anthropology Department of Columbia University will be piloting a new course next semester called â€œOccupy the Field: Global Finance, Inequality, Social Movement." Upperclassmen and graduate students will receive full course credit for this fieldwork-based class by getting involved in ongoing Occupy Wall Street (OWS) projects outside of the classroom. According to the course syllabus, the "course is designed both to maximize student exposure to the daily life of the Occupy movement, and to minimize exposure to potentially volatile or unsafe situations." The professor, Dr. Hannah Appel, is an active OWS advocate and recently wrote a series in the Social Text Journal defending the legitimacy of the movement as a practice "of an ethic of radical democracy". I think it took a lot of guts and drive for Dr. Appel to push for this course, especially with all the controversy surrounding the movement. But in a CBS News article about this course, there are many comments from readers that are skeptical about the purpose behind this course and that wonder if the OWS-veteran Appel will be able to remain objective while teaching this course. What are your thoughts and opinions about Columbia offering this class next semester? Why do or why don't you think it is necessary for Appel to remain objective despite her already evident self-disclosure?
