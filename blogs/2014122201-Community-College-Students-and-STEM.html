Community College Students and STEM

Khalil Abubakar

December 22, 2014

Improving your math and other STEM skills before attending college might be the difference between graduating on time or not at all if you are a community college student. This piece notes that 60-70 percent of community college students are assigned to remedial math programs and only five percent are able to complete this program. Equally troubling, 80 percent of students in these programs fail to complete college level courses within three years. Students from poor mathematics or STEM backgrounds might skip taking math and later on in their academic future, as it becomes a burden.

In order to address these numbers, the President and First Lady met with several college presidents, educational leaders, institutions like the National Science Foundation and Connecticut College of Technology. Some of these institutions are creating programs to change the numbers and increase minorities and women to seek careers in STEM.
