Fly Like a Drone, Pollinate like a Bee

csd2126

March 13, 2017


Scientists in Japan are experimenting with drones to solve the problem of the worldwide decline in bee populations. These miniature drones, coined BeeBots, are being programmed to pollinate flowers. The drones have a strip of fuzzy material to mimic how pollen sticks to the hair of bees. The fuzz is coated in a sticky substance to allow the drones to remove pollen and transfer it to another flower just like a regular bee.

Despite their small size, gracefully controlling these miniature drones has been a challenge for the research team. Future models of the BeeBot could use GPS or AI to plan the best path to pollinate flowers.

While flying robot bees may sound like something from science-fiction, are miniature drones a viable solution to declining bee populations? How else could we address this problem? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
