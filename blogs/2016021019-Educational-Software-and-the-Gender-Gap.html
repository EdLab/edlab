Educational Software and the Gender Gap

Jenny Shen

February 10, 2016


Whether you think of university online courses, Khan Academy, or MOOCs when you hear the words online learning, the reality is that the landscape of educational software and computer-based learning is changing rapidly. The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, along with many other initiatives across the world, have been funneling money into the development of online learning options to make education more accessible to everyone.

Gates emphasizes, however, that this plan hits a major roadblock when you consider the gender gap with respect to cell phone ownership. In Africa and Asia, women are 24% and 37% less likely, respectively, to own a cellphone. But if educational software is the proposed plan as a "leveler" in society, this gender gap would have to be addressed, or the software will only add to the inequity.

What other factors can prevent education from being equitable? What steps do we still have to take to make sure that education is accessible and high quality for everyone? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
