How Human is Too Human for Robots?

saraehardman

June 8, 2017


The development of robots and artificial intelligence is divisive, leaving many people excited about the prospects of human-robot interactions and many others afraid for humanity’s future. Robotic engineer Hiroshi Ishiguro believes the best way to bridge this divide is to create social, artificially intelligent robots that look exactly like humans. One of his recent projects is a social robot named Erica, which was featured on a recent episode of National Geographic’s Year Million series. Ishiguro’s goal in creating Erica was to blur the line between humans and humanoid androids.

As social robots become more prominent fixtures in our lives (such as in libraries, special needs classrooms, and hospitals), some engineers and designers believe that they need to become more humanlike in order to seamlessly integrate into humanity. Yet others worry that creating robots that look too human will land these bots into the Uncanny Valley, where robots with too many human characteristics suddenly become incredibly creepy. Still others believe that if robots become too human, we might trust them to a fault and wind up in situations like those depicted in Her and Ex Machina. Regardless of your view, as robots are given more human characteristics, it might cause us to reexamine what it means to be human.

What are your thoughts on humanlike robots? Should we be aiming for complete human-robot integration, or is there such a thing as robots that are too human? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
