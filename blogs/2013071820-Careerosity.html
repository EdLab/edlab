Careerosity

LauraCostello

July 18, 2013


Careerosity began in an effort to bring college career services online, though the tool has recently pivoted towards greater inclusivity and now includes information for mid-career changes and new professionals. The site connects users to their alumni network and gives them personalized resources outside of their network to help them embark on a first or new career. Most of these resources are in the form of transition stories from professionals, but the site is expanding to include in-person events, online discussion, and other resources.

Pros:

The site is quite new, but has managed to attract a diverse network. Most current activity focuses on technology and education. The Q&A portion of the site is growing quickly and operates like a career Quora to let users ask and answer questions while building clout through upvotes and networking. The story portion of the site contains mini-interviews, each with a professional who has experienced career transition(s). The interviews follow a set template and explore career fit, preparing for a new field, and advice for users on a similar path. Apart from their work as a career change resource, these stories are an interesting digital artifact describing the changing nature of work in the 21st century. The site is free and already quite active.

Cons:

It may be too soon to tell, but the site mission may benefit from a bit more field specificity. Low-change fields like homemaking or agriculture have a place in the tags and categories, but the resources on the site seem better suited to dynamic, high-turnover professions like tech and media. Current use of the site seems to reflect this pattern. The networking opportunities on the site could turn it into a powerful rally point for professionals looking to start businesses or break into the startup scene.

Our Takeaway:

Career transitions are becoming more common, especially among young people. Networks and mentor relationships are more essential as educational expectations change and new jobs are created. Careerosity, as a networking space and research center, will be able to move at the speed of 21st century jobs and serve as a place to develop soft skills, define role expectations, and inspire professional evolution.

The Bottom Line:

Careerosity facilitates career change for the digital transition.
