Raising the G.E.D. Bar Stirs Concern for Students

Bismark Appiah

October 20, 2013

The General Education Development (GED) high school equivalency exam, also known as the GED will be raising its bar in the coming years. Moreover, it has been said that fees for taking the courses will also be increasing. As we all know, the GED is an educational program created to provide a second chance for students who dropped out of high school and to provide immigrants a foothold in the American education system. However, what impact will this bring now that it is about to become harder and eventually expensive? This is what students are concerned about, especially knowing that all state exams starting from January will be aligned with the Common Core standards. Most of the instructors and officials at adult education centers are worried that with these changes in the system, students will have a hard time passing the GED. You can read more in this article.
