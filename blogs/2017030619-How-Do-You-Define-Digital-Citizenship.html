How Do You Define Digital Citizenship?

csd2126

March 6, 2017


Internet technologies have a significant impact on civic engagement, but is the information age also introducing new forms of citizenship? There are many factors influencing digital citizenship, including developing technical skills and an awareness of local and global current events. While digital citizenship has previously been difficult to measure in a meaningful way, new research points to behaviors that may help reveal how individuals perceive their participation in online communities.

This study tested a new digital citizenship scale that comprehensively measures young adults’ self-reported abilities, perceptions, and levels of participation in online communities. Scale items were based on four categories of digital citizenship: ethics, media and information literacy, participation/engagement, and critical resistance.

Research was conducted on undergraduate and graduate students at a large Midwestern university in the U.S. to determine which items of the digital citizenship scale should be selected. The study results demonstrate that this new tool is able to accurately measure an individual's sense of citizenship. The scale will help show how people perceive themselves as members of various internet communities in their everyday lives.

Given issues of trolling, misinformation, and responsible and safe internet use, digital citizenship education can have an important impact on students’ participation online. Being able to define and measure citizenship practices online will have implications for educating students to become informed and active digital citizens.

Choia, M., Glassmanb, M., & Cristolc, D. (2017). What it means to be a citizen in the internet age: Development of a reliable and valid digital citizenship scale. Computers & Education, 107(2017), 100–112
