Story Centered Curriculum (SCC)

Stephen Asunka

April 29, 2007

Roger Schank, president and CEO of Socratic Arts and former Distinguished Career Professor at Carnegie Mellon, believes that our approach to education is stuck in the nineteenth century! Schank advocates a story-based curriculum (instead of the traditional subject-based curriculum) because it more closely resembles the world that awaits students after graduation. In an article in eLearn Magazine, Schank describes a Story Centered Curriculum (SCC) as "a carefully designed apprenticeship-style learning experience in which the student encounters a planned sequence of real-world situations constructed to motivate the development and application of knowledge and skills in an integrated fashion."
