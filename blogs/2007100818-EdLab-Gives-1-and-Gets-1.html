EdLab Gives 1 and Gets 1

Joachim Castellano

October 8, 2007

Check out David Pogue's article on the $100 laptop if you haven't done so already. I'm interested in testing the machine's list of impressive capabilities, especially its mesh networking, media functionality, and programmability. A tool like this could be a godsend to schools in third world countries where supplies and energy are scarce. However, this $100 laptop by itself is not a panacea for educating the world's poor. I believe it must be accompanied by at least $100,000 worth of thoughtfulness so that the XO laptop can be implemented properly into a school curriculum. I think we should purchase at least one of the machines to test out here at the EdLab. Let's kick its tires, and see how we can make this more useful to its end users. Pogue mentions, "No, the biggest obstacle to the XO's success is not technology – it's already a wonder – but fear. Overseas ministers of education fear that changing the status quo might risk their jobs. Big-name computer makers fear that the XO will steal away an overlooked two-billion-person market. Critics fear that the poorest countries need food, malaria protection and clean water far more than computers." With two billion kids armed with this laptop, and aided by EdLab's study of the machine, I'm sure these critics would panic. Personally I'd like to see the world freak out from a better educated populace.
