EdLab Review: Quora 'Boards'

Kate Meersschaert

January 30, 2012

Quora, the leading social Q&A discussion-tool recently added a "boards" feature. This new element allows users to create and add interest-based postings to a shared "board" similar to what Pinterest (the current leader in interest-based boards) and newcomer, Mulu.me have recently offered. Pros: Quora already leads the Q&A pack, and so, enabling interest-based postings to be grouped together on user generated boards seems like a natural progression for the growing platform. The initial experience setting-up a board and identifying potential followers, because of a simple dashboard style, was easy and took only minutes to figure-out (see embedded image below). Cons: Adding embedded images to these board seems to takes longer than I would have expected. Perhaps, the Quora servers are overcapacity or experiencing growing-pains as the platform gathers new users? EdLab Relevance: As we continue to grow and evolve social platforms like Vialogues, Ednode and soon, the mSchool platform, it will be interesting and potentially beneficial to explore the option of letting users create walls and boards to share content and interest-based postings either cultivated and created within the tool or from outside sources. This seems to satisfy a user's desire to further personalize and curate information disseminated and found online. Our Takeaways: Adding a feature to an app or platform needs to feel like a natural progression, and not a "me too" addition that adds clunky weight to the user experience. The addition of "boards" to Quora feels natural and effortless.
