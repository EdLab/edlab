Machine Learning Robot Successfully Mimics Zebrafish

DebraLee

March 1, 2018


As fish go, Zebrafish are both robust and complex, which is why EPFL's Robotic Systems Laboratory chose them to study social interactions between individual fish. Researchers developed a closed-loop system in which a robotic Zebrafish was not only able to influence the fish's behavior, but also adapt its own behavior by learning how to communicate and move like they do. As the robot spent more time with the fish, its swimming mechanism improved, and when tested in different aquariums, the robot was able to mimic the fish's behavior, prompting them to change direction or swim from one room to another.

Similar studies have been carried out on cockroaches where, to integrate into an insect community, a robot just had to emit pheromones. But fish are more complicated, and integrating a robot into a community of vertebrates involves many more criteria, including imitating the fish's physical characteristics, like shape, color, stripes, etc., and mimicking behavioral characteristics such as linear velocity, acceleration speed, distance between individual fish, the size of the schools, their vibrations and motion, and the rhythm at which they move their tails.
