The 2nd SecondLife blow-up?

Anthony Cocciolo

March 10, 2007

This past Friday, Linda, Jamison and I were treated to a viewing of the beta grid for SecondLife by Chris Carella (the co-instructor of the Virtual Worlds class here at TC). In the beta-grid, Linden Labs is trialing out Voice-over-IP. Let me say that I think this new addition is very compelling. I think people who haven't quite realized the potential of virtual worlds will suddenly "get-it", leading to a second press "blow-up" (the first press blow-up being this past year, the next one coming this summer or fall). I think there are numerous applications, especially where lack of co-presence has been an issue, including e-Learning and remote collaboration/work. Two interesting things about Second Life's VoIP is that 1) it is spatially related, so that when you move away, the voice starts to fade. Or depending on how you are facing someone, the voice will come through differently via the left and right speakers, and 2) when you move your camera towards other groups talking, you can hear what they are saying (the ultimate eavesdropping device). What is very clear is that it is going to radically alter the world (it may be off-putting to people who have been in it for awhile, but possibly very engaging for new people).
