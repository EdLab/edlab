Video Stream Gets Kids Moving and Grooving

saraehardman

August 8, 2016


Kids wiggle, they jiggle, they twist! They have more energy than they know what to do with. Luckily, GoNoodle is a movement-oriented video collection designed for K–7 kids that gets the squirmies out and the fun in. As a child, parent, or educator, GoNoodle gives you free access to dozens of kid-friendly exercise videos.

Pros:

GoNoodle has a wide variety of movement-based videos. Children can try out Zumba for kids, yoga, running, coordination drills, and even anxiety-combatting breathing exercises. Whether it’s time to release some extra energy or calm down before bed, GoNoodle has videos for any hour of the day. To keep kids more engaged, each user chooses a goofy monster character (called a "champ") that grows as they complete videos.

Creating an account on GoNoodle is incredibly simple, and once parents help with this step, the website itself is easy enough for young kids to navigate on their own. All videos on GoNoodle are kid-friendly, so parents don’t have to worry about inappropriate content. In addition, GoNoodle accounts keep track of how much time kids spend completing exercises, and parents can choose to receive email updates on their progress.

Cons:

Logistically, GoNoodle is only available as a website, not as an app. Although the site can still be accessed from tablets or iPhones, connecting to the internet is necessary for accessing the content. This makes it inconvenient for traveling families or those without internet access at home.

Our Takeaway:

GoNoodle is a great site for any parents and educators who want to encourage their kids to get moving. The guided exercises can pump children up or calm them down and can even be completed in areas with limited space, such as classrooms or living rooms. It’s fun, it’s safe, and most importantly, it gives kids guided activities in what they do best: move!
