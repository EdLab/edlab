US STEM Rankings

rendy

November 22, 2013

During a recent White House Forum, Dot Harris, the Director of the Office of Economy Impact and Diversity at the Department of Energy, stated that it was unacceptable that the US was ranked low in math and science compared to other developed countries. Other panelists at the forum shared this sentiment. The President of the University of Maryland-Baltimore County, Freeman Hrabowski III, believe our schools should re-teach STEM programs, listen to the voices of students and change the culture of science and engineering. According Robert Gee, President of Gee Strategies Group LLC, the United States should focus on minority students since we will soon become a minority majority country. I believe the reason why the USA has dropped on the rankings is because the focus is too much on prescribed curriculum for teachers. Educators are essentially teaching students to pass standardized exams. Students usually tend to forget what they've learned after the exam. Other students tend to cheat so they can pass and move on, and they end up in college or in professional careers not prepared.
