Colors, Design Principles, & Neuroaesthetics

Brian.Sweeting

January 30, 2015

ALL OF THE LINKS Because I don't have any formal education in design or illustration, putting together a Visualized draft involves experimenting with perspective, symmetry, and frequently squinting my eyes together and taking a step back to arrive at something I won't hate. Lately I've been trying to develop a better sense of color theory. I was a reluctant holdout on technicolor movies too. Aside from eyeballing hues and saturation levels, I've found the Adobe Color Wheel to be a very helpful resource: it will automatically pair complimentary, analagous, and monochromatic colors; has a library of saved themes; and will detect color themes from uploaded images. This makes me wonder what wonderful ways you could pair this with Philips Hue. Because apparently it works with anything. I also stumbled on this useful PSFK guide to design principles; it's a quick primer on ideas like the peak shift principle, grouping, symmetry, and balance: Rayburn Marasigan: Making Universal Principles of Design an Applied Science It's almost enough to inspire a weekend-long Netflix binge. Also these products are pretty creative, if not impractical, combinations of design and science (thank you PSFK):

  • Laser Headphones Glow Along with Beat of Your Own Drum
  • 3D Printed Helmet Morphs According to Your State of Mind
  • Helmet Lets You Hear Your Thoughts
And if none of that is useful or inspirational, just bask in the glory of 12 Mesmerizing Disney Pencil Gifs That Will Make You Miss 2D Animation! END OF LINKS
