Improving Students’ Lives One App at a Time

dinosossi2

October 25, 2015


Graduate education programs are notoriously tough to manage. Professors usually focus on theory since this may be the last time many students will ever be in a university setting; however, students want applied experiences in the classroom. It’s a difficult balance.

Karin Forssell may have reached a sweet spot. She’s the director of Stanford’s Learning, Design and Technology (LDT) program. Karin encourages her students to apply innovation theories to pressing school issues. For example, LDT student Ashley Moulton created Nomster Chef, an app that improves children’s dietary habits. Parents order food online and receive text messages as well as recipe guides to promote healthy cooking. Ashley’s classmate Karen Wang created FeelingTalk, an app that teaches autistic students social-emotional skills. Students compare facial images to recognize, practice, and learn emotions. These graduate-level apps are applied to elementary school problems.

Karin Forssell appreciates technology’s affordances but also concedes its limits. Technology provides instant feedback, potentially huge audiences, and a safe anonymous space. Working with technology, Karin’s students step out into the real-world, solve educational problems in schools, and help improve children’s lives. Sounds like a nice balance to me.
