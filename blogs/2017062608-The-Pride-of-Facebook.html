The Pride of Facebook

dinosossi2

June 26, 2017


Facebook recently provided a new like button for users to help celebrate Pride month. However, the button did not have a homogeneous rollout.


Apparently, in major markets that had well attended Pride celebrations, users could access these types of functions. Unfortunately, in smaller ones, they are unavailable.

Â 

This development leads to a series of questions:


  • Have we entered a new era of corporate social responsibility, akin to a Rousseau-like corporate social contract?
  • If so, are major tech companies compelled to promote major social issues?
  • Finally, if companies choose to be involved, do they correspondingly have an ethical responsibility to roll out these features globally or can they pick and choose?


Companies are inherently amoral constructs at best. Although projecting an image of a socially progressive company by targeting only welcoming markets does not go as far as it could, at least Facebook is beginning to take a stand.


Given its previous week, perhaps Uber should too...
