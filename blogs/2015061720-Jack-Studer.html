Jack Studer

Carmen James

June 17, 2015


Jack Studer is co-founder of The Lamp Post Group, a venture incubator in Chattanooga, TN that encourages students to design apps through summer internships and academic scholarships. Jack is also the COO of Torch, a redesigned wifi router that allows parents to guide their kids' online experiences, and he is thinking about what it means to grow up in an all-digital environment.

How have your education and professional experiences shaped your work?
I've been very lucky to have been able to stay on the steep part of the learning curve for most of my life. Whether it was advanced classes in high school, graduate level courses in undergrad (which led to a really low GPA but great learning) or the insanity of Investment Banking and then startups. #AlwaysKeepLearning

How do you hope your work will change learning?
I hope that through Lamp Post and because of my time at Singularity University, I'll be able to help a new generation of humans be better prepared for the exponential times we live in.

What broad trends do you think will have the most impact on learning in the years ahead?
Artificial intelligence and big data. We are going to see kids that grow up in a persistent online environment that is heuristically evolving to their growth and needs in ways we can't even comprehend.

What are you currently working on and what is your next big project?
I am working on MyTorch.com, which is a full suite of parenting tools from routers to mobile device management solutions to help parents have the tools they need to raise this generation of digital natives.

Who are the most interesting people you are following on Twitter?
@johnmaeda, @tedalling, and @salimismail.
