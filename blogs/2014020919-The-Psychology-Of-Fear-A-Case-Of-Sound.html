The Psychology Of Fear: A Case Of Sound

Carmen James

February 9, 2014


Dr. Hanson, the host of Itâ€™s Ok to be Smart on PBS Digital Studios explains why and how sounds can make us scared. Have you ever been at a horror movie and told yourself it is just a movie and still felt palm-sweatingly scared? Dr. Hanson delves into our brains and explores why and how we are both hard-wired not to die (so we can reproduce) and to feel fear when we hear certain kinds of sounds.

The graphically rich video full of technical and experience-based explanations provides a tingle of fear and an understanding of its source. Sounds move faster than sight, Dr. Hanson says, giving us a clue to why our auditory experiences can unsettle and frighten us. He looks at biology, neuroscience, and of course the tricks of cinematography to tell the full story. Dr. Hanson even distinguishes between the fear that builds slowly and instant fear. His animated and engaging manner coupled with creepy video clips leave the viewer feeling ready for the next big horror film of the year.
