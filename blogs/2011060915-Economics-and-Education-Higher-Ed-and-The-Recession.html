Economics & Education: Higher Ed & The Recession

Skanda Amarnath

June 9, 2011

Kevin Carey, of Education Sector, has an article in TNR on Why the Media Is Always Wrong About the Value of a College Degree I can understand his sentiment to some degree. It has become trendy to claim that college education does not matter anymore. While I'm not a fan of the state of higher ed, anyone who has read my posts will know that I still think higher ed matters, albeit not for the right reasons. Here's where Carey makes the biggest mistake though: he relies on anecdotal evidence in stretching his argument. The fact is that those entering the labor market in a recession, whether with or without a college degree, have on average lower lifetime earnings than their non-recession counterparts. This recession will hurt lifetime earnings for many young people coming out of college.
