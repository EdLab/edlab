Visual Data-Mining: What Makes Paris Look like Paris?

Sharon Hsiao

August 10, 2012

When we talk about Paris, we immediately think of the Eiffel Tower because it's such a distinctive landmark. Now, visual data-mining allows us to see the detail distinctions of a city. Paris is not just the tower, there are so many interesting elements that could allow us to recognize that Paris is Paris, i.e. the shapes of the street signs, balcony, and facade of the buildings, etc. In this recently published paper, the authors demonstrate that the elements on the street are visually interpretable and perceptually geo-informative. This is an interesting technique. Think about all the CIA and FBI movies you've seen and the tasks they go through to locate positions of suspects, etc. These elements on the street could really help in geo-searching. Another interesting fact is the director of movie Ratatouille spent a lot of time shooting Paris street views in order to better animate the motion pictures. With this visual data-mining technique, tons of millions of images can be patched automatically. I thought I'm very familiar with Paris, but turns out I still failed this Paris-nonParis test. ref: Carl Doersch, Saurabh Singh, Abhinav Gupta, Josef Sivic, and Alexei A. Efros. What Makes Paris Look like Paris? ACM Transactions on Graphics (SIGGRAPH 2012), August 2012, vol. 31, No. 3.
