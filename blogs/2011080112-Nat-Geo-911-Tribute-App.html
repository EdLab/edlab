Nat. Geo. 9/11 Tribute App

Kate Meersschaert

August 1, 2011

In honor of the tenth anniversary of 9/11 and to correspond with "Remember 9/11 Week," National Geographic has created a FaceBook tribute app. This app allows users from around the world to share stories and track on an interactive map, exactly where they were ten years ago during the terrorist attacks in NYC. Users create geo-tags for their stories on the map and can invite friends to share their stories as well as post stories to their wall. The site describes the importance of sharing remembrances of where you were during this dark moment in US history event and likens the significance of the event to The Kennedy Assassination and other landmark events. "Pearl Harbor, The Kennedy Assassination, the Moon landing: these are among the iconic events in history that cause us to remember exactly where we were at the time. The September 11, 2001 attacks certainly fall into this category." As Angela and I work together to create a series of commemorative 9/11 vialogues, we will explore this and other ways to provide resources that facilitate meaningful conversation around the event.
