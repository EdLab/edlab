Exoplanet

Yue Fang

November 20, 2014


Exoplanet is an app that takes users on a trip through the universe to explore all known exoplanets (planets orbiting stars beyond our own solar system) and perhaps to find another planet habitable for human beings.

On an iPhone or iPad, users can zoom out to enjoy a bird’s-eye view of the Milky Way or zoom in to a specific planet for a close-up. It's also possible to rotate the planet or planetary systems for a 360° view. The app provides accurate astronomical data and background information about each planet and this information is updated every few hours. Users can also sign up to receive push notifications whenever a new planet is discovered. Intrepid explorers can snap a photo of their current view to save and share via email or social media.

Some new and creative features are being developed including an augmented reality layer in the app to help users identify the exoplanet by pointing a phone to the sky and a connection to the Philips Hue light bulb which changes the color of the light automatically to match the color of a selected planet. The app is free with a few add-ons available for purchase.

Pros:

The stunning animation of the universe in this app might inspire users to explore more deeply. Using the smooth rotation and zoom or the embedded search engine, users are able to find a specific planet and create correlation diagrams with rich astronomical data available in the app. The app is maintained by an enthusiastic astronomer and gets better and better with every update.

Cons:

The astronomical information might be too advanced for people who know little about astronomy. Without narratives that explain the specific knowledge more simply, users may not be able to make sense of the information. The app currently does not support a community for people to share their interests, discoveries, or questions. Also, it currently only works on iPad or iPhone.

Our Takeaway:

Exoplanet is a wonderful tool to raise interest in astronomy and deepen knowledge. It meets the needs of a wide range of users from astronomy beginners to experts. The visualization helps users grasp abstract astronomical concepts. Accurate concepts, updated data, comprehensive background knowledge and correlation diagrams are available for further study. Getting a peek at an infinite universe might inspire users to think more broadly and stay humble.

The Bottom Line:

You are just one tap a way from any exoplanet in the whole universe.
