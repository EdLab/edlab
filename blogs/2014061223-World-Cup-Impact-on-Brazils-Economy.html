World Cup Impact on Brazil's Economy

abagigah92

June 12, 2014

The 2010 World Cup in South Africa2010 World Cup in South Africa did not live up to the economic benefits predicted before the tournament. Economists predicted that World Cup would generate over 900 million in revenue. However, only $513 million was generated during the tournament. Additionally, only about 11% of the $4.5 billion dollars invested was recovered. The long-term goals will be easier to achieve with the foundation provided by much of the development work leading up to the World Cup.

This year's World Cup in Brazil has the same promising economic outlook for the hosts. Brazil is expecting to bring home more than $11 billion in revenue. The revenue is believed to be more than 20 times what South Africa earned in 2010. Most of the revenue will be generated through tourism; about 600,000 tourists will be visiting for the World Cup. Overall, Brazilian citizens and tourists are expected to spend up to over $11 billion. Unlike the 2010 in South Africa, economists believe that Brazil will generate $513 million, which will not match pre-tournament estimates.
