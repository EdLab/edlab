This Robot Breaks Down Social Barriers for Children with Autism

saraehardman

May 3, 2017


Kaspar the robot was designed to help young children with autism be more capable and comfortable with social interaction. Created by researchers at the University of Hertfordshire, Kaspar has realistic human features, but with easy-to-read, simplified facial expressions. This allows children with autism to be able to better understand the responses of Kaspar than they might other people, which leads to their learning how to have safe social interactions.

Geared toward children aged 5-10, Kaspar can talk, imitate eating, play the tambourine, and sing. Over 170 kids have worked with this child-sized bot over the course of 10 years of development, and the researchers hope that soon all schools and clinics that need this robot will have access to one.
