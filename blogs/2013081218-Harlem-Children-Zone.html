Harlem Children Zone

Bachir Diarrassouba

August 12, 2013

Harlem Children Zone (HCZ) is a nonprofit organization founded during the 1990s. The goal of the organization is to teach and provide strength to inner city students so that they may have the tools needed to excel in college and beyond. HCZ advocate and address social problems in Harlem such as gang violence, poverty and health. Academically, 97% of TRUCE students graduated High School and 100% of students were accepted to college. HCZ allows students to have a voice in the community with the help of media based themes. Its students are able to write articles that discuss anything involved in the community while also creating films and even designing new clothing. For students that are having problems at home, HCZ has a Family Support Center. This program aims to serve more than 100 families with an emphasis on providing crisis intervention. I personally attended Harlem Children Zone and it was a great experience. I learned how important it is to advocate change in my community.
