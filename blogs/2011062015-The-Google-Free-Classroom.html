The Google-Free Classroom

Kate Meersschaert

June 20, 2011

Steven Heller does not allow ANY Google searches in his art research & criticism class. Impossible? Ill-advised? Just plain ridiculous? Heller stands his ground ... A columnist for The Atlantic and a co-chair of the MFA Design program at the School of Visual Arts, Heller believes that students need to learn old-fashioned, gumshoe-based research techniques. Heller stresses the importance of looking beyond results found simply on the internet, "My goal is to encourage research projects based on personal or scholarly interests, historical artifacts or found objects. Analysis rooted in first-hand interplay is essential." Students are encouraged to find objects or 2-D examples of historical design in libraries, thrift shops, antique stores and other real-world locations, with the end-goal of composing an engaging narrative on the object's history to present to the class. Research is conducted using... gasp... real, live librarians and actual libraries! My questions for Heller is, are the Librarians allowed to use search engines? All joking aside, do you think this class should serve as a rallying-cry for other teachers to create classes that encourage inquiry beyond easily available search results?
