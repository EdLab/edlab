Pixar: How do they do it?

Stephanie Hunt

June 12, 2009

Many of you know that I'm a huge 3D nerd. I adore all things holographic, and when it came time to send out wedding invitations, I came *this* close to making 3D viewmaster reels from scratch to mail (along with viewmaster viewers, of course) to my family and friends. Needless to say, I was stoked when Pixar recently released their first 3D film, Up!. I was in the theater on opening day! If you've wondered how a big computer animation house like Pixar makes their magic, check out this How We Do It slideshow. I admit it, I was initially drawn in because of the fun viewmaster-themed interface. But once I flipped through the images, I knew that I wanted to add this site to our ever-growing collection of design process documentation. It's exciting to learn how Pixar does it - but I also love seeing how they present their process. The mode of delivery presented in the video is completely consistent with the company and its values. There's something to learn from how Pixar communicates their story, and it definitely makes me think about how EdLab should talk about its own design process. What mode of content delivery would best reflect our overall mission and values?
