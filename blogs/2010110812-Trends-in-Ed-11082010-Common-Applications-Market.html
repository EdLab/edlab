Trends in Ed 11.08.2010: Common Applications Market

Nantwi

November 8, 2010

With the college application process firmly underway and the pressures associated with applying to the right school, the last thing most high school seniors want to be concerned with is the process to apply to the schools of their choice. For many seniors, online applications have made the process faster and there are now many online resources that have made it easier for students to apply to college. The Common Application, the Universal Common Application and portals like Xap and ConnectEdu are a few of the companies promising to make the process faster for seniors. Students fill in their academic and personal information at once and the information is passed onto the student's list of schools. Students still have to submit additional required supplements for their prospective schools. The relationship between colleges and companies offering common applications is one of mutual benefit to both provider and receiver. The former expands its channels for students to apply, appeal to a more diverse group of students and appear selective by rejecting a large number of applicants. The latter, by appealing to students to apply using their common application, generate profit by charging institutions for every completed student application.
