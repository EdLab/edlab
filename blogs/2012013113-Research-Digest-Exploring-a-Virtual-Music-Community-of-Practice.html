Research Digest: Exploring a Virtual Music “Community of Practice”

Lu Wang

January 31, 2012

Waldron, J. (2009). Exploring a virtual music ‘community of practice': Informal music learning on the Internet. Journal of Music, Technology and Education: 2:23. pp. 97-112

It is recognized that transactional communication, which is reported to be positively effective on creativity in composition, improvisation, and interpretation, is vitally important in music learning and producing experience. Given the fact that music is actually a social activity, the formation of a music community, which boosts the interactions and collaborations among people who show appreciation for the same music genre, would thus greatly inspire creativity and enriches musicality of community members.

Based on this idea, EdLab may be able to create some project in the future “teaching” about creativity through arts such as painting, poetry, or… music. There are many resources in EdLab that can be utilized, i.e. Vialogues, to help establish an online community who share the same interest in certain art forms.
