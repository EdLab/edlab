Diwali...Well...What ?

Megha Agarwala

October 27, 2011

Yesterday was Diwali. What is Diwali? Heres a short crash course for you! Diwali is one of the most important festivals in India. The word Diwali comes from a Sanskrit word "Deepavali," which means a row of lamps. So, Diwali is basically a festival of lights. People light small clay lamps filled with oil, clean their homes and decorate it creatively with lights. The lights are supposed to ward off evil spirits from the home and their lives. Power-cuts prone India is seen glittering with lights on Diwali. People sing prayers in the evening to worship the goddess "Lakshmi" - the goddess of wealth. Another major highlight on Diwali is food and sweets. People prepare their best dishes to eat on Diwali. Sweets are exchanged with extended families and friends. After the prayers and food, people burn firecrackers on their rooftops or on the gallery space outside their home or their building. Again, the firecrackers are symbolic of driving away evil spirits. Mythologically, Diwali marked the return of king Rama after 14 years of exile. The entire kingdom was lit with lamps to welcome his return.
