The EdLab Review: Comic Life

Ting Yuan

November 18, 2009

Comic Life Comic Life is an award-winning software with millions of users including children, grownups, and schools. It aims to bring users an easy and beautiful experience of creating comics. Tags: software, instruction, literacy, comics http://plasq.com/comiclife-win Price: $24.95-29.95 (with a one-month free trial) Overall 4.5 Design 4.5 Pedagogy 4.5 [The Good] One salient design strength of this software is its child-friendly interface and functional buttons, which make children easily navigate within the software. The easy navigation makes users' creation process of comics fun and engaging. Also in the process, users (students) learn different features of comics (e.g., panels, bubbles, lines, etc.) through generating their own comics. It is an exemplary constructivist-based software for students' learning about and creating graphic texts. [The Bad] The current pity of this software (and website) lies in that it lacks online collaboration and sharing. Focusing on students' individual creation of comics, however, it has no option for students to post their works online or to invite co-contributors to work on the same "book". The option of online posting can motivate students to look at examples made by others and to share and publish their works online, while the option of co-contribution can enable students to continue their classroom collaboration beyond the classroom. [The Bottom Line] Overall, it is a nice software which is of important pedagogical meaning for teaching literacy and social studies. It is suitable for K-12 students as well as English language learners (as it supports multilingual panels). In teaching literacy, teachers can use Comic Life to teach students the concept of graphic novel and can engage English language learners by giving them the opportunity of creating conversational strips step by step. In teaching social studies, Comic Life can be a great tool for students to make critiques of contemporary issues such as Hurricane Katrina. Recommended use I strongly recommend this tool to K-12 teachers, for teaching students creative writing, practice of design, as well as critical thinking in social studies. For kindergartners, Comic Life can be used to learn comic features such as strips, bubbles, lines, while for higher graders, it can be used together with images collected online to make digital stories for book review, reflection, and/or critique.
