Google I/O

Manav Malhotra

June 27, 2012

private please Thanks to Mr. Stephen Pratt, I ducked out of EdMedia for a bit to attend the Google I/O conference in Boulder. When I arrived the speakers were going through their plans for Project Glass with an XTREME demo involving Glass, Google+ hangouts, skydiving, and rappelling. Afterwards they brought Glass back down to earth a bit, showing moms with their newborns wearing Glass to catch that millisecond when the baby smiles. It was all a bit awkward and none of the demos, which mostly involved the benefits of first-person video and pictures, seemed all that appealing or revolutionary (maybe that's because I don't have a baby). But once information access on the device gets easier, which is something they explicitly mentioned they were working on, Glass will be awesome. More to come as I go to more sessions.
