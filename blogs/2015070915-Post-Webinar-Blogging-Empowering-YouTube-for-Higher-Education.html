Post-Webinar Blogging: Empowering YouTube for Higher Education

zhou

July 9, 2015

I attended this webinar titled Empowering YouTube for Higher Education today facilitated by 3PlayMedia. The webinar turned out not to have the content I was expecting. I thought it would be an overview of diverse use cases of YouTube in higher education but it eventually is a product demo from Justin McCutcheon, CEO of Cattura Video. However I still sat through the webinar because I was interested in learning what features are considered important by the industry. I will briefly summarize the webinar. The presenter first overviewed the advantages and tradeoffs of using YouTube and Vimeo for education. To put it simple, YouTube is great because it provides first-grade technology for free with varieties of features for education, and is comparatively easier for educators to learn. The major barrier is YouTube has so much content that makes itself distracting for learning. The presenter then mentioned Vimeo and Vimeo Pro that are more concentrated on education. His company Cattura's approach is to combine YouTube and Vimeo into LMS with a clean learning environment. Then He introduced the various features Cattura offers.

  • Youtube + Vimeo in clean environment
    • Support 40+ LTI-compliant LMS/CMS including Moodle, BlackBoard, Sakai, Hiku, Wordpress, and more
  • Notepads -- timeline-based notes/annotation
  • Transcription/close captioning
    • If the user owns the YouTube video, CC can be picked up from YouTube via API
    • User can also upload CC to Cattura
    • Cattura works with 3Play to offer transcriptioning services. One can transcribe any YouTube video (doesn't have to own the video).
    • Search
      • Search within video meta info, transcriptions, and notepads
    • Create video collections
    • Batch import YT videos
    • In-LMS upload to YouTube and Vimeo
    • Google Analytics
      • Audience questions:
      Can a teacher create a controlled space that students can upload videos to for sharing projects or additional related videos?
        - Yes, with Vimeo Pro
      Can I use the platform even if my school blocks YouTube? 
      - Yes. The application uses a proxy to bypass schools' firewall.
      How do I make my video secure and locked down to my school? 
      - Use Vimeo Pro.  Finally, my evaluation about the technology this webinar uses: gotomeeting.com. I have attended several webinars delivered via this platform and in the past the video/sound quality was pretty good. But in this one I could hardly hear what the presenter was saying. Fortunately gotomeeting also offered a real-time transcription service so that I could read what the presenter had said if I didn't get it. It's pretty cool. Does anyone know if the real-time transcription done by auto voice recognition (the accuracy is surprisingly high) or by human transcribers (because I thought I heard constant keyboard clicks throughout the webinar). If it's the latter the transcriber is amazing.
