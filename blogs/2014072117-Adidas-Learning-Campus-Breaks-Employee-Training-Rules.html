Adidas Learning Campus Breaks Employee Training "Rules"

Kate Meersschaert

July 21, 2014

(Image: via CreativeHuddle.co.uk) Athletic apparel giant, Adidas, headquartered in Germany (guess whose office probably had the World Cup on 24/7), believes that workplace training should be, "light, desirable, and fun." (Read this Forbes.com article for more) With an informal "Learning Campus" (check-out the corresponding Pinterest feed!) that allows employees to partner with mentors, learn new skills and participate in both virtual and in-person (that psychedelic "learning shed" looks amazing!) training. What are your thoughts re: Adidas's out-of-the-box take on professional development? It is interesting to see both a formalized and extremely informal learning pathway created to help better their employees. Do you think we have/or could adopt a similar approach on the mSchool platform?
