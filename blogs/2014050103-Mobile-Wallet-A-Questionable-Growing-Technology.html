Mobile Wallet: A Questionable Growing Technology

Oumar Soumahoro

May 1, 2014

I recently became aware of mobile wallet apps and its profitability during a discussion in my finance class. Mobile wallet app enables big technology companies such as Google and Apple to fight PayPal to win the market. Mobile wallets can be defined as way for customers to checkout products directly from smart devices, and avoid entering their credit card information on every website.

With credit card fraud and cyber scams growing more elaborate and frequently, mobile wallet can be considered as an important way to improve customers' privacy with their credit cards. But like every technology, it has or can have drawbacks.

Is having all our information on a smart-phone app a safe thing to do? Would you switch from using your credit card online to a one-click checkout?

What implications, if any, are there for current and future EdLab products to use a mobile wallet app? Can we build our own?
