Electronic Tags Can Be Etched into Anything, Even Food

Ryan.Allen

May 8, 2018


Itâ€™s almost impossible to track the food you are eating as it travels from farm to table. Researchers at Rice University are trying to change that through a pioneering method of etching a material known as graphene onto food or other substances. Because the material is both conductive and edible, it can be utilized as RFID tags, biosensors, or other types of detectors for food.

Previously, printing could be done on a label, constricting the functionality of the technology. The new process actually converts any material to graphene, making it completely safe to consume. It can also be printed as any shape or logo.

In the future, this edible tech will be able to tell us where an item has been, where it is from, and what its path was. But, do you foresee any resistance to this kind of labelling on food? Add your voice to the discussion on Vialogues.
