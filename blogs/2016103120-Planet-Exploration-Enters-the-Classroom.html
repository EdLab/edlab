Planet Exploration Enters the Classroom

Jenny Abamu

October 31, 2016


"Planet3 is an exploration-based learning company that presents the entire Earth as a living laboratory." (What is Planet3?)

Taking Over the Planet

Planet3 is game-based learning tool that uses augmented reality to take students through missions where they explore the history and future development of the planet. It's only in the beta phase, but an investment of over $13 million has led it to open offices in Nevada, DC, Texas, and California. This ambitious startup has every intention of transforming science education into an interdisciplinary adventure. The launch of its first product, a comprehensive digital middle school science curriculum, was recently debuted at the ArtPrize event in Michigan. Planet3 plans to complete a year of beta testing before the curriculum's complete release to the general public.

Building a Strong Community

Though we might not know all the functionality of the products being developed, we do know that Planet3 has a strong team and learning community. The development team is comprised of award-winning National Geographic Explorers and Producers, software developers and former teachers. They also have strong partnerships with NASA, National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), the University of California, San Diego, and Nevada’s Clark County School District (where the beta testing will be taking place).

The Education Gaming Landscape

With Minecraft’s recent announcement of its new education edition and apps springing up everywhere, the education gaming space seems to offer a lot of competition and promise. Planet3, an all-encompassing design, is putting itself ahead of the rest through its extensive participatory action research and a comprehensive development plan.
