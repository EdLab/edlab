Seeing is Disbelieving: An Even Newer Era of Fake Images

dinosossi2

July 21, 2017


They say that a picture is worth a 1,000 words.

But if you can manipulate it so that it can be shared with 10 people, is it worth 10,000?

New research has demonstrated just how easy it is to manipulate images in ways that become undetectable. In their study, the researchers found that respondents mistakenly identified 40% of altered images as real. Ouch.

Ouch.

There have been previous uproars in the popular media regarding Photoshop manipulation of models. Given societyâ€™s obsession with the new, an industry like modeling that is premised on image seems particularly ripe for this type of visual fakery.

Although it may seem a little unseemly, the damage is relatively limited to the model or publication where (s)he is featured.

However, in criminal cases where visual evidence is shared with jury members or in the political realm where the way a governmental figure can be placed in (un)flattering light, the damage can be much more acute.

As a result, when we navigate our increasingly visually manipulated world, seeing should not necessarily be believing.
