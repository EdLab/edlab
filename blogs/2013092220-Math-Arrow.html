Math Arrow

LauraCostello

September 22, 2013


Math Arrow is a visual math app and the first offering from educational game company Sproglit. The game’s action focuses around the math arrow, a shape that maps numbers from 1 to 100 on a track with visual cues about their relationship to other numbers. Unlike a number line, the position, color, and shape of each number on the arrow reveals information about the number’s divisibility. Developed by economist Todd Buchholz, the app puts neuroscience strategies to work to reveal patterns and inspire number sensibility in users. Buchholz said he was inspired by real world examples of math to develop a learning game that would help instill practical understanding in students.

Pros:

The serious content of the game is cloaked in a fun Australian theme. The whimsical artwork and kangaroo main character seem like they will appeal to young learners. The game incorporates more math lessons through a mini boomerang game that promotes learning about angles while earning extra points. The app showed promise in a recent research study that found it could improve number sense and addition skills in 1st grade students.

Cons:

Math Arrow is designed for young students and the activities may be too repetitive for older users. The visualization is particularly useful for developing a sensibility around multiplication, but may not adequately lay the groundwork for other math concepts. Sproglit is a new company and their future offerings may target older learners with the same attention to foundational knowledge.

Our Takeaway:

Math Arrow and other math games that emphasize skills and styles not often addressed in the traditional classroom have the potential to draw reluctant math students into the fold. The app’s attention to visual learning and its focus on number sense over fact memorization is refreshing and may help its young target audience build positive math experiences.

The Bottom Line:

Math Arrow is a painless way to improve number sense in young students.
