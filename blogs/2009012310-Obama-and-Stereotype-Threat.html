Obama and Stereotype Threat

Jessica Blum

January 23, 2009

As you all know, George and I have been exploring ideas for an EdLab Obama resource -- and for content that can help teachers engage students around issues of democracy, history, identity, and service. But as luck would have it, this recent study shows that Obama himself may help reduce the black-white achievement gap, and that his successful example may reduce the testing anxiety commonly referred to as stereotype threat. It looks like our new president may prove to be the single most valuable "feature" of our teaching tool!
