Increasing PK Contributors in 3 Easy Steps

Rebecca.Sullivan

February 19, 2019

Today, Carmen led a D&R in which we outlined plans to achieve concrete, measurable goals. Our team created an action plan for Pocket Knowledge. Our goal is to increase TC student contributors. Carmen's process showed us that we can work toward that goal with three easy-to-implement, concrete steps. I'm documenting a subset of our goal that came out in our discussion that we could implement this semester.


Goal: 80% of TC Masters students who graduate in May 2019 publish their thesis on PK


1. Introduce PK to TC faculty as a self-publishing resource their Masters students can use to showcase their work. Documents on PK are searchable, although the author can restrict access to the TC community.

  • We thought a straightforward way to introduce PK to faculty would be for Gary to mention its recent update at a faculty meeting, and its potential as a self-publication resource for students

2. EdLab reps visit classrooms to show students how to upload to PK

  • Email the TC faculty of the 7 Spring 2019 courses that enroll Masters students working on theses or capstone projects and ask to do a brief demo in their class
  • If step 1 is unfeasible this semester, step 2 can be enacted without it 

3. Launch an email campaign targeted at graduating Masters students

  • Email the entire student body, advertising PK specifically as a resource to publish finished work like Masters theses. 
  • Send reminder emails at the end of the semester, after thesis deadlines but before graduation. 
  • Advertise PK Masters theses publication on MyTC

In June, we can track how many Masters theses were uploaded to PK. With this knowledge, we can form our strategy for increasing PK student contributors in Fall 2019.


Carmen's D&R produced promising, concrete, actionable ideas. What is the next step to put these strategies into effect?
