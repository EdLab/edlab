The Anti-Wealth

bhughes

October 6, 2006

A while back Anthony posted a rant about the New York Times being overly interested in wealth (e.g., lots of coverage of new cars, fine dining, travel, etc.). This struck me as a fresh observation, and has stayed with me as I read the Times online. As this thought intersects with my thinking about the TC Channel, I am reminded of the unique socio-political dispositions educators often have – and how this could be good content for the channel. The trick, I think, will be to parse out the uninspired views from the truly engaging ones. If we can get that on camera, we'd have some good stuff... So, while I am speaking in abstractions, let me add one additional point. The TC Channel is going to need content that offers a viewer a unique experience – with regard to both entertainment and education. If it's not unique, it just won't stand up against other entertainment... but it also has to be site-specific to make sense. It has to be about the essence of TC. (I know, don't tell me, I've got to lose the tie.)
