The Pragmatic Programmer

Ankit Ranka

April 7, 2011

I just completed reading the book "The Pragmatic Programmer." I came to know about this book from this stackoverflow thread. It's a nicely written book from Andrew Thomas and David Thompson. The main take away for me was the number of ways in which a programmer can go wrong and how cautiously we should be while writing our code. While the book is mostly written from the perspetive of the desktop software, I think many principles stated in the book still hold for web projects. Here is what I think are the three most important pieces of advice from the book,

  • Don't repeat yourself, popularly known as DRY principle
  • Don't live with broken windows, if you see a broken code, fix it now!
  • Test your software ruthlessly
    • As a whole, the book has been really helpful and I have already found myself using some of the concepts while coding. If anyone is interested in reading the book, just stop by my desk.
