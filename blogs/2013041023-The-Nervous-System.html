The Nervous System

Bismark Appiah

April 10, 2013

An interesting topic that I believe every student will enjoy in a psychology class is learning about the nervous system. Within the nervous system, students have the opportunity to learn about the most important structure of the body: the brain. Hence the name psychology (psych means the mind and ology means study of). The job of the nervous system is to gather and process information. In other words, any feelings, ideas, or behaviors that causes a response from a person are controlled by the nervous system. However, there are two main parts of the nervous system: the Central Nervous System (CNS) and the Peripheral Nervous System (PNS), both processes different kinds of responses from a person. The central nervous system consist of the brain and spinal cord; its function is to process, interpret, and store incoming sensory information such as: sounds, smells, color, and pressure on the skin. On the other hand, the peripheral nervous system handles the central nervous system's input and output. In essence, other nerves in the body is controlled by the peripheral nervous system.
