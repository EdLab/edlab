Live Blogging Equity in Education: Abbott v. Burke

Nantwi

November 11, 2009

I am here at the Equity in Education: Abbott v. Burke talk with Jeannie and Gary. The presentation is about the impact of the court case Abbott v. Burke on education in New Jersey. Abbott v. Burke is referred to as the “grandfather of school-financing cases” by the NY Times. The case was first filed in 1981 by the Education Law Center against the state of New Jersey. The suit claimed that the state had failed in its constitutional duty to provide a "thorough and efficient" education for students in poor urban school districts. For close to three decades, the case has come before the state Supreme Court about seven times due to different state laws about school financing. The Abbott case resembles the Campaign for Fiscal Equity's fight to ensure that every student in entitled to a “basic sound education” in New York State.
