The EdLab Review: Graspr

Arianna Choi

November 14, 2009

Graspr Tags: video, instruction, online syndication, web 2.0 www.graspr.com Price: free Overall 4.5 Design 4 Pedagogy 5 [The Good] Videos are chopped into scenes by instructors so that learners can repeat or skip to a desired point. The panel next to video player allows learners to navigate between text notes, supplementary attachments and basic stats while the instructional video is on play. As far as being considerate of individual's learning pace and creating personal collections, Graspr gets two thumbs up. [The Bad] It's repeated in the full review: be aware of advertisements. Assuming that commercial ads - no matter how personalized - distract learners, having every video page painted with Google ads and related products from Amazon can be a disservice to some. [The Bottom Line] Impressive interaction design though the aesthetic taste for visuals (e.g. icons) is open for debate. Graspr would get 5 out of 5 for all review categories if Google ads and related products from Amazon weren't so distracting. If, however, a learner finds the related (thus expandable) information helpful, then this could be considered an effective learning tool. Recommended use I'm reluctant to recommend this to K-12 classroom settings, especially if ad-free or privacy is a top priority. For informal, personal learning experience though, this is an excellent video sharing and syndication platform. Also, you can earn additional income by uploading your own instructional videos, embedding other people's videos on your blog or website, or simply by referring others to any Graspr videos since Graspr shares its net advertising revenue with producers (30%) and publishers (30%). So if you want to project your talent in producing (or promoting) instructional videos, this is for you. Read Full Review
