The Explorers Club

EdLab Studios

January 26, 2014


A venerable 100+ year old NY institution that just might be home to the real "Most Interesting Man in the World," The Explorers Club connects and supports explorers devoted to furthering scientific knowledge. They are biologists, conservationists, anthropologists, astronauts, geographers, and everyone else who sees a wide world of wonder from which we always have more to learn. Members as diverse as Jane Godall, Gene Roddenberry, Neil Armstrong, and James Cameron have swapped stories and given lectures at this club. Visitors and members alike are welcome to join events such as "Sea Stories" in which explorers share their research concerning the largely unknown depths of the ocean. In addition to lectures, The Explorers Club serves as a museum of artifacts from past legendary exploits that inspire and remind visitors of remarkable accomplishments. Perhaps the best opportunity at The Explorers Club, however, is to learn from and be inspired by fellow explorers, past and present.
