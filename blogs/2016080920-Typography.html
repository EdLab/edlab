Typography

EdLab Studios

August 9, 2016


Type enthusiasts spend hours poring over a line of type, years developing a font, and five intense weeks honing their craft at the Type@Cooper summer program. The condensed summer counterpart to Cooper Union's full year part-time program, attracts members of a small but passionate type community from around the globe yearning to unleash their inner type geek and dive quill-first into the study of typography and type design.

Before embarking on their own creations, students begin with a history lesson in type, looking at the evolution of letterforms in response to the societal demands of speed and aesthetics over time while getting their hands dirty with some traditional ink calligraphy. Classes like script lettering cultivate an understanding of texture, spacing, contrast, and proportion where students build unique pieces for logos and branding purposes. Field trips to typographically significant locations throughout NYC and access to the design ephemera, rare books, and posters housed in the on-site Herb Lubalin Study Center of Design and Typography offer students invaluable reference points and inspiration to inform their work at this certificate program and beyond. With a focus on the purpose and power of the medium, typefaces developed by students spring from usage scenarios and can evoke the decadence of a French pastry shop, speed of a new car, or authority of a news publication and be visible while on the move or in the dark. Whether designing the typographical identity of a fashion magazine, restaurant, or custom tattoo, Type@Cooper instills a true appreciation of type and a whole new way of thinking about the world of letters that surrounds us.
