Digital Textbooks

Christopher Zamora

May 26, 2013

Secretary of Education Arne Duncan and Federal Communications Commission chairman Julius Genachowski are pushing schools to use these digital textbooks within the next five years. The push for these textbooks came when Apple announced it will start selling versions of standard high school books for use on the iPad. I believe it's a great idea to bring digital texts books into the classroom because it could benefit students in many ways. For instance, students could search and find information faster with digital textbooks instead of a regular textbook where you have to go through countless pages. Secretary Duncan further expands on his approval of digital textbooks:

"Do we want kids walking around with 50-pound backpacks and every book in those backpacks costing 50, 60, 70 dollars and many of them being out of date? Or, do we want students walking around with a mobile device that has much more content than was even imaginable a couple years ago and can be constantly updated? I think it's a very simple choice," Duncan said in an interview.
