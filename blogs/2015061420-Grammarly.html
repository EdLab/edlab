Grammarly

Alexandra Lederman

June 14, 2015


Grammarly is a grammar and spell check web browser extension. Once downloaded, the extension works on any web page including email, blogs. Twitter, Facebook, and Linkedin. Grammarly also provides enhanced vocabulary usage and advanced explanations. The major learning curve for using this app is hovering over the red underlined mistake rather than the more common right-click found on most sites.

Pros:

Grammarly works for emails, blogs, Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, and other web text, so users will be able to access it seamlessly across all the sites they visit. Students and teachers can catch spelling and grammar mistakes on different media platforms before publishing to the web. Grammarly provides advanced explanations, empowering students and other users not only to correct their mistakes, but also to learn why they made a mistake, resulting in fewer future spelling and grammar issues.

Cons:

Unfortunately, Grammarly does not work in Google Docs nor in Hangouts, Google's chat client. Users must change their right-click spell check habits and learn to hover over mistakes to harness the full power of the app.

Our Takeaway:

Grammarly has the potential to change the way we spell and grammar check by providing a method to learn grammar while fixing mistakes. This app is easy to download and use. The intuitive design, cross-platform functionality, and educational characteristics make Grammarly a great spelling and grammar tool for classrooms and homework.
