Looking for the Future of Energy in Bacteria

Ryan.Allen

December 4, 2017


Scientists have discovered a kind of bacteria that can convert sunlight into energy. These microorganisms are covered with tiny semiconductors that are used as a defense against hazardous elements. This natural process produces acid that can be turned into fuel.

The transformation is similar to plant photosynthesis, but these organisms are much more efficient. Around 80% of the sunlight that hits these germs can be converted into energy. Current commercial solar panel technology can only operate at around a 20% conversion rate.
