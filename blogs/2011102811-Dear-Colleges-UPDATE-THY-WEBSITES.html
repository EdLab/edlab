Dear Colleges, UPDATE THY WEBSITES!

Janet Jang

October 28, 2011

When a high school senior wants to learn more about the colleges he or she wants to apply to, what do you think is the first thing they do? Go borrow the Princeton Review 376 Best Colleges book from their older sibling? Flip through the U.S. News & World Report: Best College Rankings magazine? Visit the school?! No, the very FIRST thing that student would probably do is check out the school's website. It is the main portal to a higher education school, especially for these young tech-savvy high school students. Not only is a school's web presence important for prospective students, but also for current students as well. Best Education Sites gathered a panel of 2,000+ specialists from the fields of graphic design, web development, and college counseling to analyze the state of academic web space in 2011.

(Interesting data about academic web presence, font use, and clean coding!)
Their comprehensive data set shows that a college's web presence is more than just marketing - but it is often an indicator of an institution's overall quality. THIS is why I was so annoyed that the web presence of my undergrad college was so blah and why I was so frustrated it took Teachers College a WHOLE YEAR to revamp their website (GAHH!) I understand that making a good website is difficult - it is a highly creative and intellectual process. However, I believe it is worth the investment for schools to improve their cyber-identity and to get more involved in social media as a whole.
