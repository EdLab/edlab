Google Street View... in Space!

Ryan.Allen

August 20, 2017


Google has teamed up with the International Space Station (ISS) and Thinkspace to bring Street View to outer space. In this vialogue, engineers, researchers, and the astronauts describe why and how they made this technological challenge a reality. The station itself is not only a marvel of science and engineering, but also a monument to international cooperation and world peace. Reflecting these values, the team members involved in this project have come from diverse backgrounds and countries from across the world. From the space station, to mission control, to Google headquarters, everyone involved played an important role in this impressive cooperative feat.

The Street View of ISS allows users to stroll through the massive station, giving unprecedented access for exploration of the work and living spaces on board. Viewers can look out portholes down onto our big blue planet, just like astronauts living up there. While Street View cannot replicate the feeling of zero gravity, it certainly can bring insight into life on the space station a little closer to earth. After watching the video have a look yourself.
