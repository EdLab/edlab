Watercliffe Meadow: "A Place for Learning"

HSC

January 4, 2009

Watercliffe Meadow Primary School, in Sheffield, South Yorkshire, England, has changed its name to "Watercliffe Meadow: A Place for Learning" because of the "negative connotations of school." Headteacher Linda Kingdon adds: "Instead, we want this to a be a place for family learning, where anyone can come. We were able to start from scratch and create a new type of learning experience. There are no whistles or bells or locked doors. We wanted to deinstitutionalise the place and bring the school closer to real life.‚Äù Many have criticized the move because it is unclear how the how "a place for learning" is different from a "school." I can understand that part of the rationale for the name change is to get students, teachers, families, and community members to think differently about what is going on in the "building with classroom and books." At the same time, the term "school" is a helpful bit of terminology. That is why I think it makes sense to refer to the EdLab set of educational programs (content and form) as the EdLab School.
