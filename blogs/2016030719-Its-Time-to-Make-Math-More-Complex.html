It's Time to Make Math More Complex

Jenny Shen

March 7, 2016


Amidst the looming presence of math Common Core standards and state exams, it might seem like it would be in teachers’ best interest to get down to the basics. But nonstop worksheets and drills are not the most effective ways to get students excited about the subject. After all, a basketball player who benefits from agility drills would probably not be thrilled if he or she didn’t get to play the game.

This is why Bridge to Enter Advanced Mathematics (BEAM) takes a different approach—providing students with challenging and complex math problems to get them thinking outside the box. And instead of focusing on right-or-wrong answers, BEAM is centered around the exploratory side of learning math.

How can educators (and parents) get students interested in math while still addressing some of the more fundamental skills behind different concepts? Can harder problems and more rigorous assignments cause students to be more engaged, or will they cause them to check out? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
