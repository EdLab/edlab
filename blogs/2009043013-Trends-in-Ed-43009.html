Trends in Ed, 4.30.09

Arianna Choi

April 30, 2009

Study on Twitter Quitters says Loyalty lasts One Month A study by Nielsen Online claims that more than 60 percent of new Twitter users give up on the service one month after signing up. Reasons for quitting twitter, however, have not been identified though the popular assumption is that many users sign up just to see what the commotion is about. Similarly, no investigation has been done on their loyal counterparts.

Twitter has enjoyed a nice ride over the last few months, but it will not be able to sustain its meteoric rise without establishing a higher level of user loyalty. Frankly, if Oprah can't accomplish that, I'm not sure who can. - David Martin, Vice President, Primary Research, Nielsen Online
Faith Fighters: Lessons on Religion? Italian game design team La Molleindustria launched Faith Fighter 2 a year after its release of the infamous flash-based fighting game that outraged over one billion Muslims across the globe. The sequel is a simpler game in which the goal is to give love and respect to all religious entities by clicking them on the screen. It's targeted at children of all ages as well as adults of diverse professional backgrounds to teach tolerance, say the game developers. But the satirical voice in their game rule design seems to keep popular media reporting about it in an un-peaceful way. To that end, achieving one of La Molleindustria's goals, which is to drive a gamevolution by using game as a means to spark social and political discussions, has been successful.
