Externality

abagigah92

March 31, 2014

In economics, externalities are considered economic side effects. It can also be costs or benefits arising from an economic activity that affect somebody other than the people engaged in the economic activity and are not reflected fully in price. For instance, smoke pumped out by a factory may impose clean-up costs on nearby residents; bees kept to produce honey may pollinate plants belonging to a nearby farmer, thus boosting his crop. This causes market failure because these costs and benefits do not form part of the calculations of the people deciding whether to go ahead with the economic activity. If the externality is beneficial, the market will provide too little; if it is a cost, the market will supply too much. A potential solution is regulation; if the externality is negative, it is a tax on the activity or, if the externality is positive, a subsidy. But the most efficient solution to externalities is to require them to be included in the overall calculations of those engaged in the economic activity, so there is self-regulation.
