A Shout-Out for Laurel and "Dimensions of PocketKnowledge"

Stephanie Hunt

August 29, 2009

One of our summer design group interns, Laurel, just wrapped up her time with us yesterday. I wanted to tell you all about this great project she designed, produced, and installed. It's called Dimensions of PocketKnowledge, and you can check it out on the first floor of Gottesman Libraries. Laurel found so many interesting historic photos and works of art as she browsed PK, and you'll find some of her favorite elements in the work on display. They're like uber-collages. Laurel also did a great job of documenting the sources she used for her work - the label that's installed alongside the graphics will point you towards the title of the source image, as well as its related pocket in PK. I'm so excited that library staff will be able to refer to Laurel's graphics as they introduce new students to the PocketKnowledge digital archive during new student orientation. This is a great way to get people excited about the beautiful images available to everyone online via PK. Kudos to Laurel from the entire Design team!!
