It's Time: A TCR Update

Pravin Sathe

August 25, 2008

The bell is tolling for TCR...to get a redesign/rearchitecture/new infrastructure. Over the next couple of months, we'll be rolling out some new wireframes and design(s) of TCR that will stabilize it for the next 2-3 years. This is not cosmetic surgery, this is rethinking it from the ground up in terms of content delivery, information hierarchy, graphic design et al. After some initial meetings here are some interesting tidbits for all you research junkies: 1. a lot of users are getting their TCR fix via the newsletter and attempting to find the information on the site in the same presentation method with (obviously) little success (The newsletter is "curated" differently than the website) 2. the payment/registration system is not the best in the world and is rather counter intuitive in some parts 3. there is little to no information hierarchy and users are having a difficult time discerning relative importance of one item versus another 4. For our institutional friends (read librarians) we have made it particularly difficult to get to the information they need in an intuitive and fast manner 5. and much much more... It's a daunting task to say the least (information from 1900 - today) but we're pretty excited about setting up a scholarly journal that can be the model not just for research but also a beacon for gaining prestige in the Education/Humanities community (read: tenure track professorships ;) so hold on tight, lots more ahead. Next update should come somewhere in the middle of September with some flowcharts. Yum, flowcharts. Awesome.
