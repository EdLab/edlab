Woodland Ecology Research Mentorship

EdLab Studios

February 9, 2014


Located at the Bronx's Wavehill public garden and cultural center, the Woodland Ecology Research Mentorship is a 14-month program based on the philosophy that high school students can do high-level ecology research and make a serious contribution to their field of study. Through WERM, students take free college courses in Geographic Information Systems and Environmental Science, employ their learning in ecological restoration work and ongoing field research/monitoring, and work with a mentor on long-term research projects to be published and used for ecological restoration projects in NYC. This program, besides challenging high school students to step up their scientific game with college coursework, real fieldwork, and professional research papers, combines hands-on scientific learning with immersion in and mentoring by the scientific community. Students work alongside environmental scientists as they gather ecological data, and they receive intensive individual mentoring as they work on long-term research projects and gain insight into the world of scientific research.
