VC's Propose the Top Seven Trends for 2012... What are Yours?

Kate Meersschaert

December 17, 2012

Check-out this post from Inc.com. Do you agree with the author, Inc.com staff writer, Eric Markowitz, and his round-up of the top tech predictions for 2013? Here is my summary of Eric's takeaways for 2013: 1) "Big data" will help us to create even "smarter" and more responsive marketing campaigns. 2) Digital natives are becoming founders at a growing rate that will only accelerate as technology becomes more a part of young-people's lives. 3) Social networks and increasingly connected platforms makes viral use acquisition even more accessible... and funding less dire to launch a MVP (minimal viable product). 4) An alternative "marketplace" is developing for courses, services and products... think Skillshare.com, Etsy.com etc.! Bartering with time, money and "knowledge!" (Also, check-out this site to find music tutors!) 5) "Curation" will become even more of a part of eCommerce... with experts making "must-have" lists for consumers. (Even in education!) 6) The "Freemium" model is here to stay... and perhaps grow and permeate even more industries. 7) B2B, once thought to be dying out... will only increase in popularity... with solutions for previously overlooked industries and fields.
