Intelligence Community Set to Launch A-Space

Nantwi

September 8, 2008

As I was scouring CNN's website for the latest on the presidential election, I came across an article about how the popularity of social networking sites such as Facebook and MySpace is spreading to our highly secretive intelligence community. On September 22nd, the intelligence community-FBI, CIA, and National Security Agency, among others-will launch its own social networking intended to share information among the various intelligence communities. The site, A-Space, is only available for analysts in the 16 U.S. intelligence agencies. The primary goal of A-Space is to protect America by assessing all the information available to spy agencies. Missing crucial data can have enormous implications as evidenced by the FBI agent who sent an e-mail before September 11, 2001, warning of people learning to fly airplanes but not learning to land them. Since the material on A-Space is highly classified, it won't be available for the general public like Facebook and MySpace. It is available to only those intelligence personnel with the proper security clearance or has a reason to be examining particular information. In addition, analysts can add friends on A-Space the way people can on Facebook but nobody outside the intelligence community will ever know.
