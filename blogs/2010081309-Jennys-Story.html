Jenny’s Story

Julia Martin

August 13, 2010

Title: Jenny's story: taking the long view of the child: Prospect's philosophy in action Authors: Patricia F. Carini & Margaret Himley ; with Carol Christine, Cecilia Espinosa, and Julia Fournier Publisher: New York : Teachers College Press, c2010 Check It Out:  LB1115 .C278 2010 c.2 From the Publisher: By carefully documenting how space was made for Jenny—a child who didn’t fit the school mold—this book offers a renewed sense of human possibility and an attainable vision of what schools can be. The authors demonstrate that it is only by attending to each and every child that schooling can begin to achieve its most noble aim: equality. .. About the Author: Patricia F. Carini is a co-founder of Prospect School and the Prospect Archives and Center for Education and Research. She is the author of Starting Strong: A Different Look at Children, Schools, and Standards. Margaret Himley served on the Prospect Board of Trustees and is a professor of writing and rhetoric at Syracuse University. Himley and Carini are co-editors of From Another Angle: Children’s Strengths and School Standards.
