Ask an Astronaut

jvazquez

April 3, 2013


Behold the future of education: space classrooms.

Thanks to the affordances of technology, International Space Station Commander Chris Hadfield can hold office hours while floating in the thermosphere. He is on a mission to bring the awesomeness of space to a younger generation and knows exactly how to reach them: social media.

It’s hard to pinpoint exactly what caused the Canadian astronaut’s meteoric rise to fame, but it’s safe to say it was likely a combination of his attempts to spread the good word of science. Between the Q&A discussion he held on Reddit, the Google+ hangout, and the great number of educational YouTube videos he’s created, Hadfield displays a level of social media savvy not often seen from astronauts. It’s Lady Gaga-level savviness.

Let’s be honest. Haven’t you always wanted to know if floating in space is like swimming? Or how astronauts wash their hands with no running water (featured above)? We now have the ability to answer these questions in a vastly more fulfilling way than by Google search alone. Hats off to Chris Hadfield for blazing the trail of new learning into the final frontier.
