Learning Sports and the Brain

AbdulMalik

March 2, 2016

Learning is an art that we should appreciate on a daily basis. Learning comes in different forms. It includes but not limited to simple and complex activities. Most adults have the misconception that certain activities cannot be performed once they reach a certain age. For some adults, learning a new language or a physical activity is too daunting. The brain goes hand in hand with learning and we essentially feed the brain with information anytime we learn. This article sheds light on the scientific prove that learning a new sport is actually good for the brain. Unlike video games, these activities can also provide physical and mental benefits as well. By learning and practicing a new sport as an adult, you develop a sense of hope in yourself while reminding yourself that your brain is as responsive as it has always been. It is not too late to learn to ride a bicycle or how to juggle a soccer ball. Learning is essentially a lifelong endeavor, regardless of the platform or what you learning.
