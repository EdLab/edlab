Volcanoes: Coming To A Classroom Near You

jvazquez

January 28, 2014


Hollywood has perfected the art of anticipation. Picture it: you’re in a movie theater, popcorn in your lap, gigantic cup of Coca-cola snug in your armrest. The lights go down and the first preview comes on. "Who are you??" A big-name actor shouts. A car screeches and crashes into a packed restaurant, people scream, and gunshots rip through the air. Then comes an explosion so intense it envelopes the entire screen in black, cutting all the lights in the theater until the words, "Coming to theaters, summer 2014" silently creep up on the screen. You turn to your friend and in an excited whisper say, "Oh, that looks good."

It’s about time education cashed in on the high production values and gripping visual displays that have captivated audiences for decades. Frank Gregorio, of Gregorio Educational Productions, has created a series of trailers designed to be used by teachers to stimulate student curiosity in a new subject area. They build anticipation in a way that makes students excited to learn more. These short, thrilling blasts of entertainment have wide appeal that extends beyond the classroom to the larger pool of learners in need of a jump start.

Want to whet your appetite for a volcano lesson? Watch the video above (especially if you aren’t interested in learning about them) and add your first impressions in the ongoing discussion on Vialogues. You can also find a link to more videos from Gregorio Educational Productions in the discussion.
