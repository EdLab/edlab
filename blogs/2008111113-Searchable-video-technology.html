Searchable video technology?

zhou

November 11, 2008

Anyone familiar with searchable video technologies? Here I don't mean the technologies such as Google Video or YouTube where one can search for a specific video clip. I mean the technology to search WITHIN a video clip for desired segments. I had this idea from Faisai's DOW (Discussion Overlay WebApp) idea he came up in yesterday's R&D research meeting. Since we can associate discussions to specific video frames, we can also allow users to label the video frames (similar to Google Image Labeler) so that the video will become searchable. If frame is too small a unit, we can use some intelligent video-recognition technology to automatically break a video clip into segments (I don't know how to call that technology. But I refer to something similar to that in Windows Movie Maker which can automatically break video clips into segments while being imported). So does such technology exist already or not? Anyway I think it will be very helpful in education to allow searching for segments and comments within a video clip.
