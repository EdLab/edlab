What can we learn from Uber?

Carmen James

June 4, 2014

Uber has been disrupting the market. Its had city officials pulling their hair out about laws and requirements. Its had taxi drivers either protesting or getting on board. And with its surge market prices, its had customers shocked. Yet, despite this, Uber is making its way into markets across the globe. When Voxy visited, the great start-up featured on NLT, we learned about how Uber hired Voxy to teach taxi drivers in Brazil English for the influx of arriving tourists for the world cup. It seems there are three secrets to Uber's success: 1. Know your market AND understand that every country let alone every city and county and district in the United States has a unique culture and set of needs. 2. Don't be afraid to try new things, try everything. Whether its acting as a delivery service or having people drive their own cars, let no rock remain unturned. 3. Keep expanding and imagining. Will Uber be the new Amazon? Everything from take-out to plants can arrive at your door with a simple pressed button? What can the education space learn from this? Education is very different because innovations directly effect the formative development of children. It seems, however, that edtech companies could do well, as many of them already do, to intimately know their audience. And understanding this means understanding a particular example and understanding what in a particular example can be universalized to a broad example. 1. Know your users. 2. Never stop iterating. 3.
