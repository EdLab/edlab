A Wearable, Disposable, Lifesaving Medical Device

DebraLee

March 10, 2018


This band-aid sized disposable peel-and-stick health sensor continuously monitors a hospital out-patient’s vitals, accurately recording electrocardiogram, heart rate and heart rate variability, respiratory rate, skin temperature, posture, and steps in real time. It can also detect a fall. The device uses Bluetooth to send data to an app or hub where it is accessed by the wearer’s care providers.

The VitalPatch is disposable, non-rechargeable, and lasts for five days, making it ideal for in-patient hospital stays which, in the US, average 4.5 days. This means nursing staff do not have to change the device, hand record data, or wake the patient in the night to check vital signs.
