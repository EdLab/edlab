Hope For Math Haters

jvazquez

March 27, 2013


Why should we use technology to teach math? First the easy answers: it makes learning fun and engaging for students. And while that’s true, there is a deeper, more profound reason: technology can bring even the most complicated (and mundane) concepts to life. With a subject like math, being able to reach students means you’ve already won half the battle.

Jeremy Roschelle, director of the Center for Technology in Learning at SRI International, and Ken Koedinger, professor of human-computer interaction and psychology at Carnegie Mellon University, explain how technology can help students succeed in math. Both have experience in building well-regarded technologies that bring math to life: Roschelle helped to create SimCalc while Koedinger brought about Cognitive Tutors. Both agree that getting students to actively participate in the learning experience is the ultimate goal; math achievement is an added benefit.

While technology can never replace a good teacher (though there have been attempts), it certainly can share in the responsibility of educating a generation of learners among whom the "majority" can’t do math. We need all the help we can get. Agree/disagree? Add your thoughts to the ongoing discussion on Vialogues.
