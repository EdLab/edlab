Photos and Recap: Walkabout NYC

Kevin M. Gong

June 13, 2011

EdLab! Full set of images here Walkabout NYC on Friday gave us a behind-the-scenes look at numerous startups around the city. Our group visited TechStars, Knewton, and Hunch to learn more about how these companies operated and gather notes for Launch Pad 39B. Our first stop (after being completely misdirected by Google Maps) was TechStars, a prominent tech startup incubator. Pivotal Labs, which shares the space with TechStars. TechStars selects 10-12 startups for its summer incubator program from a pool of thousands. Focusing primarily on mentorship, TechStars provides these startup teams with access to 200 mentors (investors, experienced entrepreneurs, etc) in the NYC area. When selecting each class of startups, TechStars focuses almost exclusively in evaluating the team composition, dynamic, etc before considering each team's ideas and market size. Our next stop was Knewton. Knewton's primary product is an adaptive test prep tool that learns how the user learns, providing more relevant lessons and an individualized learning path as the user completes more content. Priced at only $150 for their SAT prep tool, Knewton is aiming to provide an alternative to traditional test prep institutions such as Kaplan and Princeton Review. Our final stop was Hunch. Hunch focuses on developing a recommendation system based on who one follows on Twitter. As an interesting side note: Hunch had, by far, the most relaxed atmosphere of the three. More pictures here.
