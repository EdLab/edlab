Research and Reference Project

Carly Ryan

January 12, 2015

For my research project, I chose to research stuttering in bilingual children. My approach is to explore the current landscape of speech therapy for non-native speakers with this disorder. So in order to keep things current, I decided to use sources published within the last 6 years. My first source for exploring this topic is the Journal of Fluency Disorders, which provides current and comprehensive coverage of the latest research related to stuttering. TC Library has earlier copies available in the closed stacks, and anything after 2005 would have to be accessed through Columbia's electronic resources. Despite this accessibility issue, this journal would be really useful to anyone seeking recent research on stuttering. One such article is "Multilingual Children Who Stutter: Clinical Issues" by Rosalee C Shenker published Jan. 2011. Another useful journal is the Journal of Clinical Linguistics and Phonetics, which is more easily accessible through TC's online resources. One of the first relevant articles that fits the initial scope of this research is "Judging Stuttering in an Unfamiliar Language". While there are many other articles and dissertations about bilingual children with stutters, at this point I would try to narrow this research topic. Many of the dissertations on bilingual children who stutter focus on a particular native language (Korean, Chinese, Spanish, German, Russian, etc). Others focus on particular age groups (pre-schoolers, kindergartners, elementary students, etc). To be continued!
