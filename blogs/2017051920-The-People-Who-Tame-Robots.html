The People Who Tame Robots

saraehardman

May 19, 2017


Madeline Gannon is a robot tamer. Every day, she works to make robots more responsive to human gesture and movement. As she walks before the large arm of an industrial robot, it reacts to her motion in a way reminiscent of a curious dog or the Pixar lamp. She is part of a team of designers, engineers, and artists dedicated to changing the way humans and robots interact.

There has been much fear recently that robots will replace us in the workplace, and the research team Madeline’s part of is working against that notion. "Humans and robots are companion species on this planet," she says. "We need each other." Instead of thinking of it as either robots or humans, Madeline says we should think of it as robots and humans. Together, we can do more than either group individually. The key, the team thinks, is putting humans at the center of the interaction and making the robots more responsive, adaptive, and interactive.
