LearnSphere. Inspiration for Data Dashboard and mSchool. Collaboration?

tzaffi

September 29, 2015

Today, Hui Soo pointed out an interesting project that we should seriously consider as we design and build out mSchool and Data Dashboard. The project is called LearnSphere and an article at the Hechinger report describes it as a data scrubbed and secure version of the doomed inBloom initiative. But LearnSphere seems to have been carefully designed in more dimensions than just privacy concerns. So what was that cool teaser visual all about? This is a proof of concept of the type of research and analysis that the LearnSphere ecosystem is supposed to enable. It shows the connection between student online activity on a MOOC and their success in their course and their online interactions with other students. Here, an associated project called MOOCdb which is kind of like the Data Dashboard of EdX, is being used to generate the data that drives the visualization. The visualization is created on their MOOCviz site and exposes the visualization creation process. Scroll down and click on the various icons to see how data stored on the MOOCdb data warehouse is culled with a data extraction script (Python and SQL), exported to a local file, aggregated for statistical purposes (Python), converted to public data (JSON), transformed into the visualization script (HTML and D3.js) which is then displayed. I suspect that the MOOCviz platform is either based on JupyterHub (just like our new Data Dashboard) or is closely related. To learn more about the data science behind MOOCdb check out MOOCdb: Developing Standards and Systems to support MOOC Data Science
