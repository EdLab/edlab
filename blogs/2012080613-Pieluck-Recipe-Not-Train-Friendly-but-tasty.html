Pieluck Recipe: Not-Train-Friendly but tasty

Rebecca Hyams

August 6, 2012

[Keep Private]

Plum Streusel Pie

When Jo floated around the idea of having a Pieluck lunch, I was thinking of making a pie to bring. But I realized it wouldn't really survive my commute of a train and two subways. So in lieu of making it, I'm sharing the recipe. It's super easy to make (especially if you start with a pre-made frozen crust). I got the recipe from epicurious. I'm just including the most important parts of the recipe. The source also has pie crust ingredients and a lemon ice milk to have as a beverage. Filling and topping 3/4 cup finely chopped walnuts (about 3 ounces) 1/2 cup all purpose flour 3 tablespoons (packed) golden brown sugar 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon 3 tablespoons chilled unsalted butter, cut into 1/2-inch cubes 3/4 cup sugar 1/4 cup cornstarch 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1 1/2 pounds plums (about 5 large), halved, pitted, cut into 1/2-inch-thick wedges 1 large egg beaten with 2 teaspoons water (for glaze) For filling and topping: Preheat oven to 400Â°F. Mix walnuts, flour, brown sugar, and 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon in medium bowl to blend. Add butter and rub in with fingertips until coarse meal forms. Set streusel aside. Whisk 3/4 cup sugar, cornstarch, nutmeg, and remaining 1 teaspoon cinnamon in large bowl. Add plums and toss to coat. Place pie dish on baking sheet. Brush inside of pie shell lightly with egg glaze. Transfer plum filling to pie shell, mounding slightly in center. Sprinkle streusel topping evenly over filling. Bake pie 15 minutes; reduce oven temperature to 375Â°F. Bake until filling bubbles at edges, about 35 minutes, covering crust edges with foil during last 15 minutes if browning too quickly. Cool pie on rack. (Can be made 8 hours ahead. Cover and let stand at room temperature.) Cut pie into wedges.
