Online Publishing and Content Management Questions

gnatriello

November 20, 2007

EdLab has identified innovation in Online Publishing as a foundational project area in which we plan to commit resources and develop capacity. This area is important because: 1) it builds on the historical role of publishing in the academic world, 2) it builds on the traditional role of libraries, 3) it responds to one of the emerging roles of libraries, and 4)(perhaps most important) it offers opportunities to be innovative in the development, distribution, utilization, and evaluation of essential learning resources. We have not even scratched the surface in terms of innovation in online publishing. To date our efforts in the area of publishing have consisted of: 1) Website Publishing, e.g., EdLab Site, Library Site, Levees Site — largely via Drupal 2) Robust Journal Publishing, e.g., TCR, CCRC — largely via ContentWorks 3) Expedient Journal Publishing, e.g., TESOL, History of Math Ed — largely via OJS However, since we abandoned the approach (i.e., comprehensive, integrated publishing resources offered as service) embodied in ContentWorks several years ago, we have not articulated a strategy to move Online Publishing into the future in an innovative way. We need to begin to develop a strategy to innovate in online publishing and identify the tool set that will be required for this effort. Key questions include: 1) What do we view as the path of innovation in online publishing? What are the key elements of such a path? 2) Do we believe that is it possible to identify one or more existing content management tools that we will adopt as standards to support a variety of projects? What capacities will such tools provide? (e.g., presentation, search, e-commerce, registration, personalization) 3) Can we identify areas where we will need to develop content management tools essential for the type of innovation we wish to pursue? 4) What steps must we take to make our current content management configurations stable, maintainable, and transferable? 5) Do we believe that any or all of our publishing efforts should be integrated with PocketKnowledge 2.0?
