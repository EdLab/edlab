Why is Education a Crime for Baha'is?

Bismark Appiah

June 23, 2015

How can education be considered a crime? The denotation of crime is an act that is foolish or wrong. In Iran, it's a crime for Baha’is to obtain education. Baha’i is the largest religious minority group in Iran. Many Iranian Baha’is have been denied access to most basic rights, such as education. The reason for this unfair treatment towards Baha’is dates back to 1863, the year Baha’u’llah founded the Baha’i faith. According to this piece, Baha’u’llah’s teachings on the equality of men and women and universal education challenged the established Muslim clergy from the beginning. As a result, Baha’is became targets to the Iranian government. The Iranian government refers to Baha’is as “deviants”, therefore Iranian Muslims shouldn’t interact with them. I believe this treatment towards Baha’is is wrong, and it’s a crime. One can’t be denied access to its own future because of religious and ethnic reasons.
