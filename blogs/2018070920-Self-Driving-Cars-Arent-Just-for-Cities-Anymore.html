Self-Driving Cars Aren’t Just for Cities Anymore

Melanie.Hering

July 9, 2018


In a country as geographically vast as the United States, it shouldn’t come as a surprise that the majority of country roads haven't been digitally mapped. While this challenge is nothing new for people, it does present a technical challenge with the advent of self-driving cars. Autonomous vehicles rely largely on 3D maps that include the exact locations of lanes, curbs, and signs, information which isn’t available for many country roads. This makes self-driving cars far better suited to cities and other well-traveled areas.

To address the problems these vehicles face on unmapped roads, MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory has created a system called MapLite, which only requires GPS and a series of sensors that detect road conditions. The researchers hope that this technology will increase the feasibility and safety of autonomous vehicles in more rural areas.

Do you think this technology will be able to solve the problems that unmapped roads pose for self-driving cars? Would you trust a car to drive more safely in the country than a human? Join the discussion on Vialogues.
