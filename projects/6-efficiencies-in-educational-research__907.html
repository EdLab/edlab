6. EFFICIENCIES IN EDUCATIONAL RESEARCH

Finding adequate support for educational research is a continuing problem for scholars in all segments of the field. As the costs of many of the standard research approaches increase and the sources of funding fail to keep pace, educational researchers find themselves caught in what seems to be a losing battle to secure sufficient support to conduct studies that provide a sound basis to advance the field and guide improvements in educational policy and practice. Researchers with interests that depart from current policy concerns have difficulty maintaining active programs of research and those whose research agendas converge with present policy matters must guard against unacknowledged political influence. Some have redoubled efforts to compete for limited research funds while others have become advocates for increased support of research in education. To address the growing imbalance between the support for and the cost of educational research EdLab is focusing energy and attention on developing tools, techniques and strategies that promise to reduce the cost of educational research in substantial ways. Some of this work aims to streamline current processes, and some of it is oriented to reconfiguring the educational research enterprise more dramatically.
