1.06 Media Design Center at the Gottesman Libraries

EdLab founded and supports a Media Design Center (MDC) that serves as a resource for the creation of multimedia products for faculty, students, and administration at Teachers College. MDC is a service unit of the Gottesman Libraries. MDC is a hub for production, and staff engage in all kinds of design and audio/video production work for internal and external clients. They support and document this work with a goal of expanding the community's knowledge of design processes. Documentation is also a vehicle for collaboration on projects within the community. Located on the fifth floor of Russell Hall at Teachers College, MDC is situated within the EdLab Atelier. This semi-public workspace is home to several functional workgroups. Visitors experience MDC as a temporary home for their projects, as well as a source of expert knowledge on art, design, and production. Based on the atelier model, the physical layout of the Center is meant to encourage collaboration and hands-on, over-the-shoulder instruction. Examples of projects by EdLab's Design Team:

  • Multimedia Exhibitions at the Gottesman Libraries such as 100 Years: A Centennial Celebration of the Program in Nutrition at Teachers College , Inspirations (Student works from the courses of Arthur Wesley Dow), and Where They Stand (Election 2008).
  • Hosting EdLab's Digital Art Residency and Edit Jams.
  • Creating data visualizations such as A Year in the Library.
  • Supporting EdLab's Publishing Team on user interface and interaction design.
  • Supporting After Ed TV with graphic design.
Examples of projects by EdLab's Video Team:
  • Piloting and supporting EdLab's experimental scholarly online video channel project, After Ed TV.
  • Creating education-focused documentary projects such as the NY2NO Documentary.
  • Hosting youth-oriented video programs such as Your Summer Learning Contest and video production internships.
