EdLab Seminar: The Changing Landscape of Educational Technology

Jun 5, 2012 2:48 PM

Learning is moving beyond the textbook, away from the classroom and into the cloud. Software and technology are constantly reshaping the way students learn and educators teach. This presentation will explore the changing landscape of education technology, highlight important trends and resources, and discuss how educators can leverage the latest tools and devices to enhance learning experiences. Reuben S. Kerben is a serial entrepreneur that has dedicated his career to building and launching innovative software and consumer internet companies. Reuben is currently Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer of Encameo, a company that allows television and movie viewers to identify and learn more about the products, people, places and media they see or hear on screen. Reuben is also the CEO and Founder of Edsy, an online learning company that delivers live homework help and test preparation services to students. Reuben is also a managing partner in Hollywood based, Charlie Baby Productions, a film and television production company, currently producing the film Whaling, and the television show Aussie Girl. Prior to these ventures Reuben was Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer of Spiral Universe, the first cloud based school management software company. There he led the company through dramatic growth eventually serving schools, colleges and universities in 115 countries throughout the world before stepping down. Before Spiral Universe, Reuben launched several other businesses in the software space among them; Bionex, a biometric hardware and security software company, Devani Gem, an ecommerce software platform for the jewelry industry, and Spiral Software Systems, the a web based student information software system for K-12 independent schools. Reuben's drive to develop new ideas has been frequently rewarded with top honors in business competitions such as the Palo Alto Business Plan Competition, the Panasci Business Plan Competition at Syracuse University, and the Sy Syms Business Plan competition at Yeshiva University. Reuben holds a bachelor of science in finance from the Sy Syms School of Business at Yeshiva University and a juris doctor degree from Hofstra University School of Law. Reuben is an advisor and investor in several private startup companies, avidly supports environmental and wild-life causes, and is currently awaiting his admission to the NYS Bar.
