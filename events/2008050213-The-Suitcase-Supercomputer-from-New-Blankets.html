The Suitcase Supercomputer from New Blankets

May 2, 2008 1:16 PM

Joe Deken of UCSD will present a demonstration of the suitcase supercomputer project that he is developing in conjunction with his colleagues in New Blankets. New Blankets is a not-for-profit entity being formed by a group of people across many different ages, professions and other walks of life. The goal of New Blankets is to work together to invent and contribute a special kind of new technology -- technology that is expressly designed, dedicated, and interwoven with the support and launching of learning communities. The focus of New Blankets is not formal schools but rather the many re-inventions possible today of great democratic learning traditions. In particular, they wish to carry forward the historic innovations that have touched us personally: the Carnegie Public Library, the bookmobile, and the tradition of the printing press itself. The goal is the networked support of mutually enriching learning societies, the discovery of many emerging new forms of games and "books." -- New forms of "burning coals" that in Voltaire's words we can "fetch from our neighbor, kindle at home, and become the property of all." New Blankets is initially working on a "Suitcase Supercomputer" -- a portable combination of supercomputing "backstage engine" and cellular/deconstructible "video wall," -- all made by using common video game components. "Suit Sup" is designed to boost and encourage many cultural venues and learning groups, for people of all ages. The project will specifically envision and draw on experience with "after school" and other informal settings for young people.
