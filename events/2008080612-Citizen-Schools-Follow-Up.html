Citizen Schools Follow-Up

Aug 6, 2008 12:15 PM

Nitzan Pelman, Sanda Balaban, Asha Strazzero-Wild and Hui Soo Chae will meet to discuss collaboration opportunities between Citizens Schools and EdLab. Citizen Schools is a national non-profit education reform organization with sites all across the country now expanding to New York City in Fall 2008. The program caters to middle-school youth during the out of school hours, Monday to Thursday, extending the number of hours students spend in active learning environments by providing academic support and 21st century skill development opportunities critical for success in today's society. One core ingredient of the Citizen Schools experience is student apprenticeships. Citizen Schools recruits volunteers from the local community and the private sector to become Citizen Teachers who lead powerful hands-on apprenticeship courses. Each apprenticeship is an 11-week course that introduces students to a new field and in which they create a high-quality product, service, or presentation demonstrating skill mastery and giving back to the community. For example, students publish newspapers, manage stock portfolios, design public spaces, litigate mock trials, and create web sites, through which learning is made relevant.
