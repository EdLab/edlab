Seminar: Putting Your Message Out There

Mar 27, 2009 9:29 PM

Putting your message out there: An integrative project from the International Educational Development (IED) department at TC. The seminar topics will include: participatory research methods, google mapping, media education, andÂ  linguistic & semiotic resources! For this seminar Doug Beacom will share his experiences in conducting participatory research with middle school students on the topic of text in public spaces. This case study is a product of his praxis of graduate school theory and hands-on, classroom fieldwork. The project culmination includes a collaborative google map highlighting public text in a NYC neighborhood, student reflections on an open-ended project and findings/implications for the teacher-as-researcher or advocate of educational equity.Â  The EdLab connection: As this project grows into a collaborative video with photojournalist Martha Cooper, EdLab can contribute to brainstorming and building out the idea of documenting Ms.Cooper's first attempt at geo-blogging her photos (she will attempt to build a map similar to the students in this case study). *Participation Alert*Â this seminar is open to anyone, but Doug asks that you complete one simple homework assignment: please photograph (mobile phone cams will work!) 1-2 examples of 'text in public space' in preparation for this seminar (e.g., anything that could be considered "languaging" in public--street signs, posters, stickers, graffiti, shop signs, billboards, advertisement, etc.). Please email the files in JPEG format to Doug by Tuesday evening (3/31): dougbeacom at gmail dot com The photos will be used in the seminar during a hand-on session to create our own map — please remember where you shot the photo!
