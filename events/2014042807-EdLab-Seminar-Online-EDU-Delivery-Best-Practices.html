EdLab Seminar: Online EDU Delivery Best Practices

Apr 28, 2014 7:12 AM

Veteran educators from industry leader, HotChalk, will share their experiences and best practices in online teaching and instructional delivery. Learn about their successes and lessons learned in delivering student-centric, online education programs from traditional, private, non-profit universities. In collaboration with their selective partners, HotChalk delivers leading outcomes with greater than 90% persistence rates and greater than 87% on-time graduation rates. Speakers will include: Mary Jane T. Pearson - Dr. Pearson has more than forty years of teaching experience, including her service as a tenured Full Professor at the largest, most diverse university system in the nation, California State University, where she was awarded the distinction of Professor Emeritus. Dr. Pearson holds a Ph.D. in Special Education with a minor in Law from the University of Kansas, and has been an Adjunct Professor for the University of San Francisco for the past 13 years, teaching education and research courses and supervising student teachers. Nicholas Smith - Chief Learning Officer - Nicholas has spent more than 25 years in education and technology. Most recently, he was President of Macmillan Labs for Higher Education, an R&D group dedicated to building innovative technology products that solve problems for students and teachers in elegant ways. Prior to joining Macmillan, Nicholas spent nine years at Aplia, a leading provider of online homework for higher education, where he helped to grow the company from startup to profitability and finally to successful acquisition by Cengage Learning in 2007. Nicholas remained with Cengage Learning as Vice President of Software Products until 2009. His experience also includes work at SRI Consulting, Pensare, and Agile Mind, as well as a decade teaching middle school students. He earned his master's degree at Brandeis University. Scott Altschuler - VP Business Development - Scott has helped shape the K12 and Higher Education services, software, and content space for over 13 years. Prior to his work at HotChalk, Scott was a Director at Discovery Education where he helped the organization grow their streaming video offering to dominant market adoption. Scott is a graduate of Miami University (Oxford, OH) with a Bachelor of Philosophy and Interdisciplinary Studies. Open to all members of the university community.
