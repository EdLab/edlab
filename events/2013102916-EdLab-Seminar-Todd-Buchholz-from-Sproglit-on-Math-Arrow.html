EdLab Seminar: Todd Buchholz from Sproglit on Math Arrow

Oct 29, 2013 4:20 PM

Todd Buchholz, former White House economic adviser, NPR and PBS commentator, and CEO of educational software firm Sproglit, will talk about the Math Arrow, a new way to convey number sense to children. The Math Arrow has been called "ingenious" by Martin Cooper, inventor of the cellular phone, and has been featured by the Mathematical Association of America under the headline "Math Arrow to Replace Number Line?" Sproglit is creating apps for children based on the Math Arrow, and its first game, Kyle Counts, is highlighted in the most recent issue of the Economist magazine. A recent study by researchers at BYU showed that playing Kyle Counts raised arithmetic test scores among first-graders. The Math Arrow and Kyle Counts are now being used in several schools in Harlem and Bedford-Stuyvesant. Todd, who won the annual teaching prize in economics at Harvard, will also discuss the importance of numeracy and financial literacy for the economy. He is the author of such books as New Ideas from Dead Economists, From Here to Economy, Rush, et al. He is also a coproducer of the Broadway hit Jersey Boys. www.toddbuchholz.com Open to all members of the university community.
