EdLab Seminar: Distributed Mentoring: Scaffolding Learning in Educational Video Games with Padraig Nash

Mar 22, 2012 4:22 PM

Padraig Nash from the University of Wisconsin will join us for this seminar. Although relationships with mentors are theorized to provide a range of potential assets and resources to adolescents (Rhodes, 2005; Rhodes, Spencer, Keller, Liang, & Noam, 2006), strong empirical grounding for mentoring's effects is significantly lacking (DuBois & Karcher, 2005; Freedman, 1999). Distributed mentoring is a theory of learning which argues that the interaction between a learner and a mentoring system, rather than a learner and an individual mentor, is the proper unit of analysis for analyzing pedagogical support. Building on theories of distributed cognition (Pea, 1993), epistemic frame theory (Shaffer, 2006), and positive youth development (Larson, 2006), distributed mentoring offers a new theoretical and methodological approach to understand mentoring.
