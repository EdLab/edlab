EdLab Seminar: Online and Blended Teacher Professional Development from the American Museum of Natural History

Sep 7, 2014 3:01 PM

This seminar will be led by Robert V. Steiner, Director, Online Teacher Education Programs; David Randle, Senior Manager of Professional Development; and Daniel Wolff, Manager of Educational Technology, all of the American Museum of Natural History. For the past 15 years, the American Museum of Natural History has provided online teacher professional development for K-12 educators through its Seminars on Science program. The program seeks to deepen scientific understandings and to provide digital classroom resources for educators across the United States and around the world. Each of its 13 offerings is a semester-equivalent graduate course in the life, earth or physical sciences. The courses leverage the scientific expertise, the exhibitions and the expertise of Education Department staff in the creation of accessible and innovative scientific explorations for adult learners. We review the essential features of the program, including its goals, genesis and evolution, its innovative aspects and the results of its independent evaluation. We consider a number of opportunities, challenges and issues. In addition, we focus on some key extensions of the program: (1) integration with other educational programs; (2) offerings that blend online and in-person modalities; and (3) Massive Open Online Courses. Open to all members of the university community.
