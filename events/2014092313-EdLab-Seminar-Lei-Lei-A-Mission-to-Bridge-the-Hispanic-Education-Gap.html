EdLab Seminar: ¡Leí Leí! A Mission to Bridge the Hispanic Education Gap

Sep 23, 2014 1:12 PM

The Hispanic population accounts for 17% of the US population. By 2048, we are expected to rise to 31% (Pew Hispanic). My community is currently facing an education gap. ¡Leí Leí! has set out on a mission to help begin to bridge that gap. Our product focuses on Mexican families, the largest part of the Hispanic population by two-thirds, with children ages 3—5. Our goals are to provide parents with information about the education gap, unique literacy exercises to build the children's educational foundation, and connect them to initiatives and resources. ¡Leí Leí! to the rescue! We plan to make change happen through a suite of appisodes for android smartphones. They feature friendly bilingual Mexican-American children that reflect the child's identity as well as teach the early literacy skills. Please help us get this product out into the world and into the hands of the people that need it the most by donating to our Kickstarter here: http://bit.ly/llksap1 Website: www.lei-lei.org Twitter: @leilei_app Francisco Hernandez is the founder of ¡Leí Leí! and recent graduate of the School of Visual Arts MFAD program 'Designer as Author + Entrepreneur.' He is a multidisciplinary graphic designer that used his passion for education, culture and language to develop the concept of ¡Leí Leí! as his thesis in the SVA program. He currently works for Citia, a software startup, led by serial entrepreneur and his former professor Linda Holiday. Open to all members of the university community.
