Designing Educational Innovations — DesignEd

May 26, 2009 11:30 AM

Ever wonder how to design educational innovations? Could there be guidelines, principles or even a framework to help with the process? In 2007, Alvin Toefler wrote: “The public school system is designed to produce a workforce for an economy that will not be there. And therefore, with all the best intentions in the world, we're stealing the kids' future.” “I just feel it's inevitable that there will have to be change. The only question is whether we're going to do it starting now, or whether we're going to wait for catastrophe.” A design-based process framework for generating educational innovations offers a chance for preparing educators of today and tomorrow to create educational change through innovation. This would help to develop the skills students will need in the 21st century to solve our major future challenges. Based on the general idea of utilizing a design thinking process to generate better innovations, we want to focus on the following problem: How can an educational innovation design process framework be designed to support a better generation of sustainable educational innovations for the EdLab? During the May 27th seminar, Frederik will lead EdLabbers in helping to create a new board game: "DesignEd." DesignEd is a choice-chance mapping game that introduces the the six-step educational innovation design process. Observe a class! Build a Prototype! Test your concept! Methods and mayhem collide as players move from challenge to solution, mapping their design process along the way.
