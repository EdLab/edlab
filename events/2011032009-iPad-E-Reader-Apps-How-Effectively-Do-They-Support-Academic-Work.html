iPad E-Reader Apps: How Effectively Do They Support Academic Work?

Mar 20, 2011 9:49 AM

Zhou Zhou, Ting Yuan, Hui Soo Chae, and Joann Agnitti will present their paper on the potential of iPad E-Reader applications for academic work as part of the poster session on Technology, Instruction, and Cognition at the Annual Meeting of the American Educational Research Association. The session will take place at the New Orleans Sheraton hotel in Grand Ballroom C. Abstract In this pilot study, the authors examined six major functionalities across eight iPad e-reader applications in order to assess how effectively certain e-reader features support academic work. The results were then compared to the perceived usefulness of these features by 12 undergraduate and graduate students. Our analysis revealed there is a disconnection between participants' expectations for an academic e-reader and the functionalities most commonly available on iPad e-reader apps. Specifically, the features that participants believed to be most central to academic work were found to be lacking in the iPad e-reader apps in this pilot study. Furthermore, our findings underscore the need to design an e-reader model specifically for active, participatory learning in higher education institutions.
