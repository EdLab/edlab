Seminar: Education Innovation through Design, Part I of IV: The Design Process

Oct 3, 2008 3:57 PM

What is the value of approaching teaching, learning, and understanding in a designerly way? The October 8 seminar will provide an opportunity to consider and discuss the design process and how it might apply to our own work. Before attending the seminar, read chapter 2 of Designerly Ways of Knowing, by Nigel Cross (you can get a copy from Jeff, Hui Soo, Brian, Joachim, or Stephanie). We're seeking a few volunteers interested in helping to frame our seminar discussion from different perspectives. Speak with Stephanie if you'd like to contribute to the seminar in this way. Potential areas of focus include (but are not limited to):

  • Problems versus solutions
  • The apprenticeship model
  • Ill-defined problems
  • Design in the post-industrial era
  • Design and multiple intelligence theory
  • Innovation through design
  • Invention through design
  • Design and creativity
    • Everyone is also invited to seed the discussion by posting questions and comments on the dry erase board by the front door to the EdLab (by Stephanie's desk). This is the first of a four-part seminar series investigating the potential of a design approach to educational innovation.
