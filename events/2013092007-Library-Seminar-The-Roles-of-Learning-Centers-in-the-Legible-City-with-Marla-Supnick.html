Library Seminar: The Roles of Learning Centers in the Legible City with Marla Supnick

Sep 20, 2013 7:00 AM

Learning centers operate in fast moving environments that generate both challenges and possibilities. Several trends point to new possibilities for the dissemination of knowledge and ideas within intelligent liquid networks at an unprecedented rate. Trends such as big data, smart cities, cloud based collections, mobile library applications, digital way-finding, MOOCs, and social media offer learning centers and their users access to knowledge and information in an actionable form whose impact can be tracked. Unified Field will present our thinking on how to integrate new interactive platforms of technologies and media into the evolving smart cities to develop legible and learning cities. We will also explore, in a conversation with attendees, the role of learning centers in this new evolving network. Marla Supnick is the President of Unified Field, an award-winning, media and software development company, that she founded twenty years ago. She is a pioneer and recognized leader in new media experiences and interaction design. At Unified Field, Marla directs strategies for multi-channel communications programs, interactive exhibits and digital interactive experiences in public spaces, and for web and mobile applications. The firm creates content rich, experiential and interactive media for next-generation digital exhibits, learning environments and smart cities. Unified Field has forged new ground in applying media to create paradigm shifts in how we think about our world and ourselves. Their work can be experienced at world class museums, visitor's centers, major international brands and educational institutions around the world. Recent local clients include Sony Wonder Technology Museum, the New York Historical Society, Yale School of Management, Goldman Sachs, Liberty Science Center and National Geographic Society. Prior to founding Unified Field, Marla was a principal in 5 9 25 an international design collaboration. She was a board member of ThinkQuest, an organization dedicated to technological literacy for at risk students. She is an active member of National Association for Museum Education, the American Associate of Museums, the Association of Science and Technology Centers and the Women's Presidents Organization.
