EdLab Seminar: Possibility Not Prediction with Robbie McClintock

Mar 22, 2012 4:57 PM

In his recently published book, Robbie McClintock explores the difference between causal action of one thing on another in sequential time and reciprocal interactions among things that coexist in time and space. Lived, existential experience in the historical present comprises all sorts of simultaneous interactions taking place without a manifest cause unequivocally determining them. As these interactions take place, they define networks, which are dynamic and unbounded. He suggests that the long era of modernity, in which people relied primarily on causal action, is beginning to give way to a world in which reciprocal interaction is key to what takes place in life for persons and publics. He will reflect on possible long-term implications such changes for the ways people engage in their education and self-formation. Open to all members of the university community.
