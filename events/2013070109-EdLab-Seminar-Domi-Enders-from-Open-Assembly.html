EdLab Seminar: Domi Enders from Open Assembly

Jul 1, 2013 9:09 AM

Open Assembly is a cloud-based platform that allows instructors to assemble and share exceptional learning and curricular materials with their students, at a price all can afford. Our platform supports blended/hybrid and online course delivery. Educators can:

  • Intuitively assemble resources and activities in a digital playlist (proprietary and open)
  • Discover best in class open educational resources (OER)
  • Easily manage digital rights
  • Personalize learning and resources for each learner or group of students
  • Deliver learning to students in an interactive personal learning environment (PLE)
  • Analyze student engagement and progress with course analytics
Our Open Library aggregates curricular resources ranging from expert-curated, chapter-level proprietary content to best in class OERs. Our community of curators includes educators, researchers, librarians and students with a passionate commitment to their area of expertise. Learning resources include open textbooks, articles, video, audio and image resources, documentary films, and more. Domitilla Enders is an architect, educator, and industry expert in digital learning content. Prior to founding Open Assembly, she taught in the SUNY college system and ran her own NYC-based architecture firm for over 15 years. She holds degrees from Yale, The Cooper Union, and Parsons the New School of Design. Twitter: @OpenAssemblyEdu Open to all members of the university community.
