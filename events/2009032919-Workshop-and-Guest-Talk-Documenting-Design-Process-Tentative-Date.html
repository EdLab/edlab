Workshop & Guest Talk: Documenting Design Process (Tentative Date)

Mar 29, 2009 7:01 PM

The design process is rarely visible in its products. But that does not mean it does not have meaning. EdLab is interested in finding out how documenting process--the path from ideas to end-results--adds to a project's value, and increases understanding about the designer and the process itself. The EdLab Design Team has invited artists, designers, and inventors to speak about their methods of documentation and why it is important to their process. Using the exhibit 100 Years: A Centennial Celebration of the Program in Nutrition at Teachers College as a starting point, workshop participants will discuss existing methods of documentation and ways they might be improved, changed, or reimagined to contribute to the project's meaning. The workshop coincides with the closing of the 100 Years exhibit. Anyone is invited to participate in this event. Check this post often for the confirmed date, location, and list of speakers. Once confirmed, RSVP will be required to attend. Where: M. W. Offit Gallery, 3rd Floor
