EdLab Seminar: Instructional Design at General Assembly--Inside Building an Online Course

Jul 18, 2016 12:50 AM

General Assembly (GA) is a global educational company on a mission to empower a global community to pursue work they love. Focusing on the most relevant and in-demand skills across data, design, business and technology, GA is confronting a skills gap through best-in-class instruction and providing access to opportunities. GA works with students online and in person across 25 campuses in 6 countries. GA also works with employers to help companies source talent, assess talent and transform talent. Additionally, GA’s focus on affordable and accessible education combined with our education-to-employment approach is helping to create a diverse talent pipeline.​ In this EdLab Seminar, Carmen James will describe the process from start to finish for building online courses at General Assembly. Carmen James is the lead instructional designer for online courses at General Assembly, a global education-to-employment company specializing in the most in-demand skills. After completing her PhD in Philosophy and Education at Teachers College, Columbia University in May 2015, she went on to work with K12 teachers and taught at the elementary and graduate level. She has a long-standing interest in innovative teaching and learning practices. She has also served in a research and development role at Riverdale Country School, where she lead a team that implemented the IDEO human-centered design process with fellow teachers. Additionally, Carmen has worked at the EdLab, the innovation center at Teachers College, CU, and helped develop a platform for 'having meaningful discussions' online. She graduated from Harvard University with her B.A. in 2008.
