EdLab Seminar: Urban Exploration to Entrepreneurship

Mar 7, 2011 11:32 AM

Kate Meersschaert will share her her journey to entrepreneurship through her experience creating 2 blogs, one of which went on to become a successful book project. Kate used smartphone technology to document the often-overlooked signs, symbols and surfaces of Brooklyn’s quickly changing urban landscape and the discussion will center around new and emerging ways to use technology to increase entrepreneurship. In addition, we will discuss what it means to be an entrepreneur and how is entrepreneurship unique in the Education space? Kate has a passion for exploring how technology and social media can help reinvent and break-down the barriers to experiencing of creating art. She can often be found combing her neighborhood, with her dog, looking for images and urban “artifacts.” Kate received her BFA in Fine Arts and Art Education from the University of Arizona and a certificate in Urban Permaculture Design from New York’s Open Center.
