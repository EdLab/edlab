Topic: Learning Without Boundaries: Helping Teachers Utilize Space as a Pedagogic Tool

Leaders: Dr Marian Mahat, The University of Melbourne

Description:

Abstract

The Australian Research Council has invested an unprecedented amount of funding into a study that will help teachers improve their teaching. The Innovative Learning Environments and Teacher Change Project is being run over four years in Australia and New Zealand. It has eight PhDs undertaking work supervised by three Research Fellows, eight Chief Investigators and a full administration team.

This team is building evidence about types and use of learning spaces in primary and secondary schools, and from this, developing strategies to assist teachers best use of the ‘new designs’ of schools sweeping the globe. These so-called ‘innovative learning environments’ are characterized by highly adaptable spaces, specialised furniture and integrated use of ICT. They push teachers to rethink how students must learn into the coming decades, and as a consequence how teaching must change to facilitate this. The project has fifteen industry partners including state and national education departments, a world-leading furniture firm, Australia’s leading telecommunications company, architectural firms and acoustics engineering firms.

This extra-ordinary project is helping educators better understand the way the physical learning environment affects quality of learning and teaching. Its Lead Research Fellow, and Research Manager (Dr Marian Mahat) will summarize the project and explain results already achieved. The latter included world-first data proving that the spaces we learn in do have an effect on quality of teaching and student learning.

Biography

Dr Marian Mahat is a Senior Research Fellow at the University of Melbourne. As the Lead Research Fellow and Research Manager of the Innovative Learning Environments and Teacher Change (ILETC) project, she oversees all research activities and the dissemination of project findings including the management of Research Fellows and graduate researchers. Marian has more than twenty years of professional and academic experience, spanning several universities, Federal and local governments, as well as the private sector. Her career builds on five qualifications obtained at renowned institutions in Singapore, the United Kingdom and Australia.

The major focus of her research contribution is improving student learning and outcomes in education. Her work is impelled by a desire to make enduring contributions to theory, methodology and practice in various contexts from pre-school to higher education to improve student learning and outcomes. Highly proficient in both quantitative and qualitative research methods, she disseminates this work through collaborative projects, publications and presentations in education.
