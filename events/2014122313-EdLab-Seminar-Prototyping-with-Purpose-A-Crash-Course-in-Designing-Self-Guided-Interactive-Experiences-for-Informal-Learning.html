EdLab Seminar: Prototyping with Purpose: A Crash Course in Designing Self-Guided Interactive Experiences for Informal Learning

Dec 23, 2014 1:13 PM

In prototyping, change is good…change is what we are seeking. We must learn not to be emotionally attached to our original design ideas. We must be open minded and on the hunt for opportunities to make meaningful change. We will discuss strategies for planning test sessions that propel prototypes through the iterative funnel swiftly, productively, and lead us towards a more effective finished product. Have fun during your sessions, put your testers at ease, and no matter how hard it may be, don't correct them! The “mistakes” they make are invaluable to your success. Sarah Gray, interactive exhibit designer and prototyping aficionado will share experiences from her work creating informal learning spaces in the field of interactive environment design. Success in these venues often means fostering meaningful engagement through play and discovery. We must create an environment with room to fail forward, and plan for multiple outcomes and audience capabilities. Sarah will highlight the unique challenges presented by self guided learning spaces such as children's museums, science centers, and libraries; sharing strategies for designing within their quirky parameters. During the second half of our session we will break into teams and conduct our own prototyping workshop. Our tools at this stage are simple. This simplicity means making changes now–before fabrication–is simple as well. Low or no-tech paper prototypes can be changed easily. They are far superior to even the most durable plywood contraption or functional, sleek software GUI. Scotch tape adjusts faster than a wood screw, and a person hiding behind foam core going “blip bloop blap” swapping out index cards on a template is the best prototyping computer you can find.
