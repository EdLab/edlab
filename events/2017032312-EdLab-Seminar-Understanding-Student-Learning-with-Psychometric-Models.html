EdLab Seminar: Understanding Student Learning with Psychometric Models

Mar 23, 2017 12:00 PM - Mar 23, 2017 1:30 PM

Seung Yeon Lee will talk about her recent work and its relevance to the education sector.

Topic: Understanding Student Learning with Psychometric Models

Leader: Seung Yeon Lee, University of California, Berkeley

Description: The ultimate goal of education is to foster and improve student’s learning experience. To that end, education professionals - teachers and researchers alike - have dedicated significant effort to formulate various methodologies. Schools have implemented different types of intervention schemes that include different types of teaching materials and supplemental use of technological tools (e.g., iPad, computer). Beyond traditional schooling, online courses and other modes of learning (e.g., MOOC) have been introduced as well.

An important question to ask, however, is whether these tools “really” do improve students’ learning experience. That is when educational measurement steps in; educational measurement allows researchers to diagnose students’ scholastic improvements and can lead to the iterative improvement of learning tools. In this talk, SeungYeon will discuss the application of statistical measurement models (psychometric models) in order to better understand learners’ knowledge/skill mastery. In particular, she will introduce a new model from her dissertation that jointly measures knowledge states on multiple skills and learners’ growth over time.
