EdLab Seminar: UClass

Apr 2, 2013 8:42 AM

Leah Schrader, co-founder of the rapidly growing K12 global project sharing website UClass, will talk about the development of global citizenship through online exchanges as a primary method to prepare students for a 21st cenury world. She will engage participants in discussions about why global education needs to be a classroom priority and how UClass offers students a fun, relevant way to connect across the world. Participants will receive an in depth tour of UClass as well as develop both a pedagogical understanding of global education and practical action steps for implementing it in their educational settings. What is UClass? UClass is a K12 global project-sharing community that reduces teacher planning/grading time and offers students a community of global classmates. Leveraging the features of popular social networks, UClass seeks to develop global citizenship by uniting primary and secondary school classrooms across the world around teacher-created projects on a Facebook-era pen pal network. UClass -- which has partnerships with Teach For America, Teach For All and the Asia Society -- is already being used in over 70 countries around the world. Three of its founders are Columbia University alumni. Open to all members of the university community.
