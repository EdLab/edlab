Invited Dress: Literacy Unbound Remixes the Heart of Darkness

Jul 19, 2017 3:00 PM - Jul 19, 2017 4:00 PM

This is a rehearsal of the Heart of Darkness event.

Since July 10th, a group of 25 students and teachers have been busy remixing, creating, and performing in TC’s Smith Learning Theater as part of the 4th annual Literacy Unbound Summer Institute. The institute is an intensive, experimental course for the passionate and the curious, where students and teachers take risks together while collaborating side by side. This small cohort of participants (known as “players”) generate an immersive, site-specific performance inspired by a shared text - one that’s frequently read in secondary classrooms (found in many school book rooms, within many standard curricula), but often challenging for students.

This year’s selection? Joseph Conrad’s Heart of Darkness, a novel that’s been heralded as one of the greatest in the English language, yet has also been denounced as offensive and deplorable. With the help of CPET facilitators who are versed in navigating literacy from text to production and guest teaching artists who specialize in drama, movement, and music, the Literacy Unbound players step into this text through improvisational sound and movement, the interplay between physical and digital space, and the remixing of text on text.
