Seminar: Eric Rosenbaum on Tools to Cultivate Reflective Learning

Apr 24, 2009 4:29 PM

How can we help kids reflect on their learning process as they design and create? Scratch is a graphical programming language that lets children create their own games, animations and interactive art and share them on the web. With such powerful tools for creation, and the resources of a web-based community to draw on, there are many opportunities for learning. By reflecting on their learning process, children may benefit by, for example, seeing the value of mistakes, identifying emotional processes, and discovering strategies for getting unstuck. Eric will present his current master's research on the creation of the Jots system to cultivate reflective learning in Scratch. Eric Rosenbaum is a Master's student in the Lifelong Kindergarten Group at the MIT Media Lab. His interests include collaborative learning in shared spaces, authenticity in learning environments, musical improvisation for novices, and learning through constructing science simulations. He has an undergraduate degree in psychology and Mind/Brain/Behavior and a Master's degree in Technology in Education from Harvard University. View some of Eric's projects at his website.
