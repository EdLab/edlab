EdLab Seminar: Interactive Geography with Ben Shapiro

Aug 8, 2018 12:00 PM - Aug 8, 2018 1:30 PM

In this interactive talk, Ben Rydal Shapiro will outline his development and use of interaction geography, an interdisciplinary approach to describe, represent, and interpret people’s interaction as they move within and across physical environments.

Topic: Learning from Exhibits


Leader: Ben Shapiro


Description: In this interactive talk, Ben Rydal Shapiro will outline his development and use of interaction geography, an interdisciplinary approach to describe, represent, and interpret people’s interaction as they move within and across physical environments. In particular, he’ll illustrate his use of interaction geography to 1) collaborate with a cultural heritage museum to study visitor engagement and learning, 2) to visualize and support public discussion about New York City’s Stop-And-Frisk Program, and 3) to design new types of interactive learning environments for social studies and computer science education.
