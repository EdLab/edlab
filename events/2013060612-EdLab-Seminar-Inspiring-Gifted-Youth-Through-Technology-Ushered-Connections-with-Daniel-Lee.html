EdLab Seminar: Inspiring Gifted Youth Through Technology-Ushered Connections with Daniel Lee

Jun 6, 2013 12:53 PM

Many of the recent game-changing innovations in educational technology, including MOOCs and adaptive learning platforms, have focused on content and remediation. Their scope has centered on presenting quality academic content in accessible ways that help as many students as possible meet common core standards and/or pass standardized tests. Many of the inventors and visionaries leading this revolution have been identified as being academically gifted. This seminar will focus on the Duke Talent Identification Program's use of technology to enrich, connect, support, and inspire gifted students - the next generation of game-changers. Daniel B. Lee is currently the Assistant Director for Technology and Operations at Duke University's Talent Identification Program (TIP), which serves over 4,000 students per year in their distance and independent learning programs. Open to all members of the university community. If you are coming from outside the Edlab, please RSVP by June 14th by sending a message to Edlab at http://ednode.com/profiles/edlabtc/about (You must have/create an Ednode account and click on ).
