EdLab Seminar: Ponder

Jul 24, 2013 9:19 AM

Alex Selkirk, founder of the innovative critical reading tool Ponder, and Ben Stern, Academic Liaison for Ponder will seed a discussion around the use of technology in humanities classrooms, and attempt to answer the question: How can technology enhance the teaching of subjects whose fundamental questions have no single right answer? Ponder will be used during the seminar to allow participants to gather examples for discussion about the challenges the Humanities have faced in recent years. Alex and Ben will use Ponder to demonstrate that technology can quickly provide insight into each of a teachers' student's close reading efforts, help classes connect diverse resources around a single idea, and expand and deepen class discussions. Participants will receive a compelling defense of the need for a humanities education in the 21st century, an in-depth look at Ponder, and practical steps for using technology in non-STEM classes. What is Ponder? Ponder is the first “micro-reading response” tool. It gives teachers a view into the "invisible" process of learning through reading. In development since 2011, Ponder is a lightweight browser extension that adds an interactive, intellectual layer to internet content. Using the tool, students flag, react to and share excerpts from their online reading with their classmates. Their flags appear on a Class Feed, which students use to discuss their classmates' ideas and teachers use to generate further discussion in class. Meanwhile, Ponder's powerful data-collecting functionality enable a teacher to quantify how much and how deeply students are reading. Open to all members of the university community.
