Effects of the Digital Age on Research Standards and Academic Publishing - AERA Symposium

Feb 9, 2008 1:21 PM

Gary Natriello joins this interactive symposium (panel) to explore the ramifications of the digital age for academic scholarship, research and publishing, especially qualitative research. Paralleling the changes in the music entertainment industry, changes are afoot in the world of academic publishing, with the burgeoning of online journals and hybrid journals that incorporate digital and internet components. Journal editors will discuss such issues as: What does scholarship look like in a digital age? What effects have digital formatting and the internet had on research standards (i.e., rigor and peer review) and on dissemination? What are the advantages and disadvantages (and pitfalls) of digital age publishing? (For qualitative research studies, this might mean a relaxation of page limits, permitting fuller, richer descriptions of studies.) What are the other issues associated with this trend, issues such as format (graphics and video), accessibility, democratization, and fundamental changes in how we view research, scholarship and 'publishing'. New York Marriott Marquis Times Square/Duffy/Columbia Room, 7th Floor
