Failure: A Celebration

Jan 4, 2010 11:50 AM

The January issue of Wired has a special section on failure and what we can learn from it. For the first EdLab seminar of 2010, we will be considering the role of failure in our work at EdLab. Some questions to consider: How can we take value from failures? Are there good failures and bad failures? How can we tell the difference? How can we create conditions to allow for failure and learning? What have been some failures at EdLab? What did we learn from them? What EdLab failures should be reviewed further to make sure we learn from them? The Wired section has six pieces on failure. For the seminar, we need at least two people to be ready to lead a mini discussion of each of these pieces and what we might take from them for our work at the EdLab. The six pieces are: Accept Defeat: The Neuroscience of Screwing Up Stay in the Game: The Rise and Fall of Alec Baldwin Learn to Let Go: How Success Killed Duke Nukem Time Your Attack: Oracle's Lost Revolution My Greatest Mistake: Learn from Six Luminaries Accidental Art: Three Alternate Histories So, leave a comment to let us know which piece you will be responsible for at the seminar. For those not among the 12 (2 per article) signing up to lead the discussion, start thinking of a failure that you have experienced and what you learned from it.
