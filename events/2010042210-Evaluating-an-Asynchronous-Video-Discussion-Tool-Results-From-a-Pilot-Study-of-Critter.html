Evaluating an Asynchronous Video Discussion Tool: Results From a Pilot Study of "Critter"

Apr 22, 2010 10:24 AM

This presentation by Arianna Choi, Faisal Anwar, Zhou Zhou, and Hui Soo Chae (delivered by Zhou Zhou) is part of the paper session on Using Technology to Support Teaching and Learning. The paper will be delivered at the AERA Annual Meeting in Denver at the Sheraton-Denver in Governor's Square 9. Abstract: This paper reports on the results of a two-part evaluation of Critter, a web-based application for asynchronous video discussion designed to support social reflective learning. Participants (N=40) were composed of undergraduate and graduate students, education faculty, and library staff. In the first phase, participants were directed to use then complete a user experience survey. In the second phase, same participants were organized into focus groups to identify potential use cases within specific learning scenarios. The results underscore three major areas for improvement: 1) the user interface, 2) the organization of user-generated comments, and 3) the integration of popular social networking sites. We conclude with a discussion about our decision-making process and an upgrade plan for a public beta launch of Critter.
