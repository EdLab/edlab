EdLab Seminar: Doug Silver from WriterKEY

Oct 17, 2013 11:17 AM

Doug Silver (TC '94) is the Chief Academic Officer and a founder of the web-based application, WriterKEY. He will talk about how the current, K-12 school funding climate demands educators to find tools that improve the effectiveness and efficiency of instruction. Using WriterKEY as an example, Doug will engage participants in examining how digital tools can be thoughtfully designed to actually improve the engagement of all types of learners and enhance the relationship between a teacher and a student. Participants will receive an in-depth tour of how teachers and their students use WriterKEY to engage in a sustained, formative feedback loop to improve a student's writing. What is WriterKEY? WriterKEY is a web-application designed to help writing teachers give formative feedback to students effectively and efficiently. WriterKEY is not an electronic grader; in fact, every teacher comment becomes part of the rich data analytics that can guide instruction and monitor progress. Student skill building tools are fully integrated into the system to foster collaborative improvement. Across the curriculum, teachers who use WriterKEY give better, more targeted feedback to students because the tool helps teachers focus on the link between assessment and instruction.
