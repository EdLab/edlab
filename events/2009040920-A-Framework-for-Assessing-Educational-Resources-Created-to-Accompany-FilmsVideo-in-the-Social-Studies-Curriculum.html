A Framework for Assessing Educational Resources Created to Accompany Films/Video in the Social Studies Curriculum

Apr 9, 2009 8:10 PM

Thu, Apr 16 - 10:35am - 12:05pm Building/Room: San Diego Marriott Hotel & Marina / San Francisco Film use in social studies courses has grown considerably over the past two decades. Films supplement curricula by "demonstrating" topics or concepts in opposition to traditional instructional method. The educational resources/materials that accompany films are intended to enhance their value to social studies educators. However, little research has been done to theorize elements these materials should contain in order to support particular goals of social studies education. In this study, the researchers analyze a sample of educational materials created for "social studies" films and theorize a framework to assess the value of these materials with respect to critical media literacy and neuroscience. Furthermore, based on the analysis of the given materials, they hypothesize what elements would enhance students' learning experiences. Authors: Joann Agnitti, Hui Soo Chae, George Nantwi
