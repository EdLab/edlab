Participatory Culture, Web 2.0 and Networked Learning

Jan 6, 2009 7:05 PM

Advances in communications and computing technologies have empowered individuals to share their intellectual, cultural, and creative expressions with wider and more diverse audiences than ever before. Made possible through Web 2.0 Internet technologies, this emerging participatory culture has been hypothesized to having a great deal of potential for advancing education and learning by moving the locus of activity from existing power relationships (consumer/producer, expert/novice, and teacher/student) to one that focuses on the individual's empowerment and willingness to construct and contribute to one's cultural and physical reality. Despite this potential, there is little research that looks to understand how such Web 2.0 technologies deployed into specific communities do (or do not) make possible these goals. In this seminar, Anthony Cocciolo will discuss a study which investigates a Web 2.0 technology in use by a specific community over a two-year period. In particular, he will be looking at the ways in which the technology and users interact to form social and knowledge networks, and what these networks illustrate about the ability of the technology to make possible a participatory culture.
