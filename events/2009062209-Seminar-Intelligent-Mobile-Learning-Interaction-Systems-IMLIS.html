Seminar: Intelligent Mobile Learning Interaction Systems (IMLIS)

Jun 22, 2009 9:21 AM

Saeed Zare from the Digital Media in Education (DIMEB) group at the University of Bremen, Germany will present the Intelligent Mobile Learning Interaction System (IMLIS). IMLIS provides a learning environment for individuals with learning dis/abilities with mobile computing as the learning context. IMLIS provides different outputs according to the user, the history of usage, and the suitable learning method. It firstly analyzes and identifies specific requirements, constraints and conditions of the person through interaction. The system divides the learning activities and interactions into smaller, controllable and manageable workloads, repeating learning material in a period of time, using an easy language and preparing more visualized material facilitate the learning process. The design of IMLIS adjusts its content and customizes learning material via its dynamic structure and flexible patterns that allow it to adapt to the learning ability and preferred learning style of the user. The researchers apply knowledge from the field of research and practice with learning disabled people as well as pedagogical and didactical aspects in the design. Background on Saeed Saeed Zare is a Ph.D. candidate and researcher in the work group Digital Media in Education at the University of Bremen, Germany. He received his B.Sc. in computer software engineering, and his M.Sc. in Digital Media (e-learning) from University of Bremen. He is currently working on the field "Mobile Learning" for special target group "people with learning/mental disabilities". The research interests focus on educational aspects and learning approaches with respect to the pedagogy view in user interface design based on mobile technology. In addition to his research, he has also courses on mobile learning for master students in digital media in University of Bremen.
