EdLab Seminar: Kafka’s Little Coding Machines: Deleuze + Guattari, Learning with Experimental Techno-Assemblages

Sep 13, 2016 1:34 PM

The thought of Gilles Deleuze and Felix Guattari—an attempt to engage the flows and codes of our world as a process available for experimentation—is just recently being taken up seriously within educational theory. As the question of education’s own codes, machinic assemblages, and technological forms becomes foregrounded, it seems increasingly important to be able to contextualize these experimentations. What do we learn from machines of all sorts; what do we need to learn about them? This talk will use Deleuze and Guattari’s analysis of Kafka’s writing as little coding machines as a way into what we could call “educational/experimental modes of becoming.” Chris Moffett, PhD., is a philosopher of education, teaching play, spatial/urban practices, and the aesthetics of technology at Teachers College. He is especially interested in the availability of educational “codes” such as syllabuses, to be productively reworked within the digital landscape. An early adopter of Pressible (splittingskulls.com), you can also find his aesthetic and somatic work at: aestheticrelationalexercises.com, aisforeducation.xyz, and poisedmeditation.com.
