Edit Jam: How do you teach democracy?

Feb 6, 2007 5:00 PM

Edit Jam is a confluence of imaginative minds at EdLab's Media Design Center. At this event, the second Edit Jam, we will explore the theme of teaching democracy with media and technology. This event coincides with the development of a curriculum to complement Spike Lee's "When the Levees Broke: A Requiem in Four Acts," the recent HBO Documentary Film about Hurricane Katrina and its aftermath in New Orleans. Created by TC faculty, students, and staff, the Teaching the Levees curriculum project is funded by the Rockefeller Foundation and will be made available to educators by next fall. (read more)
Preparing for the Democracy Jam (embedded video) EdLab Edit Jams explore new territories of collaborative production, where participants not only observe artists working, but also are invited to engage a creative circle. As an immersive aesthetic experience, Jams are unique educational experiences for exploring new technologies, media, and ideas. Expected in attendance are video artists, project participants from the Teaching the Levees curriculum project, live musicians, and the Nuyorican poet Mundo Rivera. Participants will play a role in the creative process by creating videos about democracy on a variety of experimental workstations. The Jam will be captured and broadcast to a worldwide audience this spring. The event is hosted by the staff of the Media Design Center. Please RSVP to the Design Center to attend. Participate for 15 minutes or all 3 hours! Where: 5th floor, EdLab Atelier
