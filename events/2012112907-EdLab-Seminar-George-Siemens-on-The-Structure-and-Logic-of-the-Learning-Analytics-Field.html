EdLab Seminar: George Siemens on The Structure and Logic of the Learning Analytics Field

Nov 29, 2012 7:21 AM

Analysis of data is hardly new - it forms the basis of all research. Over the past decade, and in particular the last several years, interest in data mining and analytics in education has grown dramatically. This interest is evident in the growth of startups targeting the learning space as well as the analytics 'value add' being touted by established software vendors. The interest is also evident in schools, policy makers, teachers, and higher education administrators. As more of the learning process is captured, through the growth of online learning and open online courses, it's reasonable to expect that analytics and data mining are still only at the early stages of impacting education. This presentation will offer a "state of the field" review of learning analytics, global activity, the change drivers, prominent technologies and techniques, and opportunities for researchers. Open to all members of the university community.
