Xuyang Shi

EdLab Alumni

Xuyang Shi came to Columbia University in 2008 to pursue his M.S. in Computer Science degree. He completed his B.S. in Software Engineering degree in Jul. 2008 from Beijing University of Technology. His interests include Intelligent System, Data Mining and Web-based information management system.

During his undergraduate study, Xuyang Shi worked as a research assistant in the IBM Research Lab in the School of Software Engineering at BJUT. During 2007-2008, he finished the graduate thesis BP Neural Network-based Forecast System for Global Stock Indices (NNFS), which awarded as the Excellent Undergraduate Thesis in BJUT. Also, he has lots of professional experience in software and database development. From Aug. 2007 to Oct. 2007 he worked as an website and database developer in Chinasoft International Ltd., he also worked in Accounting and Finance Department at Siemens Ltd., China from Dec. 2007 to Mar. 2008 as a database administrator.

Some of his projects:

- Google Android Mobile Phone Application-PartyLine The PartyLine application is a collaborative notification system for the Google Android mobile phone (T-Mobile G1). It allows remote stakeholders (already in attendance) to invite their friends to attend a party, show, or other event already in progress, by issuing invitations or by sending status updates directly or indirectly to their friends list. PartyLine is developed with the environment of Java Eclipse IDE integrated with Android SDK and Emulator. The application utilizes several built-in modules (e.g., GPS, Digital Camera, Touchscreen etc.) to provide the service for users.

- Word Vector Model-based Information Retrieval System Implemented an information retrieval system by using Java. The system can exploit the user-provided relevance feedback to disambiguate queries and improve the relevance of the query results returned by Yahoo BOSS API. Utilized a new and effective clustering method based on the word vector model (WVM), which is different from the traditional vector space model (VSM) method.

- VideO Processing Language (VOPL) Developed a compiler for our own designed programming language VOPL by using a functional language O’Caml. The compiler accepts the source code file, checks its syntax and semantics and generates the corresponding Matlab code. Currently, the VOPL supports operations for editing video files with AVI format.

- XML-based Educational Resource Management System Designed the XML structure for the system, utilized the DTD and Schema techniques to verify and input the data information. Created interface of the system by using CSS and XSL, also completed the XML files operation (insert, update and delete) using DOM and SAX techniques.
