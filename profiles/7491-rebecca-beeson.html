Rebecca Beeson

EdLab Alumni

Beckie Beeson began as an intern at the Edlab in the summer of 2015, and is beyond elated to be returning to Edlab as a member of the Development and Research Team. Having finished one year of a two-year Master's program at the University of Washington in Library and Information Science, she will be completing this program remotely and working with the Learning Theater to develop her Capstone Project. Aside from being motivated by the opportunities and possibilities that exist within libraries, her passions extend into social justice in education and the movements that promote open access. Some of the most formative experiences that have prompted her into this field have been the institutional and structured inequities present in the Bay Area's education and library system. She graduated from the University of California, Santa Barbara with a BA in Comparative Literature and an emphasis in Black Studies. She has spent the past 5 years as an independent tutor, and a teacher within various after-school programs and creative-writing non-profits, such as 826 Valencia and the Bureau of Fearless Ideas. One of her favorite projects was The Writing Table, where she worked with students directly, and developed writing curriculum for student publication in a quarterly printed at 826 Valencia. The intersection of education, literacy, and technology merge within libraries, and projects like the Edlab become the purveyors of what transpires at this junction.
