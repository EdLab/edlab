Jereri Edwards

EdLab Alumni

Jereri Edwards Was Born in Jamaica where he immigrated to the Unites States at the age of 15. He regroup with his parents and sibling in Mt Vernon New York. He attended Lincoln High School in Yonkers New York. Jereri was an outstanding athlete where he played for the mens soccer team and ran track also. He was the team's captain for 3 year, where he also played a center back position in the defense. He has won numerous awards for soccer such as League MVP, all section, all league. As for track he won major awards an medals from competitions around the country.

He later went on to ST Peters College in Jersey City, New Jersey. He the first of his siblings to attend College where he Major in Accounting and Play soccer. He made his decision on attending St Peters College after visiting the school and liking the atmosphere.This school also offered him a full athletic scholarship for 4years to study and play soccer. ST Peters College is one of 28 Jesuit Schools in the nation and it fit perfectly with what he was looking for. It has very small classrooms so the learning environment is not overcrowded, students get to have a relationship with their professors. He also play on one of the best schools for soccer in the nation where he is a starter since his freshman year in school.They are ranked 48th in the nation and made the NCAA Division I Tournament in 2007. He also ran track for St Peters where he do the sprinting events.

At young age Jereri Worked and send himself through high school. He worked at the Fountainhead Caterers in New Rochelle, New York as a waiter in 2003 to 2005. He also coached youngsters from the age of 4 to 14 at the Mt Vernon youth Soccer Club. Outside of of working, going to school and coaching soccer, he played for the Kearny Scots American Soccer Club in Kearny , New Jersey. He enjoys meeting people and getting to know and learn different cultures.

He is very family oriented and live life to the fullest. He knows what he wants out of life and He knows that in order to reach his ultimate goal he has to work hard and thats what he does day in day out. He enjoys working at the Ed lab at Columbia's Teachers College where he meets new people.
