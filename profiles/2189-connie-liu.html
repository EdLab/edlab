Connie Liu

EdLab Alumni

Connie Liu is an undergraduate student majoring in Computer Science at Columbia University. Her studies concentrate on Applications and Visions & Graphics. She is currently engaged in research at the EdLab regarding User Interfaces, with an emphasis on UI design heuristics/principles and implementation, which will be applied to the Teaching and Learning Network (TLN) project.

A View into the Past:

Here are some of the projects that she has worked on:

Design Fundamentals Using Advanced Computer Technologies, Columbia University: In 2008, she worked with peers on a community project concerning adjustable art tables for the students of Mt. Pleasant-Blythedale School, who are in assistive devices such as wheelchairs and walkers. She used Maya and AutoCAD to produce models for the adjustable art table. And as a group facilitator, she compiled design journals, PowerPoint presentations, and reports.

Morgan Stanley: In 2007, she interned for the summer at Morgan Stanley in the Information Technology department. Under the guidance of her mentor she worked with a fellow intern to create an Investment Management Monitoring Console in C# ASP.NET

Other Activities:

Apart from her computer science related activities, she loves ballroom dancing. Having started in her freshman year of college, she has progressed from having two left feet to being the president of the Columbia University Ballroom Dance Club for the 2010-2011 year.

And some other activities she partakes in her spare time are pen and ink drawing, and designing and making clothes ranging from simple neckties to ballroom costumes to multi-layer Victorian style dresses.
